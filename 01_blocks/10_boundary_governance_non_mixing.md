# PMS-AXIOM: Boundary, Governance, and Non-Mixing

## 12. Boundary, Governance, and Non-Mixing Discipline

This chapter records the boundary discipline that governs PMS-AXIOM as a whole.

PMS-AXIOM is a cartographic and indexing paper. It maps closure-demands, frames, residues, operator chains, add-on signatures, drift risks, output types, and optional governance hooks. It does not create a new base model, does not replace PMS, does not turn add-ons into operators, and does not convert structural readability into diagnosis, verdict, proof, enforcement, or authority.

The preceding parts show how the same PMS operator grammar can be rendered across different domains:

- PMS core cases;
- LOGIC cases;
- ANTICIPATION cases;
- CONFLICT cases;
- CRITIQUE cases;
- EDEN cases;
- SEX cases;
- QC / QC-EXT cases.

This chapter does not add another case layer. It states how the layers must remain separated.

---

## 12.1 Why a Boundary Chapter Is Needed

AXIOM produces highly structured artefacts. That creates a recurring risk: the more coherent an artefact becomes, the easier it is to mistake it for a stronger claim than it actually makes.

A PMS rendering may look like an explanation.

An add-on signature may look like a diagnosis.

A drift marker may look like a finding.

A MIP hook may look like a governance recommendation.

An AHP hardening field may look like evidentiary upgrade.

A QC audit trace may look like certification.

A consent-validity gate may look like legal or moral adjudication.

This chapter blocks those conversions.

The central rule is:

```text
structural readability is not authority
````

AXIOM may make a configuration more legible. It may not turn that legibility into person evaluation, moral ranking, enforcement guidance, metaphysical closure, empirical validation, legal conclusion, clinical inference, or technical certification.

---

## 12.2 PMS Core Remains the Base Grammar

PMS core supplies the canonical Δ–Ψ operator grammar.

The relevant operators are:

```text
Δ  Difference
∇  Impulse
□  Frame
Λ  Non-event / residue
Α  Attractor
Ω  Asymmetry
Θ  Temporality
Φ  Recontextualization
Χ  Distance
Σ  Integration
Ψ  Self-binding
```

Within PMS-AXIOM, these operators remain the only base operators.

No add-on introduces new PMS operators.

No add-on changes the dependency logic of PMS.

No drift catalogue overrides PMS.

No MIP or AHP layer redefines PMS.

No QC macro becomes a PMS operator.

No case output validates PMS Base.

A case may show that PMS can render a closure-demand. It does not prove PMS metaphysically, empirically, legally, clinically, technically, or morally.

---

## 12.3 Add-ons as Non-Mixing Overlay Lenses

PMS add-ons are non-mixing overlay lenses.

They sharpen visibility for specific domains. They do not replace the PMS operator grammar.

Each add-on contributes domain-facing structures such as:

```text
reduced signatures
drift catalogues
regime labels
validity handles
reach levels
publicness handles
audit handles
policy checks
```

These are overlay tools, not new foundations.

The add-on rule is:

```text
PMS core carries the grammar.
The add-on sharpens a domain-specific risk field.
The add-on does not become the grammar.
```

A PMS core case remains PMS core only.

A PMS + add-on case uses the relevant overlay explicitly.

A case does not silently escalate from PMS core into an add-on because the vocabulary feels relevant.

A case does not silently escalate from an add-on into MIP or AHP because the topic feels sensitive.

Layer choice must remain visible.

## 12.4 Add-on Roles Across the Paper

The add-ons used in AXIOM have distinct roles.

### LOGIC

LOGIC sharpens reasoning-specific closure risks.

It makes visible how justification, coherence, explanation, truth, interpretation, grounding, and rule-following can drift into closure laundering, Σ-totalization, □-overconstraint, Λ-suppression, proxy substitution, or authority capture.

LOGIC does not decide truth, knowledge, warrant, interpretation, or foundation.

### ANTICIPATION

ANTICIPATION sharpens future-facing closure risks.

It makes visible how trust, forecasting, planning, commitment, and downstream-effect reasoning can drift into prophecy, probability-as-command, post-confirmation entitlement, preventive escalation, Ω-instrumentalization, or Ψ-externalization.

ANTICIPATION does not predict the future, prescribe action, validate distrust, or authorize preemption.

### CONFLICT

CONFLICT sharpens stabilized-incompatibility risks.

It makes visible where local coherence fails to become shared integration, where repair-talk becomes pressure, where tragedy leaves residue, where asymmetry prices exit, and where conflict language may travel as authority.

CONFLICT does not resolve conflict, assign blame, diagnose parties, rank maturity, or prescribe governance.

### CRITIQUE

CRITIQUE sharpens reach, scale, and publicness risks.

It makes visible how mismatch, interpretation, legitimacy, publicness, privacy, and non-uptake can drift into judgment, exposure, commentary loops, pillory, reach laundering, or covert authority.

CRITIQUE does not decide truth, authorize critique, prescribe escalation, or sanction persons.

### EDEN

EDEN sharpens relational-regime risks.

It makes visible how relationship language, recognition, trust, repair, boundary, publicness, and pseudo-symmetry can substitute for actual coordination under Ω, Θ, Χ, Σ, and Ψ.

EDEN does not diagnose relationships, prescribe repair, rank relational maturity, assign guilt, or classify persons.

### SEX

SEX sharpens consent-adjacent validity risks.

It makes visible where consent-talk, boundary-talk, mutuality language, publicness, cost topology, stop-capability, and temporal accumulation require a validity gate before stronger claims can be made.

SEX does not define sexual normality, decide consent validity in concrete scenes, infer motives, assign adultness, diagnose persons, produce legal conclusions, or provide enforcement guidance.

### QC / QC-EXT

QC sharpens workflow-audit risks.

It makes visible how frames, oracle privilege, iteration depth, measurement traces, non-events, commit points, and stabilization handles must be documented before a QC-adjacent workflow becomes structurally inspectable.

QC-EXT adds narrower extension checks only where the relevant algorithmic or measurement-domain structure is actually supplied.

QC and QC-EXT do not replace quantum mechanics, certify hardware, model physical noise, prove correctness, guarantee performance, validate implementation, or establish quantum advantage.

## 12.5 Stack Discipline

AXIOM distinguishes layer use by stack.

The basic stack forms are:

```text
PMS core
```

PMS core only. No add-on is attached.

```text
PMS core → PMS + [ADD-ON]
```

PMS core plus the named overlay lens. The add-on sharpens visibility but does not redefine PMS operators.

```text
PMS core → PMS + QC
```

PMS core plus the QC workflow-audit mapping layer.

```text
PMS core → PMS + QC → PMS + QC-EXT
```

QC-EXT is used only where extension-level QC structure is explicitly warranted.

```text
PMS core / add-on artefact → MIP
```

MIP is a downstream artefact-governance layer. It evaluates artefact use conditions, not persons.

```text
PMS core / add-on artefact → MIP → AHP
```

AHP is second-order analysis-artefact hardening. It does not rescore, activate D, upgrade transmission status, or create new findings.

The stack is never inferred silently.

If a case is PMS core, it remains PMS core.

If an add-on is used, the add-on must be named.

If MIP is used, its artefact-governance role must be explicit.

If AHP is used, its claim-hardening role must be explicit.

## 12.6 Output Types and Their Boundaries

AXIOM outputs are bounded artefacts, not traditional answers.

The admissible output types include:

```text
Boundary
Taxonomy
Boundary / anti-drift policy
Regime taxonomy
Reach policy
Disclosure / publicness rule
Validity gate
Audit trace
```

These output types do not authorize unrestricted conclusions.

### Boundary

A boundary states where closure exceeds the declared frame.

It does not solve the problem by declaring failure.

It preserves Λ where closure is not structurally supported.

### Taxonomy

A taxonomy classifies structurally distinct variants.

It does not rank persons, theories, institutions, practices, or outcomes.

### Boundary / anti-drift policy

A boundary or anti-drift policy states where a reading must stop, branch, remain reversible, preserve Λ, or refuse escalation.

It is non-prescriptive.

It is not an enforcement policy.

### Regime taxonomy

A regime taxonomy identifies relational or structural configurations.

It does not diagnose persons, classify character, rank maturity, or prescribe intervention.

### Reach policy

A reach policy specifies how far critique can structurally travel under declared conditions.

It does not decide truth, authorize public exposure, or grant sanction.

### Disclosure / publicness rule

A disclosure or publicness rule maps the structural risks of moving material into visibility.

It does not prescribe disclosure, forbid disclosure, or provide legal advice.

### Validity gate

A validity gate specifies which configuration conditions must be present before a validity description can be structurally coherent.

It does not decide guilt, consent, legality, normality, maturity, or person status.

### Audit trace

An audit trace records operator-level workflow structure, commitments, policy checks, non-events, and revision boundaries.

It supports review.

It is not certification.

## 12.7 Drift Risk Is Not Drift Finding

A drift catalogue identifies risk corridors.

A drift marker is not a finding.

A drift signature is not a verdict.

A reduced signature is not proof.

A case may show that a configuration is vulnerable to:

```text
closure laundering
Σ-totalization
Λ-suppression
probability-to-authority
repair-talk drift
publicness drift
pseudo-symmetry
consent laundering
audit-to-certificate inflation
Φ-after-Σ rewrite
Ψ externalization
```

But vulnerability is not occurrence.

Risk is not finding.

Fit is not proof.

Coherence is not evidence.

A structurally smooth reading may still be under-specified.

When evidence, chronology, frame, role map, cost topology, publicness status, trace semantics, or review authority are missing, AXIOM preserves Λ rather than converting risk into conclusion.

## 12.8 MIP as Artefact Governance

MIP is a downstream governance layer for analysis artefacts and use conditions.

It is not a person-evaluation instrument.

MIP may become relevant where an AXIOM output enters or may enter:

```text
governance
institutional review
public transmission
policy-facing discussion
audit
compliance
HR-like use
education-like use
formal complaint contexts
public legitimacy contexts
responsibility allocation
high-stakes exposure
```

MIP asks whether the analysis artefact has adequate:

```text
scope discipline
misuse boundaries
publicity control
reversibility conditions
responsibility ceilings
dignity guardrails
IA-box limits
D0-protective constraints
```

MIP does not:

```text
diagnose persons
assign maturity to persons
rank dignity
determine guilt
produce legal conclusions
produce clinical conclusions
produce enforcement recommendations
turn structural risk into finding
validate PMS Base
```

Where MIP appears in case files or block renderings, it must remain artefact-facing.

A MIP hook says:

```text
this artefact may become use-sensitive
```

It does not say:

```text
this person has been evaluated
```

```text
pseudo-certification
```

```text
claim limits
test hooks

counterfactual handles
attack surfaces
```

AHP does not:

```text
rescore
activate D
```

It does not strengthen the case result.

## 12.10 D and D0

AXIOM keeps D and D0 separate.

```text
D  = Dignity-in-Practice
D0 = ontological dignity guardrail
```

Dignity-in-Practice belongs to PMS application discipline. It constrains how structural readings are produced and used.

D0 appears as a non-derivable guardrail where governance contexts require explicit ethical protection.

PMS does not prove D0.

MIP does not reduce D0 to instrumental practice.

Neither D nor D0 may be used to rank persons, assign worth, infer guilt, or intensify a case into a verdict.

Dignity language is protective.

It is not scoring language.

## 12.11 Publicness and Transmission

AXIOM artefacts may become risky when they travel.

A private analysis may become a public accusation.

A working note may become an institutional assessment.

A validity gate may become a verdict.

A drift marker may become a label.

A QC audit trace may become pseudo-certification.

A relational regime taxonomy may become person-typing.

A critique reach policy may become sanction logic.

A consent analysis may become shaming, exposure, or legal overclaim.

Transmission changes the artefact.

Therefore every use-sensitive artefact must preserve:

```text
scope
frame
output type
non-claims
misuse boundary
publicity level
responsibility ceiling
reversibility condition
Λ-residue
```

If those constraints cannot be preserved, transmission must be treated as a new artefact-context, not as simple reuse.

## 12.13 Relation to the Case Layer

The full case structures live outside the root paper.

The case layer contains:

```text
02_cases/yaml/
02_cases/markdown/
```

The YAML files contain the complete machine-readable case artefacts.

The Markdown case notes contain the fuller readable explanations.

The block files contain shorter paper-facing renderings derived from those artefacts.

The root paper does not replace the case layer.

The root paper preserves the chapter architecture, case movement, stack discipline, output type, and misuse boundary.

Full operator detail, overlay detail, drift catalogues, and downstream hooks remain anchored in the case artefacts.

This chapter therefore governs how the paper-facing renderings should be read:

```text
as compact structural renderings
not as full dossiers
not as verdicts
not as prescriptions
not as evidence packages
not as governance instructions
```

## 12.14 Reader Discipline

Readers should treat each case by asking:

```text
What is the declared frame?
What closure-demand is being mapped?
What Λ-residue remains?
Which PMS operators are active?
Is an add-on actually attached?
What does the add-on make visible?
Is MIP active, and if so, as artefact governance only?
Is AHP active, and if so, as analysis hardening only?
What output type is declared?
What does the output explicitly not authorize?
What misuse boundary must remain attached?
```

A reader should not ask:

```text
Who is guilty?
Who is mature?
Which theory wins?
Which person is diagnosed?
Which practice is normal?
Which policy follows?
Which system is certified?
Which interpretation is final?
```

Those questions may belong to other frameworks.

They do not belong to PMS-AXIOM as such.

---

## 12.15 Boundary Summary

PMS-AXIOM is useful only while its layer discipline remains intact.

PMS core maps closure-demands through Δ–Ψ.

Add-ons sharpen domain-specific visibility without redefining PMS.

MIP governs artefact use conditions without evaluating persons.

AHP hardens analysis artefacts without creating new findings.

QC and QC-EXT annotate workflow structure without certifying quantum systems.

Outputs remain bounded artefacts.

Drift risks remain risks.

Λ remains visible where closure is not warranted.

The final rule of the boundary layer is:

```text
AXIOM may render where closure is possible.
AXIOM must preserve where closure is not possible.
AXIOM must not convert either result into authority.
```
