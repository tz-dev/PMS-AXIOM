# PMS-AXIOM  
  
## A Praxeological Cartography of Classical Closure-Demands Across the PMS Stack (Δ–Ψ)  

## Assumption Statement  
  
This paper treats **PMS (Δ–Ψ)** and **MIP** as *established reference models* and does not re-derive them from first principles.  
PMS-AXIOM operates as a **meta-cartography and indexing paper**: it maps classical “axioms/problems” to **closure-demands inside explicit frames (□)**, identifies **residue structures (Λ)**, and specifies **stack-level output artefacts** (boundary statements, taxonomies, policies, audit-traces) under strict misuse-resistance constraints.  
  
## Abstract

PMS-AXIOM presents a stack-explicit cartography of classical philosophical, governance-relevant, relational, consent-adjacent, and QC-workflow problem families as **structural closure-demands** rather than ontological disputes, diagnostic claims, or verdict procedures. Using the Praxeological Meta-Structure (PMS; Δ–Ψ) as the canonical operator grammar, and treating PMS overlays (LOGIC, ANTICIPATION, CONFLICT, CRITIQUE, EDEN, SEX, QC/QC-EXT) as **non-mixing lenses**, the paper maps cases into bounded structural artefacts: frames (□), closure-demands, Λ-residues, operator chains, add-on signatures, drift risks, output types, and optional downstream hooks.

The full case structures are held in the rebuilt case layer (`02_cases/yaml/` and `02_cases/markdown/`). The root paper renders compact chapter-facing case sections derived from that layer. MIP and AHP appear only as downstream artefact-facing layers where governance use, public transmission, audit use, or claim-hardening requires additional guardrails. They do not evaluate persons, assign guilt, rank dignity, rescore cases, or validate PMS Base.

The contribution is a reproducible, audit-friendly methodology for converting diffuse “grand problems” into bounded structural artefacts that preserve frame discipline, reversibility, Λ-residue, dignity-in-practice constraints, and misuse boundaries.
  
## Contributions (high-level)

- **Axiom cartography as structural compilation:** classical problem labels are rendered as bounded frames (□), closure-demands, and Λ-residue structures.
- **Stack discipline without operator mixing:** PMS core remains the canonical Δ–Ψ grammar; add-ons remain non-mixing overlay lenses.
- **Case-layer reproducibility:** complete machine-readable YAML artefacts and fuller Markdown case notes provide the basis for compact paper-facing renderings.
- **Misuse-resistance by construction:** explicit frame discipline, Χ-distance, Ω-asymmetry accounting, reversibility, Dignity-in-Practice constraints, and misuse boundaries.
- **Downstream artefact governance only where triggered:** MIP and AHP harden artefacts and use conditions; they do not evaluate persons or create verdicts.

## 1. Introduction: What PMS-AXIOM Does  
  
### 1.1 Motivation  
  
PMS-AXIOM is motivated by a persistent pattern in the treatment of classical philosophical and governance-relevant problems: they have typically been framed as **ontological**, **psychological**, **moral**, or **purely logical** questions. Within those framings, many of the outcomes produced by the Praxeological Meta-Structure (PMS) would previously have been judged *a priori* impossible.  
  
Not because they were logically contradictory, but because:  
  
- problems were cut differently,  
- success criteria were defined differently,  
- and disciplinary boundaries were treated as *natural limits* rather than contingent constraints.  
  
From the standpoint of those traditions, a framework that is simultaneously structural rather than ontological, praxeological rather than mentalistic, self-binding rather than authoritative, and minimal rather than additive would have appeared suspect or unserious.  
  
In practice, this led to a recurring implicit claim:  
  
> “A model that can do all of this at once cannot exist.”  
  
PMS-AXIOM challenges that claim by showing that what was excluded was not a logical possibility, but a **problem formulation**.  
  
#### A Misplaced Impossibility Claim  
  
Historically, the dominant options were framed as mutually exclusive:  
  
- psychological *or* structural    
- metaphysical *or* formal    
- moral *or* descriptive    
- empirical *or* logical    
  
Within each field, this produced a defensive conclusion:  
  
> “Any model that crosses all of these boundaries must be incoherent.”  
  
What was ruled out, however, was not coherence but a different design stance:  
  
- structural instead of ontological,  
- praxeological instead of mentalistic,  
- self-binding instead of authoritarian,  
- minimal instead of additive.  
  
The impossibility was not demonstrated; it was **imposed by framing**.  
  
#### The Missing Toolchain  
  
A second source of resistance was instrumental rather than theoretical. The available tools were primarily:  
  
- theories that aim to *explain*,  
- not grammars that can *generate*,  
- disciplines with guarded territories,  
- not operator algebras for praxis.  
  
What PMS requires is a specific bundle of constraints:  
  
- formal minimalism,  
- discipline against moralization,  
- discipline against psychologization,  
- discipline against metaphysical inflation,  
- discipline against authority claims.  
  
This bundle did not exist as a standard toolkit. As a result, the relevant components were present in isolation, but never assembled into a single, coherent operator grammar.  
  
#### The Core Misunderstanding  
  
Underlying both issues was a deeper assumption: that models must primarily be *about* something—    
the world, the mind, morality, truth, or being.  
  
PMS instead formalizes the **form of acting itself**.  
  
This shift rendered the target invisible within traditional categories. As a consequence:  
  
> One could not build what one had not yet learned to think as buildable.  
  
Claims of impossibility thus reflected disciplinary blindness rather than structural contradiction.  
  
In short:  
  
- problems were misframed,    
- tools were mismatched,    
- viable outcomes were rendered invisible,    
- and impossibility was asserted where only categorical limits existed.  
  
PMS-AXIOM takes this diagnosis seriously and treats classical problems accordingly: not as metaphysical dead ends, but as **closure-demands inside explicit frames (□)** with identifiable residues (Λ).  
  
### 1.2 Contribution of the Paper  
  
Against this background, PMS-AXIOM contributes:  
  
- an **axiomatic cartography** across the PMS stack, mapping classical problem labels to structural closure-demands,  
- a **uniform case artefact**, enabling reproducible comparison across domains and overlays,  
- and a **machine-readable index**, replacing assertion-driven discourse with explicit, inspectable structures.  
  
The paper does not argue for PMS by authority or ambition; it demonstrates what becomes visible once the framing changes.  
  
### 1.3 What This Paper Explicitly Is Not  
  
PMS-AXIOM is deliberately scoped. It is:  
  
- not a “new grand theory,”  
- not a diagnostic, moral, or political instrument,  
- not a performance or capability claim (including with respect to QC).  
  
Its value lies in **structure and discipline**, not in claims of ultimate explanation or solution.

## 2. Presupposed Models (Sufficient, Not Didactic)  
  
This paper presupposes the PMS ecosystem as a set of **already specified, separately maintained** model layers. PMS-AXIOM does not re-teach these layers; it uses them as a **stable toolchain** for compiling cases into uniform artefacts. The division of labour is explicit:

- **PMS core** provides the canonical operator grammar (Δ–Ψ), dependencies, derived axes, and application gates.
- **Add-ons** provide **overlay lenses** with reduced signatures and drift catalogues, without redefining PMS operators.
- **MIP + AHP** provide downstream artefact governance and claim-hardening where triggered, not person-evaluation and not stronger case authority.
  
### 2.1 PMS (Δ–Ψ) as Canonical Base  
  
PMS is treated as the canonical, irreducible operator grammar for praxeological modelling. Within PMS-AXIOM, PMS is assumed “as given” in three senses:  
  
1. **Grammar, not narrative.**    
   PMS is used as a generative specification: operator chains, dependency constraints, and derived structures are taken as the basic vocabulary for describing praxeological form.  
  
2. **Entry condition as an application gate.**    
   PMS includes an explicit entry condition that functions as a *use-constraint* rather than an empirical claim: boundary framing (□), non-coercion / distance discipline (Χ), asymmetry accounting (Ω), reversibility where possible (R), and dignity-in-practice (D). PMS-AXIOM treats this gate as mandatory whenever a case is presented as applicable rather than purely structural.  
  
3. **Self-binding (Ψ) as commitment discipline.**    
   Ψ is presupposed as the operator that stabilizes commitments over time under constraints, not as a psychological thesis. In PMS-AXIOM, Ψ is used to mark where conclusions become *binding* and therefore where misuse risk rises (e.g., authority creep, totalization, coercive framing).  
  
### 2.2 PMS Add-ons as Overlay Lenses  
  
PMS add-ons are treated as **non-mixing overlays**: they do not extend PMS by adding new base operators or altering Δ–Ψ semantics. Instead, they provide domain-specific lenses as formal contracts that can be attached to a case artefact.  
  
PMS-AXIOM relies on two machine-readable overlay primitives:  
  
- **reduced_signatures**    
  Minimal, compositional signatures that express recurring structural configurations (often as short operator formulas). These are used to annotate cases with overlay-relevant patterns without inflating the base grammar.  
  
- **drift_catalogues**    
  Enumerations of failure modes and misuse patterns (e.g., closure laundering, Σ-totalization, consent laundering, coercion creep). These function as *risk annotations* and policy triggers: they specify how a case can be distorted when moved from structural analysis to authoritative or evaluative use.  
  
In PMS-AXIOM, overlays are treated as “lenses” in a strict technical sense: they add **classification power, visibility, and guardrail specificity**, not new foundational operators. A PMS core case therefore remains PMS core only. A PMS + add-on case uses the relevant overlay explicitly. QC-EXT is used only where extension-level QC structure is actually present. This keeps the stack legible and prevents silent layer escalation or operator redefinition drift across domains.
  
### 2.3 MIP + AHP as Downstream Artefact Governance

PMS-AXIOM treats MIP and AHP as optional downstream layers for analysis artefacts, not as extensions of PMS Base and not as person-evaluation instruments. They are invoked only where the produced artefact is likely to enter governance, institutional review, public transmission, policy-facing use, audit, or other contexts in which structural language could acquire coercive or decision-facing force.

- **MIP evaluates analysis artefacts and use conditions, not people.**  
  MIP is used to check whether a case output has adequate scope discipline, misuse boundaries, reversibility conditions, publicity control, responsibility ceilings, and dignity guardrails. It does not assign maturity to persons, diagnose actors, rank dignity, determine guilt, or turn structural risk into finding.

- **AHP is second-order analysis-artifact hardening.**  
  AHP is used where the analysis itself requires precision hardening: explicit claim limits, test hooks, counterfactual handles, failure conditions, attack surfaces, excerptability risks, and transmission constraints. AHP does not rescore, does not activate D, does not upgrade transmission status, and does not create new case findings.

- **D vs D0 is fixed once, explicitly.**  
  PMS-AXIOM assumes a clean separation between:
  - **D (Dignity-in-Practice)** as a praxeological constraint within PMS application discipline, and
  - **D0 (ontological dignity)** as a non-derivable MIP-level guardrail where governance contexts require explicit ethical protection.

  PMS does not prove D0. MIP does not collapse D0 into instrumental practice. The two-track handling of dignity prevents moral laundering, metaphysical smuggling, and dignity-ranking across layers.

## 3. Methodology: Axiomatic Cartography  
  
PMS-AXIOM applies an axiomatic cartography methodology: instead of proposing new theories or resolving problems at the level of truth claims, it **maps classical problem formulations onto explicit structural configurations** within a fixed operator grammar. The goal is not explanation, but **legibility**—to make clear *what kind of closure is being demanded*, *under which frame*, *with which residues*, and *at what stack level meaningful outputs are possible*.  
  
This methodology is deliberately compositional, stack-aware, and artefact-oriented.  
  
### 3.1 Stack Depths and Layer Escalation
  
PMS-AXIOM distinguishes stack depths by layer use, not by authority. The levels are not progressive “improvements”; they are distinct analytical regimes. Movement between them is a substantive escalation and must be explicit.

**Level I: PMS core only**  
At this level, cases are analysed exclusively through the canonical PMS operator grammar (Δ–Ψ). No add-on is attached. No overlay signature or drift catalogue is used. Outputs remain descriptive, delimiting, and frame-bound.

**Level II: PMS core + one add-on overlay**  
At this level, PMS core remains the base grammar and exactly the relevant add-on is used as a non-mixing overlay lens. The add-on sharpens domain-specific visibility through reduced signatures, drift risks, regime labels, validity handles, or audit handles. It does not redefine PMS operators and does not add case authority. QC-EXT is narrower still: it is used only where extension-level QC structure is explicitly warranted.

**Level III: downstream artefact governance / hardening where triggered**  
At this level, the produced structural artefact becomes use-sensitive. It may enter governance, institutional review, public transmission, policy-facing discussion, audit, or another context where structural language could acquire coercive or decision-facing force. MIP may then be invoked to evaluate the artefact’s scope, misuse corridors, publicity level, reversibility, responsibility ceilings, and dignity guardrails. AHP may be added where the analysis artefact requires claim-hardening, attack-surface exposure, excerptability control, or transmission discipline.

Level III does not mean stronger truth, stronger verdict, or a more authoritative case result. MIP and AHP remain artefact-facing layers. They do not diagnose persons, assign guilt, rank dignity, rescore the case, activate D as a finding, or validate PMS Base.
  
### 3.2 The Case Artefact (Standard)  
  
The core methodological unit of PMS-AXIOM is the **case artefact**. Each problem instance is compiled into a shared structure for comparison, indexing, and machine readability. The full artefact belongs to the case layer; the root paper renders only compact chapter-facing versions.
  
A standard case artefact contains:  
  
- **Frame (□):** the explicit boundary within which the problem is formulated and evaluated.  
- **Closure demand:** what the problem implicitly or explicitly demands to be closed, resolved, or settled.  
- **Λ-residue:** the remainder that persists after all structurally admissible operations are applied.  
- **Operator chain:** the minimal Δ–Ψ sequence required to describe the configuration.  
- **Add-on signatures:** any reduced signatures activated by an overlay lens.  
- **Drift risks:** relevant failure modes or misuse patterns drawn from drift catalogues.  
- **Output type:** the kind of artefact the case legitimately yields.  
- **Optional governance hooks:** MIP gates and, where applicable, AHP fields.  

In the rebuilt AXIOM structure, the complete case artefacts are held outside the root paper. `02_cases/yaml/` contains the full machine-readable case artefacts. `02_cases/markdown/` contains the fuller readable case notes. The block files in `01_blocks/` contain shorter paper-facing renderings derived from that case layer.

The root paper therefore preserves the chapter architecture and case movement, but does not reproduce full case bodies. Each paper-facing case rendering preserves the case purpose, active stack, add-on choice or no-add-on status, structural result, output type, and misuse boundary needed for the paper argument.

By enforcing this structure, PMS-AXIOM prevents problems from being “solved” by rhetorical expansion, moral substitution, or implicit authority claims. What cannot be expressed in this artefact remains explicitly unresolved.  
  
### 3.3 Output Types (Operational)  
  
PMS-AXIOM restricts admissible outcomes to a small set of operational output types. These are not answers in the traditional philosophical sense and not prescriptions. They are **deliverables with defined scope**.
  
- **Boundary:** a precise statement of where a problem stops yielding further structural closure under the given frame.
- **Taxonomy:** a classification of structurally distinct variants of a problem.
- **Boundary / anti-drift policy:** a non-prescriptive guardrail stating where a structural reading must stop, branch, remain reversible, preserve Λ, or refuse escalation under specified asymmetries and risks.
- **Validity gate:** a non-prescriptive configuration check specifying which conditions must be present before a validity description can be structurally coherent.
- **Audit trace:** a documented operator-level trace of frames, commitments, non-events, policy checks, and revision boundaries, suitable for review but not certification.
  
Each case must declare its output type explicitly. Claims beyond the declared output type are treated as category errors or drift risks, not as legitimate extensions of the analysis. Output type is therefore a boundary device, not a licence to prescribe, diagnose, enforce, certify, or evaluate persons.
