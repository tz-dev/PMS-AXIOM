### Why this case matters

This case matters because it exposes a recurrent closure-demand in any serious analysis of consent. Consent is often treated as if it should settle the field. The demand appears in several forms: either consent was present or absent; either agreement was sufficient or compromised; either asymmetry invalidated the scene or did not matter; either later contestation reveals the truth or is irrelevant. The completed YAML analyses resist those closures. They do not deny that consent distinctions matter. They insist that consent distinctions become structurally unstable when asymmetry, temporality, dependency, refusal, pressure-response, exposure, exit cost, and later contestation are present but under-specified.

The philosophical difficulty is not that consent is meaningless. It is that consent often promises more closure than the available structure permits. A statement, gesture, lack of refusal, formal procedure, institutional category, mutuality-language, or autonomy-language can appear to settle the matter. PMS Core refuses this premature settlement. It treats consent not as a single terminal state but as a field of distinctions and tensions: agreement can differ from meaningful consent; refusal can differ from non-response; compliance can differ from free orientation; delayed contestation can alter the interpretation of an earlier scene without automatically proving that the earlier scene was coercive. The point is not to make consent impossible. The point is to prevent consent-language from becoming a closure machine where the underlying configuration remains structurally unresolved.

The case belongs in AXIOM because it is not merely an example of a model applied to a familiar topic. It is a stress test for whether a formal grammar can preserve distinctions under pressure. Consent under asymmetry is structurally difficult because every easy reading risks becoming false in the opposite direction. If formal agreement is treated as sufficient, asymmetry, dependency, pressure, cost externalization, or constrained exit may be erased. If asymmetry is treated as automatically invalidating, agency, mutuality, real consent, and rival reconstructions may be erased. If later contestation is treated as decisive, temporality collapses into retrospective certainty. If initial appearance is treated as final, temporality is frozen. If non-response is treated as consent, refusal or constrained silence may disappear. If every under-specified consent field is treated as harm, analytic caution becomes accusation.

The case therefore requires a grammar that can remain precise without becoming punitive, open without becoming vague, and structurally critical without becoming diagnostic. PMS Core provides that initial grammar. PMS-SEX adds domain-specific visibility where sexual praxis introduces cost topology, direct otherness, body-boundary relevance, withdrawal/binding, and after-scene dignity. In the consent-under-asymmetry case, Δ also prevents the most tempting compression: “consent” as a single word that collapses agreement, capacity, desire, permission, absence of refusal, lack of resistance, and later acceptance into one term. If Δ were pushed too far, however, every agreement would become suspicious merely because distinctions can be made. That would be another invalid closure. Δ must therefore remain active but calibrated.

∇ follows as the operator of directional tension. In the consent-under-asymmetry field, ∇ marks the movement toward agreement, refusal, compliance, withdrawal, contestation, or pressure-response. □ is the frame operator and is active in a strong but bounded way. The YAML stack prevents that move. At the same time, suppressing Ψ entirely would make the analysis blind to the possibility that agreement can generate or conceal real after-costs. The correct posture is conditional visibility.

Χ prevents dependency shortcuts from becoming authoritative. It requires application restraint at every stage. If Ω is active, Χ prevents asymmetry from becoming guilt. If Θ is active, Χ prevents later contestation from becoming proof. If Φ is active, Χ prevents recontextualization from becoming retrospective omnipotence. If PMS-SEX adds cost topology, Χ prevents cost language from becoming score. If MIP adds maturity, Χ prevents maturity language from becoming person rank. If AHP adds precision, Χ prevents precision from becoming truth.

Coherence is not proof because the case can be internally coherent as an analytic construction while remaining empirically undecidable. This is one of the most important scientific points in the stack. A structured YAML can be precise, complete, and well ordered, yet still be a working note over abstract material. AHP later identifies exactly this attack surface: formal completeness may be mistaken for evidentiary completeness. PMS dependency discipline already anticipates the risk by preventing later operators from being treated as established merely because the analysis reads smoothly.

Non-capture is not failure. PMS does not need to decide what law, clinical capacity, empirical reconstruction, phenomenology, communicative consent theory, relational autonomy, or ethics must decide in their own terms. A case that remains partially non-captured may be more scientifically honest than a case forced into closure. Non-capture preserves rival readings and prevents PMS from becoming imperial.

Invalid dependency shortcuts would include treating consent distinctions as legal conclusions, treating Ω as automatic coercion, treating Θ as retrospective proof, treating Φ as license to rewrite the scene, treating Χ as mere caution rather than a governing restraint, treating Σ as established because the analysis is coherent, treating Ψ as present because sexual stakes are serious, treating PMS-SEX constructs as new PMS operators, treating MIP A/M bands as person scores, and treating AHP precision as evidentiary strength. Each shortcut would create closure by authority rather than closure by warranted structure.

Resistance, refusal, non-fit, disagreement, rupture, or absence are also not automatically drift. This is a crucial calibration point. A refusal may be mature. A rupture may be protective. Non-fit may preserve dignity. Disagreement may keep the other real. Absence may mean missing evidence rather than hidden failure. PMS drift analysis must therefore not become a machine for pathologizing resistance or nonconformity. Drift requires structural support, not mere discomfort.

### Derived structures

Awareness_A is active only as a derived, configuration-level structure. It is not primitive. It depends on the operator field: distinctions must be visible, tensions recognized, frames named, asymmetry and temporality tracked, recontextualization handled, and distance preserved. In the Core and MIP analyses, Awareness is structurally improved by naming the consent/asymmetry problem. However, awareness remains limited at the concrete level because actual role knowledge is unknown. The derived A structure can say that the analysis has strong structural awareness and weak concrete-role awareness. It cannot say that role_A or role_B was maturely aware in a scene.

This means that Awareness_A belongs partly to the analysis artefact and only conditionally to the case object. The article can say that the YAML stack is aware of asymmetry, cost topology, temporality, publicness risk, direct otherness, and non-capture. It cannot say that any role in a concrete scene was aware of these things. The distinction is central to MIP’s later A-band hygiene. A-band language describes configuration-handling in the abstract analysis object, not personal maturity, character, adulthood, guilt, dignity, sexuality, or scene-level factual enactment by role_A or role_B.

Coherence_C is also derived, not primitive. It concerns whether the configuration can be held together without collapsing distinctions. The YAML stack shows analytic coherence: PMS Core, PMS-SEX, MIP, and AHP are layered in a way that preserves boundaries. But scene-level coherence remains indeterminate. There is no concrete word-deed relation, no practice sequence, no role narrative, and no incident chronology. The case therefore distinguishes analytic coherence from empirical coherence. Coherence_C is active as a property of the analysis and conditional as a property of the scene.

Coherence_C must therefore be read with caution. The article may be coherent; the event remains unknown. The stack may integrate operators; the scene’s integration is unproven. A reader may find the case elegant; elegance is not evidence. This is one of the reasons AHP’s attack surface is not external to the article but belongs to its scientific structure.

Responsibility_R is visible as a structural question but not assignable as guilt or person fault. Derived Responsibility depends on awareness, coherence, asymmetry, power-to-act, temporality, and self-binding conditions. The case can say that role-specific responsibility would become relevant if actual power, cost externalization, predictability, and alternatives were evidenced. It cannot say that responsibility has been assumed, refused, shifted, or violated by any concrete person. MIP later caps M-band precisely because knowledge, control, predictability, alternatives, and serious integration attempt are missing as scene evidence. Responsibility_R is therefore conditional and role-contextual.

The case preserves a distinction between responsibility as a structural demand and responsibility as attribution. The former is visible. The latter is not established. In an abstract consent-under-asymmetry field, one can say that a role with more power, more frame-control, or lower exit cost would carry stronger responsibility to make cost topology, refusal, correction, and reversibility real. But one cannot say that role_A actually held such power or failed such responsibility. Responsibility_R must remain a conditional analytic structure.

Action_E is weak or not concretely active. The case provides no practice instruction and no concrete action sequence. E may appear only as analytic action: the analysis preserves guardrails, keeps layers distinct, maintains non-capture, and identifies what evidence would be required for stronger claims. It may not become conduct advice. If Action_E were pushed too far, the article would become a manual, intervention plan, legal recommendation, therapeutic guidance, or relationship advice. The YAML stack explicitly prohibits that.

The closest legitimate Action_E is methodological: convert abstract Ω/Θ cost claims into documented questions with evidence status, counter-reading, prohibited inference, and reversibility condition before any stronger claim. This is analysis hygiene, not practice guidance. It tells the analyst how not to overclaim. It does not tell participants how to act.

Dignity_in_Practice_D is present as a guard and later becomes qualitatively active in MIP, but it remains derived and non-scoring. In PMS terms, Dignity-in-Practice depends on Ψ, Χ, and Ω; it is not a primitive dignity rank. In this case, D protects the analysis from shame, humiliation, public punishment, person-ranking, and dignity hierarchy. MIP activates D as qualitative dignity-in-practice protection because the case involves asymmetry, sexual cost topology, publicness risk, direct otherness, withdrawal, and after-scene dignity. But D0 remains untouched. D1/D2 are role-context enactment lenses only. No dignity score, dignity deficit, or person-level dignity judgment is produced.

Dignity-in-Practice remains praxeological, not ontological. It concerns how configurations, roles, and analyses handle dignity-relevant enactment conditions. It does not touch the ontological dignity of persons. This distinction is non-negotiable. If D-language becomes a way to intensify accusation, rank persons, or imply a dignity deficit, it violates both MIP and AHP boundaries. In this case, D is activated only as a protection lens. It does not raise A or M, does not create guilt or shame, and does not produce a public dignity reading.

SELF_FIXPOINT is analytic-only if present at all. The case does not establish a self-stabilizing personal or relational structure. There is no evidence of repeated voluntary configuration, durable self-binding, integration over time, or stable identity formation. At most, the analysis itself seeks a kind of methodological fixpoint: it repeatedly returns to the same constraints—non-capture, reversibility, no person verdict, no legal/clinical/forensic conclusion, no closure by authority. That is not a finding about the case participants. It is a discipline of the analytic artefact.

The derived structures therefore remain carefully separated from primitive operator claims. Awareness, Coherence, Responsibility, Action, Dignity-in-Practice, and SELF_FIXPOINT are not independent axes that can be invoked at will. They are formula-derived and dependency-sensitive. Their restrained status is one of the main reasons the YAML stack remains scientifically usable.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS drift-pattern layer does not convert structural risk into factual finding. Its function is to identify where the Core configuration could become unstable if later evidence showed that the consent frame, asymmetry structure, or application discipline had been mishandled. The drift-pattern analysis therefore belongs after the operator and dependency reading: it presupposes the earlier calibration of Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, and Ψ, and it cannot be used to bypass that calibration.

The first relevant drift tendency is the risk captured by an `AD_A>>E`-type pattern: awareness or conceptual legibility outrunning enacted capacity. In this case, the field is highly visible at the analytic level. The analyses can name agreement/consent distinctions, asymmetry, temporal contestability, cost topology, direct otherness, exit realism, and dignity-in-practice conditions. Yet visibility at the level of analysis does not prove that a concrete scene contained corresponding enacted awareness, repair capacity, review conditions, or real alternatives. The risk is therefore not that “awareness was too high” in any person. The risk is that a reader may confuse analytic awareness with enacted maturity in the scene.

This distinction is crucial. If `AD_A>>E` were treated as a finding, it would imply that someone knew more than they enacted or failed to enact what they understood. The YAML stack does not support that. No concrete role-holder knowledge is supplied. No sequence of decision, refusal, repair, or after-scene response is supplied. No concrete alternatives are supplied. The proper status of the drift is therefore a calibration warning: the analysis has enough awareness to formulate the problem, but not enough scene evidence to assign enacted failure.

The second relevant drift tendency is an `AD_Sigma_low`-type pattern: the danger that integration remains too weak to support a full coherent reading. In PMS terms, Σ requires more than the presence of distinctions and tensions. It requires some integrated enactment of frame, recontextualization, distance, and role-context handling. The case does not provide this. It supplies a consent-under-asymmetry field, not a fully integrated practical sequence. Therefore, any attempt to move directly from analytic coherence to scene-level integration would be over-assimilative.

`AD_Sigma_low` is also not a low score. Under-specification is not failure. The case’s lack of concrete Σ evidence means only that integration cannot be asserted. It does not prove that the underlying scene, if later supplied, would be incoherent. A later bounded reconstruction might show clear communication, realistic refusal/exit, transparent asymmetry, explicit cost recognition, and intact after-scene dignity. Such evidence would weaken the low-Σ risk. Conversely, if later evidence showed that the consent frame remained formal while cost, exit, refusal, or repair were structurally unavailable, the Σ strain would become stronger. At present, the risk remains conditional.

A related coherence risk concerns under-discrimination. Consent under asymmetry can be under-discriminated in two opposite ways. One form flattens the field into formal agreement: if someone agreed, the matter is closed. The other form flattens the field into asymmetry: if asymmetry existed, the matter is closed in the other direction. The PMS drift-pattern layer rejects both flattenings. Formal agreement is not enough to erase Ω and Θ. But Ω and Θ are also not enough to decide the case without Δ, ∇, □, Φ, Χ, Σ, and Ψ discipline. Under-discrimination is therefore a risk of premature closure, not a verdict about the scene.

Another related risk is coherence-overproduction. A layered YAML analysis can appear more complete than the material warrants. The Core reading is coherent, the PMS-SEX overlay is coherent, the MIP layer is coherent, and the AHP overlay marks high precision. Yet the underlying case remains abstract. AHP later names this directly: formal completeness can be mistaken for evidentiary completeness. The PMS drift-pattern status already requires that this not happen. A complete analytic artefact is not a completed empirical reconstruction.

The drift-pattern status can therefore be summarized as follows: drift risks are visible, but they remain risks. `AD_A>>E` marks the possibility that conceptual visibility outruns enacted capacity. `AD_Sigma_low` marks the conditional weakness of scene-level integration. Related coherence and under-discrimination risks mark the danger of closure by agreement, closure by asymmetry, or closure by analytic elegance. None of these is a person verdict, a low maturity score, a dignity finding, or a legal conclusion.

### Add-on / overlay reading

The relevant add-on in this case is PMS-SEX. It is not a replacement for PMS Core and not an independent operator system. It is an overlay/application profile applied after the completed Core case. Its reason for inclusion is that the Core case concerns consent under asymmetry in a field where sexual-praxeological structure is relevant. PMS Core can map distinctions, tensions, framing, asymmetry, temporality, recontextualization, distance, integration, and self-binding. PMS-SEX makes visible a more specific question: what happens when consent under asymmetry involves sexual cost topology, direct otherness, embodiment, access, withdrawal, binding, after-scene dignity, and the risk that parity or autonomy language hides unequal cost?

PMS Core alone is not the final layer here because it does not isolate sexual cost topology as such. Core can say that Ω is central and Θ is under-specified. It can say that agreement and meaningful consent must remain distinct. It can say that Φ and Χ are active while Σ and Ψ remain conditional. PMS-SEX sharpens the sexual-praxeological stakes of that structure. It asks whether formal consent, autonomy, mutuality, parity, or non-binding language remains structurally viable once bodily, exposure, temporal, exit, binding, responsibility, and after-scene costs are made explicit. This does not make PMS-SEX superior to PMS Core. It makes it a domain-specific overlay where the domain demands additional visibility.

The central PMS-SEX construct is sexual cost topology. In the completed overlay, this topology is partial. Bodily costs, exposure costs, reputational costs, publicness costs, body-boundary costs, and after-scene dignity costs are not concretely evidenced. Temporal costs, exit costs, access costs, and responsibility-externalization risks are structurally visible but only partially supported because the case is abstract. The overlay therefore names cost categories without distributing them as facts. Sexual cost topology adds something PMS Core alone cannot isolate with equal specificity. Core can identify asymmetry, but the overlay asks how asymmetry is carried in a sexual field. Direct otherness is another major overlay construct. The overlay therefore adds specificity without adding authority. It makes the case richer but also more dangerous if misread. AHP then becomes second-order. It does not reanalyze the consent case. It analyzes the quality, precision, attack surface, transmission risk, and next-iteration hardening needs of the MIP artefact. The PH reading is grounded in six precision dimensions: term definition, scope boundaries, conditions and thresholds, evidence linking, reversibility clause, and language hygiene. These dimensions harden the readability of the artefact; they do not add evidence, rescore A or M, revise the IA-box, activate D independently, or upgrade transmission status.

### Example 1 — Formal Agreement and Real Exit

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** After “Scene and frame structure” or after the Ω/Θ discussion in “PMS Core structural reading.”

A bounded vignette can begin with a deliberately minimal scene. The vignette is constructed for analytical illustration and is not supplied case material. Role_A and role_B are not persons but analytic role positions. Role_A proposes an intimate or sexually charged situation inside a context where role_A has more control over timing, framing, or social interpretation. Role_B does not refuse. Role_B may even agree in ordinary language. Nothing in the vignette states that role_A used force, that role_B lacked capacity, that a legal threshold was crossed, or that any concrete harm occurred. The scene remains bounded to a structural question: what follows, analytically, when formal agreement is present but real exit, real refusal, and cost distribution are not yet specified?

The example demonstrates why the PMS Core analysis cannot stop at the surface distinction “agreement occurred.” Agreement matters, but it does not exhaust the field. Δ separates formal agreement from meaningful consent, refusal from non-response, and free orientation from pressure-response. ∇ marks the directional tension between agreement, hesitation, compliance, withdrawal, or later contestation. □ defines the frame as a consent-under-asymmetry problem rather than as a legal, clinical, forensic, or moral verdict. Ω becomes relevant because the case turns on whether role_A and role_B bear equal or unequal costs of continuing, refusing, pausing, or exiting. Θ becomes relevant because the meaning of agreement may change if later withdrawal, delay, or contestation becomes structurally salient.

The key point is that the example does not allow either closure. It does not permit the sentence “Role_B agreed, therefore the case is settled.” But it also does not permit the opposite sentence “There was asymmetry, therefore consent was invalid.” PMS Core requires the analysis to stay inside the difficulty. Φ allows the agreement to be recontextualized if later evidence shows pressure, dependency, cost externalization, or constrained exit. Χ prevents that recontextualization from becoming a retrospective verdict. Σ remains conditional because no integrated scene-level pattern is established. Ψ remains conditional because no durable self-binding, after-scene obligation, or structured expectation has been evidenced.

The PMS-SEX overlay becomes relevant because this is not merely a generic asymmetry example. It is a sexual-praxeological field in which cost topology may matter. The add-on asks what kind of cost is carried by whom, and whether formal agreement hides that distribution. Does role_A have a lower cost of exit? Does role_B carry more exposure, interpretive burden, bodily vulnerability, reputational risk, or after-scene ambiguity? None of these costs may be asserted as findings in the vignette. They are questions the overlay makes visible. Sexual cost topology is therefore a calibration interface, not a score. It does not rank suffering, maturity, dignity, sex, identity, or personhood.

Direct otherness is also relevant. The vignette must leave role_B capable of refusal, correction, delay, and non-identity with role_A’s framing. If the example were written so that role_B’s silence or agreement became an access entitlement, it would violate the PMS-SEX boundary. If it were written so that role_A must always be treated as coercive because role_A initiated, it would also violate the boundary. The add-on does not decide who is right. It preserves the other as real.

MIP enters only as a guarded maturity-in-practice lens. The example may ask whether the configuration-handling would require awareness, coherence, responsibility, and power-to-act. It may ask whether real alternatives were available, whether asymmetry was transparent, whether refusal was permitted, whether exit was realistic, and whether review or repair could occur. It may not assign a maturity score to role_A or role_B. It may not call role_A immature or role_B harmed. It may not infer guilt, pathology, dignity failure, or adultness. MIP’s function is to show that the situation is maturity-sensitive, not to transform the vignette into a person assessment.

AHP and QC are relevant as transmission controls. The example should not be quoted as a decision-ready scenario. It is a worked illustration of why formal completeness is not evidentiary completeness. AHP would flag any wording that makes the example sound like a finding. QC would require the example to remain non-diagnostic, non-legal, non-forensic, non-clinical, non-prescriptive, and non-person-ranking.

The forbidden claims are therefore clear. The example cannot claim that valid consent occurred. It cannot claim that invalid consent occurred. ### Example 2 — Later Contestation and the Reversibility Problem

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** After the Θ, Φ, and Χ portions of “PMS Core structural reading,” or before “PMS dependency and calibration discipline.”

A second bounded vignette can focus on temporality. The vignette is constructed for analytical illustration and is not supplied case material. Role_A and role_B pass through an initially ambiguous consent field. At the time of the interaction, the field appears settled enough for the participants or observers to describe it as mutually acceptable. Later, role_B contests the meaning of the earlier scene. The example does not specify whether role_B’s later contestation is legally relevant, clinically significant, morally justified, or empirically accurate. It also does not specify whether role_A acted wrongly. The only bounded question is structural: how should the analysis handle a scene whose meaning is re-opened over time?

This example demonstrates why Θ is not an optional embellishment in the case. Consent is temporally structured. Agreement, refusal, withdrawal, revision, and contestation do not always occur in a single clean moment. A person may understand a cost only later. A role may reinterpret pressure only after the frame changes. A later contestation may reveal an earlier asymmetry. But a later contestation may also overread, misremember, or reframe an event in ways that require independent evaluation. PMS therefore cannot treat the later statement as automatically decisive. It also cannot ignore it.

Δ remains necessary because the example requires distinctions among initial agreement, later contestation, withdrawal, revision, regret, pressure-response, and reinterpretation. ∇ marks the directional movement from apparent closure toward reopened meaning. □ keeps the analysis inside a structural consent-under-asymmetry frame and prevents immediate migration into legal, clinical, or moral judgment. Λ may become conditionally relevant if the scene contains meaningful non-events: no explicit refusal, no review point, no pause, no opportunity for correction, no after-scene repair. But Λ cannot be used cheaply. The absence of a refusal does not prove consent, and the absence of a recorded review point does not prove coercion.

Ω remains central because later contestation often concerns asymmetry: who had more control over timing, interpretation, social cost, exit, or public framing? Yet Ω does not decide the case. It only keeps uneven role conditions visible. Θ carries the main structural load. It marks that the meaning of the scene may not be exhausted by the initial description. Φ then allows recontextualization. The earlier scene may be reread in light of later contestation, but rereading is not rewriting. Φ is not retrospective omnipotence.

Χ is the operator that keeps the example scientifically usable. It prevents two invalid closures. The first invalid closure says: “Role_B later contested the scene, therefore the earlier consent was not meaningful.” The second says: “The scene seemed acceptable at the time, therefore later contestation is irrelevant.” Χ requires distance from both. It preserves the capacity to stop, review, test rival readings, and avoid verdict.

Σ remains conditional. There is no integrated enactment unless the example supplies concrete chronology, communication, role knowledge, alternatives, and after-scene handling. Ψ remains conditional because later contestation may imply that some binding expectation, after-scene dignity condition, or self-binding failure matters, but the vignette does not establish that. Ψ cannot be inferred simply because the topic is sexual or emotionally serious.

The PMS-SEX overlay helps specify what later contestation might reveal without deciding whether it does reveal it. Sexual cost topology may become visible only after the scene: exposure cost, reputational risk, bodily boundary concern, emotional residue, publicness, or responsibility externalization. But these remain possible cost dimensions. The example must not assert them as facts unless the vignette supplies evidence. PMS-SEX also keeps withdrawal and binding from being moralized. Later withdrawal is neither automatic guilt for role_A nor automatic exculpation for role_B. It is a temporally structured event whose meaning depends on the concrete scene.

Direct otherness is again central. Later contestation may be one way the other reappears as real rather than as a category inside the original script. Yet direct otherness cannot become an entitlement to explanation, confession, or public exposure. The example should show that correction matters without making correction compulsory in a way that violates the other’s boundary.

MIP reads the example as a maturity-in-practice problem only at the level of configuration-handling. The relevant questions are: Was the possibility of later recontextualization anticipated? Were review, pause, clarification, refusal, or repair available? Could role_A or role_B realistically carry the consequences of ambiguity? Did the frame permit correction without punishment? It may only show why the configuration would require careful assessment before stronger claims.

AHP/QC would flag the example if it turned later contestation into a conclusion. AHP’s attack-surface concern is especially relevant here: the more polished the example becomes, the more it may seem evidentiary. QC therefore requires repeated boundary language. The example is a structural demonstration, not a reconstruction.

Forbidden claims include: “later contestation proves the earlier scene was invalid,” “initial agreement disproves later contestation,” “role_A is responsible because role_B later objected,” “role_B is unreliable because the objection came later,” “the situation is clinically explained,” or “the law would decide it one way.” All of these exceed the vignette.

The non-capture note should point to rival frameworks. A phenomenological reading might ask how role_B’s experience changed over time. A legal reading might ask whether later contestation affects evidentiary standards. A communicative-consent reading might ask whether there were adequate opportunities for clarification. PMS does not replace those analyses. It clarifies why temporality and recontextualization must not be erased.

The article takeaway is that later contestation is structurally meaningful but not automatically decisive. Θ makes time visible. Φ permits rereading. Χ prevents overclaim. PMS-SEX shows why after-scene cost and dignity may matter. MIP shows why review and reversibility are maturity-sensitive. AHP/QC show why the example must remain a bounded analytic tool rather than a verdict.

### Example 3 — A High-Precision Working Note Is Not a Decision

**Recommended insertion point:** After “Layer interaction,” before “Misuse boundary,” or as a bridge into the AHP/QC discussion.

A third example should shift from the consent scene to the analysis artefact itself. The vignette is constructed for analytical illustration and is not supplied case material. A research team has produced a layered article draft from PMS Core, PMS-SEX, MIP, and AHP. The draft is formally complete. It does not ask what happened in the underlying consent field. It asks how a high-precision analysis can itself become a misuse risk.

This example demonstrates the importance of AHP and QC. The previous examples show why the case cannot be closed by consent or later contestation. This example shows why it also cannot be closed by formal analysis. A complete YAML-derived article can create authority-pressure. It can appear more evidentiary than it is. It can make abstract role positions sound like actual people. It can make attack points sound like case findings. It can make Precision Heuristic sound like validation. AHP exists to prevent that second-order misuse.

PMS Core hooks remain present. Χ is the most important. Application restraint applies not only to the original case but also to the article that renders it. If the article loses Χ, it becomes a closure machine. Φ is also relevant because the article itself can recontextualize the case: once a conceptually abstract consent field is translated into fluent academic prose, readers may treat it as a concrete scenario. Θ appears because transmission over time changes the artefact’s risk. A working note used internally has one status; the same text quoted publicly or applied institutionally has another.

Σ and Ψ must remain guarded. A polished article may seem integrated, but textual integration is not scene integration. The existence of a coherent chapter section does not establish an integrated event. Likewise, the article may discuss self-binding, responsibility, and after-scene dignity, but it cannot produce Ψ as a concrete finding. This example therefore demonstrates a meta-level version of the PMS dependency discipline: later textual operators cannot be used cheaply merely because the article is well written.

The PMS-SEX hooks are also present. Sexual cost topology, direct otherness, parity-to-exit, responsibility-to-authority, and after-scene dignity are rhetorically strong terms. If extracted from their guardrails, they can be misused as accusation language. A reader might treat “cost-topology mismatch” as a finding of harm, or “direct otherness” as proof that access was wrongly demanded, or “after-scene dignity” as proof of dignity violation. The example should show that PMS-SEX terms must remain calibration lenses unless concrete evidence supports stronger use. They are not scene findings by themselves.

MIP hooks are central. The MIP layer may contain A-band and M-band language, IA-risk, and D-module activation as qualitative protection. These terms are particularly vulnerable to misuse. A reader may treat A-band as person maturity, M-band as guilt, IA-risk as legal or moral verdict, and D-language as dignity judgment. The example must show why MIP’s own guardrails matter: configuration-handling is not character; shared structural responsibility is not blame; IA-risk is assessability risk; D0 remains untouched; D1/D2 refer only to role-context enactment where evidence supports them.

AHP is the main layer illustrated. AHP identifies attack surfaces in the analysis artefact: formal completeness mistaken for evidentiary completeness, working-note status weakened by formal presentation, red-zone exclusions misread as known absence, IA thresholds not operationally staged, M-score ceiling needing further evidence categories, D-language needing explicit boundaries, and PMS-SEX terms inviting over-reading. The example demonstrates why these attack points are not embarrassments. They are part of scientific cleanliness. A serious article names how it can be misread.

QC status is likewise relevant. QC does not ask whether the article is rhetorically persuasive. It asks whether the article remains within permitted claims. It checks that no diagnosis is made, no person ranking occurs, no legal or forensic conclusion is implied, no clinical claim is introduced, no add-on becomes an operator, no drift becomes a verdict, no PH becomes validation, and no working note becomes decision basis. In this example, QC would require the article to display its own non-transmission boundary before any reader could treat it as usable outside scholarly analysis.

Forbidden claims include: “The high PH score means the case is true,” “the article is complete, so the event is decidable,” “AHP confirms the MIP reading,” “QC proves PMS is valid,” “attack points show defects in role_A or role_B,” “hardening actions are practical interventions,” or “working-note coherence authorizes public transmission.” Each claim confuses artefact quality with case truth.

The non-capture note is especially important here. AHP does not replace peer review, empirical method, legal procedure, clinical expertise, ethical deliberation, or affected-party testimony. It is a second-order overlay for analysis quality. It can harden the article, but it cannot supply missing facts. A high-precision working note may be more responsible than a vague note, but it is still a working note.

The article takeaway is that precision must not become authority. A well-structured PMS/PMS-SEX/MIP/AHP article demonstrates analytic discipline only if it preserves its own limits. The stronger the architecture, the more explicit the misuse boundary must be. This example prepares the final conclusion by showing that the case’s central lesson is not simply about consent under asymmetry. It is also about the ethics of formal analysis under conditions of missing evidence.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The epistemological rival reading would emphasize the limits of knowledge. From this perspective, the most important fact about the case is not the consent-asymmetry structure but the absence of concrete evidence. The epistemological lens preserves the distinction between conceptual possibility and warranted belief. It would ask what is known, how it is known, who knows it, and which claims are inferential rather than evidential. PMS preserves some of this through Θ, Χ, and non-capture, but epistemology may make the evidentiary boundary more explicit than PMS alone.

The phenomenological rival reading would emphasize lived experience, ambiguity, embodiment, hesitation, felt pressure, vulnerability, shame, desire, refusal, and later reorientation. PMS-SEX gestures toward embodiment and direct otherness, but the case lacks first-person material. A phenomenological reading would preserve the irreducibility of lived consent experience to operator structure. It would ask how agreement was experienced, how refusal was felt or inhibited, whether temporality changed meaning, and whether bodily exposure altered the field. Because no such accounts are supplied, phenomenology remains necessary but unavailable.

The language-practice rival reading would focus on how terms such as consent, autonomy, mutuality, pressure, refusal, non-binding, dignity, maturity, and responsibility are used in practices. It would examine the social grammar by which consent-language closes, opens, excuses, accuses, or stabilizes interactions. PMS Core and PMS-SEX already recognize frame language and parity/autonomy risks, but a language-practice lens could more directly analyze how words function in actual institutions, relationships, or communities. It preserves ordinary use, pragmatic context, and discourse norms that PMS may formalize too quickly.

The ethical rival reading would ask what obligations, virtues, duties, harms, or responsibilities arise under asymmetry. PMS and MIP explicitly avoid turning the case into moral verdict. That restraint is necessary. Yet ethical analysis may still be needed if a concrete scene emerges. Ethics could preserve normative questions that PMS leaves open: what care is owed under vulnerability, how refusal should be supported, how responsibility should be distributed, how repair should occur, and how after-scene dignity should be protected. The YAML stack does not deny these questions; it refuses to answer them without the proper frame.

The legal rival reading would focus on consent doctrine, capacity, evidence, coercion standards, burden of proof, jurisdictional categories, and institutional procedure. It preserves decision conditions that PMS cannot supply. A legally valid consent finding might diverge from a PMS structural concern; a PMS cost-topology concern might not be legally actionable. Neither divergence invalidates the other. The legal lens is necessary if a legal question is asked, and the YAML stack explicitly blocks substituting PMS/PMS-SEX/MIP for law.

The clinical rival reading would focus on capacity, trauma, impairment, coercive control, dissociation, compulsion, or other clinical categories only if clinically appropriate evidence and mandate were present. The YAML stack excludes clinical diagnosis. This does not mean clinical relevance is known absent; it means the present analysis is not a clinical analysis. AHP’s attack surface correctly notes that red-zone false values describe analysis-use exclusions, not empirical absence of legal, clinical, or institutional relevance.

The relational-autonomy rival reading would focus on how autonomy is shaped by dependency, relationship, recognition, care, and social context. PMS-SEX partly overlaps with this through cost topology and direct otherness, but relational autonomy could preserve a more nuanced account of dependence without immediately treating dependence as defect or coercion. It could also challenge overly individualistic consent frames. The PMS stack allows this rival reading and does not treat it as a failure.

The communicative-consent rival reading would focus on how consent is communicated, confirmed, revised, paused, withdrawn, or repaired. It would ask for concrete signals, communicative norms, misunderstandings, and opportunities for clarification. PMS Core marks the distinctions and tensions; PMS-SEX marks correction channels; MIP marks awareness and power-to-act. But a communicative-consent framework may better specify what counts as adequate communication in a concrete setting.

The anti-capture rival reading would resist formalization itself. It would warn that consent, sexuality, dignity, maturity, and asymmetry may exceed any operator grammar. The YAML stack partially incorporates this warning through non-capture. It does not claim totality. It does not claim that PMS, PMS-SEX, MIP, or AHP exhaust the case. The anti-capture reading preserves humility: some scenes may exceed the grammar or require refusal of the frame itself.

These rival readings are not defects in the PMS stack. They are part of the stack’s scientific restraint. A model that knows where rival frameworks are needed is stronger than a model that explains everything by force. In this case, non-capture is not merely a defensive caveat. It is one of the article’s core methodological results. The case remains meaningful precisely because it does not claim to be complete.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS reading would be weakened if a concrete bounded scene showed that no consent-relevant asymmetry was present. If role_A and role_B had clear, symmetrical capacities, realistic refusal and exit, explicit cost recognition, no pressure, no dependency, no publicness, no after-scene residue, and no ambiguity about agreement or withdrawal, the Core asymmetry reading would become less central. It might still remain a consent case, but not a consent-under-asymmetry case in the strong PMS sense.

The PMS reading would also be weakened if agreement, refusal, exit, and later contestation were fully specified by a non-PMS framework in a way that left PMS with no discriminative contribution. For example, a legal, communicative-consent, or relational-autonomy analysis might account for the relevant distinctions more directly. PMS non-capture requires acknowledging this possibility. The goal is not to force PMS relevance where another grammar is more precise.

The PMS reading would become under-discriminating if it treated consent under asymmetry as one uniform structure. Not every asymmetry has the same meaning. Not every unequal cost invalidates consent. Not every later contestation redefines earlier agreement. Not every formal agreement closes responsibility. Not every absence of refusal is meaningful. Not every rupture is drift. Stronger PMS claims would therefore require more precise material: bounded chronology, role positions, concrete dependencies, communication conditions, exit options, publicness, and after-scene handling.

The PMS reading would become over-assimilative if it absorbed every rival reading into its own grammar. Legal doctrine, clinical capacity, phenomenology, relational autonomy, communicative ethics, and empirical reconstruction may each preserve dimensions that PMS cannot or should not decide. Over-assimilation would occur if PMS treated those frameworks as merely incomplete versions of itself. The correct posture is layered modesty: PMS can map structural conditions, while other frameworks may answer other questions.

The PMS-SEX overlay would be weakened if no sexual praxis frame were actually in scope. If the case were purely abstract ethics, legal consent, communicative theory, or non-sexual authority asymmetry, PMS-SEX would add little or might over-sexualize the problem. The overlay would also be weakened if no Ω direction or cost-topology asymmetry could be identified. If concrete evidence showed explicit cost topology, realistic exit, preserved direct otherness, and no externalized after-scene burden, the PMS-SEX drift risks would become weak or irrelevant.

The PMS-SEX overlay would be defeated if a concrete scene showed no asymmetry relevant to consent, cost, exit, temporality, or sexual praxis. It would also be defeated if the issue were purely legal, forensic, clinical, or empirical and PMS-SEX added no discriminative structure. Another weakening condition would be over-assimilation: if PMS-SEX terms such as direct otherness, sexual cost topology, after-scene dignity, or parity-to-exit were applied where the evidence did not support them, the overlay would become rhetorically strong but analytically weak.

PMS-SEX would also be weakened if its overlay terms began to function as operators. Sexual cost topology, direct otherness, parity-to-exit, withdrawal/binding, responsibility-to-authority, and after-scene dignity are handles. The MIP reading would be weakened if the case were not appropriately maturity-sensitive. ### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It does not diagnose persons, motives, pathologies, trauma, capacity, personality, immaturity, manipulation, victimhood, guilt, innocence, or sexual identity. Any attempt to infer “what kind of person” a role represents would violate the Core, PMS-SEX, MIP, and AHP guardrails.

It must not be used for person ranking. Role_A and role_B are scene-bound analytic positions, not better or worse persons. A-score and M-score bands in the MIP layer are configuration-level and provisional. They do not rank adults, maturity, dignity, responsibility, sexuality, or character. Dignity-in-Practice does not create a dignity hierarchy. D0 remains untouched.

It must not be used for legal or forensic conclusion. PMS distinctions are not legal findings. PMS-SEX cost topology is not proof of harm or liability. MIP IA-risk is not legal invalidity. AHP precision is not admissibility, reliability, or evidentiary completeness. Legal and forensic use remains outside the authorized scope.

It must not be used as metaphysical proof. The case does not prove what consent, sexuality, maturity, dignity, agency, embodiment, or responsibility essentially is. It provides a structured reading of a bounded abstract problem. Metaphysical closure by model authority is prohibited.

It must not be used as empirical proof. The YAML stack’s completeness does not supply missing facts. The abstract case remains abstract. No chronology, no role testimony, no publicness, no bodily fact, no after-scene conduct, and no legal/clinical fact may be inferred from the structure alone.

It must not be used as implementation validation. The case does not show that PMS, PMS-SEX, MIP, or AHP works in applied institutions, courts, clinics, relationships, education, governance, or public discourse. It may demonstrate internal formal discipline, but not field validation.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. Applying PMS successfully to a structured case does not prove PMS Base. PMS-SEX remains an overlay, not a base extension. MIP remains a maturity-in-practice layer, not a PMS operator. AHP remains a second-order precision overlay, not a truth validator.

It must not allow add-on-as-operator drift. PMS-SEX constructs such as sexual cost topology, direct otherness, withdrawal/binding, after-scene dignity, parity-to-exit, and responsibility-to-authority are overlay handles, not PMS operators. They do not alter the dependency spine. QC-EXT reduced signatures are shorthand only.

It must not allow drift-as-verdict. Drift risks are not findings. Cost-topology mismatch, pseudo-symmetry drift, parity-to-exit, direct-otherness failure, access-entitlement drift, covert-Ψ expectation drift, and after-scene dignity failure remain conditional unless concrete evidence supports them. PMS drift patterns such as `AD_A>>E` and `AD_Sigma_low` are calibration risks, not scores.

It must not permit closure by authority. A polished article, complete YAML, formal operator signature, high PH score, or layered stack may create authority-pressure. AHP explicitly identifies this risk. The proper response is not to weaken the analysis but to strengthen its boundaries: working note, abstract case, non-decisional use, no public punishment, no person verdict, no legal/clinical/forensic conclusion, and no transmission beyond the authorized scholarly context without review.

It must not treat resistance, refusal, non-fit, disagreement, rupture, or absence as automatically pathological, immature, or drift-marked. A refusal may protect dignity. A rupture may preserve non-capture. A non-fit may show that the model should stop. Absence may mean missing evidence rather than hidden failure. PMS, PMS-SEX, MIP, and AHP must all preserve the right not to fit the model.

It must not use examples as evidence. The three examples in this article are article-ready illustrations of the analysis. They demonstrate structural distinctions, temporality, reversibility, and second-order precision risk. They do not create a new case record. They do not supply facts missing from the YAMLs. They do not justify stronger claims than the completed analyses allow.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result of this case is a disciplined non-closure. “Consent Under Asymmetry” can be analysed as a structurally demanding consent field, but it cannot be decided as a concrete event. PMS Core shows why the field cannot be reduced to surface agreement, later contestation, asymmetry alone, or analytic coherence. Its operator sequence keeps distinctions, tensions, framing, asymmetry, temporality, recontextualization, and application restraint visible while preventing later operators such as Σ and Ψ from being used cheaply. The Core analysis therefore succeeds not by closing the case, but by showing where closure would require more than the abstract material supplies.

PMS-SEX adds a necessary sexual-praxeological sharpening. It shows that consent under asymmetry may involve sexual cost topology, direct otherness, embodiment and boundary relevance, withdrawal and binding, publicness risk, and after-scene dignity. But it does not turn those constructs into findings. Sexual cost topology remains a calibration handle, not a score. Direct otherness remains a protection against access entitlement, not an entitlement in reverse. Dignity-in-Practice remains praxeological, not ontological. The overlay makes visible what PMS Core alone does not isolate, while remaining subordinate to PMS Base.

MIP adds a maturity-in-practice layer without converting the case into a maturity verdict. It asks how configuration-handling appears through awareness, coherence, responsibility, power-to-act, IA criteria, trajectory, and dignity protection. Its provisional A/M language belongs to the abstract configuration and the analysis object, not to persons. Its IA-risk means assessability risk under under-specified asymmetry, not an event verdict. Its D activation is qualitative dignity-in-practice protection, not a dignity score or public reading. MIP therefore clarifies the maturity-sensitive structure of the case while preserving score hygiene.

AHP adds second-order quality discipline. It shows that the analysis artefact is high-precision, but also attackable. A polished YAML-derived article can create authority-pressure precisely because it is coherent and layered. AHP therefore does not validate the case. It hardens the article against misuse. It requires the analysis to preserve working-note status, distinguish analysis-use boundaries from known absence of domain relevance, stage future IA and M evidence needs, repeat D-boundaries, and keep PMS-SEX terminology from becoming scene findings.

The three examples contribute by making the case’s boundaries readable without replacing the analysis. The final methodological takeaway is that a mature formal analysis of consent under asymmetry must preserve the difference between structure and decision. It must be able to name asymmetry without condemning, preserve agreement without closing, recognize later contestation without making it omnipotent, discuss dignity without ranking persons, and produce precision without manufacturing evidence. Consent-talk remains usable only if the frame can carry boundary, asymmetry, temporality, stop-capability, integration, and binding discipline; where it cannot, the analysis must remain open rather than close by label. In this case, the analysis does not culminate in a stronger conclusion about what happened. It culminates in a stronger account of what would be required before any such conclusion could responsibly be made.
