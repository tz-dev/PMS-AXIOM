### Why this case matters

This case matters because it turns one of the strongest closure-demands in technical and philosophical reasoning into a structurally visible audit problem: what happens when an interpretation depends not only on recorded events, but also on delayed readings, unrealized alternatives, missing trace entries, and the status of what did not occur.

The phrase “measurement and non-events” carries a powerful temptation. In a quantum workflow, measurement seems to demand a final outcome: something is read, something becomes the recorded result, and the alternatives recede. In an audit trace, however, interpretation is rarely exhausted by the recorded event. A missing log entry may matter. A delayed reading may matter. A non-selected alternative may matter. A trace expected under the workflow frame may fail to appear. The absence itself can become structurally relevant. This is precisely the kind of case AXIOM is built to map: not because AXIOM resolves the metaphysics of measurement, but because it can show where closure is being demanded, where the frame must be declared, where absence becomes structured, and where non-capture must remain visible.

The closure-demand is difficult because it operates at several levels at once. At the most immediate level, the workflow asks for a recorded outcome. At the audit level, the trace asks for a reconstruction of what was expected, what was recorded, what was delayed, and what is missing. At the interpretive level, the unrealized alternatives may affect the meaning of the recorded result, but only if the frame treats them as relevant. At the governance or verification-adjacent level, the temptation is to convert trace coherence into proof: proof of physical adequacy, proof of implementation correctness, proof of legal evidentiary sufficiency, proof of complete explanation, or proof that the PMS/QC mapping captured the case. The completed YAML analyses explicitly resist that conversion.

This case belongs in AXIOM because AXIOM is a cartographic and indexing project. Its task is not to produce a new theory of quantum measurement, not to adjudicate between interpretations of quantum mechanics, and not to certify audit systems. Its task is to render the closure-demand legible. In this case, the closure-demand takes the form: “Given a measurement workflow and an audit trace, what may be interpreted from recorded outcomes, unrealized alternatives, delayed readings, and missing entries?” PMS Core can map the structural operators involved in that demand. PMS-QC can add a quantum-computational overlay vocabulary without redefining PMS. PMS-QC-EXT can sharpen the measurement-branching aspect through a paper-specific extension. None of these layers is allowed to become a verdict machine.

The structural difficulty lies in the ambiguity of absence. Absence can mean many different things. It can mean that an event did not occur. It can mean that an event occurred but was not recorded. It can mean that the recording is delayed. It can mean that the trace schema did not require a record at all. It can mean that the expectation was imported from the wrong frame. It can mean that the missing item belongs to a rival formalism rather than to PMS. PMS Core names this problem through Λ, the structured non-event, but it does not allow every absence to become Λ automatically. Λ depends on □: only a frame-defined expected occurrence can fail, be delayed, or remain absent in the PMS sense.

This is also why the case cannot be solved metaphysically. The phrase “unrealized alternatives” could tempt a metaphysical reading: possible worlds, branches, collapse, reality of alternatives, or observer-dependent ontology. The YAML analyses refuse that move. Within the PMS rendering, unrealized alternatives are treated structurally: they are alternatives that matter inside a declared workflow and measurement/audit frame. PMS does not decide what they are ontologically.

Nor is the case solved empirically. The existence of a quantum workflow in the case material does not supply data, probabilities, hardware behavior, measurement distributions, backend traces, or experimental validation. It therefore cannot support claims about the correctness of a quantum process. PMS-QC and QC-EXT annotate structural roles; they do not replace quantum mechanics, circuit semantics, QASM, Qiskit, Cirq, Braket, noise models, tensor networks, ZX-calculus, or formal verification.

Nor is the case solved clinically, diagnostically, or personally. There is no person whose cognition, intention, responsibility, maturity, pathology, or competence is being evaluated. There is no subject to diagnose. No drift pattern is a person-type. No missing trace entry becomes blame.

Nor is the case solved legally or forensically. “Audit trace” resembles legal evidence language, but the YAML makes clear that no chain-of-custody standard, admissibility rule, evidentiary threshold, liability frame, institutional mandate, or tribunal context has been supplied. The output may be an audit trace in the structural sense, but not a legal finding.

Finally, the case is not PMS Base validation. The fact that PMS can generate a coherent reading is not proof that PMS is complete, uniquely adequate, empirically validated, or superior to rival formalisms. Indeed, the YAML explicitly marks coherence risk: PMS fits this case smoothly because Λ, Θ, Φ, and Χ are naturally relevant to missing entries, delay, reinterpretation, and suspended verdict. That smoothness is itself dangerous if it becomes under-discriminating. AXIOM therefore preserves the rival readings and non-capture zones rather than treating PMS coverage as final success.

### Scene and frame structure

The case supplies a conceptual scene rather than a concrete scene. The scene is a quantum workflow containing measurement steps. These steps generate recorded outcomes, unrealized alternatives, delayed readings, or missing trace entries, and those elements affect how the workflow is interpreted. The scene boundary is therefore not “the quantum world” and not “a particular audit system.” It is the structural interpretation of measurement records and non-events inside an under-specified workflow/audit frame.

The frame □ is the central organizing constraint. At Core level, □ is the workflow/audit frame that determines what counts as an outcome, what counts as a trace entry, what counts as a delayed reading, and what counts as a missing expected event. Without □, there is no PMS Non-Event. A missing item can be meaningful only if the frame first establishes that the item was expected, relevant, or required. This is why the YAML repeatedly insists that missingness is not automatically Λ. A missing trace entry may be irrelevant. A delayed reading may be procedurally acceptable. An unrealized alternative may be outside the audit scope. PMS can only treat these as structurally active when the frame authorizes them.

Inside the frame are the elements that the case material explicitly makes relevant: measurement steps, recorded outcomes, delayed readings, missing trace entries, and unrealized alternatives insofar as they affect workflow interpretation. Also inside the frame are the interpretive operations needed to distinguish, sequence, reframe, and provisionally integrate those elements.

Outside the frame are empirical claims about quantum mechanics, metaphysical claims about measurement, diagnostic claims about persons, legal/forensic conclusions, implementation validation, and PMS Base validation. These remain outside even if the vocabulary of the case resembles those domains. The case can say that an audit trace is structurally incomplete; it cannot say that a legal audit has failed. It can say that a delayed reading changes interpretation; it cannot say that a physical measurement has a particular ontological status. It can say that a trace schema would be needed; it cannot certify a logging system.

The protected constraints are Χ, reversibility, and Dignity-in-Practice. Χ requires reflective distance: do not collapse the case into certainty, blame, metaphysics, implementation verdict, or PMS sufficiency. Reversibility requires that operator assignments remain revisable as soon as a concrete protocol, timestamp model, trace schema, or rival formalization is introduced. Dignity-in-Practice constrains any later human or institutional role analysis: missing traces and delayed readings must not be converted into shaming, ranking, humiliation, or irreversible person labels.

Roles are not concretely present. The YAML mentions possible roles only conditionally: recorder, reader, workflow system, auditor, authority, downstream user, protocol maintainer. Since these roles are not specified, they cannot ground strong Ω or Responsibility_R claims. If a future concrete scene supplied roles, PMS would ask who records, who reads, who controls the frame, who has access, who bears consequences, who may contest, and who can revise the interpretation. In the current case, those questions are operationalization requirements, not findings.

Temporality Θ is active because the case is built around sequence and delay. Measurement time, record time, reading time, delay interval, audit reconstruction time, and retrospective interpretation time must be distinguished. A delayed reading is not the same as a missing entry. A missing entry is not the same as a null outcome. A null outcome is not the same as an unrealized alternative. A later reading may recontextualize the earlier trace, but only if the frame permits that movement.

The reversibility window remains wide. Since the scene is abstract, most assignments can be revised. Λ may disappear if a protocol shows that a missing entry was never expected. Φ may weaken if later readings do not alter interpretation. Ω may become active if a concrete authority gradient is supplied. Σ may strengthen if integration criteria are specified. Ψ may activate if stable protocol commitments or invariants are documented. Until then, the case remains deliberately open.

Exposure and asymmetry conditions are weak. There may be asymmetries of access, obligation, authority, and exposure in a real audit workflow, but the case material does not state them. PMS therefore refuses to inflate Ω. This is a key mark of discipline: quantum-to-classical readout may be structurally asymmetrical in a broad sense, and audit authority may be asymmetrical in a governance sense, but neither is strong enough here to support person-level responsibility, legal-forensic findings, or robust asymmetry drift classification.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading proceeds through the eleven operators in order. This order is not merely decorative. It prevents the analysis from jumping cheaply to coherence, responsibility, self-binding, drift, or final closure.

**Δ — Difference.**
Δ is active and foundational. The case begins with distinctions: recorded outcome versus unrealized alternative, recorded trace versus missing trace entry, timely reading versus delayed reading, measurement step versus later interpretation, null result versus absent record, relevant absence versus irrelevant absence. These distinctions are what make the case legible.

The calibration risk is under-discrimination. If the analysis treats all non-recorded material as one homogeneous “absence,” it fails Δ. A delayed reading is not a missing trace entry. An unrealized alternative is not a failed log. A missing expected entry is not necessarily a physical non-event. Δ must separate outcome status, trace status, temporal status, and interpretive status. Overclaim begins when this differentiation is collapsed and the case is treated as if every gap had the same structural meaning.

**∇ — Impulse.**
∇ is weakly active. It marks the directional pressure to interpret the workflow once a difference matters. A missing entry generates pressure to ask whether something failed. A delayed reading generates pressure to ask whether the result should be reinterpreted. An unrealized alternative generates pressure to ask whether the recorded outcome is enough. But this is structural directedness only. It is not a subjective drive, intention, desire, or psychological impulse.

The calibration risk is anthropomorphism. A workflow does not “want” closure. The audit trace does not “intend” interpretation. ∇ belongs here only as pressure within the interpretive configuration: the difference calls for orientation. It would be overclaim to infer agency or consciousness.

**□ — Frame.**
□ is active and major. It defines the measurement/audit workflow in which the case has meaning. The frame determines what counts as an expected trace, what counts as a reading, what counts as delay, what alternatives are represented, and what interpretive consequences follow from absence.

The dependency relation between □ and Λ is decisive. Without □, there is no structured non-event. A missing trace entry is meaningful only if the frame says it should have been present. A delayed reading matters only if the frame has timing expectations. An unrealized alternative matters only if the workflow tracks alternatives as relevant to interpretation. Overclaim occurs when the analyst imports expectations from quantum theory, software engineering, law, or PMS itself without showing that the case frame contains them.

**Λ — Non-Event.**
Λ is strongly active. The case explicitly names missing trace entries, delayed readings, and unrealized alternatives. PMS treats these as structured absences only where they are expected, relevant, or consequential within □. Λ is therefore the central operator for the audit-trace aspect of the case.

The calibration burden is high. The YAML distinguishes missing data, delayed data, null outcome, unrealized alternative, unobserved branch, failed logging, expected-but-absent event, and irrelevant absence. A PMS reading that cannot hold these apart becomes too smooth. Λ should not become a universal container for all uncertainty. It is specifically meaningful absence or delay within a frame.

Overclaim occurs if Λ is used to assert failure, guilt, drift, metaphysical non-being, or quantum ontology. A missing trace entry may be a logging issue, an irrelevant field, a delayed write, a permissions issue, a data-retention rule, an audit design choice, or a rival formalism’s object. PMS must preserve those possibilities.

**Α — Attractor.**
Α is weak. The case says that a workflow includes measurement steps, which may suggest repetition, but it does not establish recurrence, stabilized patterning, path dependence, or a durable trace routine. Α would become stronger if repeated trace gaps, recurrent delayed readings, stable measurement scripts, or repeating audit interpretation patterns were supplied. They are not.

The dependency risk is important because Ω depends on Α in PMS. If Α is weak, then later strong asymmetry claims must also be treated carefully. Overclaim would occur if a single missing entry were treated as evidence of a stable audit pattern, recurring failure mode, or drift attractor.

**Ω — Asymmetry.**
Ω is weak and conditional. Possible asymmetries exist: a recorder may have access that a later reader lacks; an auditor may have authority to interpret; a system may expose users to trace errors; a protocol maintainer may control what counts as valid evidence. But the case material does not specify these gradients.

The calibration points are concrete: who records, who reads, who interprets, who lacks access, who bears costs, who can contest, who can correct, who controls the schema, and who is exposed to downstream consequences. Without these, Ω remains weak. Overclaim occurs if the analysis treats the mere existence of measurement or audit as sufficient for responsibility, authority, blame, or governance asymmetry.

This is especially important because the case could tempt a legal-forensic or institutional reading. The YAML blocks that move. Audit language does not automatically create legal responsibility. Measurement language does not automatically create agentive asymmetry. Ω must be specified, not assumed.

**Θ — Temporality.**
Θ is active, but dependency-qualified. Timing is central: measurement sequence, recording time, reading delay, missing trace order, reconstruction time, and retrospective interpretation all matter. The case is impossible to analyse without temporal distinctions. Yet full PMS dependency strength is qualified because Α and Ω are weak. Θ is therefore strongly relevant for bounded temporal analysis, but it should not be inflated into a full developmental trajectory or responsibility arc.

Calibration requires separating event time, record time, expected read time, actual read time, audit time, and interpretation time. A delayed reading can reframe an earlier trace. A missing entry can become meaningful only after a later check. A recorded outcome may change significance when the trace schema is re-read. But none of that proves a maturity trajectory, institutional lock-in, or self-binding commitment.

Overclaim occurs when temporal sequence alone is treated as development, responsibility, or stable pattern. The case has delay and reconstruction; it does not yet have a full Θ/Ω responsibility trajectory.

**Φ — Recontextualization.**
Φ is active, but also dependency-qualified. The case explicitly concerns how recorded outcomes, unrealized alternatives, delayed readings, and missing entries affect interpretation. That is recontextualization: an element changes meaning when moved from measurement execution to audit trace, from real-time workflow to delayed reading, or from recorded outcome to retrospective reconstruction.

Calibration requires naming source and target frames. “Measurement frame → audit frame” is one movement. “Real-time record → delayed review” is another. “Outcome-only reading → outcome-plus-missing-trace reading” is another. Φ must not become a generic explanation for any change in interpretation.

Overclaim occurs if every unresolved absence or rival formalization is absorbed as recontextualization. A rival statistical model may not be a PMS frame shift; it may be a better formalization. A missing log may not need Φ; it may simply be missing data. A delayed reading may not change interpretation if the frame already allows delay. Φ must sharpen, not swallow.

**Χ — Distance.**
Χ is active and central. It is the main application discipline of the case. Χ suspends premature verdicts about empirical truth, metaphysical meaning, legal sufficiency, implementation correctness, responsibility, safety, deployment readiness, and PMS adequacy. It also prevents the analyst from converting coherence into closure.

Χ is not empty neutrality. It does not mean refusing to analyse. It means analysing with reversibility, discrimination, and non-capture. It requires explicit falsifier conditions, rival readings, and operationalization requirements. It protects against closure-by-authority.

Overclaim occurs if Χ is dropped in order to say: the audit trace proves failure; the missing entry proves guilt; the delayed reading proves the workflow is invalid; the unrealized alternative proves a metaphysical claim; the PMS mapping proves PMS sufficiency. Any of these would violate the completed YAML’s application discipline.

**Σ — Integration.**
Σ is weak to conditional. The case can support provisional integration: a disciplined interpretation may coordinate recorded outcomes, missing entries, delayed readings, unrealized alternatives, frame shifts, and temporal order into a coherent audit-trace reading. But this integration is not proof.

The calibration risk is forced coherence. PMS can produce a coherent structural account too easily here. If the analysis integrates all gaps into one elegant chain, it may under-discriminate precisely where the case requires care. Σ must be tested against whether it preserves distinctions among missing data, delayed data, null outcome, unrealized alternative, logging failure, irrelevant absence, and non-capture.

Overclaim occurs when Σ is treated as empirical adequacy, legal sufficiency, implementation correctness, physical validity, or PMS superiority. Integration is a structural achievement, not a guarantee.

**Ψ — Self-Binding.**
Ψ is weak and mostly analytic-only. In PMS Core, Ψ could apply if there were stable protocol commitments, audit standards, self-binding analytic discipline, or policy constraints across time. The abstract case does not supply those. The YAML therefore refuses SELF_FIXPOINT and refuses any inference of consciousness, subjectivity, or literal selfhood.

Ψ can appear in two limited ways. First, the analysis itself is self-bound by PMS application discipline: it commits to Χ, reversibility, non-diagnosis, and non-capture. Second, a future concrete workflow might contain explicit protocol commitments: for example, a rule that every measurement event must produce a trace entry within a specified time. But no such rule is present here.

Overclaim occurs if workflow persistence is treated as selfhood, if protocol stability is treated as consciousness, if audit constraints are treated as moral agency, or if weak Ψ is used to establish responsibility without concrete Ω, Θ, and Σ support.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The completed YAML analyses insist that later operators cannot be used cheaply. This is not a formal nicety; it is the difference between structural cartography and closure machinery.

Λ depends on □. This means absence cannot be treated as meaningful until a frame defines the expected occurrence. If an audit trace is missing but no audit schema required it, Λ is not established. If an alternative is unrealized but the workflow does not track alternatives, Λ may not apply. If a reading is delayed but the protocol allows delay, the delay may not function as a non-event. This dependency prevents the case from becoming an absence-amplifier.

Α depends on Δ, ∇, □, and Λ. Pattern formation requires repeated structured interaction, not a single gap. Therefore the case cannot infer recurrent drift, stable failure, or attractor dynamics from an abstract mention of measurement steps. Α remains weak because recurrence has not been demonstrated.

Ω depends on Α. Strong asymmetry claims require more than possible access differences. The analysis would need stable role differentiation, authority, obligation, exposure, or capacity gradients. Because Α is weak and concrete roles are absent, Ω remains weak. This prevents the case from becoming a responsibility or blame analysis.

Θ depends on Ω and Α in the PMS dependency table, but the case has a bounded temporal reading because timing, delay, and reconstruction are central. This is why the corrected status is dependency-qualified rather than simply satisfied. Θ is active for sequence and delay; it is not fully active as a mature responsibility trajectory.

Φ depends on Θ, Ω, and □. The case has enough □ and Θ for bounded recontextualization, but Ω remains weak. Therefore Φ is active for frame shifts in interpretation, but it should not be expanded into authority-loaded or responsibility-loaded recontextualization unless a concrete asymmetry map is provided.

Χ depends on Φ, Θ, and □. In this case, Χ is well-supported as application discipline because the frame and temporal/recontextual structure are clear enough to demand restraint. Χ is what keeps the analysis from rushing past its own evidentiary limits.

Σ depends on Χ and Φ. Integration is invalid if Χ is absent. This is why coherence cannot be proof. A coherent PMS story that suppresses missingness, rival readings, or operational uncertainty is not a valid Σ; it is forced closure. Proper Σ must preserve the residues it integrates.

Ψ depends on Σ, Θ, and Χ. Since Σ is weak and Θ is dependency-qualified, Ψ remains weak. This blocks SELF_FIXPOINT and any strong responsibility or protocol-binding claim. Ψ may be analytic discipline or future protocol commitment, but not a finding of selfhood or agency.

The main dependency shortcut to avoid is Δ/∇ → Ψ collapse. In this case, that would look like: something is missing, therefore the workflow failed; the workflow failed, therefore someone or something is responsible; responsibility is fixed, therefore the interpretation is closed. PMS disallows this. The route from difference to binding must pass through frame, non-event calibration, pattern evidence, asymmetry mapping, temporality, recontextualization, distance, and integration. Most of those are absent or weak here.

Another invalid shortcut is Λ → Φ → Σ totalization. That would mean: because something is absent, we reinterpret the workflow; because the reinterpretation is coherent, it is sufficient. The YAML explicitly marks this as coherence risk. Non-capture is not failure. Some missingness may remain outside PMS. Some rival readings may be better. Some data may simply be unavailable. PMS must not become a machine that converts every gap into its own evidence.

This is why the case’s strongest formula remains conditional:

**Σ? ∘ Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ω? ∘ Α? ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇? ∘ Δ**

The formula is deliberately marked with question marks. It says that Δ, □, Λ, Θ, Φ, and Χ carry the main structural load, while ∇, Α, Ω, Σ, and Ψ remain weak or conditional. This is the correct shape of the case: not closure, but disciplined traceability.

### Derived structures

Derived structures are not primitive. The completed YAML repeatedly preserves this discipline. Awareness_A, Coherence_C, Responsibility_R, Action_E, Dignity_in_Practice_D, and SELF_FIXPOINT are not independent axes that can be scored or applied directly. They are formula-derived structures. If their required operators are weak, the derived structure must also remain weak, conditional, analytic-only, or not applicable.

**Awareness_A.**
Awareness_A is derived from Θ / □ / Δ: sustained, framed differentiation across time. In this case it is partially active, and only structurally. The workflow/audit configuration distinguishes recorded outcome, unrealized alternative, delayed reading, and missing trace entry across a temporal sequence inside a frame. That is enough for structural awareness in the PMS sense: differences are maintained across time within a frame.

It is not phenomenological awareness. The workflow is not conscious. The audit trace is not aware. A quantum process is not being anthropomorphized. Awareness_A here means that the analysis can track framed distinctions over time. It does not imply experience, perception, subjectivity, or selfhood.

The calibration issue is that Awareness_A must keep outcome status, trace status, reading status, and interpretive status distinct. If all of those are fused, the derived structure loses discriminative value. Awareness_A is therefore weak-to-partial, not a strong claim.

**Coherence_C.**
Coherence_C is derived from Θ / Λ / □ / ∇: temporally stabilized structuring of impulse and expectation within a frame. In this case it is active but fragile. It is active because the frame establishes expectations, Λ marks meaningful absence or delay, ∇ marks interpretive pressure, and Θ orders the sequence. Together these can produce a provisional workflow interpretation.

It is fragile because the same material that allows coherence also threatens it. Missing trace entries, delayed readings, and unrealized alternatives can destabilize the account. A coherent story may be premature. PMS coherence is insufficient by itself. The YAML therefore treats Coherence_C as available but never as proof of empirical, legal, implementation, or physical adequacy.

The key point is that coherence is not closure. A coherent PMS trace can still be wrong, under-specified, over-assimilative, or inferior to a rival formalization.

**Responsibility_R.**
Responsibility_R is derived from Ψ / Φ / Θ / Ω. It is weak and conditional. The current case does not specify a responsible role, an authority gradient, an obligation, a duty, a legal standard, a self-binding commitment, or a concrete scene of accountability. Θ and Φ are present in bounded ways, but Ω and Ψ are weak. Therefore Responsibility_R cannot be established.

This matters because the audit-trace vocabulary might tempt responsibility assignment. Missing entries often invite the question “who failed?” Delayed readings invite “who should have checked?” PMS blocks that jump. Responsibility_R would require concrete asymmetry, temporal obligation, recontextualization of duty, and self-binding commitment. None is supplied.

Therefore Responsibility_R is not blame, not liability, not moral fault, and not institutional accountability. It remains a possible later structure if additional data are supplied.

**Action_E.**
Action_E is derived from Σ / Θ / ∇: integrated realization of directedness across time. In this case it is under-specified. There is interpretive pressure and temporal sequence, but no concrete integrated action by an actor, system, institution, or protocol is described. The case is about interpretation of workflow traces, not about a completed action plan or enacted response.

Action_E could become relevant if the case were operationalized: for example, if an audit procedure required a concrete response to missing trace entries, or if a workflow controller acted on delayed readings. But the abstract case does not include that. The correct status is therefore under-specified, not failed. Non-assignment is not a low score; it is the preservation of scope.

**Dignity_in_Practice_D.**
Dignity_in_Practice_D is derived from Ψ / Χ / Ω. In this case it functions mainly as an application constraint, not as an ontological claim. Since Ω and Ψ are weak, D is not being used to describe a fully active asymmetrical human responsibility relation. Instead, it governs how the analysis must be conducted: do not use missing traces, delayed readings, or audit ambiguity to shame, rank, diagnose, humiliate, blame, or irreversibly label persons or roles.

This distinction is crucial. Dignity-in-Practice is not ontological dignity and not a ranking of worth. It is a praxeological constraint on application under asymmetry. If later human or institutional roles are introduced, D requires restraint. The analysis must not become a tribunal.

**SELF_FIXPOINT.**
SELF_FIXPOINT is derived from the full chain:

**Ψ ∘ Σ ∘ Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ω ∘ Α ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ**

It is not established. The case does not concern a self-relation, autobiographical identity, subjectivity, consciousness, or personhood. It concerns workflow interpretation and audit-trace non-events. Since Α, Ω, Σ, and Ψ are weak, the full chain cannot be activated.

The only acceptable Ψ-adjacent reading is analytic self-binding: the analysis binds itself to PMS discipline, QC overlay discipline, non-capture, and non-guarantee. A future concrete system might also show protocol self-binding, but this would still not imply consciousness. In artificial or technical systems, Ψ means constraint or policy stability, not subjective experience.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The completed PMS Core YAML treats drift patterns as possible structural risks, not as findings. This distinction is central. A drift pattern in PMS is not a diagnosis, not a person-type, not a clinical category, not a moral verdict, and not a score. It marks a way in which an operator configuration could distort if the relevant conditions were present. In this case, those conditions are mostly under-specified.

The first drift pattern considered is **AD_A>>E**, excessive distance between awareness and enactment. In the completed YAML, this pattern is marked as possible but not entailed. The case could generate this risk if the audit workflow over-expanded interpretive awareness while practical action remained suppressed, delayed, or chronically displaced. For example, a workflow could endlessly notice missing entries, delayed readings, and unrealized alternatives without ever producing a disciplined response, correction rule, or explicit boundary. In such a configuration, awareness-like differentiation would expand while action-like enactment remained weak.

But the YAML does not classify AD_A>>E as present. The reason is dependency discipline. The case does not establish a concrete actor, repeated pattern, stabilized attractor, asymmetry structure, obligation, or suppressed action pathway. Awareness_A is only partially active as structural differentiation across time. Action_E is under-specified. Therefore the axis effects remain under-specified rather than active. This is not a negative rating. It is a refusal to over-read an abstract case.

The relevant axis effects are therefore:

* awareness axis: under-specified;
* action axis: under-specified.

This means that the analysis cannot say that interpretive awareness is over-expanded, nor that action is suppressed or chronically delayed. It can only mark that such a pattern would become a risk if the abstract workflow were later operationalized into a system that repeatedly noticed trace irregularities without resolving, escalating, or bounding them.

The second drift pattern considered is **AD_Sigma_low**, fragmented or weak integration. This risk is more intuitive in the case, because missing trace entries, delayed readings, and unrealized alternatives can easily destabilize coherence. If Φ recontextualization happens repeatedly while Σ integration remains weak, the workflow interpretation can become volatile. Each new reading, absence, delay, or alternative might shift the frame before a coherent account has been formed. The result could be unstable interpretation: the case keeps changing meaning without producing a disciplined, revisable, frame-bound integration.

Still, the YAML does not classify AD_Sigma_low as present. It marks the pattern as possible but not entailed. The reason is again dependency discipline. Σ is weak and conditional, but weak Σ alone is not drift. It is only an indication that the abstract case does not provide enough integration criteria. There is no evidence of chronic volatility, no repeated failed integration, no responsibility inconsistency, and no concrete workflow under operational stress.

The relevant axis effects are therefore:

* coherence axis: under-specified;
* responsibility axis: under-specified.

This matters because PMS must not convert under-specification into failure. The absence of a strong Σ finding does not mean that the workflow is incoherent. The absence of Responsibility_R does not mean responsibility is inconsistent. It means that the case material lacks the data needed to make those assignments.

The broader coherence risk remains high, but that risk is methodological rather than diagnostic. PMS fits the case smoothly: Λ handles missingness, Θ handles delay, Φ handles reinterpretation, Χ handles restraint, and Σ can coordinate the resulting structure. This smooth fit creates the danger of a too-elegant reading. The case could be made to look more integrated than the material warrants. That is why the YAML repeatedly insists that PMS coherence is insufficient by itself. A coherent PMS derivation must still discriminate. It must distinguish missing entry, delayed reading, null outcome, unrealized alternative, logging failure, irrelevant absence, empirical evidence gap, legal-forensic evidence issue, implementation bug, and non-capture.

Under-discrimination is therefore the main drift-adjacent risk. It is not that the case already shows drift. It is that PMS could become too smooth if every trace irregularity were assimilated into Λ, every later interpretation into Φ, every provisional account into Σ, and every protocol rule into Ψ. Such a reading would turn PMS into a closure machine. The completed YAML prevents that by marking Α, Ω, Σ, Ψ, Responsibility_R, Action_E, and SELF_FIXPOINT as weak, conditional, under-specified, or not established.

Risk is not finding. Under-specification is not failure. Non-assignment is not a low score. These three points are the drift-discipline core of the case.

The reading weakens if all records are complete, timely, and unambiguous. In that case the main Λ/Θ/Φ structure would be unnecessary. The case would become a straightforward measurement record, not an audit-trace non-event case.

The reading weakens if no recurrence or stabilization exists. That keeps Α weak and blocks stronger attractor, drift, or pattern claims. A single missing or delayed entry is not a stable failure pattern.

The reading weakens if no asymmetry exists. If no one controls access, no one bears differential exposure, no authority interprets, no obligation exists, and no capacity gradient is present, Ω remains weak. This blocks Responsibility_R and Dignity-in-Practice as active relational structures, though D remains an application constraint.

The reading weakens if no valid recontextualization rule exists. If later readings or missing entries do not legitimately alter interpretation across frames, Φ must be reduced. PMS cannot declare recontextualization merely because the analyst sees an interesting shift.

The reading weakens if integration criteria are absent. If there is no way to coordinate outcomes, absences, delays, and alternatives into a coherent account without suppressing uncertainty, Σ must remain weak. A forced story is not integration.

The reading weakens if no stable protocol commitment exists. Without explicit protocol, policy, invariant, or self-binding discipline, Ψ remains analytic-only. SELF_FIXPOINT remains not established.

The PMS reading becomes under-discriminating if it cannot separate missing entry, delayed reading, null outcome, unrealized alternative, logging failure, irrelevant absence, empirical evidence gap, legal evidence problem, and implementation bug. This is the key quality-control condition. If PMS turns all of these into Λ or Φ, it loses discriminative force.

The PMS-QC overlay weakens if no iteration metadata is supplied. `QITERATE` cannot be validated without `k`, loop body, stopping condition, maximum depth, and ordering. The abstract mention of measurement steps is not enough.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It does not evaluate persons, researchers, operators, engineers, auditors, institutions, or users. No missing trace entry implies incompetence. No delayed reading implies avoidance. No unrealized alternative implies confusion. No PMS drift pattern implies pathology.

It must not be used for person ranking. PMS operators and derived structures are not dignity scores, maturity scores, rationality scores, competence scores, or moral rankings. Awareness_A, Coherence_C, Responsibility_R, Action_E, Dignity_in_Practice_D, and SELF_FIXPOINT are derived structural readings, not measures of human worth.

It must not be used for legal or forensic conclusion. The term “audit trace” here is structural. It does not establish chain of custody, admissibility, proof, liability, negligence, culpability, or evidentiary sufficiency. Any such use would require a separate legal-forensic frame not present in the case.

It must not be used as metaphysical proof. PMS does not decide what quantum measurement is. PMS-QC does not decide the reality of unrealized alternatives. QC-EXT does not settle branching, collapse, observer-dependence, possible worlds, or the ontology of measurement.

It must not be used as empirical proof. No data, probability distribution, experimental setup, hardware trace, measurement sample, or validation result is supplied. A structural reading of a workflow is not evidence that the workflow behaved correctly.

It must not be used as implementation validation. YAML is not code. A macro program is not an executable circuit. A structural trace is not a verified logging system. `QFRAME`, `QMEASURE`, and `MEASUREMENT_PROJECTION_MODEL` do not certify software, hardware, compiler behavior, or backend compatibility.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. The case shows that PMS can render a structural projection. It does not prove PMS completeness, uniqueness, empirical adequacy, or superiority. PMS-QC and QC-EXT also remain unvalidated as universal QC formalisms.

It must not treat add-ons as operators. `QFRAME`, `QMEASURE`, `QITERATE`, `QSTABILIZE`, and `MEASUREMENT_PROJECTION_MODEL` are overlay handles. They are not new PMS operators and do not alter Δ–Ψ.

It must not treat drift as verdict. AD_A>>E and AD_Sigma_low are possible risks, not findings. QC policy risks are under-specification notes, not failures. `basis_reframe_guard` is weak risk-level caution, not governance violation.

It must not perform closure by authority. Neither PMS nor QC nor QC-EXT may be used to force closure where Λ remains. The analysis must preserve rival readings, non-capture, and falsifier conditions. The strongest valid output is an audit-trace-oriented structural map with explicit non-guarantee discipline.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result of this case is a bounded, layered audit-trace reading of measurement and non-events. PMS Core renders the central structure: a workflow/audit frame in which recorded outcomes, delayed readings, unrealized alternatives, and missing trace entries become legible only through careful differentiation, frame declaration, temporal ordering, recontextualization, reflective distance, and provisional integration. The strongest Core reading remains conditional: Δ, □, Λ, Θ, Φ, and Χ carry the main analytic weight, while ∇, Α, Ω, Σ, and Ψ remain weak or conditional unless further evidence is supplied. The case therefore does not terminate by asserting that absence means failure, that delay means fault, that alternatives imply metaphysical branching, or that coherence proves adequacy. Its legitimate endpoint is a disciplined structural map of where interpretation is possible and where Λ-residue must remain visible.

The QC layer adds useful but bounded visibility. PMS-QC makes the measurement/audit workflow annotatable through `QFRAME`, `QMEASURE`, weak `QITERATE`, provisional `Σ?`, and optional `QSTABILIZE?`. This helps distinguish generic PMS non-event analysis from a quantum-computational workflow rendering. PMS-QC-EXT then adds the narrower `MEASUREMENT_PROJECTION_MODEL`, which sharpens the measurement-domain boundary between actual recorded outcome, unrealized branch or alternative, delayed reading, and missing trace entry. These additions are analytically useful precisely because they remain overlays. They do not redefine PMS operators, do not replace QC formalisms, and do not create physical, implementation, safety, legal, or governance guarantees.

The three examples contribute by making the boundary discipline concrete. The missing-trace example shows that Λ is not generic absence but frame-bound missingness. The delayed-reading example shows that Θ and Φ preserve reversibility: what first appears absent may later become delayed, if the frame permits that reinterpretation. The unrealized-alternative example shows why QC-EXT’s measurement projection is useful without becoming metaphysics: it keeps actual outcome and unrealized branch distinguishable while refusing to decide what unrealized alternatives are ontologically. The examples demonstrate the analysis; they do not replace it and do not function as evidence.

What remains unclaimed is extensive and intentionally preserved. The case does not claim empirical truth about quantum measurement, metaphysical resolution of unrealized alternatives, diagnostic insight into persons, legal or forensic sufficiency, clinical relevance, implementation correctness, software validation, hardware validity, safety, deployment readiness, or PMS Base validation. It does not classify drift as present. It does not assign responsibility. It does not score awareness, coherence, action, dignity, or self-binding. It does not decide among quantum-formal, audit-log, Bayesian, causal, formal-methods, information-theoretic, legal-forensic, governance, or anti-capture readings.

The boundary discipline is therefore the substantive result. PMS coherence is not enough. A smooth operator derivation can still under-discriminate or over-assimilate. Add-on notation is not authority. QC macro structure is not physical correctness. EXT refinement is not ontology. Non-capture is not failure. Under-specification is not deficiency. Non-assignment is not a low score. The case remains valid only while Χ, reversibility, and Dignity-in-Practice constrain its use.

The final methodological takeaway is that measurement-related audit traces become structurally legible only when recorded events and non-events are held apart with disciplined frame logic. PMS Core shows how to map that structure; PMS-QC and QC-EXT sharpen the quantum-computational annotation; but no layer is allowed to convert structural legibility into final closure. The case demonstrates AXIOM’s core method: map the closure-demand, preserve the Λ-residue, justify each overlay, keep rival formalisms visible, and refuse the slide from coherent rendering into verdict.
