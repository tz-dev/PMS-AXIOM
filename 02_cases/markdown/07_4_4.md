### Why this case matters

“Tragedy” names one of the strongest philosophical closure-demands because it arises precisely where ordinary closure becomes unavailable, insufficient, or suspect. A tragic situation is not merely sad, difficult, unfortunate, or unresolved. The category becomes meaningful where loss, conflict, guilt, responsibility, or irreversibility continue to matter even though no action, interpretation, reconciliation, or explanatory synthesis can fully repair what has occurred. This makes tragedy structurally difficult: it asks for recognition without solution, intelligibility without completion, and responsibility without the guarantee that responsibility can restore the damaged field.

The closure-demand is therefore double. On one side, the category of tragedy asks that something be understood. It asks that loss not be treated as random noise, that conflict not be dissolved into mere misunderstanding, that guilt and responsibility not be evacuated by saying “nothing can be done,” and that irreversibility not be neutralized by retrospective explanation. On the other side, tragedy resists the very closure it demands. If the analysis makes the tragic situation too coherent, too well-integrated, or too cleanly resolved, then it risks destroying the category it was meant to clarify. Tragedy is not a puzzle whose success condition is disappearance. Its residue is not necessarily a defect in the analysis.

This is why the case belongs in AXIOM. AXIOM is not a verdict machine, a diagnostic tool, or a theory of metaphysical solutions. It is a cartographic and indexing project: it maps closure-demands, identifies the frame in which a demand appears, and preserves the residue that remains when legitimate closure stops. “Tragedy as a category” is therefore a high-value case because it tests whether the PMS stack can remain disciplined under a closure-demand that strongly tempts over-integration. The case asks whether PMS can make tragedy structurally legible without turning tragedy into failure, immaturity, drift, incomplete calibration, or insufficient maturity.

The case is especially important because many treatments of tragedy oscillate between two excesses. One excess is metaphysical inflation: tragedy becomes a claim about fate, being, mortality, evil, finitude, or the structure of existence as such. The other excess is reductive explanation: tragedy is translated into error, system failure, psychological defect, social conflict, legal culpability, or insufficient repair. The PMS reading rejects both shortcuts. It does not deny that metaphysical, theological, aesthetic, existential, or ethical readings may be powerful. It simply does not claim them. It also does not reduce the tragic remainder to a missing variable, a failed action, an incomplete integration, or an immature response.

The central philosophical pressure is the demand to understand how something can remain meaningful without becoming resolvable. A loss may remain loss even after explanation. A conflict may retain real incompatibility even after all sides are understood. Guilt may remain structurally intelligible without becoming a legal verdict. Responsibility may remain relevant without enabling full repair. Irreversibility may remain decisive even where no further action can undo the event. The category of tragedy gathers these pressures, but the analysis must not use the category as a final authority. Calling something tragic is not itself a proof, a diagnosis, or a moral conclusion.

The case is therefore not being solved metaphysically, empirically, clinically, legally, or diagnostically. No claim is made about tragedy as an ontological feature of existence. No empirical study is supplied. No clinical subject is present. No legal standard is invoked. No person is evaluated. No institution is judged. No responsibility is assigned. The analysis works only at the level of structural intelligibility: it asks what operator configuration makes the category of tragedy legible, what that configuration cannot claim, where it remains weak, and where rival readings may preserve something PMS does not capture.

This makes the case methodologically revealing. PMS must show restraint exactly where its formal power is strongest. It can produce a coherent operator chain, but it must not treat that coherence as proof. It can identify Λ-residue, but it must not treat naming the residue as resolving it. It can mark possible Ω-asymmetry, but it must not convert that asymmetry into blame. It can use PMS-CONFLICT to sharpen possible non-integrability, but it must not treat terminality as finality. It can use MIP and AHP to protect transmission, but it must not let those downstream layers become hidden evaluation.

In this sense, the case matters not because it “solves tragedy,” but because it asks whether a structural grammar can remain honest before tragedy. The proper result is not closure-by-authority. It is a disciplined map of where closure is demanded, where it remains unavailable, and where residue must remain visible.

The scope discipline can therefore be summarized as follows: this case is allowed to render tragedy structurally; it is not allowed to settle tragedy ontologically, empirically, clinically, legally, or morally. ### Scene and frame structure

The scene is conceptual rather than concrete. The “scene” consists of a philosophical category being examined under structural constraints. The boundary is therefore not an event boundary but a frame boundary: the case asks how tragedy should be understood as a category for situations in which meaningful loss, conflict, guilt, responsibility, or irreversibility may remain without full resolution.

The active frame, □, is “tragedy as a category.” Inside this frame are structural relations among loss, conflict, guilt, responsibility, irreversibility, unavailable repair, unavailable reconciliation, unavailable final interpretation, and meaningful non-resolution. Also inside the frame is the question of whether such non-resolution can remain intelligible without being converted into failure. The frame allows PMS to ask what operators are needed to make this category structurally legible.

Outside the frame are metaphysical proofs of tragedy, empirical accounts of tragic experience, clinical interpretations of grief or guilt, legal determinations of culpability, aesthetic theories of dramatic genre as such, and governance decisions about response. These may be rival domains, but they are not included as claims in the PMS Core analysis. The case may remain adjacent to them without absorbing them.

Several protected constraints govern the frame. First, unresolved absence must not automatically become PMS drift. Second, non-fit must not automatically become incomplete calibration. Third, rival framing must not automatically become failed integration. Fourth, tragedy must not be treated as maturity failure. Fifth, coherence must not become sufficiency. Sixth, PMS must not become a closure machine. These constraints are not decorative; they preserve the tragic category from being dissolved by the very analysis that renders it legible.

Roles are not present in the ordinary scene sense. There is no agent, sufferer, judge, institution, victim, perpetrator, witness, or repair actor. There are only analytic roles: PMS Core as base structural grammar, PMS-CONFLICT as overlay lens, MIP as working-note review, AHP as analysis-artifact hardening. These analytic roles are not actors in a tragedy; they are layers in the case artefact. Their separation matters because each layer has a different authority boundary.

Temporality, Θ, is present even without a concrete chronology because irreversibility is explicitly part of the case material. The case is not about timeless contradiction. It concerns what has happened, what cannot be undone, what remains meaningful after the possibility of full repair has closed, and what later interpretation cannot fully reconcile. This gives Θ major analytic weight. But the absence of concrete sequence means Θ cannot be scaled into a timeline, trajectory band, lock-in window, or MIP responsibility horizon.

The reversibility window is therefore asymmetrical. The analysis itself remains reversible: its structural reading can be revised, narrowed, challenged, or replaced by rival formalizations. But the category of tragedy refers to situations where some object-level irreversibility may be present. PMS must hold both facts at once. The analysis must remain reversible even when the category it analyzes concerns irreversibility.

Exposure and asymmetry conditions are indicated but not calibrated. The terms guilt, responsibility, loss, and conflict imply possible asymmetries: who bears loss, who carries guilt, who has capacity to repair, who is exposed to consequence, who can interpret, who can withdraw, who can demand closure, who must live with non-closure. But none of these distributions is specified. Ω is therefore active as a thematic structural concern, but under-specified as a concrete operator field.

The result is a conceptual scene with strong frame and temporality, central non-event structure, bounded recontextualization, and necessary distance, but weak concrete asymmetry, weak attractor formation, weak self-binding, and no scoreable enactment. This is not a weakness of the article; it is the correct consequence of the case type.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core analysis begins with the full operator sequence Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, Ψ. The sequence is not a checklist to be force-filled. Each operator must be calibrated, and later operators cannot be activated cheaply. In this case, the strongest operators are Δ, □, Λ, Θ, Φ, and Χ. Ω is relevant but under-specified. Α is weak. Σ is limited. Ψ is conditional or analytic-only.

**Δ — Difference** is active and foundational. Tragedy requires distinctions. It distinguishes loss from non-loss, conflict from harmony, guilt from innocence, responsibility from non-responsibility, reversible from irreversible, resolved from unresolved, repair from non-repair, reconciliation from non-reconciliation. Without Δ, tragedy would not be structurally intelligible because there would be no contrast that demands meaning. However, Δ must not be overclaimed. The mere presence of difference does not make a situation tragic. Many differences are ordinary, reparable, trivial, or non-meaningful. For tragedy, Δ must enter a frame in which the difference matters under loss, conflict, responsibility, or irreversibility.

**∇ — Impulse** is weak. The case implies directed pressure toward resolution, interpretation, repair, reconciliation, or responsibility. A tragic situation often produces a demand: something should be answered, healed, understood, forgiven, repaired, or carried. Yet no concrete actor or impulse is supplied. Therefore ∇ is structural directedness, not psychological motivation. It would be an overclaim to say that someone desires closure, avoids closure, seeks repair, or fails to act. The correct reading is weaker: the category of tragedy contains a directional pressure toward closure that may not be satisfiable.

**□ — Frame** is active and central. The case is explicitly about tragedy as a category. This means that the relevant object is not raw suffering or raw conflict, but a frame under which certain situations become legible as tragic. The tragic frame decides which absences matter, which losses remain meaningful, which conflicts are not merely disagreements, and which irreversible structures resist full integration. Calibration of □ is crucial because tragedy can be framed aesthetically, ethically, metaphysically, religiously, narratively, legally, or existentially. PMS does not collapse these into one frame. It uses □ to declare the frame currently in use: conceptual structural category analysis. If □ is pushed too far, it becomes frame imperialism: “tragedy” becomes the master frame under which all unresolved situations are absorbed. That would be under-discriminating and invalid.

**Λ — Non-Event** is one of the strongest operators in the case. The case material explicitly names what is absent: no fully resolving action, interpretation, or reconciliation may be available. Λ is not mere lack. It is structured absence: an expected event fails to occur within a frame where its absence matters. In this case, the expected event may be repair, reconciliation, final interpretation, forgiveness, restitution, recognition, answer, or resolving action. The tragic force comes from the fact that this non-event remains meaningful. The absence does not simply disappear because it cannot be fulfilled. Calibration of Λ must distinguish between ordinary missing information and tragic non-resolution. If no expectation exists, Λ is not warranted. If the absence is merely temporary or practically solvable, tragedy may be overextended. If Λ is suppressed, the analysis becomes a closure machine. If every absence is called tragic, the reading becomes too broad.

**Α — Attractor** is weak. The category “tragedy” may function as a stabilized interpretive pattern across many cases. It can gather recurring structures of loss, conflict, guilt, responsibility, and irreversibility. But no concrete repeated interaction, role-script, institutional pattern, or path-dependent field is supplied. Therefore Α cannot be strongly activated as a scene-level attractor. It is weakly present only as category-level stabilization. Overclaiming Α would turn a conceptual category into a demonstrated pattern of praxis without evidence. The dependency risk is important because Ω and Θ depend on Α in PMS. Since Α is weak, later claims involving asymmetry and temporality must be calibrated carefully.

**Ω — Asymmetry** is active but under-specified. The case names guilt and responsibility, and these imply possible asymmetries of obligation, exposure, capacity, vulnerability, power, burden, or repair. Loss is rarely evenly distributed; responsibility is rarely evenly available; guilt may attach unevenly; irreversibility may expose some roles more than others. Yet no concrete distribution is given. There is no specified who, toward whom, under which frame, with what authority, and at what cost. Ω therefore remains a major calibration point but not a completed finding. Pushing Ω too far would convert tragedy into blame, hierarchy, culpability, or moral ranking. Underusing Ω would erase the fact that tragedy often involves unequal exposure and responsibility. The disciplined reading is: Ω is structurally indicated but not concretely assignable.

**Θ — Temporality** is active and central. Irreversibility is explicitly named in the case. Tragedy depends on temporal structure: something has happened, something cannot be undone, consequences persist, and later action may not fully repair the field. Θ is not merely sequence. It is the operator that makes persistence, delay, trajectory, consequence, and closure windows meaningful. The calibration risk is to treat any before/after structure as tragic temporality. A schedule is not tragedy. A delay is not tragedy. Θ becomes tragic when irreversible consequence or non-repair remains meaningful within the frame. Because Ω and Α are weak, Θ is strong at the conceptual level but not yet scene-calibrated as a trajectory.

**Φ — Recontextualization** is active but bounded. The case itself asks how tragedy should be understood as a category. That is a recontextualization: situations involving loss, conflict, guilt, responsibility, and irreversibility are placed under a tragic frame. Φ can clarify what PMS Core alone makes structurally visible. But Φ must not become cure. To call something tragic may preserve non-resolution rather than solve it. A tragic reframing can deepen legibility while leaving the loss unrepaired. The risk is over-assimilation: every rupture, disagreement, absence, or rival frame could be redescribed as PMS recontextualization. That would erase non-capture. Φ must therefore remain bounded by Χ and by rival-readings discipline.

**Χ — Distance** is active as the central application discipline. Distance is required because the case contains language with high misuse potential: guilt, responsibility, irreversibility, tragedy, non-resolution. Χ suspends premature verdict. It prevents the analysis from becoming a tribunal, a metaphysical proof, a diagnosis, or a closure-by-authority. It also protects against emotional or conceptual urgency: the fact that tragedy feels serious does not authorize overclaim. But Χ must not become sterile detachment. Distance does not erase loss, responsibility, or guilt. It maintains reflective restraint while preserving the meaningfulness of the tragic remainder. If Χ is dropped, the analysis risks blame, ranking, forced reconciliation, or finality. If Χ becomes excessive abstraction, the analysis risks neutralizing tragedy.

**Σ — Integration** is limited. PMS can integrate the structural elements of the case into a coherent reading: framed difference, closure pressure, meaningful non-event, weak category attractor, possible asymmetry, irreversibility, bounded recontextualization, and reflective distance. But the case explicitly warns that no fully resolving action, interpretation, or reconciliation may be available. Therefore Σ cannot become the success condition. Tragic coherence is not tragic resolution. A PMS reading may be coherent and still incomplete in the sense that the tragic remainder remains. This is one of the most important calibration points: coherence is not proof, integration is not reconciliation, and structural intelligibility is not adequacy.

**Ψ — Self-Binding** is weak and conditional. Responsibility is named, but no concrete agent, promise, commitment, self-relation, role-duty, or longitudinal identity structure is supplied. Therefore Ψ cannot be claimed as a case-internal self-binding structure. It remains relevant in two limited ways. First, it is a possible future operator if a concrete tragic scene supplies self-binding commitments. Second, it applies to the analysis itself as discipline: the analysis is self-bound not to overclaim, not to diagnose, not to rank, not to assign guilt, and not to validate PMS. But SELF_FIXPOINT is not derivable from the case material. Pushing Ψ too far would create unsupported responsibility or selfhood claims.

The final PMS Core formula therefore had to be dependency-conscious. The dependency-complete formula is:

Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ω ∘ Α ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ

This formula includes Α and Ω for dependency hygiene even though both remain weak or under-specified. It excludes Σ and Ψ from the minimal final formula because integration and self-binding require stronger conditions than the abstract case supplies. The formula says: tragedy as a PMS-projected category is framed difference under weak directedness, meaningful non-event, weak category-level attractor status, under-specified asymmetry, and irreversible temporality, held through bounded recontextualization and reflective distance. It does not say that tragedy has been fully integrated or self-bound.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The most important discipline in this case is that later PMS operators cannot be used cheaply. It is tempting to move too quickly from tragedy to responsibility, from responsibility to guilt, from guilt to self-binding, from irreversibility to terminality, from coherence to integration, and from integration to closure. The YAML analyses explicitly prevent this.

The dependency issue begins with Α. Since attractor status is weak, downstream operators that depend on stabilized patterning cannot be treated as fully calibrated. Ω can be thematically active because the case names responsibility and guilt, but it cannot become a concrete asymmetry gradient without a scene. Θ is central because irreversibility is explicit, but without concrete Α and Ω it remains a conceptual temporality rather than a calibrated trajectory. Φ is active because tragedy is a reframing category, but its dependencies remind us that recontextualization should not float freely above the case. Χ is active because the application discipline can be satisfied even in a conceptual analysis. Σ and Ψ remain the most restricted.

Σ remains conditional because tragedy resists being solved by coherence. PMS can produce a coherent description of why tragedy is structurally difficult, but that coherence does not eliminate the tragic remainder. In fact, if the PMS reading becomes too smooth, it becomes suspect. A perfectly integrated account of tragedy may fail because tragedy is partly about what cannot be fully integrated. The correct use of Σ here is limited: it may coordinate the analysis, but it may not claim reconciliation.

Ψ remains even more conditional. Responsibility-language may suggest self-binding, but PMS requires more than thematic proximity. Ψ requires integrated structures across time, reflective distance, and a self-relation or binding commitment. The abstract category of tragedy supplies none of this as a concrete scene. Therefore responsibility remains weak, and SELF_FIXPOINT remains unavailable. The analysis itself can be self-bound to its guardrails, but the category cannot be treated as a self.

Coherence is not proof. This point recurs across PMS Core, MIP, and AHP. A YAML artefact can be internally consistent, dependency-aware, and terminologically disciplined while still failing to capture the target adequately. In this case, coherence risk is high because the PMS chain can make tragedy appear too structurally elegant. If the tragic remainder becomes merely a well-placed Λ-field inside a smooth formula, something may have been lost. PMS coherence therefore has to be paired with discrimination tests, rival readings, falsifiers, and non-capture zones.

Non-capture is not failure. This is especially important for tragedy. Some aspects of tragedy may be aesthetic, metaphysical, religious, existential, historical, affective, or phenomenological in ways PMS does not capture. PMS does not need to absorb those remainders in order to be useful. Its task is to make one structural projection legible and bounded. If rival readings preserve something PMS cannot, that does not invalidate the PMS reading; it marks its limit.

PMS must not become a closure machine. A closure machine would take every residue and classify it as incomplete integration, insufficient recontextualization, immature praxis, drift, or missing calibration. This case explicitly blocks that move. Resistance, rupture, non-fit, disagreement, absence, and rival framing are not automatically signs of PMS failure or of the case’s immaturity. They may be the very field in which tragedy remains meaningful.

Several dependency shortcuts would be invalid. A Δ/∇ → Ψ shortcut would move from “there is conflict and pressure” directly to “someone is bound or responsible.” A Λ → drift shortcut would move from “resolution is absent” to “integration has failed.” A Θ → terminality shortcut would move from “irreversibility exists” to “no further analysis or action is possible.” An Ω → blame shortcut would move from “asymmetry may exist” to “culpability has been assigned.” A Φ → capture shortcut would move from “the case can be reframed as tragedy” to “PMS has captured tragedy.” A Σ → sufficiency shortcut would move from “the analysis is coherent” to “the analysis is adequate.” Each shortcut is blocked by the completed YAMLs.

The calibration discipline can be stated plainly: tragedy becomes PMS-legible only when the analysis preserves the difference between intelligibility and resolution. PMS may show where closure is demanded, where closure is structurally unavailable, and where residue must remain visible. It may not treat the visibility of residue as its elimination.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS Core YAML treats drift-pattern status with particular caution. The case contains several features that might tempt drift classification: strong awareness of unresolved loss, weak or blocked action, limited integration, and persistent non-resolution. Yet the analysis explicitly refuses to convert those features into findings of drift. This is crucial. A tragic structure may involve blocked repair, unavailable reconciliation, or unresolved responsibility without thereby becoming a maturity failure, calibration failure, or integration failure.

The first relevant drift pattern is **AD_A>>E**, excessive distance between Awareness and Enactment. In a concrete case, this pattern would indicate that awareness, reflection, evaluation, or framing expands while action remains suppressed, chronically delayed, or structurally disconnected. In the present case, however, AD_A>>E is only a risk. The analysis of tragedy could over-expand awareness: it could produce an elaborate category, refine the distinctions, preserve the residue, and endlessly describe non-resolution while never asking what action remains possible. But the case material itself says that no fully resolving action may be available. Therefore absent action cannot automatically be classified as drift. The fact that Action_E is under-specified does not mean action is suppressed by avoidance, immaturity, or over-reflection. It means the case does not supply a concrete action field, and the tragic category may remain meaningful precisely where action cannot close the loss.

The axis effects for AD_A>>E are therefore under-specified. The awareness axis cannot be called over-expanded as a finding because no concrete enactment is present. The action axis cannot be called suppressed or chronically delayed because no actor, option set, or action sequence is provided. The correct status is risk-only. The analysis must guard against a possible over-theorization of tragedy, but it must not label tragedy itself as awareness without action.

The second relevant drift pattern is **AD_Sigma_low**, fragmented or low integration. This pattern is also only a risk. A superficial reading might say: if no full reconciliation is available, then integration must be low. The YAML explicitly blocks this inference. PMS Base allows tragedy under structural maturity. Non-resolution is not automatically fragmentation. A tragic situation may be structurally coherent precisely because it holds together incompatible demands without falsely reconciling them. Conversely, an analysis that forces integration may become less mature, not more.

The axis effects for AD_Sigma_low are again under-specified. The coherence axis is not shown to be volatile, because no concrete trajectory of unstable reframing is provided. The responsibility axis is not shown to be inconsistent, because no concrete responsibility field exists. Tragic non-resolution is therefore not a finding of low coherence or inconsistent responsibility. It is a structural category in which coherence and non-resolution may coexist.

A related coherence risk remains high. PMS can make tragedy look too clean. It can organize loss, guilt, responsibility, non-event, irreversibility, recontextualization, and distance into a stable operator chain. That chain can be useful, but its elegance is dangerous. The smoother the structure appears, the more carefully the analysis must preserve what it cannot claim. If the PMS reading starts to make tragedy feel “resolved as structure,” then it is drifting toward under-discrimination. It would risk treating all tragic residue as merely a named Λ-field inside a completed system.

Under-discrimination is the main cross-cutting risk. If any unresolved loss, conflict, guilt, responsibility, or absence becomes “tragedy,” then the category loses force. The YAML therefore requires discrimination tests. Tragedy should require more than sadness, difficulty, non-closure, ethical disagreement, or practical failure. At minimum, the case requires framed meaningful non-resolution under temporality, with possible asymmetry and irreversibility, and without guaranteed Σ-resolution or Action_E repair. Even that remains a conceptual criterion, not a universal definition.

The central point is that **risk is not finding**. PMS drift language is not a verdict engine. A risk annotation marks where misuse or structural distortion could occur if the analysis were moved into a concrete scene, public context, or decision setting. It does not say that tragedy is drift. It does not say that unresolved responsibility is failure. It does not say that weak action is avoidance. It does not say that non-integration is immaturity.

Likewise, **under-specification is not failure**. In this case, several structures remain weak because the case is abstract. Weak Ω does not mean asymmetry is absent; it means it is not calibrated. Weak Ψ does not mean responsibility is impossible; it means no self-binding agent has been supplied. Under-specified Action_E does not mean action is deficient; it means the material does not provide action data. Non-assignment is not a low score.

This distinction becomes even more important once MIP and AHP enter the stack. MIP explicitly refuses A-score, M-score, IA-box verdict, D-profile, and D-Quick-Grid assignment. AHP later notes that repeated terms such as “weak,” “under-specified,” “not assigned,” and “not applicable” could be misread as quasi-scores. They are not. They are boundary markers. They preserve analytic honesty where the supplied material does not warrant stronger claims.

### Add-on / overlay reading

The relevant add-on for this case is **PMS-CONFLICT**. It is used as an overlay/application profile only. It does not redefine PMS operators, does not alter PMS dependencies, does not create new base operators, and does not rewrite the PMS Core pass. Its reduced signatures are shorthand lenses. Its drift catalogues are risk annotations. Its non-operator constructs are overlay handles, not additions to PMS Base.

PMS Core alone is sufficient to render tragedy structurally legible as framed non-resolution under irreversibility and possible asymmetry. However, PMS Core does not isolate the conflict-specific question: when does unresolved tragedy become legible as stabilized incompatibility under binding, time, cost, exposure, or terminal descriptive positioning? PMS-CONFLICT adds precision at exactly this point. It does not make the case stronger in claim status; it makes a narrower kind of risk and legibility visible.

The overlay frames conflict as stabilized incompatibility under binding, temporality, asymmetry, and costly or asymmetric distance. Applied to “Tragedy as a category,” this means that tragedy may become conflict-legible where meaningful incompatibility persists despite reflective distance, and where integration may not restore shared continuation. But in this case the applicability remains weak and conceptual. The material does not supply concrete bindings, asymmetric exit costs, exposure structures, publicness dynamics, role-specific obligations, or a stabilized conflict attractor.

The overlay therefore strengthens visibility without strengthening the underlying claim status. This is one of the most important results of the CONFLICT pass. It can say that tragedy may be read as conflict-legible as possible non-integrability. It cannot say that the case demonstrates a terminal conflict regime. It can mark binding collision as a risk. It cannot infer colliding commitments. It can mark exposure capture as a transfer risk. It cannot claim public exposure capture inside the case. It can mark dignity collapse as misuse risk. It cannot claim dignity collapse is active.

The overlay’s **CONFLICT terminal core** signature is weak. The terminal core involves Σ under load with Ψ-binding, Ω-asymmetry, Θ-temporality, and priced or asymmetric Χ. In this case, Θ is central, Σ is limited, Ω is under-specified, Ψ is weak, and Χ is analytic distance rather than priced exit. The signature therefore cannot be activated as a full finding. It remains a conceptual lens: tragedy may point toward the kind of regime in which integration no longer carries, but the case does not establish the regime.

The **CONFLICT stabilized pattern** signature is also weak. A stabilized conflict attractor would require recurrent incompatibility under asymmetry, time pressure, binding, and limited or costly distance. The PMS Core case already marked Α as weak. The category of tragedy may stabilize interpretively, but no repeated conflict field is supplied. There is no demonstrated pattern of recurring escalation, repeated non-repair, or stabilized role script. The overlay must therefore not treat the tragic category as a conflict attractor merely because it is meaningful.

The **DRIFT_binding_collision** signature is risk-only. Binding collision would require two or more commitments that cannot be jointly carried under a shared frame and temporal trajectory. Tragedy often evokes such structures: incompatible obligations, responsibility without repair, guilt without reconciliation, duty under loss. But the abstract case does not give concrete commitments. Therefore the overlay may mark binding-collision pressure as plausible, but not active. It may not infer blame, betrayal, culpability, failed self-binding, or moral inadequacy.

The **DRIFT_asymmetry_lock_in** signature is not applicable as a case finding. Asymmetry lock-in would require exit or distance costs that are structurally unequal: one role can withdraw, delay, reframe, or reflect cheaply while another pays a higher price. The case gives no such role structure. Ω remains under-specified. Χ remains analytic distance. Therefore asymmetry lock-in cannot be claimed.

The **DRIFT_exposure_capture** signature is risk-only. Exposure capture occurs when interpretation hardens under publicness or transmission pressure: a scene-bound reading becomes durable record, enforcement affordance, or irreversible pressure. The case itself is private and conceptual. Publicness P is not active. Misuse Gradient MG is low in the completed YAML context. But the terms tragedy, guilt, responsibility, terminality, maturity, and conflict carry high transfer sensitivity. If excerpted into public, institutional, legal, diagnostic, disciplinary, or person-near contexts, the language could harden into verdict. The overlay correctly marks this as a transfer risk, not a case finding.

The **DRIFT_dignity_collapse** signature is likewise risk-only. Dignity collapse would occur if tragedy, guilt, responsibility, or conflict language became shaming, ranking, humiliating, coercive, or person-near. The completed PMS Core and CONFLICT analyses do the opposite: they maintain Χ, reversibility, Dignity-in-Practice, and non-person-ranking. Therefore dignity collapse is not active. It remains a misuse risk if the analysis is detached from its boundary clauses.

Several PMS-CONFLICT non-operator constructs appear but remain carefully bounded. **Conflict attractor** is weak, because no repeatable conflict field is shown. **Binding collision** is weak, because no concrete Ψ-bindings are supplied. **Asymmetry lock-in** is not applicable, because no exit-cost gradient is supplied. **Exposure capture** is not applicable internally but remains a transfer risk. **Dignity collapse** is not active but remains a misuse risk. Each construct is a handle for overlay visibility, not a PMS operator.

The overlay’s terminal positioning is the most delicate part. PMS-CONFLICT can mark a terminal descriptive regime: the point at which conflict remains legible but no further internal reintegration grammar is generated within the CONFLICT pathway. However, terminality is stack-internal and grammar-internal. It is not finality. It is not metaphysical irreparability. It is not legal closure. It is not diagnostic closure. It is not proof that no ethics, politics, care, religion, law, art, or rival theory can continue the analysis. In this case, terminality is weak and conceptual because the case names non-resolution but does not supply a full terminal conflict regime.

The overlay also adds boundary discipline around downstream docking. If a case reaches CONFLICT terminality, continuation into governance, institutional decision, legal procedure, policy, or evaluation is a mode shift. It is not another internal PMS-CONFLICT step. In the present case, downstream docking is not triggered. No governance, institutional, decision, legal, clinical, or implementation use is warranted. Any such move would require its own authority, evidence, procedure, accountability, and boundary conditions.

In short, PMS-CONFLICT adds the following beyond PMS Core: it shows how tragedy may become conflict-legible as possible stabilized incompatibility and possible non-integrability; it names reduced signatures that must remain weak or risk-only; it marks transfer risks around exposure, terminality, and dignity collapse; and it prevents tragedy from being converted into solution procedure or verdict. Its central contribution is not escalation but restraint under sharper conflict visibility.

The overlay also adds several scale and boundary sensitivities that matter even though they do not become active case findings. Θ-history scaling is not applicable because the case gives no concrete regime history, acceleration pattern, or young attractor field. Institutionalized Χ scaling is also not applicable because no institutional distance regime, mandate, or coercive distance structure is supplied. Application-boundary scaling, however, is active as a safety interface: the case remains descriptive, private, reversible, and non-person-near, while any public, institutional, legal, diagnostic, disciplinary, governance, or person-near reuse would require a new mode declaration and misuse-gradient review. This is why the overlay treats publicness and transmission not as ordinary afterthoughts but as structural risk channels. A phrase that is safe inside a bounded conceptual artefact may become unsafe if it travels without the boundary clauses that make it reversible.

### Layer interaction

The PMS Core reading remains the base. It supplies the operator grammar, dependency order, derived-structure discipline, and claim boundary. The CONFLICT overlay does not replace this grammar. It only sharpens a specific family of questions: incompatibility, binding collision, asymmetry lock-in, exposure capture, dignity collapse, terminal descriptive positioning, publicness, and misuse gradient.

What remains PMS Core is the main structural formula: framed difference, weak directedness, meaningful non-event, weak category-level attractor, under-specified asymmetry, irreversible temporality, bounded recontextualization, and reflective distance. PMS Core also preserves the derived-structure statuses: Awareness_A weak-to-active, Coherence_C active but limited, Responsibility_R weak, Action_E under-specified, Dignity-in-Practice application-only, SELF_FIXPOINT unavailable.

What the add-on sharpens is the conflict-specific underside of non-resolution. PMS Core says: tragedy involves meaningful non-resolution under irreversibility and possible asymmetry. PMS-CONFLICT asks: does this non-resolution become stabilized incompatibility under binding, time, cost, and exposure? In this case, the answer is: only weakly and conceptually. That answer is useful because it prevents both overclaim and under-reading. The overlay makes the conflict dimension visible without claiming that the case demonstrates a full conflict regime.

The add-on must not redefine any PMS operator. PMS-CONFLICT reduced signatures may mention Σ, Ψ, Ω, Θ, and Χ in compact forms, but those are shorthand. They do not change the dependency requirements of PMS. They do not allow Χ without its PMS prerequisites. They do not allow Ψ without Σ, Θ, and Χ. They do not allow terminality to bypass PMS’s non-capture and reversibility discipline. They do not turn CONFLICT constructs into operators.

Overlay language could overreach in several ways. “Terminal” could sound like finality. “Conflict attractor” could sound like a finding of entrenched conflict. “Binding collision” could sound like guilt or betrayal. “Exposure capture” could sound like public harm already occurred. “Dignity collapse” could sound like a dignity evaluation. Each of these is blocked in the completed YAML. Their status is weak, risk-only, transfer-risk, or not applicable. The article must preserve those statuses.

MIP adds a different kind of layer interaction. It does not intensify the case analysis. It reviews the analysis artefact as a maturity-in-practice working note. The MIP pass asks whether the structured analysis handles claim type, publicness, scoring, IA, D, role mapping, bias, transmission, and guardrails responsibly. Because the case is abstract, MIP refuses A-score, M-score, IA verdict, D-profile, and D-Quick-Grid. It treats the output as a private working note only. This confirms the scope discipline rather than adding new case findings.

The MIP pass is therefore not a new case judgment. It reviews the prior PMS Core and PMS-CONFLICT analyses as a structured maturity-in-practice artefact. Its role is downstream and guardrail-oriented: to determine whether the analysis is being handled with sufficient role clarity, score hygiene, dignity protection, publicness awareness, transmission discipline, and reversibility.

The MIP case type remains conceptual. The object of observation is not a person, institution, legal dispute, conflict scene, therapy context, or HR case. It is the analysis artefact around the category “tragedy.” The observer position is an external structural analyst using MIP as a case template and application lens. This matters because MIP can easily be misused if a formal template is mistaken for a person-evaluation framework. The completed MIP YAML repeatedly blocks that misuse.

The MIP pass marks the application zone as cautionary structural reflection, not a decision basis. The intended use is structural case review and conceptual clarification only. D activation is kept off as a direct module; dignity guardrails are used as protection constraints only. The primary material is the completed PMS Core and PMS-CONFLICT analyses. The secondary material is the MIP YAML itself. There is no empirical evidence, concrete scene record, witness material, clinical material, legal record, or implementation data. This evidence boundary is crucial. The MIP output can be complete as a template filling while still having low evidence coverage for concrete enactments. That is not a contradiction. It is the difference between formal coverage and evidentiary coverage.

The A–C–R–P profile is therefore structural but not scoreable. Awareness is strong as analytic boundary awareness: the analysis knows its case type, limits, publicness risks, and non-capture zones. Coherence is strong as a layered artefact: PMS Core, CONFLICT, and MIP align without silently escalating claims. Responsibility is analytic only: the analysis is responsible for preserving guardrails, not for assigning responsibility to a case actor. Power-to-act is limited to wording, transmission hygiene, no-score discipline, and boundary preservation. There is no practical action field.

A-score is not assigned. This does not mean a low A-score. It means the object is not an enactment. MIP requires role, time, context, observable behaviour, and evidence to assign a band. None is supplied. The previous MIP output correctly says that structural awareness is visible but not scoreable.

M-score is not assigned. Again, this is not a low M-band. M-score describes shared structural responsibility, not guilt. It requires a role-holder, knowledge, predictability, access to alternatives, power/control, and serious integration attempt. No actor or responsibility field is supplied. The ceiling rule is not triggered because there is no M assignment at all. In a future concrete case, absent predictability or absent real alternatives would cap responsibility, but that is not the present case.

The IA-box is not applied as a verdict. Possible asymmetry is named, but the IA-box requires transparency, justification, time-boundedness, and reversibility in a concrete asymmetry field. Here, no asymmetry is imposed. The analysis itself remains reversible, private, non-scoring, and non-decisional. Therefore IA is a guardrail check only.

The D-module remains off. D0 is untouched. D1 and D2 are not profiled. D-R and D-P are not activated as dignity readings. Dignity appears only as protection: no shaming, no ranking, no humiliation, no public D-reading of identifiable persons, no dignity score, no person labels. This is a significant discipline point because tragedy-language can easily become dignity-adjacent. The MIP output prevents this by refusing D-profile activation.

The MIP trajectory is a documentation trajectory, not a maturity trajectory of a person. At t0, PMS Core established conceptual structure and boundary conditions. At t1, PMS-CONFLICT added weak conflict-legibility and risk-only drift signatures. MIP then reviews the artefact as a working note. This trajectory shows increasing structural legibility, not increasing maturity of an actor.

MIP also names bias risks: coherence inflation, hindsight inflation, moral-tribunal drift, non-capture erasure, and terminality-finality confusion. These are directly relevant to tragedy. Coherence inflation would make the layered YAML seem like proof. Hindsight inflation would treat irreversibility as responsibility evidence. Moral-tribunal drift would convert guilt-language into blame. Non-capture erasure would absorb rival readings into PMS/MIP. Terminality-finality confusion would turn CONFLICT’s weak terminality marker into a final claim.

The transmission check is conservative. The status remains working note. Allowed uses are internal reflection, structural case review, conceptual boundary check, and private YAML integration. Not allowed uses include person label, public shaming, individual sanction, HR decision, forensic use, clinical diagnosis, legal or liability assessment, public D-reading, PMS Base validation, or solution procedure. Review is required before any public, institutional, legal, clinical, HR, governance, disciplinary, pedagogical, or concrete-scene use.

MIP therefore adds one main result: the article and YAML stack are structurally mature as analysis practice only insofar as they withhold scoring, verdicts, D activation, and operational claims. Their maturity lies in restraint, not in stronger conclusion.

AHP then adds second-order hardening. It does not re-analyze tragedy. It analyzes the MIP output as an artefact. It identifies attack surfaces: public excerpting risk, term ambiguity, formal-completeness/evidence-completeness confusion, need for concrete-scene transition gates, untested rival readings, score-hygiene risk, and D-profile boundary risk. These are not defects in persons or in tragedy. They are vulnerabilities in the analysis artefact if reused, excerpted, or moved into applied settings.

The AHP pass assigns a high Precision Heuristic: 6/6. This is not an A-score, not an M-score, not a D-score, and not an IA verdict. It is a precision heuristic for the analysis artefact. The six satisfied dimensions are term definition, scope boundaries, conditions and thresholds, evidence linking, reversibility clause, and language hygiene. The previous MIP output is therefore judged high precision as a private working note.

The AHP overlay effect increases confidence in the A-reading and C-reading, but only as artefact quality. It makes IA-box non-assignment more decidable: there is enough information to decide that no IA verdict should be assigned. It reduces D-R/D-P misreading risk only under the condition that language hygiene and scope boundaries remain intact. This correction matters: the risk goes down if those conditions remain true. It does not disappear.

The previous MIP output is safe as a private working note, but terms such as tragedy, guilt, responsibility, maturity, terminality, conflict, non-integrability, and dignity can harden under public excerpting. Detached from boundary clauses, they could be misread as score, diagnosis, responsibility finding, D-profile, IA verdict, legal claim, or model validation. A second attack point concerns term hardening. The terms are clear in the full YAML, but not necessarily standalone-safe. “Tragedy,” “terminality,” “maturity-in-practice,” “non-integrability,” and “responsibility-pressure” could be over-read as metaphysical, moral, diagnostic, legal, or final if removed from context. AHP recommends a compact local glossary or boundary sentence.

A third attack point concerns formal completeness. The MIP template is fully populated, and that can create a false impression that the underlying case is empirically specified. AHP correctly identifies this as an artefact risk. Template coverage records non-assignment and guardrail discipline, not observed enactment evidence. This distinction should travel with the document.

A fourth attack point concerns the transition from conceptual category analysis to concrete scene analysis. The MIP output says a new run is required if a concrete tragedy case is supplied, but AHP recommends a more explicit if/then gate: if concrete actors, roles, harms, claims, publicness, or decisions are introduced, then restart MIP with fresh role mapping, evidence separation, publicness classification, IA-box review, D-module decision, A/M score hygiene, and transmission review.

A fifth attack point concerns rival readings. The previous MIP output preserves rival readings but does not comparatively test them. This is acceptable for a working note, but it becomes an attack surface if someone later implies PMS/MIP superiority. AHP recommends explicitly stating that rival-reading preservation is not comparative validation.

The sixth attack point concerns score hygiene. Repeated terms such as “not assigned,” “weak,” “under-specified,” and “not applicable” could be mistaken for low scores or negative evaluations. AHP recommends a local note: these are non-score states, not deficits.

The seventh attack point concerns D-language. Even though the D-module remains off, the analysis discusses D0, D1/D2, D-R, D-P, and dignity protection. A reader could mistake this for a soft D-profile. AHP recommends a local boundary: D-language here names guardrail risk only; it is not D-module activation, not D-profile creation, and not D-Quick-Grid use.

AHP’s hardening backlog follows from these attack points. It does not change the base results. It does not rescore. It does not activate D. It does not upgrade transmission status. It does not make the analysis usable as public report or decision basis. It only prepares the next iteration to be safer, clearer, and less attackable.

The full stack therefore functions as layered restraint. PMS Core gives structural legibility. PMS-CONFLICT sharpens conflict-specific legibility while keeping findings weak. MIP checks that the analysis is not overused as score, decision, diagnosis, or public judgment. AHP hardens the artefact against misuse in transmission. The layers do not accumulate authority; they accumulate boundary discipline.

Non-closure remains visible at every layer. PMS Core preserves Λ. PMS-CONFLICT preserves terminality without finality. MIP preserves non-assignment. AHP preserves attack surfaces and hardening backlog. None of the layers eliminates the tragic remainder.

### Example 1 — The apology that cannot restore

**Type:** micro
**Recommended insertion point:** after “PMS Core structural reading,” especially after the discussion of Λ and Θ.

A person breaks an object that had significance for another person: not because of malice, not because of a dramatic betrayal, but through an ordinary failure of attention. The object can be replaced materially, and an apology can be offered sincerely. Yet the original object, with its history and singular relation to the other person, cannot be restored. The apology matters. The replacement may matter. A new practice of care may matter. But none of these actions makes the loss disappear as loss.

The example demonstrates why tragedy cannot be reduced to the absence of action. There is action: apology, repair attempt, changed conduct. But the action does not fully resolve the structure. The tragic element lies in the persistence of meaningful non-restoration. The case is not tragic because no one does anything; it is tragic because what can be done does not undo what cannot be undone.

At the same time, the vignette must not be overread. Not every broken object is tragic, and not every irreversible loss warrants the tragic category. The object becomes structurally relevant only because it is framed by a relation, a history, and an expectation that cannot be reproduced by replacement. The example therefore illustrates the minimum PMS pressure: Δ distinguishes replacement from restoration, □ makes the distinction matter, Λ marks the unavailable return of the original, and Θ preserves the irreversible residue. Without that framed residue, the example would be only an ordinary accident.

The PMS Core hooks are clear. **Δ** appears in the distinction between material replacement and historical restoration. **∇** appears as the pressure to repair. **□** is the relational frame in which the object matters. **Λ** is the missing event: the original cannot return. **Θ** marks irreversibility. **Φ** may recontextualize the event as accident, negligence, apology, or learning, but no recontextualization fully restores the original relation. **Χ** is required so the analysis does not rush into blame, exoneration, or sentimental closure. **Σ** remains limited: a coherent account can be formed, but coherence is not restoration. **Ψ** would require further concrete commitments and cannot be inferred from the vignette alone.

The CONFLICT overlay is only weakly relevant. There may be conflict, but no stabilized conflict attractor, binding collision, or asymmetry lock-in is established. MIP would not assign scores from this vignette; AHP would warn that terms such as “responsibility” or “irreversibility” must not be excerpted into blame.

Forbidden claims are therefore central. The example does not establish legal liability, moral guilt, psychological defect, or metaphysical tragedy. It does not rank either person. It does not show that apology is useless. It shows only that meaningful repair and irreversible residue can coexist.

The non-capture note is that lived grief over the object may exceed PMS rendering. The article takeaway is simple: tragedy becomes visible where repair remains meaningful but not total.

### Example 2 — The two duties that cannot both be carried

**Type:** compact
**Recommended insertion point:** after the PMS-CONFLICT overlay section, especially where binding collision is discussed as risk rather than finding.

A caregiver has promised to remain present for an aging parent during a difficult medical transition. At the same time, the caregiver has a professional obligation to appear for a critical responsibility that cannot easily be transferred. Both obligations are real within their frames. Neither is trivial. The caregiver does not face a simple preference conflict between comfort and inconvenience, nor a clear moral hierarchy where one duty obviously cancels the other. Whichever path is taken, something remains uncarried.

This vignette demonstrates the kind of structure that can make tragedy conflict-legible without turning the example into a verdict. There is loss even if the caregiver acts with seriousness. There may be guilt even if no malicious intention exists. There may be responsibility even if full satisfaction of all obligations is impossible. The point is not to decide what the caregiver should do, but to show how tragedy can emerge where multiple meaningful bindings cannot be jointly integrated without remainder.

PMS Core hooks begin with **Δ**, the distinction between the two obligations and their incompatible demands. **∇** appears as the pressure to decide and to restore coherence. **□** matters because each duty belongs to a different frame: care, family, profession, institutional reliability. **Λ** appears as the missing event of full joint fulfilment. **Α** could become relevant if such conflicts recur, but it is not established from one vignette. **Ω** is possible but must be calibrated: who has flexibility, who bears cost, who can forgive, who cannot? **Θ** is active because the obligations unfold over time and because some missed presence cannot be recovered later. **Φ** may reframe the situation as sacrifice, failure, necessity, or misaligned systems. **Χ** is crucial because the analysis must not become a tribunal. **Σ** is limited: the caregiver may integrate reasons enough to act, but not enough to erase remainder. **Ψ** is possible because promises and role commitments are involved, but a full self-binding analysis would require a richer scene.

PMS-CONFLICT adds a useful but bounded lens. This could be read as a possible **binding collision**, but only as an illustrative structure. It is not a finding of drift, immaturity, betrayal, or guilt. The vignette does not yet show whether the obligations are truly non-integrable, whether one frame has priority, whether others could share the burden, whether institutional design produced the conflict, or whether a later action could partially reopen the field. The **CONFLICT terminal core** therefore remains unavailable unless the scene shows sustained incompatibility under asymmetry, time, binding pressure, and priced or asymmetric distance. MIP would require concrete evidence before any A/M/IA/D analysis. AHP would mark public retelling as risky if the caregiver were identifiable, because a compact illustration of tragic pressure could quickly harden into a judgment about loyalty, failure, or responsibility.

Forbidden claims are explicit: the example does not say which duty is higher, who is blameworthy, whether the caregiver acted maturely, or whether the conflict is legally relevant. The non-capture note is that ethical theory may preserve normative features PMS does not decide. The article takeaway is that tragedy can arise where responsibility is not absent, yet no available integration fully carries every binding.

### Example 3 — The public sentence that hardens too quickly

**Type:** compact
**Recommended insertion point:** after the misuse boundary section or in the later transition toward the article conclusion.

A short passage from a structural analysis says: “the situation is tragically legible as possible non-integrability.” Within the full article, the sentence is careful. It means that, under the PMS-CONFLICT overlay, a configuration may become understandable as a case where integration does not fully restore shared continuation. It is not a verdict. It is not a diagnosis. It is not a claim that repair is impossible. It is not a judgment about persons.

Now imagine the sentence copied into a public discussion without its surrounding boundary clauses. The phrase “tragically legible” is read as “the people involved are trapped.” “Possible non-integrability” is read as “nothing more can be done.” A weak conceptual marker becomes a public conclusion. A guardrail-bound phrase becomes an authority-sounding label. The analysis has not changed internally, but its transmission context has changed its risk.

This example demonstrates why MIP and AHP were included in the stack. PMS Core can mark **Λ** and **Θ**; PMS-CONFLICT can mark weak terminality or non-integrability; but MIP and AHP are needed to protect the analysis artefact from misuse in transmission. The vignette is not about the original tragic situation. It is about the analysis itself becoming risky when excerpted.

The PMS hooks are indirect but important. **□** shifts: a private conceptual working note becomes public discourse. **Ω** shifts: the person quoting the analysis may gain interpretive authority over those described. **Θ** shifts: a sentence can persist as record. **Φ** shifts: structural language becomes social judgment. **Χ** may fail if the excerpt no longer carries distance, reversibility, and boundary discipline. **Σ** is falsely overread as completed interpretation. **Ψ** may appear if the label hardens into an institutional or reputational commitment.

The CONFLICT overlay marks this as **exposure capture risk**, not exposure capture finding. MIP would keep the artefact in working-note status unless publicness and misuse-gradient review were performed. AHP directly identifies this kind of public excerpting as a high-severity attack surface and recommends an excerpt boundary: the passage is not a score, diagnosis, responsibility finding, D-profile, IA verdict, legal claim, implementation claim, or PMS/MIP validation.

Forbidden claims are the lesson. The example does not show that public discussion is always invalid, that tragedy-language must never be used, or that structural analysis should remain private forever. It shows only that transmission changes risk. The non-capture note is that public language has rhetorical and social effects no structural formula fully controls. The article takeaway is that tragedy-language must travel with its guardrails, or it can become the closure-by-authority the analysis was designed to prevent.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The YAML stack preserves several rival readings. These are not defects. They mark domains where PMS, PMS-CONFLICT, MIP, or AHP may not fully capture what tragedy means.

An **aesthetic or genre reading** treats tragedy through dramatic form, plot, reversal, recognition, catharsis, spectatorship, tone, and literary history. PMS can model some structural features of non-resolution, but it does not supply a theory of dramatic form. It does not explain why tragedy as art moves an audience, how genre conventions operate, or how performance transforms suffering into aesthetic experience. This reading preserves the artistic specificity of tragedy.

An **ethical conflict reading** treats tragedy as collision among incompatible but partially legitimate obligations. PMS-CONFLICT can approach this through binding collision, but the current case does not supply concrete bindings. Ethical theory may preserve normative content that PMS deliberately does not claim: the sense that each side may have a real claim, that guilt may be unavoidable, or that responsibility may persist even without fault. PMS can map the structure; it does not settle the ethics.

A **metaphysical reading** treats tragedy as finitude, fate, mortality, absurdity, contradiction in being, or the structure of existence. PMS does not make such claims. It can show how irreversibility and non-event operate within a frame, but it does not prove that existence is tragic. This reading preserves ontological depth that PMS leaves outside.

A **religious or theological reading** may treat tragedy as sin, sacrifice, providence, fallenness, redemption, divine silence, or sacred loss. PMS has no authority to absorb these readings into operator grammar. Λ may structurally resemble divine absence or unanswered prayer, but such resemblance is not capture. The theological frame has its own commitments.

A **phenomenological reading** treats tragedy as lived experience: grief, guilt, dread, rupture, mourning, recognition, or the felt impossibility of repair. PMS explicitly does not claim phenomenological consciousness. Awareness_A is structural, not experiential. Therefore phenomenology preserves first-person or lived dimensions the PMS case does not reach.

A **language-practice reading** may ask how the word “tragedy” is used in ordinary speech, ritual, media, politics, literature, or law. PMS can model framing and drift, but it does not replace ordinary-language analysis. Such a reading may show how tragedy-language itself changes across contexts and communities.

A **legal-moral reading** may focus on culpability, fault, liability, negligence, mitigation, and accountability. PMS blocks legal overclaim in the present case, but a legal theory may legitimately analyze responsibility where concrete evidence and jurisdiction exist. PMS does not deny this; it simply does not perform it here.

An **anti-capture reading** may say that tragedy is precisely what resists formalization. This is not automatically a refutation of PMS. It is a rival stance that PMS must respect. PMS may still offer a bounded structural projection, but it cannot use that projection to prove that anti-capture is wrong. The tragic remainder may include resistance to being fully rendered as a case artefact.

The non-capture zones are therefore substantial. Tragedy may exceed PMS as aesthetic form, metaphysical condition, religious category, existential problem, affective experience, historical catastrophe, ethical remainder, or language-practice phenomenon. PMS-CONFLICT may sharpen conflict-legibility, but it does not absorb all tragedy into conflict. MIP may harden guardrails, but it does not evaluate the tragic situation. AHP may harden the analysis, but it does not resolve the philosophical category.

The three examples reinforce this non-capture discipline. The broken object example shows that lived grief may exceed structural repair. The incompatible duties example shows that ethical theory may preserve normative force PMS does not settle. The public sentence example shows that language has social and rhetorical effects no formula fully controls. Each example demonstrates the analysis without replacing it.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS reading would weaken if no structured expectation exists. Λ requires an expected event whose absence matters within a frame. If a case merely contains sadness, difficulty, or lack without an expected repair, reconciliation, interpretation, recognition, answer, or action, then tragedy as PMS-structured non-event is not warranted.

The reading would also weaken if no temporal irreversibility or persistence exists. Θ is central to this case. If the situation is fully reversible, without enduring consequence, without non-repair, and without temporal residue, then the tragic category may be overextended. Not every inconvenience, delay, or conflict is tragedy.

The reading would weaken if no asymmetry of exposure, obligation, vulnerability, power, or responsibility can be identified in a concrete case. In the abstract case, Ω is under-specified but relevant. In a concrete tragedy claim, stronger Ω would require calibration. If no asymmetry appears, then responsibility and guilt language should be downgraded.

The reading would weaken if the situation is fully resolvable without remainder. If action, interpretation, reconciliation, repair, or restitution can close the relevant field without persistent residue, then the tragic category may not be structurally needed. This does not mean the event is unimportant. It means tragedy may be too strong a category.

The reading would fail if PMS analysis treats non-fit or resistance as immaturity by default. That would violate the case’s own guardrails. Tragic non-resolution must not be automatically converted into drift, failed integration, or incomplete maturity.

The CONFLICT overlay would weaken if no stabilized incompatibility appears. The abstract case suggests possible incompatibility, but a full CONFLICT regime would require concrete bindings, role structures, repeated incompatibility, asymmetry, temporal load, and costly or asymmetric distance. Without those, terminal conflict language must remain weak.

The CONFLICT overlay would overreach if terminality is treated as finality. Terminality is a grammar-internal boundary, not proof that no further analysis, care, politics, ethics, religion, law, or action can continue. If terminality is used to stop appeal, repair, or attention, the overlay becomes misuse.

The MIP layer would become invalid if it assigned A-score, M-score, IA verdict, D-profile, or D-Quick-Grid level from the abstract case. It would also become invalid if it treated working-note completeness as evidence completeness, or if it moved into decision-support use without concrete data.

The AHP layer would become invalid if its attack points were read as first-order findings about persons or tragedy. AHP hardens the analysis artefact only. It does not rescore, diagnose, assign responsibility, activate D, or upgrade transmission.

The reading becomes under-discriminating if every unresolved conflict or loss is called tragic. It becomes over-assimilative if every rival reading is treated as merely incomplete PMS. It becomes too smooth if the PMS formula is used to dissolve the very non-resolution it should preserve. It becomes too authoritative if the YAML form is treated as proof.

Stronger claims would require evidence and scene data: concrete actors, roles, losses, obligations, temporal sequence, available and unavailable actions, repair attempts, asymmetry gradients, publicness conditions, responsibility claims, institutional context, and rival-model comparison. Comparative validation would require explicit criteria for why PMS, PMS-CONFLICT, MIP, or AHP outperforms rival theories. Empirical validation would require cases and method. Legal validation would require legal procedure. Clinical validation would require clinical context and qualified standards. None of these is supplied.

Operationalization remains unfulfilled. The current article is a human-readable rendering of completed YAML analyses. It is not a deployment specification, governance instrument, diagnostic protocol, legal memo, clinical report, or philosophical proof. Its valid use is conceptual cartography and boundary discipline.

The examples do not change this. The apology example does not become empirical evidence for a theory of tragedy. The two duties example does not become a moral decision procedure. The public sentence example does not become proof that public use is always invalid. They are illustrative probes. They show how the structures may become readable, and they show why the boundaries must travel with that readability.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It does not identify pathology, trauma, avoidance, personality, maturity level, clinical risk, or mental state. Tragedy-language is not a clinical instrument.

It must not be used for person ranking. No person, group, role, or institution is evaluated. Dignity-in-Practice is not a dignity score. Weak, under-specified, risk-only, and not-applicable fields are not negative ratings.

It must not be used for legal or forensic conclusions. Guilt and responsibility remain structural terms in the abstract case. They are not findings of culpability, liability, negligence, blameworthiness, or fault.

It must not be used as metaphysical proof. PMS does not prove that tragedy is fate, finitude, being, mortality, evil, absurdity, or ontological contradiction. Those remain rival or external readings.

It must not be used as empirical proof. No evidence is supplied. No population, event, dataset, or observation is analyzed.

It must not be used as implementation validation. YAML representation is not deployment. Reduced signatures are not executable modules. MIP and AHP are not software safety proofs.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. A case application is not proof of model completeness, uniqueness, correctness, or superiority.

It must not treat add-ons as operators. PMS-CONFLICT introduces overlay handles, reduced signatures, drift risks, publicness, misuse gradient, and terminal-positioning language. None of these redefine Δ–Ψ.

It must not treat drift as verdict. AD_A>>E, AD_Sigma_low, binding collision, exposure capture, and dignity collapse are risks or non-applicable constructs in this case. They are not findings.

It must not produce closure by authority. The fact that PMS, CONFLICT, MIP, and AHP align around a disciplined analysis does not settle tragedy. The stack improves legibility and guardrails; it does not eliminate Λ.

The strongest misuse risk is public excerpting. If phrases such as “terminality,” “non-integrability,” “guilt,” “responsibility,” “maturity,” or “dignity collapse” are detached from their boundary clauses, they may harden into public judgment. Any excerpt should carry the AHP boundary: this is not a score, diagnosis, responsibility finding, D-profile, IA verdict, legal claim, implementation claim, or PMS/MIP validation.

The third example makes this boundary concrete. The sentence “tragically legible as possible non-integrability” is careful inside the article because it is surrounded by weak status, overlay discipline, and non-capture. Outside the article, it could become a public label. This is the misuse boundary in miniature: structurally precise language can become structurally unsafe when transmission strips its constraints.

The correct use of this case is therefore narrow but meaningful. It may be used to understand how tragedy can remain structurally legible without being resolved. It may be used to test PMS dependency discipline under a strong closure-demand. It may be used to show how an overlay can sharpen visibility without becoming base theory. It may be used to demonstrate why MIP and AHP guardrails matter. It may not be used to decide who is guilty, who is mature, who is dignified, what metaphysically follows, or what must be done.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result is that “Tragedy as a category” can be rendered through the PMS stack as meaningful non-resolution under framed difference, irreversible temporality, possible but under-specified asymmetry, bounded recontextualization, and reflective distance. PMS Core makes the structural grammar visible: Δ distinguishes the relevant losses and conflicts; ∇ marks weak closure pressure; □ frames the tragic category; Λ identifies meaningful non-event; Α remains weak as category-level stabilization; Ω is relevant but under-specified; Θ is central through irreversibility; Φ reframes without curing; Χ protects distance; Σ remains limited; Ψ remains conditional. The resulting structure is coherent, but coherence is not proof and not reconciliation.

PMS-CONFLICT adds a sharper conflict lens without changing the base reading. It shows that tragedy may become conflict-legible as possible stabilized incompatibility or possible non-integrability. Yet the overlay remains weak and conceptual because the case supplies no concrete bindings, no calibrated asymmetry gradient, no priced or asymmetric distance, no exposure field, and no stabilized conflict attractor. Binding collision, exposure capture, and dignity collapse are risks or transfer risks, not findings. Terminality remains grammar-internal and weak; it is not finality.

MIP and AHP contribute a second kind of result. They do not deepen the case claim; they discipline the analysis artefact. MIP refuses A-score, M-score, IA verdict, D-profile, and D-Quick-Grid assignment. Its point is not to evaluate tragedy but to keep the working note from becoming a decision instrument. AHP then hardens the artefact against public excerpting, term ambiguity, false evidence-completeness, concrete-scene transition errors, rival-reading erasure, score-hygiene confusion, and D-profile boundary drift. Together, these layers show that the strength of the stack lies in restraint, not escalation.

The examples contribute three controlled demonstrations. The apology that cannot restore shows how repair may remain meaningful without becoming total. The two duties that cannot both be carried shows how responsibility can be present without yielding a clean integration or blame verdict. The public sentence that hardens too quickly shows why the analysis itself needs transmission guardrails. None of the examples functions as evidence for PMS, proof of tragedy, or a concrete verdict. They are bounded illustrations of the structural claims and their misuse risks.

What remains unclaimed is as important as what is claimed. The article does not prove what tragedy essentially is. It does not claim that PMS captures tragedy exhaustively. It does not decide among aesthetic, ethical, metaphysical, theological, phenomenological, legal, language-practice, or anti-capture readings. It does not establish empirical truth, clinical relevance, legal responsibility, implementation validity, or PMS Base validation. It does not assign maturity, guilt, dignity, or responsibility to anyone. It does not treat unresolved residue as drift.

The boundary discipline is therefore the central methodological achievement. PMS can map a closure-demand without satisfying it falsely. PMS-CONFLICT can sharpen non-integrability without declaring terminal finality. MIP can review the artefact without scoring the case. AHP can identify vulnerabilities without changing the base result. Risk remains risk. Weak status remains weak status. Non-assignment is not a low score. Non-capture is not failure. Resistance, rupture, non-fit, disagreement, absence, and rival framing are not automatically drift.

The final methodological takeaway is that tragedy is a decisive test of whether a structural grammar can remain non-totalizing. A weak reading would either dissolve tragedy into closure or elevate it into metaphysical authority. The disciplined AXIOM reading does neither. It shows where tragedy becomes legible, where integration must not be presumed, where add-on language must remain subordinate, where transmission becomes risky, and where the remainder must stay visible. The case therefore does not end by solving tragedy. It ends by preserving the exact place where solution, verdict, or final integration would become overclaim.
