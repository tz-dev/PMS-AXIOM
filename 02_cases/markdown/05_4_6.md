## Hermeneutic circle

**Case file(s):** `PMS_CORE_05_4_6.yaml`, `PMS_LOGIC_05_4_6.yaml`

**Stack:** PMS Core + PMS-LOGIC overlay.

**Case type:** philosophical conceptual domain projection about part-whole interpretation.

**Output type:** boundary / conceptual structural reading / overlay boundary-management trace.

### Why this case matters

The hermeneutic circle is a classical philosophical closure-demand because it asks whether understanding can ever begin without already having what it seeks. A part appears intelligible only through the whole in which it belongs; the whole appears intelligible only through the parts that compose, disclose, or revise it. The problem therefore does not merely ask for a definition of interpretation. It asks whether interpretation is trapped in a vicious circle, whether the circle is a productive recursive structure, or whether understanding depends on revisable fore-structures, contexts, horizons, and frames that are neither arbitrary nor finally closed.

This makes the case structurally difficult in a way that is especially relevant to AXIOM. It is not simply a problem of ignorance: the interpreter lacks the whole, but the lack is structured. It is not simply a problem of method: the method may itself depend on a prior understanding of what counts as a relevant part or an admissible whole. It is not simply a problem of logic: the circle may resemble invalid circular justification, but hermeneutic traditions often treat circularity as the condition of understanding rather than as an error to be eliminated. It is not simply a problem of coherence: an interpretation can become internally coherent while still being assimilative, reductive, historically blind, or unable to answer rival accounts.

The case matters because it tests whether an analysis can preserve circularity without immediately moralizing, psychologizing, formalizing, or metaphysically resolving it. In many philosophical contexts, the pressure is to determine whether the circle is “really” vicious or “really” productive. AXIOM does not take that route. It instead asks what kind of closure is being demanded, what frame is required for that demand to make sense, what residue remains after structural analysis, and what kind of output can legitimately follow. In this case, the output is not a solution to hermeneutics. It is a boundary-conscious structural rendering of the part-whole recursion and of the conditions under which non-closure remains visible.

The closure-demand is unusually exposed. If one insists that every interpretive movement must terminate in a fully justified whole, the case can appear vicious. If one insists that all circularity is productive recursion, the case can become too smooth and lose the ability to distinguish re-reading from frame-locking. If one treats the whole as already present in a tradition, horizon, language, or historical field, one risks importing authority into the analysis. If one treats every failure of fit as merely incomplete integration, one absorbs non-capture instead of preserving it.

The case belongs in AXIOM because AXIOM is concerned with mapping closure-demands without solving them by authority. The hermeneutic circle is almost a stress test for that orientation. It asks whether a framework can describe a closure-demand while leaving the residue visible. PMS Core can map the part-whole structure through Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, and conditional Σ / Ψ. PMS-LOGIC is justified because the case does not merely contain recursion; it contains closure pressure around recursion. The unresolved whole tempts the analysis either to declare failure, to force integration, or to treat non-closure as a hidden metaphysical pointer. LOGIC sharpens the boundary at which ordering remains useful but authority-smuggling begins. The analysis is therefore not an attempt to end the hermeneutic problem. It is an attempt to render the structure of the problem legible without collapsing it into closure.

The case is not being solved metaphysically because the material does not supply a theory of being, meaning, subjectivity, language, or historical existence. It is not being solved empirically because no observations, corpus, interpretive practice, experiment, or comparative dataset is supplied. It is not clinical or diagnostic because no person or psychological state is being assessed. It is not legal or forensic because no evidence standard, culpability question, liability frame, or institutional decision is present. It is not an implementation case because no algorithm, agent architecture, software protocol, or operational interpreter is specified. Finally, it is not PMS Base validation: a successful PMS rendering of one philosophical problem does not validate PMS as a base model, prove completeness, or defeat rival grammars.

The case is therefore valuable precisely because it resists cheap closure. It allows AXIOM to show what its case artefacts are for: not to replace philosophical work with a verdict, but to make the structure of the closure-demand explicit, to distinguish what can be claimed from what cannot, and to leave Λ-residue visible where final settlement would require more than the case supplies.

### Scene and frame structure

The conceptual scene is the structure of interpretive dependence itself. A part is not fully intelligible without some whole: a sentence without a text, an action without a situation, a fragment without a tradition, a sign without a field of relevance. At the same time, the whole is not available independently of parts: a text is approached through passages, a situation through details, a tradition through instances, a horizon through what becomes salient inside it. The scene is therefore not an event but a relation of mutual interpretive dependence.

The frame □ is the declared philosophical problem-frame: understanding under part-whole dependence. Inside the frame are the part, the whole, the interpretive horizon, the fore-structure, the context, and the demand to determine whether circularity is vicious, productive, or structurally recursive. The frame also includes the possibility that interpretation proceeds through revisable movement rather than through immediate possession of the whole.

Outside the frame are concrete empirical interpretive practices, legal interpretation, clinical interpretation, historical claims about particular hermeneutic traditions, and metaphysical claims about the ultimate nature of meaning. These may become relevant in other cases, but they are not supplied here. The frame also excludes the use of PMS to decide that a rival reading is immature merely because it does not accept PMS vocabulary.

The protected constraints are reversibility, non-capture, non-diagnosis, and no closure by authority. The analysis must preserve the possibility that the circle is vicious in some formal contexts, productive in some hermeneutic contexts, and under-described in others. It must not convert “PMS can map this” into “PMS has solved this.” It must not convert “Λ persists” into “the case fails.” It must not convert “Σ is possible” into “integration has been achieved.”

Roles are not concretely present. There is no interpreter-role with responsibility, no institution enforcing a reading, no affected party, no decision-maker, and no authority. At most, the article may speak abstractly of an interpreter, a method, a tradition, or an analysis, but these are conceptual positions, not scene actors. Because roles are absent, responsibility and post-moral effects remain weak.

Temporality Θ is central even without a concrete scene. The circle is not simply a static paradox. Understanding unfolds through return, anticipation, revision, and delayed stabilization. A part is first understood under an initial whole; the whole is revised through attention to parts; the part is then re-read through the revised whole. This temporal movement is what makes the case more than a bare logical loop. However, because no empirical sequence is supplied, the temporal reading remains conceptual rather than observed.

The reversibility window remains open throughout. No final interpretation is bound by Ψ. No conclusion becomes irreversible. No integration is treated as settled. This reversibility is not weakness; it is the condition under which the case can be analyzed without becoming a closure machine.

Exposure and asymmetry are weak. There is a structural dependence between part and whole, and perhaps between inherited horizon and local meaning, but this is not yet a power relation. Ω cannot be used here to claim vulnerability, domination, obligation, culpability, or institutional authority. It remains a weak structural asymmetry of priority or dependence.

This absence of a concrete scene has important consequences. It limits the case’s output type to conceptual structural reading and boundary articulation. It prevents the analysis from assigning responsibility or diagnosing drift. It keeps MIP, AHP, and QC from activating. It also prevents LOGIC’s post-moral effects from becoming more than a guardrail. The scene is strong enough for PMS Core and LOGIC boundary analysis, but not strong enough for applied attribution.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading begins with **Δ, Difference**. In the hermeneutic circle, difference appears first as the distinction between part and whole. But this is not the only difference. There is also the difference between initial understanding and revised understanding, between a vicious circle and a productive recursion, between context and item, between frame and what the frame makes legible. Δ is active because without difference there is no problem: if part and whole collapsed into one another, no circular demand would arise. Calibration requires care: the analysis must distinguish logical circularity from interpretive recursion and contextual dependence. Pushing Δ too far would fragment the case into separate elements and lose the mutual dependence that makes it hermeneutic.

**∇, Impulse**, appears as directional pressure toward understanding. The part does not merely differ from the whole; it creates a tension. The interpreter wants to know how the part fits, what the whole means, and whether the relation can be stabilized. This is not psychological desire. It is structural directedness generated by the insufficiency of either pole alone. The risk is to psychologize ∇ as motive or subjective need. In this case, ∇ should remain an abstract interpretive pressure.

**□, Frame**, is strongly active. The hermeneutic circle only becomes legible within a horizon, context, or fore-structure. A part is a part only relative to some whole; a whole is a whole only relative to some field of relevance. Frame determines what counts as relevant, what counts as a part, what counts as a whole, and what kind of circularity is at issue. Calibration requires specifying whether the frame is logical, hermeneutic, phenomenological, textual, historical, or methodological. Overclaim begins when □ is treated as exhaustive. A frame may clarify the case, but it does not capture all possible accounts of interpretation.

**Λ, Non-Event**, is one of the central operators. The fully understood whole is expected but not initially given. The interpreter needs the whole, but the whole is absent, delayed, or only provisionally anticipated. This absence is not nothing. It structures the interpretive problem. Λ is therefore active as the missing completion that drives the circle. However, Λ must not be treated automatically as failure. The absence of the whole may indicate vicious circularity, productive delay, constitutive non-closure, or an external limit of the PMS reading. Calibration must keep these possibilities open.

**Α, Attractor**, appears in the recurrence of part-whole movement. Interpretation stabilizes as a repeated pattern: part to whole, whole to part, revised part to revised whole. This can become a productive interpretive rhythm, but it can also become frame-locking. The key calibration issue is whether there is real revision or only repetition. If the same frame merely protects itself from challenge, the attractor becomes a risk of circular reinforcement. If the movement allows parts and wholes to alter one another, the attractor can support productive recursion. Α is active, but its value is not predetermined.

**Ω, Asymmetry**, is weak and bounded. The case supports structural asymmetry only in the sense that part and whole are not equivalent functions. The whole frames the part; the parts compose or revise the whole. A prior horizon may shape what appears as a part; a local detail may destabilize the horizon. But this is not yet social power, moral priority, vulnerability, authority, or obligation. The main risk is Ω inflation: turning structural dependence into dominance, or treating an inherited horizon as if it had legitimate authority. PMS Core therefore marks Ω as weak. It may become stronger in a concrete scene of interpretation under institutional, legal, educational, or political asymmetry, but not here.

**Θ, Temporality**, is strongly active. The hermeneutic circle is best understood as a temporal structure of return and revision rather than a static contradiction. Understanding begins somewhere, but that beginning is not final. Parts are revisited; wholes are adjusted; expectations are revised; absence persists or changes form. Θ makes it possible to distinguish an invalid loop from a revisable trajectory. Yet Θ must not be overextended. If a rival formalization defines the case as a strictly synchronic circular justification problem, then temporal recursion may be beside the point. The PMS reading depends on the case being treated as interpretive movement, not merely formal invalidity.

**Φ, Recontextualization**, is central. The part is re-read through a revised whole; the whole is re-read through revised parts. This is the primary mechanism by which the circle becomes potentially productive rather than merely vicious. But Φ is also dangerous if overused. Not every disagreement is recontextualization. Not every resistance to PMS is a frame shift. Not every non-fit should be absorbed as “incomplete Φ.” Proper calibration requires identifying a source frame and a target frame. What changed? Which part was re-read? Which whole was revised? If those questions cannot be answered, Φ becomes a smoothing term rather than an analytic operator.

**Χ, Distance**, is strongly active as the discipline that prevents premature closure. The analysis must not decide too quickly that the hermeneutic circle is vicious, productive, resolved, or captured. Distance allows the case to be held open long enough to distinguish recursion from circular justification, non-closure from failure, and integration from forced coherence. Χ is therefore not optional. If Χ is suspended, PMS becomes a closure device. The major overclaim would be to treat the PMS rendering itself as the resolution of the case.

**Σ, Integration**, is conditional. The movement between part and whole may yield a coherent interpretation. But it may also yield merely apparent coherence, frame-locking, assimilation of difference, or totalization. PMS Core therefore does not claim that the hermeneutic circle is integrated. Σ can be examined only where recursive movement yields a more coherent coordination of part, whole, frame, absence, and temporal revision. Even then, coherence is not proof of truth. Σ must remain conditional, marked with a question rather than silently achieved.

**Ψ, Self-Binding**, is weak and mostly analytic. The abstract case does not establish a self-bound interpreter, method, institution, or practice. It does not show responsibility, identity, or long-term commitment. Ψ applies primarily to the analysis itself: the PMS application must bind itself to its own guardrails, including reversibility, distance, and non-capture. It would be overclaim to treat the hermeneutic circle itself as a self or to infer responsibility from interpretive structure alone.

The resulting PMS Core formula is therefore not a triumphant full chain but a disciplined, bounded one. The primary formula can be rendered as:

**Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ω ∘ Α ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ**

with Ω weak, Σ conditional, and Ψ not entailed. A cleaner reduced version, where weak asymmetry is not accepted beyond structural dependence, is:

**Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Α ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ**

A conditional integration formula may be considered only as:

**Σ? ∘ Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ω? ∘ Α ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ**

The question mark is not decorative. It is the safeguard against making integration appear already achieved merely because the analysis is coherent.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The dependency discipline is essential because the hermeneutic circle tempts premature movement toward later operators. It is easy to see the recursive pattern and immediately claim integration. It is easy to see interpretive revision and claim self-binding. It is easy to see structural dependence and claim asymmetry in a stronger moral or social sense. PMS prohibits these shortcuts.

Later operators cannot be used cheaply. Σ depends on Χ and Φ; it requires reflective distance and real recontextualization. If the analysis cannot show that frames have shifted and that distance has prevented premature closure, then Σ is not warranted. Ψ depends on Σ, Θ, and Χ. If integration is conditional, self-binding cannot be strong. This is why the case does not become a SELF_FIXPOINT case, even though it has a recursive structure.

Coherence is not proof. The PMS reading of the hermeneutic circle is elegant because the case naturally involves frame, absence, recursion, temporality, recontextualization, and distance. That elegance creates risk. A coherent PMS derivation can become too smooth and under-discriminating. It might map both vicious circularity and productive recursion with similar terms unless additional discrimination tests are applied. The analysis therefore requires explicit tests: What changes between iterations? What frame is revised? What counts as clarification rather than reinforcement? What remains outside PMS capture?

Non-capture is not failure. A phenomenological account of lived understanding, a formal-logical account of circular justification, a deconstructive account of non-closure, or a historical account of tradition may preserve dimensions that PMS does not capture. That does not weaken PMS by itself; it marks the boundary of this application. PMS must not automatically translate rival framing into drift, incomplete integration, or failed maturity.

Invalid dependency shortcuts would include moving from Δ and ∇ directly to Σ by saying that the case is coherent because PMS can describe it; moving from □ directly to Ψ by treating the interpretive horizon as a self-bound authority; moving from Λ directly to failure by treating absence as defect; or moving from Ω weak structural dependence to moral or institutional asymmetry. These shortcuts would turn PMS into the kind of closure machine that AXIOM is designed to resist.

The most important calibration points are concentrated around Χ, Φ, Σ, Ψ, Ω, Θ, and Λ. Χ must remain active enough to prevent premature closure. Φ must show real recontextualization rather than mere absorption of non-fit. Σ must remain conditional and must not be equated with philosophical adequacy. Ψ must remain analytic unless an actual self-binding interpreter, method, or institution is supplied. Ω must stay weak unless a concrete asymmetry appears. Θ must be justified by interpretive return and revision rather than imported into a purely synchronic logical problem. Λ must remain structured absence, not defect, nihilism, or metaphysical pointer.

The PMS dependency discipline therefore does two things at once. It allows the hermeneutic circle to become structurally legible, and it blocks the analysis from becoming too successful. That double movement is central: the analysis is valuable because it maps the closure-demand, not because it eliminates the problem.

For this reason, the case requires explicit discrimination checks. First, the analysis must distinguish productive recursion from vicious circularity: if each return to the whole changes what can be seen in the part, Φ and Θ may be doing real work; if each return merely protects the original frame, Α has become circular reinforcement. Second, it must distinguish recontextualization from assimilation: a rival reading or non-fit is not recontextualized merely because PMS can redescribe it. Third, it must distinguish integration from closure-laundering: Σ is warranted only if the relation between part, whole, frame, and residue is actually coordinated without suppressing Λ. Fourth, it must distinguish structural asymmetry from authority: Ω remains weak unless a concrete scene shows power, vulnerability, obligation, or exposure. These checks are what prevent PMS coherence from becoming a substitute for philosophical adequacy.

### Derived structures

**Awareness_A** is active. Its formula is **Θ + □ + Δ**: sustained, framed differentiation across time. The hermeneutic circle clearly involves this structure. The part-whole distinction is maintained across interpretive time within frames or horizons. Awareness here is structural, not phenomenological consciousness. The analysis does not claim that PMS explains lived awareness. It only claims that the case requires sustained framed differentiation.

Awareness_A matters because the hermeneutic circle does not consist of a single distinction made once. The distinction between part and whole must be held open over time. The reader or interpreter must remain aware that local meaning is not self-sufficient and that the whole is not independently complete. This is a structural awareness condition, not a mental-state report.

**Coherence_C** is active but unstable. Its formula is **Θ + Λ + □ + ∇**: temporally stabilized structuring of impulse and expectation within a frame. The case involves a pressure toward understanding within a frame while the fully given whole remains absent. Coherence can be pursued by carrying this absence rather than eliminating it. However, coherence remains a risk because the analysis can become too smooth. A coherent PMS rendering does not show that the hermeneutic circle is resolved.

Coherence_C therefore has two sides. On one side, the hermeneutic circle is not chaos: interpretation can proceed despite missing totality. On the other side, coherence can become seductive. The analysis must distinguish coherence under non-closure from closure disguised as coherence. In this case, Coherence_C is active precisely because Λ remains part of the structure.

**Responsibility_R** is weak. Its formula is **Ψ + Φ + Θ + Ω**. The case does not supply a role, obligation, effective capacity, consequence, or self-bound agent. Ω is weak; Ψ is conditional; therefore Responsibility_R cannot be activated as a case-level finding. It would require a concrete interpretive practice, such as a judge, teacher, institution, or scholarly tradition that binds itself to interpretive obligations under asymmetry across time. That is not present here.

This weakness is not a defect of the case. It is a boundary condition. The hermeneutic circle can become responsibility-relevant in applied contexts, but the abstract philosophical case does not itself justify responsibility attribution. Non-assignment is not a low score. It is the correct refusal to escalate.

**Action_E** is weak or under-specified. Its formula is **Σ + Θ + ∇**: integrated realization of directedness across time. The hermeneutic circle involves interpretive movement, but not concrete action. No enactment, policy, decision, or practical intervention is given. Action_E may become relevant in an applied hermeneutic scene, but in this case it remains weak.

Action_E is especially constrained by the conditional status of Σ. If integration is not established, action cannot be treated as integrated realization. The case may show directed interpretive movement across time, but it does not show integrated enactment.

**Dignity_in_Practice_D** is an analytic and application constraint. Its formula is **Ψ + Χ + Ω**. Because Ω is weak and no person is being assessed, D is not activated as a substantive scene finding. It constrains the application: do not rank interpreters, traditions, or rival theories; do not shame; do not diagnose; do not tribunalize disagreement; do not turn weak structural asymmetry into moral hierarchy. Dignity-in-Practice is not ontological dignity and not a claim of moral superiority.

Dignity-in-Practice matters here because interpretation disputes can easily become authority disputes. A framework may be tempted to say that rival readings are less mature, less coherent, less integrated, or less responsible. This case forbids that move. D operates as restraint, not as a dignity theorem.

**SELF_FIXPOINT** is analytic-only. Its sequence is:

**Ψ ∘ Σ ∘ Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ω ∘ Α ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ**

The hermeneutic circle may superficially resemble self-relation because it is recursive, but recursion is not selfhood. The case does not establish a self-bound integrated trajectory. SELF_FIXPOINT therefore remains a discipline for the analysis itself, not a structure assigned to the case object.

The derived structures reinforce the central claim boundary. Awareness_A and Coherence_C are genuinely active. Responsibility_R and Action_E are weak or under-specified. Dignity-in-Practice_D is a praxeological application constraint. SELF_FIXPOINT is analytic-only. None of these derived structures becomes primitive. None functions as diagnosis, ranking, metaphysical proof, or governance finding.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS drift-pattern analysis remains cautious. Drift patterns are treated as conditional structural risks, not findings. The case does not diagnose a person, tradition, school, or interpretive method. It does not assign failure. It only identifies possible distortions if the structure is handled badly.

**AD_A>>E**, excessive distance between Awareness and Enactment, is possible but not established. The hermeneutic circle can encourage expanding interpretive awareness while delaying closure, decision, or enactment. A reader might repeatedly re-open context, horizon, and part-whole dependency until no interpretation can proceed. But the case material supplies no concrete action demand, no suppressed enactment, and no evaluative asymmetry. The awareness axis and action axis therefore remain under-specified. The non-assignment of this drift pattern is not a low score. It simply means the required scene conditions are absent.

The axis effect on awareness is therefore under-specified rather than over-expanded. The axis effect on action is also under-specified rather than suppressed or chronically delayed. There is no action scene in which delay could be assessed. The risk can be named only as a possible pattern if the hermeneutic circle were moved into a concrete interpretive practice where action was repeatedly postponed by expanding contextual reflection.

**AD_Sigma_low**, fragmented integration, is also possible but not established. If recontextualization continually outpaces integration, interpretation may remain volatile. Parts may keep revising wholes; wholes may keep revising parts; no coherent coordination stabilizes. But again, this is only a risk reading. The case does not show actual fragmented integration, inconsistent responsibility, or a concrete praxis distortion. The coherence axis and responsibility axis remain under-specified.

The axis effect on coherence is therefore under-specified rather than volatile. The axis effect on responsibility is under-specified rather than inconsistent. Responsibility_R is weak because the case lacks the conditions for responsibility, not because the case has failed responsibility.

The larger coherence risk is different: PMS may fit the case too well. Because the hermeneutic circle involves frame, absence, recursion, temporality, recontextualization, and distance, PMS can produce a highly plausible rendering. That plausibility can under-discriminate rival readings. The analysis therefore marks coherence risk as high and mitigates it by keeping Σ conditional, Ψ weak, Ω weak, non-capture open, and falsifier conditions explicit.

This means that the drift-pattern section does not add a hidden verdict. It does not say that hermeneutics drifts, that interpreters evade action, or that recursive interpretation is under-integrated. It says only that the structure has identifiable misuse risks if later applied carelessly. Risk is not finding. Under-specification is not failure. Non-assignment is not low score.

### Add-on / overlay reading

The relevant add-on is **PMS-LOGIC**. It is used only as an overlay/application profile. It does not redefine PMS operators, does not change PMS dependencies, does not rewrite the PMS Core pass, and does not introduce new base operators. Its reduced signatures are shorthand only. PMS.yaml remains the only PMS Base reference; PMS-LOGIC adds an overlay vocabulary for logical boundary management, non-closure, closure-drive, responsibility-readability limits, and LOGIC-specific drift risks.

PMS Core alone makes the hermeneutic circle structurally legible as part-whole recursion under frame, absence, temporality, recontextualization, distance, and conditional integration. LOGIC is justified because the case centrally concerns the limits of logical closure. The question is not merely “what operators are present?” but “what happens when a system of interpretation wants closure but cannot obtain it without presupposing what it seeks?” LOGIC sharpens that boundary. It makes explicit that the case is not only a recursive structure but also a boundary-management problem: logic may organize consistency, expose dependence, and test circularity, but it must not turn coherence into final authority where Λ persists.

The LOGIC tripartition is active in different degrees.

The **pre-moral justification layer** is active. At this level, the case is legible before any moral, normative, or responsibility-oriented interpretation appears. Δ marks part-whole distinction. ∇ marks interpretive pressure. □ marks horizon and fore-structure. Λ marks the absent fully given whole. Α marks recurring circular or recursive movement. This level does not say whether the recursion is good, bad, valid, necessary, rational, or sufficient. It only renders the structure readable before normativity. No value, duty, prescription, blame, guilt, sanction, or moral command is introduced. The pre-moral layer is important precisely because the hermeneutic circle can be structurally analyzed without becoming an ethical or evaluative case.

The **logical boundary-management layer** is strongly active. The hermeneutic circle directly tests the boundary of logic as ordering. Logic can distinguish, frame, identify dependence, expose circularity, and support coherence. But it cannot become final authority where Λ persists. In this case, the logical boundary is reached where the relation between part and whole cannot be finally closed without presupposing what is being interpreted. LOGIC therefore treats the case not as a demand for final closure, but as a boundary-management problem. □ sets the scope of interpretation; Λ preserves the unresolved whole; Χ prevents forced closure or authority-smuggling; Σ remains conditional rather than achieved.

This is the main added value of PMS-LOGIC. PMS Core says that the absent whole is structurally meaningful. LOGIC says that this absence marks a boundary for closure-drive. The analysis may remain disciplined without pretending that the hermeneutic circle has been solved. Logic is still useful: it can test whether an interpretation is merely circular, whether a frame is protecting itself, whether a whole is being assumed too early, and whether a coherent integration has become totalizing. But logic becomes unstable when it converts its ordering power into authority over meaning.

The **post-moral effects layer** is weak. There is no concrete praxis scene, no agent, no action, no consequence, no effective capacity, no role obligation, and no responsibility pathway. Θ is active as interpretive temporality. Ω is weak as structural dependence between part, whole, and horizon. Ψ is analytic or conditional. Therefore no responsibility, blame, duty, sanction, guilt, or moral verdict follows. The post-moral layer is protective: it prevents readers from smuggling obligation, guilt, or blame into an abstract case of interpretive recursion.

Non-closure is the core LOGIC contribution. Persistent Λ is not a mere deficiency. It is the structured absence of a fully available whole. The strength of LOGIC here is not that it closes the hermeneutic circle, but that it makes disciplined non-closure readable. The unresolved part-whole relation can be carried without dogmatic replacement, metaphysical filling, nihilistic flattening, or premature integration. The boundary is not silence, failure, or withdrawal from analysis. It is the point at which logic remains honest by not overclaiming.

Effective capacity is under-specified. LOGIC treats responsibility as dependent on effective frame-bound capacity plus temporal availability. The case has □ and Θ in a conceptual sense, but no agent with access, means, authority, role, action window, or consequence pathway. Therefore effective capacity is not established. No responsibility-readability follows from the abstract part-whole structure alone.

Non-innocence is absent. LOGIC’s notion of non-innocence concerns attributable consequences without intent, command, guilt, or blame. That structure is not present. The unresolved absence in the hermeneutic circle remains Λ as interpretive non-closure, not non-innocence. The absence of a fully given whole is not an omission by an agent, not a failure of duty, and not a ground for attribution.

Responsibility without ought is not applicable as an attribution in this case. LOGIC distinguishes responsibility as attributability from obligation as prescriptive demand. Since the case has no agent whose consequences can be attributed, responsibility-language remains out of scope. Even if later applied to a concrete interpretive institution, LOGIC would require effective capacity, temporal availability, asymmetry, and self-binding before attributability could be considered. It still would not produce an ought.

The active reduced signatures are selective. **PRE_MORAL_justification** is active: Δ, ∇, □, Λ, and Α make the case legible before normativity. **LOGIC_boundary_management** is active: □, Λ, Χ, and conditional Σ mark ordering under non-closure. **NON_CLOSURE_stability** is active: Δ, □, Λ, and Α show how openness can be carried within a frame rather than filled. **POST_MORAL_effects** is weak because Ω and Ψ are weak. **MORAL_READABILITY_min** is weak and guardrail-only. **POTENTIAL_effective** is under-specified. **HUME_KNOT_reconstruction** is not applicable because the case does not involve “potential obligates,” non-innocence, or consequence attribution.

LOGIC’s boundary constructs are non-operator handles. Logic itself is not a PMS operator; it is an overlay construct for consistency, derivation, ordering, and closure-drive within frames. Morality is not an operator; it is a readability field under certain constellations. Responsibility versus ought is not an operator distinction in PMS Base; it is a LOGIC overlay clarification. Potential effective capacity and non-innocence are also overlay handles, not base operators. These terms sharpen the reading only if they remain subordinate to PMS Core.

The failure-mode constraints are important. The most active is: forced closure where Λ persists produces structural instability. If the hermeneutic circle is declared merely solved, merely vicious, or merely productive without preserving the unresolved whole, LOGIC treats that as a failure-mode risk. Another active constraint is that explanatory power converted into sense-making authority causes overreach. PMS-LOGIC may explain why the hermeneutic circle can be structurally productive, but explanatory elegance must not become authority over hermeneutic, logical, phenomenological, or deconstructive rivals. A further active constraint is that stable self-binding takes the form of restraint rather than assertion: the overlay must bind itself to non-closure, not command rivals to integrate.

The LOGIC drift risks are active as risks, not findings. The main risk is **dogmatism / Σ-totalization**: integration forced into total closure, coherence treated as final truth, Λ denied instead of carried. The case is especially vulnerable to this because PMS and LOGIC can render it elegantly. **Authority capture / Ω-inflation** is a risk only by over-reading; weak structural dependence must not become authority. **Moralism / pseudo-ought injection** is a guardrail risk: methodological restraint must not become a moral command. **Nihilism / Λ-suppression** is a risk if unresolved whole-understanding is flattened into mere ignorance or meaninglessness. **Technocratic reduction / □-overconstraint** is a risk if PMS vocabulary is treated as exhaustive.

The counter-readings remain open. “This collapses into relativism” is active because hermeneutic circularity can be mistaken for arbitrary interpretation. LOGIC responds by treating frames as scope constraints and Λ as structured non-closure, without denying rival formalizations. “This wins by translation, not by final superiority” is also active: PMS-LOGIC can translate the case elegantly, but translation is not victory. “Relation to adjacent frameworks” remains active because formal logic, phenomenological hermeneutics, coherentism, contextualism, systems recursion, and deconstructive non-closure all remain live.

MIP and AHP are not triggered. There is no governance artefact, applied decision, institutional policy, responsibility attribution, public audit, or claim-hardening need. QC is also not triggered. The final LOGIC overlay signature is:

**LOGIC_boundary_management + NON_CLOSURE_stability**, with **PRE_MORAL_justification** active, **POST_MORAL_effects** weak, **MORAL_READABILITY_min** weak, **POTENTIAL_effective** under-specified, and **HUME_KNOT_reconstruction** not applicable.

This formula is not a new PMS chain. It is an overlay summary of what LOGIC adds to the Core pass: the case is structurally readable before normativity, logically boundary-sensitive under non-closure, weak in post-moral consequence structure, and not eligible for responsibility or ought derivation.

### Layer interaction

PMS Core remains the base structural grammar. It supplies the operator inventory, order, dependencies, derived structures, and application discipline. The Core reading says: the hermeneutic circle is a reversible interpretive recursion in which framed part-whole difference and structured absence recur over time, requiring recontextualization and distance before conditional integration can even be considered.

PMS-LOGIC sharpens the closure boundary. It does not add a new operator; it adds overlay visibility. It asks whether the desire for logical settlement is trying to cross a boundary that Λ makes structurally unavailable. It clarifies that logic remains useful as ordering, distinction, consistency, and derivation, but becomes unstable when it claims final authority over non-closure.

The interaction is therefore layered rather than mixed. PMS Core identifies the operators. LOGIC identifies the closure-risk pattern. PMS Core says that Λ is structured absence in the part-whole recursion. LOGIC says that this structured absence must not be compensated by dogma, substitute narrative, pseudo-certainty, or premature Σ. PMS Core marks Ω as weak. LOGIC prevents that weak Ω from becoming authority capture. PMS Core marks Ψ as analytic or conditional. LOGIC prevents analytic restraint from becoming a prescriptive ought.

The overlay must not redefine PMS operators. Λ remains PMS Non-Event, not a LOGIC-only concept. Χ remains PMS Distance, not a generic intellectual virtue. Σ remains PMS Integration, not final truth. Ω remains PMS Asymmetry, not moral authority. Ψ remains PMS Self-Binding, not obligation or conscience. LOGIC may name closure-drive and non-closure as boundary constructs, but it cannot convert them into new base operators.

Overlay language could overreach if “non-closure as strength” became a slogan that forbids closure even where closure is warranted. It could also overreach if “logic is not final authority” were used to dismiss formal objections. The correct interaction is more disciplined: Core maps the structure; LOGIC marks the closure boundary; both preserve rival readings and do not claim final resolution.

Non-closure remains visible at both layers. In PMS Core, Λ is the absent fully given whole. In LOGIC, Λ becomes the boundary where logic must stop short of authority. The two layers reinforce each other without mixing: PMS identifies structured absence; LOGIC prevents the absence from being misread as defect, nihilism, or a demand for dogmatic completion.

This interaction also explains why no further layer is added. MIP would be appropriate only if the case were used in governance, institutional responsibility, policy justification, or applied accountability. AHP would be appropriate only if an analysis artefact required second-order hardening through test hooks, counterfactual handles, attack surfaces, or revision mechanics. QC would be appropriate only if there were a quality-control or verification layer to assess. None is present. Escalating beyond PMS Core + LOGIC would therefore be silent layer escalation.

### Example 1 — Reading a sentence before the whole text is settled

**Type:** compact
**Recommended insertion point:** after **Scene and frame structure**

A reader encounters the sentence: “He finally returned the key.” Taken in isolation, the sentence seems simple. Someone had a key, and now the key has been returned. Yet almost immediately, interpretation depends on a larger whole that is not yet given. Was the key stolen, borrowed, inherited, entrusted, withheld, or symbolically returned? Is the return an act of repair, resignation, betrayal, compliance, reconciliation, or defeat? The part is legible enough to begin reading, but not legible enough to close the interpretation.

As the reader continues, later passages change the meaning of the sentence. A previous argument reveals that the key belonged to a shared apartment. A later scene suggests that returning it ended a relationship. Another passage complicates the matter further: the key may also have opened a workplace archive. The sentence does not become meaningless because the whole was initially absent. Rather, its meaning is revised through the unfolding whole. At the same time, the whole itself is assembled through such parts. The text does not arrive as a completed totality; it becomes readable through successive local interpretations.

This example demonstrates the basic hermeneutic-circle structure without turning it into a metaphysical thesis. PMS Core marks the initial distinction through **Δ**: sentence and text, part and whole, initial reading and revised reading. **∇** appears as the pressure to understand what the sentence is doing. **□** is the interpretive frame: reading a literary passage under the assumption that local details matter within a wider narrative. **Λ** is the absent whole: the reader needs a full context that is not yet available. **Α** appears as the recurring part-whole movement. **Θ** is active because the reading unfolds over time. **Φ** appears when the sentence is recontextualized by later material. **Χ** is required so the reader does not close too early. **Σ** remains conditional: a coherent interpretation may emerge, but it is not guaranteed. **Ψ** is not a case-level finding; no self-binding responsibility structure is established.

The example also shows why the circle is not automatically vicious. The first reading is not simply false; it is provisional. Later context does not erase the sentence but changes what can be responsibly said about it. At the same time, the example does not prove that all circular interpretation is productive. If the reader decided from the first sentence that the story must be about betrayal and then forced every later detail into that frame, the same part-whole movement would become frame-locking rather than recontextualization. This is why Φ and Χ must remain calibrated and why Σ cannot be assumed merely because a coherent reading becomes available.

The PMS-LOGIC overlay is relevant because the example shows why non-closure is not automatically failure. LOGIC does not say the sentence has no meaning until the whole text is known. It also does not say that any interpretation is equally valid. Instead, it treats the missing whole as structured **Λ** within a frame. The reader can continue in disciplined non-closure, revising without pretending that the initial reading was final.

MIP, AHP, and QC are not triggered. There is no governance artefact, no institutional decision, no claim-hardening need, and no quality-control audit. The example must not be used to infer anything about the reader’s intelligence, maturity, psychology, or interpretive competence. It also must not be used to prove a theory of literary meaning.

The non-capture note is important: a literary theorist, phenomenologist, or deconstructive reader might preserve dimensions that PMS does not capture, such as affective tone, historical language, genre conventions, or constitutive textual undecidability. The article takeaway is narrower: the example makes visible why part-whole interpretation can be structurally recursive without requiring premature closure.

### Example 2 — A legal phrase whose meaning depends on the whole statute

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** after **PMS Core structural reading**, especially after the discussion of **Φ**, **Χ**, and conditional **Σ**

Consider a statute containing the phrase “reasonable access.” A reader begins with the phrase as a local part. At first glance, “reasonable access” may appear to mean that access must be practically possible, not merely formally available. But the phrase cannot be interpreted from ordinary language alone. Its meaning depends on the statute’s purpose, surrounding provisions, definitions, exceptions, remedies, and institutional context. The part requires the whole.

Yet the whole statute is not understood independently of its parts. The statute’s purpose is inferred through repeated engagement with provisions like “reasonable access,” “timely notice,” “undue burden,” and “public interest.” Each local phrase contributes to the reader’s sense of the whole. If “reasonable access” is read broadly, the statute may appear protective and inclusion-oriented. If it is read narrowly, the statute may appear administrative and compliance-oriented. The whole changes the part, but the part also changes the whole.

The example is bounded. It does not ask what the law actually is in any jurisdiction. It does not decide how a court should rule. It does not supply legal advice. It uses a legal-looking phrase only as a structured interpretive vignette.

The example demonstrates why the hermeneutic circle cannot be reduced to either simple circularity or simple coherence. A purely local reading of “reasonable access” is underdetermined. A purely global reading of the statute risks importing the very whole that is still being constructed. Interpretation proceeds by return: phrase to statute, statute to phrase, revised phrase to revised statute. That movement may be productive, but it may also become circular reinforcement if the reader decides too early what the statute “must” be about and then reads every provision as confirmation.

PMS Core hooks are visible throughout. **Δ** marks the distinction between phrase and statute, local meaning and global purpose, ordinary language and legal frame. **∇** appears as the pressure to settle the phrase because practical consequences seem to depend on it. **□** is the declared interpretive frame: statutory interpretation, not literary appreciation, metaphysical semantics, or psychological intention-reading. **Λ** is the absent fully settled whole: the statute’s operative meaning is expected but not immediately given. **Α** appears in repeated interpretive return, especially if the same reading pattern stabilizes across provisions. **Ω** is weak in the abstract example but potentially stronger in real legal contexts, because institutions can impose readings and affected parties may bear consequences. In this bounded article example, however, Ω is not activated as a legal-forensic claim. **Θ** is active because meaning unfolds across interpretive sequence. **Φ** appears when the phrase is re-read under a revised understanding of statutory purpose. **Χ** is crucial: the reader must suspend premature closure. **Σ** remains conditional: the reader may integrate phrase, purpose, exception, and remedy into a coherent interpretation, but coherence alone does not prove legal correctness. **Ψ** is not established unless an institution binds itself to a formal interpretive rule, precedent, or policy; that is outside the vignette.

The LOGIC overlay sharpens the boundary. The case may tempt closure: the phrase seems to demand a final meaning. LOGIC marks the point at which logical ordering helps but cannot become final authority. The reader can identify dependencies, test consistency, and expose circular support. But if the reader declares the statute solved merely because one interpretation is coherent, LOGIC would mark a **Σ-totalization risk**. **NON_CLOSURE_stability** is active: the phrase can remain interpretable under structured non-closure while more context is examined. **LOGIC_boundary_management** is active: the example shows how logic orders the relation between part and whole without eliminating **Λ**.

Effective capacity, non-innocence, and responsibility without ought are not activated. A real legal institution might introduce these later, but the vignette does not. MIP, AHP, and QC are therefore not triggered. There is no governance evaluation, no artefact audit, and no claim-hardening layer.

Forbidden claims are explicit. The example does not claim that PMS decides legal interpretation. It does not claim that judges, lawyers, or readers are responsible, irresponsible, mature, or immature. It does not claim that one statutory interpretation is legally correct. It does not derive an ought. It does not validate PMS or PMS-LOGIC.

The non-capture note is substantial. Legal hermeneutics may require doctrines, jurisdictional rules, precedent, institutional authority, evidentiary standards, and democratic legitimacy that PMS Core and LOGIC do not supply. Formal legal methods may preserve distinctions that this structural example does not capture. The article takeaway is therefore limited: the example shows how the hermeneutic circle can be structurally mapped while preserving the difference between recursive interpretation and authoritative legal closure.

### Example 3 — A historical artifact interpreted through a culture that is itself reconstructed from artifacts

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** in the **Add-on / overlay reading** section, after the discussion of non-closure and LOGIC boundary management

An archaeologist finds a decorated vessel fragment. The local part is concrete: a shard with a pattern, material, curvature, and traces of use. But the fragment is not self-interpreting. Its meaning depends on a larger cultural whole: Was the vessel domestic, ceremonial, commercial, symbolic, imported, copied, repaired, or repurposed? The fragment seems to require knowledge of the culture in which it belonged.

The difficulty is that the culture is not directly available as a completed whole. It is reconstructed through fragments: vessels, tools, burial patterns, settlement layouts, inscriptions, trade routes, residues, absences, and comparisons. The whole needed to interpret the artifact is itself built through artifacts like the one being interpreted. The hermeneutic circle appears with particular force: the part needs the whole, and the whole is inferred from parts.

The vignette is bounded and abstract. It does not describe an actual excavation, culture, or dataset. It does not make an empirical archaeological claim. It does not evaluate archaeologists. It does not settle the philosophy of historical reconstruction. It provides a reusable article example for conceptual analysis only.

The example demonstrates the productive and risky sides of recursive interpretation. A first frame may classify the shard as domestic pottery because of its material and shape. Later comparison with burial contexts may recontextualize it as ritual. Chemical residue analysis might re-open the frame again. A pattern initially treated as decoration might become a marker of exchange, imitation, or social identity. Each revision can clarify, but each can also over-assimilate. If the researcher decides too early that the culture is primarily ritualistic, then every fragment may be interpreted as ritual evidence. The circle then risks becoming frame-locking rather than productive recursion.

PMS Core gives the structural hooks. **Δ** marks fragment and culture, local feature and reconstructed whole, initial classification and revised classification. **∇** is the pressure to make the fragment intelligible. **□** is the archaeological-historical frame that determines what counts as context, evidence, comparison, and admissible inference. **Λ** is central: the culture as a fully present whole is absent. So are many expected events or data points: missing records, incomplete sites, lost practices, unpreserved materials. **Α** appears as repeated classification patterns, typologies, and interpretive habits. **Ω** remains bounded in this abstract example, but it could become relevant in real institutional contexts where museums, disciplines, or colonial archives impose categories. Here, it is not activated as a finding. **Θ** is strongly active because historical reconstruction unfolds through sequences of discovery, comparison, revision, and delayed confirmation. **Φ** appears whenever the fragment is re-read under a new cultural or comparative frame. **Χ** is essential: the researcher must resist closure by typology alone. **Σ** is conditional: an integrated reconstruction may become plausible, but plausibility is not proof. **Ψ** would become relevant only if a research program, institution, or publication binds itself to a reconstruction over time; the vignette does not claim that.

The PMS-LOGIC overlay is especially useful here because the example contains a strong closure temptation. The absent cultural whole can invite substitute narratives. A missing context can be filled with a familiar story: ritual, trade, hierarchy, identity, decline, progress, influence. LOGIC marks this as a boundary problem. **Λ** must be carried as structured absence, not filled automatically. **LOGIC_boundary_management** is active because the analysis must distinguish disciplined inference from closure-laundering. **NON_CLOSURE_stability** is active because the absence of the whole can remain part of a valid reconstruction rather than a defect to be erased. **PRE_MORAL_justification** is active because the case is structurally legible before any normative or ethical evaluation. **POST_MORAL_effects** remain weak unless a concrete institutional scene is introduced.

The LOGIC drift risks are only risks. **Σ-totalization** would occur if a coherent cultural reconstruction were treated as final authority. **□-overconstraint** would occur if the selected archaeological frame dismissed unmodeled possibilities as irrelevant. **Λ-suppression** would occur if missing data were treated as if they did not matter. **Ω-inflation** could occur in a concrete institutional setting if a dominant archive or discipline imposed categories without preserving distance. None of these are findings in the vignette.

MIP, AHP, and QC are not triggered in this article example. They would become relevant only if the reconstruction were used in a public institutional artefact, repatriation dispute, museum policy, governance setting, or high-stakes claim requiring hardening and review. The example itself remains conceptual.

Forbidden claims must be kept clear. The example does not claim that archaeology is circular in a defective sense. It does not claim that archaeological interpretation is arbitrary. It does not claim that PMS is an archaeological method. It does not diagnose researchers or rank interpretive traditions. It does not decide empirical truth.

The non-capture note is important. Archaeology has its own evidentiary methods, material constraints, dating techniques, statistical tools, and ethical histories. A phenomenological account of material presence, a postcolonial critique of archive authority, or a technical archaeological method may preserve dimensions that PMS and LOGIC do not capture. The article takeaway is limited but useful: the example shows how a missing whole can be structurally meaningful without licensing either arbitrary interpretation or premature closure.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The formal-logical rival reading treats the hermeneutic circle as a problem of circular justification. On that reading, the key issue may be validity: whether a conclusion is being presupposed in its premises. PMS may under-capture this if it shifts too quickly from circularity to temporal recursion. The rival preserves sharper distinctions between invalid circular argument, mutual support, coherentist justification, and methodological dependence.

This rival reading matters because some uses of “circle” really do concern inferential form. If a conclusion is used as a premise for itself, no amount of temporal recontextualization necessarily makes the argument valid. PMS and LOGIC must therefore avoid treating every circle as productive recursion. The formal-logical reading preserves a stricter boundary around proof, derivation, and justificatory dependence.

The phenomenological-hermeneutic rival reading emphasizes lived understanding, historicity, fore-having, language, tradition, and horizonality. PMS can model frames and recontextualization, but it does not thereby capture lived experience or historical being. This rival preserves the depth of situated understanding that may not reduce to operator relations.

This is especially important because the hermeneutic circle has a philosophical history that cannot be exhausted by structural abstraction. A phenomenological account may treat the circle not as a problem to be solved but as a condition of being-in-the-world, historical understanding, or linguistic existence. PMS can map the structure of dependency and revision, but it does not claim to replace that philosophical depth.

The coherentist epistemology reading treats the circle as a structure of mutual support among beliefs or interpretations. It may accept non-foundational justification without requiring PMS operators. This rival preserves the epistemic question of whether circularity can be non-vicious through coherence constraints.

This rival also protects against PMS overreach. PMS can model coherence, but it does not by itself decide epistemic justification. A coherentist may ask whether the system of beliefs has explanatory support, inferential resilience, or holistic warrant. PMS Coherence_C is not identical to epistemic coherentism.

The language-practice or contextualist reading emphasizes use, convention, context, and rule-following. It may treat meaning as dependent on practices rather than on part-whole recursion in a general structural sense. PMS captures some of this through □, Α, Θ, and Φ, but may not fully capture the internal grammar of language-games or semantic practice.

This reading preserves the possibility that the relevant whole is not a totality but a practice. A word, sentence, gesture, or rule may be intelligible through use rather than through a reconstructable whole. PMS can map frames and recurrent patterns, but it should not silently convert all practice-dependence into part-whole interpretation.

The systems-recursive reading models part-whole dependence as feedback dynamics. It may preserve formal recursive structure without invoking self-binding, dignity constraints, or praxeological interpretation. PMS may be richer in praxis terms but less mathematically precise in recursion terms.

This rival is important because the hermeneutic circle can be modeled as iteration, feedback, or recursive updating. Such models may have clearer formal tools for convergence, stability, oscillation, or attractor dynamics. PMS can use Α and Θ to identify recurrence and trajectory, but it should not claim technical superiority without formal comparison.

The deconstructive or anti-capture reading treats non-closure not as a temporary residue but as constitutive. It may reject Σ as the wrong target. This rival is especially important because PMS can be tempted to treat integration as maturity. The deconstructive reading preserves the possibility that unresolvedness is not a failure to integrate but the condition of signification or textuality.

This rival protects the case’s strongest boundary. The hermeneutic circle may not merely contain a residue; it may require residue. If so, non-closure is not something to be managed until integration becomes available. It is constitutive of the field. PMS and LOGIC can preserve this possibility only if Σ remains conditional and Λ remains visible.

The ethical reading is weak in this case but possible in later applications. It might ask how interpretive horizons shape inclusion, exclusion, authority, or recognition. PMS and LOGIC do not deny this, but the present case does not supply the scene conditions needed for ethical or governance analysis.

A legal-hermeneutic rival reading may also become relevant in applied contexts. It would require jurisdiction, doctrine, interpretive authority, precedent, consequence, and procedural review. Example 2 gestures toward that possibility but explicitly does not activate it. PMS and LOGIC can show the structure of part-whole dependence in legal interpretation, but they do not decide the law.

An archaeological or historical-method rival reading may become relevant in material reconstruction contexts. Example 3 gestures toward this possibility but does not claim empirical archaeology. Such methods have evidentiary standards, dating techniques, material constraints, and ethical histories that PMS and LOGIC do not replace.

None of these rival readings is a defect. They are non-capture zones. PMS and LOGIC become stronger, not weaker, by naming what they do not settle. The article therefore preserves the possibility that a rival framework may discriminate some aspect of the hermeneutic circle more sharply than PMS or LOGIC in a given context.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS reading would be weakened if the case were defined strictly as formal logical circularity with no interpretive movement, no temporality, no praxis, and no frame revision. In that case, the PMS emphasis on Θ and Φ would be under-motivated. A formal circular argument may need formal-logical evaluation rather than praxeological recursion analysis.

The PMS reading would also be weakened if no structured expectation of whole-understanding were present. Without Λ, the case would lose one of its central operators. If there is no absent whole, delayed completion, expected context, or structured non-event, the hermeneutic circle becomes something else.

The reading would be weakened if part-whole dependence were shown to be only a semantic relation with no structured enactment, interpretive trajectory, or revisable frame. PMS can still describe semantic framing in some cases, but the full operator reading would become overextended.

The reading would be weakened if recontextualization were not involved. If parts and wholes do not revise one another, then Φ should not be active. The case might then be a static dependency relation, a formal circularity, or a classificatory problem, not the hermeneutic-circle structure analyzed here.

The reading would be weakened if integration were explicitly rejected as an inappropriate target. This would not invalidate PMS Core entirely, but it would require Σ to be marked not merely conditional but inappropriate for that rival frame. Deconstructive and anti-capture readings may press exactly this point.

The reading would be under-discriminating if it could not distinguish vicious circularity from productive recursion. This is the main analytic risk. A strong PMS application must specify what changes between iterations and how recontextualization differs from the original frame protecting itself. If PMS cannot make that distinction, it may merely rename the problem.

The reading would be over-assimilative if rival framings, unresolved absence, refusal of integration, rupture, disagreement, or resistance were automatically redescribed as PMS drift, incomplete calibration, incomplete integration, recontextualization, or maturity failure. That would violate the non-capture rule.

The LOGIC overlay would be weakened if the case did not involve closure pressure. If the hermeneutic circle were framed not as a problem of closure or logical boundary but only as a descriptive feature of interpretation, LOGIC would add less. It would also be weakened if the overlay treated non-closure as a universal success rather than a disciplined boundary condition.

The LOGIC overlay would be invalid if it derived an ought from the structure. The overlay may say that forced closure where Λ persists is a failure-mode risk within the analysis. It may not say that interpreters have a moral duty to remain open, that traditions are guilty when they close, or that non-closure is ethically superior. Methodological restraint is not a moral command.

The LOGIC overlay would also be overextended if it treated logical boundary management as a defeat of formal logic. The point is not that logic fails. The point is that logic becomes unstable when it functions as authority beyond its frame. Logic remains important for consistency, derivation, distinction, and testing; it simply does not eliminate Λ where the whole cannot be non-circularly supplied.

Operationalization remains unfulfilled. Stronger empirical claims would require a concrete interpretive corpus, observed revisions, coded part-whole relations, explicit frame shifts, and comparison with rival models. Stronger computational claims would require an architecture that can represent parts, wholes, frames, Λ-residue, recontextualization, and distance. Stronger legal or institutional claims would require roles, authority, evidence standards, consequences, and review procedures. Stronger responsibility claims would require effective capacity, asymmetry, temporal consequence, and self-binding. None of that is supplied.

For empirical operationalization, one would need observable indicators for the operator sequence. Δ would require identifiable part-whole distinctions. ∇ would require traceable interpretive pressure, such as revision attempts or problem-solving movement. □ would require explicit frames or horizons. Λ would require documented absence, delay, missing context, or unresolved expectation. Α would require recurrence across interpretive iterations. Θ would require temporal ordering. Φ would require identifiable source and target frames. Χ would require evidence of suspension or restraint. Σ would require criteria for integration. Ψ would require durable self-binding by an agent, method, or institution.

For comparative operationalization, one would need explicit criteria showing that PMS or LOGIC discriminates the case better than formal logic, phenomenological hermeneutics, coherentism, contextualism, systems recursion, or deconstructive non-closure. Mere translatability into PMS vocabulary would not be enough.

For governance operationalization, MIP would become relevant only if the case were used to inform institutional practice, public interpretation, accountability, policy, audit, or decision-making. AHP would become relevant only if the analysis artefact required claim-hardening, counterfactual handles, test hooks, attack surfaces, and revision mechanics. QC would become relevant only if there were a defined quality-control object. None of these conditions is met.

The case can therefore support a chapter-ready structural article, but not an empirical study, a governance recommendation, a diagnostic criterion, a legal conclusion, a clinical assessment, or a philosophical final verdict.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It cannot classify interpreters, traditions, schools, or persons as mature, immature, circular, evasive, integrated, fragmented, or deficient.

It must not be used for person ranking. No interpretive stance is assigned higher dignity, moral status, intelligence, responsibility, or human worth.

It must not be used legally or forensically. The analysis gives no evidentiary standard, no liability test, no culpability criterion, and no adjudicative rule.

It must not be used clinically. No psychological, psychiatric, therapeutic, or personality inference follows from the structure.

It must not be used metaphysically. PMS and LOGIC do not prove what meaning, being, language, consciousness, understanding, or truth ultimately are.

It must not be used empirically unless supported by data. The current case contains no observations, experiments, corpus study, historical dataset, or validation.

It must not be used as implementation validation. YAML and prose are representations, not working systems. They do not implement interpretation, agency, reasoning, or hermeneutic understanding.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. A case application does not confirm the base model, prove its completeness, or establish superiority over rivals.

It must not treat PMS-LOGIC as a new operator layer. LOGIC is an overlay, not a replacement grammar. Reduced signatures are shorthand, not new dependencies.

It must not treat add-on constructs as PMS operators. Logic, moral readability, effective capacity, non-innocence, and responsibility-without-ought are overlay handles or boundary constructs, not additions to Δ–Ψ.

It must not treat drift as verdict. Drift risks are conditional warnings, not findings. Σ-totalization, Ω-inflation, Λ-suppression, pseudo-ought injection, and frame overconstraint are risks only unless a concrete scene shows them.

It must not turn risk into diagnosis. A possible tendency toward over-coherence or under-discrimination is not a psychological trait, institutional accusation, or philosophical defect.

It must not close by authority. The deepest misuse would be to say: because PMS and LOGIC can render the hermeneutic circle coherently, the problem is solved. That would betray both the Core and the overlay. The correct result is more restrained: AXIOM shows where the closure-demand arises, where recursion can be structurally productive, and where Λ-residue must remain visible.

### Case conclusion

The integrated result is that the hermeneutic circle can be rendered as a bounded PMS Core structure of recursive interpretation under frame, absence, temporality, recontextualization, and distance, with integration remaining conditional and self-binding remaining weak or analytic. The case begins with Δ between part and whole, develops through ∇ pressure toward understanding, stabilizes within □ frames and Λ absence of the fully given whole, recurs through Α, unfolds through Θ, shifts through Φ, and requires Χ to prevent premature closure. Ω is present only weakly as structural dependence; Σ is possible but not entailed; Ψ is not established as a case-level structure.

The PMS-LOGIC overlay adds a second layer of precision without changing the Core. It makes visible that the hermeneutic circle is not merely a recursion problem but a boundary-management problem. Logic can order the relation between part and whole, expose circular dependence, and test coherence. But where the whole cannot be supplied without presupposition, LOGIC marks Λ as structured non-closure rather than as failure. The overlay’s main contribution is therefore disciplined restraint: logic remains a tool, not final authority; non-closure remains visible; Σ-totalization is blocked.

The examples contribute by showing how this structure can be made concrete without becoming evidence. The sentence-and-text example shows how a local part can be provisionally readable while the whole remains absent. The statute example shows why recursive interpretation may be practically significant while still not becoming a legal conclusion. The archaeological artifact example shows how an absent whole can guide interpretation without licensing substitute narratives or premature reconstruction. Each example demonstrates the analysis, but none proves it. Each keeps the boundary between illustration and validation intact.

What remains unclaimed is extensive. The article does not claim that the hermeneutic circle is solved. It does not claim that all hermeneutic circularity is productive. It does not claim that formal logic is inadequate, that phenomenology is reducible to PMS, that deconstructive non-closure is wrong, or that coherentist epistemology is defeated. It does not claim empirical confirmation, metaphysical truth, legal applicability, clinical relevance, implementation success, or PMS Base validation. It does not assign responsibility, diagnose drift, or rank interpretive traditions.

The boundary discipline is therefore not an accessory to the analysis; it is part of the result. Χ must remain active so that PMS does not become a closure machine. Λ must remain visible so that absence is not erased by coherence. Φ must remain calibrated so that recontextualization does not absorb every rival reading. Σ must remain conditional so that integration does not become totalization. Ψ must remain weak unless actual self-binding is supplied. Ω must remain bounded unless a concrete asymmetry appears. Dignity-in-Practice remains a praxeological application constraint, not an ontological dignity thesis.

The final methodological takeaway is that AXIOM can map the hermeneutic circle as a closure-demand without deciding it by authority. PMS Core shows the operator structure; PMS-LOGIC shows the boundary at which logical ordering must carry non-closure rather than overcome it. The case therefore becomes a strong demonstration of AXIOM’s cartographic role: it does not abolish classical philosophical problems, but converts their closure-demands into inspectable structures, marks the residues that remain, and prevents the analysis itself from becoming the closure it is meant to examine.

This is also the methodological limit of the case. The article succeeds only insofar as it preserves that restraint. If the PMS reading were treated as a final theory of hermeneutics, the case would fail its own Χ discipline. If LOGIC were treated as a superior authority over rival accounts, the overlay would become the kind of closure it is designed to block. The correct result is more modest and more useful: the hermeneutic circle remains open, but its closure-demand, recursion structure, Λ-residue, and misuse risks become explicit enough to be compared, contested, revised, and reused without pretending that the circle has disappeared.
