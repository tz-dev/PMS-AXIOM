### Why this case matters

Underdetermination is one of the clearest philosophical closure-demands because it asks what happens when a field of evidence does not compel a unique theoretical, explanatory, or interpretive result. The case is not merely that evidence can be incomplete. The sharper problem is that evidence may be adequate enough to support serious inquiry, model-building, explanation, or interpretation while still failing to decide between multiple compatible alternatives. That is why the case is structurally difficult: it does not collapse into simple ignorance, error, confusion, or relativism. It concerns the possibility of structured non-closure inside a meaningful frame.

The philosophical pressure in this case comes from the demand for unique determination. Inquiry often wants to move from evidence to one theory, from data to one model, from text to one interpretation, from observed pattern to one explanation. That demand can be legitimate as a practical pressure: research must publish, institutions must decide, models must be selected, explanations must be coordinated, and interpretations must become usable. But the case of underdetermination makes visible a limit: the same available evidence may remain compatible with more than one candidate. A structure can be orderly, coherent, and operationally useful without thereby becoming uniquely determined.

This is exactly the kind of closure-demand AXIOM is designed to map. AXIOM does not need to decide metaphysically whether the world itself is indeterminate, whether truth is inaccessible, whether rationality is impossible, or whether all theories are equally valid. Instead, it asks: what kind of closure is being demanded, inside what frame, with what residue, and under what risk of misuse? The underdetermination case belongs in AXIOM because it tests whether PMS and its LOGIC overlay can preserve a Λ-residue without treating it as failure. It asks whether the analysis can hold a disciplined stopping point without turning that stopping point into skepticism, nihilism, or authority.

The difficult feature of the case is that it tempts two opposite overreactions. One overreaction is forced closure: one coherent theory or explanation is treated as uniquely determined because the frame needs a result. The other overreaction is collapse: because evidence does not uniquely determine one theory, inquiry itself is treated as arbitrary, futile, or merely perspectival. Both moves erase the structural point. Underdetermination is not the same as “anything goes.” Nor is it solved by declaring one integration final. It requires a reading that can distinguish operational coordination from unique determination.

The completed YAML analyses therefore treat underdetermination as a bounded conceptual projection. PMS Core makes the case legible through Δ, □, Λ, Φ, and Χ: alternatives are differentiated, evidence is framed, decisive discrimination is absent, rival recontextualizations remain possible, and reflective distance prevents premature closure. LOGIC then sharpens the same structure as logical boundary management: the absence of unique determination is not a defect to be repaired by authority, but a non-closure condition to be carried.

The case is not solved empirically because no dataset, observation series, experiment, or benchmark is supplied. It is not solved metaphysically because the analysis does not decide whether reality has one final structure behind all possible theories. It is not solved clinically or diagnostically because no person, group, cognition, pathology, or maturity state is assessed. It is not solved legally or forensically because there is no tribunal, burden of proof, legal standard, or evidentiary rule. It is not solved as implementation because no software system, model-selection pipeline, or AI architecture is specified. Its value lies elsewhere: it shows how a closure-demand can remain structurally intelligible even when closure is not warranted.

### Scene and frame structure

The case has a conceptual scene rather than a concrete scene. Its minimal scene is the situation in which available evidence is compared against more than one candidate theory, explanation, or interpretation, and the evidence does not select exactly one candidate. This “scene” is not a social episode, not a courtroom, not a laboratory record, and not a model-selection benchmark. It is a structural configuration: alternatives, evidence, compatibility, missing discrimination, and pressure toward closure.

The frame, marked by □, is the evidence-and-alternative comparison frame. Inside this frame are the elements required for underdetermination to arise: a set of differentiated alternatives; a body of evidence, observation, text, data, or reasons; criteria for compatibility; and an expected or desired discriminating step. The frame defines what counts as evidence, what counts as an alternative, what counts as compatibility, and what would count as determination.

What is inside the frame is therefore limited. The analysis may speak about theories, explanations, interpretations, evidence, compatibility, missing criteria, and non-unique determination. It may also speak about the reflective posture required when the frame does not yield closure. It may not, without further scene material, speak as if a specific institution, researcher, court, clinician, model, or community has failed.

What is outside the frame is equally important. Outside the frame are metaphysical conclusions about reality, psychological accounts of belief, person-level claims, moral judgments about disagreement, legal determinations, empirical findings about actual theories, and implementation claims about actual systems. Also outside the frame are any claims that PMS itself has defeated rival epistemologies merely by rendering the structure legible.

Protected constraints include reversibility, Χ-distance, and dignity-in-practice. Reversibility means that the PMS reading can be revised if new evidence, a sharper frame, or a rival formalization changes the analysis. Χ-distance means the analysis must not collapse into verdict, authority, or forced closure. Dignity-in-practice means that even if the case later enters a concrete scene with persons or institutions, the analysis must not rank, shame, diagnose, humiliate, or label them.

Roles are not present in the case-internal sense. There is no model proposer, judge, expert, critic, user, or affected party in the completed Core YAML. The LOGIC overlay can describe roles that would appear in applied contexts, but in this article’s Core reading they remain hypothetical. This prevents Ω from being smuggled in. A disagreement among theories is not yet an asymmetry. A plurality of interpretations is not yet a power relation.

Temporality, Θ, is weak. Underdetermination can be described at a single moment: given this evidence, multiple alternatives remain compatible. Θ becomes more important if inquiry persists, evidence is awaited, revisions occur, or institutional lock-in develops over time. But the abstract case does not establish any of that. Therefore Θ is conditional rather than central in the Core layer.

The reversibility window remains open. Because no concrete decision, binding, policy, or enforcement structure is supplied, nothing in the case requires irreversible closure. The proper posture is reversible non-closure: the analysis can say what is structurally present now, while leaving room for later evidence, re-framing, or rival formalization.

Exposure and asymmetry conditions are not established. No one bears a cost in the abstract case. No one holds power to impose a model. No downstream user is exposed to error. No authority declares a theory final. Because Ω is not case-internal, responsibility, moral readability, non-innocence, and post-moral effects do not follow at the Core level.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading begins with **Δ — Difference**. Underdetermination requires distinguishable alternatives. If there is only one candidate, or if the supposed alternatives are merely paraphrases of the same structure, the case does not begin. Δ marks the plurality of candidate theories, explanations, or interpretations. It also marks the difference between evidence and theory, between compatibility and determination, and between local fit and unique selection. In this case Δ is active and analytically central. The calibration issue is to identify what makes the alternatives genuinely distinct. If the analysis merely says “there are multiple views” without specifying the difference between them, Δ is too weak. If it treats every minor wording difference as a distinct theory, Δ is overextended.

**∇ — Impulse** is weak but present as structural pressure. Underdetermination is not only plurality; it is plurality under a pressure to decide. The impulse may be explanatory, interpretive, institutional, or methodological: one wants to know which theory is right, which explanation should be used, which interpretation should guide understanding. But the case material does not supply an agent, desire, urgency, or concrete decision demand. Therefore ∇ must not be psychologized. It is not anxiety, indecision, motivation, or will. It is only the directional pressure toward determination that arises once alternatives have been differentiated.

**□ — Frame** is active and indispensable. Underdetermination exists only relative to a frame that defines evidence, compatibility, relevance, and success. The same set of alternatives may be underdetermined in one frame and distinguishable in another. For example, a prediction frame, causal-explanation frame, hermeneutic frame, legal frame, or mathematical frame may set different criteria. The Core YAML therefore treats □ as a major operator. The calibration problem is that the frame must be explicit. Without a frame, underdetermination becomes vague uncertainty. If the frame is silently narrowed to force uniqueness, the analysis risks closure laundering.

**Λ — Non-Event** is central. The decisive event does not occur. No discriminating evidence arrives; no criterion selects one alternative; no interpretive result compels a single reading. This absence is not mere nothingness. It is structured absence inside a frame that expected or desired determination. In PMS terms, Λ marks a meaningful non-event: the expected determination fails to happen. The calibration issue is to identify precisely what is absent. Is it a measurement? a tie-breaker? a criterion? a future observation? a shared interpretive standard? The absence must be frame-relative and structurally relevant; it cannot simply be the fact that the prompt, archive, dataset, or analyst currently lacks more detail. If this is left vague, Λ becomes a rhetorical label for ignorance. If it is overread, every missing detail becomes a structural non-event.

**Α — Attractor** is weak and conditional. A single underdetermined comparison does not automatically produce a pattern. Α becomes relevant if underdetermination recurs across inquiry cycles, if benchmark regimes stabilize non-unique model selection, if schools repeatedly reproduce rival interpretations, or if institutional routines normalize selection despite persistent non-closure. In the abstract case, none of that is given. Therefore Α is not inactive in principle, but it is not strongly established. Overclaim would occur if the analysis treated underdetermination itself as a stabilized attractor without evidence of recurrence.

**Ω — Asymmetry** is not case-internally active. This is one of the most important boundary points. Underdetermination does not by itself create power, vulnerability, authority, or exposure asymmetry. If an institution certifies one model as “best,” if a court imposes a legal conclusion, if a benchmark privileges one theory, or if downstream users bear the cost of model choice, then Ω may become active. But none of those conditions appears in the abstract case. Therefore Ω must not be inferred from disagreement, plurality, uncertainty, or rival framing. To do so would turn an epistemic structure into a social or moral structure without warrant.

**Θ — Temporality** is weak to conditional. Underdetermination can be momentary: at this point, under this evidence, alternatives remain compatible. It can also become temporal if inquiry unfolds over time, if evidence is awaited, if standards harden, if a decision is delayed, or if non-closure becomes stable. The completed YAML preserves this distinction. Θ matters because future inquiry may change the frame, add evidence, or alter the compatibility relation. But without a concrete trajectory, Θ remains weak. The overclaim would be to describe the case as a historical process or responsibility trajectory when only an abstract relation has been given.

**Φ — Recontextualization** is active but must be carefully bounded. The same evidence can be placed into different theoretical or interpretive frames. In this sense, underdetermination is strongly connected to Φ: alternatives may be different ways of rendering the same evidence meaningful. But the YAML also warns against over-assimilation. Not every rival formalization is merely a PMS recontextualization. Some rival approaches may define evidence, theory, compatibility, or warrant in ways PMS does not fully capture. Φ is useful because it shows how evidence can travel across frames, but it must not domesticate every rival reading as just another internal PMS frame-shift.

**Χ — Distance** is active and central. The case requires reflective suspension. Χ prevents the analysis from forcing closure where Λ remains. It also prevents PMS itself from becoming a verdict machine. In underdetermination, distance is not a decorative virtue; it is a structural condition of valid application. Without Χ, the analyst may convert fit, coherence, elegance, institutional consensus, or PMS readability into a false determination. The calibration issue is that Χ must remain more than passivity. It is not refusal to decide forever. If later evidence genuinely discriminates, Χ permits revision. In the current case, however, it protects reversibility and prevents premature integration.

**Σ — Integration** is weak and constrained. Underdetermination is precisely the case where integration into one uniquely warranted theory is not established. PMS may integrate the problem-space: it can show how alternatives, evidence, frame, non-event, and distance relate. But that is not the same as integrating the alternatives into one correct answer. The Core YAML repeatedly warns that PMS coherence is not sufficient. A coherent PMS derivation can make the case legible without resolving it. Overclaim occurs if Σ is treated as proof that the PMS reading has captured the phenomenon completely or selected a theory uniquely.

**Ψ — Self-Binding** is weak and procedural only. The case material does not provide an agent or institution binding itself over time to a rule, standard, policy, theory, or commitment. Ψ appears only as the analysis’s own discipline: the artefact self-binds to non-closure, reversibility, and scope restraint. That is not a case-internal self-model. Strong Ψ would require an agent, community, institution, or practice that commits itself over time to a method of handling underdetermination. Without such material, Ψ cannot be used to derive responsibility, identity, maturity, or SELF_FIXPOINT.

The Core formula therefore remains minimal: **Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ Δ**. This formula says that underdetermination is best read as differentiated alternatives within a frame, a missing discriminating event or criterion, possible recontextualization among compatible readings, and reflective distance from premature closure. Weak or conditional operators may be discussed, but they must not be silently activated.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The dependency discipline matters because underdetermination is especially vulnerable to cheap use of later PMS operators. It is easy to move from “there are rival theories” to “there is a failure of integration,” or from “evidence does not decide” to “someone refuses responsibility,” or from “the analysis is coherent” to “PMS has resolved the problem.” The completed YAML blocks those shortcuts.

Later operators cannot be used cheaply because PMS dependencies are not decorative. Σ depends on Χ and Φ; Ψ depends on Σ, Θ, and Χ; Ω depends on stabilized patterning through Α; Θ depends on Ω and Α in the PMS dependency table. Even where an application uses reduced or compressed formulas, the dependency hygiene remains binding. The case can mention Ω, Θ, Σ, or Ψ as possible applied extensions, but it cannot treat them as fully established without the required structures.

Σ remains conditional because the case is about non-unique determination. The analysis may integrate the structure of the problem, but not the truth of the alternatives. That distinction is essential. Problem-space coherence does not equal theory-choice closure. PMS can say: here is the frame, here are the alternatives, here is the missing discriminator, here is why closure is not warranted. It cannot, from that coherence alone, say which alternative is correct.

Ψ remains even more conditional. Self-binding requires a temporally extended commitment to an integrated structure. No such commitment is part of the abstract case. The analysis may bind itself to methodological restraint, but that is application discipline rather than case content. To infer Ψ as responsibility, identity, or stable self-relation would be a dependency shortcut.

Coherence is not proof. This is one of the main quality-control lessons of the YAML. The PMS reading fits underdetermination very well: Δ, □, Λ, Φ, and Χ align strongly with the case. But precisely because the fit is smooth, there is a risk that PMS becomes under-discriminating. A grammar can map a structure elegantly and still fail to distinguish important subtypes: empirical equivalence, semantic ambiguity, hermeneutic plurality, temporary lack of evidence, principled non-identifiability, methodological pluralism, or Bayesian non-identifiability.

Non-capture is not failure. A rival formalization may preserve something PMS does not isolate sharply. Confirmation theory may better model evidential support; Bayesian epistemology may better model posterior distributions; model theory may better express equivalence classes; hermeneutics may better preserve interpretive plurality; decision theory may better handle action under unresolved alternatives. PMS does not need to defeat these approaches in order to be useful. Its task here is cartographic: to mark the closure-demand, the frame, and the Λ-residue.

PMS must not become a closure machine. The danger is not only that a theory chooses too quickly. The danger is that the meta-theory claims to have “solved” the problem by translating it. If the PMS article itself says that underdetermination is now resolved because it has been mapped, it commits the very drift the case warns against. The valid result is disciplined non-closure, not closure-by-operator.

Invalid dependency shortcuts include **Δ/∇ → Ψ**, where difference plus pressure becomes binding conclusion; **□ → Σ**, where a frame becomes a completed integration merely because it is explicit; **Λ → drift**, where absence is treated as failure; **Φ → capture**, where every rival reading is absorbed as recontextualization; and **Χ → neutrality**, where reflective distance becomes refusal to discriminate even if later evidence appears. The completed YAML carefully avoids all of these.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS drift-pattern analysis is deliberately conservative. It identifies possible structural risks only where the YAML warrants them; it does not convert underdetermination into a finding of drift.

**AD_A>>E**, the pattern of excessive distance between awareness and enactment, is not established. The abstract case may superficially resemble expanded awareness with delayed action: multiple alternatives remain visible, and no single determination occurs. But that resemblance is not enough. AD_A>>E requires more than plurality or suspended judgment. It requires a structural imbalance in which evaluative or interpretive awareness becomes over-expanded while action is suppressed or chronically delayed under conditions that make action relevant. The case material does not provide an action scene, an agent, an obligation to decide, or a stabilized pattern of delayed enactment. Therefore the axis effects remain under-specified rather than active: the awareness axis is **under-specified**, not over-expanded; the action axis is **under-specified**, not suppressed or chronically delayed. Non-assignment is not a low score; it is the correct result under the available material.

**AD_Sigma_low**, the pattern of fragmented integration, is also not established. Underdetermination involves weak or deferred integration, but that alone is not AD_Sigma_low. A case may lack unique integration because the evidence does not warrant it; that is not the same as fragmented praxis, volatile coherence, or responsibility inconsistency. AD_Sigma_low would require recontextualization outpacing integration in a way that destabilizes action or responsibility under asymmetrically loaded conditions. None of that is supplied. Therefore the coherence axis is **under-specified**, not volatile; the responsibility axis is **not applicable**, not inconsistent.

The main Core-level risk is not drift finding but coherence risk. PMS maps the case elegantly. That elegance can tempt the reader to treat PMS as having solved underdetermination. But the completed YAML explicitly blocks that conclusion. The PMS reading may become too smooth or under-discriminating if it fails to distinguish types of underdetermination: empirical equivalence, interpretive plurality, temporary ignorance, semantic ambiguity, methodological pluralism, and principled non-identifiability. These distinctions matter because different rival formalizations may capture them more precisely.

The under-discrimination risk is therefore central. PMS may correctly identify a frame, alternatives, a non-event, and reflective distance, while still not specifying the evidential relation with enough technical precision. That is not a failure of the case article; it is a boundary condition. AXIOM’s purpose is to show where closure is demanded and where Λ-residue remains visible, not to replace every domain-specific theory of confirmation, evidence, interpretation, or model choice.

Finally, absence is not drift. Missing evidence is not immaturity. Rival framing is not resistance. Disagreement is not failed integration. Non-fit is not pathology. The case’s structural discipline depends on preserving these differences. Drift patterns remain conditional risks for later applied scenes, not findings in the present conceptual projection.

This also prepares the transition to the LOGIC overlay. LOGIC may sharpen non-closure, boundary management, and drift-risk visibility, but it must not convert the Core result into stronger claims. The Core result remains what it is: a reversible PMS projection centered on Δ, □, Λ, Φ, and Χ, with later operators weak, conditional, or not applicable unless a concrete scene supplies the missing structures.

### Add-on / overlay reading

The relevant add-on is **PMS-LOGIC**. It is used here as an overlay / application profile only. It does not redefine PMS operators, does not change PMS dependencies, does not replace PMS Core, and does not introduce a new base grammar. Its role is to sharpen what PMS Core already made visible: underdetermination is a structured non-closure condition inside a frame, and logical coherence must not be treated as unique determination.

PMS Core is already able to model the basic case: differentiated alternatives under Δ, an evidential or interpretive frame under □, the missing discriminating event under Λ, rival recontextualizations under Φ, and reflective distance under Χ. The reason PMS Core is not the final layer here is that underdetermination is specifically a logic-facing case. It concerns the boundary between evidence and determination, between coherence and warrant, between model fit and uniqueness, between interpretation and finality. PMS-LOGIC therefore adds precision not by adding new operators, but by naming the boundary-management risks characteristic of reasoning itself.

The LOGIC overlay is especially relevant because underdetermination often appears where reasoning practices are tempted to overstate themselves. A theory fits the data, a model performs well, an interpretation is coherent, or a benchmark produces a ranking. These are not trivial achievements. But PMS-LOGIC insists that such achievements do not automatically become necessity, uniqueness, or final authority. The overlay makes visible the point at which logical ordering remains useful while final closure remains unwarranted.

#### Pre-moral justification layer

The first LOGIC layer is the **pre-moral level of justification**. In this context, “pre-moral” does not mean morally superior, morally prior in a value hierarchy, or ethically neutral in any global sense. It means structurally legible before moral readability, responsibility, blame, guilt, duty, or ought-claims are introduced.

For underdetermination, this layer is active. The case first appears as a structural configuration: multiple alternatives are differentiated, evidence is placed inside a frame, and a discriminating result is absent. The reduced LOGIC shorthand here is **PRE_MORAL_justification**, associated with **Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α**. In full PMS dependency terms, this shorthand must remain subordinate to the Core operator discipline. It does not replace PMS dependencies and does not make Α strongly active where recurrence is not shown.

At this pre-moral level, Δ marks the plurality of compatible theories, explanations, or interpretations. □ marks the evidential or interpretive frame within which compatibility is assessed. Λ marks the meaningful absence of the discriminating event, criterion, observation, or interpretive result that would select one alternative. ∇ remains weak because the case contains structural pressure toward determination, but no concrete agentive demand to decide. Α remains weak because the material does not show recurrence, stabilization, or path-dependent repetition.

This means that the pre-moral layer makes underdetermination legible without importing blame, duty, guilt, failure, or moralized evaluation. The fact that evidence does not uniquely determine a theory is not itself a defect of persons, institutions, or rationality. It is a structural condition that must be handled before any applied responsibility question could arise.

#### Logical boundary management

The central LOGIC layer for this case is **logical boundary management**. The active reduced signature is **LOGIC_boundary_management**, expressed in the overlay as **□ ∘ Λ ∘ Χ ∘ Σ**. This signature is reduced shorthand only. It does not mean that Χ and Σ can be used without their PMS dependencies. It means that, for LOGIC purposes, these four elements are the most visible handles: frame, non-closure, reflective distance, and constrained integration.

In underdetermination, □ organizes evidence and compatible alternatives. It determines what counts as relevant data, admissible interpretation, explanatory success, model fit, or theoretical adequacy. Λ marks the absent discriminator: the frame does not yield a unique selection. Χ prevents forced closure. It blocks the move from “this account is coherent” to “this account is uniquely determined.” Σ remains constrained. It may coordinate a workable account, but it must not become totalizing closure.

The LOGIC aphorism that fits this case is: **logic is the beginning of everything, not the end**. Logic can order alternatives, expose inconsistency, define admissibility, and show what follows from a frame. But logic cannot manufacture a missing discriminating event merely by being orderly. Where Λ persists, the proper logical result may be sustainable non-assertion rather than final assertion.

This is why the overlay does not treat underdetermination as a failure of logic. The case shows a limit condition for logical ordering. Evidence, alternatives, and frame remain coherent; what is absent is unique determination. The boundary is reached where available evidence and accepted criteria do not select one alternative. Λ is carried as sustainable non-closure inside □, while Χ blocks closure-drive and prevents Σ from becoming a substitute for missing discrimination.

#### Post-moral effects

The **post-moral effects** layer is not applicable in this case. PMS-LOGIC treats moral readability as emerging only where praxis unfolds under asymmetry, temporality, and responsibility-attributability conditions. The abstract underdetermination case supplies none of these. There is no concrete praxis scene, no agent, no effective capacity, no consequence field, no asymmetry, no temporal responsibility trajectory, and no self-binding responsibility.

This negative result matters. The case has Λ, but Λ alone does not generate moral readability. Missing determination is not guilt. Non-closure is not blame. Rival interpretation is not evasion. Failure to uniquely select a theory is not non-innocence. The overlay therefore preserves the distinction between logical non-closure and moral readability.

The LOGIC claim that responsibility is attribution rather than ought is relevant here primarily as a boundary. No responsibility attribution follows. No obligation is derived. No one is told what they ought to believe, choose, enforce, or accept. If a later concrete scene introduced an institution that selected a model under underdetermination and exposed downstream parties to risk, then responsibility-readability might become relevant. But that would be a new scene with additional PMS structures, not an implication of the present case.

#### Non-closure

The strongest LOGIC contribution is the treatment of non-closure as a structural strength. Underdetermination is not interpreted as a hole to be covered with dogma, authority, or metaphysical compensation. It is treated as a disciplined stopping condition. Where evidence does not determine one alternative, the analysis carries Λ rather than suppressing it.

This does not mean inquiry stops in the practical sense. A researcher may continue gathering evidence. A historian may search for more documents. A model evaluator may define a decision criterion. A policy body may decide under uncertainty. But those are additional actions under additional frames. The LOGIC reading distinguishes such operational continuation from logical finality. The fact that practice may proceed does not mean the theory-evidence relation has been uniquely closed.

The active reduced signature **NON_CLOSURE_stability** captures this. Its shorthand involves **Δ, □, Λ, Α**. For this case, Δ, □, and Λ are strong; Α remains weak unless recurrence or stabilization is shown. This means the case supports sustainable non-closure without yet proving a stabilized attractor. Non-closure can be held as a boundary even if no long-term inquiry routine is described.

#### Effective capacity, non-innocence, and responsibility without ought

Effective capacity is not applicable. The case has no actor, no authority, no means, no access, no temporal window, and no consequence field. PMS-LOGIC treats effective capacity as frame-bound capacity plus temporal availability. An evidential frame alone is insufficient. The fact that an analyst can describe underdetermination does not make them responsible for resolving it. The fact that alternatives remain open does not create a duty to choose.

Non-innocence is also not applicable. PMS-LOGIC uses non-innocence for situations in which consequences become attributable even without intent, command, guilt, or blame. That requires a consequence field, asymmetry, temporality, and attribution structure. The abstract underdetermination case has none of these. Λ here is not an omission by an agent; it is an evidential or logical boundary. It must not be confused with guilt, blame, responsibility, or failure.

Responsibility without ought is therefore active only as a guardrail distinction. The overlay keeps responsibility separate from prescription. It refuses to derive an ought from the structural fact that evidence underdetermines theory. The case does not say: one ought to suspend judgment, one ought to choose pluralism, one ought to accept all alternatives, or one ought to avoid decision forever. It says only that, under the supplied material, unique determination is not structurally warranted, and any decision beyond that would require additional frame declarations.

#### Reduced signatures

The active LOGIC reduced signatures are **LOGIC_boundary_management**, **NON_CLOSURE_stability**, and **PRE_MORAL_justification**.

**LOGIC_boundary_management** is the primary overlay signature. It captures the central configuration: frame-bound evidence, persistent Λ, reflective distance, and constrained integration. In this case, it prevents coherence from becoming authority. It says that a model, explanation, or interpretation may be integrated enough to be usable without being uniquely determined.

**NON_CLOSURE_stability** is active with a weak Α component. The case demonstrates that non-closure can be carried without collapse. The inquiry can remain intelligible even when no unique selection occurs. However, since the abstract material does not show repeated cycles, institutional stabilization, or path-dependent routines, the attractor element remains weak.

**PRE_MORAL_justification** is active as a structural legibility layer. It shows how underdetermination appears before moral, legal, diagnostic, or responsibility claims are introduced. The structure is: alternatives become visible, the frame makes them comparable, pressure toward determination arises, and the missing discriminator appears as Λ. This layer does not generate normativity.

The non-applicable or weak signatures are equally important. **POST_MORAL_effects** is not applicable. **MORAL_READABILITY_min** is not applicable because Ω, Θ, and Ψ are not established. **POTENTIAL_effective** is not applicable because no effective capacity is supplied. **HUME_KNOT_reconstruction** is weak only as boundary hygiene: the case preserves the is/ought boundary by refusing to derive normative demand from underdetermination.

#### Boundary constructs and non-operator handles

PMS-LOGIC contains several non-operator constructs. They are overlay handles, not PMS operators. In this case, the relevant handles are logic as ordering tool, responsibility versus ought, apparent norms as constraints, non-closure, and failure-mode constraints.

“Logic” here is not a new operator. It names a procedural discipline of consistency, derivation, and closure-drive operating within frames. In the underdetermination case, logic orders the relation between evidence and alternatives, but it reaches a boundary at Λ. Χ keeps the closure-drive reversible, and Σ is limited to problem-space coherence.

“Morality as readability field” is not applicable. The case does not become morally readable because Ω, Θ, and Ψ are not active responsibility structures. This prevents the overlay from treating unresolved evidence as ethical failure.

“Responsibility versus ought” is active as a distinction. The case does not attribute responsibility and does not derive prescriptions. It uses the distinction negatively: unresolved evidence is not a demand, accusation, or command.

“Apparent norms as constraints” is weakly relevant only to application discipline. Statements such as “do not force closure” are not moral commandments. They are PMS-LOGIC constraints against structural failure: forced closure where Λ persists produces instability, dogmatism, or authority-smuggling.

#### Failure-mode constraints

The most relevant failure-mode constraint is that **forced closure where Λ persists produces structural instability**. This is active. If a theory, interpretation, or model is declared uniquely determined despite the missing discriminating event, closure has been manufactured rather than earned. Such forced closure may appear as dogma, false certainty, metaphysical compensation, or authority laundering.

A second active failure-mode constraint is that **explanatory power converted into sense-making authority causes structural overreach**. A coherent explanation may be strong. It may be the best currently available. It may be useful for coordination. But explanatory success does not confer authority over all rival readings. In this case, LOGIC prevents the move from “this explanation works” to “this explanation is uniquely warranted.”

A third active constraint is that **stable self-binding takes the form of restraint rather than assertion**. The artefact binds itself to non-closure. It does not command others to accept a theory, reject a theory, or treat PMS as decisive. This is a methodological self-binding, not a case-internal Ψ finding.

Other failure-mode constraints remain weak or not applicable. Boundary occupation as authority is weakly relevant, because the abstract case has no agent occupying the boundary. Proximity-generated power is not applicable because there is no proximity relation or concrete asymmetry.

#### LOGIC drift risks

The primary LOGIC drift risk is **dogmatism / Σ-totalization**. This occurs when integration is forced into total closure, treating coherence as final truth rather than provisional coordination. In underdetermination, this risk appears whenever a coherent theory, elegant explanation, successful model, or persuasive interpretation is treated as uniquely determined merely because it integrates the available evidence well. The risk is active as a risk, not as a finding.

A second active risk is **Λ-suppression**. This occurs when the missing discriminator is flattened into irrelevance or ignored. In underdetermination, Λ-suppression may appear when analysts say “nothing more is needed,” even though the frame has not supplied a unique selector. It may also appear in the opposite direction, where Λ is treated as nihilistic emptiness rather than structured absence. Both moves misread the non-event.

A third active risk is **□-overconstraint**, or technocratic reduction. This occurs when the frame is treated as exhaustive reality. In underdetermination, a benchmark, archive, dataset, metric, or formal system may be treated as if it contains all relevant conditions. The result is that what falls outside the frame is not preserved as non-capture but dismissed as non-existent or irrelevant. PMS-LOGIC blocks this by requiring explicit frame boundaries and rival openness.

**Ω-inflation / authority capture** is a weak risk. Ω is not active in the abstract case, but it could become active if an authority declares one model, interpretation, or theory uniquely valid and suppresses alternatives. This is a possible applied risk, not a present finding.

**Moralism / pseudo-ought injection** is also weak. It would appear if underdetermination were translated into a demand: one must accept pluralism, one must reject closure, one must obey the chosen model, or one must treat disagreement as a moral defect. The overlay prevents this by keeping ought-statements out of scope.

#### Counter-readings

Several counter-readings are preserved.

The first says that this collapses into relativism. PMS-LOGIC responds by distinguishing non-closure from arbitrariness. The frame still matters. Evidence still constrains. Alternatives must remain compatible with the evidence. The absence of unique determination does not imply that every alternative is equal.

The second says that this is nihilism with extra steps. PMS-LOGIC responds that Λ is not meaninglessness. It is structured absence inside a frame. The missing discriminator matters because the frame expects or desires determination. Non-closure is therefore meaningful, not empty.

The third says that this is hidden metaphysics or theology. PMS-LOGIC responds by refusing metaphysical filling. It does not introduce an external ground, final lawgiver, ultimate justification, or hidden metaphysical structure. It carries non-closure rather than compensating for it.

The fourth says that the analysis cannot guide action. PMS-LOGIC accepts that it does not prescribe action. It orients by constraining overreach. Guidance is replaced by boundary discipline: state the frame, carry Λ, preserve Χ, and avoid Σ-totalization.

The fifth says that PMS wins by translation rather than superiority. This counter-reading is active and important. The overlay does not claim that translation is victory. It preserves rival formalisms and asks whether PMS adds useful boundary visibility. It does not claim final framework superiority.

#### Final LOGIC overlay signature

The final LOGIC overlay signature is:

**LOGIC_boundary_management + NON_CLOSURE_stability + PRE_MORAL_justification**

This means that the underdetermination case is best read, at the overlay level, as a logically structured boundary condition where evidence and alternatives remain frame-bound, Λ remains visible, and Χ prevents coherence from becoming authority. The overlay does not add a new solution. It clarifies why the Core result must remain reversible, non-totalizing, and resistant to premature closure.

What remains PMS Core is the case’s basic operator configuration: Δ differentiates alternatives, □ frames evidence and relevance, Λ marks the absent discriminator, Φ permits rival recontextualizations, and Χ maintains reflective non-closure. ### Example 1 — Two explanations for the same small dataset

**Type:** compact
**Recommended insertion point:** after **Scene and frame structure** or early in **PMS Core structural reading**

A small research team studies a bounded set of observations: several plants in a controlled classroom experiment grew at different rates. The available records include watering schedule, approximate light exposure, pot size, soil type, and weekly height measurements. Two explanations remain compatible with the recorded evidence. One explanation treats light exposure as the dominant factor; the other treats soil variation as the dominant factor. The available data do not decisively select between them, because the classroom setup did not independently vary light and soil in a way that would discriminate the two explanations.

The example demonstrates underdetermination in a deliberately modest form. It does not claim that the real cause is unknowable, that science fails, or that all explanations are equally good. It only shows a framed case in which the available evidence supports more than one explanatory continuation. The closure-demand is the wish to say: “This is the explanation.” The structural limit is that the frame does not yet contain the discriminating event, measurement, or criterion that would justify that unique selection.

The PMS Core hooks are straightforward. **Δ** appears in the difference between two candidate explanations. **□** is the classroom-experiment frame: what was measured, what was controlled, and what counts as evidence. **Λ** appears as the missing discriminating observation: the experiment did not isolate light from soil variation. **Φ** is available because the same observations can be recontextualized into different explanatory accounts. **Χ** is required because the team must not treat one coherent explanation as uniquely determined merely because it is convenient or narratively clear. **Σ** remains weak: the team can integrate the problem-space, but it cannot yet integrate the evidence into one uniquely warranted theory. **Ψ** is not active unless the team later binds itself to a reporting standard, a research protocol, or a declared model-selection rule over time.

The LOGIC overlay sharpens the example by naming the boundary-management problem. The active LOGIC signature is **LOGIC_boundary_management**: the frame, the missing discriminator, reflective distance, and constrained integration must be held together. **NON_CLOSURE_stability** is also visible in a weak form: the inquiry can remain stable without forcing a final explanation. The drift risks are risks only. A report that says “soil caused the growth difference” because that explanation is simpler may risk **Σ-totalization** if it treats coherent integration as final truth. A report that treats the classroom measurements as exhaustive may risk **□-overconstraint**. But those are not findings about the team; they are structural warnings about possible misuse of the reasoning.

MIP, AHP, and QC are not activated. If the example were turned into a school policy, grading decision, product claim, or institutional benchmark, a governance or claim-hardening layer might become relevant. In the vignette as stated, it remains an epistemic illustration only.

The forbidden claims are important. The example does not prove anything about botany, scientific realism, researcher competence, or actual causality. It does not diagnose the team as indecisive. It does not show that evidence is useless. It does not validate PMS Base. It only demonstrates a bounded structure: evidence can be usable while still not being uniquely determining.

The non-capture note is that a philosophy of science, experimental-design, or Bayesian account may describe the evidential relation more precisely than PMS. PMS is useful here because it marks the closure-demand and the Λ-residue, not because it replaces statistical or methodological analysis.

The article takeaway is that underdetermination is not failure by itself. A disciplined analysis can keep the inquiry coherent, preserve the missing discriminator, and avoid premature closure without collapsing into “anything goes.”

### Example 2 — An ambiguous historical letter

**Type:** compact
**Recommended insertion point:** after **PMS dependency and calibration discipline** or within **Non-capture and rival readings**

A historian finds a short letter in an archive. The letter says that a local official “understood what had to be done” before a controversial administrative decision was made. One interpretation reads the phrase as evidence of prior approval. Another reads it as a vague acknowledgment of procedural pressure. A third treats it as formulaic bureaucratic language that should not be over-weighted. The surrounding documents confirm that the decision happened, but they do not clearly show what the official intended, authorized, or understood.

The vignette demonstrates underdetermination in an interpretive rather than experimental register. The evidence is not empty. The letter exists, the phrase is real, and the administrative context is relevant. But the phrase does not determine one interpretation by itself. The closure-demand is the desire to convert an ambiguous trace into a single historical explanation. The structural problem is that the available context does not yet decide which interpretive frame should dominate.

The PMS Core hooks show why the case must remain bounded. **Δ** distinguishes the rival interpretations: approval, procedural acknowledgment, and formulaic language. **□** is the archival-interpretive frame, including genre conventions, document chronology, institutional context, and evidentiary standards. **Λ** is the absent clarifying event: there is no explicit follow-up, testimony, annotation, or corroborating document that settles the phrase’s force. **Φ** is central because the same sentence changes meaning when placed into different frames: political intent, bureaucratic routine, institutional pressure, or rhetorical formula. **Χ** prevents the historian from turning a preferred interpretive integration into a verdict. **Σ** can produce a careful interpretive account, but that account remains conditional unless further evidence discriminates. **Ω** is not automatically active merely because one interpretation is more politically charged; it would become active only if an institution used the interpretation to assign blame, status, or authority. **Ψ** is not active unless a research community, publication, or institution binds itself to a settled interpretation over time.

The LOGIC overlay is especially useful here because it distinguishes non-closure from interpretive laziness. The phrase can be read in several ways not because interpretation is arbitrary, but because the evidence-frame does not force one continuation. The active LOGIC emphasis is **sustainable non-closure**: the historian may state that several readings remain admissible under the current archive. **LOGIC_boundary_management** prevents the article from saying “this must mean approval” merely because that reading creates a cleaner narrative. The relevant drift risks are **Σ-totalization**, where a coherent story becomes treated as final, and **Λ-suppression**, where the missing corroboration is ignored because it interrupts the desired explanation.

MIP and AHP remain inactive. They would become relevant only if the interpretation were used in a public accusation, institutional report, museum claim, legal inquiry, or reputational judgment. As an academic example, it requires interpretive caution, not governance scoring.

The forbidden claims are clear. The example does not determine the official’s intention. It does not assign guilt, complicity, innocence, or moral status. It does not claim that historical inquiry cannot reach conclusions. It does not treat archival ambiguity as proof of metaphysical indeterminacy. It also does not turn rival historians into drift cases or maturity failures.

The non-capture note matters because hermeneutics, discourse analysis, archival method, and historiography may preserve features that PMS does not fully model. PMS marks the structure of frame-bound non-closure; it does not replace domain expertise about genre, institutional language, or historical context.

The article takeaway is that underdetermination can be interpretively responsible. A strong reading may still preserve alternatives, name the missing discriminator, and refuse to convert narrative coherence into historical certainty.

### Example 3 — A benchmark that does not settle model choice

**Type:** compact
**Recommended insertion point:** in **Add-on / overlay reading**, especially after the LOGIC discussion of reduced signatures and drift risks

A research group compares two predictive models on a shared benchmark. Model A performs slightly better on average accuracy. Model B performs slightly better on robustness under one stress test and is easier to interpret. The benchmark documentation does not specify whether average performance, robustness, interpretability, latency, or failure transparency should dominate. Both models can be defended as “best” under different success criteria. The benchmark therefore supports comparison, but it does not by itself determine a unique model choice.

The example demonstrates the LOGIC-specific form of underdetermination. There is a frame, but the frame contains several possible success criteria. The evidence is not meaningless: the benchmark results show differences. Yet those differences do not automatically select one model unless the evaluation frame declares which criterion carries priority. The closure-demand is the desire to move from “these are the results” to “this is the best model.” The missing structure is not necessarily more data; it may be an explicit criterion for what kind of success is being selected.

The PMS Core hooks are visible throughout. **Δ** distinguishes Model A, Model B, and their performance profiles. **□** is the benchmark frame: dataset, metrics, stress tests, admissible comparison rules, and evaluation purpose. **Λ** appears because no frame-internal tie-breaker determines which success criterion should dominate. **Α** may appear weakly if the benchmark becomes a repeated selection routine. **Ω** is not automatically present, but it becomes relevant if a lab, company, institution, or regulator certifies one model as “best” and downstream users bear the risk. **Θ** becomes relevant if the choice locks in over time through deployment, documentation, infrastructure, or procurement. **Φ** appears when the same result is recontextualized: a research setting may value interpretability, a deployment setting may value robustness, and a leaderboard may value average score. **Χ** is essential: the comparison must not treat metric superiority as universal superiority. **Σ** can integrate the criteria into a decision package, but only after the frame declares how trade-offs are handled. **Ψ** becomes active only if the organization binds itself to the chosen standard, model, or evaluation rule.

The LOGIC overlay is highly relevant. The active reduced signature is **LOGIC_boundary_management**: □ defines the evaluation frame, Λ marks the missing unique discriminator, Χ prevents authority-smuggling, and Σ must remain constrained. **NON_CLOSURE_stability** is also relevant because a benchmark can support stable comparison without producing final determination. **PRE_MORAL_justification** applies only as structural legibility before normative or governance claims; it does not say what the group ought to deploy.

The main LOGIC drift risks are present as risks, not findings. **Technocratic reduction / □-overconstraint** would occur if the benchmark were treated as the whole reality of model quality. **Σ-totalization** would occur if an integrated leaderboard result were treated as final truth. **Authority capture / Ω-inflation** would occur if certification power converted “best under this metric” into “best without qualification.” These risks are not accusations against the research group; they are boundary warnings for how benchmark language can overreach.

MIP and AHP are not activated in the vignette as stated. They would become relevant if the benchmark result were used for procurement, public deployment, safety certification, institutional policy, or high-stakes governance. AHP would remain second-order claim-hardening only: it would not rescore the models, diagnose actors, or transform the benchmark into a verdict machine.

The forbidden claims are explicit. The example does not claim that either model is actually better, safer, fairer, or more truthful. It does not validate a benchmark methodology. It does not make an implementation claim about any real system. It does not rank researchers or users. It does not prove that model choice is arbitrary.

The non-capture note is that statistical evaluation, ML benchmarking methodology, decision theory, and domain-specific risk analysis may be needed to make an actual model choice. PMS-LOGIC does not replace those methods. It identifies the closure-demand and prevents benchmark coherence from being mistaken for universal determination.

The article takeaway is that underdetermination often survives inside apparently precise evaluation systems. A benchmark can be useful, disciplined, and repeatable while still failing to justify a unique conclusion unless the frame declares what kind of success is being privileged.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The non-capture discipline is central to this case. Underdetermination may be readable through PMS and sharpened through PMS-LOGIC, but that does not mean PMS or LOGIC exhausts it. The completed YAML explicitly preserves rival readings and rival formalizations. This is not a weakness of the article; it is part of the case’s structural honesty.

One rival reading is **epistemological**. On this reading, underdetermination concerns the relation between evidence and theory. The relevant questions are about confirmation, warrant, empirical equivalence, explanatory virtues, and inference to the best explanation. PMS can map the closure-demand, but it does not replace epistemology. A confirmation-theoretic account may more precisely distinguish support, probability, evidential relevance, and non-unique confirmation.

A second rival reading is **model-theoretic or formal**. Some forms of underdetermination may be better described through equivalence classes, formal models, semantic structures, or non-identifiability. PMS does not itself provide the mathematics of model equivalence. It can mark the frame and the missing discriminator, but it does not generate the formal proof that two models are equivalent or empirically indistinguishable.

A third rival reading is **Bayesian or probabilistic**. Under this view, evidence may not determine a single theory, but it may shift probabilities among alternatives. This rival reading preserves degrees of support rather than binary determination. PMS can identify the non-closure condition, but Bayesian methods may better express graded confidence, priors, likelihoods, posterior distributions, and decision thresholds.

A fourth rival reading is **hermeneutic**. In interpretive cases, underdetermination may not be a defect of evidence but a feature of meaning, context, genre, and historically situated interpretation. Multiple readings may remain admissible because texts, symbols, or practices are not exhausted by one frame. PMS can model Φ and Λ, but hermeneutics may better preserve the depth of interpretive plurality and the productive role of context.

A fifth rival reading is **language-practice-oriented**. Under this approach, underdetermination may arise because terms, rules, or meanings are stabilized by use rather than fixed by evidence alone. The issue is not simply missing data but the open texture of language and practice. PMS can map frame-dependence and recontextualization, but it does not replace ordinary-language philosophy, pragmatics, or rule-following analysis.

A sixth rival reading is **decision-theoretic**. In applied contexts, the problem may not be which theory is uniquely determined, but how to act when several remain compatible with available evidence. Decision theory may preserve the difference between epistemic non-closure and practical choice under uncertainty. PMS Core and LOGIC do not prescribe action. They can identify the structural boundary that decision theory would then have to handle.

An anti-capture reading is also preserved. This reading says that PMS translation may itself be too smooth. Perhaps underdetermination is not primarily a praxis structure, but a formal or epistemic relation. Perhaps the relevant issue is not closure-demand but truth-conditional support. Perhaps PMS is useful for the discourse around underdetermination but less precise for underdetermination itself. The article accepts this possibility. Translation into PMS is not victory over rival grammars.

These rival readings preserve important distinctions. Epistemology preserves warrant. Formal theory preserves equivalence and structure. Bayesianism preserves graded support. Hermeneutics preserves contextual plurality. Language-practice analysis preserves use and rule-following. Decision theory preserves action under unresolved alternatives. PMS and LOGIC contribute a cartographic layer: they identify frame, closure-demand, Λ-residue, boundary discipline, and misuse risk. They do not replace every domain-specific account.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS reading would be weakened if the alternatives were not genuinely distinct. If the supposed rival theories, explanations, or interpretations are merely terminological variants, then Δ is insufficiently established. Underdetermination requires real differentiation among alternatives.

The PMS reading would also be weakened if no explicit frame could be specified. Without □, there is no stable account of what counts as evidence, compatibility, relevance, or determination. The case would then be vague plurality rather than underdetermination.

A third weakening condition concerns Λ. If no structured expectation of discrimination exists, then the missing event is not a PMS non-event. Mere lack of detail in the prompt, archive, dataset, or analyst’s knowledge is not enough. Λ must be frame-relative and structurally relevant. It must mark a missing discriminator that matters inside the evidence-and-alternative frame.

The reading would become under-discriminating if it failed to distinguish empirical equivalence, interpretive plurality, temporary ignorance, semantic ambiguity, methodological pluralism, and principled non-identifiability. These are not interchangeable. A PMS mapping that collapses them into one generic Λ structure would be too coarse.

The reading would become over-assimilative if every rival formalization, disagreement, absence, or non-fit were translated into PMS terms such as Φ, incomplete Σ, drift, maturity failure, or calibration residue. Rival readings may mark genuine limits of PMS capture. They must not be domesticated merely because PMS can describe some of their surface structure.

The reading would be overextended if it inferred Ω, Responsibility_R, Dignity-in-Practice as a case-internal axis, SELF_FIXPOINT, or drift patterns without a concrete scene involving asymmetry, temporality, obligation, or self-binding. This is a major boundary condition. Abstract underdetermination does not license responsibility, action, post-moral effects, or person-level evaluation.

The reading would fail as a PMS application if Χ were suspended. If the analysis forces closure, ranks rival theories by authority, claims PMS superiority, treats coherence as sufficient evidence of capture, or uses underdetermination to shame disagreement, then it violates its own application gate.

The LOGIC overlay would be weakened if its reduced signatures were treated as new PMS operators. LOGIC is an overlay, not a base grammar. It adds boundary visibility but does not change the PMS operator inventory or dependencies.

The LOGIC overlay would also be weakened if it converted non-closure into a doctrine. LOGIC does not say that closure is never possible. It says that where Λ persists inside the declared frame, closure must not be manufactured. If later evidence or a revised frame supplies a genuine discriminator, the analysis must remain revisable.

The overlay would become under-discriminating if every case of uncertainty were called underdetermination. A lack of information, a vague question, a poorly designed inquiry, a temporary evidence gap, and principled non-uniqueness are not the same. LOGIC must preserve these distinctions.

The overlay would become over-assimilative if it treated every successful model-selection rule as dogmatism. Operational choice is not drift by itself. Drift appears only when a choice is misrepresented as unique determination, authority, finality, or frame-exhaustive truth.

Operationalization remains unfulfilled. Stronger empirical use would require a specified evidence set, specified alternatives, compatibility criteria, discriminating tests, and a method for deciding whether evidence actually underdetermines the alternatives. Comparative use would require rival formalizations and explicit criteria for assessing which grammar preserves more discriminative force. Computational use would require formal inputs, model-selection procedures, validation protocols, and architecture-specific constraints. Legal use would require legal standards, burdens of proof, admissibility rules, and procedural context. Clinical use is not warranted and would require an entirely different evidentiary and ethical frame. Governance use would require a downstream artefact, affected roles, stakes, contest channels, and accountability requirements.

The operationalization requirements are therefore named but not fulfilled. The present article remains a conceptual and structural rendering, not an empirical test, formal proof, implementation, legal standard, governance policy, or diagnostic tool.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It does not identify cognitive defects, indecision, pathology, immaturity, evasion, or failure. A person or group holding multiple interpretations open is not thereby diagnosed.

It must not be used for person-ranking. ### Case conclusion

The integrative result is that underdetermination can be rendered as a structured closure-demand without being solved by closure. PMS Core identifies the minimal configuration: differentiated alternatives under Δ, an evidential or interpretive frame under □, a missing discriminator under Λ, possible recontextualizations under Φ, and reflective distance under Χ. The later operators remain weak, conditional, or not applicable unless a concrete scene supplies recurrence, asymmetry, temporality, integration, action, responsibility, or self-binding.

The LOGIC overlay sharpens this Core result. It shows that underdetermination is not merely a lack of information but a logical boundary-management problem: evidence and alternatives may remain coherent inside a frame while unique determination remains absent. The primary overlay signature is **LOGIC_boundary_management + NON_CLOSURE_stability + PRE_MORAL_justification**. This signature does not add a new answer. It makes the Core restraint more visible: carry Λ, preserve □, maintain Χ, and prevent Σ from becoming authority.

The examples contribute readability, not evidence. The plant dataset shows that ordinary empirical explanation can remain underdetermined without making science arbitrary. The historical letter shows that interpretive evidence may support multiple readings without implying laziness, guilt, or relativism. The benchmark example shows that apparently precise evaluation systems can still fail to determine a unique model choice unless the frame declares which success criterion dominates. Each example demonstrates the analysis; none expands the claim boundary.

What remains unclaimed is as important as what is claimed. The article does not prove that any actual theory is underdetermined. It does not decide a metaphysical question. It does not diagnose persons or communities. It does not rank interpreters or models. It does not provide a legal standard, governance policy, clinical category, or implementation procedure. It does not validate PMS Base or prove LOGIC superiority over rival formalisms.

The boundary discipline is therefore the case’s central achievement. Coherence is not proof. Non-closure is not failure. Rival readings are not defects. Missing evidence is not drift. Disagreement is not immaturity. Operational choice is not automatically dogmatism, but operational choice becomes risky when it is misrepresented as unique determination. PMS and LOGIC remain useful only while they preserve these distinctions.

The final methodological takeaway is that underdetermination should be mapped as a frame-bound Λ-residue under closure pressure, not repaired by authority. AXIOM’s value is cartographic: it shows where closure is demanded, where the frame does and does not support that demand, what risks arise when closure is forced, and where non-capture must remain visible. In this case, the strongest result is not a solution to underdetermination, but a disciplined way of refusing to mistake usable coherence for final determination.
