## Oracle-Asymmetry Audit (Audit Trace)

### Case capsule

**Case file(s):** `PMS_CORE_11_4_2.yaml`, `PMS_QC_11_4_2.yaml`, `PMS_QC_EXT_11_4_2.yaml`
**Stack:** PMS Core → PMS-QC overlay → PMS-QC-EXT optional refinement overlay
**Case type:** Conceptual domain projection / structural QC audit projection for oracle-like quantum workflow components
**Concrete-scene status:** Not a concrete praxis scene; no specific circuit, DAG, macro-program, oracle interface, trace log, measurement result, backend, register layout, stakeholder role, validation protocol, or failure event is supplied.
**Output type:** Audit trace, with boundary and non-capture controls
**Add-on decision:** PMS Core is necessary but not the final layer because the case explicitly concerns quantum-computational workflow structure. PMS-QC is justified as a domain overlay. PMS-QC-EXT is optional and weak: it adds paper-specific refinement checks for QPE-like ladders, Grover-like centering, attractor schedules, measurement-domain projection, and basis-reframe policy, but no EXT construct is activated as an operative case formula.
**MIP / AHP / QC status:** MIP not invoked; AHP not invoked; PMS-QC invoked as generic QC overlay; PMS-QC-EXT invoked only as optional, weak, paper-specific refinement audit.
**Claim boundary:** Structural, reversible, non-diagnostic, non-metaphysical, non-legal-forensic, non-clinical, non-implementation-validating, non-person-ranking, non-PMS-Base-validating. The analysis maps framed directionality, oracle asymmetry, possible trace residues, weak integration, and optional QC overlay risks; it does not prove physical correctness, quantum-mechanical adequacy, safety, convergence, legal sufficiency, governance validity, or deployment readiness.

### Why this case matters

The Oracle-Asymmetry Audit case belongs in AXIOM because it represents a modern form of closure-demand that is neither classically metaphysical nor merely technical. A quantum workflow containing oracle-like components produces a particular pressure: the workflow contains something that acts directionally, asymmetrically, and often opaquely, yet the analyst or auditor is asked to say what this means, whether it is traceable, whether it is legitimate, whether the asymmetry is structurally controlled, and whether the resulting workflow can be rendered intelligible.

The closure-demand is therefore not simply “what is the oracle?” or “does the circuit compute correctly?” It is more precise: can a strongly directional black-box or control component be mapped in a way that preserves frame, temporality, asymmetry, non-events, and audit distance without pretending to solve the quantum, implementation, or legal questions? PMS-AXIOM is designed for precisely this kind of case. It does not replace the domain formalism; it maps the structural demand that arises when a domain formalism becomes the site of closure pressure.

Oracle-like components are structurally difficult because they sit at the intersection of several closure temptations. A black-box function invites the temptation to treat opacity as meaning. A marking or phase-kickback primitive invites the temptation to treat directional influence as explanatory authority. A controlled unitary invites the temptation to treat control as responsibility or agency. A measurement mapping invites the temptation to treat outcome resolution as complete interpretive closure. A hybrid DAG or macro program invites the temptation to treat traceability as validation. Each of these moves may be useful within a technical frame, but PMS requires that the frame be declared and that the claim be bounded.

The case is also difficult because the word “oracle” already carries an ambiguity. In quantum computing, the oracle can be a formal component, a black-box query function, a problem-specific operation, a phase-marking primitive, a controlled unitary, or a macro-level placeholder in an algorithmic workflow. In ordinary language, an oracle suggests authority or hidden knowledge. PMS must not collapse these meanings. The PMS reading therefore has to maintain distance: oracle-like structure is read as directional asymmetry inside a frame, not as knowledge, consciousness, judgment, or metaphysical revelation.

This case is not being solved metaphysically. The analysis does not ask what quantum reality ultimately is, whether the oracle “really knows” anything, or whether measurement has some final ontological interpretation. Nor is the case solved empirically. No run results, hardware traces, error rates, decoherence models, measurement statistics, or benchmark outputs are supplied. It is not solved clinically or diagnostically because no human subject, mental state, trait, pathology, intention, or maturity profile is being analyzed. It is not solved legally or forensically because “audit trace” here means structural traceability, not admissibility, culpability, compliance, negligence, liability, or evidentiary sufficiency.

The case matters for AXIOM because it tests whether PMS can remain disciplined when the object is abstract, technical, and highly attractive to over-reading. A weaker framework might either retreat into “this is only quantum formalism” or overextend into “the oracle expresses agency, responsibility, governance, or intelligence.” The PMS-AXIOM approach does neither. It maps the closure-demand, shows where PMS Core applies, justifies why the QC overlay adds specificity, and preserves rival formalizations.

The philosophical closure-demand is therefore: how can a structurally directional, partially opaque computational component be made legible without being turned into a metaphysical, empirical, legal, or implementation claim? PMS Core gives the base operator grammar for this question. PMS-QC adds domain visibility for frames, oracle calls, measurement, iteration, and stabilization. PMS-QC-EXT adds optional paper-specific review handles where the case resembles QPE, Grover, attractor schedules, or measurement-domain projection. Yet none of these layers is allowed to become a verdict machine.

### Claim status and scope discipline

The claim status of the case is a conceptual PMS projection. It is not an empirical finding, not a concrete-scene analysis, and not an implementation assessment. The completed YAML analyses explicitly mark the case as not a concrete scene. This matters because a concrete PMS scene would require a specific event or structured praxis configuration with identifiable roles, frame boundaries, temporal windows, exposure gradients, and evidential traces. Here, the material describes a class of possible quantum workflows: circuits, hybrid DAGs, or macro programs containing oracle-like components. That is enough for structural pre-analysis, but not enough for concrete adjudication.

The case type is best described as a structural audit projection for quantum workflow components with oracle-like directionality. It is conceptual-domain analysis: PMS is being used to map a class of structures, not to read a single observed event. The claim is therefore reversible and case-type-bound. It may become more determinate only if a concrete workflow representation is supplied: a circuit diagram, gate sequence, oracle specification, control relation, measurement basis, register allocation, trace log, hybrid classical-quantum control path, backend information, or validation protocol.

The empirical boundary is strict. The case does not claim that a workflow works, that an algorithm converges, that a measurement outcome is reliable, that a device implements the described operation, that a simulator reproduces the intended state, or that a particular oracle call has been verified. PMS can structure what would need to be checked, but it cannot replace empirical testing, simulation evidence, formal verification, hardware characterization, or benchmark evaluation.

The metaphysical boundary is equally strict. PMS does not interpret quantum processes as conscious, intentional, subjective, morally responsible, or ontologically self-binding. The terms asymmetry, attractor, self-binding, distance, and frame are PMS structural terms. When applied to quantum-computational workflows, they remain structural renderings. They do not imply that quantum systems have awareness, agency, dignity, responsibility, or selfhood. Even SELF_FIXPOINT remains analytic-only in this case.

The diagnostic and clinical boundary is non-negotiable. No person is being evaluated. No mental state, competence, maturity level, pathology, intention, or character is inferred. Even where the PMS vocabulary includes responsibility, action, dignity-in-practice, or drift, these are formula-derived structural constructs within PMS, not diagnostic categories. Drift risks are not findings, and non-assignment of a derived structure is not a low score.

The legal and forensic boundary is also explicit. Although the output type is an audit trace, this is a structural audit trace, not a legal audit. The analysis does not establish legal admissibility, chain of custody, negligence, culpability, regulatory compliance, forensic sufficiency, institutional authority, or enforcement guidance. A legal or forensic use would require external standards and evidence that are absent here.

The implementation boundary is central because the domain is computational. The YAML analyses repeatedly state that formal YAML representation, PMS operator coherence, PMS-QC macro compatibility, and QC-EXT annotation do not establish implementation validity. Structural well-formedness under PMS-QC does not imply physical adequacy, numerical correctness, optimal compilation, stability, safety, backend interoperability, or deployment readiness. PMS-QC may annotate a workflow, but annotation is not execution.

PMS Base validation is not claimed. The case may show that PMS_1.3 can render a certain class of structures legible, but successful rendering does not validate PMS Base, prove its completeness, prove its operator order, or establish superiority over rival formalisms. PMS coherence is insufficient by itself. The analysis must preserve possible non-capture, rival readings, falsifier conditions, and operationalization requirements.

Operationalization is not fulfilled. Stronger claims would require a concrete workflow, explicit oracle definitions, input-output boundaries, control relations, measurement mappings, trace semantics, expected events and non-events, recurrence criteria, asymmetry indicators, integration criteria, and comparison with rival formalisms. Until those are supplied, the case remains a bounded structural projection.

### Scene and frame structure

The conceptual scene is a quantum workflow that contains one or more oracle-like components. The workflow may be a circuit, a hybrid DAG, or a macro program. The components may be black-box functions, marking primitives, phase-kickback structures, controlled unitaries, or measurement mappings. What unites these cases is not a single quantum-mechanical implementation but a structural feature: strong directionality.

The scene boundary is therefore not a physical laboratory boundary, not a specific register allocation, and not a particular circuit diagram. It is the boundary of a conceptual audit frame: a workflow contains a component whose operation creates an asymmetrical relation between inputs, hidden mappings, controls, marked states, phase relations, or measurement outcomes. PMS reads this as a frame in which difference, direction, and asymmetry become structurally salient.

The main frame, □, is the declared workflow frame. This may be a circuit frame, a DAG frame, a macro-program frame, an oracle-interface frame, a measurement frame, or an audit-trace frame. The analysis requires that the active frame be named because the same component can have different structural status under different frames. A controlled unitary in a circuit model is not identical to a black-box function in a software audit, and neither is identical to a measurement mapping in an output trace. PMS does not flatten these.

Inside the frame are the elements that can be structurally mapped: oracle boundaries, query relations, control dependencies, marked and unmarked alternatives, phase-shifted and unshifted states, measurement mappings, trace-visible and trace-hidden components, expected events, missing events, recurrence patterns, and possible integration checkpoints. The audit frame asks how these elements hang together as a structured chain rather than as isolated technical details.

Outside the frame remain quantum-mechanical truth, physical implementation behavior, hardware noise, decoherence, error correction, compiler correctness, backend semantics, formal proof obligations, and legal audit sufficiency. These are not irrelevant; they are non-capture zones for PMS unless separate evidence and formal methods are supplied. Keeping them outside the PMS frame is not failure. It is part of the case’s claim discipline.

The protected constraints are: no metaphysical inflation, no anthropomorphism, no legal-forensic overclaim, no implementation validation, no PMS Base validation, no add-on-as-operator, and no drift-as-verdict. Oracle opacity must not be treated as guilt, defect, agency, intention, or proof. Directionality must not be treated as moral asymmetry unless an external normative frame is supplied. Measurement mapping must not be treated as complete physical theory.

Roles are present only structurally and weakly. The YAML does not specify human actors. At most, there are abstract roles: the workflow component, the audit frame, the possible analyst, the possible downstream user, and the possible validation environment. These are not person roles. They are locations in a structural relation. If a later concrete scene introduced a designer, auditor, institution, user, or affected party, Ω exposure gradients would require recalibration.

Temporality, Θ, is present because workflows unfold in ordered structures: query sequence, controlled-operation order, macro-program steps, measurement timing, hybrid feedback, trace accumulation, and possible iteration. However, the case does not specify iteration count, loop depth, recurrence schedule, or long-term policy binding. Temporality is active as workflow ordering but weak as specific iteration.

The reversibility window remains broad. Because the case is conceptual and under-specified, almost every assignment remains revisable. Λ may become active if an expected trace event is absent; Α may become active if recurrence is shown; Σ may become active if integration checkpoints are supplied; Ψ may become active if stable policy or invariant commitments are specified. Until then, these remain weak or conditional.

Exposure and asymmetry conditions are central but bounded. Ω is strongly suggested by the case material because oracle-like components impose directionality. But the kind of asymmetry must be specified: informational, causal, access-based, control-based, measurement-based, interpretive, exposure-based, or audit-based. Without such specification, Ω is active but not morally, legally, or diagnostically loaded.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading proceeds through the eleven PMS operators in order: Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, Ψ. The order matters because later operators are structurally more expensive. The case is not allowed to jump from “there is an oracle” to “there is self-binding,” from “there is directionality” to “there is responsibility,” or from “there is an audit trace” to “there is validated implementation.”

Δ, Difference, is active and foundational. The oracle-asymmetry case begins with distinctions: oracle-like versus non-oracle-like component, black-box versus transparent access, queried versus unqueried path, marked versus unmarked state, controlled versus uncontrolled operation, phase-shifted versus unshifted relation, measured versus unmeasured mapping, trace-visible versus trace-hidden component. These distinctions do not yet prove anything. They make the field readable. The main calibration issue is to avoid treating all distinctions as the same kind of distinction. A black-box boundary is not the same as a phase mark; a measurement outcome distinction is not the same as a control dependency; a trace omission is not the same as a hidden internal mapping. Overclaim begins when Δ is treated as if it already established direction, risk, integration, or audit sufficiency.

∇, Impulse, is active as structural directedness. In this case, ∇ does not mean desire, intention, motivation, or psychological drive. It appears as query flow, control activation, marking operation, phase-kickback relation, or measurement-channel activation. A directed operation moves the workflow away from a neutral or prior state toward a differentiated result. The dependency on Δ is satisfied where a difference becomes the basis of a direction: queried rather than unqueried, marked rather than unmarked, controlled rather than uncontrolled. The calibration problem is to specify the type of directed activation. If the analysis merely says “there is a quantum component” and then assigns ∇, the operator is under-specified. ∇ must be grounded in actual directional structure.

□, Frame, is active and carries major analytic weight. The frame determines what counts as input, output, oracle, control, phase mark, measurement, trace, dependency, omission, or error. The case contains several possible frames: circuit, hybrid DAG, macro program, oracle interface, measurement mapping, and audit trace. PMS requires that these not be silently merged. A circuit frame may define correctness differently from an audit frame; a measurement frame may define outcome resolution differently from a macro-program frame. The main dependency risk is ∇ → □ overrun: treating directionality as meaningful without declaring the governing frame. If the frame is not specified, the analysis becomes too smooth and loses discriminative force.

Λ, Non-Event, is active only weakly and conditionally. Oracle opacity is not automatically Λ. A black-box function may be opaque without generating a structured absence in the PMS sense. Λ requires an expected occurrence within a frame whose absence, delay, or failure matters. Examples include a missing trace entry, an unobserved branch where the frame requires branch visibility, a non-triggered control where a trigger was expected, an absent mark, a delayed measurement, or an explanation that the audit frame explicitly demands but does not receive. The calibration issue is crucial: the analyst must distinguish unknown information from structured absence. Overclaim occurs when every opacity is treated as Λ. PMS must also preserve the possibility that unresolved absence marks non-capture by PMS rather than a defect in the workflow.

Α, Attractor, is weak. It may become active if oracle calls, phase mappings, controlled transformations, measurement pathways, audit patterns, or trace omissions recur and stabilize. A single oracle-like component does not by itself create an attractor. Recurrence, patterning, or stabilization must be shown. In a Grover-like context, amplitude amplification or diffusion might support attractor-like reading. In a QPE-like context, Fourier-domain alignment might support a weak attractor reading. But the case material does not provide these details. The dependency risk is Λ → Α inflation: inferring a recurring pattern from one missing event or one opaque component. PMS should not turn resemblance into recurrence.

Ω, Asymmetry, is strongly active. The case explicitly says that oracle-like components impose strong directionality. PMS reads this as structural asymmetry: access asymmetry, information asymmetry, control asymmetry, interpretive asymmetry, exposure asymmetry, or audit asymmetry. However, Ω depends in PMS on stabilized patterns, and here Α is only weak. This means Ω is materially supported by the case description but still requires careful dependency hygiene. The operator is active, but its specific mode must be named. Overclaim occurs if Ω is moralized, juridified, or anthropomorphized. A controlled unitary does not become a responsible agent; a measurement mapping does not become legal authority; an oracle does not become a moral subject.

Θ, Temporality, is active as workflow ordering and trace temporality. Quantum workflows unfold through sequences: preparation, oracle call, controlled operation, phase kickback, measurement, classical feedback, audit logging, and possible iteration. A hybrid DAG or macro program also has dependency order, even if not linear time in an ordinary narrative sense. The calibration issue is to distinguish several temporal forms: simple circuit order, repeated oracle calls, delayed measurement, accumulated trace dependency, long Θ chains, and policy-bound iteration. The case supports workflow temporality, but not specific iteration depth, convergence schedule, recurrence horizon, or long-term invariant enforcement. Overclaim occurs when ordered structure is treated as developmental trajectory or stable commitment without evidence.

Φ, Recontextualization, is active as audit recontextualization and weak as concrete QC basis shift. The same component can be re-read across frames: black-box function, controlled unitary, phase-kickback primitive, measurement mapping, audit dependency, PMS Ω structure, PMS-QC QORACLE_CALL, or QC-EXT resemblance to QPE or Grover. Φ makes these frame shifts visible. But Φ must not become an assimilation engine. Rival formalizations must remain rival. A formal verification reading, quantum circuit semantics reading, or measurement-theory reading is not simply an “incomplete PMS reframing.” The calibration issue is to name the source frame and target frame for each recontextualization. Overclaim occurs when missing evidence, non-fit, disagreement, or rival analysis is absorbed as PMS recontextualization.

Χ, Distance, is active and required as an application condition. In this case Χ prevents fusion between structural readability and verdict. It blocks the conversion of oracle opacity into defect, directionality into blame, traceability into validation, QC overlay compliance into safety, and PMS coherence into PMS Base proof. Χ also sustains the audit stance: the trace must be readable without becoming tribunal-like. The calibration issue is to distinguish distance from avoidance. Χ is not a refusal to analyze; it is the condition that analysis remains bounded, reversible, and non-coercive. If Χ is suspended to force closure, the PMS application becomes invalid.

Σ, Integration, is weak to moderate and under-specified. Integration would require coordination among circuit logic, oracle semantics, control flow, measurement mapping, trace representation, audit objective, and perhaps policy constraints. The abstract case does not provide enough detail to claim such coordination. PMS can ask where Σ would occur: a checkpoint, diffusion commit, post-QFT commit, measurement commit, trace-integration boundary, or validated workflow handoff. But it cannot assert that Σ has occurred. The main risk is coherence laundering: because the PMS reading is internally coherent, the analyst may be tempted to treat that coherence as integration. That would be invalid. PMS coherence is not proof of workflow integration.

Ψ, Self-Binding, is weak and mostly analytic-only. At object level, the case does not specify stable policy, constraint, invariant, audit commitment, or system-level binding across time. PMS permits artificial-system Ψ only as policy or constraint stability, not as consciousness. Therefore Ψ may apply to the analysis itself: the case binds itself to claim boundaries, reversibility, and non-capture. It may also apply if a future workflow supplies k-bounds, stabilizer routines, invariants, QSTABILIZE structures, audit policies, or Ψ-bound recontextualization rules. But absent those, Ψ must remain weak. Overclaim occurs when self-binding is anthropomorphized or when stable syntax is mistaken for selfhood.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The central dependency discipline of this case is that later PMS operators cannot be used cheaply. The case easily invites a full Δ–Ψ chain because it contains rich structure: difference, direction, frame, opacity, possible recurrence, strong asymmetry, workflow order, audit recontextualization, distance, possible integration, and possible policy constraints. But PMS requires not only that operators be nameable; it requires that their dependencies and statuses be respected.

The minimal safe PMS Core formula is:

Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ω ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ

This formula is safe because the case explicitly supports framed directional distinctions, strong asymmetry, workflow temporality, audit recontextualization, and reflective distance. Λ and Α are not included in the minimal formula because they require additional evidence: meaningful absence and recurrence. Σ and Ψ are not included because concrete integration and self-binding are not established.

A fuller conditional formula is:

Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ω ∘ Α? ∘ Λ? ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ

Here, Λ and Α are marked conditional. This is important. The analysis does not deny that oracle workflows may contain structured absences or recurrent patterns. It says only that the abstract case material does not prove them. An actual trace log might make Λ central. A repeated oracle block might make Α central. An amplitude-amplification routine might make Α stronger. A missing measurement outcome expected by a declared `□_meas` frame might make Λ stronger. But none of this can be assumed.

Σ remains conditional because integration is not the same as ordered operation. A circuit may be syntactically ordered without being audit-integrated. An oracle may function in an algorithm without its black-box relation being trace-integrated. A measurement may produce an outcome without the outcome being integrated into a valid audit structure. PMS requires the analyst to show the integration boundary: what was coordinated, what was committed, what conflicts were resolved, and what remains residual.

Ψ remains conditional because self-binding requires stable commitment to integrated structures across time. In artificial-system contexts, Ψ refers to policy and constraint stability, not consciousness. Therefore, a workflow might show Ψ if it has explicit invariants, stabilizer checks, bounded iteration policies, non-reframing rules after Σ, or audit commitments that constrain future operations. But a black-box oracle does not have Ψ simply because it is black-boxed, and a YAML representation does not have Ψ simply because it is formally coherent.

Coherence is not proof. This case has a high coherence risk precisely because PMS maps it well. Oracle-like components naturally invite Δ, ∇, □, Ω, Θ, Φ, and Χ. That smoothness can become under-discriminating. If all black-box functions, controlled unitaries, phase-kickback primitives, measurement mappings, hybrid DAG dependencies, and audit traces are rendered merely as “Ω directionality,” the reading loses analytic force. PMS must distinguish the kinds of directionality at stake.

Non-capture is not failure. Quantum mechanics, circuit semantics, hardware noise, decoherence, tensor networks, ZX-calculus, categorical quantum mechanics, compiler frameworks, QASM/Qiskit/Cirq/Braket semantics, and formal verification may capture aspects of the case that PMS does not. PMS-AXIOM does not need to absorb them. A rival formalism may be more precise for a concrete workflow. PMS contributes structural cartography, not monopoly.

PMS must not become a closure machine. The purpose of the reading is not to convert every unresolved detail into an operator, every absence into Λ, every repetition into Α, every direction into Ω, every sequence into Θ, every frame shift into Φ, every coherent YAML into Σ, and every policy note into Ψ. The purpose is to preserve which closures are structurally warranted and which must remain open.

Invalid dependency shortcuts include: Δ/∇ → Ω without frame; opacity → Λ without expected event; one oracle call → Α without recurrence; Ω → responsibility without role or Ψ; Θ order → commitment without Σ; Φ re-reading → absorption of rivals; Χ distance → proof of neutrality; Σ coherence → implementation validity; Ψ policy language → consciousness or selfhood. Each shortcut would violate the case’s PMS discipline.

### Derived structures

The derived structures are formula-derived and must not be treated as primitives. Awareness_A, Coherence_C, Responsibility_R, Action_E, Dignity_in_Practice_D, and SELF_FIXPOINT do not add new operators to PMS. They are higher-order constructs derived from specified operator compositions. Their status in this case is mixed: one is active, one weak, one under-specified, one not applicable at object level, one active only as application constraint, and one analytic-only.

Awareness_A is formula-derived from Θ, □, and Δ. In this case it is active as structural trace differentiation. The workflow can be made audit-readable only if distinctions are maintained across a frame over time: oracle boundary, query state, control condition, mark state, phase relation, measurement mapping, trace-visible versus trace-hidden relation. This is not phenomenological awareness. It does not imply that the workflow, oracle, or quantum system is aware. It means that the audit structure can sustain framed distinctions over workflow temporality. If no temporal trace or frame-stable distinction is supplied, Awareness_A would weaken.

Coherence_C is formula-derived from Θ, Λ, □, and ∇. It is weak because Λ is weak. Coherence_C would require directed operations stabilized within a frame over time while meaningful non-events or expectations are carried. A concrete trace might show this: for example, a measurement expected by a frame does not occur, or a branch remains unrealized in a way that matters for audit. But the abstract case does not supply enough trace semantics to establish Coherence_C strongly. Coherence must not be confused with correctness. A coherent PMS analysis does not prove a coherent quantum workflow, and a coherent workflow does not prove physical adequacy.

Responsibility_R is formula-derived from Ψ, Φ, Θ, and Ω. It is not applicable at object level. The case contains Ω and Θ, and it contains Φ as audit recontextualization, but it does not establish Ψ. Nor does it specify an accountable agent, role obligation, governance authority, legal duty, policy commitment, or responsibility-bearing institution. Therefore Responsibility_R cannot be claimed. This is not a deficiency or a low score; it is correct non-assignment. Responsibility would require additional scene data.

Action_E is formula-derived from Σ, Θ, and ∇. It is under-specified. The workflow has directed temporal operation: oracle calls, controlled operations, phase effects, or measurement mappings. But PMS Action_E requires integration, and Σ is not established. A circuit can operate without the PMS analysis being able to claim integrated action in the derived-structure sense. Action_E would become stronger if the workflow showed coherent integration across oracle semantics, control flow, measurement mapping, trace representation, and audit objective. Without that, Action_E remains partial.

Dignity_in_Practice_D is formula-derived from Ψ, Χ, and Ω. In this case it is active as a praxeological application constraint, not as an object-level ontological dignity claim. It governs how the analysis handles asymmetry. Because Ω is central and Χ is required, Dignity-in-Practice requires restraint: do not shame, rank, anthropomorphize, moralize, or tribunalize the workflow; do not turn opacity into blame; do not turn non-fit into failure; do not convert structural asymmetry into person-worth or legal status. Dignity-in-Practice does not mean the oracle has dignity as an ontological subject. It means the analysis must handle asymmetry without coercive misuse.

SELF_FIXPOINT is the full derived chain Ψ ∘ Σ ∘ Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ω ∘ Α ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ. It is not established. At most, it functions as an analytic reference for the full PMS chain and as a reminder that self-binding claims require the whole dependency path. The quantum workflow is not modeled as selfhood. A limited artificial-system analogue would require explicit stable policy or constraint binding across time, but even then it would remain policy/constraint stability, not consciousness. In this case, SELF_FIXPOINT is analytic-only and non-operative.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS drift-pattern layer remains conditional. The completed YAML analyses identify two PMS drift risks: AD_A>>E and AD_Sigma_low. Neither is classified as a finding. Both are treated as possible structural risks that would require concrete workflow evidence before activation. This distinction is essential: the case is about oracle-asymmetry audit, but asymmetry alone does not prove drift.

AD_A>>E names excessive distance between Awareness and Enactment. In PMS terms, it becomes relevant where awareness-like evaluation expands while integrated action remains suppressed, delayed, or structurally unavailable. In this case, AD_A>>E is possible because an audit trace could become disproportionately evaluative: the workflow may be repeatedly inspected, flagged, re-read, or framed as risky without any corresponding integrated operational response. A system might produce a growing map of oracle asymmetry while leaving concrete enactment, repair, validation, or integration unresolved.

But the YAML is careful: this risk is not established. The case material provides no concrete audit-response cycle, no action axis, no evidence that awareness has over-expanded, and no proof that enactment has been suppressed. The correct status is under-specified. The awareness axis is under-specified, and the action axis is under-specified. That means the pattern is available only as a future structural review handle for the artefact, not as a diagnosis, score, or verdict about the present case. Strong directionality in an oracle-like component is not enough to claim AD_A>>E.

AD_Sigma_low names fragmented integration, or low Σ. It becomes relevant where recontextualization outpaces integration: a structure is repeatedly shifted across frames without being coherently consolidated. This case has clear risk conditions for AD_Sigma_low because the same component can be read as a black-box function, oracle call, controlled unitary, phase-kickback primitive, measurement mapping, DAG node, audit dependency, PMS Ω structure, PMS-QC macro, or QC-EXT resemblance. That is a wide Φ-field. If those frame shifts accumulate without integration, the analysis could become volatile.

Again, the YAML does not classify this as a finding. Rival formalizations, unresolved technical questions, or legitimate non-fit must not be treated as drift by default. A quantum circuit reading may resist PMS not because integration failed, but because circuit semantics are the sharper tool for that layer. A measurement-theory reading may not map neatly to PMS because PMS is not a physical theory of measurement. A legal audit reading may remain unavailable because legal criteria are absent. These are not necessarily Σ failures.

The coherence risk is high. The PMS reading is smooth: Δ, ∇, □, Ω, Θ, Φ, and Χ all fit the case naturally. That smoothness is itself dangerous. A coherent PMS mapping can under-discriminate. If every oracle-like element is simply called “asymmetry,” the analysis loses contact with the important differences between black-box functions, controlled unitaries, phase-kickback mechanisms, measurement mappings, and audit traces. The drift-pattern section therefore functions as a restraint: it marks possible over-extension before it becomes hidden authority.

Under-discrimination risk is also high. The YAML explicitly warns that oracle, black-box function, controlled unitary, phase kickback, measurement mapping, hybrid DAG, macro-program, and audit trace are not equivalent. PMS can map the family resemblance, but it must not erase formal difference. AD_Sigma_low would become plausible if the article or artefact moved too quickly among these without declaring which frame is active. AD_A>>E would become plausible if the audit layer expanded into a large evaluative apparatus while concrete integration remained absent.

The non-assignment of drift is not a weakness. It is the correct structural result. PMS drift patterns are not scoring tools. They do not say the case is defective, immature, unstable, or low-performing. They say: if the case later acquires concrete workflow evidence, these are the structural risks to check. Until then, the drift layer preserves reversibility.

### Add-on / overlay reading

### Layer interaction

The PMS Core reading remains the base. It supplies the operator grammar, dependencies, derived-structure formulas, entry discipline, dignity-in-practice constraint, reversibility requirement, and non-capture rule. Everything else is subordinate to this layer. PMS-QC and PMS-QC-EXT may add visibility, but they cannot redefine Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, or Ψ. They cannot change the dependency path. They cannot convert derived structures into primitives.

PMS Core identifies the case as a framed directional asymmetry problem. The main Core structure is Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ω ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ, with Λ, Α, Σ, and Ψ conditional. This remains the core formula even after the overlays are added.

PMS-QC sharpens the domain reading. It says: this is not any generic Ω problem, but a quantum-computational workflow in which oracle-like directionality should be annotated through QFRAME and QORACLE_CALL, with conditional QMEASURE, QITERATE, QDIFFUSION, QFT_ALIGN, Σ, and QSTABILIZE depending on supplied evidence. PMS-QC also provides structural policy checks: no unframed Ω, measurement requires measurement frame, Ω-heavy chains require review, reframing after Σ requires Ψ, iteration depth requires bounds, and long Θ chains require stabilization review.

PMS-QC-EXT sharpens a narrower paper-specific question: does the case instantiate, or merely resemble, QPE ladders, Grover centering, fixed-point attractor schedules, measurement projection, or basis-reframe policy? The answer is mostly “merely resemble,” with measurement projection weakly selected as a conditional review handle. EXT does not become the operative formula.

Overlay language could overreach in several ways. It could treat QORACLE_CALL as if every oracle-like component were fully classified. It could treat controlled unitaries as QPE ladders without ladder evidence. It could treat marking as Grover centering without diffusion or fixed-point regulation. It could treat measurement mapping as a complete measurement theory. It could treat QC policies as safety guarantees. It could treat EXT review flags as failures. Each of these would violate the layer discipline.

Non-closure remains visible at every layer. PMS Core preserves Λ and non-capture. PMS-QC preserves physical, implementation, and formal non-capture. PMS-QC-EXT preserves the base/extension boundary and keeps paper-specific refinements conditional. The layers do not converge into closure; they create a more precise map of where closure is not warranted.

### Example 1 — A black-box marker inside a declared problem frame

**Type:** micro
**Recommended insertion point:** After the PMS Core discussion of Δ, ∇, □, and Ω.

A small quantum search workflow contains a black-box component that marks some inputs and leaves others unmarked. The article does not need to know the internal implementation of the marker. It is enough, for this example, that the workflow distinguishes marked from unmarked alternatives within a declared problem frame. The frame might be: “evaluate candidate states for membership in the target set.” Inside that frame, the black-box component creates a clear directional structure: a query enters, a marking relation is applied, and the downstream workflow treats the marked/unmarked difference as relevant.

This example demonstrates the minimal Core structure of the case. Δ appears as the distinction between marked and unmarked alternatives. ∇ appears as the directed query or activation of the black-box marker. □ appears as the problem frame that makes the distinction meaningful. Ω appears because the marker has privileged directional status: it determines which alternatives receive the special structural treatment, while the rest of the workflow depends on that operation.

The example does not yet establish Λ, Α, Σ, or Ψ. The internal opacity of the marker is not automatically a PMS Non-Event. It becomes Λ only if the audit frame expects some trace, explanation, branch visibility, or confirmation event that is absent. Likewise, a single use of the marker does not establish Α; recurrence or stabilization would have to be shown. The workflow may be ordered in time, but that does not by itself establish integration. There is no self-binding unless a stable policy, invariant, or audit commitment is specified.

The PMS-QC overlay is relevant but minimal. The marker can be annotated as a QORACLE_CALL only if it is dominated by a declared QFRAME. That is the QC-specific contribution: it prevents the case from treating oracle asymmetry as free-floating. The overlay does not prove that the oracle is physically implemented, mathematically correct, or safe. PMS-QC only makes the structural relation more visible.

QC-EXT is not triggered by this micro-example. A black-box marker may resemble a Grover-style motif, but it does not by itself supply diffusion, fixed-point regulation, overshoot control, an Α→Σ boundary, or Ψ-bound stabilization. Therefore the example remains PMS Core plus PMS-QC only; it does not activate GROVER_CENTERING, ATTRACTOR_FIXED_POINT, or any EXT chain pattern.

MIP and AHP are not triggered. There is no governance artefact, no institutional decision, no downstream maturity assessment, and no analysis-hardening task. QC is active only as a structural overlay.

Forbidden claims are straightforward: the marker is not an agent, not a subject, not an authority, not a legal actor, and not a source of metaphysical meaning. Its directionality is not moral responsibility. Its opacity is not guilt, defect, or diagnosis. Its successful annotation as QORACLE_CALL is not implementation validation.

The non-capture note is that circuit semantics, oracle specification, query complexity, and physical backend behavior remain outside this example. PMS maps the framed asymmetry; it does not replace the quantum formalism.

The article takeaway is that the Core case can begin modestly: a black-box marker becomes PMS-readable when a frame turns a distinction into directional asymmetry. Nothing stronger follows unless the scene supplies stronger evidence.

### Example 2 — A controlled unitary that resembles QPE but does not activate QC-EXT

**Type:** compact
**Recommended insertion point:** In the QC-EXT overlay section, after the discussion of QPE_ORACLE_LADDER remaining weak.

Consider a hybrid workflow that contains a controlled unitary. A control register conditionally applies a unitary operation to a target register, and the analyst notices that this looks “QPE-like.” The resemblance is real enough to justify a review note, but not enough to activate the QC-EXT QPE_ORACLE_LADDER refinement.

The bounded vignette is this: the workflow has one controlled operation, and the audit trace records that the operation is directionally dependent on the state of a control subsystem. The control relation is structurally asymmetrical. One register conditions the operation applied to another. This is enough for PMS Core to identify Ω under □, and enough for PMS-QC to treat the component as QORACLE_CALL, provided the active frame is declared. It also gives Θ in the weak sense of workflow ordering, because the controlled operation occurs at a specific point in the workflow.

What the example demonstrates is the difference between resemblance and activation. PMS Core can read the controlled unitary as framed directional asymmetry. PMS-QC can annotate it as an oracle-like or controlled-unitary operation. But PMS-QC-EXT cannot yet call it QPE_ORACLE_LADDER. The EXT macro requires more: an explicit unitary identifier, a ladder depth t, a U^(2^j) structure, a control-register preparation context, a phase-estimation target, and a later readout or alignment structure. Without these, the EXT reading remains a weak comparison handle.

The PMS Core hooks are Δ, ∇, □, Ω, Θ, Φ, and Χ. Δ distinguishes control and target conditions. ∇ appears as the directed application of the operation. □ is the circuit or workflow frame. Ω is the control asymmetry. Θ is ordering. Φ appears because the same operation can be read as a circuit component, a macro-program node, an audit object, or a PMS-QC overlay handle. The add-on hooks are QFRAME and QORACLE_CALL. QPE_ORACLE_LADDER is mentioned only as QC-EXT non-activation. This is exactly where the extension layer is useful: it prevents a vague “controlled unitary means QPE” slide. EXT does not merely add more labels; it disciplines which labels may be used.

MIP and AHP are not invoked. QC is active. QC-EXT is weak and conditional. There is no governance claim and no claim-hardening layer.

The forbidden claims are: this is not proof that the workflow performs phase estimation; not proof that a QPE ladder exists; not proof of convergence; not proof of physical adequacy; not proof of implementation correctness. It also does not imply that the controlled operation has agency or responsibility.

The non-capture note is that a formal circuit analysis may say more precisely what the controlled unitary does. PMS-QC maps the structural asymmetry, but it does not replace matrix-level semantics, circuit equivalence proof, compilation analysis, or backend verification.

The article takeaway is that QC-EXT is valuable precisely when it refuses over-activation. It can record a QPE-like resemblance while preserving the boundary: controlled-unitary asymmetry is not yet a QPE ladder.

### Example 3 — Measurement mapping, Λ calibration, and the limits of audit trace

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** After the PMS-QC discussion of QMEASURE and MEASUREMENT_PROJECTION_MODEL, or in the falsifier / operationalization section.

Imagine a workflow in which a quantum register is measured and the resulting classical bit string is passed into a downstream audit trace. The trace records the observed output but does not record the measurement basis, register identity, expected outcome set, or whether any branch structure was relevant to the audit. The workflow documentation says only: “measurement mapping applied; output recorded.”

This example is intentionally bounded. It is not a complete quantum measurement theory, not a hardware report, and not an empirical validation record. It gives just enough structure to show why PMS and PMS-QC must calibrate Λ carefully. The observed output creates Δ: one outcome is distinguished from alternatives. The measurement operation is directed, so ∇ is present. A measurement frame, `□_meas`, is required, but in the vignette it is incomplete. The audit trace wants to treat the output as settled, but PMS asks what exactly was framed, what was expected, and what remains absent.

The key PMS Core question is whether Λ is active. It would be easy to say: the unobserved alternatives are “non-events,” so Λ is active. But the completed YAML analysis is more careful. Λ is active only where an expected occurrence within a frame fails, is delayed, or remains absent in a structurally meaningful way. If the audit frame merely records one outcome and does not require branch visibility, then the unobserved alternatives may be part of the quantum measurement description but not yet PMS Λ in the audit sense. If, however, the audit frame required a declared basis, expected outcome set, branch-retention rule, or trace of discarded alternatives, and those are missing, then Λ becomes active as structured absence.

This example demonstrates why opacity and absence are not identical. The fact that a measurement yields one outcome does not automatically produce a PMS Non-Event. A non-event must be tied to a frame. The missing basis may be Λ if the audit frame expects basis declaration. The missing register identity may be Λ if the trace cannot be interpreted without it. The unrealized alternatives may be Λ if the measurement-domain model requires branch accounting. Without those frame expectations, Λ remains under-specified.

The PMS Core hooks are Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, and possibly Σ. Δ is the outcome distinction. ∇ is the measurement-channel activation. □ is the measurement frame, which is incomplete in the vignette. Λ is conditional, depending on expected-but-absent trace elements. Ω appears because the measurement mapping imposes a direction from quantum state to classical outcome and gives the trace a privileged outcome. Θ appears through workflow order: measurement occurs at a point and affects downstream interpretation. Φ appears when the same event is re-read as quantum measurement, classical output, audit trace, or PMS-QC QMEASURE. Χ prevents the output from being treated as complete closure. Σ remains weak unless the trace integrates basis, register, outcome set, and audit purpose into a coherent checkpoint.

The PMS-QC overlay is directly relevant. QMEASURE may be used only if the measurement has a measurement-stable frame. The macro requires basis and register parameters in any operationally meaningful version. In this vignette, both are under-specified, so QMEASURE is relevant but incomplete. The PMS-QC policy `measurement_requires_meas_frame` is therefore a review risk, not a failure finding. It says what must be supplied before the audit trace can make stronger claims.

QC-EXT adds one optional refinement: MEASUREMENT_PROJECTION_MODEL. This is weakly relevant because the example concerns measurement-domain branching. Yet the EXT macro is not active as a full model because the branch structure, basis, register, and outcome set are not specified. The refinement helps formulate what is missing; it does not fill the gap.

MIP and AHP remain inactive. A governance layer might later become relevant if the audit trace were used for institutional decision-making. AHP might become relevant if the analysis artefact itself needed second-order claim hardening. In the present example, neither is triggered.

Forbidden claims are especially important here. The example does not claim that the recorded output is physically correct, that the measurement was properly implemented, that the hardware behaved ideally, that the audit trace is legally sufficient, or that the workflow is safe. It also does not claim that the unobserved alternatives are metaphysical absences, moral residues, or diagnostic signs.

The non-capture note is that measurement theory, hardware readout behavior, noise, decoherence, error correction, and backend semantics remain outside PMS. A physicist, formal verifier, or compiler engineer may need to supply the decisive account. PMS contributes the structural audit question: what was framed, what was expected, what was recorded, what is absent, and what cannot be claimed?

The article takeaway is that Λ discipline is one of the strongest safeguards in the case. A measurement mapping can support audit trace only when its frame is explicit. Otherwise, the correct result is not closure, but a bounded statement of under-specification.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The YAML analyses preserve a large rival space. This is not a weakness. The case is structurally readable through PMS, but it is not exhausted by PMS.

A quantum circuit semantics reading may be more precise for gate-level behavior, unitary structure, input-output mapping, and formal algorithmic correctness. PMS can say that an oracle call is a framed Ω operation, but circuit semantics can specify the actual transformation. PMS must not claim to replace that.

A formal verification reading may be more precise where the question concerns equivalence, correctness, invariants, trace properties, preconditions, postconditions, or proof obligations. PMS can identify where Σ or Ψ would need to be shown, but it cannot supply a proof.

A black-box testing or oracle-access security reading may be more precise where the issue concerns query complexity, hidden mappings, adversarial access, leakage, or security boundaries. PMS can mark opacity and asymmetry, but it does not define the security model.

A causal DAG or software traceability reading may be more precise where the workflow is a hybrid classical-quantum system. PMS can map frames and dependencies, but a DAG formalism can specify data dependencies, causal paths, and execution order more concretely.

A measurement-theoretic reading may be more precise where the case involves measurement mappings. PMS-QC and QC-EXT can mark QMEASURE and MEASUREMENT_PROJECTION_MODEL, but they do not offer a physical theory of measurement. They do not settle collapse, decoherence, Born-rule interpretation, or hardware readout behavior.

A tensor-network, ZX-calculus, categorical quantum mechanics, or Hamiltonian simulation reading may be more precise for certain quantum structures. PMS-QC explicitly preserves these rival formalisms. Structural comparability is not ontological identity and not equal explanatory depth.

A compiler-framework reading may be more precise where the question concerns QASM, Qiskit, Cirq, Braket, backend semantics, optimization, transpilation, or hardware compatibility. PMS-QC may annotate or wrap such artefacts, but compatibility as metadata is not semantic equivalence.

A legal, governance, or compliance reading may become relevant if the audit trace is intended for institutional use. But that requires external legal or governance criteria. The current case does not supply them, so MIP and AHP are not invoked.

An anti-capture reading is also preserved. It may say that the PMS and QC overlay stack itself risks symbolic overreach: converting technical structures into a rich operator vocabulary that sounds explanatory beyond its warrant. The completed YAMLs explicitly anticipate this risk through coherence-risk, non-capture zones, and rival-formalism preservation.

These rival readings preserve what PMS may not fully capture: formal precision, physical adequacy, mathematical proof, implementation behavior, hardware constraints, legal standards, and domain-specific semantics. PMS contributes a structural audit grammar. It does not absorb the whole field.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS Core reading would be weakened if no stable distinctions are identifiable. Without Δ, the case has no structural entry point. If the workflow does not distinguish oracle-like from non-oracle-like components, marked from unmarked states, controlled from uncontrolled operations, or measured from unmeasured mappings, the reading becomes empty.

It would be weakened if no directed activation follows from those distinctions. Without ∇, there is no query flow, control activation, marking, phase-kickback relation, or measurement-channel direction. The case would then no longer be an oracle-asymmetry case but perhaps merely a static classification.

It would be weakened if no frame is specified. Without □, the analysis cannot determine what counts as oracle, trace, expectation, measurement, recurrence, integration, or failure. A frame-free oracle reading would be exactly the kind of closure-smuggling PMS is designed to prevent.

It would be weakened if Λ is asserted without a structured expectation. A missing internal mapping is not automatically a non-event. The analyst must show what was expected, by whom or by what frame, and why its absence matters.

It would be weakened if Α is asserted without recurrence or stabilization. One oracle-like component is not a pattern. One opacity is not an attractor. One measurement is not a stabilized measurement regime.

It would be weakened if Ω is left generic. The case supports strong directionality, but the analysis must specify what kind of asymmetry is present: access, information, control, exposure, obligation, interpretive authority, causal direction, measurement readout, or audit visibility.

It would be weakened if Θ is treated as more than workflow ordering without evidence. Iteration, recurrence, convergence, and long-term commitment require concrete sequence structures.

It would be weakened if Φ becomes over-assimilative. Recontextualization is valid only when source and target frames are named. Rival formalizations must not be absorbed merely because PMS can rephrase them.

It would be weakened if Χ is dropped. Without distance, the analysis becomes tribunal-like: opacity becomes blame, directionality becomes authority, coherence becomes proof, and audit trace becomes validation.

It would be weakened if Σ is claimed without integration criteria. Integration requires coordination among workflow semantics, oracle specification, control flow, measurement mapping, trace representation, and audit objective. Without these, Σ remains weak.

It would be weakened if Ψ is attributed to the workflow without explicit policy or constraint stability. Ψ must not become anthropomorphic. In artificial-system contexts, it means stable constraints, not consciousness.

The PMS-QC overlay would be weakened if no QC frame can be specified. QFRAME must dominate QORACLE_CALL. If the oracle-like component cannot be placed in a problem frame, eigenproblem frame, oracle-domain frame, or measurement frame, the QC overlay remains too abstract.

It would be weakened if measurement mappings are invoked without basis, register, outcome structure, and measurement-stable frame. QMEASURE requires `□_meas`. Without it, measurement language is only a topic label.

It would be weakened if iteration policies are invoked without k, repeated block, iteration depth, or k_max. QITERATE and max_iteration_depth require concrete parameters.

It would be weakened if diffusion, QFT alignment, or stabilization is invoked without corresponding workflow structures. QDIFFUSION, QFT_ALIGN, and QSTABILIZE are not decorative labels; they require evidence.

The QC-EXT overlay would be weakened if EXT resemblance is treated as activation. Controlled unitaries do not automatically instantiate QPE_ORACLE_LADDER. Marking does not automatically instantiate GROVER_CENTERING. Measurement mapping does not automatically instantiate full measurement projection. Basis-reframe risk does not imply a post-Σ violation. All EXT constructs remain weak unless concrete algorithmic structure is supplied.

The reading would become under-discriminating if it treated all oracle-like components as the same. It would become over-assimilative if missing evidence, non-fit, or rival formalization were redescribed as drift. It would become overextended if PMS-QC structural compliance were treated as physical adequacy or safety. It would become invalid if it claimed PMS Base validation from case coherence.

Operationalization would require a concrete workflow representation; exact oracle definitions; input-output boundaries; control relations; measurement mappings; trace semantics; expected events and non-events; recurrence criteria; asymmetry indicators; temporal ordering; recontextualization rules; integration criteria; policy or constraint stability criteria; and comparison with rival formalizations. Empirical, computational, comparative, and applied validation are required for stronger use. None is fulfilled by the abstract case material.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It does not evaluate a person, team, designer, auditor, researcher, institution, or user. It does not infer competence, intention, maturity, pathology, responsibility, negligence, or character.

It must not be used for person ranking. PMS operators and derived structures are not dignity scores, intelligence scores, responsibility scores, or moral standings. Non-assignment of Ψ, Σ, or Responsibility_R is not a negative judgment.

It must not be used as a legal or forensic conclusion. “Audit trace” in this case means structural traceability and reviewability, not admissibility, liability, culpability, compliance, negligence, enforcement, or forensic sufficiency.

It must not be used as metaphysical proof. Nothing in the PMS, PMS-QC, or PMS-QC-EXT reading establishes what quantum reality ultimately is, what measurement ultimately means, or whether any computational process has consciousness, agency, or selfhood.

It must not be used as empirical proof. The analysis contains no runs, measurements, benchmarks, error rates, noise models, hardware data, simulations, or validation results.

It must not be used as implementation validation. YAML representation, PMS coherence, QC macro annotation, and EXT review do not prove that a circuit executes, that a compiler preserves semantics, that a backend supports the workflow, or that a system is safe.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. A case that maps well to PMS does not validate PMS as a model, prove its completeness, or establish its superiority over rival formalisms.

It must not treat add-ons as operators. PMS-QC macros and QC-EXT refinements are overlay handles. QFRAME, QORACLE_CALL, QMEASURE, QPE_ORACLE_LADDER, GROVER_CENTERING, ATTRACTOR_FIXED_POINT, and MEASUREMENT_PROJECTION_MODEL are not PMS operators and do not alter Δ–Ψ.

It must not treat drift as verdict. AD_A>>E, AD_Sigma_low, unstable_asymmetry_guard, qpe_depth_guard, grover_no_overshoot, basis_reframe_guard, and attractor_cycle_integrity are risks or review flags, not findings unless concrete evidence supports them.

It must not close by authority. PMS-AXIOM maps closure-demands; it does not solve by declaration. Where the workflow remains under-specified, the correct output is not stronger language but preserved non-closure.
