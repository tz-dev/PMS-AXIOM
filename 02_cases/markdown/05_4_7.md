### Why this case matters

The case of truth theories is a central AXIOM case because it concentrates a classical closure-demand in unusually pure form. The question is not merely whether this or that statement is true. The deeper demand concerns what it means for any statement, belief, proposition, assertion, model, report, or claim to be true at all. Correspondence theories tend to demand closure through relation to fact or reality. Coherence theories seek closure through fit within a system. Pragmatic theories look toward successful use, practical consequence, or warranted practice. Semantic theories search for closure through formal structure and truth-conditions. Deflationary theories resist substantive closure by treating truth as a logical, expressive, or grammatical device. Pluralist accounts distribute truth-functions across domains rather than forcing one master account.

This makes the case structurally difficult. Truth theories do not merely disagree over an answer inside a shared frame; they often disagree about the frame that would make an answer count as successful. One account may ask for correspondence to reality, another for systemic coherence, another for practical success, another for semantic regimentation, another for deflationary use, and another for domain-sensitive plurality. The dispute therefore does not present a single determinate object that PMS can simply classify. It presents a field of competing closure protocols. The closure-demand is itself plural.

For AXIOM, this matters because the project is not designed to win such disputes by authority. AXIOM maps closure-demands. It does not replace them with a new metaphysical verdict. The point is to show what becomes visible when truth theories are treated as explicit frames, attractors, recontextualizations, and non-closure pressures. This allows the analysis to say: here is where correspondence demands closure; here is where coherence demands closure; here is where pragmatism changes the success condition; here is where deflationism resists the premise that a deep structure is needed; here is where pluralism prevents forced monism; here is where PMS itself must stop.

The case belongs in AXIOM precisely because it tests whether PMS can remain disciplined under a high-risk philosophical term. “Truth” is a concept that easily attracts authority. It invites finality. It invites the temptation to say that one frame has absorbed all others. It also tempts a framework like PMS to mistake structural readability for philosophical resolution. If PMS can map the truth-theory field without claiming to solve the metaphysics of truth, then the case demonstrates AXIOM’s cartographic discipline: structural visibility without closure by force.

The case is not being solved metaphysically because the supplied material does not contain, and PMS does not provide, a final account of truth-makers, facts, propositions, semantic satisfaction, correspondence relations, or ontological grounding. It is not being solved empirically because no empirical data, observation set, experimental protocol, linguistic corpus, or measurement procedure is supplied. It is not being solved clinically or diagnostically because no person, mental state, pathology, trait, philosophical school as person-type, or clinical pattern is under analysis. It is not being solved legally or forensically because no evidentiary standard, burden of proof, adjudicative forum, liability regime, or forensic scene is present. It is not being solved computationally because the YAML artefact and this prose rendering do not implement a truth-evaluation architecture.

The case is therefore handled as a conceptual-domain projection. The analysis asks what structure appears when truth theories are read through PMS Core and then through the PMS-LOGIC overlay. It asks which operators become active, which remain weak, which would be overclaimed if pushed further, where non-capture must remain, and how the add-on sharpens boundary discipline without redefining PMS Base.

### Scene and frame structure

Because the case is not a concrete praxis scene, the “scene” is conceptual. The boundary is the philosophical problem-space in which theories of truth compete over what counts as truth, truth-making, justification-like fit, semantic validity, successful use, or expressive function. The relevant object is not a person or institution but a field of account-types.

The frame □ consists of truth-account frames. Each named theory type defines a different relevance structure. A correspondence frame treats truth as relation to facts, reality, states of affairs, or truth-makers. A coherence frame treats truth as fit within a wider system of beliefs, propositions, commitments, or inferential relations. A pragmatic frame treats truth in relation to successful practice, inquiry, usefulness, warranted assertibility, or consequences of belief. A semantic frame treats truth through formal structure, satisfaction conditions, models, reference, or language-world relations. A deflationary frame reduces or minimizes substantive truth-theory ambition, often treating truth as a logical or expressive device. A plural-function frame allows different truth roles or properties across domains.

Inside the frame are the distinctions among these accounts, the closure-demands they generate, the points where each account claims sufficiency, the points where each account fails to close for rivals, and the possibility that some residues are not PMS-translatable. Also inside the frame are the PMS application constraints: the analysis may map differences, frames, attractors, recontextualizations, and non-closure; it may not turn those mappings into final truth.

Outside the frame are metaphysical proof, semantic validation, empirical confirmation, diagnosis, legal fact-finding, implementation, governance enforcement, and PMS Base validation. A metaphysical account of correspondence might be correct or incorrect, but this case does not decide that. A deflationary account may resist the very depth-structure that PMS tends to map, and that resistance must remain possible non-capture rather than being redescribed as failure.

Protected constraints are central. The analysis must maintain Χ, meaning reflective distance from verdict. It must preserve reversibility: any structural reading can be revised if a more determinate dispute, formal argument, or concrete scene is supplied. It must preserve Dignity-in-Practice as application restraint, even though no interpersonal dignity scene is active. That means no philosopher, school, or tradition is ranked, humiliated, pathologized, or treated as less mature because of its truth theory.

Roles are not concrete roles in the usual PMS sense. There is no agent, attributor, authority, affected party, or institution described in the case material. One can speak analogically of “theory-frames,” “truth accounts,” “candidate closure protocols,” or “rival formalizations,” but these must not be anthropomorphized. If one later supplied a concrete institutional scene, such as a court, scientific body, AI fact-checking system, or academic controversy, roles and Ω relations could be calibrated. In the present case, they remain absent or merely conceptual.

Temporality Θ is weak. Truth theories obviously have histories in philosophy, but the supplied case material does not provide a historical sequence, genealogy, revision path, institutional trajectory, or action timeline. Therefore Θ cannot be activated as a concrete temporal operator. It can be mentioned only conditionally: theories may stabilize over time, become traditions, or undergo recontextualization across periods, but that is not supplied by the case material itself.

The reversibility window is fully open at the analytic level. Because no concrete binding decision, policy, legal finding, or implementation exists, nothing in the case hardens into Ψ at object level. The analysis remains revisable under rival formalizations and under later case enrichment.

Exposure and asymmetry conditions are weak. There may be conceptual asymmetry if one theory claims explanatory priority over another, or if one criterion is treated as authoritative. But this is not yet Ω in the concrete PMS sense of role, power, exposure, vulnerability, or responsibility gradient. Any stronger Ω claim would overreach.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading begins with Δ and proceeds through the operator inventory in order. The point is not to force all eleven operators into equal activation. The point is to preserve dependency discipline: some operators carry the case strongly, some weakly, some only as application constraints, and some are not applicable at object level.

**Δ — Difference** is active and foundational. The case exists because differences among truth theories are legible. Correspondence differs from coherence; coherence differs from pragmatic success; pragmatic success differs from semantic structure; semantic structure differs from deflationary use; deflationary use differs from plural-function accounts. The case also differentiates truth-bearers and truth-conditions: statements, beliefs, propositions, claims, models, assertions, and their possible relations to facts, systems, practices, semantic rules, or uses. Without Δ, there is no case. The calibration issue is that PMS must preserve these differences rather than flatten them into “all are frames.” The overclaim would be to treat structural differentiability as proof that PMS has captured the philosophical content of each theory.

**∇ — Impulse** is weak to moderately active. The case material speaks of philosophical problems that arise when truth is understood through different accounts. That creates explanatory pressure: one wants to know what makes a claim true, why one account should be preferred, how disagreement should be handled, or whether a plural solution is needed. But ∇ is not a psychological drive, desire, anxiety, or will-to-certainty attributed to philosophers. It is a structural pressure in the problem-space. The overclaim would be to infer motives, pathologies, or maturity failures from the existence of truth-theory disagreement.

**□ — Frame** is strongly active. Each theory of truth functions as a frame that defines what counts as relevant. In a correspondence frame, the relevant question is whether the truth-bearer corresponds to fact or reality. In a coherence frame, the relevant question is whether it fits within a system. In a pragmatic frame, the relevant question is whether it succeeds in practice or inquiry. In a semantic frame, the relevant question is whether formal conditions are satisfied. In a deflationary frame, the relevant question may be whether talk of truth performs a logical or expressive function without requiring a deep property. In a pluralist frame, the relevant question is how truth-functions vary by domain. The calibration issue is to keep each frame explicit. The overclaim would be to assume that because PMS can identify frames, PMS can decide the truth of the frames.

**Λ — Non-Event / meaningful absence** is active but bounded. Truth theories often contain expected closure-events that fail to arrive for rivals. A correspondence account may be asked for a fully satisfactory account of the relation between proposition and fact. A coherence account may be asked why coherent systems cannot be mutually incompatible. A pragmatic account may be asked why success should equal truth rather than usefulness. A semantic account may be asked whether formal satisfaction exhausts truth’s full role. A deflationary account may be asked whether it explains truth’s apparent normative and explanatory roles. A pluralist account may be asked whether plurality collapses into relativism or a higher-order monism. These failures or absences are Λ-relevant. But Λ must be handled with discipline. Absence of closure is not automatically defect, nihilism, immaturity, drift, or incomplete integration. It may be genuine philosophical residue or PMS non-capture. The overclaim would be to treat every unresolved truth-theory problem as PMS-coded failure.

**Α — Attractor** is active. The named truth theories are not random examples; they are stabilized account-families. Correspondence, coherence, pragmatic, semantic, deflationary, and pluralist accounts recur in the philosophical field because they organize the truth problem in durable ways. They attract arguments, objections, refinements, and pedagogical classification. In PMS terms, they are theoretical attractors: patterns that draw repetition and orientation. The calibration issue is to distinguish genuine theoretical stabilization from mere labels. The overclaim would be to infer that attractor stability equals adequacy or authority.

**Ω — Asymmetry** is weak and conditional. The case allows only conceptual asymmetry: a theory may claim priority, a criterion may dominate a debate, or a frame may structure what counts as admissible. But no concrete asymmetry of power, vulnerability, exposure, obligation, authority, or responsibility is supplied. PMS therefore cannot treat truth theories as a scene of domination, accountability, or role-based responsibility. The overclaim would be to read philosophical disagreement itself as Ω in the full sense. Another overclaim would be to say that a theory’s influence or explanatory power gives it structural authority over the others.

**Θ — Temporality** is weak and conditional. A richer historical case could track how truth theories develop, stabilize, split, recur, or become institutionalized over time. This case does not supply that history. Θ therefore remains under-specified. It can be mentioned only as a possible later axis: traditions persist, debates recur, and theoretical attractors may stabilize over time. But no temporal trajectory is available in the completed Core case. The overclaim would be to impose a developmental story, maturity sequence, or historical evolution without evidence.

**Φ — Recontextualization** is active as conceptual frame-shift, but not as fully grounded concrete recontextualization. The case changes when truth is placed under a correspondence frame, then under a coherence frame, then under a pragmatic frame, then under semantic, deflationary, or pluralist frames. Each shift changes what “truth” is expected to do. This is a strong PMS-readable feature. However, Φ normally depends on Θ, Ω, and □ in the PMS dependency table. Because Ω and Θ are weak here, the analysis must qualify Φ: it is active as conceptual recontextualization, not as a concrete transformation across enacted role asymmetry and temporal consequence. The overclaim would be to say that all truth theories are merely PMS recontextualizations and therefore subordinated to PMS.

**Χ — Distance** is strongly active as application discipline. The analysis must suspend verdict. Χ prevents the PMS reading from fusing with a final truth theory. It blocks the move from “PMS can map these accounts” to “PMS has solved truth.” It also blocks the move from “coherence appears structurally important” to “coherence theory wins,” or from “pluralism can coordinate accounts” to “pluralism is therefore true.” Χ is not empty neutrality; it is disciplined non-fusion. The calibration issue is that object-level Χ is not grounded in a concrete scene, but application-level Χ is fully required. The overclaim would be to suspend Χ in order to declare a final metaphysical, semantic, or PMS-superiority result.

**Σ — Integration** is weak and contested. Truth theories can be coordinated, compared, or placed into a plural map. A pluralist account may itself attempt integration. PMS can integrate the field structurally by showing how different closure-demands operate. But integration is not proof. PMS coherence is not sufficient for truth. A smooth map may under-discriminate the very differences that matter most philosophically. Σ therefore remains conditional and must be kept under Χ. The overclaim would be to treat structural integration as philosophical resolution.

**Ψ — Self-Binding** is weak at object level and active only as application discipline. The case does not describe a self-binding agent, institution, practice, or responsibility-bearing trajectory. Truth theories are not subjects. They do not literally bind themselves. The PMS application, however, is self-bound to its own guardrails: no PMS Base revision, no operator mixing, no diagnosis, no person ranking, no closure by authority, no add-on-as-operator, and no drift-as-verdict. Ψ here is analytic discipline, not object-level selfhood. The overclaim would be to anthropomorphize theories or to treat adherence to a theory as a self-binding identity finding.

The Core formula therefore remains bounded:

Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Α ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ, with Ω, Θ, Σ, and Ψ marked weak, conditional, or application-disciplinary in the case-status notes.

This formula should not be read as a new operator order. It is a case formula, not a PMS Base revision. It indicates which operators carry the reading most directly: difference, problem-pressure, frames, unresolved sufficiency, stabilized theory-attractors, conceptual recontextualization, and reflective distance.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The case is especially useful because it shows why later PMS operators cannot be used cheaply. PMS dependency discipline prevents a smooth philosophical map from becoming a closure machine.

Ω cannot be activated merely because one truth theory dominates another in a textbook or argument. Full Ω requires directional imbalance in role, power, exposure, vulnerability, capacity, or responsibility. The case material gives no such scene. Therefore Ω remains weak. If later material described an institution declaring one theory of truth as mandatory in a governance or educational context, Ω could become stronger. But that would be a new scene, not a property of the current case.

Θ cannot be activated merely because the theories have histories in the real world. The case material does not specify a sequence, development, revision path, or temporal trajectory. A historical study of truth theories could activate Θ; the present conceptual comparison does not. This matters because higher structures like Awareness_A, Coherence_C, Responsibility_R, Action_E, and SELF_FIXPOINT depend in different ways on Θ. Weak Θ limits what can be derived.

Φ is therefore calibrated as partially satisfied in conceptual projection. The frame-shifts are real and analytically important, but they are not fully grounded in a concrete temporal-asymmetrical scene. This is why the analysis may speak of conceptual recontextualization, but not of enacted recontextualization under role pressure or consequence chains.

Χ is fully active as application discipline, even where object-level scene conditions are absent. This is not a dependency shortcut, because the analysis is itself an application practice. It must maintain distance from its own closure temptation. Without Χ, PMS would convert its mapping success into authority. Χ prevents that.

Σ remains weak because coherence is not proof. This point is crucial. The truth-theory case includes philosophical coherence theory as one of its object-level accounts. PMS also contains Coherence_C as a derived structure and Σ as Integration. These must not be conflated. A PMS reading can be internally coherent and still fail to discriminate the philosophical problem. It can map correspondence, coherence, pragmatism, semantics, deflationism, and pluralism as frames, while still missing the formal or metaphysical stakes of each. Therefore PMS coherence is not sufficient by itself.

Ψ remains analytic-only because the case does not contain a self-binding subject. The PMS application binds itself to guardrails, but the truth-theory field does not become a self. There is no agentic commitment, no institutional policy, no responsibility-bearing trajectory, and no identity-through-praxis in the case object.

Non-capture is not failure. This is one of the central calibration rules. If a deflationary account resists deep structural modeling, that may be because deflationism is precisely a rival formalization that denies the need for such depth. If formal semantics handles truth-conditions more precisely than PMS, that is not a PMS drift marker; it may be a domain-specific advantage of semantic theory. If metaphysical correspondence raises questions PMS cannot answer, that is not necessarily a defect of the metaphysical account or of PMS; it marks a boundary.

Dependency shortcuts would be invalid in several forms. It would be invalid to move from Δ and □ directly to Ψ by saying: “these theories differ, therefore PMS binds the correct structural account.” It would be invalid to move from Α to Ω by saying: “because a theory is historically stable, it has authority.” It would be invalid to move from Φ to Σ by saying: “because PMS can recontextualize all theories, it integrates them completely.” It would be invalid to move from Σ to truth by saying: “because the PMS account is coherent, the PMS account is correct.” It would be invalid to use Λ as proof of failure, or to treat non-closure as something PMS must eliminate.

The dependency discipline therefore protects both PMS and its rivals. It prevents PMS from overclaiming, and it prevents rival theories from being reduced to mere symptoms of incomplete integration.

### Derived structures

Derived structures are not primitive PMS operators. They are formula-derived constructs. Their use must respect operator dependencies, activation status, and case scope. In this case, most derived structures are weak, not applicable, or analytic-only because the case lacks a concrete praxis scene.

**Awareness_A** is weak. In PMS, Awareness_A is derived from Θ, □, and Δ as sustained, framed differentiation across time. The case clearly has Δ: truth theories differ. It clearly has □: each theory frames truth differently. But Θ is under-specified because no temporal sequence or sustained scene is supplied. Therefore Awareness_A can be projected only weakly. The analysis can say that the case supports framed differentiation among truth accounts, but not that there is a concrete temporally sustained awareness structure. It would be overclaim to infer phenomenological awareness, intellectual maturity, or subject-level reflection from the conceptual field itself.

**Coherence_C** is weak and contested. In PMS, Coherence_C is derived from Θ, Λ, □, and ∇: temporally stabilized structuring of impulse and expectation within a frame. The case contains ∇ as explanatory pressure, □ as theory-frame, and Λ as unresolved sufficiency or non-closure. But Θ is weak. More importantly, the object-level case includes the philosophical coherence theory of truth. That creates a major risk of confusion. PMS Coherence_C is not the coherence theory of truth. PMS Coherence_C does not establish truth. It describes a structural organization of expectation and frame-stability. Therefore Coherence_C remains weak and must be handled with explicit distinction. The strongest correct claim is that PMS can mark coherence pressure; it cannot treat coherence as a truth criterion by itself.

**Responsibility_R** is not applicable at object level. In PMS, Responsibility_R is derived from Ψ, Φ, Θ, and Ω as self-binding under asymmetrical temporal conditions. The truth-theory case supplies no concrete agent, no role obligation, no authority relation, no vulnerability, no consequence chain, no duty, no repair demand, and no self-binding trajectory. Therefore Responsibility_R cannot be activated. It would be a misuse to say that correspondence theorists, coherentists, pragmatists, semantic theorists, deflationists, or pluralists are responsible in any PMS-derived sense merely because they hold or develop a view. Responsibility may become relevant only in a later governance or institutional scene where truth claims are used with consequences.

**Action_E** is not applicable at object level. In PMS, Action_E is derived from Σ, Θ, and ∇ as integrated realization of directedness across time. The case has explanatory pressure, and it may have weak or contested integration, but it lacks an enacted trajectory. There is no decision, intervention, practice, implementation, policy, or behavior sequence. The article itself is an analytic rendering, not a truth-action scene. Therefore Action_E is not activated. The overclaim would be to treat theoretical comparison as action in the full PMS sense.

**Dignity_in_Practice_D** is active only as application constraint. In PMS, Dignity-in-Practice is derived from Ψ, Χ, and Ω as self-binding to restraint and protection in asymmetrical relations. Because Ω and object-level Ψ are not present in a concrete case, D is not an object-level finding. It does not rank theories or persons. It functions as a guardrail on the analysis: no humiliation, no person-ranking, no diagnostic labeling, no moral superiority, no tribunal use, no conversion of disagreement into immaturity. Dignity-in-Practice here means the PMS application must handle rival theories with restraint under possible interpretive asymmetry. It is not ontological dignity and not a moral assessment.

**SELF_FIXPOINT** is not applicable to the case object. The SELF_FIXPOINT chain in PMS represents self-binding as an integrated, reflexively modulated, temporally extended, asymmetry-aware pattern of praxis bound into relatively stable self-relation. Truth theories are not selves. The case provides no subject, no identity-through-praxis, no agentic continuity, and no self-relation. The only relevant use of SELF_FIXPOINT is negative: it reminds the analyst not to project selfhood onto abstract theories or traditions. If an institution later bound itself to a truth-procedure over time, a different case might model structural self-binding. That is not the present case.

The derived-structure result is therefore disciplined: Awareness_A weak; Coherence_C weak and contested; Responsibility_R not applicable; Action_E not applicable; Dignity_in_Practice_D application constraint; SELF_FIXPOINT not applicable. This is not a low score. It is correct non-activation under the case type.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS drift-pattern analysis remains deliberately restrained. No drift is classified as present in the case object. The case concerns abstract truth-theory comparison, not a concrete praxis scene in which conduct, institutional procedure, asymmetrical enforcement, or responsibility-bearing action can be observed. Therefore the drift-pattern section functions as a monitoring layer: it identifies where misuse or overextension would become structurally plausible, without treating that plausibility as a finding.

**AD_A>>E — Excessive distance between Awareness and Enactment** is not established. In PMS terms, this drift concerns a structural imbalance in which awareness or evaluative distance becomes over-expanded while enactment is suppressed, delayed, or structurally disconnected. For the truth-theory case, the relevant axis effects are not a finding but an under-specification: the awareness axis is under-specified, and the action axis is not applicable. The case contains conceptual differentiation, but no action scene. It would therefore be wrong to say that truth theories exhibit awareness without action, or that philosophical reflection here is a maturity failure, evasion, paralysis, or enactment deficit.

The only legitimate use of AD_A>>E in this case is as a caution against over-theorized closure. A highly abstract truth-theory map can become self-satisfied: it can generate increasingly refined distinctions without any corresponding operational scene. But that is not a defect in the case. It is simply a boundary of case type. Because the case does not ask for policy, action, enforcement, implementation, or governance decision, the absence of Action_E is not failure. It is correct non-activation.

**AD_Sigma_low — fragmented integration / low integration** is also not established. It remains a possible but unassigned risk. Truth theories disagree; pluralism remains unresolved; deflationism may resist deeper theory; correspondence and coherence may pull in incompatible directions. But disagreement is not automatically low Σ. Rival theories are not fragments merely because they do not integrate under PMS. PMS must not translate unresolved plurality into incomplete integration by default.

In the completed YAML, the axis effects for AD_Sigma_low are likewise restrained: the coherence axis is under-specified, and the responsibility axis is not applicable. This matters. The case is not saying that truth theories are incoherent. It is saying that PMS cannot infer a full integration failure from the supplied material. The case might instead be one where rival formalizations preserve different aspects of truth that PMS should not collapse: semantic rigor, metaphysical realism, pragmatic practice, deflationary economy, plural-domain sensitivity, or coherence constraints.

The main PMS-level risk is therefore not drift in the object but drift in the analysis. The analysis could become too smooth. It could say: correspondence is a frame, coherence is a frame, pragmatism is a frame, semantics is a frame, deflationism is a frame, pluralism is a frame; therefore PMS maps them all; therefore PMS has the superior structural view. That would be an under-discrimination risk. It would convert coverage into adequacy. The completed YAML marks this risk as high. A framework can cover many things shallowly. The burden is not only to map but to preserve differences that matter.

The PMS drift-pattern status therefore has three conclusions. First, no PMS drift pattern is assigned as a case finding. Second, the main drift risks attach to application misuse: smoothing, over-assimilation, premature integration, and closure by PMS authority. Third, under-specification is not failure. In a non-concrete philosophical case, many higher-order structures must remain weak because the case has not supplied the conditions under which they could responsibly activate.

### Add-on / overlay reading

The relevant add-on is **PMS-LOGIC**. It is used strictly as an overlay/application profile. It does not redefine PMS operators, does not alter PMS dependencies, does not introduce new PMS Base structures, and does not rewrite the PMS Core pass. PMS.yaml remains the only PMS Base reference. LOGIC contributes reduced signatures, boundary constructs, failure-mode constraints, and drift-risk visibility. These are overlay handles, not new operators.

### Example 1 — One sentence, several truth frames

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** after the PMS Core discussion of □, Φ, and Λ, before the dependency-discipline section.

Consider the simple sentence: “The lamp is on.” In an ordinary room, this looks like a trivial truth claim. Someone says it, another person looks at the lamp, and the statement is either accepted or corrected. As an example for the case article, however, the point is not to decide whether the lamp is in fact on. The point is to show how the same sentence becomes structurally different when it is placed into different truth-theory frames.

Under a correspondence frame, the claim demands a relation between the sentence and a state of affairs: the lamp’s being on. The relevant closure condition is whether the statement corresponds to the relevant fact. Under a coherence frame, the sentence is assessed by fit with a wider account: the switch position, the visible light, the room’s electrical state, prior testimony, and other accepted claims. Under a pragmatic frame, the question shifts again: does treating the lamp as on successfully guide action, such as reading, repairing, or leaving the room? Under a semantic frame, the focus moves to the language-system: what “lamp,” “is,” and “on” mean in the relevant model or language, and under what satisfaction conditions the sentence counts as true. Under a deflationary frame, the sentence “It is true that the lamp is on” may add little beyond endorsing or reasserting “The lamp is on.” Under a pluralist frame, several of these uses may be legitimate for different purposes.

The example demonstrates why the case is not simply about one truth claim. PMS Core does not need to decide whether the lamp is on in order to show the frame structure. The same sentence is pulled through different closure protocols. Each frame changes what counts as relevant, what kind of sufficiency is being demanded, and what kind of residue remains when that sufficiency is challenged.

Δ appears in the distinction among the truth frames and in the difference between claim, fact, coherence relation, practical success, semantic condition, and deflationary use. ∇ appears as the pressure to settle the sentence as true or false. □ is central: each truth theory supplies a frame that decides what is relevant. Λ appears where expected closure does not fully arrive. For example, a correspondence account may still face questions about how statement and fact are related; a coherence account may face the possibility of multiple coherent systems; a pragmatic account may face the difference between usefulness and truth; a semantic account may leave open whether formal satisfaction captures truth’s full role; a deflationary account may resist the whole demand for deeper structure.

Α appears because the truth theories operate as stabilized attractors: each provides a familiar way to organize the sentence. Φ appears when the sentence is recontextualized from one frame to another. Χ is required so the analysis does not declare one frame victorious merely because the example is easy to imagine. Σ remains weak: the frames can be coordinated, but their coordination is not proof of a final theory of truth. Ψ remains application-level only: no agent, institution, or self-binding responsibility scene is being analyzed.

The PMS-LOGIC overlay sharpens the example by showing the difference between ordering and final authority. LOGIC can help compare the closure conditions of the correspondence, coherence, pragmatic, semantic, deflationary, and pluralist readings. But the overlay must stop where Λ persists. The example therefore activates `LOGIC_boundary_management` and `NON_CLOSURE_stability`, while leaving post-moral effects, moral readability, effective capacity, and non-innocence inactive.

MIP and AHP are not triggered. The example is not a governance artefact, policy decision, legal fact-finding procedure, institutional truth protocol, or analysis-hardening task. QC is relevant only as internal article discipline: the example must not become evidence for any truth theory, PMS, or PMS-LOGIC.

The example must not claim that correspondence, coherence, pragmatism, semantics, deflationism, pluralism, PMS, or PMS-LOGIC is correct. The example is intentionally simple. Formal semantic theories, metaphysical correspondence theories, ordinary-language approaches, and deflationary accounts may each preserve details that PMS does not capture. The example shows frame-shift, not philosophical victory.

The article takeaway is that a single truth claim can be structurally redistributed across several closure frames. The PMS result is not a final theory of truth, but a disciplined map of how the demand for truth changes when the operative frame changes.

Such a reading may discriminate semantic issues far more precisely than PMS. PMS can map semantic theory as a frame, but it does not replace formal semantics.

A **metaphysical correspondence reading** may ask what facts, states of affairs, truth-makers, propositions, or reality-relations are required for truth. PMS can map the closure-demand of correspondence, but it does not settle the ontology of truth-makers. If metaphysical correspondence theory preserves a question PMS cannot answer, that is not automatically a PMS failure or a correspondence failure; it is a boundary.

A **coherence-theoretic reading** may treat truth as systemic fit, mutual support, or integration within a set of beliefs or propositions. PMS must be especially careful here because PMS contains Coherence_C and Σ. The philosophical coherence theory of truth is not the same as PMS Coherence_C. PMS can map coherence pressure, but it cannot treat its own coherence as proof that coherence theory is true.

A **pragmatic reading** may preserve the role of inquiry, use, success, warranted assertibility, or consequences in ways PMS-LOGIC can only partially capture. PMS may map pragmatic success as a frame or attractor, but pragmatism may have its own account of practice that is not reducible to PMS structure.

A **deflationary or minimalist reading** may resist the entire assumption that truth requires a deep structural account. This is a particularly important non-capture zone. Deflationism may say that the truth predicate performs a logical, expressive, or generalizing function without naming a substantive property. PMS must not treat that resistance as shallow, immature, incomplete, or under-integrated. It may be a rival formalization that intentionally blocks the kind of structural depth PMS tends to generate.

A **pluralist reading** may preserve domain-differentiation more carefully than PMS. It may say that mathematical truth, moral truth, empirical truth, legal truth, and everyday truth do not share one essence but operate through different functions or properties. PMS can map pluralism as a frame and perhaps as an integration attempt, but it must not assume that its own map is the higher-order solution.

An **epistemological reading** may shift the focus from truth to justification, knowledge, warrant, evidence, or belief. PMS-LOGIC can map closure-pressure in justification, but it does not replace epistemology.

A **language-practice reading** may treat truth-talk as embedded in ordinary language, forms of life, assertion, correction, agreement, or rule-governed practice. PMS can map frame and use, but ordinary-language or practice-based approaches may preserve nuances that PMS abstracts away.

An **anti-capture reading** may reject the attempt to map truth theories under a single structural grammar. It may argue that the act of mapping already imposes a meta-frame that distorts the dispute. PMS must keep that objection open. AXIOM is cartographic, not sovereign.

The non-capture zones include the metaphysical nature of truth, formal semantic adequacy, logical validity of specific arguments, truth-maker theory, paradox-handling, domain-specific pluralism, deflationary resistance, and any question requiring detailed argument beyond the supplied case material. None of these may be redescribed automatically as PMS drift, incomplete calibration, incomplete integration, or maturity failure.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS Core reading would be weakened if it failed to discriminate the truth theories it claims to map. If correspondence, coherence, pragmatic, semantic, deflationary, and pluralist accounts all become merely “frames” without preserving their substantive differences, the PMS reading becomes under-discriminating. It may still be elegant, but elegance would not equal adequacy.

The Core reading would also be weakened if it treated non-fit as failure. A rival theory’s resistance to PMS translation may be philosophically meaningful. Deflationism, for example, may reject substantive truth structures for principled reasons. Formal semantics may outperform PMS within formal truth-condition analysis. Metaphysics may pursue truth-maker questions PMS does not address. If the PMS reading absorbs all resistance as Λ-residue inside PMS, it becomes over-assimilative.

The Core reading would be invalidated if it claimed metaphysical truth, empirical validation, diagnostic insight, legal/forensic status, implementation success, or PMS Base validation. None of those claims is supported by the case.

The LOGIC overlay would be weakened if it turned non-closure into a doctrine. LOGIC may say that final closure is not warranted by the supplied case material. It may not say that truth is essentially non-closure, that all truth theories fail, or that logic proves the impossibility of truth. That would convert boundary discipline into metaphysical assertion.

The LOGIC overlay would also be weakened if it treated drift risks as findings. Dogmatism, authority capture, pseudo-ought injection, Λ-suppression, and frame-overconstraint are misuse risks. They are not assigned to the truth theories or their adherents. If the overlay said “coherence theory is dogmatic” or “correspondence theory is authority capture” without a concrete scene, it would overreach.

The reading becomes under-discriminating if it cannot explain what the add-on adds beyond PMS Core. The add-on must not merely restate that frames exist. It must specifically clarify logical boundary management: closure-pressure, sustainable non-closure, Σ-totalization risk, pseudo-authority risk, and the distinction between ordering and final authority. If that extra visibility disappears, the LOGIC overlay becomes decorative.

The reading becomes over-assimilative if it treats every rival formalization as a PMS-LOGIC subcase. Semantic theory is not merely `LOGIC_boundary_management`. Deflationism is not merely Λ. Pragmatism is not merely Α or success-attractor. Pluralism is not merely weak Σ. These mappings may be useful, but they remain partial.

Stronger claims would require additional evidence or scene data. A stronger empirical claim would require observations of how truth claims are used in actual discourse, institutions, science, law, AI systems, or social coordination. A stronger metaphysical claim would require independent philosophical argument about truth-makers, facts, propositions, correspondence, coherence, or plural properties. A stronger semantic claim would require formal semantic analysis. A stronger responsibility claim would require a concrete actor, effective capacity, asymmetry, temporality, and consequence attribution. A stronger implementation claim would require a system architecture, operational criteria, test data, and evaluation.

Operationalization remains unfulfilled. The completed YAML marks comparative validation as required but not fulfilled. To operationalize the case, one would need explicit comparison criteria, calibrated thresholds for operator activation, case-specific truth-theory arguments, rival-model comparison, and external evaluation. For computational use, one would need architecture-specific calibration. For legal or institutional use, one would need governance standards and MIP/AHP-style artefact hardening. None of that is present here.

The falsifier conditions can be summarized as follows. The reading fails if PMS collapses materially different truth theories into one undifferentiated frame. It fails if PMS integration erases disagreement rather than preserving it. It fails if LOGIC treats non-closure as final doctrine. It fails if drift risks become verdicts. It fails if the analysis claims empirical, metaphysical, diagnostic, legal, clinical, implementation, or PMS Base validation. It fails if rival formalizations explain the relevant distinctions more precisely and PMS refuses to acknowledge that limit.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It says nothing about the psychology, pathology, maturity, intelligence, sincerity, or competence of persons who endorse any truth theory.

It must not be used for person ranking. No philosopher, school, tradition, institution, or theory-community is ranked as higher or lower, mature or immature, coherent or incoherent as a human or social type.

It must not be used for legal or forensic conclusion. The analysis does not establish truth standards for courts, evidence, liability, guilt, innocence, testimony, or adjudication.

It must not be used as metaphysical proof. PMS and PMS-LOGIC do not prove correspondence, coherence, pragmatism, semantics, deflationism, pluralism, or any final theory of truth.

It must not be used as empirical proof. No observations, experiments, corpus data, or behavioral evidence are supplied.

It must not be used as implementation validation. A YAML artefact or Markdown article is not a truth-evaluation engine, fact-checking model, semantic parser, theorem prover, or AI reasoning architecture.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. The case may show that PMS can map a truth-theory field in a disciplined way. It does not prove PMS completeness, uniqueness, superiority, or finality.

It must not treat PMS-LOGIC as a new operator system. LOGIC reduced signatures are overlay shorthand only. They do not redefine Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, or Ψ.

It must not treat drift as verdict. Dogmatism, authority capture, pseudo-ought injection, Λ-suppression, and frame-overconstraint are risks in reasoning or application. They are not findings about theories or persons unless a concrete scene separately warrants that classification.

It must not close by authority. PMS, LOGIC, coherence, semantic form, pragmatic success, correspondence, deflationary elegance, or pluralist inclusion may organize a frame. None may silently become final authority in this case. Where Λ remains, it must remain visible.

The misuse boundary is not an add-on to the analysis. It is part of the result. The case demonstrates AXIOM’s core discipline: map closure-demands, show structural visibility, preserve non-capture, and refuse verdict inflation.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result is that **Truth theories** is best treated as a layered boundary case: PMS Core renders the field as a structured plurality of truth-account frames, while PMS-LOGIC sharpens the specific closure-pressure generated by logical, semantic, coherentist, pragmatic, correspondence, deflationary, and pluralist demands. PMS Core identifies Δ distinctions, □ frames, Λ residues, Α theoretical attractors, Φ conceptual recontextualizations, and Χ application distance. It also correctly weakens Ω, Θ, Σ, and Ψ where the case lacks a concrete praxis scene. PMS-LOGIC then adds a more precise account of the logical boundary: truth theories generate pressure toward closure, but the absence of final closure must be carried rather than filled.

The examples contribute by making this result readable without changing its claim status. The lamp example shows how one simple truth claim changes when placed into different frames; it demonstrates frame-shift, not a theory of truth. The deflationary example shows why resistance to deeper structural mapping can itself be a legitimate rival reading rather than a failure to integrate. The model-report example shows how integrated support can be mistaken for final truth when the frame, scope, Λ-residue, and revision conditions remain underdeclared. None of the examples functions as evidence for PMS, LOGIC, or any truth theory. They also do not activate empirical, legal, implementation, governance, or responsibility layers. They are demonstrations of the article’s boundary discipline: each example shows a closure-pressure, then stops before converting structural readability into proof.

What remains unclaimed is as important as what is claimed. The case does not establish the metaphysics of truth. It does not choose between correspondence, coherence, pragmatism, semantics, deflationism, pluralism, or any other account. It does not validate PMS Base. It does not diagnose philosophical positions. It does not rank traditions. It does not provide legal, forensic, clinical, empirical, or implementation conclusions. It does not show that PMS-LOGIC is superior to rival formalisms. It shows only what becomes structurally visible when the completed PMS Core case is inspected through the LOGIC overlay.

The boundary discipline is therefore the article’s central achievement. Coherence is not proof. Translation is not capture. Non-closure is not failure. Resistance is not drift. Risk is not finding. Non-assignment is not low score. Dignity-in-Practice remains a praxeological application constraint, not ontological ranking. LOGIC reduced signatures remain overlay handles, not PMS operators. MIP and AHP remain inactive because no governance or hardening task has been triggered.

The final methodological takeaway is that AXIOM can make truth-theory disputes structurally legible without claiming to solve them by authority. The case shows where closure is demanded, where frames differ, where integration is tempting, where LOGIC must stop, and where Λ-residue must remain visible. Its value is not a new truth theory, but a disciplined cartography of truth-theory closure-demands under PMS Core and PMS-LOGIC boundary management.
