### Why this case matters

Conflict is one of the places where philosophical closure-demand becomes most tempting. The word often invites immediate sorting: conflict must be solved, adjudicated, integrated, overcome, diagnosed, escalated, mediated, or assigned to a cause. In ordinary discourse, this pressure is so strong that the existence of conflict is quickly converted into a claim about defect. If two trajectories do not fit, one must be irrational, immature, incoherent, morally wrong, insufficiently integrated, or inadequately calibrated. The present case resists that closure move.

The case concerns conflict in a specifically philosophical sense: practical, conceptual, or institutional trajectories may become structurally incompatible even when each remains locally coherent within its own frame. This is difficult because it breaks a common assumption about coherence. A trajectory may be coherent locally and still fail globally. A conceptual scheme may make sense inside its own terms and still collide with another scheme under shared use. An institution may follow its own mandate and still create incompatibility with another institutional logic. A practical commitment may remain internally disciplined while becoming non-viable when carried into a different frame, time horizon, or coordination environment.

That is why the case belongs in AXIOM. AXIOM is not concerned with producing a final theory of conflict. It maps closure-demands: where a case demands settlement, what kind of settlement is being demanded, which frame supports that demand, which residues remain, and which layer of the stack can make the demand legible without turning the analysis into a verdict. Conflict as structural incompatibility is an especially good AXIOM case because it tests the discipline of the stack. PMS Core can render difference, frame, trajectory, recontextualization, integration, self-binding, and non-capture. PMS-CONFLICT can then ask whether this already amounts to conflict in the stronger sense of stabilized incompatibility under binding, time, cost, exposure, and asymmetry. MIP can ask whether the produced artefact is being handled responsibly. AHP can ask whether the analysis itself has attack surfaces that should be hardened before transmission.

The closure-demand here is not merely “what is conflict?” It is more precise: when two structures remain locally coherent but cannot be jointly carried, must the situation be treated as failed integration, or can incompatibility remain visible without being converted into defect? That demand matters because philosophical and institutional reasoning often presupposes that enough integration, enough recontextualization, enough dialogue, or enough maturity should dissolve conflict. The completed YAML analyses refuse that presupposition. They hold open the possibility that some conflicts are not merely under-integrated; they may be non-integrable under the available frame, or better captured by rival grammars such as semantic incommensurability, logical contradiction, institutional rule collision, material scarcity, systems differentiation, or tragic pluralism.

This is also why the case is not solved metaphysically. The analysis does not ask what conflict ultimately is in being. It does not claim that incompatibility belongs to the ontology of the world, consciousness, society, or value. Nor is it solved empirically: no data are supplied about actual actors, decisions, failures, or institutions. It is not solved clinically or diagnostically: no person, group, or institution is assessed for pathology, maturity, personality, cognitive failure, or emotional deficiency. It is not solved legally or forensically: no culpability, liability, evidence standard, responsibility allocation, or sanction is derived. And it is not solved as implementation: no algorithm, governance procedure, intervention script, or operational decision rule follows.

The case matters because it demonstrates a disciplined form of non-closure. PMS and the CONFLICT overlay make something visible, but they do not license capture. MIP then confirms that the analysis should remain a private structural working note unless concrete roles, evidence, publicness, reversibility, and affected perspectives are supplied. AHP confirms that the analysis artefact is precise enough for private structural use, but still vulnerable to density overread, D-language overread, IA-box overread, and hidden-score overread if excerpted or transmitted without guardrails.

The philosophical importance of the case lies in that layered restraint. The analysis is strongest where it does not force strength. It can say: local coherence and shared integration are distinct problems. It can say: weak conflict-legibility is not terminal conflict. It can say: non-capture is not analytical failure. It can say: rival readings are not defects. It can say: PMS coherence is not proof. That combination makes the case a substantial test of AXIOM’s method.

### Scene and frame structure

Because the case is not a concrete scene, the “scene” must be understood as a conceptual scene. The scene is the structural question itself: how practical, conceptual, or institutional trajectories can remain coherent within their own frames while becoming incompatible across frames. This means the case has a frame problem rather than a scene record.

The primary frame □ is the frame of local coherence. Each trajectory is assumed to have internal conditions under which its continuation makes sense. A practical trajectory may be coherent under a role, commitment, strategy, or action horizon. A conceptual trajectory may be coherent under a theory, vocabulary, inference pattern, or distinction scheme. An institutional trajectory may be coherent under a mandate, procedure, rule-system, or accountability structure. The case becomes structurally interesting because local coherence does not settle cross-frame compatibility.

Inside the frame are differentiated trajectories, their local standards of coherence, their directionality, their temporal extension, and the question of cross-frame relation. The analysis may ask how Δ distinguishes the trajectories, how ∇ gives them directional pressure, how □ frames their local coherence, how Θ makes them trajectories rather than static claims, how Φ tests what happens when one structure is placed into another frame, how Χ prevents premature adjudication, and how Σ remains contested as possible integration, false smoothing, coercive assimilation, partial coordination, or genuine non-integrability.

Outside the frame are empirical findings about actual conflicts, diagnostic interpretations of people, legal conclusions about liability, clinical claims, governance decisions, institutional prescriptions, metaphysical accounts of conflict, and claims about final explanatory adequacy. Also outside the frame is any automatic conversion of non-fit into drift. The analysis must not treat resistance, rupture, absence, disagreement, rival framing, or non-alignment as failed integration or maturity failure by default.

The protected constraints are those that keep the analysis valid as PMS application: Distance Χ, reversibility, and Dignity-in-Practice as praxeological application constraint. Distance requires the analysis not to fuse into verdict. Reversibility requires all structural readings to remain revisable. Dignity-in-Practice requires the analysis not to shame, rank, humiliate, expose, or ontologically evaluate persons or groups. In the MIP output, this is strengthened by the insistence that D0 remains untouched, D1/D2 are not assessed, no dignity score exists, and D remains inactive as case content.

Roles are mostly absent as case content. The PMS Core analysis names no concrete roles. The MIP analysis, however, identifies analytic roles for guardrail purposes: the object of observation is a conceptual structural constellation; the observer is a formalization assistant and structural analyst; possible abstract roles include frame-holder, affected role, mediator, institution, and analyst. These are not findings about participants. They are safeguards against role confusion.

Temporality Θ is central at the conceptual level because the case uses the language of trajectories. Conflict is not rendered as an isolated contradiction between propositions, but as incompatibility between developments, continuations, sequences, or path-dependent structures. However, the case does not provide temporal scale, rate of lock-in, accumulated cost, recurrence history, or compression. The CONFLICT overlay therefore treats Θ as conceptually active but insufficient for terminal conflict classification.

The reversibility window remains open. Since no concrete binding, sanction, public record, or irreversible consequence is supplied, the analysis can be revised, rejected, replaced, or compared with rival formalizations. MIP classifies the current artefact as a private structural working note. AHP confirms that its transmission status should not be upgraded. Reversibility would shrink if the analysis were attached to actors, inserted into institutional records, used in public, or converted into governance/evaluation language.

Exposure and asymmetry conditions remain weak. Ω is not established as case content. There may be asymmetry in many actual conflicts, but this abstract case does not state power, exposure, capacity, vulnerability, authority, obligation, liability, publicness, or exit-cost gradients. The only active asymmetry named by MIP concerns the analysis artefact: formal model language may have interpretive authority and could harden into verdict if misused. That is an analysis-transmission asymmetry, not a case-content finding.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading proceeds through the canonical operator inventory: Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, Ψ. The order matters because later operators cannot be used cheaply. Each operator has a case-specific role, but not every operator is equally active.

**Δ — Difference** is active. The case cannot begin without distinguishable trajectories, frames, positions, norms, practices, concepts, or institutional logics. The primary difference is not yet conflict; it is structural distinction. The analysis therefore begins by separating local coherence from cross-frame compatibility. A trajectory may be internally coherent, but difference becomes conflict only when additional conditions arise: coordination pressure, frame collision, temporal extension, failed translation, asymmetry, blocked integration, or non-event structure. The overclaim would be to treat difference as opposition by itself. PMS discipline requires Δ to remain minimal: it makes contrast legible; it does not already decide the relation between contrasted structures.

**∇ — Impulse** is active but must remain structural rather than psychological. The case speaks of trajectories, and trajectories imply directionality: continuation, enactment, explanation, institutional reproduction, conceptual development, or commitment pressure. ∇ names this directional tension. It does not mean subjective desire, will, intention, affect, motivation, or pathology. The calibration issue is whether the trajectory is merely a static difference or has genuine directional pressure. If no continuation tendency, orientation, or activation can be shown, ∇ would be weakened. The overclaim would be to infer psychology from trajectory language.

**□ — Frame** is active and central. The case explicitly states that each trajectory remains locally coherent within its own frame. The frame defines relevance, admissibility, coherence criteria, role-space, and boundary conditions. Without □, “locally coherent” has no structural force. The analysis must ask: coherent under what practical frame, conceptual frame, institutional frame, normative frame, procedural frame, or interpretive frame? It must also avoid frame imperialism: one frame’s coherence criteria cannot simply be imposed on another. The overclaim would be to treat a single frame as globally authoritative, or to silently shift frames while claiming continuity.

**Λ — Non-Event** is weak and conditional. It becomes relevant only where an expected occurrence fails, delays, or remains absent within a frame. In conflict cases, possible Λ events include absent mediation, failed translation, missing recognition, non-occurring convergence, delayed coordination, or absent shared criterion. But incompatibility alone does not establish Λ. A conflict may be present without a meaningful expected event. The calibration issue is therefore strict: what was expected, by whom or by which frame, and what did not occur? The overclaim would be to infer structured absence from mere disagreement, rupture, missing evidence, or non-fit. PMS must preserve the possibility that absence is not a PMS Non-Event but a sign of rival formalization, semantic incommensurability, or non-capture.

**Α — Attractor** is moderately active but under-specified. The word “trajectory” suggests stabilization, recurrence, or path-dependence. Practical, conceptual, and institutional trajectories often repeat, sediment, and become self-reinforcing. However, the abstract material does not give repeated episodes, evidence of routine, institutional history, or recurrence. Α is therefore plausible but not fully established. The overclaim would be to treat any trajectory as a stabilized attractor. A single incompatibility is not enough. Α requires pattern formation, not merely difference.

**Ω — Asymmetry** is weak and conditional. Actual conflicts often involve power, exposure, authority, vulnerability, obligation, capacity, publicness, or exit-cost gradients. The case material does not specify any of these. PMS Core therefore refuses to infer Ω from incompatibility alone. This is a major calibration point. Conflict does not equal asymmetry. Incompatibility does not equal domination. Local coherence does not imply equal exposure, but neither does cross-frame non-fit prove unequal power. The overclaim would be to treat every conflict as already structured by power imbalance, or to convert Ω into moral blame. In this case, Ω is mainly a guardrail: if future concrete material supplies asymmetry, it must be made explicit before responsibility, dignity-stress, or CONFLICT drift risks are activated.

**Θ — Temporality** is active in the conceptual sense. The case concerns trajectories, not isolated claims. Θ makes sequence, continuation, accumulation, horizon, delay, and path-dependence available. It allows the analysis to ask how locally coherent structures become incompatible over time, under repeated enactment, institutional hardening, or cross-frame carryover. Yet full PMS dependency hygiene is important: Θ depends on Ω and Α in the PMS table, and Ω is weak here. The analysis therefore treats Θ as strongly indicated by case language but dependency-limited as applied finding. The overclaim would be to turn trajectory language into detailed temporal scaling without evidence: no lock-in rate, history compression, accumulated cost, or recurrence band can be assigned.

**Φ — Recontextualization** is active as an analytic test. The case turns on what happens when a structure coherent in one frame is placed in relation to another. Recontextualization may clarify incompatibility, transform it, worsen it, reveal a hidden fit, or falsely assimilate a non-fit. It does not automatically repair. The PMS Core reading must specify source and target frames before claiming Φ: conceptual-to-practical, institutional-to-conceptual, institutional-to-institutional, or practical-to-institutional. The overclaim would be to absorb every rival framing into PMS by saying it has simply been recontextualized. Proper Φ preserves non-capture. It may reveal that integration is not available.

**Χ — Distance** is active as the application stance. It is not merely one operator among others here; it is the condition that prevents the analysis from becoming a tribunal. Because the case is about conflict, the pressure to adjudicate is high. Χ suspends the urge to decide which trajectory is more mature, more coherent, more rational, or more PMS-compatible. It preserves reversibility and non-verdict status. It distinguishes structural analysis from enforcement, diagnosis, moral ranking, or closure by authority. But Χ is not avoidance, denial, false neutrality, disengagement, or refusal to acknowledge consequences in applied scenes. If a concrete scene later supplies harm, power, or duty, Χ cannot be used to evade accountability. In this abstract case, however, Χ is the main safeguard against premature closure.

**Σ — Integration** is weak and contested. The case explicitly raises the question of whether locally coherent but mutually incompatible trajectories can be coordinated or synthesized. But it does not show that integration is possible, required, mature, or adequate. This is one of the most important points in the whole analysis. PMS coherence is not sufficient by itself. A clean operator mapping does not prove that the conflict should be integrated. Lack of integration may indicate under-integration, but it may also indicate genuine non-integrability, tragic pluralism, semantic incommensurability, institutional rule collision, material scarcity, or a conflict whose “resolution” would be coercive assimilation. The overclaim would be to treat every incompatibility as incomplete Σ or to confuse forced coherence with maturity.

**Ψ — Self-Binding** is weak and conditional. Ψ becomes relevant if a trajectory is durably bound into commitment, institutional self-relation, role obligation, stable normativity, or self-binding over time. The abstract case does not establish such binding. It does not model a person, a concrete institution, a policy, or a role-holder. However, Ψ is active as an application discipline: the analysis must bind itself to its own guardrails. It must not use the elegance of PMS to erase rival formalizations, silence non-capture, or convert structural incompatibility into maturity hierarchy. As case content, Ψ remains unestablished. The overclaim would be to treat abstract trajectories as literal selfhood, consciousness, or stable commitment without evidence.

The PMS Core reading therefore produces a bounded formula: under Χ, two or more differentiated, directionally organized, framed, temporally extended, possibly stabilized trajectories may remain locally coherent while becoming incompatible across frames. Φ, Σ, Λ, Ω, and Ψ remain conditional. The core result is not “conflict explained,” but “conflict rendered structurally readable without final capture.”

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The completed YAML analysis places strong emphasis on dependency hygiene. Later PMS operators cannot be treated as decorative vocabulary. They carry prerequisites and risk. This matters because abstract philosophical cases often invite smooth formulae. The smoother the mapping, the greater the danger that PMS becomes a closure machine.

The first dependency risk is premature Ω. Because conflict frequently appears with power, it is tempting to infer asymmetry from incompatibility. The case refuses this. Ω requires actual structural imbalance: power, exposure, capacity, vulnerability, obligation, authority, or role-gradient. Without such evidence, Ω remains weak. That refusal is not a failure to notice politics; it is a discipline against overclaiming.

The second risk is premature Λ. It is tempting to call every absence, silence, rupture, delay, missing agreement, or unresolved relation a Non-Event. PMS does not allow that. Λ requires a structured expectation within a frame. If no expected coordination, recognition, mediation, translation, convergence, or decision is specified, Λ remains conditional. This is crucial because absence may also be evidence of non-capture or rival incommensurability rather than an internal PMS residue.

The third risk is premature Α. Trajectories may stabilize, but not every trajectory is an attractor. Attractor claims require recurrence, repetition, path-dependence, routine, or sedimentation. The abstract case does not supply that evidence. Α remains plausible but under-specified.

The fourth risk is premature Θ in the strong sense. The case language justifies temporality as trajectory structure, but not temporal scaling. It does not justify claims about compression, lock-in, accumulated cost, closing reversibility windows, or history bands. The CONFLICT overlay later preserves this point by rejecting terminality and Θ-history classification.

The fifth risk is premature Φ. Recontextualization is a powerful operator because it can make cross-frame incompatibility legible. But it can also become assimilative. If every rival reading is redescribed as a recontextualization inside PMS, PMS stops being a disciplined grammar and becomes an absorption mechanism. Valid Φ requires source frame, target frame, transformation, and preserved possibility that the translation fails.

The sixth risk is premature Σ. This is the central dependency danger. Because the case concerns incompatibility, there is strong pressure to ask whether the conflict can be integrated. But integration in PMS depends on Χ and Φ. It is not simply coherence. It is synthesis of disparate or conflicting elements into a more coherent whole. In this case, Σ must discriminate between genuine synthesis, partial coordination, false smoothing, coercive assimilation, and non-integrable pluralism. If it cannot, PMS is under-discriminating.

The seventh risk is premature Ψ. Self-binding depends on Σ, Θ, and Χ. If Σ is weak, Θ is abstract, and Χ is application stance, then Ψ cannot become a robust case-content claim. It can remain an analytic constraint on the analysis itself, but it cannot be used to infer identity, responsibility, or institutional commitment unless further material is supplied.

PMS coherence is therefore not proof. The YAML artefact can be valid, complete, and internally coherent while still leaving the phenomenon under-captured. The analysis openly names this coherence risk. Abstract conflict can be mapped onto many PMS operators, and that mapping may become too smooth. High coherence may hide low discrimination. The solution is not to weaken the analysis artificially, but to preserve calibration thresholds, rival readings, falsifier conditions, non-capture zones, and operationalization requirements.

Non-capture is not failure. It is part of the result. If a conflict is better formalized as logical contradiction, semantic incommensurability, institutional rule collision, material scarcity, systems differentiation, game-theoretic conflict, political antagonism, or tragic pluralism, that does not defeat the case. It prevents PMS from overstepping. AXIOM is precisely a cartography of such boundaries: where closure is possible, where an overlay is justified, and where residue must remain visible.

Dependency shortcuts would be invalid in several forms. Δ/∇ → Ψ would be invalid: difference plus closure pressure does not license binding commitment or verdict. □ → Σ would be invalid: local frame coherence does not establish shared integration. Φ → Σ would be invalid: recontextualization does not guarantee synthesis. Ω → R would be invalid: asymmetry does not by itself establish responsibility. Λ → drift would be invalid: absence does not automatically mean failure, pathology, or under-integration. Σ → PMS validation would be invalid: coherent integration inside the model does not validate the model.

The PMS Core layer is therefore not a machine for resolving conflict. It is a disciplined grammar for refusing the wrong closures.

Together, these derived-structure results show the discipline of the PMS Core pass. Awareness is structurally active; local coherence is visible; responsibility is not established; action is under-specified; dignity is application-constraint only; SELF_FIXPOINT is analytic-only. This pattern is not a failure of analysis. It is the analysis doing its work: refusing to fill absent scene material with stronger claims than the case warrants.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS Core pass treats drift patterns as conditional structural risks, not findings. This distinction is decisive. A drift pattern in PMS is not a diagnosis, not a person-type, not a moral verdict, not a maturity label, and not evidence that a case has failed. It is a possible distortion form that becomes relevant only when the required operators and dependency conditions are actually present.

In this case, two PMS drift patterns are considered: **AD_A>>E** and **AD_Sigma_low**.

**AD_A>>E** remains possible but not established. This pattern would require a configuration in which evaluative or positional asymmetry becomes stabilized through Α, then recontextualized through Φ in a way that expands awareness or evaluative framing while suppressing or chronically delaying enactment. In more ordinary terms, the analysis would have to show that a conflict structure generates enlarged interpretive or evaluative visibility but does not translate into integrated action. The completed PMS YAML does not support that claim. The case material contains no concrete asymmetry, no stabilized evaluative hierarchy, no exposure gradient, no action sequence, and no evidence that enactment is being suppressed or delayed. Therefore the awareness axis and action axis remain under-specified, not over-expanded or suppressed.

This non-assignment matters. It prevents the analysis from converting philosophical conflict into a hidden complaint about inaction, evasion, over-reflection, or maturity failure. A conflict may remain unresolved without being an AD_A>>E pattern. A frame may resist integration without being a case of excessive awareness. An abstract philosophical incompatibility may be structurally legible without becoming a drift finding.

**AD_Sigma_low** is the more relevant candidate, but it too remains weak and conditional. The pattern would apply if repeated recontextualization outpaced integration, producing volatile coherence and inconsistent responsibility. The case does concern local coherence without established shared integration, so there is an understandable temptation to classify it as low Σ. The completed PMS YAML deliberately resists that step. Structural incompatibility alone does not prove under-integration. It may indicate low Σ, but it may also indicate genuine non-integrability, tragic pluralism, semantic incommensurability, institutional rule collision, material scarcity, or the absence of a shared frame.

The axis effects therefore remain under-specified. The coherence axis is not classified as volatile. The responsibility axis is not classified as inconsistent. Responsibility_R itself is not established, because the case lacks the required Ψ, Φ, Θ, and Ω configuration. A low-Σ reading would become stronger only if the analysis could show that integration was structurally available, non-coercive, required by the frame, and nevertheless not carried. None of that is supplied.

The resulting PMS drift-pattern status is disciplined: risk is not finding, under-specification is not failure, and non-assignment is not a low score. The case does not say that conflict is mature or immature, integrated or under-integrated, responsible or irresponsible. It says that conflict may be structurally readable as local coherence without established shared integration, while preserving the possibility that integration is not the right standard at all.

The coherence risk remains high. Because the PMS operator grammar can produce a smooth map of the case, the analysis could become under-discriminating if it treats the map as sufficient. This is why the YAMLs emphasize falsifier conditions, rival readings, and operationalization requirements. A coherent PMS derivation is not enough. It must show discriminative force: it must distinguish mere difference from conflict, local coherence from shared integration, non-event from missing evidence, recontextualization from assimilation, and non-integrability from failed maturity.

### Add-on / overlay reading

The relevant add-on is **PMS-CONFLICT**. It is applied as an overlay/application profile only. It does not redefine PMS operators, does not alter PMS dependencies, does not rewrite the PMS Core pass, and does not add new base operators. Its reduced signatures are shorthand lenses and threshold markers, not proof chains. Its drift catalogue provides conditional risk visibility, not verdicts.

PMS Core alone makes the case structurally legible: differentiated trajectories, framed local coherence, conditional non-events, possible attractor formation, weak or absent asymmetry, temporality as trajectory, recontextualization as cross-frame test, distance as application discipline, contested integration, and weak self-binding. However, PMS Core does not isolate the specifically conflict-theoretical threshold between ordinary incompatibility and the stronger CONFLICT regime. That is what the overlay adds.

The CONFLICT overlay asks whether structural incompatibility has become stabilized under binding, time, cost, exposure, and asymmetry. It sharpens the difference between weak conflict-legibility and terminal conflict classification. This is especially important here because the case title itself contains the word “conflict.” Without the overlay, the analysis might stop at the PMS Core finding that local coherence and shared integration are distinct. With the overlay, it can say more carefully: this case is conflict-legible, but it does not reach the strong PMS-CONFLICT terminal regime.

The overlay’s **core conflict-domain layer** is marked weak. The case approaches the conflict-domain layer only as conceptual projection. It is conflict-legible because it explicitly concerns locally coherent trajectories that may become structurally incompatible across frames. But PMS-CONFLICT in the strong sense requires more: stabilized incompatibility under binding Ψ, temporality Θ, asymmetry Ω, and costly or asymmetric distance Χ, with Σ no longer carrying as shared integration. The present case supplies only part of this. Θ is conceptually active because trajectories are central. Σ is at issue because local coherence may fail to become shared integration. Χ is maintained as analytic distance. But Ψ and Ω are under-specified, and Χ is not shown to be priced or asymmetric. Therefore the correct result is weak CONFLICT projection, not terminal CONFLICT classification.

The **CONFLICT terminal core** reduced signature is not applicable. Its operators are Σ, Ψ, Ω, Θ, and Χ. It marks the terminal descriptive regime in which Σ is under load with binding Ψ and priced or asymmetric distance Χ under Ω/Θ. This is not fulfilled. The case does not establish binding, asymmetry, priced distance, or terminal exhaustion of integration. The signature remains a future comparison threshold only.

The **stabilized conflict attractor** signature is weak. It would require Α, Ω, Θ, Ψ, and Χ in a configuration where conflict itself stabilizes as an attractor and distance remains present but priced or asymmetric. The abstract case supports only the possibility that incompatibility could stabilize across trajectories. It does not show recurrence, hardening, exposure pressure, binding persistence, asymmetric cost, or priced exit. Therefore Α_conflict remains a weak candidate, not a finding.

The **binding collision** signature is a risk, not active drift. Binding collision would require two or more Ψ-bindings that collide and remain jointly non-viable under the same frame and time trajectory, with shared Σ unable to produce an acceptable common spine. The case mentions practical, conceptual, or institutional trajectories, which may imply commitments in future concrete versions. But it does not identify two or more explicit self-bindings, institutional obligations, contracts, role duties, or durable commitments. Binding collision is therefore a structural risk only if later material supplies incompatible bindings.

The **asymmetry lock-in** signature is not applicable. It would require Ω, Θ, and Χ in a configuration where exit, reflection, delay, or withdrawal becomes asymmetrically priced. One position can withdraw, reframe, or delay at lower cost, while another bears exposure or liability. The case gives no such cost distribution. It does not show unequal exit, reflection, delay, liability, exposure, or withdrawal cost. Ω remains unestablished, and priced/asymmetric Χ is absent.

The **exposure capture** signature is not applicable. Exposure capture occurs when recontextualization under scaled exposure converts interpretation into enforcement affordance: statements harden into irreversible pressure rather than remaining revisable, scene-bound readings. The case contains no publicness scale, no audience coupling, no durable public record, no enforcement affordance, and no irreversible pressure channel. Exposure capture remains only a misuse-boundary risk if the analysis is excerpted or transferred into public or institutional circulation.

The **dignity collapse** signature is not applicable. Dignity collapse would require failure of Dignity-in-Practice constraints under load: Χ is delegitimized or priced, Ψ no longer binds restraint, and language drifts toward shaming, ranking, humiliation, or coercive transfer under Ω/Θ pressure. The completed analyses do the opposite. They preserve Χ, reversibility, non-diagnosis, non-ranking, and D as application constraint. No shaming, ranking, humiliation, person-near verdict, or coercive exposure appears.

The strongest CONFLICT-relevant drift risk is **Σ-incompatibility clash**. This drift class captures cases where local integrations remain coherent but shared integration fails or is missing under load. The present case clearly resembles the first half of that pattern: it explicitly names local coherence with cross-frame structural incompatibility. The overlay therefore sharpens a distinction already present in PMS Core: local coherence inside a frame is not the same as shared integration across frames.

But the overlay refuses to classify this as an active drift. The full Σ-incompatibility clash signature involves Σ_local, missing or failed Σ_shared, and load conditions involving Ψ, Ω, Θ, and often priced/asymmetric Χ. The present case does not establish binding Ψ, asymmetry Ω, priced/asymmetric Χ, unacceptable integration cost, recurrence, or terminal exhaustion of Σ. It would be overreach to conclude that integration has failed, that a party is immature, that the conflict is merely a misunderstanding, or that the incompatibility must be repaired through a higher PMS synthesis. The disciplined status is: Σ_local/Σ_shared tension is visible; Σ-incompatibility clash remains conditional.

The CONFLICT overlay also introduces non-operator constructs. These include conflict attractor Α_conflict, binding collision, asymmetry lock-in, exposure capture, dignity collapse, young Α-field dynamics, institutionalized distance regimes, application-boundary scaling, and terminal regime projection. Each construct is explicitly marked as non-operator. They organize existing PMS operators; they do not add to the PMS operator inventory. This is crucial for non-mixing discipline. The overlay may add visibility, but it cannot become a second base model.

The overlay’s scale matrices are mostly not applicable as case content. Θ-history scaling is weak because no temporal pressure, compression, lock-in pace, or recent attractor formation is supplied. Young Α-field dynamics are not applicable. Institutionalized Χ scaling is not applicable because Χ appears as analytic distance, not as an institutional distance regime. Terminal regime projection is not applicable because the case remains below the threshold where internal reintegration grammar is exhausted under sustained Θ/Ω/Ψ and costly or asymmetric Χ.

Application-boundary scaling is active, but as safety interface rather than case content. The mode is descriptive. Publicness is private. Misuse gradient is low within this bounded use, rising if excerpted without boundary declarations or transferred into public, institutional, evaluative, or person-near contexts. This is one of the overlay’s most important contributions: it does not merely analyze conflict; it marks how conflict language itself can become dangerous if moved into exposure regimes.

The CONFLICT firewall is therefore part of the result. PMS-CONFLICT must not become a solution manual, diagnostic tool, moral tribunal, personality model, enforcement script, governance authority, or PMS Base validation device. It may make stabilized incompatibility, local/shared Σ tension, binding-collision risk, asymmetry lock-in risk, exposure capture, dignity-collapse risk, and terminal-regime thresholds more visible; it may not convert those handles into findings without the required operator configuration. In this case, the firewall matters because the case title itself invites escalation. The overlay prevents that escalation by distinguishing weak conflict-legibility from terminal CONFLICT classification.

The CONFLICT synthesis therefore reads as follows: PMS-CONFLICT makes visible that the core case most strongly resembles a local/shared Σ tension. Locally coherent trajectories may fail to consolidate into shared integration, but the full CONFLICT terminal regime is not reached because binding Ψ, asymmetry Ω, priced/asymmetric Χ, exposure capture, and terminal Σ exhaustion are not established. The overlay strengthens discrimination rather than classification. It prevents the case from being read too quickly either as mere disagreement or as terminal conflict.

### Layer interaction

The PMS Core reading remains the base structural analysis. It supplies the operator grammar, dependency discipline, derived structures, and boundary conditions. It shows how conflict as structural incompatibility can be rendered through Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, and Ψ without turning the case into a verdict. It establishes the core distinction between local coherence and shared integration. It also establishes that many operators remain weak or conditional because the case is abstract and non-concrete.

The PMS-CONFLICT overlay sharpens the conflict-specific threshold structure. It does not change the PMS Core result; it specifies what would be needed for stronger CONFLICT claims. It separates weak conflict-legibility from stabilized conflict attractor, binding collision, asymmetry lock-in, exposure capture, dignity collapse, and terminal conflict. Its main contribution is not to intensify the result, but to prevent overclassification. The overlay says: this is a conflict-readable case, but not terminal conflict; it shows Σ_local/Σ_shared tension, but not established Σ-incompatibility drift; it raises binding collision as future risk, but not current finding.

MIP then shifts the object of analysis. It does not re-analyze conflict as a case-content maturity problem. Instead, it examines the analysis artefact and its use conditions. MIP asks whether the completed PMS and CONFLICT analyses remain non-scoring, non-diagnostic, reversible, private, and transmission-safe. It confirms that A-score and M-score should not be assigned because no concrete actor, role, event, knowledge condition, power/control lever, predictability field, alternative set, or dignity enactment is supplied. The IA-box is not a case-content verdict; it functions only as a transmission and interpretive-authority check. D remains inactive as a module; dignity protection operates only as a guardrail.

This MIP layer adds something neither PMS Core nor PMS-CONFLICT alone supplies: a structured discipline around score hygiene, role/context separation, publicness, transmission, red zones, D0/D1/D2 distinction, and the danger of turning a structural case into a person-near artefact. Its central conclusion is that the previous layers are disciplined only when kept as a private structural working note. They are not mature for decision-basis transmission.

AHP then operates at second order. It does not evaluate the case. It evaluates the MIP output as an analysis artefact. It assigns a high Precision Heuristic band, but PH is not an A-score, not an M-score, not a D-score, not an IA verdict, and not a validation of PMS, PMS-CONFLICT, or MIP. The high PH result means only that the analysis artefact is comparatively precise under its declared private-working-note boundaries. AHP identifies seven attack points: full-template density may simulate more case substance than the abstract material supports; minimal-adulthood gate wording may be misread as a person-status assessment; repeated D-language may invite D-profile overreading; the IA-box may be mistaken for a case-content IA finding; hypothetical M-ceiling language may be mistaken for hidden M-band; conclusion language could be read as validation rather than artifact-level conformity; and counter-readings could be more directly tied to non-scoring.

AHP’s hardening backlog therefore does not repair the case or change its findings. It proposes next-iteration patches: add a density warning; clarify minimal-adulthood as applicability-gate review only; repeat the D-boundary near D-language; label IA as analysis-artifact-only; mark M-ceiling language as non-operative safeguard; qualify the conclusion as artifact-bound rather than model-validating; and connect rival readings explicitly to non-scoring and D non-activation. These patches make the analysis safer to transmit, but they do not upgrade transmission status.

The layers therefore interact by progressive constraint, not escalation into authority. PMS Core renders the structure. PMS-CONFLICT sharpens the conflict threshold. MIP prevents the artefact from becoming a score, verdict, or decision basis. AHP hardens the artefact against misreading. None of the layers validates the previous one by authority. None modifies PMS Base. None turns add-on language into operators. None converts drift risk into finding.

The main overreach risk lies in the elegance of the layered stack. Because the analysis is coherent across PMS, CONFLICT, MIP, and AHP, it may appear stronger than the case material warrants. The layered result must therefore be read as disciplined non-closure, not cumulative proof. Each layer adds precision by refusing specific overclaims.

### Example 1 — Local mandates, shared impossibility

A public archive and a privacy office share responsibility for a collection of historically important records. The archive’s mandate is locally coherent: preserve access, maintain continuity of public memory, and prevent institutional history from disappearing. Its procedures favor stable cataloguing, durable references, and long-term discoverability. Within that frame, removing or heavily obscuring records can look like damage to the archive’s purpose.

The privacy office’s mandate is also locally coherent: reduce exposure, prevent unnecessary circulation of sensitive personal data, and make sure that records do not continue to harm people merely because they were once collected. Its procedures favor minimization, restricted access, and periodic review. Within that frame, unrestricted preservation can look like an avoidable continuation of exposure.

The conflict appears when the same record must be handled under both mandates. The archive is not simply “careless,” and the privacy office is not simply “censorious.” Each trajectory is coherent inside its own frame. The incompatibility arises when both are asked to generate a single shared handling rule: preserve the record fully enough for historical continuity, yet restrict it fully enough to prevent renewed exposure.

What this example demonstrates is the core distinction between local coherence and shared integration. The archive trajectory and the privacy trajectory are not incoherent merely because they collide. Their conflict is not automatically a maturity failure, communication failure, or lack of goodwill. It is a structural incompatibility produced by different frames carrying different protected constraints.

The PMS Core hooks are direct. **Δ** appears in the distinction between preservation and minimization. **∇** appears in the directional pressure of each mandate: one moves toward access and continuity, the other toward restriction and exposure reduction. **□** is central because each mandate is locally framed. **Θ** appears because the record is not a one-time object; it persists across time and continues to generate access, memory, exposure, and revision problems. **Φ** becomes relevant when the same record is moved from the archive frame into the privacy frame, or back again. The meaning of “responsible handling” changes with the frame. **Χ** is required so the analysis does not prematurely declare one mandate the mature one and the other the obstacle. **Σ** remains contested: a shared policy may be possible, but it may also be partial, unstable, or costly. **Ψ** remains conditional unless a durable institutional commitment is specified.

The PMS-CONFLICT overlay sharpens the example without escalating it. The case is conflict-legible because it shows local/shared Σ tension: each office may have a locally integrated practice, while shared integration remains unresolved. But the example does not yet establish terminal CONFLICT. It does not show that distance is priced or asymmetric, that binding collision is irreversible, that exposure capture is active, or that Σ has terminally failed. The right overlay status is therefore weak CONFLICT projection, not terminal conflict classification.

MIP and AHP would remain mostly guardrail-level here unless the vignette were developed into an applied institutional case. No A-score, M-score, IA verdict, or D-profile should be assigned from this example. A concrete analysis would need actual roles, authority structures, publicness, affected perspectives, records of decision, review channels, and evidence of exposure or harm. AHP would be relevant only if the written analysis were to be transmitted, because terms like “exposure,” “restriction,” or “public memory” could harden into blame or institutional positioning.

The forbidden claims are important. The example does not prove that archival access should win. It does not prove that privacy should win. It does not diagnose either office. It does not establish legal liability, moral guilt, institutional bad faith, or empirical harm. It does not validate PMS or PMS-CONFLICT. It also does not show that every such conflict can be repaired by better framing.

The non-capture note is equally important. A legal analysis might be more appropriate if the question concerns statutory obligations. A political theory reading might better capture public memory and historical accountability. An ethics of care reading might foreground affected persons in ways PMS should not absorb. The PMS analysis preserves these rival readings rather than treating them as defects.

The article takeaway is that structural conflict can exist without local incoherence. PMS makes the frame relation visible; PMS-CONFLICT clarifies why the case is conflict-legible but not automatically terminal. The example prepares the reader to see conflict as a disciplined boundary problem, not as an immediate verdict about which trajectory is wrong.

### Example 2 — The interface that cannot satisfy both clients

A software team maintains a shared interface used by two internal systems. The first system needs stable field names because it archives transactions over long periods. Its local logic values backward compatibility: if a field name changes, old records become harder to interpret, audit, or compare. From that frame, interface stability is not conservatism; it is the condition for continuity.

The second system needs the same interface to reflect a new classification scheme. Its local logic values semantic accuracy: if the interface continues using outdated field names, new outputs will be misleading or require translation layers that introduce errors. From that frame, changing the field names is not disruption; it is the condition for correctness.

Both systems are locally coherent. The archive system is not irrational for resisting change. The classification system is not reckless for demanding it. The conflict emerges because one shared interface is expected to carry two different continuities: historical stability and updated semantic fit. If the interface remains unchanged, the second system carries misfit. If it changes, the first system carries migration cost and possible audit discontinuity. The practical object is singular; the frames are not.

This example demonstrates why recontextualization is not repair by default. When the interface is viewed from the archive frame, “same field name” means continuity. When it is viewed from the classification frame, “same field name” may mean semantic drift. The same structure changes meaning when placed in a different frame. **Φ** therefore makes the conflict visible, but it does not automatically solve it.

The PMS Core hooks are especially clear. **Δ** distinguishes two requirements: stability and semantic revision. **∇** appears as directional pressure in both systems: preserve continuity versus update classification. **□** defines what counts as acceptable change in each system. **Λ** may become active if an expected translation layer, migration path, or shared naming convention fails to appear, but it should not be assumed merely because the systems disagree. **Α** may become relevant if interface conventions have stabilized through repeated use. **Ω** remains conditional: if one team can force changes while another bears the migration burden, asymmetry becomes active; if no such distribution is given, it must remain weak. **Θ** is central because both requirements unfold across time: archives depend on long-term persistence, while classification schemes evolve. **Φ** is active because the same interface is re-read under different frames. **Χ** is required so the analyst does not declare one system more mature simply because it appears more modern or more stable. **Σ** is the contested operator: a versioned interface, aliasing scheme, or migration layer may integrate the requirements, but that must be shown. **Ψ** becomes relevant only if the organization binds itself to a durable interface policy.

The CONFLICT overlay adds threshold discipline. The example may show a **Σ_local / Σ_shared** tension: each system can integrate its own requirements locally, while the shared interface cannot yet integrate both. It may also become a binding collision if each system is durably committed to incompatible interface contracts. But that stronger reading requires evidence: formal commitments, non-negotiable dependencies, asymmetric migration cost, time pressure, and exhaustion of shared Σ. Without those, the example remains weakly conflict-legible, not terminal.

MIP status remains non-scoring. It would be misuse to assign maturity to either team based on this vignette. A MIP analysis would first ask for role mapping, publicness, responsibility levers, alternatives, predictability, and real power to act. If one team had authority to impose a change while another bore unacknowledged costs, IA might become relevant. But in this bounded example, IA is not a verdict; it is only a possible future asymmetry check. AHP would be useful if a report about the teams were circulated, because “resistance to change” or “semantic correctness” could become person-near or team-near labels.

Forbidden claims must be excluded. The example does not show that backward compatibility is superior to semantic accuracy. It does not show that innovation beats stability. It does not establish negligence, incompetence, irrationality, or bad faith. It does not prescribe a software architecture. It does not validate PMS as an implementation method. It is not an engineering decision rule.

The non-capture note is straightforward. A software architecture analysis might solve the technical interface problem more directly. A cost analysis might determine migration feasibility. A governance analysis might decide authority and deadlines. PMS does not replace those. It clarifies the structural form of the incompatibility: two locally coherent trajectories become incompatible when forced through one shared object.

The article takeaway is that Φ can expose conflict without resolving it, and Σ must remain a tested possibility rather than an assumed obligation. The example makes visible why PMS must not become a closure machine: sometimes the responsible result is not “integrate now,” but “name the frame collision, preserve non-capture, and specify what additional evidence would be needed before integration or binding is claimed.”

### Example 3 — The report that becomes too authoritative

An internal analyst writes a careful structural note about a recurring coordination problem between two departments. The note says that Department A and Department B operate under different frames: one is optimized for speed of response, the other for verification and documentation. The note explicitly states that no person is being evaluated, no diagnosis is made, no legal conclusion follows, and no maturity score is assigned. It uses PMS Core to distinguish local coherence from shared integration, and it uses the CONFLICT overlay to mark only a weak local/shared Σ tension.

Inside the analyst’s private working context, the note is disciplined. It remains reversible, bounded, and non-personal. It does not say that either department is immature. It does not claim terminal conflict. It does not prescribe intervention. It keeps non-capture open: perhaps the problem is resource scarcity, perhaps incompatible incentives, perhaps unclear governance, perhaps a technical workflow issue.

The risk appears when a short excerpt from the note is copied into a management slide: “Department A and Department B show structural incompatibility and weak conflict projection.” The slide omits the boundary language. It omits that PMS-CONFLICT did not find terminal conflict. It omits that MIP did not assign A or M scores. It omits that D was inactive. The phrase “structural incompatibility” now begins to function as an organizational label. A bounded analysis artefact starts to look like an authoritative classification of teams.

This example demonstrates why the MIP and AHP layers matter. The original structural analysis may be correct within its scope, but transmission changes the situation. A phrase that was safe inside a private working note can become risky when excerpted into a public, semi-public, or institutional decision environment. The danger is not that PMS Core was wrong. The danger is that the artefact’s boundary conditions are removed.

The PMS Core hooks are indirect but important. **Χ** is the central operator: distance is preserved in the original note but weakened by excerpting. **□** shifts: the text moves from analysis frame to management frame. **Φ** occurs as recontextualization of the report itself. **Ω** may become active if management has authority over the departments and the excerpt affects reputation, resources, or evaluation. **Θ** matters because a label inserted into slides or records can persist. **Ψ** becomes a risk if the organization binds the phrase into policy, performance review, or official narrative. **Σ** may be falsely assumed: a management audience may read “structural incompatibility” as if the analysis had already integrated all relevant evidence.

The PMS-CONFLICT overlay hooks are transmission-related. Publicness and misuse gradient are not PMS operators, but they matter as overlay controls. Exposure capture could become relevant if the excerpt hardens interpretation into institutional pressure. Dignity collapse could become a risk if the language shifts toward humiliation, ranking, or person-near verdict. But even here, these are risks, not findings. The example does not claim that management has acted coercively; it shows how an artefact can drift if its boundaries are stripped.

MIP status is central. The original note should remain a working note unless roles, publicness, affected perspectives, evidence, review conditions, and reversibility are checked. No A-score or M-score should be inferred. The IA-box, if used, applies to the analysis artefact and transmission conditions, not to the departments as case content. D remains guardrail-only unless a concrete dignity-in-practice scene is supplied.

AHP status is also central. This is exactly the kind of case where AHP attack points matter: density may simulate more substance than the abstract material supports; D-language can be overread; IA-box language can be mistaken for a case-content finding; conclusion language can be read as validation; and a non-operative ceiling rule can be mistaken for a hidden score. AHP does not change the underlying analysis. It hardens the artefact against foreseeable misuse.

Forbidden claims must be kept explicit. The example does not show that the departments are dysfunctional. It does not show that management is acting in bad faith. It does not establish organizational liability, clinical harm, legal risk, or empirical fact. It does not prove that PMS language is unsafe in general. It shows only that bounded analysis can become misleading when recontextualized without its safeguards.

The non-capture note is that organizational communication, management practice, labor relations, legal compliance, and workplace ethics may all be needed in a real case. PMS, CONFLICT, MIP, and AHP do not replace those domains. They help identify when a structural note is being moved into a higher-risk transmission environment.

The article takeaway is that the analysis artefact itself can become part of the conflict structure. This is why AXIOM requires claim boundaries, add-on discipline, MIP guardrails, and AHP hardening. A good structural reading is not only one that maps the case; it is one that prevents its own language from becoming an unauthorized closure device.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The completed YAML analyses preserve multiple non-capture zones. These are not defects; they are part of the case result. A rival reading may explain the phenomenon more sharply in a given context, or preserve dimensions that PMS and CONFLICT should not absorb.

A **logical contradiction** reading may treat structural incompatibility as formal inconsistency rather than praxeological conflict. If two propositions cannot both be true under a fixed formal system, PMS trajectory language may be unnecessary or overcomplicated. This rival reading preserves strict logical incompatibility, where frame, impulse, temporality, and self-binding may not add discriminative value.

A **semantic incommensurability** reading may treat the case as a failure of shared meaning or commensurable criteria. Two frames may use terms, distinctions, or standards that cannot be translated without remainder. This preserves the possibility that recontextualization is not a bridge but a distortion. PMS must not automatically redescribe incommensurability as incomplete Φ or failed Σ.

An **institutional rule-collision** reading may treat conflict as the product of rule systems whose procedures cannot be jointly satisfied. In such cases, legal, administrative, or organizational analysis may be more precise than PMS. This rival reading preserves the specificity of jurisdiction, mandate, procedure, authority, and formal contradiction between institutional requirements.

A **systems differentiation** reading may treat practical, conceptual, and institutional trajectories as operating under distinct internal codes. Conflict then arises not because integration has failed, but because differentiated systems cannot be reduced to one another without losing their function. This preserves plurality and functional differentiation against premature synthesis.

A **game-theoretic or resource-conflict** reading may explain incompatibility through incentives, payoffs, scarcity, strategic interaction, or material constraints. This preserves cases where conflict is not primarily about frame coherence but about distribution, competition, or incompatible payoff structures.

A **political antagonism** reading may treat conflict as structured by power, interests, legitimacy, domination, or collective struggle. The PMS Core analysis keeps Ω weak because the abstract material does not specify power, but in a concrete political case, Ω may become central. PMS must not neutralize political antagonism by translating it too quickly into frame incompatibility.

A **tragic pluralism** reading may treat two coherent, responsible, and mature trajectories as genuinely incompatible without deficiency. This is one of the most important rival readings. It preserves the possibility that conflict remains even under distance, awareness, responsibility, and integration attempts. The CONFLICT overlay explicitly avoids strong tragic-legibility here because the terminal configuration is not established, but it also preserves tragedy as structurally possible.

An **anti-capture** reading may object that any operator grammar risks assimilating what should remain resistant to formalization. This reading preserves the fact that being able to encode a case in YAML or PMS terms does not prove that the phenomenon has been captured. AXIOM must take this seriously because its own method is cartographic: it maps closure-demands; it does not own them.

The MIP and AHP layers add additional non-capture discipline. MIP warns that viable rival readings are one reason A/M scoring and D activation remain inappropriate. AHP reinforces this by identifying the need to connect counter-readings more directly to non-scoring. A structurally coherent article can still be too smooth; rival readings keep the analysis attackable and revisable.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS Core reading would weaken if no distinct frames or trajectories can be identified. Without Δ and □, the case loses its structural entry point. If “conflict” names only a vague disagreement, mood, or rhetorical label, PMS analysis would be under-specified.

It would weaken if there is no directional tension. ∇ requires some orientation, continuation pressure, activation, or trajectory. If the structures are static and not directed, the case may be better treated as static contradiction or classification difference.

It would weaken if no temporal extension is required. The case uses trajectory language, but if a concrete version turns out to be a one-step incompatibility with no sequence, persistence, delay, path dependence, or development, Θ would be overextended. A formal or semantic reading might then be sharper.

It would weaken if Λ is asserted without a structured expectation. Missing agreement, unresolved difference, or lack of evidence is not automatically a Non-Event. The analysis must specify expected coordination, translation, recognition, mediation, convergence, repair, or decision within a frame.

It would weaken if Α is asserted without recurrence or stabilization. A one-time conflict is not a conflict attractor. A trajectory is not automatically a stabilized pattern. Evidence of repetition, routine, path-dependence, or sedimentation would be required.

It would weaken if Ω is inferred from conflict alone. A concrete case may indeed involve asymmetry, but that must be shown through power, exposure, authority, vulnerability, capacity, obligation, liability, publicness, or exit-cost gradients. Without those, Ω-based responsibility or dignity-stress claims must remain weak.

It would weaken if Φ becomes an assimilation machine. If every rival frame is treated as a recontextualization inside PMS, the analysis becomes over-assimilative. Source and target frames must be named, and failed translation must remain possible.

It would weaken if Σ is treated as the expected solution. PMS integration is not inherently superior to non-integration. If integration would erase legitimate difference, suppress a rival frame, or impose coherence coercively, Σ must be marked false, coercive, partial, or not applicable.

It would weaken if Ψ is applied without a defined self-binding structure. Durable commitment, institutional norm-stability, role obligation, or self-relation must be shown. Otherwise Ψ remains analytic discipline, not case content.

The CONFLICT overlay would weaken if the analysis classified terminal conflict without the required signature. Terminality requires more than incompatibility. It requires sustained Θ/Ω/Ψ load, costly or asymmetric Χ, and exhausted Σ within the descriptive pathway. None of that is supplied here. If the overlay were to call the case terminal, it would overreach.

The overlay would also weaken if it treated Σ-incompatibility clash as an active drift rather than a risk. The case supports local/shared Σ tension, but not failed shared Σ under load. It lacks binding, asymmetry, priced distance, recurrence, and unacceptable integration cost.

The MIP reading would weaken if it began assigning A or M bands. A-score requires an enactment context; M-score requires actors, knowledge, power/control, predictability, alternatives, and integration attempts. The abstract case provides none of these. A forced score would be false precision.

The MIP reading would also weaken if D were activated. D-module activation requires concrete dignity-in-practice enactments and safe, role-bound, non-public communication space. The case provides no self-treatment, relational dignity, bodily/material self-care, public dignity scene, shaming conduct, or dignity-repair enactment. D belongs here only as guardrail.

The AHP overlay would weaken if its attack points were read as defects in persons or in the case itself. AHP attack points are vulnerabilities in the analysis artefact. They concern wording, scope, inference, evidence linkage, publicness, reversibility, D-language, IA language, and transmission risk. They are not moral faults, maturity deficits, dignity deficits, or case-content findings.

Operationalization remains unfulfilled. A stronger applied analysis would require: a concrete scene; named frames; identifiable trajectories; role mapping; publicness level; temporal scale; evidence of recurrence; explicit asymmetry conditions; structured non-events; attempted recontextualizations; integration criteria; binding commitments; affected perspectives; rival-model comparison; transmission review; and, if governance use is intended, external accountability. None of that is present. Therefore the analysis remains a private structural working note and chapter-ready philosophical article, not an applied decision artefact.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It does not identify immaturity, pathology, personality type, mental state, cognitive failure, emotional deficiency, or clinical risk. The presence of conflict, non-fit, rupture, resistance, or non-integration must not be translated into diagnosis.

It must not be used for person ranking. No person, group, institution, or frame is classified as more mature, more coherent, more dignified, less integrated, deficient, or superior. Local coherence does not rank frames. Shared non-integration does not rank actors. Dignity-in-Practice is not ontological dignity or moral superiority.

It must not be used as a legal or forensic conclusion. No liability, culpability, evidence standard, due process finding, sanction, enforcement action, or institutional responsibility follows from the analysis. Responsibility_R is not established as case content.

It must not be used as metaphysical proof. The analysis does not show what conflict ultimately is. It does not prove the nature of incompatibility, truth, coherence, consciousness, or social reality. It remains a structural projection under declared frames.

It must not be used as empirical proof. The case contains no observations, records, measurements, interviews, events, institutional files, or causal evidence. Formal YAML completeness is not data.

It must not be used as implementation validation. The YAML representation is not software, not governance logic, not an intervention script, not a policy procedure, and not an agent architecture. Any operational use would require calibration, constraints, testing, and accountability outside this article.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. A successful mapping is not proof that PMS is complete, uniquely minimal, empirically adequate, or superior to all rival grammars. PMS-CONFLICT’s usefulness here does not validate the overlay as exhaustive conflict theory. MIP’s guardrail value does not validate MIP as person-evaluation. AHP’s high precision band does not validate PMS, CONFLICT, or MIP.

It must not treat add-on constructs as operators. Publicness, misuse gradient, conflict attractor, binding collision, asymmetry lock-in, exposure capture, dignity collapse, young Α-field, terminal regime, and downstream docking are overlay handles or scale constructs. They organize PMS operators; they do not extend the PMS operator inventory.

It must not treat drift as verdict. AD_A>>E, AD_Sigma_low, Σ-incompatibility clash, binding collision drift, asymmetry lock-in drift, exposure capture drift, and dignity collapse drift are conditional risks. They are not findings unless the required structure is shown. They are never person labels.

It must not produce closure by authority. The analysis may be polished, layered, and precise, but it remains reversible. PMS, PMS-CONFLICT, MIP, and AHP do not authorize final settlement. The case’s strongest discipline is precisely that it leaves non-capture visible.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result of this case is a bounded structural reading of conflict as local coherence without established shared integration. PMS Core makes the basic configuration legible: differentiated trajectories can be directionally organized, frame-bound, temporally extended, recontextualized across frames, and still fail to become jointly coherent. But PMS Core also prevents the wrong closure: difference is not yet conflict; absence is not automatically Λ; trajectory is not automatically attractor; incompatibility is not automatically asymmetry; recontextualization is not repair; integration is not proof; and self-binding is not available without demonstrated commitment.

The PMS-CONFLICT overlay sharpens the result by showing that the case is conflict-legible but not terminal. The strongest overlay finding is not a drift finding, but a disciplined threshold distinction: the case resembles a **Σ_local / Σ_shared** tension, yet it does not establish binding collision, asymmetry lock-in, exposure capture, dignity collapse, stabilized conflict attractor, or terminal conflict. This is the central CONFLICT contribution. It prevents both under-reading and over-reading. The case is more than mere disagreement, but less than terminal stabilized incompatibility under binding, time, exposure, cost, and asymmetry.

MIP contributes a further shift in object. It does not score the case. It evaluates the analysis as an artefact and keeps it within safe use boundaries. Because no concrete actors, roles, decisions, alternatives, predictability fields, dignity enactments, or asymmetry conditions are supplied, A-score and M-score remain unassigned, IA remains analysis-transmission only, and D remains inactive as case content. MIP confirms that the output is a private structural working note unless future evidence and transmission conditions justify more.

AHP then hardens the analysis without changing it. It identifies how even a careful, bounded, non-diagnostic analysis can be misread: full-template density can simulate empirical substance; D-language can look like a hidden dignity profile; IA-box language can look like a case-content verdict; M-ceiling language can look like a hidden score; conclusion language can look like model validation. These are attack surfaces in the artefact, not defects in persons or findings about the case. AHP therefore improves the analysis by making its own vulnerability visible.

The examples contribute three different clarifications. The archive/privacy example demonstrates local mandates becoming jointly difficult without either mandate becoming incoherent or defective. The interface example shows why Φ can reveal incompatibility without solving it, and why Σ must remain a tested possibility rather than an assumed obligation. The report-transmission example shows that the analysis artefact itself can become part of the conflict structure if excerpted without its boundaries. Together, the examples make the article more readable while preserving the YAML boundary: they demonstrate the analysis, but they do not replace it, do not become evidence, and do not enlarge the claim beyond the completed PMS Core, PMS-CONFLICT, MIP, and AHP artefacts.

What remains unclaimed is as important as what is claimed. The article does not prove a metaphysical theory of conflict. It does not establish empirical facts about any institution, person, or event. It does not diagnose conflict, rank maturity, assign responsibility, prescribe repair, or authorize governance. It does not validate PMS, PMS-CONFLICT, MIP, or AHP. It does not claim that every conflict is under-integration, that every non-fit should be recontextualized, or that every incompatibility can or should be integrated.

The boundary discipline is therefore the case’s central achievement. Conflict remains readable without becoming verdict. Local coherence remains visible without becoming global justification. Non-capture remains possible without being treated as analytical failure. Rival readings remain open without being treated as weaknesses. Risk remains risk, not finding. Non-assignment remains non-assignment, not low score. Dignity-in-Practice remains a praxeological application constraint, not ontological dignity. Add-on constructs remain overlay handles, not PMS operators. AHP hardening remains second-order artefact hardening, not rescoring.

The final methodological takeaway is that AXIOM can render closure-demands without satisfying them falsely. In this case, it shows where closure is tempting: the desire to decide which frame is right, which trajectory should integrate, which incompatibility is failure, and whether conflict has become terminal. The layered analysis does not deny that such decisions may become necessary in concrete settings. It says that this abstract case does not yet warrant them. The proper output is a disciplined map: structural incompatibility is legible, local/shared integration tension is visible, terminal conflict is not established, and the residue of non-capture must remain available.
