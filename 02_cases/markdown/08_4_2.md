### Why this case matters

“Interpretability vs. Reality” matters because it targets one of the deepest closure-demands in reflective practice: the demand that what can be explained should coincide with what remains real. In morally difficult situations, this demand often appears quietly. A frame is built; the situation is interpreted; a language is found; the analysis becomes coherent; the case starts to feel handled. Yet the practical situation may not be dissolved by that coherence. Harm, exposure, obligation, loss, uncertainty, vulnerability, or responsibility may remain even after interpretation has become fluent.

The structural difficulty is not simply that some things are hard to explain. It is that explanation can become too successful at the level of form. A difficult situation may become legible, narratable, reframable, and even internally coherent while the practical residue remains untouched. The case therefore asks for a disciplined distinction between interpretability and reality-in-practice without turning that distinction into a metaphysical thesis. It does not say that reality is inaccessible in principle. It also does not say that interpretation is worthless. It asks what happens when interpretation and practical remainder do not fully coincide.

This makes the case central for AXIOM. AXIOM is a cartographic and indexing paper: it maps closure-demands, operator chains, add-on visibility, drift risks, output types, and misuse boundaries. It does not solve by authority. This case is exemplary because it tests whether the stack can mark its own limits. A weaker system would either absorb the remainder as merely “not yet integrated,” or abandon structure because reality exceeds interpretation. The completed analyses avoid both moves. PMS Core marks the framed difference and the absent coincidence. PMS-CRITIQUE marks the critique threshold and the risk that explanation becomes commentary or narrative repair. MIP marks the output as a working note rather than a decision basis. AHP then hardens the analysis artefact by identifying where the analysis itself remains vulnerable to over-smoothness, IA overreading, D-language misreading, and model-stack coherence bias.

The philosophical closure-demand is therefore twofold. First, there is a demand for intelligibility: morally difficult situations seem to call for interpretation, explanation, and frame-building. Second, there is a demand for adequacy: once a situation has been interpreted, the analysis is tempted to claim that the practical remainder has been handled. The case denies that second demand by default. It preserves the possibility that interpretation is necessary but insufficient.

This is not being solved metaphysically. The phrase “reality” is not treated as a final ontology, substance, noumenon, or ultimate ground. It is treated structurally as practical remainder: what continues to matter in practice after explanation has occurred. Nor is the case solved empirically. No data, observations, events, interviews, documents, institutional records, harms, outcomes, or evidence set is provided. Nor is it solved clinically or diagnostically. No person, psyche, trauma, maturity level, pathology, or mental state is being analysed. Nor is it solved legally or forensically. There is no evidentiary threshold, liability question, culpability finding, witness assessment, or adjudicative standard. The case is also not an implementation study. No system, architecture, user interface, deployed model, automation, policy pipeline, or computational agent is described.

The point is narrower and more difficult: to show how a model stack can remain structurally useful without claiming that usefulness equals capture. The completed YAMLs repeatedly preserve this boundary. PMS coherence is not enough. CRITIQUE threshold clarity is not enough. MIP guardrail discipline is not enough. AHP precision is not enough. Each layer contributes visibility, and each layer must also preserve the possibility that the practical remainder has not been exhausted.

### Scene and frame structure

The case has no concrete scene packet in the ordinary sense. There is no micro-scene, no institutional setting, no group interaction, no legal case, no clinical encounter, no public controversy, no system deployment, and no action episode. This absence is not a weakness to be repaired by invention. It is a constitutive scope condition. Because the case is non-concrete, all scene language must be conceptual and carefully bounded.

The scene boundary is therefore the conceptual boundary between what can be interpreted or explained and what remains real in practice. The operative scene is not “a person explaining something badly” or “an institution misunderstanding reality.” Those would be invented scenes. The actual case object is the structural relation between explanation and remainder. The analysis may inspect how that relation is framed, how absent coincidence becomes meaningful, how recontextualization may help or overreach, and how Distance prevents closure. It may not infer who is harmed, who is responsible, or what should be done.

The PMS frame, □, is philosophical-praxeological. It is philosophical because it addresses the status of interpretation, explanation, and reality. It is praxeological because “what remains real” is not treated as abstract metaphysical being, but as what continues to matter in practice. Inside the frame are: the distinction between interpretability and practical remainder; the possible absent coincidence between them; the risk of explanatory over-smoothness; the need for reflective distance; the possibility of non-capture; and the legitimacy of rival formalizations. Outside the frame are: metaphysical proof, empirical validation, legal adjudication, clinical diagnosis, implementation authority, person-level evaluation, and PMS Base validation.

The protected constraints are strong. PMS entry discipline requires Distance, reversibility, and Dignity-in-Practice as application constraints. CRITIQUE adds non-coercion, critique threshold discipline, and the rule that reduced signatures remain overlay shorthand. MIP adds score hygiene, D-off discipline, working-note transmission, red-zone avoidance, and the distinction between analysis artefact and case reality. AHP adds a second-order hardening check: even a high-precision analysis remains vulnerable if model-stack coherence is mistaken for practical capture.

Roles are not present as case roles. The only roles that can be named are artefact roles. PMS Core functions as structural grammar. PMS-CRITIQUE functions as an overlay profile for critique-readiness and drift risks. MIP functions as downstream maturity-in-practice review of the analysis artefact. AHP functions as second-order precision, attack-surface, and hardening overlay. The analyst role has framing power over the artefact, but no legitimate power over persons, institutions, or the practical remainder itself. Future concrete roles would have to be supplied before responsibility, action, or dignity-profile claims could be made.

Temporality, Θ, is weak but not irrelevant. The phrase “remains real in practice” suggests that something persists after interpretation. Something may continue to matter after explanation has occurred. However, no actual temporal sequence is given. There is no before and after, no delayed consequence, no review window, no accumulation, no recurrence, no commitment trajectory. Θ therefore appears as a conditional operator: it becomes active only if later material shows persistence, sequence, lag, delay, lock-in, or trajectory. Until then, temporal claims remain conceptual.

The reversibility window is wide. Because the case is not concrete, all readings remain revisable. PMS operator statuses can be downgraded or upgraded if future case material supplies actors, evidence, asymmetry, time, or consequences. MIP marks the output as a working note, not a decision basis. AHP reinforces that the overlay cannot upgrade transmission status. This reversibility is essential: a case about the limits of interpretation would contradict itself if the analysis hardened into a verdict.

Exposure and asymmetry conditions are similarly weak. Moral difficulty often involves exposure, vulnerability, obligation, or power. But this case does not name any of these concretely. Ω therefore remains weak at the case-object level. A conceptual asymmetry is visible: model language, explanation, or interpretive authority may become dominant over practical remainder. But this is not a concrete asymmetry between persons or institutions. No actor has been shown to impose explanation on another. No affected party has been identified. No cost distribution has been documented.

The consequence of non-concrete status is decisive: no scores, no verdicts, no D-profile, no action recommendation, no legal or clinical reading, no responsibility assignment, no empirical adequacy claim, and no implementation validity claim. The case can only be rendered as disciplined structural boundary analysis.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading is built from the canonical operator inventory Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, Ψ. The completed Core YAML treats Δ, □, Λ, Φ, and Χ as the primary analytic operators. ∇, Α, Ω, Θ, Σ, and Ψ remain weak, conditional, under-specified, or analytic-only. This distribution is crucial. The case would be overread if the entire PMS chain were activated as though a full praxis scene had been supplied.

**Δ — Difference** is the entry point. The case becomes structurally readable because it distinguishes interpretability or explanation from practical reality. The relevant difference is not between truth and illusion, mature and immature actors, good and bad interpretation, or PMS and non-PMS thinking. It is the difference between what can be made intelligible and what may remain practically real after that intelligibility has been achieved. Δ is active and major, but it must not be metaphysically inflated. It does not establish a dualism between appearance and reality. It marks a structural contrast inside the case frame.

The calibration issue for Δ is precision. If the distinction becomes too broad, everything that resists explanation becomes “reality,” and everything that is legible becomes “interpretation.” That would reduce the case to a vague anti-formalist slogan. If the distinction becomes too narrow, reality is reduced to the current model’s missing information. That would erase the case’s non-capture point. The correct calibration is a bounded distinction: interpretable or explainable structure on one side; practical remainder on the other.

**∇ — Impulse** is weak. The case implies pressure toward understanding, explanation, and closure, but no actor’s drive, motivation, affect, urgency, or institutional demand is described. ∇ therefore names only a structural pressure: the pull toward making morally difficult situations intelligible. It is not a psychological claim. It must not be used to infer desire, anxiety, defensiveness, avoidance, or closure hunger in any person or group.

The overclaim would be to treat the case as evidence that someone or some system is driven to explain away reality. That may be possible in a future concrete scene, but it is not present here. ∇ remains a weak background pressure.

**□ — Frame** is active and major. The case only becomes meaningful within a frame that distinguishes interpretation, explanation, moral difficulty, practice, and reality. PMS does not treat frames as optional decorations; frames determine what counts as relevant, what counts as explanation, what counts as remainder, and what kind of closure is being demanded. In this case, the frame is philosophical-praxeological. It is not empirical, clinical, legal, metaphysical, or implementation-oriented.

The calibration issue for □ is domain discipline. If the frame silently shifts, the case changes. A metaphysical frame would ask whether reality exceeds conceptual scheme. An empirical frame would ask what evidence shows. A clinical frame would ask about perception or coping. A legal frame would ask about evidence and responsibility. An implementation frame would ask whether an interpretability system captures operational risk. None of those frames is currently licensed. The PMS frame must hold the case as a conceptual projection unless more material is supplied.

The dependency risk is that □ can make the analysis feel more determinate than it is. A well-declared frame improves legibility, but it does not create missing evidence. It also does not eliminate rival frames. The frame is necessary for the analysis; it is not proof of capture.

**Λ — Non-Event** is active and central. The case turns on an absent or incomplete coincidence: what can be explained does not fully coincide with what remains real in practice. This is a paradigmatic Λ structure if, within the frame, one might have expected interpretation and reality to line up. The expected event would be something like: explanation arrives and practical remainder is handled. The case says this expected coincidence may fail.

The calibration of Λ is delicate. The absent coincidence may be a structured absence within the PMS frame, but it may also be a genuine non-capture zone. Not every remainder is a PMS-legible non-event awaiting fuller modelling. Some remainder may indicate that the model’s frame is not sufficient, or that another formalism preserves something PMS backgrounds. The Core YAML explicitly warns against automatically redescribing unresolved absence as drift, incomplete calibration, incomplete integration, or maturity failure.

The overclaim would be to turn Λ into a hidden promise of capture. If the analysis says “there is a non-event, therefore PMS has captured the missing event as a structural absence,” it overreaches. Λ marks an absence; it does not own the absent reality.

**Α — Attractor** is weak. The case does not show recurrence, stabilization, habit, script, or repeated pattern. However, the YAML notes a possible risk: explanation could stabilize as a substitute for reality. If interpretive framing repeatedly becomes the default response to practical remainder, an attractor could form. But no such repetition is evidenced.

The calibration issue is recurrence. Attractor claims require repeated scenes or path dependence. Without them, Α must remain weak. The overclaim would be to say that the case already shows a stabilized explanatory-substitution pattern. It does not. It only makes that pattern thinkable as a future risk.

**Ω — Asymmetry** is weak and conditionally relevant. Morally difficult situations often involve vulnerability, obligation, unequal exposure, power, authority, or limited exit. But the supplied case material does not identify any concrete asymmetry. PMS therefore keeps Ω visible without activating it as a finding.

The central conceptual Ω risk is interpretive authority over practical remainder. A model, theory, or analyst may acquire the power to make the situation legible while the affected practical reality remains underrepresented. MIP later formulates this as a conceptual IA risk. Yet this is not a concrete asymmetry between persons or institutions. No actor has been shown to impose explanation on another. No affected party has been identified. No cost distribution has been documented.

The overclaim would be to treat moral difficulty as sufficient evidence of asymmetry. In PMS discipline, Ω requires a directional imbalance of power, exposure, capacity, or obligation. The case hints at such possibilities, but it does not establish them.

**Θ — Temporality** is weak and conditionally relevant. “Remains real in practice” suggests that something persists after explanation. That temporal residue matters conceptually. But no sequence is supplied. There is no timeline, no delayed consequence, no recurrence, no lock-in, no review window, and no ongoing trajectory.

The calibration issue is persistence versus sequence. A phrase like “remains” may imply persistence, but it does not by itself supply a temporally structured praxis field. Θ becomes stronger only if future material shows that explanation happens at one point and practical consequence continues, accumulates, or becomes harder to reverse later. Until then, Θ remains weak.

The overclaim would be to build a developmental or responsibility trajectory from the abstract phrase alone. The Core YAML refuses this. MIP also refuses it by treating the trajectory as an analysis-artefact trajectory, not a maturity trajectory of a person or concrete situation.

**Φ — Recontextualization** is active and major. Interpretation and explanation are forms of recontextualization: they place a difficult practical reality into a frame where it can be understood. This is one of the main structural movements in the case. Φ is what makes reality legible through explanation. It is also where the risk lies. Recontextualization may clarify; it may also substitute explanation for practical adequacy.

The calibration issue for Φ is direction and limit. The source field is morally difficult practical reality. The target frame is interpretation or explanation. But PMS must also preserve the reverse pressure: practice may resist the explanatory frame. If Φ is treated as inherently successful, the case collapses. The YAML therefore insists that recontextualization must not absorb every remainder as a need for stronger framing. The practical remainder may remain outside the recontextualizing move.

The overclaim would be to say that once a situation has been recontextualized, it has been structurally understood. That would make Φ into a closure operator. PMS does not permit that. Φ changes legibility; it does not guarantee adequacy.

**Χ — Distance** is active, major, and required. This case depends on the analyst’s ability to maintain distance from the seductive smoothness of interpretation. Χ is the operator that prevents explanation from becoming verdict, PMS mapping from becoming capture, critique threshold clarity from becoming correction, MIP structure from becoming scoring, and AHP precision from becoming validation.

The calibration issue for Χ is that Distance must not become indifference. It is not refusal to analyse. It is disciplined non-fusion. The analysis can interpret the case, but it must not collapse interpretation into practical resolution. It must hold the distinction between structural legibility and real adequacy open. Χ also protects reversibility. If the analysis cannot be revised, downgraded, or challenged by rival formalizations, Χ has failed.

The overclaim would be to suspend Distance in order to announce that PMS has shown the true structure of the case. Such a move would contradict the PMS entry condition and the case’s own theme. In this article, Χ is not ornamental. It is the operator that keeps the whole layered stack valid.

**Σ — Integration** is weak and risky. The case is explicitly about the danger that coherence may not equal reality. PMS can integrate disparate elements into a coherent whole, but here integration is not a result to be celebrated. It is a danger to be disciplined. A clean formula may feel like a solution while leaving the practical remainder untouched.

The calibration issue for Σ is discriminative force. Integration must be tested against whether it distinguishes practical remainder from missing evidence, explanation from correction, and coherence from adequacy. The YAML warns that PMS coherence is insufficient by itself. The AHP overlay later identifies this as a high-severity attack point: cross-model coherence may be mistaken for practical capture.

The overclaim would be to treat the integrated PMS Core → CRITIQUE → MIP → AHP stack as evidence that the case has been handled. The stack may be precise; it may still not capture reality. Therefore Σ remains weak and guarded.

**Ψ — Self-Binding** is weak and analytic-only. No case actor is present. No self-relation, promise, institutional commitment, follow-up owner, or identity trajectory is described. Ψ therefore cannot be applied as a case-agent structure. It appears only as analytic self-binding: the analyst and the artefact bind themselves to the guardrails of non-capture, reversibility, Dignity-in-Practice, score hygiene, and non-overclaim.

The calibration issue is avoiding Ψ→Other coercion. Especially in the CRITIQUE overlay, stable critique would require follow-up binding, but no such binding exists. The analysis must not demand that the case, rival readings, or practical remainder submit to PMS integration. Ψ binds the analysis to restraint; it does not bind reality to the analysis.

The overclaim would be to say that the case has achieved stable critique, responsibility, or self-binding. It has not. Ψ remains analytic-only.

Taken together, the PMS Core reading yields the compressed formula:

**Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ Δ**

This formula is intentionally compressed. It excludes weak and conditional operators from the primary model. It reads: reflective distance over recontextualization of an absent or incomplete coincidence within a frame generated by the difference between interpretability and practical reality. The formula is not a proof. It is a bounded structural projection.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The dependency discipline of this case is stricter than the operator list might first suggest. PMS permits all eleven operators to be represented, but it does not permit them to be activated cheaply. Later operators depend on earlier conditions, and derived structures cannot be treated as primitive.

The major invalid shortcut would be to move from Δ to Σ too quickly: because there is a difference between explanation and reality, the analysis might try to integrate that difference into a coherent philosophical reading. But this would miss the point. The case asks whether coherence itself can become insufficient. Therefore Δ cannot simply be fed into Σ as if integration were the goal.

A second invalid shortcut would be □ to Ψ: because the frame is clear, the analysis might bind the result as an authoritative case conclusion. But a declared frame is not a license for binding. Ψ requires integration, temporality, and Distance, and in this case no case-agent self-binding exists. Any binding is analytic only.

A third invalid shortcut would be Λ to Φ to closure: because an absence is identified, the analysis might recontextualize it as a modelled non-event and thereby claim capture. This is exactly the kind of closure the case resists. Λ must be preserved as possible non-capture, not automatically converted into a PMS-internal deficit.

Σ remains conditional because no practical synthesis is shown. There is no corrective action, no integrated plan, no operational decision, no institutional procedure, and no enacted transformation. At most, there is an integrated analysis artefact. Even that artefact must be treated cautiously because cross-model coherence can produce false confidence.

Ψ remains conditional or analytic-only because no self-binding actor exists. There is no promise, duty, role-commitment, follow-up owner, stable identity trajectory, or review obligation inside the case material. MIP makes this especially clear: no M-score is assigned because no actor or role-holder exists whose knowledge, power, predictability, alternatives, or integration attempt can be assessed.

Coherence is not proof. This is the case’s central discipline. PMS coherence, CRITIQUE threshold clarity, MIP guardrail completeness, and AHP high PH all improve the analysis artefact. None of them increases the evidence quality of the underlying case. AHP explicitly identifies this as a high-severity attack point: the model stack may appear to have handled the practical remainder more fully than evidence permits. The required hardening action is to say plainly that cross-model coherence is not practical capture.

Non-capture is not failure. In this case, non-capture is not a bug to be eliminated. It is one of the main structures the analysis must preserve. The practical remainder after explanation may be the very point of the case. PMS can mark that remainder; it cannot assume that marking equals owning. CRITIQUE can show that critique remains minimal rather than productive; it cannot turn critique-readiness into correction. MIP can mark the output as a working note; it cannot make it action-ready. AHP can harden the analysis; it cannot make the case transmittable.

PMS must therefore not become a closure machine. It must not convert resistance, disagreement, non-fit, rupture, absence, or rival framing into drift by default. It must not treat rival readings as immature, incomplete, or insufficiently integrated. It must not turn structural legibility into authority.

The invalid dependency shortcuts are clear. Χ cannot be treated as a standalone trait; it depends on Φ, Θ, and □ in PMS dependency terms, even when used as reduced signature shorthand in CRITIQUE. Σ cannot be invoked without Χ and Φ. Ψ cannot be invoked without Σ, Θ, and Χ. Ω and Θ cannot be upgraded from weak to active merely because the case is morally difficult. Α cannot be claimed without recurrence. Responsibility cannot be derived without Ω, Θ, Φ, and Ψ. Action cannot be derived without ∇, Θ, and Σ. Dignity-in-Practice cannot be treated as ontological dignity.

The dependency discipline therefore supports the central boundary: the case is structurally interpretable, but not practically resolved.

The completed YAMLs avoid this. SELF_FIXPOINT is not used to model a person, institution, or the case itself as a subject. It is used only to remind the analyst that formal self-binding must remain restraint-bound.

The derived-structure result is therefore disciplined and mostly negative: Awareness_A weak; Coherence_C weak and contested; Responsibility_R not established; Action_E under-specified; Dignity_in_Practice_D application-constraint only; SELF_FIXPOINT analytic-only or not applicable. These non-assignments are not gaps in the analysis. They are evidence that the YAML artefacts are not overreaching.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS Core pass considered two asymmetry drift-patterns: **AD_A>>E** and **AD_Sigma_low**. Neither is treated as an established finding. Both are preserved as conditional structural risks that would require concrete scene evidence before activation.

**AD_A>>E** names excessive distance between Awareness and Enactment. In the completed YAML, this pattern is marked as possible but not established. The reason is straightforward: the case contains conceptual awareness, but no concrete enactment axis. It is possible to imagine a drift in which interpretive awareness expands while action is suppressed or chronically delayed. In such a drift, the analysis of morally difficult situations would become increasingly refined, while nothing changes in practice. The case’s theme makes that risk salient. A system, institution, or analyst could become better and better at explaining why explanation is insufficient, while still leaving the practical remainder untouched.

But this risk is not a finding. The case gives no actor, no scene, no repeated pattern, no institutional script, no obligation, no decision point, and no action window. Therefore the YAML correctly marks the axis effects as under-specified rather than over-expanded or suppressed. The awareness axis is not shown to be over-expanded in an actual scene; the action axis is not shown to be suppressed or chronically delayed in practice. The drift remains a possible failure mode for later applications, not a status of the current case.

The important distinction is that under-specification is not low performance. It is not a weak maturity band, not a diagnosis, and not evidence that a person, institution, or framework is stuck in reflection. It simply means the case lacks the material needed to assess whether awareness has displaced enactment.

**AD_Sigma_low** names fragmented or insufficient integration. It is also marked as possible but not established. This is especially important because the case explicitly weakens Σ. The analysis says that integration is risky because coherence may not equal reality. That could be misread as AD_Sigma_low: perhaps the case fails because it refuses integration. The YAML blocks that misreading. In this case, weak Σ is not automatically drift. It may be disciplined restraint.

AD_Sigma_low would become relevant if repeated recontextualization occurred without adequate consolidation, producing unstable praxis, volatile coherence, inconsistent responsibility, or inability to coordinate action. But no such pattern is given. There is no series of reframings, no failed repair sequence, no unstable institutional process, no role-holder unable to consolidate responsibility. The axis effects are therefore under-specified: coherence is not shown to be volatile, and responsibility is not shown to be inconsistent.

This distinction matters for the whole article. A case can refuse closure without being immature. A model can preserve non-capture without failing integration. An analysis can withhold action findings because no action scene exists. If the stack were to treat every weak Σ as AD_Sigma_low, it would become a closure machine: every remainder would become a defect, every refusal would become under-integration, and every rival framing would become a maturity failure. The completed YAMLs avoid this.

The related coherence risk is high. The danger is not that the case lacks a formula. The danger is that the formula is too smooth. PMS can render the case elegantly as Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ Δ. CRITIQUE can render it as CRITIQUE_MIN with weak Φ and weak Σ. MIP can render it as a working-note maturity artefact. AHP can render it as high precision with documented attack surfaces. These renderings are coherent. That coherence must not be mistaken for capture.

The under-discrimination risk is also high. The terms “interpretability,” “explanation,” “reality,” “practice,” “remainder,” and “non-capture” remain philosophically broad. The Core YAML explicitly warns that PMS would be under-discriminating if it cannot separate practical remainder from missing evidence, ordinary ambiguity, rhetorical contrast, or incomplete explanation. AHP later sharpens this as an attack point: the terms are scoped, but not operationally differentiated. This is not fatal for a conceptual article, but it is a real limit.

Thus the PMS drift-pattern status is disciplined: drift risks are visible, but not activated; coherence risk is high, but mitigated by non-capture; under-specification is correct, not a failure; and non-assignment is not a low score.

### Add-on / overlay reading

The add-on used in this case is **PMS-CRITIQUE**. It is applied as an overlay/application profile only. It does not redefine PMS operators, change PMS dependencies, add base operators, or rewrite the PMS Core pass. Its reduced signatures are overlay shorthand, not new PMS primitives.

PMS Core alone already makes the primary structure legible: a framed difference between interpretability and practical reality, an absent or incomplete coincidence, recontextualization through explanation, and the need for Distance. PMS Core can also preserve non-capture and weaken integration. However, PMS Core alone does not isolate the specifically critique-relevant question: when does the mismatch between interpretation and practical remainder become critique? When is it merely irritation, reaction, judgment, commentary, or narrative repair? When has it crossed into correction? When is follow-up actually bound rather than merely discussed?

PMS-CRITIQUE adds exactly this visibility. It distinguishes critique thresholds. It separates minimal critique from productive critique and stable critique. It identifies drift risks specific to critique practices: reaction drift, judgment drift, narrative repair, commentary drift, diffusion drift, integration bypass, and public pillory risks where publicness is active. In this case, publicness is not active, but the critique threshold and drift distinctions are highly relevant.

The overlay reaches **CRITIQUE_MIN** only at the conceptual/application level. Minimal critique is defined as Χ-stabilized framed differentiation: a mismatch Δ becomes relevant within a frame □, and the configuration remains interruptible through Χ. In this case, Δ is the mismatch between interpretability or explanation and practical remainder. □ is the philosophical-praxeological frame that makes the mismatch relevant. Χ is the maintained stop-capability that prevents verdict, sanction, forced integration, PMS triumphalism, or conversion of remainder into defect.

This is why the case is not mere irritation or reaction. The mismatch is not simply felt as discomfort. It is framed and held. It also does not collapse into Δ + ∇ impulse discharge. The CRITIQUE overlay therefore marks a minimal critique threshold: the case is critique-ready in a conceptual sense.

However, the case does **not** reach **CRITIQUE_PROD**. Productive critique would require Δ, □, Χ, Φ, and Σ. Here, Φ is active: the case is about interpretation and explanation as recontextualizing moves. But Σ remains weak. There is no corrective step, no action plan, no repair, no procedure, no institutional adjustment, no shared follow-up, and no consolidated practical transformation. Explanation alone does not become correction. Recontextualization alone does not become productive critique. The overlay therefore marks productive critique as a nearby but unfulfilled threshold.

This is one of the most important additions beyond PMS Core. PMS Core says integration is weak and risky. PMS-CRITIQUE says why that matters for critique: without Σ, the critique remains interruptive and legible, but not corrective. It may show that explanation is insufficient, but it does not yet change the practical situation. It can name critique-readiness; it cannot claim critique completion.

The case also does **not** reach **CRITIQUE_STABLE**. Stable critique would require Δ, □, Χ, Φ, Σ, and Ψ. It would bind correction into a trajectory without coercing others. Follow-up would become owned over time while remaining revisable and restrained by Dignity-in-Practice. In this case, no follow-up owner exists. No temporally extended correction trajectory exists. No review condition, shared praxis scene, or case-actor self-binding exists. Ψ appears only as analytic self-binding of the PMS and CRITIQUE application to restraint, reversibility, non-capture, and Dignity-in-Practice.

The overlay also introduces **horizontal scale**. The selected scale is generic structural critique. This is not a single-subject critique, not a small-group critique, not an organizational critique, and not a public critique. Publicness P is not applicable. It does not amplify Ω, Θ, or Α. There is no platform, crowd, reputational exposure, public target, mass trace, or public pillory dynamic.

Several CRITIQUE drift risks are active as risks, not findings.

**Judgment drift** is a risk because a framed difference can easily become a verdict: explanation fails, reality wins; PMS captures, rival readings fail; critique sees what interpretation misses. The overlay blocks this by preserving Χ and non-capture.

**Narrative repair drift** is a risk because explanation may re-narrate the practical remainder so smoothly that the remainder disappears. In this case, the risk is central. Interpretation may become the story that makes reality feel handled. The Core pass mitigates this by weakening Σ and preserving non-capture.

**Commentary drift** is a risk because the case is abstract. It could remain a refined commentary about critique, interpretation, and remainder without any corrective implication. The YAML does not treat that as failure; it marks it as a risk of the article form itself.

**Diffusion drift** is a weak risk because abstraction can diffuse responsibility. Without actors, affected parties, obligations, or follow-up owners, responsibility cannot be assigned. This is analytically proper, but it can also become a way for everything to remain unowned if a concrete scene later appears. MIP therefore withholds Responsibility_R and M-score, while requiring future concrete-scene material before responsibility analysis.

**Integration bypass** is a central risk. Explanation may bypass Σ by appearing coherent without consolidating corrective practice. The overlay keeps Σ weak. It refuses to call the analysis productive critique merely because it is structurally elegant.

The overlay constructs remain non-operator handles. CRITIQUE_MIN, CRITIQUE_PROD, CRITIQUE_STABLE, vertical reach, horizontal scale, publicness P, drift classes, and modulators are not PMS operators. They organize existing PMS operators for critique analysis. They do not modify Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, or Ψ. This subordination is not merely formal; it is a misuse boundary. If CRITIQUE reduced signatures were treated as new base operators, the overlay would silently rewrite PMS and violate the case’s own non-mixing discipline.

The overlay’s final signature is therefore: CRITIQUE_MIN with active Δ, □, and Χ; active-but-bounded Φ; weak Σ; analytic-only Ψ; weak Ω and Θ; constrained drift risks D2/D3/D4/C7/C9; not CRITIQUE_PROD; not CRITIQUE_STABLE; no P activation; no MIP docking.

This signature adds real value beyond PMS Core. PMS Core says what the mismatch is. PMS-CRITIQUE says what kind of critique threshold the mismatch reaches and where it fails to become correction. It makes visible that the case is structurally critique-ready, not correction-complete.

### Layer interaction

The layer interaction is disciplined and non-mixing. PMS Core remains the base structural grammar. PMS-CRITIQUE sharpens critique-specific thresholds and drift risks. MIP reads the completed PMS and CRITIQUE artefacts as a downstream maturity-in-practice and guardrail problem. AHP then assesses the quality and attack surface of the MIP analysis artefact. None of these layers overwrites the previous one.

What remains PMS Core is the operator reading: Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, Ψ; the compressed formula Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ Δ; the derived-structure discipline; the non-capture rule; and the claim that the case is a bounded conceptual projection, not a concrete scene. PMS Core defines what can and cannot be claimed structurally. It also defines why later operators cannot be activated cheaply.

What PMS-CRITIQUE sharpens is the critique status of the mismatch. It tells us that the case reaches minimal critique because the mismatch is framed and interruptible. It also tells us that productive and stable critique are not reached. This distinction is not supplied by PMS Core alone. PMS Core can say Σ is weak; CRITIQUE says weak Σ means no correction threshold. PMS Core can say Ψ is analytic-only; CRITIQUE says no follow-up binding is present. PMS Core can say Φ is active; CRITIQUE says Φ may become narrative repair if it substitutes for correction.

MIP adds a different kind of visibility. It does not sharpen the philosophical structure directly. Instead, it asks whether the analysis artefact is mature for use. Its answer is restrained: the analysis is a working note. It is green-zone structural reflection, not a decision basis. A-score is not assigned because the object is not a bounded enactment or role-context episode. M-score is not assigned because there is no actor or role-holder. IA-box yields no concrete asymmetry classification; it flags only conceptual IA risk. D-module remains off; D₀ is untouched; no D₁/D₂ profile is created. Transmission status remains working_note, not transmittable as decision basis.

This MIP layer is important because it prevents the YAML stack from becoming institutionally usable in the wrong way. A philosophically elegant case could be misused as a scoring artefact, a maturity judgment, a governance rationale, or a dignity reading. MIP blocks that escalation. It says: not enough scene material, no scores, no D activation, no decision use.

AHP then hardens the MIP output as an analysis artefact. It assigns a high Precision Heuristic, 6/6, but explicitly says PH is not A-score, not M-score, not D-score, and not an IA verdict. It identifies seven analysis-level attack points: model-stack coherence may be mistaken for practical capture; IA non-classification may be overread as an IA judgment; D-language is clean but frequent enough to create D-profile misreading risk; key philosophical terms remain broad; rival formalism openness is preserved but not operationalized; future concrete-scene requirements are complete but not prioritized; and the transmission boundary is clear but needs a portable export warning.

AHP is especially valuable because it shows that even a high-precision artefact remains vulnerable. It does not say the analysis is complete. It says the analysis is precise enough to reveal where it is attackable. That is exactly the kind of second-order discipline this case needs. Its hardening function is therefore methodological rather than substantive: it improves the handling of the analysis artefact, not the case’s evidentiary basis. The high Precision Heuristic confirms boundary clarity, scope control, reversibility, and language hygiene; it does not upgrade the case into empirical knowledge, practical resolution, IA classification, D-profile, or transmittable decision artefact.

Overlay language could overreach in several places. CRITIQUE could overreach if minimal critique were treated as productive critique. It could overreach if drift risks were treated as findings. It could overreach if publicness P were activated without a public scene. MIP could overreach if no-score became low score, if working-note status became implicit authority, or if D-guardrail language became a D-profile. AHP could overreach if high PH were treated as validation of the case rather than precision of the analysis artefact.

Non-closure remains visible at every layer. PMS preserves Λ and non-capture. CRITIQUE refuses to upgrade critique-readiness to correction. MIP refuses scores and decision transmission. AHP refuses rescoring and identifies attack surfaces. The layered result is not closure by accumulation. It is boundary reinforcement by accumulation.

### Example 1 — The Explained Complaint That Still Leaves a Cost

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** after “Why this case matters” or after the PMS Core discussion of Λ, Φ, and Χ.

A small team receives a complaint from one member that a recurring review process leaves them carrying extra work. The explanation arrives quickly and is not obviously false. The process was designed under time pressure. The review checklist grew gradually. Several people assumed that the extra coordination step was informal, not assigned. The person who now carries it happens to be the one with the most context, so the work keeps returning to them. Once this is explained, the situation becomes intelligible. The team can now say why the burden appeared, why no one originally intended it, and why the pattern persisted.

Yet the practical reality has not disappeared. The person still lost time. The recurring burden still exists. The explanation may reduce blame, clarify origin, or prevent a crude accusation, but it does not by itself redistribute the work, acknowledge the cost, or create a revised process. The complaint has become interpretable; the practical remainder remains.

This example demonstrates the central structure of the case: explanation can be valid as interpretation without being sufficient as practical response. The important point is not that the explanation is bad. On the contrary, it may be a good explanation. The problem is the closure-demand that tries to make explanation coincide with repair. The case shows why PMS must distinguish interpretive coherence from practical adequacy.

The PMS Core hooks are direct. **Δ** appears as the difference between “the process is now explained” and “the burden remains real.” **□** is the team-process frame: within that frame, the issue is not metaphysical reality or personal character, but role allocation, workload, and process expectation. **Λ** appears as the missing event: after explanation, one might expect the burden to be handled, but that handling has not occurred. **Φ** appears in the recontextualization of the complaint: it is reframed from “someone is being unfair” into “a process gradually produced an unassigned burden.” That recontextualization may be useful, but it is not repair. **Χ** is required to prevent either side from collapsing the matter into verdict. The complaint should not become a person-accusation; the explanation should not become a closure device. **Σ** remains conditional because no integrated redistribution has yet occurred. **Ψ** remains absent unless someone explicitly binds themselves to a follow-up change.

The PMS-CRITIQUE overlay adds a sharper reading. The case reaches **CRITIQUE_MIN** if the mismatch is framed and held interruptibly: the complaint marks a real difference, the frame makes it relevant, and the team does not immediately retaliate, dismiss, or moralize. But the case does not yet reach **CRITIQUE_PROD**, because no corrective step has been consolidated. A meeting that explains the burden without changing the process risks **commentary drift** or **narrative repair drift**. The team may now have a better story about the problem while the practical burden remains. That is a risk, not a finding about the people involved.

MIP would remain limited unless more concrete material were supplied. If this were a real case, MIP would need role-context-time-window, affected-side perspective, authority to change the process, available alternatives, and review conditions before any responsibility or maturity analysis could be attempted. In the example as written, no A-score, M-score, IA classification, or D-profile is warranted. AHP would be relevant only as analysis-artifact hardening: it would ask whether the written analysis makes too much of the explanation, whether it preserves the practical cost, and whether it avoids turning the complaint or the explanation into a verdict.

The forbidden claims are important. This example does not show that anyone is malicious, immature, avoidant, irresponsible, or morally deficient. It does not establish a legal claim, a clinical pattern, or an empirical generalization about teams. It does not prove that explanation is useless. It also does not prove that every unresolved burden is a PMS Λ-residue. The vignette is only a bounded illustration of how explanation and practical remainder can diverge.

The non-capture note is that the affected person’s actual experience, the real cost of the work, and the practical adequacy of any future repair are not exhausted by the structural description. PMS can mark the gap between explanation and remaining burden, but the burden itself would still require concrete handling.

The article takeaway is that interpretation may be necessary for repair, but it is not identical with repair. A case can become clearer while remaining unresolved. The correct structural result is not to reject explanation, but to keep the absent practical follow-up visible.

### Example 2 — The Interpretability Report That Does Not Settle Deployment Risk

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** after the PMS-CRITIQUE overlay section, especially where the article distinguishes CRITIQUE_MIN from CRITIQUE_PROD and warns against integration bypass.

A technical review group examines a model output that behaved unexpectedly in a sensitive workflow. The team produces an interpretability report. The report identifies the main input features that contributed to the output, reconstructs the decision path, compares several nearby cases, and explains why the system behaved as it did under the configured objective. The report is careful, bounded, and useful. It shows that the output was not random. It also shows that the model followed patterns consistent with its training and deployment configuration.

After the report, the situation is more interpretable. But the deployment risk is not automatically resolved. The practical question remains: should the workflow continue unchanged, be paused, be redesigned, be monitored differently, or be removed from that setting? The interpretability report may support such decisions, but it is not itself the decision. It may explain the output without proving that the output is acceptable, safe, fair, lawful, clinically meaningful, or operationally legitimate.

This example demonstrates the case’s core warning in a domain where the temptation is especially strong: interpretability can be mistaken for reality-management. Once a technical explanation exists, there is pressure to treat the issue as handled. But a model’s legible internal path does not settle the practical consequences of using the model. The report can reduce opacity; it cannot by itself resolve the deployment frame.

The PMS Core hooks are again precise. **Δ** is the difference between interpretability of the model output and the practical reality of deployment risk. **∇** appears as pressure to move from “we understand why it happened” to “therefore the situation is under control.” **□** is the technical-governance frame: what counts as explanation, risk, admissible evidence, and decision relevance must be declared. **Λ** appears as several missing events: no decision has yet been made; no affected-side review is supplied; no operational validation is complete; no future-risk assessment is established merely by explanation. **Α** would become relevant only if interpretability reports repeatedly stabilize as a substitute for governance review. In this vignette, that is a risk, not a finding. **Ω** is possible because some roles may authorize deployment while others bear downstream effects, but the example does not specify enough to assign a concrete asymmetry finding. **Θ** is possible because deployment risk unfolds over time, but no actual trajectory is supplied. **Φ** is active because the unexplained output is recontextualized as a legible model behaviour. **Χ** is required to stop the report from becoming a verdict. **Σ** remains weak unless the report is integrated with risk review, affected context, and operational decision criteria. **Ψ** remains absent unless a responsible body binds itself to a review, redesign, monitoring, or withdrawal commitment.

The PMS-CRITIQUE overlay is particularly useful here. The interpretability report may bring the case to **CRITIQUE_MIN**: a mismatch is framed, made legible, and held open for review. But it does not reach **CRITIQUE_PROD** unless it leads to a coherent corrective step. It does not reach **CRITIQUE_STABLE** unless follow-up is owned over time without coercion or responsibility diffusion. The main drift risks are **integration bypass** and **commentary drift**. Integration bypass occurs if explanation appears to do the work that practical risk review should do. Commentary drift occurs if the report generates further discussion and documentation without changing the deployment conditions. These remain risks, not findings, unless a concrete workflow shows them recurring.

MIP status must stay guarded. A real deployment case might eventually require MIP because governance relevance would be present: roles, asymmetries, consequences, reversibility, and public or institutional stakes might matter. But in this example, as stated, MIP should not assign A-score or M-score. It can only say what would be required: role mapping, affected-side perspective, authority distribution, consequence horizon, alternatives, review windows, and reversibility conditions. AHP would be useful as a second-order hardening layer for the interpretability report itself. It would ask whether the report’s precision is being confused with practical adequacy, whether non-capture is explicit, whether rival risk interpretations remain open, and whether the transmission status is clear.

The forbidden claims are strict. The example does not claim that the system is unsafe or safe. It does not validate or invalidate the interpretability method. It does not make a legal, clinical, empirical, or implementation finding. It does not diagnose the review team as negligent or overconfident. It does not prove that technical interpretability is inadequate in general. It only shows that interpretability and practical deployment adequacy are distinct.

The non-capture note is that the real deployment effects, affected-side experience, institutional obligations, and future consequences are not contained in the interpretability report. They may be informed by it, but not replaced by it. A model can be explainable and still practically unresolved.

The article takeaway is that interpretability becomes dangerous when it is treated as closure. Its proper role is bounded: it can support critique-readiness and possible correction, but it does not itself prove that correction has occurred.

### Example 3 — The Philosophical Explanation That Leaves the Tragedy Intact

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** after “Non-capture and rival readings,” where the article discusses moral remainder, tragic readings, and the refusal to treat non-capture as failure.

A philosophical discussion examines a morally painful situation: two commitments conflict, and any available choice leaves someone harmed or something important unrepaired. The participants carefully analyse the structure. They distinguish the roles involved, identify the competing obligations, clarify the frame, and explain why no option fully satisfies all constraints. The discussion is rigorous. It avoids cheap blame. It prevents a simplistic verdict. It shows why the situation is difficult rather than merely confused.

The analysis succeeds at one level. It makes the situation interpretable. It explains why closure is unavailable. It may even protect the participants from false moralization by showing that the conflict is structurally tragic rather than caused by one person’s failure. But the tragedy remains. The person who loses support still loses support. The promise that cannot be kept is still broken. The burden that cannot be fairly distributed still lands somewhere. The explanation may be humane and clarifying, but it does not undo the practical loss.

This example demonstrates the deepest non-capture zone in the case. Sometimes the remainder is not missing information. It is not an unintegrated frame. It is not an immature refusal to accept complexity. It is the persistence of practical loss after explanation. A structural analysis can protect against false closure, but it cannot make the tragedy disappear.

The PMS Core hooks are substantial. **Δ** is the difference between interpretive clarity and remaining loss. **□** is the moral-praxeological frame within which conflicting commitments are considered. **Λ** is not merely a missing fact; it is the absent event of full repair or non-tragic closure. **∇** appears as pressure to find a satisfying resolution, but it should remain weak unless a concrete actor or decision pressure is specified. **Ω** may be relevant if one party carries more cost or has less say, but it must not be asserted without a concrete scene. **Θ** may be relevant because commitments and losses unfold over time, but the vignette does not give enough sequence to ground a full trajectory. **Φ** is active because the situation is recontextualized from personal failure into structural tragedy. **Χ** is essential because it prevents the analysis from turning tragedy into either blame or consolation. **Σ** remains limited: one can integrate the explanation, but not the loss itself. **Ψ** is absent unless some actor binds themselves to future care, repair, acknowledgment, or prevention.

The PMS-CRITIQUE overlay helps distinguish the levels. The discussion may achieve **CRITIQUE_MIN** because the mismatch between explanation and remaining loss is framed and held without coercive verdict. It may approach productive critique if the analysis leads to a concrete practice of acknowledgment, redistribution, or future prevention, but that is not supplied here. It does not become stable critique unless follow-up is bound over time. The main risks are **narrative repair drift** and **commentary drift**. Narrative repair drift would occur if the tragic explanation makes participants feel the loss has been morally processed and therefore no longer needs attention. Commentary drift would occur if the discussion remains refined but disconnected from any possible care, repair, or acknowledgment. These are risks, not findings.

MIP must remain careful. If this were a real case, MIP might become relevant because harms, obligations, asymmetries, and responsibility could be present. But this example is intentionally bounded. It does not supply enough information for A-score, M-score, IA classification, or D-profile. MIP would remain a working-note guardrail unless the concrete role-context-time-window were supplied. AHP would be relevant to harden the analysis by asking whether the language of tragedy is being used to preserve non-capture or to avoid responsibility. It would also check whether the analysis is too elegant, whether affected-side adequacy is missing, and whether rival readings are operationalized rather than merely listed.

The forbidden claims are especially important here. The example does not claim that tragedy excuses harm. It does not claim that no one is responsible. It does not claim that structural explanation is morally superior to grief, anger, refusal, or testimony. It does not diagnose anyone as unable to integrate complexity. It does not convert suffering into a model residue. It does not say that PMS, CRITIQUE, MIP, or AHP has solved the tragic situation. It also does not make a metaphysical claim that all moral reality exceeds interpretation.

The non-capture note is that the loss itself remains. The broken promise, the unavailable repair, the unequal burden, or the grief may be structurally interpretable, but not thereby dissolved. A rival tragic, phenomenological, testimony-centered, or ethical reading may preserve the weight of this remainder better than an operator grammar.

The article takeaway is that non-capture can be a disciplined result rather than an analytic failure. The best structural reading may be the one that knows where to stop: it clarifies why closure is unavailable without pretending that clarification is itself repair.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The case preserves non-capture as a central result. What remains outside PMS capture is not simply an omitted field. It is the practical reality or moral remainder that may persist despite interpretation. The analysis can mark this remainder; it cannot make it disappear.

The three examples show why this matters. In the complaint example, the explanation of the process does not remove the actual workload cost. In the interpretability report example, technical legibility does not settle deployment risk. In the tragedy example, structural explanation does not undo the loss. In all three, interpretation has real value. None treats explanation as worthless. But each shows that explanation and practical resolution are distinct.

The YAMLs identify several rival readings, each preserving something important.

An **epistemic humility reading** says that explanation is useful but not equivalent to reality. This reading preserves the caution that intelligibility is not adequacy. PMS shares this caution, but PMS still translates it into operator language. The humility reading may resist that translation by emphasizing the attitude of restraint rather than the structure of restraint.

A **moral remainder reading** says morally difficult situations may leave residues no formal grammar resolves. This reading preserves tragedy, loss, harm, obligation, or exposure that does not become less real when explained. PMS can mark Λ residue, but the moral remainder reading may insist that residue is not merely a structural absence. It may be ethically decisive in a way that operator mapping can only partially display.

An **anti-formalist reading** treats the case as a challenge to operator grammars as such. From this perspective, the fact that PMS can render non-capture may itself be suspicious: perhaps the model is absorbing its own limit into its vocabulary. This rival reading is not a defect. It is an important guard against formal triumphalism. The AHP attack point about model-stack coherence directly supports this caution.

A **praxis-priority reading** says enacted consequence has priority over interpretive coherence. What matters is not whether the analysis is elegant, but whether practical reality is handled differently. This reading is especially important because the case lacks action. It reminds the article that critique-readiness is not correction, and working-note maturity is not practical repair.

A **hermeneutic reading** says interpretation is unavoidable but never identical with the lived or enacted situation it interprets. This reading preserves the embeddedness of understanding: no analysis stands outside interpretation, but no interpretation exhausts its object. PMS can model frame, recontextualization, and distance; hermeneutics may preserve the irreducible circularity of understanding more richly.

A **phenomenological reading** may emphasize lived reality, suffering, exposure, or experience. PMS deliberately avoids phenomenological claims. That is a strength for non-diagnostic structural analysis, but also a limit. If the practical remainder is primarily lived experience, PMS may be too formal unless supplemented by a method designed for that domain.

A **pragmatic reading** says the adequacy of interpretation depends on whether it improves situated handling, not on formal coherence alone. This reading preserves the question of consequence. It is close to the MIP concern: what can actually be done, by whom, under what constraints, and with what review conditions?

A **discourse-ethical reading** would ask whether affected parties can participate in the justification of the interpretation. The current case has no affected-side perspective. This rival reading therefore exposes a major gap if the case were ever applied in public or institutional contexts.

A **testimony-centered reading** would ask whether practical reality is being reported by someone whose voice cannot be replaced by structural mapping. Since the current case has no testimony, this remains hypothetical. But it is an important rival because it reminds the model not to turn absence of testimony into absence of reality.

A **tragic reading** would preserve irreducible loss, conflict, or non-repair. It would be especially relevant if the practical remainder were not merely unknown but genuinely unfixable. PMS can preserve Λ; tragedy may preserve the human weight of non-resolution that formal structures can understate.

None of these rival readings is treated as failure, immaturity, drift, or incomplete integration. They are epistemically open. PMS, CRITIQUE, MIP, and AHP do not defeat them by rendering them. AHP explicitly recommends a rival-comparison checklist using discrimination, non-assimilation risk, affected-side adequacy, and practical consequence handling. That recommendation is important: rival openness is currently preserved, but not yet operationalized.

The non-capture zones are therefore multiple. Practical reality may remain after explanation. Moral remainder may persist after coherent interpretation. Rival formalisms may preserve dimensions PMS backgrounds. Affected-side experience may be absent from the current artefact. Operational consequence may remain untested. Public legitimacy may require discourse conditions absent here. These are not defects to be patched away. They are the living boundary of the case.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS reading would be weakened if the distinction between explanation and practical remainder cannot be sustained. If later material shows that the case does not actually involve a difference between interpretability and what remains real, Δ would lose its entry role. The formula Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ Δ would then be too strong.

The PMS reading would also be weakened if no relevant frame can be specified. Since □ is central, the analysis depends on a frame that makes interpretation, explanation, and practical remainder meaningful. If the case remains a loose slogan without a stable frame, PMS can still note ambiguity, but it cannot deliver the current structural reading.

The PMS reading would be weakened if the supposed non-event is not a structured absence but merely missing information. Λ requires an expected occurrence within a frame. If the problem is only that the case material is sparse, then the absent coincidence between explanation and reality may not be a case structure at all. It may be an artefact of under-description. This is why operationalization would require distinguishing non-capture from missing evidence.

The PMS reading would become under-discriminating if it cannot distinguish practical remainder from incomplete explanation, missing evidence, ordinary ambiguity, rhetorical contrast, or metaphysical speculation. This is one of the main AHP attack points. Future hardening should define practical consequence, moral remainder, missing evidence, and non-capture more explicitly.

The PMS reading would become over-assimilative if rupture, disagreement, non-fit, rival framing, unresolved absence, or resistance were automatically redescribed as PMS drift, incomplete integration, incomplete calibration, recontextualization demand, or PMS-legible non-event. This is the central misuse threat. The article must preserve the possibility that a rival reading is not PMS material waiting to be integrated.

The PMS reading would also be overextended if Ω, Θ, Α, Σ, or Ψ were upgraded from weak or conditional status to active case findings without concrete material. Moral difficulty alone does not activate Ω. The phrase “remains” alone does not activate Θ. A possible substitution pattern does not activate Α. Coherence does not activate Σ. Analytic restraint does not activate case-agent Ψ.

The PMS-CRITIQUE reading would be weakened if the mismatch does not reach framed interruptibility. CRITIQUE_MIN requires Δ, □, and Χ. If Distance collapses, the case becomes reaction, judgment, or closure. If the frame is too unstable, the mismatch does not become critique-ready. If the mismatch is not relevant, critique does not arise.

The CRITIQUE reading would be overclaimed if CRITIQUE_MIN were treated as productive critique. Productive critique requires Σ consolidation into correction. No such correction exists. It would also be overclaimed if stable critique were asserted. Stable critique requires Ψ follow-up binding. No follow-up owner exists. Any claim of correction-completion would violate the overlay.

The CRITIQUE drift reading would be weakened if the identified drift risks were treated as active failures. They are risks, not findings. Judgment drift, narrative repair, commentary drift, diffusion drift, and integration bypass remain possible vulnerabilities of the analysis and of future concrete scenes. They are not present as demonstrated events.

The MIP reading would be weakened if it assigned scores despite non-concrete status. It does not. If a later revision tried to assign an A-band or M-band to the abstract case, it would violate score hygiene. A-score requires a bounded enactment or role-context episode. M-score requires an actor or role-holder with assessable knowledge, power/control, predictability, alternatives, and integration attempt. None is present.

The MIP IA-box would be invalid if the conceptual IA risk were treated as a concrete asymmetry classification. The AHP overlay flags this as a high-severity attack point. T/J/TB/R entries in the IA-box describe analysis-artefact handling only; they do not classify a concrete asymmetry.

The D-module would be invalid if D-language were treated as a D-profile. D remains off. D₀ is untouched. No D₁/D₂ enactment is observed. No D-Quick-Grid level is assigned. The AHP overlay flags the frequency of D-language as a medium-risk misreading surface.

The AHP reading would be weakened if PH were treated as a base score. It is not. PH is a precision heuristic for the analysis artefact. It increases confidence that the analysis is well bounded; it does not increase evidence quality, practical adequacy, or case truth. It also does not make the analysis transmittable.

Operationalization remains unfulfilled across all layers. To move beyond conceptual analysis, the case would need at least: a concrete actor or role; an affected side; a time window; an action or non-action; a consequence; power/control conditions; alternatives; review conditions; frame declaration; evidence; publicness status; and rival-formalism comparison. If the case became empirical, it would need observable indicators. If legal, it would need legal standards. If clinical, it would require clinical context and proper non-MIP safeguards. If computational, it would require architecture-specific calibration and evaluation. If public or institutional, it would require participation, contest channels, reversibility, and transmission review.

Until then, the correct transmission status remains working_note. The analysis is chapter-ready as human-readable structural rendering, but not decision-ready as governance artefact.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It does not identify psychological traits, pathologies, cognitive deficits, trauma responses, maturity levels, or mental states. No person appears in the case. Even if future examples include persons, the current analysis cannot be used to label them as over-interpreting, under-integrated, reality-avoidant, immature, defensive, or incoherent.

It must not be used for person ranking. Dignity-in-Practice is not a ranking. CRITIQUE reach levels are not virtue levels. MIP withheld scores are not low scores. AHP attack points are not defects in persons. No person, group, institution, theory, or rival formalism is ranked as superior or inferior.

It must not be used as a legal or forensic conclusion. It does not establish liability, culpability, evidence sufficiency, intent, causation, witness reliability, or admissibility. The distinction between interpretation and reality may be relevant in legal contexts, but this case does not supply legal material.

It must not be used as metaphysical proof. “Reality” is not solved. PMS does not prove what reality is. The analysis does not establish realism, anti-realism, constructivism, phenomenology, or any final ontology. It only marks practical remainder within a bounded frame.

It must not be used as empirical proof. No observation supports the case. No dataset is analysed. No behavioural claim is made. No comparative validation has been performed. The model stack’s coherence is not evidence.

It must not be used as implementation validation. YAML representation is not a working system. No interpretability tool, AI architecture, governance workflow, or decision procedure is validated. A computational rendering would require implementation-specific calibration, testing, and accountability.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. A case that PMS can render does not prove PMS complete, uniquely sufficient, or superior to rival grammars. The same holds for PMS-CRITIQUE, MIP, and AHP. AXIOM maps closure-demands; it does not validate models by successful-looking outputs.

It must not treat the add-on as an operator. PMS-CRITIQUE constructs such as CRITIQUE_MIN, CRITIQUE_PROD, CRITIQUE_STABLE, publicness P, vertical reach, drift classes, and modulators are overlay handles. They do not alter Δ–Ψ. Reduced signatures are shorthand and remain dependency-hygienic.

It must not treat drift as verdict. PMS drift risks and CRITIQUE drift risks are structural vulnerabilities, not accusations. Judgment drift, narrative repair, commentary drift, diffusion drift, and integration bypass are not findings about a person, institution, or theory. They are risks to watch if the structure is applied.

It must not use closure by authority. The article may be long, precise, and layered, but length and precision do not authorize closure. The final boundary remains: interpretation may become disciplined and still not coincide with what remains real in practice.

It must not use non-capture as dismissal. Saying that something remains non-captured is not a way to ignore it. It is a way to prevent false capture. If future material shows harm, obligation, responsibility, or exposure, those must be handled in the appropriate concrete frame, not hidden behind “non-capture.”

It must not use working-note status as evasion. MIP’s refusal to score is not a refusal of responsibility where responsibility is later established. It only says that this abstract case lacks the material required for responsibility analysis. If a concrete scene appears, a new analysis must be run.

Finally, it must not convert rival readings into deficiencies. A hermeneutic, phenomenological, pragmatic, moral-remainder, discourse-ethical, tragic, or anti-formalist reading is not invalid because it resists PMS vocabulary. Rival readings may preserve dimensions that PMS, CRITIQUE, MIP, or AHP background by design.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result of this case is deliberately bounded. PMS Core shows that “Interpretability vs. Reality” can be structurally rendered as a framed difference between explanation and practical remainder, organized around an absent or incomplete coincidence and held under Distance. The primary PMS formula, **Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ Δ**, is not a solution to reality, but a disciplined way of keeping interpretation, recontextualization, non-event, frame, and difference in view without forcing closure. The weak status of ∇, Α, Ω, Θ, Σ, and Ψ is part of the result, not a defect. The case is abstract; it is not a concrete praxis scene.

PMS-CRITIQUE adds the second result: the case reaches **minimal critique** at the conceptual level, but not productive or stable critique. It is critique-ready because the mismatch is framed and interruptible. It is not correction-complete because no Σ consolidation occurs. It is not follow-up-bound because no Ψ owner, review condition, or action trajectory exists. This distinction is essential. The case does not merely say “interpretability is limited.” It says that the recognition of that limit can itself become a minimal critique, while still remaining far from repair.

MIP adds the third result: the layered analysis remains a working note. It is not score-eligible, not decision-ready, not a concrete IA classification, and not a D-profile. The maturity signal is not a high score; it is disciplined restraint. AHP adds the fourth result: the analysis artefact is precise, but still attackable. Its most important attack surface is precisely the one the case predicts: cross-model coherence may be mistaken for practical capture. The AHP hardening therefore reinforces the case’s own methodological lesson.

The three examples contribute by making the same boundary visible in different registers. The explained complaint shows that a practical burden can remain after the process is understood. The interpretability report shows that technical legibility does not settle deployment risk. The philosophical tragedy shows that structural explanation can clarify non-closure without undoing loss. None of these examples functions as evidence. They are article-ready illustrations of the completed YAML structure. They show why explanation matters and why explanation must not be mistaken for completion.

What remains unclaimed is as important as what is claimed. The article does not prove anything metaphysical about reality. It does not establish empirical findings about interpretation. It does not diagnose interpreters, institutions, technical teams, or philosophical participants. It does not produce legal, clinical, implementation, or governance conclusions. It does not validate PMS, PMS-CRITIQUE, MIP, or AHP. It does not show that rival readings are weaker. It does not claim that non-capture is always the right final word. It only claims that, in this case as supplied, non-capture must remain possible and visible.

The boundary discipline is therefore the heart of the case. Coherence is not proof. Risk is not finding. Weak operator status is not failure. Non-assignment is not low score. Drift risk is not diagnosis. Dignity-in-Practice is a praxeological application constraint, not ontological ranking. Add-on terms are overlay handles, not new PMS operators. AHP hardens the analysis, but does not rescore it. Resistance, refusal, non-fit, disagreement, rupture, and absence are not automatically drift, incomplete integration, or maturity failure.

The final methodological takeaway is that AXIOM’s value lies not in making difficult realities disappear into structure, but in showing where structure can responsibly stop. A strong analysis does not have to become a closure machine. It can clarify the frame, identify the closure-demand, preserve the Λ-residue, mark critique-readiness, withhold scores, expose its own attack surface, and still leave practical reality unclaimed. The article can therefore be chapter-ready while the underlying case analysis remains non-decisional as a practical artefact. In this case, restraint is not an unfinished gesture. It is the result.
