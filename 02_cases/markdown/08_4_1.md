### Why this case matters

Critique is often treated as if its meaning were stable across scale. A local objection, a private disagreement, an institutional complaint, a public accusation, a systemic critique, and a philosophical diagnosis may all be called “critique,” yet they do not operate under the same structural conditions. The same sentence, gesture, or claim can change when the audience changes, when institutional roles are introduced, when public visibility amplifies exposure, when temporal residue accumulates, or when a local mismatch is recontextualized as a systemic pattern.

This case matters because it isolates a closure-demand that is philosophically common but structurally unstable: the demand to know what critique “is” or what critique “does” when it moves from micro to macro contexts. In small-scale settings, critique may be interruptive, corrective, relational, or dialogical. At wider scales, critique may become institutional, public, political, systemic, archival, reputational, or symbolic. These are not simply larger versions of the same thing. Scale changes the frame, the asymmetry, the temporal horizon, the audience, the available correction path, the risk of irreversibility, and the dignity-in-practice constraints around exposure.

The philosophical closure-demand is therefore double. First, there is a demand to identify what remains continuous across critique’s movement from micro to macro. Second, there is a demand to determine what changes so strongly that the same word may conceal different praxeological structures. PMS-AXIOM treats this not as a metaphysical question about the essence of critique, but as a cartographic case: a case in which a classical closure-demand is mapped into explicit operator structure, overlay selection, drift risk, and misuse boundary.

The case belongs in AXIOM because AXIOM is not a new base model and not a verdict machine. It is a cartographic and indexing paper. This case is exemplary for AXIOM because it requires a layered reading. PMS Core can map the core transformation: Δ distinction, ∇ critical directedness, □ frame shift, Λ possible absence or non-response, Α possible stabilization, Ω exposure and asymmetry, Θ temporal extension, Φ recontextualization, Χ distance, Σ integration, and Ψ possible self-binding. But critique reach also has domain-specific features that PMS Core does not isolate as critique thresholds. PMS-CRITIQUE makes those visible: minimal critique, productive critique, stable critique, publicness as a non-operator modulator, critique drift classes, and the difference between interruption, correction, and non-coercive follow-up.

The case is structurally difficult because critique is neither merely speech nor merely judgment nor merely evaluation nor merely correction. It can be a mismatch signal, an interruption, a frame challenge, a demand for response, a public act, a structural diagnosis, a refusal, or an institutional practice. Some critiques aim at correction; others aim at refusal, rupture, exposure, consciousness-raising, symbolic contestation, or preservation of non-closure. A structurally responsible analysis must not force all critique into one correction-oriented grammar. It must keep open the possibility that some forms of critique resist PMS-CRITIQUE capture without thereby becoming immature, irrational, drifted, or deficient.

The case is not being solved metaphysically. It does not claim what critique ultimately is. It does not claim that critique has an essence, a final telos, or a universal normative form. It is not being solved empirically, because no corpus, case sequence, interview material, platform event, institutional complaint file, or public discourse dataset is supplied. It is not being solved clinically or diagnostically, because no critic, target, audience, or institution is psychologically assessed. It is not being solved legally or forensically, because no evidence chain, liability question, procedural standard, or culpability claim is given. It is not being solved by implementation, because no governance protocol, software workflow, moderation system, or institutional process is produced. It is also not being used to validate PMS, PMS-CRITIQUE, MIP, or AHP. The case shows what the stack can make legible; it does not prove that the stack exhausts critique.

The central difficulty is therefore a disciplined non-closure: critique reach can be mapped, but not finally captured. PMS Core shows the operator structure of scale transition. PMS-CRITIQUE sharpens threshold and drift visibility. MIP asks what maturity-in-practice risks arise if this artefact is used beyond theory. AHP then hardens the MIP output by identifying where formal completeness could be mistaken for evidentiary completeness. Across all layers, the case demonstrates AXIOM’s basic discipline: closure is possible only where the frame warrants it; where not, Λ-residue must remain visible.

### Scene and frame structure

The case has no concrete scene, but it does have a conceptual scene. The conceptual scene is the scale transition of critique: critique begins in a small-scale interpersonal or local frame and moves toward institutional, public, or systemic frames. This movement is not a mere enlargement of audience. It changes what counts as relevant evidence, who can respond, who is exposed, what becomes irreversible, what counts as correction, how long effects persist, and whether the critique remains interruptible.

The scene boundary is therefore the structural transformation of critique reach. Inside the frame are the following elements: local critique, macro critique, scale movement, frame shift, asymmetry change, publicness risk, temporal extension, possible non-response, possible stabilization into scripts, recontextualization, distance, integration, and possible follow-up binding. Outside the frame are the truth of any particular critique, the moral worth of critics or addressees, the empirical frequency of drift, the legal validity of accusations, the clinical condition of participants, and the legitimacy of any public or institutional action.

The core frame □ is explicitly scale-sensitive. At the micro level, critique may be situated in interpersonal roles, local norms, shared history, direct response, and shorter reversibility windows. A local criticism may be addressed to someone who can respond immediately, clarify, repair, refuse, or reframe. However, even micro critique may involve strong asymmetry: dependency, vulnerability, role authority, intimacy, or fear of retaliation. Therefore micro does not mean harmless or symmetrical.

At the macro level, critique may be institutional, public, systemic, generalized, mediated, archived, or circulated. It may address a role, a procedure, a profession, a public discourse, a policy, an institution, or a structural pattern. Macro critique may reveal conditions that micro frames cannot see, but it may also flatten local specificity. It may create public residue, reputational effects, audience-driven pressure, or diminished reversibility. Macro does not mean better, truer, more responsible, more mature, or necessarily more distorted. It means structurally different.

The protected constraints are reversibility, distance, dignity-in-practice, non-capture, rival openness, claim-typing, and layer discipline. Reversibility means the analysis must remain revisable and must not harden into verdict. Distance Χ means the analysis must not fuse critique with sanction, exposure, public shaming, or person-level classification. Dignity-in-Practice means asymmetry must be handled without humiliation, degradation, person-ranking, or dignity scoring. Non-capture means refusal, rupture, disagreement, symbolic protest, public rupture, and rival theory may remain outside PMS-CRITIQUE without being treated as failure. Layer discipline means PMS Core remains the operator grammar, PMS-CRITIQUE remains overlay shorthand, MIP remains downstream guardrail reading, and AHP remains second-order artefact hardening.

The roles are abstract. There may be a critic role, an addressee or target role, an institution or public role, an affected-party role, and an analyst role. But none of these is a concrete person. The critic role can interrupt, object, reframe, expose mismatch, or seek correction. The addressee role may receive critique, respond, repair, contest, or be exposed. The public or institutional field may amplify, stabilize, ignore, absorb, distort, legitimate, or contest the critique. The analyst role has representational power over the artefact and must therefore observe strict boundary discipline.

Temporality Θ is central. Critique reach is not only a spatial or social expansion; it is a temporal transformation. A local objection may be momentary, but macro critique often accumulates across time: delayed response, institutional memory, public archive, repeated non-events, historical pattern recognition, future-oriented reform pressure, or slow accountability trajectories. Temporality also affects reversibility. Once critique becomes public or institutionalized, it may leave records, reputational traces, procedural consequences, or narrative lock-in.

The reversibility window is broad at the level of analysis but unknown at the level of real praxis. Analytically, the case remains reversible because it is abstract and bounded. In real public critique, reversibility may be low. A critique that circulates publicly may be difficult to retract or recontextualize; an institutional complaint may produce records; a systemic critique may attach local cases to broader patterns. Since no concrete scene is given, the case can only mark reversibility risk, not adjudicate it.

Exposure and asymmetry conditions are conceptually active but empirically under-specified. As critique moves from micro to macro, exposure may shift. The critic may gain audience power but also become vulnerable to backlash. The addressee may gain institutional defense or become publicly exposed. Institutions may hold procedural authority but also face legitimacy pressure. Publics may amplify critique without bearing responsibility for consequences. These are structural possibilities. They must not be converted into findings without evidence.

The absence of a concrete scene has direct consequences. No operator that depends on recurrence, non-response, self-binding, correction, or public drift can be claimed as an observed fact. Λ may be relevant, but no specific non-event is supplied. Α may be possible, but no repeated pattern is evidenced. Ψ may constrain responsible critique, but no durable follow-up is shown. Drift risks can be named only as risks. MIP can evaluate the analysis artefact, not persons. AHP can harden the artefact, not the case itself.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading treats critique reach as a scale-sensitive transformation of praxis. The recommended PMS formula from the YAML is:

Critique Reach = Φ-scale transition of critique across □-frames under Ω-asymmetry and Θ-temporality, requiring Χ-distance and possible Σ-integration, while preserving non-capture and withholding Ψ, drift, empirical, diagnostic, legal, implementation, and PMS-validation claims unless further scene material warrants them.

In expanded dependency order, the formula is:

Δ → ∇ → □micro → Λ? → Α? → Ω → Θ → Φ(□micro → □institutional/public/systemic) → Χ → Σ? → Ψ?

This sequence matters. The later operators cannot be used cheaply. Integration cannot be assumed because the analysis is coherent. Self-binding cannot be inferred because critique is intense, public, or repeated. Publicness cannot be treated as authority. Recontextualization cannot be allowed to absorb every rival framing. The reading remains strong only if each operator is kept within its warrant.

**Δ — Difference**

Δ is active. The case begins with differences: micro versus macro, interpersonal versus institutional, local versus public, immediate versus extended, situated critique versus systemic critique, correction versus exposure, frame-bound objection versus generalized structural claim. Critique itself presupposes a difference: something does not fit, something is contested, something is being challenged, something is being distinguished from what it is taken to be.

In this case, Δ makes visible the scale distinction. Without Δ, there would be no case: critique would remain one undifferentiated phenomenon. But Δ must be calibrated carefully. Micro and macro must not be treated as fixed binaries. A local scene may contain institutional power; a public critique may concern one concrete event. A micro scene may have high asymmetry; a macro critique may be diffuse and uncertain. Overclaim would occur if Δ were used to impose a rigid taxonomy: micro = personal, macro = structural; micro = reversible, macro = irreversible; micro = concrete, macro = abstract. These may be tendencies in some cases, but they are not warranted here.

The dependency risk is that Δ can be mistaken for diagnosis. Naming a difference between local and systemic critique does not show that one is defective or superior. It only gives the first structural contrast.

**∇ — Impulse**

∇ is weak-to-active. Critique implies directional pressure: objection, interruption, clarification, correction, exposure, resistance, or reframing. The case does not provide an actual impulse, but critique as praxis contains a directionality toward a mismatch. Something is not simply different; it is made into a point of challenge.

This operator must not be psychologized. ∇ does not mean anger, resentment, courage, purity, aggression, fragility, or moral seriousness. It is structural directedness. In a concrete case, ∇ might be observable in a complaint, challenge, question, protest, refusal, or demand. Here it remains conceptual. Overclaim would occur if the analysis inferred motive from critique: that macro critique is driven by resentment, or that local critique is more sincere, or that refusal to integrate reveals immaturity. None of that follows.

The dependency risk is Δ → ∇ inflation. Not every difference generates critique. A difference becomes critique only when directed pressure is structurally present. Since the material names critique, ∇ can be marked weak-to-active, but it cannot be filled with psychological content.

**□ — Frame**

□ is strongly active. The case is fundamentally about frame transformation. Critique changes as it moves from one frame to another. A local frame may include direct relational history, shared norms, immediate repair, or role expectations. An institutional frame may include procedure, complaint channels, authority, documentation, and rule-bound response. A public frame may include audience, visibility, circulation, reputational residue, and narrative contest. A systemic frame may include patterns, structures, repeatability, historical accumulation, and abstraction.

The frame decides what counts as critique, evidence, correction, addressee, response, and success. A statement that functions as a repair-oriented objection in a local conversation may function as public accusation in a mass context. A local grievance may become institutional evidence. A systemic critique may return to reinterpret local material. PMS Core therefore identifies □ not merely as background, but as the boundary that makes critique intelligible.

Calibration requires preserving both source and target frames. Macro framing must not simply erase local constraints. Local framing must not be used to deny broader patterns. A valid reading asks what changes under the new frame and what must remain bound to the original scene. Overclaim would occur if the analysis assumed that moving to a wider frame automatically clarifies the truth, or automatically distorts it. Both are possible. Neither is given.

The dependency risk is ∇ → □ collapse: critique pressure may outrun framing. Without □, critique becomes unbounded reaction or generalized judgment. With too narrow a □, critique is domesticated and its macro relevance is suppressed. With too wide a □, local specificity may disappear.

**Λ — Non-Event**

Λ is relevant but conditionally active. Critique often forms around absence: silence, non-response, ignored harm, missing uptake, absent repair, delayed acknowledgment, refusal of institutional answer, missing accountability, or a gap between expected and actual recognition. A local absence may become visible as a macro structure: not one ignored complaint, but a pattern of non-response.

However, the supplied case material does not specify any concrete absence. Therefore Λ cannot be strongly activated as a finding. It is a possible structural zone. A later concrete case would need to identify the expectation frame: what response was expected, by whom, under which norm, in what time window, and with what consequence when it did not occur. Without that, absence remains under-specified.

The calibration issue is crucial. Not every silence is a meaningful non-event. Not every refusal is avoidance. Not every missing integration is failure. Non-response may be a boundary, a refusal of an invalid frame, a protection against coercion, lack of authority, lack of evidence, or non-capture. Overclaim would occur if every absence were turned into Λ, and every Λ into drift, guilt, or maturity failure.

The dependency risk is □ → Λ overreading. Λ requires a frame of expectation. If the frame is not declared, the absence cannot be responsibly interpreted.

**Α — Attractor**

Α is conditionally relevant and weak. Critique can stabilize into patterns: complaint routines, public scripts, outrage cycles, silence attractors, institutional deferral, discourse repertoires, academic critique genres, or recurrent modes of systemic explanation. Macro critique may be more susceptible to attractor formation because it circulates, repeats, is archived, and can become a public script.

But no recurrence is supplied. There is no sequence showing repeated non-response, repeated critique, repeated public amplification, or institutional routine. Therefore Α remains possible but not established. It can be named as a risk area, not as an active finding.

The calibration issue is to distinguish recurrence from one-time scale movement. A single macro recontextualization is not an attractor. A repeated pattern may be an attractor, but the pattern must be evidenced. Overclaim would occur if the analysis inferred a public drift script merely because critique is macro or public.

The dependency risk is Λ → Α inflation. Repeated absence may become a silence attractor, but only if repeated absence is shown. A general philosophical case cannot supply that evidence.

**Ω — Asymmetry**

Ω is strongly active at the conceptual level. Critique reach changes the distribution of exposure, capacity, power, vulnerability, obligation, audience, and response. In micro contexts, asymmetry may involve dependency, intimacy, hierarchy, role authority, emotional exposure, or unequal capacity to respond. In macro contexts, asymmetry may involve institutions, publics, platforms, majorities, expert authority, documentation power, reputational residue, procedural capacity, or systemic invisibility.

The PMS Core reading is careful not to moralize Ω. Asymmetry is not automatically oppression, guilt, innocence, authority, or legitimacy. It is directional imbalance. Critique may challenge asymmetry, but it may also create new asymmetry. Public critique can expose a powerful institution, but it can also expose individuals or minor roles to disproportionate public judgment. Institutional critique can create procedural accountability, but it can also absorb critique into bureaucracy. Systemic critique can reveal patterns, but it can also reduce local agency.

Calibration requires asking who becomes visible, who becomes exposed, who can answer, who can impose a frame, who bears delayed costs, who has exit options, and who can contest the critique. Since no concrete case exists, these questions remain conceptual. Overclaim would occur if macro critique were assumed to be either speaking truth to power or mob exposure by default.

The dependency risk is Α/Λ → Ω inference without support. PMS dependency logic lists Ω after prior structures, but in this conceptual case Ω is warranted by the scale transition itself as a structural condition. Still, no specific asymmetry direction can be assigned.

**Θ — Temporality**

Θ is strongly active. Critique reach is temporal because its effects unfold across time. A local critique may occur in a short exchange; macro critique may accumulate, circulate, archive, return, harden, or be delayed. Institutional critique can involve review windows, complaint timelines, procedural memory, and delayed response. Public critique can create residue long after the initial event. Systemic critique may depend on historical patterns and future reform horizons.

Temporality also shapes responsibility and reversibility. If critique is immediate and local, correction may be possible in the same scene. If critique is public, correction may be delayed or fragmented. If critique becomes institutional record, reversal may require procedure. If critique becomes systemic narrative, local nuance may be difficult to recover.

The calibration issue is that temporality does not imply progress. Longer scale does not mean deeper truth. Historical framing can clarify patterns, but it can also project continuity where evidence is thin. Overclaim would occur if the analysis treated macro temporal extension as maturity, necessity, or historical inevitability.

The dependency risk is Ω → Θ overextension. Asymmetry across time can become responsibility-relevant, but duration alone does not establish responsibility. Temporal accumulation must be tied to frame, evidence, and role.

**Φ — Recontextualization**

Φ is central. The case is, above all, a recontextualization case. Critique moves from one frame to another. A local complaint becomes an institutional pattern. A local pattern becomes public critique. A public critique becomes systemic diagnosis. A systemic diagnosis returns to reinterpret a local scene. Each movement changes the meaning of the critique.

PMS Core treats Φ as both productive and risky. It may reveal hidden structures, connect local scenes to broader patterns, redistribute visibility, and create possibilities for correction. But it may also overgeneralize, flatten specificity, moralize ambiguity, politicize prematurely, replace evidence with abstraction, or absorb rival framings.

Calibration for Φ requires preserving the relation between source and target frames. What exactly is carried over? What is lost? What becomes visible only at macro scale? What was locally specific and should not be generalized? What new asymmetry is introduced by the recontextualization? A valid Φ-reading must distinguish scale extension from abstraction, generalization, institutionalization, publicity, and systemic formalization.

Overclaim would occur if every resistance or rival reading were redescribed as incomplete recontextualization. If a critique refuses available frames, that may be a genuine non-capture zone. If macro framing replaces micro evidence entirely, Φ is overclaimed. If local specificity is erased, the reading becomes assimilative.

The dependency risk is Θ/Ω/□ → Φ inflation. Because the case obviously involves frame shift, there is temptation to treat Φ as fully explanatory. But Φ only marks transformation; it does not guarantee adequacy.

**Χ — Distance**

Χ is strongly active as an application condition. Distance prevents critique reach from fusing with verdict, sanction, exposure, urgency, public shaming, person-ranking, or closure by authority. It is especially important in this case because macro critique tends to increase abstraction, audience size, temporal residue, and asymmetry risk.

Distance is not neutrality. It is not avoidance, passivity, false balance, or refusal of critique. It is the capacity to keep the analysis interruptible, revisable, and non-totalizing. Χ allows critique to remain critique rather than becoming a tribunal. It keeps macro critique from becoming authority merely by scale. It also prevents the analysis itself from becoming a closure machine.

Calibration requires maintaining reversibility and rival openness even where the PMS reading is coherent. The analysis must not use Χ to neutralize critique or protect status quo asymmetry. Χ disciplines reach; it does not deflate legitimate interruption. Overclaim would occur if distance became a demand that critique always soften itself, wait indefinitely, or integrate before speaking. That would convert Χ into suppression.

The dependency risk is Φ → Χ bypass. Recontextualization without distance can become verdict. A macro frame can feel more authoritative than a local one; Χ is what prevents that feeling from becoming premature closure.

**Σ — Integration**

Σ is active as a test, not as an established result. Macro critique requires integration if it is to become more than enlarged reaction or abstract commentary. It must coordinate local specificity, institutional patterning, asymmetry, temporality, evidence limits, publicness, rival readings, and non-capture. But the case does not show that such integration occurs.

Σ is therefore not merely coherence. It is a discrimination demand. It asks whether critique can hold multiple levels without collapsing them. Local evidence cannot be erased by systemic language. Systemic pattern cannot be denied by local exceptionalism. Public uptake cannot substitute for correction. Institutional procedure cannot substitute for truth. Integration must preserve tension rather than smoothing it.

Overclaim would occur if PMS coherence were treated as proof that integration has succeeded. The YAMLs repeatedly mark coherence risk as high. The PMS reading fits the case smoothly through □, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, and Σ. That smoothness is dangerous. It may under-discriminate between interpersonal critique, public accusation, systemic analysis, legal complaint, moral judgment, political rupture, and rhetorical performance.

The dependency risk is Χ → Σ optimism. Distance does not produce integration automatically. An analysis can remain distanced but suspended, evasive, or too abstract. Σ requires concrete coordination of levels, and here it remains a condition rather than a finding.

**Ψ — Self-Binding**

Ψ is weak and conditional. In PMS Core, Ψ would become relevant if critique becomes a durable commitment: sustained follow-up, revision discipline, role responsibility, non-coercive accountability, public self-binding, institutional ownership, or long-term corrective practice. In PMS-CRITIQUE, stable critique requires Ψ as non-coercive follow-up ownership. In MIP, responsibility would require role-bound knowledge, power, predictability, alternatives, and serious integration attempt.

The abstract case does not establish any of this. It does not identify a critic who binds themselves to revision. It does not identify an institution that commits to response. It does not show review windows, correction paths, or ownership. Therefore Ψ remains weak. It can be discussed as a condition of stable critique, not as an achieved structure.

Overclaim would occur if public commitment, intensity, repetition, or identity language were treated as Ψ. A public declaration is not necessarily self-binding. Repeated critique is not necessarily responsibility. A systemic claim is not necessarily follow-up. Ψ requires structured binding over time under Σ, Χ, Θ, and Ω.

The dependency risk is Σ → Ψ escalation. Even if a critique integrates multiple levels, it does not automatically become self-bound. Self-binding must be evidenced. In this case, it is withheld.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The PMS dependency discipline is central because this case tempts shortcutting. Critique reach creates strong pressure toward closure: if critique becomes macro, one may want to say it has become systemic; if it becomes public, one may want to say it has become legitimate or dangerous; if it reveals asymmetry, one may want to assign responsibility; if it is coherent, one may want to treat it as mature; if it resists integration, one may want to call it drift. PMS blocks these shortcuts.

Later operators cannot be used cheaply. Σ requires prior distance, recontextualization, temporality, and frame awareness. It cannot be inferred from a smooth explanation. Ψ requires integration, distance, temporality, and asymmetry. It cannot be inferred from publicness, repetition, seriousness, or moral language. Dignity-in-Practice requires asymmetry, distance, and self-binding as an application constraint; it cannot become ontological dignity theory or a dignity score. Drift patterns require structural evidence; they cannot be assigned because the case feels risky.

Σ remains conditional because the case lacks the concrete material needed to test integration. A real critique might integrate local scene, institutional pattern, publicness, temporality, evidence, and rival readings. But this abstract case only says critique moves from micro to macro. Therefore Σ is a requirement for responsible macro critique, not a completed finding.

Ψ remains conditional for an even stronger reason. No actor, role, institution, or practice is shown binding itself to follow-up. Stable critique cannot be claimed. PMS-CRITIQUE can say that CRITIQUE_STABLE would require Ψ, but the case does not reach it. MIP can say that responsibility is not assignable to a role-holder. AHP can harden that boundary. But no layer can fill the missing evidence.

Coherence is not proof. This is perhaps the most important calibration point in the case. The PMS reading works very well: micro → macro is naturally rendered through □, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, and Σ. PMS-CRITIQUE also fits well: critique thresholds, publicness amplification, drift risks, and follow-up conditions all map cleanly. MIP and AHP then produce strong artefact discipline. But formal coherence can become a trap. The more coherent the model, the greater the risk that it appears to have captured the phenomenon fully. The YAMLs explicitly mark this as coherence risk and under-discrimination risk.

Non-capture is not failure. Some critique may not aim at correction. Some critique may function as symbolic refusal, public rupture, ethical contestation, political antagonism, aesthetic intervention, rhetorical disruption, or refusal of available frames. PMS-CRITIQUE’s correction-oriented threshold ladder is useful, but it must not turn non-corrective critique into failed critique. A refusal to integrate may be a justified refusal of a bad frame. An absence may be self-protection. A rupture may be the point of critique. Non-capture must remain visible.

PMS must not become a closure machine. The aim is not to make critique safely formal by absorbing everything into Δ–Ψ. The aim is to map what can be mapped and protect what remains unresolved. PMS becomes invalid as an application if it suspends Χ, reversibility, or Dignity-in-Practice in order to force clarity. It also becomes invalid if it treats rival readings as mere incomplete PMS readings.

Several dependency shortcuts would be invalid. A Δ/∇ → Ψ shortcut would move from mismatch and critique pressure directly to binding responsibility or public verdict. A □ → Ω shortcut would treat frame position as moral asymmetry without evidence. A Λ → drift shortcut would treat absence or silence as failure. A Φ → Σ shortcut would treat recontextualization as integration. A Χ → neutrality shortcut would use distance to suppress critique. A Σ → validation shortcut would treat coherence as truth. A Ψ → person-label shortcut would convert self-binding or role responsibility into person type. All are prohibited.

The case’s structural strength depends on refusing these shortcuts. Its final PMS formula must remain bounded: Φ-scale transition across □-frames under Ω and Θ, constrained by Χ, tested for Σ, and withholding Ψ unless concrete self-binding is supplied.

### Derived structures

The derived structures are formula-derived, not primitive. They do not add new PMS operators. They are higher-order projections from operator compositions. The case uses them cautiously.

**Awareness_A**

Awareness_A is derived from Θ + □ + Δ. It is active as scale-awareness. The case distinguishes micro and macro frames, tracks temporal extension, and marks how critique changes when its frame changes. Awareness_A here is not consciousness, sentience, subjective insight, or moral awareness. It is structural awareness: framed differentiation across time.

In this case, Awareness_A is relatively strong at the artefact level. The PMS Core analysis notices frame movement, asymmetry, temporality, recontextualization, distance, integration risk, and non-capture. The MIP analysis later gives the artefact a provisional A-band of 4–5, but only for the analysis artefact and only as a working estimate. That band must not be transferred to people, critique forms, publics, institutions, or actual cases.

Awareness_A would be overclaimed if it were treated as empirical awareness by actual participants. No participant is supplied. It would also be overclaimed if macro awareness were treated as maturity. Seeing larger structures does not prove action, responsibility, truth, or integration.

**Coherence_C**

Coherence_C is derived from Θ + Λ + □ + ∇. It is weak-to-active. The case has directed critique pressure and frame structure across time. Λ is relevant because critique reach often concerns missing response, absent uptake, or non-recognition. But Λ is not concretely specified. Therefore Coherence_C cannot be fully active as a scene result.

At the artefact level, coherence is strong. The PMS Core, PMS-CRITIQUE, MIP, and AHP layers align. But the YAMLs repeatedly warn that coherence is not adequacy. A coherent reading can still under-discriminate, over-assimilate, or erase rival readings. Therefore Coherence_C is both a strength and a risk.

Coherence_C would be overclaimed if narrative smoothness were treated as truth. It would also be overclaimed if lack of coherence in a concrete critique were treated as immaturity. Some critique may preserve rupture for good reasons.

**Responsibility_R**

Responsibility_R is derived from Ψ + Φ + Θ + Ω. It is weak and conditional. The case clearly involves possible responsibility questions: if critique becomes macro, who owns its consequences? Who must respond? Who bears exposure? Who preserves reversibility? Who is bound to correction? But these questions cannot be answered without Ψ evidence.

The PMS Core YAML marks Responsibility_R as weak-to-active only if critique is self-bound to asymmetry across time and recontextualized obligations. PMS-CRITIQUE marks stable critique as requiring non-coercive follow-up ownership. MIP refuses to assign M-score to any person or role-holder because no concrete role-holder, power, predictability, alternatives, or integration attempt is supplied. This is the correct result.

Responsibility_R would be overclaimed if macro scale were treated as responsibility by itself. Public critique does not automatically create responsibility. Institutional critique does not automatically assign duty. Systemic critique does not automatically identify a responsible party. Responsibility requires role, capacity, time, asymmetry, and binding.

**Action_E**

Action_E is derived from Σ + Θ + ∇. It is under-specified. The case concerns critique reach, not concrete action. Critique may become action if it leads to correction, repair, publication, withdrawal, institutional change, policy revision, public response, or sustained practice. But no such enactment is supplied.

In PMS-CRITIQUE terms, productive critique would require a corrective step; stable critique would require follow-up binding. The case reaches only conceptual CRITIQUE_MIN, projects weakly toward CRITIQUE_PROD, and does not establish CRITIQUE_STABLE. Therefore Action_E remains under-specified.

Action_E would be overclaimed if critique-as-speech were treated as action in the strong PMS sense without integration and temporal enactment. It would also be overclaimed if lack of action were treated as failure. Non-action may reflect lack of authority, insufficient evidence, harm avoidance, or non-capture.

**Dignity_in_Practice_D**

Dignity_in_Practice_D is derived from Ψ + Χ + Ω. In this case it functions primarily as an application constraint, not as an ontological claim. It is not D0. It is not a dignity score. It does not rank persons. It does not identify dignity deficits. It constrains how critique is handled under asymmetry.

D is relevant because critique reach can create exposure. Macro critique may increase humiliation risk, public pillory risk, irreversible labeling, person-typing, degradation, and exposure-as-correction. PMS Core preserves D as a praxeological guardrail. PMS-CRITIQUE explicitly rejects publicness as correction and drift labels as moral rank. MIP activates D only as qualitative dignity-in-practice protection, not as person evaluation. AHP flags D-language as safe internally but extractable as dignity assessment if quoted without D0 and no-score boundaries.

D would be overclaimed if it became politeness, avoidance of critique, suppression of protest, or status quo protection. D does not prohibit critique. It prohibits degrading use of critique under asymmetry. It is also overclaimed if it becomes ontology. PMS does not prove D0; MIP preserves D0 as untouched.

**SELF_FIXPOINT**

SELF_FIXPOINT is derived from the full chain Ψ ∘ Σ ∘ Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ω ∘ Α ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ. It is not established in this case. The material does not describe a self, an agent continuity, an autobiographical structure, a stable institutional self-model, or a durable self-binding practice.

SELF_FIXPOINT may become relevant only if a concrete critic, institution, collective, or analysis practice binds itself over time through integrated, distanced, recontextualized praxis under asymmetry. Here the only self-binding-like structure is the analysis artefact’s own discipline: it binds itself to not overclaim, not diagnose, not rank, not validate, not implement, and not convert drift into verdict. But that is an analytic discipline, not a full SELF_FIXPOINT claim.

SELF_FIXPOINT would be overclaimed if critique itself were personified, if a public were treated as a self without warrant, or if an institution were assigned selfhood merely because it has procedures. The YAMLs correctly treat it as not applicable or analytic-only.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS Core analysis treats drift patterns as conditional structural risks, not as findings. This distinction is essential. A drift pattern is not a diagnosis, not a person type, not a moral verdict, not a maturity score, and not a claim that critique has failed. It is a structural warning about how a configuration may mutate under pressure if later scene material supplies the relevant evidence.

The two PMS drift-patterns explicitly considered are **AD_A>>E** and **AD_Sigma_low**. Both are possible in critique reach cases, but neither is established by the abstract material.

**AD_A>>E — Excessive distance between Awareness and Enactment**

AD_A>>E names a possible structural gap between expanded awareness and suppressed or chronically delayed action. In this case, the risk is intelligible because critique can expand from micro to macro scale without producing correction, repair, follow-up, or integrated enactment. Macro critique may generate more visibility, more abstraction, more systemic vocabulary, more public attention, or more evaluative framing while leaving Action_E under-specified.

This risk is especially visible where critique becomes highly articulate but does not connect to feasible correction. A local mismatch may be recontextualized into a systemic pattern; the systemic pattern may be narrated coherently; publicness may amplify the critique; but no corrective pathway appears. In PMS terms, Awareness_A may expand while Action_E remains unformed. In PMS-CRITIQUE terms, critique may reach conceptual CRITIQUE_MIN but fail to consolidate into CRITIQUE_PROD or CRITIQUE_STABLE.

However, the case does not establish AD_A>>E. The YAML correctly marks the axis effects as under-specified. No concrete sequence shows awareness expanding while enactment is suppressed. No critic, institution, or public is shown refusing action. No correction opportunity is supplied. No review window is missed. No follow-up is delayed. Therefore AD_A>>E remains a risk condition only.

The misuse boundary is important: non-action is not automatically drift. Delay may be appropriate when evidence is insufficient. Non-enactment may protect against harm. Refusal to act may preserve non-capture. A critique may be symbolic, ethical, rhetorical, or disruptive rather than correction-oriented. PMS must not translate every gap between awareness and action into immaturity, evasion, or failed praxis.

**AD_Sigma_low — Fragmented integration**

AD_Sigma_low names a possible under-integration pattern. In this case, the risk is visible because critique reach involves multiple frames: local, institutional, public, systemic. If critique shifts rapidly among these frames without integrating their different constraints, it may become volatile. A local example may be made to stand for a system without adequate support. A systemic critique may erase the local scene. Public visibility may replace evidence. Institutional procedure may absorb the critique without addressing its structural point. Rival readings may be mentioned but not integrated.

The PMS Core analysis therefore marks AD_Sigma_low as possible but not confirmed. The relevant axis effects are under-specified: coherence_axis is under-specified, responsibility_axis is under-specified. No concrete evidence shows volatile frame shifts or inconsistent responsibility. The abstract case only says critique moves from micro to macro. It does not show whether the movement is careful or fragmented.

The calibration is again strict. Weak or withheld Σ is not failure by itself. A case may refuse integration because integration would be premature. A critique may preserve unresolved tension because the object is genuinely not yet integrable. Rival framing may remain outside PMS capture. A rupture may be structurally meaningful precisely because it resists smooth synthesis. The analysis must not convert non-integration into defect.

The broader coherence risk is high. PMS can map the case very smoothly. That smoothness can hide under-discrimination. The same formula could apply to interpersonal feedback, public accusation, institutional complaint, political critique, legal reporting, rhetorical refusal, or systemic theory unless additional scene details are supplied. The YAML therefore treats coherence as a risk as well as a strength.

The central drift conclusion is:

Risk is not finding.
Under-specification is not failure.
Non-assignment is not low score.
Non-capture is not immaturity.
PMS coherence is not proof.

### Add-on / overlay reading

The relevant add-on is **PMS-CRITIQUE**. It is used as an overlay/application profile only. PMS.yaml remains the only PMS Base reference. PMS-CRITIQUE does not redefine PMS operators, does not alter PMS dependencies, does not introduce new PMS base operators, and does not convert its reduced signatures into replacement definitions.

PMS Core alone is not the final layer because the case is not merely a generic frame transition. It is specifically about critique reach. PMS Core identifies the scale-sensitive operator structure: Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, Ψ. But it does not isolate critique-specific threshold questions: when does mismatch become critique? When does critique become productive? When does critique become stable? When does publicness amplify exposure? When does critique drift into judgment, commentary, narrative repair, public pillory, silence, diffusion, integration bypass, or commitment evasion? PMS-CRITIQUE supplies those overlay handles.

What PMS-CRITIQUE makes visible is not a new grammar, but a critique-facing readability layer. It asks whether a configuration reaches **CRITIQUE_MIN**, **CRITIQUE_PROD**, or **CRITIQUE_STABLE**. It identifies **publicness P** as a non-operator overlay modulator. It supplies a drift typology for critique mutation under load. It introduces modulators such as frame clarity, non-event density, asymmetry visibility, temporal compression, recontextualization habituation, distance availability, attractor inertia, and publicness overlay. These are not PMS operators. They organize existing PMS operators for critique-specific analysis.

The add-on relationship to Core is subordinate. The completed PMS Core case remains controlling. PMS-CRITIQUE adds visibility for threshold classification, drift sensitivity, scale/publicness modulation, critique-facing derived-axis projections, optional MIP docking, and misuse guardrails. It does not rewrite the Core pass. It does not upgrade weak PMS operators into strong findings. It does not turn possible drift into active drift. It does not make critique legitimate, true, mature, dangerous, or successful.

#### CRITIQUE_MIN

CRITIQUE_MIN is the minimal critique threshold. Its reduced signature is **Δ + □ + Χ**. This is reduced shorthand only. It does not mean that Χ loses its PMS dependencies. Within PMS dependency discipline, Χ presupposes Φ, Θ, and Ω. The reduced signature marks critique-level interruptibility: a mismatch becomes framed and interruptible.

In this case, CRITIQUE_MIN is active at the conceptual-overlay level. Δ is supplied by the mismatch between micro and macro critique reach. □ is supplied by the frame movement from interpersonal/local settings toward institutional/public/systemic contexts. Χ is supplied by the analysis’s maintained distance: the case remains interruptible, reversible, non-tribunal, non-person-ranking, and non-coercive.

This does not establish a concrete critique episode. It does not show that any actual critique is valid, justified, successful, legitimate, or corrective. It only says that at the conceptual level, the case can be read as a minimal critique configuration because mismatch, frame, and interruptibility are present.

CRITIQUE_MIN is therefore not truth. It is not legitimacy. It is not moral superiority. It is not empirical success. It is not stable correction. It is the lowest threshold at which a mismatch is framed and held at enough distance to be reviewed rather than discharged as mere reaction.

#### CRITIQUE_PROD

CRITIQUE_PROD is productive critique. Its reduced signature is **Δ + □ + Χ + Φ + Σ**. It marks the point where framed interruption is recontextualized and integrated into a corrective step. Again, this is overlay shorthand, not PMS Base revision.

In this case, CRITIQUE_PROD is weak. Φ is central because the case is about micro-to-macro recontextualization. Σ is present as a required integration test. But no concrete corrective step is supplied. The case does not show that macro critique improves, redirects, coordinates, repairs, or consolidates anything. It does not show a response, policy change, institutional correction, revised frame, or agreed follow-up.

The case therefore projects toward CRITIQUE_PROD without establishing it. It shows why productive critique would require more than scale expansion. Macro vocabulary is not correction. Publicness is not correction. Systemic framing is not correction. Integration would require preserving local specificity, macro patterning, asymmetry, temporality, evidence limits, rival readings, and non-capture while producing an actionable correction path. That is not shown.

CRITIQUE_PROD is not implementation success. It is not empirical validation. It is not evidence that critique worked. It is only a possible threshold if Φ and Σ are concretely fulfilled.

#### CRITIQUE_STABLE

CRITIQUE_STABLE is stable critique. Its reduced signature is **Δ + □ + Χ + Φ + Σ + Ψ**. It requires non-coercive follow-up binding. Ψ remains PMS Self-Binding and must not be converted into coercive binding of others.

In this case, CRITIQUE_STABLE is not established. Ψ is weak. No durable follow-up is supplied. There is no actor continuity, no ownership structure, no review window, no correction practice, no institutional commitment, no self-bound revision discipline, and no stable non-coercive responsibility. Therefore stable critique remains a withheld claim.

This is not a failure. An abstract conceptual case cannot be expected to supply stable critique. The absence of CRITIQUE_STABLE is not immaturity. It is a correct boundary marking. Stable critique would require self-bound follow-up without coercion, ownership without person-ranking, revisability, dignity-in-practice, temporal review conditions, and preservation of non-capture.

#### Publicness P

P is an overlay construct, not a PMS operator. It amplifies Ω, Θ, and Α. Publicness may increase exposure, temporal residue, audience sensitivity, irreversibility, and script stabilization. It can intensify critique’s drift sensitivity. But P does not create critique, does not correct, does not legitimate, does not validate, and does not add a new operator.

In this case, P is weak-to-relevant. The case concerns movement toward institutional, public, or systemic contexts, so publicness is structurally relevant. But no concrete public episode is supplied. Therefore P is best treated as a scale modulator. It explains why macro critique may become more irreversible, audience-sensitive, and exposure-sensitive without becoming more truthful, mature, justified, corrective, or legitimate.

#### PMS-CRITIQUE drift risks

The CRITIQUE overlay identifies several drift risks. None is asserted as an active finding.

**D1 Reaction masquerading as critique** is not applicable. Reaction drift would involve Δ + ∇ with Χ absent: impulse discharge without interruptibility. Here Χ is maintained by the analysis, and no concrete reaction scene is supplied.

**D2 Judgment masquerading as critique** is a risk. Macro critique can become evaluative labeling if Φ and Σ fail to produce correction. Public or institutional frames may stabilize clarity at the cost of actionability. But no active judgment drift is shown.

**D3 Narrative repair masquerading as critique** is a risk. Since Φ is central, recontextualization could become the endpoint: explanation expands while correction remains unreachable. The case keeps this as a risk because Σ is not guaranteed.

**D4 Commentary / analysis paralysis** is a risk. Χ can suspend immediate closure, but if Σ never consolidates, meta-commentary may replace correction. This is especially relevant because the present artefact is highly formal and reflective. But again, this is an artefact-level risk, not a finding about critique.

**D5 Public pillory** is a risk under P. If publicness amplifies Ω, Θ, and Α while restraint collapses, exposure can replace correction. Visibility becomes sanction. The case explicitly rejects exposure-as-correction. No active public pillory is shown.

**D6 Silence attractor** is a weak risk. If Λ persisted and stabilized into Α under Ω/Θ, non-response could become a default script. But Λ and Α are under-specified here. No silence script is established.

The compact catalogue adds related risks: moralization drift, narrative reset drift, commentary drift, silence as steering, diffusion drift, public pillory, integration bypass, and commitment evasion. All remain risk-level. They are useful because they show how critique can mutate when scale increases. They must not become accusations.

#### Modulators

The CRITIQUE overlay’s modulators are also descriptive. Frame clarity is active because the case depends on distinguishing micro/local from macro/institutional/public/systemic frames. Asymmetry visibility is active conceptually because critique reach changes exposure and obligation. Recontextualization habituation is active as a calibration risk because Φ may become bridge or substitute. Distance availability is active because the analysis maintains Χ.

Non-event density is weak because Λ is under-specified. Temporal compression is weak because Θ is active but no specific urgency or review window is supplied. Attractor inertia is weak because Α is not evidenced. Publicness overlay is weak-to-relevant because macro critique may amplify Ω, Θ, and Α, but no concrete public episode is given.

#### MIP docking

PMS-CRITIQUE includes optional MIP docking, but the docking is not triggered by the CRITIQUE layer itself. It becomes relevant only because the user requested MIP analysis and because the case has publicness, D-language, score-hygiene, transmission, and artefact-hardening relevance. PMS-CRITIQUE remains structurally complete without MIP. MIP does not retroactively evaluate the truth of the critique. It inspects the analysis artefact and its guardrails.

The final CRITIQUE overlay formula is:

CRITIQUE_MIN active; CRITIQUE_PROD weak; CRITIQUE_STABLE not established; drift risks constrained; horizontal scale mixed with weak P overlay.

This is the right level of closure. It says critique is conceptually interruptible, may become productive if Φ and Σ are fulfilled, and would become stable only if Ψ is supplied. It refuses to claim more.

### Layer interaction

The layer interaction is strict and non-mixing.

PMS Core remains the operator grammar. All structural claims about Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, and Ψ belong to PMS Core. Operator order, dependency logic, derived structures, entry conditions, and application discipline remain governed by PMS.yaml. PMS Core supplies the deep grammar of critique reach: difference, directed pressure, frame, possible non-event, possible attractor, asymmetry, temporality, recontextualization, distance, integration, and possible self-binding.

PMS-CRITIQUE sharpens the reading by adding critique-facing overlay handles. It names minimal critique, productive critique, stable critique, vertical reach levels, horizontal scale tags, publicness P, drift classes, modulators, and optional MIP docking. These constructs help render critique reach more discriminatingly. They show that the case is not merely scale transition but critique threshold transition.

What the add-on must not do is redefine PMS. CRITIQUE_MIN’s Δ + □ + Χ must not be mistaken for a new dependency path. Publicness P must not become an operator. Drift classes must not become PMS-derived diagnoses. CRITIQUE_STABLE must not convert Ψ into coercive binding of others. CRITIQUE_PROD must not treat recontextualization as correction by itself.

Overlay language could overreach in several ways. It could make correction the implicit telos of all critique, thereby misreading critique as always needing to become productive. It could make publicness seem inherently dangerous, thereby deflating public critique. It could classify symbolic refusal as failed integration. It could treat CRITIQUE_STABLE as a maturity ideal and then read its absence as low maturity. It could treat drift risk as finding. The YAMLs block these moves by repeatedly marking risks as risks and preserving non-capture.

MIP adds a different layer. It does not refine PMS operators or CRITIQUE signatures. It asks what maturity-in-practice conditions are visible in the completed analysis artefact. It inspects awareness, coherence, responsibility, power-to-act, dignity-in-practice, IA risk, trajectory, bias, transmission, and practical limits. MIP’s object is the artefact, not the underlying real-world critique. It therefore strengthens guardrail discipline without turning the case into an applied evaluation.

The MIP result is deliberately non-escalatory. The analysis artefact shows relatively strong awareness and coherence because it keeps scale, frame, publicness, asymmetry, non-capture, score hygiene, and transmission limits visible. But this does not become a maturity finding about critique itself. A provisional A-band belongs only to the analysis artefact. M-score remains unassignable because there is no concrete role-holder with known power, predictability, alternatives, or integration attempt. IA remains a risk check rather than a verdict because publicness, reversibility, purpose, protected goods, and role evidence are not concretely supplied. D remains qualitative dignity-in-practice protection: it guards against humiliation, exposure-as-correction, public pillory, and dignity-language misuse without becoming a dignity score.

AHP then adds second-order hardening. It does not add another substantive case reading. It checks the prior MIP output for precision, attack surface, and hardening backlog. It identifies that the MIP output is high-precision as an internal working note, but vulnerable to misreadings: formal completeness could be mistaken for evidentiary completeness; A-band notation could be misread as case maturity; IA-risk could be misread as IA finding; D-language could be extracted as dignity assessment; transmission boundaries could be lost in reuse. AHP therefore strengthens the analysis by making these risks explicit, not by changing any first-order result.

The interaction across layers is therefore cumulative but not escalatory. PMS Core maps. PMS-CRITIQUE sharpens critique-specific visibility. MIP checks maturity-in-practice and downstream misuse risk. AHP hardens the analysis artefact. None of the later layers authorizes stronger first-order claims. If anything, each layer adds restraint.

Non-closure remains visible at every level. PMS leaves Λ and non-capture. PMS-CRITIQUE leaves CRITIQUE_PROD weak and CRITIQUE_STABLE unestablished. MIP leaves M-score unassignable and IA only risk-level. AHP leaves transmission status unchanged and warns that full-depth structure does not increase evidence.

### Example 1 — From local objection to institutional pattern

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** after **Scene and frame structure**, before **PMS Core structural reading**

A small seminar group has developed a habit: whenever one participant raises methodological concerns, the group acknowledges the point briefly and then returns to the original discussion. In one session, the participant says, “I think this keeps happening: the concern is recognized, but it never changes how the work proceeds.” At the micro level, this can be heard as a local objection about conversational uptake. It concerns a specific room, a specific practice, and a small set of participants who can respond directly.

The structure changes if the same objection is moved into an institutional frame. Suppose the participant later writes to the department that the seminar’s review process repeatedly records objections without creating any pathway for revision. The critique is no longer only about one exchange. It now concerns a procedure: how concerns are received, documented, acted on, or allowed to disappear. If the case is then discussed publicly as an example of how academic settings absorb critique without changing practice, the reach changes again. The critique becomes public, more generalized, and more exposed to audience effects.

This example demonstrates why critique reach cannot be treated as simple enlargement. The local objection is not identical to the institutional critique, and the institutional critique is not identical to the public critique. Each frame changes the relevant expectations. In the seminar, the expected response may be conversational adjustment. In the department, the expected response may be procedural review. In public, the expected response may become reputational accountability, symbolic recognition, or wider reform. The same concern may remain traceable, but it is not structurally unchanged.

The PMS Core hooks are clear. **Δ** appears in the mismatch between acknowledgment and actual change. **∇** appears as the directed pressure to interrupt the pattern. **□** changes as the critique moves from seminar conversation to institutional procedure to public discussion. **Λ** may be relevant if the repeated “acknowledgment without follow-up” is treated as a structured non-event, but it would need evidence: how often did it occur, what response was expected, and under which frame? **Α** may become relevant if the pattern stabilizes into a recognizable routine: concerns are heard, logged, and then neutralized. But again, recurrence must be shown, not assumed.

The major operators become more visible at wider scale. **Ω** changes because the participant may be vulnerable in the seminar, the department may hold procedural authority, and a public discussion may expose both the institution and specific participants. **Θ** matters because the concern is not only a momentary interruption; it accumulates through repetition and delayed non-response. **Φ** is central because the objection is recontextualized from a conversational scene into an institutional pattern and then possibly into a public critique. **Χ** is required so the analysis does not immediately turn the seminar group into culprits, the participant into a mature critic, or the institution into a verdictive object. **Σ** remains conditional: the critique would become more integrated only if it coordinated the local scene, procedural frame, repetition evidence, possible institutional constraints, and rival accounts. **Ψ** is not established unless some role-holder binds themselves to follow-up, review, or correction.

PMS-CRITIQUE sharpens the example. At the local level, the objection may reach **CRITIQUE_MIN** if the mismatch is framed and interruptible. It becomes weakly oriented toward **CRITIQUE_PROD** only if the recontextualization produces an actual corrective pathway, such as a revised review protocol or a clear response mechanism. It does not reach **CRITIQUE_STABLE** unless there is non-coercive follow-up over time: review dates, ownership, revisability, and protection against retaliation or humiliation. Publicness **P** is relevant only if the matter circulates beyond the institution; then it amplifies exposure and residue, but it does not automatically make the critique more true or more corrective.

The MIP and AHP status remains guarded. No A-score, M-score, or D reading should be assigned to any participant. The example could support a future MIP analysis only if concrete roles, knowledge, power-to-act, alternatives, predictability, and follow-up conditions were supplied. AHP would warn that a polished description of “institutional absorption” can look more evidenced than it is. The phrase may be analytically useful, but it requires documentation before it becomes a finding.

The forbidden claims are therefore explicit. This example does not prove that the seminar group acted wrongly. It does not prove that the institution silenced critique. It does not diagnose anyone as defensive, avoidant, immature, or oppressive. It does not establish a legal or procedural breach. It does not validate PMS or PMS-CRITIQUE. It does not show that public critique is justified, only that publicness changes the structure.

The non-capture note is equally important. The participant’s objection may not aim at correction in the PMS-CRITIQUE sense. It may be a refusal to continue under a frame that repeatedly absorbs objections. It may be an ethical protest, a demand for recognition, or a marker of exhaustion. If so, failure to reach CRITIQUE_PROD or CRITIQUE_STABLE is not automatically a defect. The critique may preserve rupture because the available correction pathway is itself part of the problem.

The article takeaway is that critique reach changes the scene. A local objection can become an institutional critique only through recontextualization, and that recontextualization requires distance, evidence discipline, integration, and dignity-in-practice. Without those, macro reach may clarify a pattern, but it may also overgeneralize a local scene.

### Example 2 — Public exposure is not correction

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** inside **Add-on / overlay reading**, after the discussion of **Publicness P** and before **PMS-CRITIQUE drift risks**

A member of a small organization notices that meeting summaries repeatedly omit dissenting comments. Initially, the concern is raised internally: “The minutes make it look as if there was agreement, but several objections were made.” This is a local critique about documentation. The relevant frame is narrow: meeting procedure, record accuracy, and the possibility of correction. A simple correction may be available: amend the minutes, add a dissent note, or clarify the record.

Now imagine that the concern is posted publicly: “This organization systematically erases dissent.” The critique may be understandable, especially if internal correction has repeatedly failed. But the public form is structurally different from the internal objection. It changes the audience, raises reputational stakes, increases temporal residue, and may expose people who cannot easily answer in the same medium. Publicness may be necessary in some cases, but it is not equivalent to correction. The act of making a pattern visible does not by itself integrate the local evidence, institutional procedure, role obligations, and possible response path.

This example demonstrates the difference between critique reach and critique completion. The public statement may reach more people, but reach is not the same as integration. Exposure may create pressure, but pressure is not the same as correction. A public audience may amplify a critique, but amplification is not proof. A wider frame may reveal a real pattern, but it may also compress distinctions among negligence, error, procedural habit, disagreement about documentation norms, or deliberate suppression.

PMS Core begins with **Δ**: there is a mismatch between actual dissent and the official record. **∇** appears as pressure to object. **□** first frames the issue as documentation accuracy. If objections have repeatedly disappeared from the minutes despite prior requests, **Λ** may become active as structured omission, but only if the expectation and recurrence are specified. **Α** may become relevant if omission stabilizes as a pattern. **Ω** appears because whoever controls the record has disproportionate power over institutional memory. **Θ** matters because minutes persist; they become the archive through which later readers understand what happened. **Φ** appears when the local documentation issue is recontextualized as an organizational or public critique. **Χ** is necessary to prevent the public statement from becoming a verdict before the pattern is evidenced. **Σ** would require coordinating the meeting record, prior objections, procedural standards, the organization’s response capacity, and the risks of public exposure. **Ψ** would require follow-up: who owns correction, what changes, and how future minutes are reviewed.

PMS-CRITIQUE makes the publicness problem especially visible. The internal objection may satisfy **CRITIQUE_MIN** if the mismatch is framed and interruptible. It may approach **CRITIQUE_PROD** if the record is corrected and the procedure is adjusted. But the public statement does not automatically become productive critique. It may widen the frame through **Φ**, but without **Σ** it can become a public label rather than correction. It reaches a broader audience, yet the correction path may become less clear.

Publicness **P** is not a PMS operator. It amplifies **Ω**, **Θ**, and **Α**. It may increase exposure, harden residue, and stabilize a public script: “this organization erases dissent.” That script may be warranted in a concrete case, but it is not warranted merely by the form of the statement. The PMS-CRITIQUE drift risks are therefore clear but not findings. **Judgment drift** is possible if the public label replaces a correction pathway. **Narrative repair drift** is possible if a broader explanation becomes the endpoint. **Public pillory** is possible if exposure becomes punishment. **Integration bypass** is possible if the critique moves from local evidence to public conclusion without preserving intermediate frames. None of these is active without more evidence.

The MIP status is guardrail-bound. No M-score can be assigned to the person posting publicly, the person keeping minutes, or the organization. A future MIP analysis would need to know who controlled the record, what they knew, what alternatives were available, how predictable the consequences were, whether prior correction was attempted, and whether public exposure was proportionate or avoidable. Dignity-in-Practice would be relevant because public critique can expose persons or roles. But D must not be used to suppress legitimate critique. The issue is not whether public criticism is impolite; the issue is whether exposure replaces correction, whether voice and reversibility remain possible, and whether persons are degraded rather than roles or procedures being challenged.

AHP would flag a likely analysis-artifact risk: a tidy phrase such as “public exposure is not correction” can be misread as a rule against public critique. That would be wrong. Some critique must become public because internal frames block correction, because affected parties lack safe response channels, or because the relevant pattern is invisible within the local frame. The point is therefore not to demote public critique. The point is to prevent publicness from being mistaken for completion. Public exposure may be necessary, but it does not by itself satisfy correction, integration, reversibility, proportionality, or self-binding.

The forbidden claims must remain visible. This example does not prove that the organization erases dissent. It does not prove that the public post is unjustified. It does not prove that internal handling was adequate. It does not diagnose anyone as manipulative, cowardly, punitive, or immature. It does not make a legal claim about defamation, liability, or due process. It does not produce a governance recommendation.

The non-capture note is important. A public statement may be intended not as correction but as testimony, warning, refusal, or solidarity with others who experienced the same omission. PMS-CRITIQUE can describe the correction threshold, but not all critique is correction-oriented. If the available institutional frame has repeatedly neutralized critique, public rupture may preserve something that procedural integration would erase. That possibility must remain open.

The article takeaway is that macro reach increases structural load. Publicness can make a critique visible, but it also changes asymmetry, temporality, exposure, and reversibility. The question is not whether public critique is allowed; the question is what its reach changes and what claims remain unwarranted without integration.

### Example 3 — Systemic framing and the risk of erasing the local scene

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** inside **PMS dependency and calibration discipline** or **Non-capture and rival readings**

A workplace team has a recurring conflict about task allocation. One member says that undesirable tasks keep being assigned to the same few people. In the immediate scene, the issue can be framed as a local coordination problem: who is assigning tasks, what criteria are used, whether the pattern is visible, and whether it can be corrected. A local response might involve reviewing recent assignments, clarifying responsibilities, and changing the distribution.

The critique becomes more complex when the member frames the pattern as part of a wider systemic problem: “This is how invisible labor gets reproduced.” That recontextualization may be important. It can connect a local pattern to broader structures of recognition, role expectation, gendered or status-based labor, institutional habit, or normalized unfairness. The systemic frame may reveal something the local frame cannot see. But it may also risk erasing the local scene if it moves too quickly. The people assigning tasks may have acted under constraints, incomplete information, rotating emergencies, or unclear procedures. The systemic frame may be illuminating, but it does not automatically settle the local case.

This example demonstrates why **Φ** is central and why **Σ** cannot be assumed. Recontextualization may be necessary for critique to reach its object. If the problem is genuinely systemic, staying local may domesticate the critique and protect the pattern. Yet if the systemic frame replaces the local scene entirely, it can over-assimilate the evidence. The challenge is to preserve both: the local sequence and the macro pattern, the immediate correction and the structural diagnosis, the role-specific responsibility and the wider context.

The PMS Core reading begins with **Δ**: there is a mismatch in task distribution. **∇** appears as pressure to object. **□** first frames the issue as team coordination, then as systemic reproduction of invisible labor. **Λ** may be relevant if previous concerns were ignored or if invisible labor is structured by non-recognition, but this must be shown. **Α** may be relevant if task allocation repeatedly stabilizes around the same people. **Ω** is central because task distribution may reflect unequal authority, status, visibility, or capacity to refuse. **Θ** matters because a single assignment may not show much, but recurrence over time can create burden and expectation. **Φ** recontextualizes the local pattern as systemic. **Χ** is required so the systemic frame does not become immediate verdict. **Σ** would require integrating assignment records, role constraints, repeated patterns, affected perspectives, possible systemic literature or institutional norms, and counter-readings. **Ψ** would require follow-up ownership: who changes the allocation process, how it is reviewed, and how future invisibility is prevented.

The dependency discipline matters because this example tempts shortcuts. One invalid shortcut would be **Δ → systemic verdict**: unequal task distribution is noticed, therefore the team is reproducing structural injustice. Another shortcut would be **local frame → dismissal**: because the issue can be explained locally, the systemic critique is overblown. Both shortcuts fail. PMS requires the analysis to hold the transition open. A local mismatch can be systemically meaningful, but only through calibrated recontextualization and integration.

PMS-CRITIQUE adds threshold clarity. The initial objection may reach **CRITIQUE_MIN** if the mismatch is framed and interruptible. It may become **CRITIQUE_PROD** if the team reviews the pattern and changes the allocation process while preserving the systemic insight. It does not become **CRITIQUE_STABLE** unless the team or institution binds itself to non-coercive follow-up: review cycles, transparent criteria, revisability, and protection against retaliation or humiliation.

The drift risks are again risks, not findings. **Narrative repair drift** is possible if systemic explanation becomes the endpoint and no allocation process changes. **Judgment drift** is possible if the team or assigner is labeled without a correction path. **Integration bypass** is possible if the critique moves from local pattern to systemic conclusion without preserving evidence. **Commitment evasion** is possible if the team agrees with the systemic language but never changes the practice. Yet none of these can be claimed from the vignette alone.

MIP would treat this as requiring careful role-context analysis. If a concrete case were supplied, MIP would ask who knew about the distribution, who had power to change it, how predictable the burden was, what alternatives existed, and whether serious integration attempts were made. Without such data, M-score cannot be assigned. Dignity-in-Practice would matter because both affected members and accused role-holders can be exposed. The aim would be to protect against degradation while not neutralizing legitimate critique.

AHP would warn that systemic language can create false precision. “Invisible labor” may be a powerful and appropriate concept, but in a concrete analysis it must be linked to the scene rather than used as an automatic conclusion. AHP would also warn against the opposite misuse: demanding perfect evidence before allowing any systemic framing can protect the very pattern being criticized. The hardening requirement is to state what evidence would strengthen the systemic reading, what would weaken it, and what rival readings remain possible.

The forbidden claims are clear. This example does not prove discrimination, exploitation, bad faith, legal wrongdoing, or moral failure. It does not diagnose the assigner or the affected worker. It does not rank the team’s maturity. It does not validate systemic theory or PMS. It does not prescribe a workplace policy. It only illustrates the structural problem of moving from a local distribution complaint to a systemic critique.

The non-capture note is decisive. Some systemic critique works by naming what local procedures cannot recognize. If the team demands that the critique remain local, it may erase the very structure at issue. Conversely, some local complexity may resist systemic capture. PMS must not decide in advance which is true. It must keep the recontextualization open, preserve the local scene, and require integration before stronger claims are made.

The article takeaway is that systemic framing is neither automatic overreach nor automatic maturity. It is a powerful Φ-move whose adequacy depends on Ω, Θ, Χ, and Σ. It can reveal structure, but it can also erase local constraints. The case’s discipline lies in refusing both premature closure and premature dismissal.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The case preserves substantial non-capture zones. These are not defects. They are necessary because critique is a rich phenomenon that may exceed PMS and PMS-CRITIQUE.

An **epistemological rival reading** could treat critique reach as epistemic expansion. On this view, moving from micro to macro allows critique to know more: local cases are reinterpreted as evidence of patterns, institutions, or systems. PMS can model this through Φ, Θ, Ω, and Σ, but it does not decide whether the expansion is epistemically valid. Epistemology may ask about evidence, justification, standpoint, inference, underdetermination, and warrant in ways that PMS-CRITIQUE does not exhaust.

A **phenomenological rival reading** could focus on how critique feels or is experienced: the experience of being criticized, the vulnerability of speaking publicly, the affective transformation from private objection to public exposure, or the lived difference between local misunderstanding and systemic invisibility. PMS explicitly does not claim phenomenological access. Its structural mapping may miss affective intensity, shame, courage, fear, recognition, alienation, or solidarity as lived phenomena.

A **language-practice rival reading** could treat critique as a practice within forms of life, discourse games, genre conventions, or institutional speech acts. It may ask how the word “critique” functions in academic, political, legal, activist, relational, or organizational contexts. PMS maps frame and recontextualization, but it does not replace a theory of language games, speech acts, hermeneutics, discourse norms, or rhetoric.

An **ethical rival reading** could focus on the norms of critique: fairness, respect, truthfulness, courage, care, responsibility, justice, harm, solidarity, or accountability. PMS and MIP preserve dignity-in-practice constraints, but they do not derive a full ethical theory. The case does not decide when critique is morally required, when public exposure is justified, or when refusing politeness is ethically necessary.

A **political rival reading** could treat critique reach as a question of power, domination, public sphere, legitimacy, ideology, hegemony, resistance, representation, or structural injustice. PMS Core can map Ω and publicness, but it does not replace political theory. Some critiques must become public precisely because local frames suppress them. A too-strong distance requirement could protect power. This rival reading must remain open.

A **genealogical or ideology-critical rival reading** could argue that critique must expose the conditions under which local experience is already structured by macro power. From that angle, the micro/macro distinction may itself be unstable. PMS can model recontextualization, but it may understate how macro structures inhabit micro scenes from the beginning.

An **anti-capture rival reading** is especially important. Some critique may function by refusing the available grammar. It may not aim at correction, integration, or stable follow-up. It may aim to interrupt a field, expose impossibility, mark injury, refuse recognition on imposed terms, or preserve rupture. PMS-CRITIQUE’s threshold ladder must not treat such critique as failed CRITIQUE_PROD or failed CRITIQUE_STABLE. Non-capture may be the structural result.

A **rhetorical or aesthetic rival reading** could emphasize form, tone, irony, genre, repetition, exaggeration, satire, visibility, timing, and affective force. PMS can note frame and temporality, but it may not fully capture the performative and rhetorical dimensions of critique.

A **sociological rival reading** could require empirical data about publics, institutions, media, movements, professions, bureaucracy, platforms, or complaint systems. PMS offers a structural schema; sociology could test or disconfirm it in practice.

A **legal/procedural rival reading** could treat critique as complaint, allegation, whistleblowing, testimony, report, review, or due process problem. This would require evidentiary standards, procedural rights, proportionality, and institutional rules not supplied here.

Each rival reading preserves something PMS or PMS-CRITIQUE may background. The article therefore does not rank these readings below PMS. It shows what PMS makes legible while marking where other grammars may be necessary.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS reading would be weakened if the movement from micro to macro did not involve meaningful frame change. If a concrete case showed that the local and macro critique were structurally identical in relevant respects, the central Φ reading would weaken. If scale did not alter asymmetry, temporality, exposure, response path, or integration demand, the PMS formula would be overbuilt.

The PMS reading would also weaken if critique reach could be explained more precisely by another formalization with fewer assumptions. For example, if a language-practice account fully explained the case as a shift in speech genre, or if a political theory explained it as public legitimacy formation, PMS would remain useful but not sufficient.

The reading would become under-discriminating if the same formula fit every form of critique without distinctions. Interpersonal feedback, institutional complaint, public accusation, systemic analysis, political protest, legal reporting, moral judgment, satire, and refusal cannot all be treated as the same structure. If PMS cannot preserve those differences, the case becomes too smooth.

The reading would become over-assimilative if every rupture, refusal, disagreement, rival framing, missing evidence, or non-response were redescribed as PMS drift, incomplete integration, incomplete calibration, or recontextualization. This is one of the strongest falsifier conditions. PMS must preserve resistance as possible non-capture.

The reading would be invalid as PMS application if Χ were suspended. If the analysis became a verdict, public tribunal, person-ranking, or enforcement frame, it would violate PMS entry discipline. If Dignity-in-Practice were used to shame, rank, or suppress critique, it would also fail. If Ψ were imposed as coercive binding of others rather than self-binding, the analysis would drift.

The PMS-CRITIQUE reading would be weakened if critique reach did not involve critique-specific thresholds. If the case were only a generic frame transition with no relevant distinction between interruption, correction, and follow-up, the CRITIQUE overlay would add little. If publicness were irrelevant, P would drop out. If drift typology did not help discriminate critique mutations, the overlay would be excessive.

The CRITIQUE reading would be overextended if CRITIQUE_MIN, CRITIQUE_PROD, and CRITIQUE_STABLE were treated as a hierarchy of worth. They are structural thresholds, not maturity ranks. A critique may remain at CRITIQUE_MIN for good reasons. A critique may reject CRITIQUE_PROD because correction is not its aim. A critique may avoid CRITIQUE_STABLE because binding would be coercive or premature. The overlay would also be weakened if it treated productive critique as the telos of all critique.

The MIP reading would be weakened if the A-band were read as case maturity rather than artefact maturity. The MIP output itself is careful: A-band 4–5 applies only to the structured analysis artefact, not persons or actual cases. If that boundary is lost, MIP becomes a scoring tool, which is prohibited. MIP would also be overextended if M-score were assigned to a critic, institution, or public without concrete role-holder evidence.

The IA-box remains only IA-risk. It would require concrete publicness, protected-good, purpose, role, reversibility, review, and evidence conditions before escalation. Without those, IA cannot become a finding. AHP explicitly identifies this as an attack point: IA-risk classification is clearer than IA evidence.

The D reading would be weakened if D-language were extracted as dignity assessment. D is active only as a qualitative guardrail. D0 is untouched. D1 is not assessed. D2/D-P appear as caution against humiliation, degradation, public pillory, irreversible labeling, and exposure-as-correction. If D is used to evaluate a person’s dignity, the analysis is invalid.

Operationalization remains unfulfilled. To move from conceptual analysis to stronger claims, one would need:

a concrete critique scene;
identified critic, addressee, affected parties, public, and institutions;
the actual critique content;
the frame in which it was made;
the publicness level;
the sequence of response and non-response;
the evidence base;
the institutional or public uptake path;
the correction or repair possibility;
the review window;
the reversibility conditions;
the exposure distribution;
the role-holder capacities;
rival perspectives;
indicators for Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, Ψ;
criteria for CRITIQUE_MIN, CRITIQUE_PROD, and CRITIQUE_STABLE;
evidence for or against drift;
MIP conditions for A, C, R, P, D;
IA criteria;
and AHP hardening of the resulting artefact.

None of this is supplied. Therefore all stronger claims remain withheld.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It cannot diagnose critics, addressees, publics, institutions, movements, professions, or systems. It cannot classify a person as immature, overcritical, reactive, avoidant, moralizing, dogmatic, fragile, or irresponsible.

It must not be used for person ranking. No A-band, M-band, D-language, CRITIQUE threshold, or drift risk may be transferred to persons. CRITIQUE_MIN is not low maturity. CRITIQUE_STABLE is not moral superiority. M-score is not guilt. D is not dignity score.

It must not be used legally or forensically. It does not establish evidence, liability, culpability, wrongdoing, procedural sufficiency, admissibility, or sanction justification. A critique reaching macro scale may resemble a public complaint or institutional allegation, but this article does not decide such matters.

It must not be used metaphysically. It does not define critique’s essence, critique’s final purpose, or the ontology of public reason, power, truth, or social structure. It does not prove that PMS captures critique as such.

It must not be used empirically. It does not prove how critique behaves in real publics, institutions, movements, or interpersonal settings. It does not show frequencies, tendencies, causal patterns, or outcomes.

It must not be used clinically. It contains no mental health assessment, no personality structure, no trauma inference, no risk assessment, no therapy planning, and no clinical recommendation.

It must not be used as implementation validation. It is not a moderation protocol, governance workflow, complaint-handling procedure, institutional policy, software design, or public communication guide. Any implementation would require additional design, testing, accountability, and review.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. A coherent case application does not prove PMS. It also does not validate PMS-CRITIQUE, MIP, or AHP.

It must not treat add-ons as operators. PMS-CRITIQUE constructs such as CRITIQUE_MIN, CRITIQUE_PROD, CRITIQUE_STABLE, publicness P, drift classes, and modulators are overlay handles. They are not PMS operators and do not alter Δ–Ψ.

It must not treat drift as verdict. AD_A>>E, AD_Sigma_low, judgment drift, narrative repair drift, commentary drift, public pillory risk, silence attractor, diffusion drift, integration bypass, and commitment evasion are possible structural risks. They are not accusations, findings, diagnoses, or moral labels.

It must not close by authority. Neither the article’s academic tone, YAML depth, formal structure, nor layered stack gives authority to settle real disputes. AHP explicitly warns that formal completeness may be mistaken for evidentiary completeness. That warning must remain visible.

The safest use of the case is as a structural article: a full human-readable rendering of how the completed PMS, PMS-CRITIQUE, MIP, and AHP analyses map critique reach while preserving boundaries.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result is that **Critique Reach (Micro → Macro)** can be mapped as a scale-sensitive transformation of critique across frames, but it cannot be closed as a verdict about critique itself. PMS Core shows the structural movement: critique begins with difference and directed pressure, becomes frame-bound, may involve absence or stabilized patterns, changes asymmetry and temporality, and then depends on recontextualization, distance, and possible integration. The core formula remains bounded: critique reach is a Φ-scale transition across □-frames under Ω and Θ, constrained by Χ, tested for Σ, and withholding Ψ unless concrete self-binding is supplied.

PMS-CRITIQUE adds a useful but subordinate sharpening. It shows that the case reaches **CRITIQUE_MIN** at the conceptual-overlay level because mismatch, frame, and interruptibility are present. It projects only weakly toward **CRITIQUE_PROD**, because no corrective consolidation is supplied. It does not establish **CRITIQUE_STABLE**, because no non-coercive follow-up binding is shown. Publicness P clarifies why macro critique may become more exposed, irreversible, audience-sensitive, and drift-prone, but P does not become an operator and does not make critique true, legitimate, corrective, or mature.

The examples contribute by making the scale movement legible without turning illustration into evidence. The seminar example shows how a local objection can become an institutional pattern claim and then a public critique, while preserving the need for evidence, distance, and integration. The public-exposure example shows why visibility is not correction and why publicness amplifies risk without deciding legitimacy. The systemic-framing example shows why Φ is powerful but dangerous: systemic critique can reveal what the local frame hides, yet it can also erase local constraints if Σ is not supplied. Together, the examples demonstrate the article’s main structural claim: critique reach changes the frame, and frame change changes the analysis.

What remains unclaimed is extensive and important. The article does not claim that macro critique is better than micro critique, or that public critique is inherently dangerous, or that systemic framing is overreach, or that local framing is evasive. It does not claim empirical proof, metaphysical proof, clinical diagnosis, legal or forensic conclusion, implementation validation, PMS Base validation, CRITIQUE validation, MIP validation, or AHP validation. It does not assign A-score, M-score, D-status, maturity, guilt, dignity, or responsibility to any person, institution, critic, addressee, or public. It does not convert drift risk into drift finding. It does not treat non-assignment as low score. It does not treat non-capture as failure.

The boundary discipline is therefore not a peripheral safety note; it is part of the case result. Χ must remain active so the analysis does not become a tribunal. Σ must remain conditional so coherence does not become proof. Ψ must remain withheld where no self-binding is supplied. Dignity-in-Practice must remain praxeological, not ontological. PMS-CRITIQUE terms must remain overlay handles, not PMS operators. MIP must remain an artefact-level maturity-in-practice check, not person evaluation. AHP must remain analysis-hardening, not rescoring. Resistance, refusal, rupture, disagreement, absence, and rival framing must not be automatically converted into drift, incomplete integration, or maturity failure.

The final methodological takeaway is that critique reach is precisely the kind of case AXIOM is built to handle: not by resolving critique into a final theory, but by showing where closure is structurally warranted, where it remains conditional, and where Λ-residue must remain visible. PMS Core maps the operator grammar. PMS-CRITIQUE sharpens critique-specific thresholds and drift risks. MIP checks downstream maturity and misuse boundaries. AHP hardens the analysis against false precision. The result is not a verdict about critique. It is a disciplined cartography of how critique changes when it moves from micro to macro, why that movement can matter, why it can become risky, and why some remainder must stay visible rather than be forced into closure. The case therefore does not solve critique; it gives critique reach a bounded structural map.
