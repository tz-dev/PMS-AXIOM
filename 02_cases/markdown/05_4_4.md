### Why this case matters

Grounding regress is one of the clearest cases in which philosophical closure-demand becomes visible as a structure rather than merely as a doctrine. The problem begins from a simple and powerful pressure: if something is explained, justified, reasoned, or grounded by something else, then the ground itself appears to invite the same question. What grounds the ground? If that further ground is supplied, the same demand can be repeated. The pattern therefore threatens to generate an indefinite chain: ground, further ground, further ground of the ground, and so on.

The difficulty is not merely that the chain is long. The difficulty is that the chain tests what counts as a legitimate stopping point. A theory may stop at a primitive ground, a brute fact, a self-evident principle, a coherent circle, an infinite chain, a pragmatic sufficiency threshold, or a deflationary rejection of the question. Each of these responses changes the frame in which “grounding” is being asked. The case therefore belongs in AXIOM because it is not simply a problem of insufficient explanation. It is a problem about how closure is demanded, where the demand becomes self-replicating, and which residues remain once the demand is made explicit.

This makes grounding regress structurally difficult in several ways. First, the relation between ground and grounded is already directional: one item is treated as depending on another. Second, the demand for a further ground applies recursively to the very thing that was supposed to stop the demand. Third, the absence of a terminal ground can be interpreted in rival ways: as a defect, as an acceptable infinite structure, as a sign that grounding is being misframed, as a need for foundational stopping, or as evidence that the relevant closure is pragmatic rather than metaphysical. Fourth, the language of grounding has high inflation risk. A merely operational stopping point can easily be presented as ultimate foundation; a coherent integration can be mistaken for final warrant; an authoritative frame can be mistaken for truth.

The AXIOM treatment does not solve this metaphysically. It does not decide whether grounding relations are real, whether an infinite regress is vicious, whether brute facts are acceptable, whether coherentism succeeds, or whether a final metaphysical ground exists. It also does not treat the regress as empirical evidence, clinical material, legal evidence, or an implementation problem. The case is instead rendered as a structural artefact: a differentiated ground/grounded relation generates a further-ground demand inside a frame; closure does not arrive; recurrence stabilizes; weak dependency-direction may appear; the sequence continues as regress.

PMS-LOGIC is then added because the case is not just any recurrence structure. It is specifically a case in which reasoning itself encounters a limit. The question is not only “what is the next ground?” but “what happens when the demand for grounding cannot be terminated without changing the frame, imposing authority, or substituting coherence for foundation?” This is why PMS-LOGIC is not ornamental. It sharpens a boundary that PMS Core already exposes: logic can order the chain, distinguish frames, and identify non-closure, but it must not become a final authority merely because it can organize the problem.

This is precisely why the case matters for AXIOM. It shows how a classical philosophical problem can be made structurally inspectable without being forcibly resolved. PMS Core supplies the Δ–Ψ grammar for the regress-form. PMS-LOGIC adds the overlay discipline needed when reasoning itself becomes tempted to convert coherence into foundation. The outcome is not a metaphysical answer but a boundary-conscious rendering: the regress can be mapped, its closure-demand can be described, its drift risks can be named, and its non-capture zones can remain visible.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading begins with **Δ, Difference**. Grounding regress requires a distinction between what is grounded and what grounds it. The grounded item may be a fact, claim, reason, explanation, norm, truth, or state of affairs. The ground is what is supposed to support or explain it. Without this distinction, there is no regress. The first calibration point is therefore to identify which contrast is actually in play. A metaphysical ground, epistemic reason, causal explanation, semantic justification, and pragmatic warrant are not the same thing. If PMS collapses these distinctions into one generic ground/grounded contrast, it loses discriminative force.

From Δ follows **∇, Impulse**. In this case ∇ is not a psychological urge. It is the directional pressure generated by the frame: if a ground is offered, the inquiry moves toward asking for its ground. The demand is structurally directional. It does not merely list two items; it presses from one item toward another. The calibration risk is psychologization. PMS must not infer that a person “wants certainty,” “cannot tolerate ambiguity,” or “seeks control.” The supplied material only supports a further-ground demand inside a conceptual frame.

The third operator is **□, Frame**. The regress arises only within a frame that treats grounding as requiring further grounding. The frame defines what counts as a ground, what counts as sufficiency, and what counts as a legitimate stopping condition. A metaphysical frame may demand ontological dependence; an epistemic frame may demand reasons; a semantic frame may demand meaning-relations; a pragmatic frame may accept sufficient-for-purpose stopping. The case therefore depends heavily on □. If the frame changes, the regress changes. A major dependency risk is treating the regress as if it were frame-independent when the analysis explicitly preserves frame-dependence.

**Λ, Non-Event**, is central. The expected event is closure: a terminal ground, sufficient reason, final explanation, or accepted stop-rule. In grounding regress, that event does not arrive, or its arrival is not warranted by the material. Λ is not a mere absence. It is a structured absence inside a frame that expects closure. This is why Λ must be calibrated carefully. If the operative frame does not require final closure, then Λ weakens. An infinitist, for example, may not regard non-termination as failure. A coherentist may reject linear termination. A deflationary reading may reject the demand itself. PMS must therefore not treat non-arrival as automatically defective.

**Α, Attractor**, appears when the demand repeats. Each proposed ground becomes the next grounded item. The recurrence stabilizes into a pattern: no ground is simply the end; every ground can be re-entered as a candidate requiring grounding. This makes the regress not just a single missing answer but a recurrent structure. The calibration risk is over-reading one unresolved question as an attractor. Α is warranted only insofar as the case material describes potential iteration. Here it does, because the case explicitly concerns a chain in which one ground requires another.

**Ω, Asymmetry**, is weak and conditional. There is a structural direction between ground and grounded: one is positioned as supporting the other. This may be called dependency-direction. But it is not yet power, exposure, moral hierarchy, institutional authority, or responsibility gradient. Pushing Ω too far would be a serious overclaim. In a concrete scene, a philosophical authority might impose a stopping point, or an institution might enforce a grounding standard. But this case does not supply that scene. Ω must therefore remain narrow: weak dependency-asymmetry only.

**Θ, Temporality**, is active but abstract. The regress has ordered continuation. One ground leads to another demand, and that demand leads to another. The chain has seriality. But Θ must not be literalized as empirical time unless a concrete inquiry process is supplied. The sequence may be logical or explanatory. The major calibration point is the distinction between temporal unfolding and inferential order. PMS can mark the chain as Θ-structured because it has ordered continuation, but it must not pretend that the case supplies a historical trajectory, long-term commitment, institutional memory, or developmental arc.

**Φ, Recontextualization**, is conditional. The base case does not itself perform a reframing; it only makes reframing possible. Foundationalism, coherentism, infinitism, pragmatic stopping, semantic analysis, and deflationary dissolution are all possible Φ moves. Each shifts the frame in which the regress is interpreted. But PMS must not absorb every rival theory as if it were merely a PMS-internal recontextualization. A rival formalization may be genuinely external to the PMS reading or may preserve something PMS does not capture. Thus Φ is relevant but weak: it names a possible transformation, not a completed operation.

**Χ, Distance**, is major. It governs the analysis itself. Χ prevents the PMS reading from becoming a closure machine. It requires the analyst to hold back from metaphysical verdicts, from declaring PMS superiority, from treating non-closure as failure, and from converting a coherent chain into proof. In this case Χ is not merely one operator among others; it is an application discipline. Without Χ, the analysis would likely repeat the very error it maps: it would respond to the regress by imposing a final structural closure. The Core reading therefore marks Χ as active and necessary.

**Σ, Integration**, is weak and problematic. PMS can integrate the elements of the regress into a coherent structural account: distinction, demand, frame, non-closure, recurrence, weak dependency-direction, and ordered continuation. But that integration is not a solution. A coherent PMS derivation does not settle the regress. It does not decide between foundationalism, coherentism, infinitism, pragmatism, or deflation. Σ is therefore available as provisional coordination, not as achieved philosophical closure. The danger is Σ-totalization: treating the organized PMS account as though it were the missing ground.

**Ψ, Self-Binding**, is not active as case content. There is no agent, theory, institution, or practice binding itself to a grounding policy across time. Ψ becomes relevant only if a philosophical school, institution, or system commits itself to a stopping rule, methodology, or standard of justification. In the completed Core case, Ψ appears only as application self-binding: the analysis binds itself to restraint, reversibility, and non-overclaim. It does not attribute selfhood to the regress.

The Core formula therefore remains intentionally bounded:

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The PMS dependency discipline is important because grounding regress tempts cheap use of later operators. The completed analysis preserves the operator order Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, Ψ. It also preserves the dependency logic: later operators cannot be invoked as if they were free-floating labels. Χ, Σ, and Ψ are especially expensive. They require prior structures and must not be used to simulate closure.

A first invalid shortcut would be **Δ/∇ → Ψ**. This would occur if the mere presence of a ground/grounded distinction and closure-demand were used to bind a final doctrine, stopping point, or identity claim. In ordinary language, this would sound like: because a regress appears, one must accept a final ground; or because the chain is unstable, one must bind oneself to a specific foundation. PMS rejects that shortcut. Ψ requires integration, temporality, and distance; none are supplied as case content.

A second invalid shortcut would be **□ → Λ → Σ as proof**. The analysis can show that a frame generates a meaningful absence and that PMS can coordinate that absence into a coherent map. But this does not prove that the map resolves the problem. PMS coherence is not sufficient. The analysis explicitly marks the coherence risk as high: grounding regress maps readily onto PMS operators, and that smooth fit may make the reading appear stronger than its discriminative warrant. A good PMS rendering must therefore preserve uncertainty rather than treat elegance as validation.

A third invalid shortcut would be **Α → Ω inflation**. Recurrence can stabilize a pattern, but recurrence does not imply authority. The fact that every ground may invite a further ground does not mean that one role dominates another, that a person is responsible, or that a philosophical tradition is guilty of failure. Ω remains weak unless a concrete asymmetry is supplied. Dependency-direction is not moral hierarchy.

A fourth invalid shortcut would be **Θ literalization**. The chain has ordered continuation, but the analysis must not convert logical or explanatory order into empirical time. No historical development, biography, institutional sequence, or implementation timeline is supplied. Treating Θ as chronological would overextend the case.

A fifth invalid shortcut would be **Φ assimilation**. Rival readings are not automatically PMS-internal reframings. A foundationalist solution, coherentist account, infinitist theory, semantic analysis, or deflationary critique may preserve distinctions that PMS does not fully capture. PMS may map the shift from one frame to another, but it may not use Φ to absorb all rivals and then claim victory.

A sixth invalid shortcut would be **Σ-totalization**. This is the central danger. PMS can integrate the regress-form into a structural account, and PMS-LOGIC later names this as a drift risk. But integration is coordination, not ultimate foundation. If Σ becomes final authority, the analysis repeats the closure-pressure it was supposed to discipline. The completed analysis therefore keeps Σ weak and states explicitly that PMS coherence does not solve the regress.

A seventh invalid shortcut would be **Ψ activation without a self-binding target**. The regress is not a self. It does not bind itself. It does not have responsibility, autobiography, identity, or commitment. A theory or institution could bind itself to a grounding policy, but that would require additional material. In the present case, Ψ remains analytic-only as application self-restraint.

The dependency discipline therefore has a philosophical function. It prevents PMS from becoming a closure machine. The regress is not solved by naming operators. It is made legible under conditions that preserve non-closure. Non-capture is not failure. If PMS cannot decide whether the regress is metaphysical, epistemic, semantic, or pragmatic, that is not automatically a defect. It may be an accurate preservation of the case’s under-specification.

This also means that falsification and weakening conditions are internal to the discipline. The PMS reading weakens if the case is shown to be purely formal in a way that PMS does not discriminate. It weakens if a rival formalization explains the regress more precisely with fewer assumptions. It weakens if PMS cannot distinguish vicious regress from benign infinitism, coherentist support, foundational stopping, pragmatic sufficiency, or deflationary dissolution. It fails if it turns rival framing into drift, non-fit into immaturity, or non-closure into a defect requiring repair.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS drift-pattern analysis is deliberately cautious. No drift is classified as present. The case is abstract, not a concrete praxis scene; therefore drift patterns can only be marked as conditional risks or possible distortions of analysis. Risk is not finding. Under-specification is not failure. Non-assignment is not a low score. No person, group, theory, or institution is described as drifting.

The first relevant drift-pattern is **AD_A>>E**, excessive distance between Awareness and Enactment. In this case it is weak or possible but not entailed. Grounding regress may resemble a configuration in which awareness expands while enactment or closure is delayed. The chain can become highly reflective: each ground is inspected, then the ground of that ground, then the ground of that ground. But this does not automatically mean that action is suppressed, maturity is lacking, or integration has failed. Philosophical non-closure may be legitimate inquiry, not imbalance.

In the drift-pattern’s axis language, the awareness axis remains **under-specified** rather than straightforwardly over-expanded. The action axis also remains **under-specified** because no concrete action is supplied. The case contains argumentative movement, not an enacted project. Therefore AD_A>>E cannot be diagnosed, scored, or treated as an active finding. It remains a structural risk only if a later concrete scene shows reflective expansion chronically preventing appropriate enactment within a frame where action is required.

The second relevant drift-pattern is **AD_Sigma_low**, fragmented or weak integration. Again, this is possible but not entailed. Grounding regress may appear to involve low integration because no final account consolidates the chain. However, non-integration may be legitimate. An infinitist may accept endless continuation. A coherentist may integrate differently. A deflationary reading may reject the demand. A pragmatic reading may stop without metaphysical totalization. PMS must not assume that failure to reach final ground equals failed Σ.

The coherence axis for AD_Sigma_low is therefore **under-specified**, not volatile by evidence. The responsibility axis is **under-specified** because Responsibility_R is not active. There is no agent or system whose responsibility becomes inconsistent. The drift-pattern cannot be used to say that the regress is a failure of responsibility, maturity, or self-binding.

The main coherence risk is different: PMS itself may become too smooth. Grounding regress maps readily onto Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, and Θ. This is analytically useful, but it can create under-discrimination. If every regress, uncertainty, non-closure, absence, or rival formalization is rendered as the same PMS pattern, the analysis loses precision. The case therefore requires explicit rival readings and falsifier conditions.

The major under-discrimination risk is that PMS might collapse metaphysical grounding, epistemic justification, causal explanation, semantic explanation, pragmatic sufficiency, formal derivation, and institutional accountability into one generic “further ground” structure. The completed analysis rejects that collapse. It treats the Core formula as a minimal structural model, not an exhaustive account of all grounding problems.

The major over-assimilation risk is that PMS might translate resistance, refusal, non-fit, disagreement, rupture, unresolved absence, or rival framing into drift, incomplete calibration, incomplete integration, recontextualization, or maturity failure. That move is explicitly forbidden by the completed analysis. A rival account may simply be rival. A non-captured feature may remain outside PMS. An unresolved boundary may be a legitimate Λ, not an error.

The drift-pattern conclusion is therefore narrow. PMS Core identifies possible structural risks around reflective expansion and weak integration, but it does not classify drift. The case remains reversible, bounded, non-diagnostic, non-tribunal, non-person-ranking, and open to rival formalizations.

### Add-on / overlay reading

The relevant add-on for this case is **PMS-LOGIC**. It is used strictly as an overlay / application profile. It does not redefine PMS operators, does not alter PMS dependencies, does not introduce new PMS primitives, and does not revise the Core pass. Its function is to make visible a LOGIC-specific profile that PMS Core already prepares but does not isolate with the same precision: the relation between logical ordering, closure-pressure, non-termination, sustainable non-assertion, and the drift risk of converting coherence or authority into substitute grounding.

PMS Core can already render grounding regress as a sequence of differentiated demand: a ground/grounded distinction activates a further-ground demand inside a frame; the expected terminal ground does not arrive; the demand recurs; weak dependency-direction and ordered continuation may be marked. This is structurally sufficient for a PMS Core reading. But grounding regress is not only any recurrence pattern. It is a case in which reasoning itself is tempted to become authoritative by producing, enforcing, or simulating a final ground. PMS-LOGIC is therefore justified because it sharpens the logical boundary condition: the point at which logic remains a valid ordering tool but cannot legitimately become final authority.

The overlay begins with its **pre-moral justification layer**. In this case, “pre-moral” does not mean ethically prior in a ranking sense. It means that the structure is readable before norms, values, duties, or prescriptions are introduced. The relevant reduced signature is:

**PRE_MORAL_justification:** Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α.

This signature is active. Grounding regress begins with a distinction between what is grounded and what grounds it. It activates a further-ground demand. That demand is meaningful only inside a frame that treats grounding as relevant. The non-arrival of a terminal ground appears as Λ. Repetition may stabilize as Α when each candidate ground becomes the next item requiring grounding. None of this yet introduces obligation, blame, moral evaluation, duty, or prescription. The regress is structurally legible before any moral or post-moral layer appears.

This matters because grounding language can easily become normative by implication. A frame-setter may say that a position “lacks ground,” that a claim “must be grounded,” or that a theory “fails” because it does not terminate. PMS-LOGIC blocks that escalation. The pre-moral layer says only that the structure of demand is readable. It does not say that a participant is irrational, deficient, irresponsible, or morally at fault for failing to provide an ultimate ground.

Grounding regress is especially vulnerable to this failure. The LOGIC drift risks are central, but they remain risks rather than findings. The most important is **dogmatism / Σ-totalization**. This occurs when integration is forced into total closure and coherence is treated as final truth. Grounding regress invites this drift because a coherent account can feel like a foundation. PMS-LOGIC blocks that move: Σ may coordinate, but it does not become ultimate ground.

The second drift risk is **technocratic reduction / □-overconstraint**. This occurs when a frame is treated as exhaustive reality. In grounding regress, a frame may define what counts as a ground so narrowly that rival forms of grounding disappear by definition. For example, a frame might admit only deductive foundations, only metaphysical dependence, only pragmatic sufficiency, or only semantic dissolution. PMS-LOGIC treats such narrowing as a risk when it is used to manufacture closure rather than declare scope.

The third drift risk is **authority capture / Ω-inflation**. Since Ω is weak in the Core case, this remains only a risk by analogy at the level of analysis. It would become relevant if a philosophical authority, institution, tradition, or formal system imposed a stopping point as authoritative. In the abstract case, however, no such authority is supplied. The overlay therefore warns against inflating weak dependency-direction into justificatory authority.

The fourth drift risk is **moralism / pseudo-ought injection**. This is a risk only if the analysis converts non-closure into a demand: one must accept PMS, one must stop at foundations, one must reject infinite regress, or one must treat unresolvedness as failure. The completed reading does not do this. It marks the risk and prevents it from becoming active.

The fifth drift risk is **nihilism / Λ-suppression**. This may seem counterintuitive: nihilism here is not “too much non-closure” but the erasure of Λ’s structure. If one says “no final ground appears, therefore nothing matters” or “the regress is meaningless,” Λ has been flattened rather than carried. PMS-LOGIC instead treats non-closure as structured absence within a frame.

The overlay’s counter-readings are also preserved. The reading is not ethics in disguise, because no PMS operator generates values, norms, or prescriptions. It is not relativism, because frames are explicit constraints and reversibility is disciplined rather than arbitrary. It is not nihilism, because Λ is meaningful absence, not meaninglessness. It is not a hidden theological argument, because no external ground, lawgiver, or ultimate justification is introduced. It does not make responsibility unfair, because responsibility is not attributed. It does not guide action in the ordinary prescriptive sense; it constrains overreach. And it does not win by translation. Translation success is not final superiority. The relevant test is whether the grammar preserves differentiating force under pressure.

MIP and AHP are not activated by the LOGIC overlay. This formula says that the case is primarily a logical-boundary case. ### Layer interaction

The PMS Core layer remains the base. It supplies the operator grammar, dependencies, derived structures, and application discipline. The Core reading says that grounding regress is structurally legible as a framed, recurrent, ordered further-ground demand with suspended closure. This remains unchanged after the LOGIC overlay is added.

The LOGIC overlay sharpens one specific aspect of that Core reading: the boundary condition of reasoning under non-termination. PMS Core identifies Λ as the non-arrival of closure. LOGIC asks what happens when logic itself encounters that Λ and is tempted to repair it. PMS Core identifies Σ as weak and provisional. LOGIC names the drift risk of Σ-totalization. PMS Core marks Ω as weak dependency-direction. LOGIC warns against Ω-inflation, where authority substitutes for warrant. PMS Core keeps Φ conditional. LOGIC clarifies that rival reframings must not be reduced to internal PMS moves.

The interaction is therefore additive in visibility but not additive in ontology. LOGIC does not introduce a new base grammar. Its reduced signatures are overlay shorthand. When LOGIC says `□, Λ, Χ, Σ`, it is not changing PMS dependencies. Χ still depends on prior structures; Σ still depends on Χ and Φ. The reduced signature marks analytic salience, not dependency freedom.

This distinction matters because the case itself concerns grounding. If the overlay were allowed to redefine PMS operators, it would create precisely the kind of hidden foundation it is supposed to resist. It would smuggle a new base into an application layer. The completed analysis blocks that move. PMS remains PMS Base. LOGIC remains a subordinate application profile. The overlay helps the reader see reasoning-specific closure risks, but it does not become the ground of PMS or the ground of grounding.

The add-on must also not convert non-closure into proof of its own correctness. LOGIC can say that non-closure must be carried when the case does not warrant closure. It cannot say that sustainable non-assertion is the final metaphysical truth of grounding. It can say that forced closure is a structural risk. It cannot say that all closure is invalid. It can distinguish functional stopping from ultimate foundation. It cannot decide in advance that foundationalism, coherentism, infinitism, pragmatism, or deflation is false.

The interaction between Core and overlay is therefore best understood as two-stage boundary discipline. Core maps the regress. LOGIC prevents the map from becoming an authority that closes the regress. Core gives the structural formula. LOGIC names the failure modes that arise when the formula is over-read. Core preserves non-capture. LOGIC explains why non-capture is especially important in reasoning cases: the desire for closure can itself become the mechanism of overreach.

### Example 1 — The “because” chain in a seminar

**Type:** compact
**Recommended insertion point:** After “PMS Core structural reading,” before “PMS dependency and calibration discipline.”

A seminar discussion begins with a simple explanatory claim: a participant says that a social phenomenon occurs because of a particular institutional rule. Another participant asks why that rule has authority. A third answers that the rule derives from a constitutional framework. Someone then asks why that framework is binding. The conversation continues: convention grounds the framework; collective recognition grounds convention; shared practice grounds recognition; historical continuity grounds shared practice. At each step, the proposed ground becomes the next object requiring grounding.

The vignette is bounded. It does not claim that the seminar participants are confused, dogmatic, evasive, immature, or psychologically unable to tolerate uncertainty. It also does not decide whether constitutional authority, convention, collective recognition, or practice is the correct theory of grounding. The example only shows the structure of regress: a ground/grounded distinction activates a further-ground demand inside a frame that expects explanatory sufficiency.

The PMS Core hooks are straightforward. **Δ** appears in the distinction between the phenomenon and its ground, and then between each proposed ground and its own ground. **∇** appears as the repeated demand for “why?” or “in virtue of what?” **□** is the seminar’s justificatory frame: explanation is expected to continue until an adequate stopping point is reached. **Λ** appears when no uncontested terminal ground arrives. **Α** appears as the repeated pattern by which each answer becomes the next question. **Θ** is present as ordered argumentative continuation, not empirical time. **Ω** remains weak: there is dependency-direction among claims, but no social or moral asymmetry is established by the vignette. **Φ** becomes possible if the participants shift to another theory of authority. **Χ** is required if the discussion avoids turning one answer into forced closure. **Σ** remains provisional if the participants coordinate a usable explanation without claiming final foundation. **Ψ** is not case content unless the seminar later binds itself to a doctrine or methodology.

The LOGIC overlay clarifies what the example demonstrates. The discussion is not a failure merely because it does not end. It becomes a LOGIC boundary case when the group recognizes that continuing the chain and imposing a final stop are different operations. The active reduced signatures are **PRE_MORAL_justification** and **LOGIC_boundary_management**. **NON_CLOSURE_stability** becomes visible if the group can preserve the open boundary without declaring the inquiry meaningless. **POST_MORAL_effects**, effective capacity, non-innocence, and responsibility without ought are not activated.

MIP, AHP, and QC are not triggered by this example. The vignette is not a governance artefact, policy decision, audit trace, or applied responsibility assessment. It remains an illustrative conceptual scene.

The forbidden claims are important. The example must not be used to prove that constitutional authority lacks ground, that convention is circular, that all grounding is impossible, or that PMS-LOGIC has solved the problem. It also must not be used to rank the seminar participants. The non-capture note is that a political theorist, legal philosopher, pragmatist, or semantic analyst may describe the same discussion more precisely within their own framework.

The article takeaway is limited but useful: grounding regress becomes visible when each offered explanation is re-entered as an object requiring explanation. PMS maps this recurrence; PMS-LOGIC prevents the seminar’s need for order from being mistaken for permission to impose ultimate ground.

### Example 2 — A formal stopping rule that works but does not become ultimate

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** Inside “Add-on / overlay reading,” after the discussion of LOGIC boundary management.

A research group adopts a formal stopping rule for a theory-building project. They agree that, for the current paper, a claim counts as “grounded enough” if it is supported by three independent arguments, consistent with the accepted definitions, and not contradicted by the available counterexamples. The rule works. It prevents endless deferral, keeps the project readable, and allows the group to publish a coherent account. Later, however, a reviewer asks whether the stopping rule itself is grounded. The group can explain why the rule is useful, but they cannot show that it is the final or uniquely rational stopping point for every possible inquiry.

This example is bounded because it concerns a functional stopping rule, not an ultimate foundation. It does not claim that the research group has failed. It does not claim that their paper is invalid. It does not claim that all stopping rules are arbitrary. It also does not claim that a formal rule becomes metaphysically final simply because it organizes inquiry successfully.

The PMS Core structure is precise. **Δ** distinguishes the claim from its support, and later distinguishes the stopping rule from the claims it regulates. **∇** appears as the need to stop inquiry in order to complete the paper. **□** is the project frame: the rule is valid for a given paper, purpose, audience, and disciplinary standard. **Λ** appears when the rule cannot ground itself as ultimate. **Α** appears because the rule may stabilize repeated inquiry practices. **Ω** remains weak unless the rule is imposed by an authority or institution. **Θ** appears as the project’s temporal horizon: inquiry must proceed, drafts must close, and revision cycles exist. **Φ** becomes available if the group reframes the rule as pragmatic rather than metaphysical. **Χ** is necessary to keep the group from overstating the rule. **Σ** is active only as provisional integration: it coordinates evidence and argument for the paper. **Ψ** appears only if the group or discipline binds itself to the rule across contexts.

The LOGIC overlay makes the example especially useful. It shows the difference between operational stopping and final grounding. PMS-LOGIC does not condemn the stopping rule. On the contrary, it allows the rule to function as a bounded **Σ** integration. What it blocks is the drift from “this rule is sufficient for this frame” to “this rule is the ultimate ground of justification.” This is exactly the difference between LOGIC as tool and LOGIC as authority.

The active LOGIC signatures are **LOGIC_boundary_management** and **NON_CLOSURE_stability**. The rule manages closure inside **□** while **Λ** remains visible at the meta-level. **Χ** prevents the group from laundering functional success into ultimate warrant. The main drift risk is **dogmatism / Σ-totalization**: the rule might be treated as final simply because it produces a coherent paper. A second risk is **□-overconstraint**: the project frame might be mistaken for the whole space of possible justification. **Ω-inflation** would become relevant only if a gatekeeping institution enforced the rule as the only legitimate standard.

MIP and AHP remain inactive unless the stopping rule is used in downstream governance, certification, evaluation, or exclusion. If the rule becomes part of an institutional assessment procedure, MIP may become relevant to audit scope, reversibility, and guardrails. AHP may become relevant only as analysis-artifact hardening: it would require explicit claims, attack surfaces, and falsifier conditions. It would not rescore persons or upgrade the rule into authority. QC is not triggered unless the artefact is being quality-checked as a transmissible analysis product.

Forbidden claims include: the rule proves grounding; the rule is invalid because it is not ultimate; the group is dogmatic merely for using it; PMS-LOGIC is superior because it can describe the rule. The non-capture note is that pragmatism, epistemology of inquiry, formal methodology, or sociology of science might capture other dimensions of the example.

The article takeaway is that a stopping rule can be legitimate without being ultimate. PMS Core maps its frame-bound integration. PMS-LOGIC protects the distinction between usable closure and final foundation.

### Example 3 — The temptation to declare non-closure meaningless

**Type:** compact
**Recommended insertion point:** Before “Non-capture and rival readings,” after “Layer interaction.”

A reader encounters a grounding regress and reacts: “If no final ground can be given, then the whole discussion is pointless.” This response seems to preserve non-closure, but it actually suppresses it. Instead of carrying **Λ** as a structured boundary, it flattens the absence of final ground into meaninglessness. The regress is no longer treated as a specific pattern of framed demand and absent termination; it is dismissed as nothing worth analyzing.

The example is bounded. It does not claim that the reader is nihilistic as a person. It does not diagnose a temperament, belief system, or intellectual failure. It does not say that all refusal of grounding questions is invalid. Some deflationary readings may be legitimate rival formalizations. The example only illustrates one possible misuse: treating non-closure as if it had no structure.

PMS Core hooks show why this is a distortion. **Δ** initially distinguishes grounded and ground. **∇** activates the further-ground demand. **□** frames the problem as one of grounding. **Λ** appears when terminal closure is absent. The reader’s dismissal attempts to bypass the analysis of **Α**, **Θ**, **Φ**, **Χ**, and **Σ** by refusing to inspect how the absence functions. If the dismissal is a carefully argued deflationary reframing, it may be a legitimate **Φ**. If it is merely “no final ground, therefore nothing matters,” it suppresses **Λ** rather than analyzing it.

The LOGIC overlay makes this difference visible through the risk of **nihilism / Λ-suppression**. In PMS-LOGIC, **Λ** is not meaninglessness. It is structured absence inside a frame. The disciplined response to grounding regress is not forced closure, but also not collapse into “nothing matters.” It is sustainable non-assertion: the analysis may stop where the material does not warrant further closure, while still preserving what has been structurally shown.

The active reduced signature is **NON_CLOSURE_stability**. **LOGIC_boundary_management** is active because the reader’s response tests whether non-closure is carried or erased. **PRE_MORAL_justification** remains active at the basic structural level. **POST_MORAL_effects**, effective capacity, non-innocence, responsibility, guilt, and obligation remain not applicable.

MIP, AHP, and QC are not triggered. There is no governance artefact, institutional decision, policy effect, or analysis-hardening need beyond the conceptual boundary already stated.

Forbidden claims include: the reader is morally wrong; deflationary philosophy is invalid; non-closure proves nihilism; PMS-LOGIC has refuted all anti-capture readings. The non-capture note is especially important: a rigorous deflationary account may preserve something PMS-LOGIC does not capture. The example targets only the flattening of **Λ**, not all refusals of grounding.

The article takeaway is that sustainable non-closure is not silence, defeat, or nihilism. It is an analytic discipline that keeps a boundary visible without replacing it by dogma or dismissing it as empty.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The completed YAML analyses preserve a broad non-capture zone. This is not incidental. Grounding regress is a problem with many established rival formalizations, and PMS-AXIOM must not treat them as merely incomplete PMS translations. The purpose of the case is to make the closure-demand structurally inspectable, not to absorb every rival theory into PMS terms.

A **metaphysical grounding reading** treats the regress as a question about ontological dependence. On this reading, some facts, entities, truths, or states of affairs depend on others. The regress problem concerns whether dependence must terminate, whether infinite dependence chains are possible, and whether some entities are fundamental. PMS can map the structure of further-ground demand, but it does not decide whether metaphysical grounding relations exist. The metaphysical reading preserves ontological questions that PMS intentionally does not settle.

An **epistemological reading** treats the regress as a justification regress. A belief is justified by a reason; that reason requires another reason; the chain continues. Here the problem concerns warrant, rational acceptability, basic beliefs, coherent support, or infinitist justification. PMS-LOGIC is relevant because it handles warrant gaps and boundary management, but it does not supply a final epistemology. Foundationalism, coherentism, and infinitism remain genuine rivals.

A **logical or formal reading** may treat the regress as a structure of derivation, proof, or formal dependence. This may involve questions about axioms, rules of inference, circularity, consistency, completeness, or infinite proof structures. PMS can represent closure-pressure and non-termination, but it may not capture the technical content of a formal system. If proof theory or formal logic explains the case with greater precision, that is not a PMS failure; it is a domain-specific rival capture.

A **semantic or language-practice reading** may treat the regress as arising from the use of “because,” “ground,” “reason,” “in virtue of,” or “explanation.” On this reading, the problem may dissolve once language use is clarified. PMS-LOGIC can mark this as possible Φ, but it must not reduce the semantic account to an internal recontextualization. A semantic reading may preserve distinctions that PMS does not model, especially if the issue is not praxis but meaning, grammar, or use.

A **pragmatic reading** treats stopping as practice-relative. A ground is sufficient when it is sufficient for inquiry, coordination, action, or explanation under a particular purpose. This reading preserves the difference between functional sufficiency and ultimate foundation. PMS-LOGIC is friendly to this distinction, especially through logical boundary management, but it does not own it. Pragmatism remains a rival or adjacent framework.

A **foundationalist reading** holds that some grounds do not require further grounding, or that some beliefs, facts, principles, or structures are basic. PMS-LOGIC does not refute this. It only requires that the foundational stop be declared as a frame-specific or theory-specific move unless independently warranted. If a foundationalist account supplies criteria for basic grounds, PMS must treat those criteria as rival formalization, not as automatic drift.

A **coherentist reading** rejects linear termination and treats mutual support as sufficient. PMS-LOGIC can identify the risk of Σ-totalization, but it must not confuse all coherentism with dogmatism. Coherentist mutual support may be a legitimate theory of justification or explanation. The risk arises only when coherence is treated as final authority without carrying Λ or acknowledging frame conditions.

An **infinitist reading** accepts infinite chains under certain conditions. For infinitism, non-termination may not be a defect. This directly weakens any PMS reading that assumes terminal closure is expected. The Core analysis therefore calibrates Λ carefully: Λ is strong only inside frames that expect closure. If the operative frame accepts infinite continuation, the non-event of terminal ground loses some of its problem status.

A **deflationary or anti-capture reading** may reject the grounding demand itself. It may argue that the regress is a pseudo-problem created by a mistaken frame. PMS can mark this as possible Φ, but it cannot assume that deflation is merely a recontextualization inside PMS. Deflation may displace the PMS framing by denying the need for the closure-demand.

A **phenomenological reading** might focus on the experience of seeking ground, certainty, or intelligibility. The completed analysis does not take this route and must not smuggle it in. The case material does not provide lived experience. PMS therefore does not claim to capture the phenomenology of grounding anxiety, intellectual need, or existential uncertainty.

An **ethical reading** might ask whether demands for ultimate ground are fair, coercive, exclusionary, or oppressive in actual discourse. This is not supplied by the abstract case. It could become relevant in a concrete scene where grounding demands are used to silence or dominate, but the Core case does not activate that layer. PMS-LOGIC marks the risk of authority capture but does not claim an ethical finding.

A **governance reading** might ask whether stopping rules, justification regimes, or grounding policies become institutional standards. That reading is not active here. It would require a concrete decision environment, role distribution, stakes, accountability mechanisms, and contest channels. Only then might MIP or AHP become relevant. The present case remains a conceptual LOGIC case, not a governance case.

These rival readings preserve what PMS and PMS-LOGIC deliberately leave open: metaphysics, epistemology, formal logic, semantics, pragmatism, phenomenology, ethics, and governance can each supply domain-specific content. PMS-AXIOM’s contribution is cartographic. It shows where the closure-demand is structurally located, where Λ remains, and where overlay discipline is justified. It does not replace the rival traditions by authority.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS Core reading would weaken if the case were specified as purely formal in a way that PMS does not discriminate. For example, if the regress were fully captured by a formal proof-theoretic relation with no relevant praxis frame, then PMS might add only metaphorical structure. The reading would also weaken if the case supplied a precise metaphysical account of grounding as a primitive relation not analyzable through frame-bound praxis. PMS can still map how such a theory functions in discourse, but it would not capture the ontology of the theory itself.

The reading would weaken if no further-ground demand is actually present. A chain is not a regress merely because multiple explanations exist. ∇ must be active as a demand for further grounding. Similarly, Λ weakens if there is no expectation of closure. In an infinitist frame, endless continuation may be acceptable rather than problematic. In a coherentist frame, circular or mutual support may be sufficient. In a pragmatic frame, inquiry may stop without pretending to finality. The PMS reading must therefore distinguish closure-seeking frames from non-closure-bearing frames.

The reading would become under-discriminating if it coded every unresolved explanation as the same PMS pattern. Grounding regress must be distinguished from ordinary incompleteness, empirical uncertainty, semantic ambiguity, causal complexity, institutional responsibility, or psychological doubt. The YAML’s discrimination tests require distinguishing closure-seeking regress from no-closure-required frames; dependency-asymmetry from social or moral asymmetry; recontextualization from rival formalization; and integration from premature closure.

The reading would become over-assimilative if it treated every rival response as PMS drift. Foundationalism is not automatically dogmatism. Coherentism is not automatically Σ-totalization. Infinitism is not automatically failure of closure. Deflation is not automatically avoidance. Pragmatic stopping is not automatically incomplete integration. PMS must compare rival readings by explicit criteria rather than absorbing them as internal deviations.

The reading would fail if weak dependency-direction were inflated into moral, social, diagnostic, or institutional Ω. Ground/grounded direction is not power. It does not assign authority, exposure, vulnerability, or obligation. Such conditions require a concrete scene. The same applies to Responsibility_R and Ψ: without an agent, institution, or self-binding trajectory, responsibility is not in scope.

The reading would also fail if PMS coherence were treated as proof. A full, elegant, internally consistent PMS formula does not solve grounding regress. It may make the problem more legible, but it does not establish metaphysical truth, epistemic warrant, or model superiority. The completed analysis explicitly marks coherence risk as high because this case fits PMS very smoothly.

The LOGIC overlay would weaken if non-closure were not the central issue. If a concrete grounding theory supplied a frame-internal stop with clear criteria, LOGIC_boundary_management might still apply but would not carry the same weight. If the case were primarily about effective capacity, consequence attribution, or institutional responsibility, another layer might be needed. If the case were used in governance or decision contexts, MIP could become relevant. But in the supplied abstract philosophical form, PMS-LOGIC is the relevant overlay.

The LOGIC overlay would overreach if it treated sustainable non-assertion as the final philosophical solution. PMS-LOGIC may say that non-closure must be carried where closure is not warranted. It may not say that all grounding must remain non-closed. It may not pre-empt foundationalist, coherentist, infinitist, pragmatic, semantic, or deflationary alternatives.

The overlay would also overreach if its drift catalogue became a verdict engine. Dogmatism / Σ-totalization, authority capture / Ω-inflation, moralism / pseudo-ought injection, nihilism / Λ-suppression, and technocratic reduction / □-overconstraint are risks. The completed analysis does not classify a concrete drift finding. No person, group, institution, theory, or tradition is diagnosed as dogmatic, nihilistic, moralizing, technocratic, or authority-capturing.

Operationalization remains unfulfilled. Stronger empirical or applied use would require specifying the type of grounding relation, the active frame, the expected closure condition, the admissible stopping rules, the relevant rival theories, the context of use, and any concrete roles or institutions involved. If the case were used to analyze a real debate, one would need evidence of who sets the frame, who can contest it, what counts as a ground, what consequences follow from accepting or rejecting a stop, and how revision remains possible.

Computational operationalization would require still more: indicators for Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, and Ψ; rules for distinguishing logical order from temporal sequence; thresholds for active versus weak Ω; criteria for identifying closure-expecting frames; and safeguards against treating unresolvedness as drift. ### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used as a metaphysical proof. It does not prove that ultimate grounds exist. It does not prove that they do not exist. It does not prove that all grounding is circular, infinite, pragmatic, semantic, or impossible. It does not settle the metaphysics of grounding.

It must not be used as an empirical proof. No empirical pattern, population, behavior, institution, historical development, or measurable recurrence has been established. The recurrence in the analysis is conceptual and structural.

It must not be used diagnostically. The presence of a grounding demand does not diagnose a person, school, tradition, or institution. It must not be used legally or forensically. The analysis supplies no evidence chain, standard of proof, culpability finding, liability assignment, or adjudicative conclusion.

It must not be used clinically. No mental state, trauma pattern, personality structure, symptom, diagnosis, or treatment implication is present.

It must not be used as implementation validation. A YAML artefact, formal signature, or operator chain is not an implementation, proof engine, AI architecture, or automated decision system. Any computational use would require separate design, calibration, evaluation, and accountability.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. The case’s fit with PMS does not prove PMS complete, final, uniquely superior, or universally adequate. Translation success is not final settlement.

It must not turn drift risk into drift finding. Dogmatism, authority capture, moralism, nihilism, and frame-overconstraint are named as risks in the artefact. They are not findings about any concrete actor or theory.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result is that **Grounding regress** is structurally legible as a closure-demand case in which a ground/grounded distinction activates a further-ground demand inside a grounding-relevant frame, while terminal closure remains absent or unwarranted. PMS Core maps this through Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, weak Ω, abstract Θ, conditional Φ, active Χ, weak Σ, and non-active Ψ. The primary Core formula remains bounded: a recurrent, framed, ordered demand under non-closure, with only weak dependency-direction and without self-binding as case content.

PMS-LOGIC adds the layer that this case specifically requires: logical boundary management. It shows why the regress must not be treated merely as a missing answer. It is a site where logic can clarify the problem but may also overreach. Logic can order, distinguish, and integrate. It cannot, by its own ordering success, become ultimate foundation. The overlay’s final signature — **LOGIC_boundary_management + NON_CLOSURE_stability**, with **PRE_MORAL_justification** active and **POST_MORAL_effects** not applicable — captures the case’s exact profile.

The examples contribute by making the abstract structure readable without turning examples into evidence. The seminar example shows how each answer can become the next object of grounding. The stopping-rule example shows how functional closure can be legitimate without becoming ultimate. The non-closure example shows why Λ must be carried rather than dismissed as meaningless. Together they demonstrate three different pressures around the same structure: recursive demand, functional stopping, and Λ-suppression. They demonstrate the analysis, but they do not prove it. They remain bounded vignettes, not empirical cases and not verdicts.

What remains unclaimed is substantial. The case does not decide metaphysical grounding. It does not refute foundationalism, coherentism, infinitism, pragmatism, semantic deflation, or formal logic. It does not diagnose any philosophical attitude. It does not assign responsibility. It does not derive an ought. It does not authorize institutional use. It does not validate PMS Base. It does not turn non-closure into nihilism, proof, or doctrine.

The boundary discipline is therefore the main achievement. PMS-AXIOM does not solve the regress by authority. It makes visible where closure is being demanded, what the frame requires, where Λ-residue persists, which operators are active or weak, which add-on risks are relevant, and which rival readings remain open. PMS-LOGIC then hardens the key methodological warning: coherence must not be laundered into foundation, and logical ordering must not become authority.

The final methodological takeaway is that grounding regress is best rendered here as a **structured non-closure artefact**. It can be mapped, stabilized, compared, and guarded against misuse, but not closed by the mere fact of mapping. The value of the analysis lies in preserving the difference between functional stopping and ultimate grounding. Where a frame legitimately stops, the stop should be named as frame-bound. Where a terminal ground is not supplied, Λ must remain visible.
