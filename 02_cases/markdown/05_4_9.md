### Why this case matters

Dogmatism is a structurally important case for AXIOM because it sits very close to the central danger that the whole project must avoid: the conversion of a coherent framework into a final authority. The case concerns a philosophical tendency to treat a concept, argument, framework, foundation, or account as finally settled, resistant to revision, and protected against unresolved uncertainty or rival interpretation. That tendency is not merely a familiar polemical charge. It names a deep closure-demand: the desire to end interpretive plurality, stabilize a foundation, absorb or neutralize disagreement, and present an account as no longer structurally exposed to revision.

For AXIOM, this makes the case especially delicate. A model that maps closure-demands can itself become dogmatic if it treats its mapping as exhaustive closure. The case therefore tests whether PMS and LOGIC can analyze closure without performing the same closure they analyze. A weak rendering would say simply that dogmatism is “bad closure,” “lack of openness,” or “rigid thinking.” The completed analyses do something more disciplined. They treat dogmatism as a configuration in which Difference Δ, closure-drive ∇, Frame □, Non-Event Λ, Attractor Α, Temporality Θ, Recontextualization Φ, Distance Χ, and Integration Σ interact in a way that can mimic mature coherence while actually suppressing uncertainty and rival framing.

The case belongs in AXIOM because AXIOM is a cartographic and indexing paper. It does not resolve classical philosophical problems by authority; it maps the structural demands for closure that such problems generate. Dogmatism is one of the clearest cases where closure itself becomes the issue. It asks not only whether an argument is valid or a concept is stable, but whether a framework has become protected against the very uncertainty that should remain visible. AXIOM needs this case because it shows how a philosophical system, explanatory structure, or conceptual foundation can become internally coherent while still overreaching.

The structural difficulty lies in avoiding two opposite errors. The first error is over-capture: treating every strong commitment, stable doctrine, principled refusal, or scope-bound closure as dogmatism. PMS must not automatically translate resistance, non-fit, disagreement, rival framing, refusal of revision, or absence of uptake into failed Recontextualization Φ or deficient Integration Σ. A framework may reject a rival interpretation for good internal reasons. A discipline may use axioms without pretending to metaphysical finality. A legal, mathematical, or methodological system may require operational closure without claiming exhaustive truth. These possibilities must remain open.

The second error is under-discrimination: treating dogmatism so broadly that the analysis becomes too smooth. If every closure-demand can be described in PMS terms, PMS still has to show why this specific case differs from ordinary coherence, justified stabilization, or provisional settlement. Dogmatism / Σ-Totalization is therefore valuable because it forces the analysis to distinguish mature Σ from pseudo-Σ, reflective Χ from mere neutrality, Φ-capable stability from preemptive exclusion, and Λ-bearing non-closure from suppressed uncertainty.

The case is not being solved metaphysically. PMS does not decide whether a dogmatic doctrine is true or false in itself. Nor does LOGIC prove that final foundations are impossible in every possible sense. The case is also not empirical: no observed person, institution, dialogue, text, or historical episode is supplied. It is not clinical or diagnostic: no psychological rigidity, pathology, temperament, motive, or personality type is inferred. It is not legal or forensic: no culpability, liability, evidence chain, or adjudicative standard is involved. It is not implementation validation: the YAML representation is not a detector of dogmatism. It is not PMS Base validation: a successful mapping of dogmatism does not prove PMS complete, final, or superior to rival formalisms.

The philosophical importance of the case is precisely that it keeps closure visible as a problem without replacing it with anti-closure dogma. The analysis does not say that closure is always illegitimate. It says that closure becomes structurally risky when it denies its own limits, suppresses Λ, blocks Φ, weakens Χ, and presents claimed Σ as final integration.

### Scene and frame structure

The scene is conceptual rather than concrete. There is no specified actor, institution, dialogue, author, doctrinal community, legal setting, classroom, publication, governance structure, or implementation environment. This matters because PMS is scene-sensitive. When the scene is abstract, the analysis can map structural possibilities, but it cannot assign scene-level status to responsibility, action, asymmetry, or self-binding.

The frame □ is the conceptual or argumentative framework that treats something as settled: a concept, argument, foundation, doctrine, theory, or account. Inside the frame are rules of admissibility: what counts as a legitimate objection, what counts as revision, what kind of uncertainty is recognized, what rival interpretations may enter, and what is treated as already resolved. Outside the frame are rival interpretations, unresolved uncertainties, objections, possible recontextualizations, and alternative formalisms that the settled account may exclude, contain, or delegitimize.

The protected constraints are not empirical facts. They are application constraints. The analysis must preserve reversibility, Distance Χ, and Dignity-in-Practice. It must not use dogmatism as a label for persons. It must not treat frame stability as automatically suspect. It must not treat PMS coherence as proof that PMS has captured the case completely. It must not turn LOGIC into an authority that condemns logic.

Roles are not concretely present. The YAML identifies possible roles only conditionally. A future scene might include a framework-holder, critic, institution, teacher, gatekeeper, subordinate, audience, or affected party. But none of these is supplied by the case material. Therefore Ω remains weak unless a later scene introduces power, exposure, capacity, obligation, or vulnerability gradients.

Temporality Θ is implied but not concretely evidenced. Dogmatism as “resistant to revision” suggests persistence across time, but the case gives no actual sequence. There is no record of repeated refusals, no history of engagement with objections, no developmental arc, and no observable stabilization process. The analysis can say that Θ would matter, and that dogmatism usually requires more than a single assertion, but it cannot claim a fully established temporal trajectory.

The reversibility window remains open. Because the case is conceptual and not scene-bound, all major operator readings remain revisable. A later text or scene could show explicit fallibility, genuine engagement with rivals, named revision conditions, legitimate scope-bound closure, or active recontextualization. Such evidence would weaken the dogmatism reading.

Exposure and asymmetry conditions are under-specified. If a dogmatic frame were imposed by an institution, authority, classroom, doctrine, legal body, or governance system, Ω could become active. In the current case, however, no authority relation is supplied. Mere disagreement, confidence, or refusal of a rival frame does not establish Ω.

This distinction matters for the whole article. A concrete dogmatism case would require a scene packet: who speaks, who can contest, what frame governs admissibility, what revision was expected, what non-event occurred, what costs were distributed, what time horizon applies, and what bindings became durable. The current article does not have that. It has a conceptual frame with conditional structural projections. The correct result is not weaker prose, but stronger boundary discipline.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading proceeds through the eleven PMS operators in order. This order matters because later operators cannot be used cheaply. The case is not simply “Σ is bad.” The case is about how a structure can claim integration while weakening the conditions that would make integration valid.

**Δ — Difference** is active. Dogmatism becomes legible only because distinctions are drawn: settled versus unsettled, final versus revisable, admissible versus inadmissible, foundation versus challenge, accepted account versus rival interpretation, integration versus forced closure. These distinctions make the case analyzable. Without them, there would be no structural object for PMS to map.

The calibration risk is that PMS may impose a crude binary too quickly. Not every stable distinction is dogmatic. A framework can distinguish valid from invalid objections without thereby suppressing uncertainty. A theory can declare what falls inside or outside its scope without claiming global authority. Δ supplies the field of contrast; it does not yet diagnose overclosure.

**∇ — Impulse** is active. The case includes directional pressure toward closure, stabilization, protection, and reduction of uncertainty. This is not a psychological claim about fear, anxiety, or need for certainty. It is a structural reading of the tendency to move from contestability toward settlement.

The overclaim would be to treat every movement toward clarity as dogmatic closure. In many contexts, closure is necessary for coordination. Arguments must sometimes terminate; concepts must sometimes stabilize; institutions must sometimes decide. The relevant ∇ in this case is not mere desire for order, but the drive to protect a settled account against unresolved uncertainty or rival interpretation.

**□ — Frame** is active and central. Dogmatism depends on a frame that determines admissibility. The frame says what counts as a relevant objection, what kind of revision is allowed, and what rival interpretations may enter. A dogmatic frame is not merely a frame with rules. All inquiry requires framing. The dogmatic risk appears when the frame protects itself against recontextualization by making rival frames inadmissible before engagement.

The overclaim would be to treat any explicit frame-boundary as closure abuse. PMS must distinguish declared scope from immunizing enclosure. A framework that says “this question is outside my present scope” is not automatically dogmatic. A framework that says “no rival frame can count as a legitimate context of reinterpretation” moves closer to the case pattern.

**Λ — Non-Event** is active but calibration-dependent. Dogmatism concerns unresolved uncertainty and missing revision events. Expected reconsideration, answerability, acknowledgment of limits, or engagement with rivals may fail to occur. Yet Λ must be handled carefully. Absence of revision is not automatically a meaningful non-event. It becomes Λ only where the frame itself creates an expectation of revision, reply, openness, or uncertainty-handling.

If a framework is explicitly axiomatic, operationally scoped, or has already considered the objection, absence of further revision may not indicate dogmatism. The overclaim would be to treat any unanswered rival as suppressed Λ. The correct question is: within this frame, was there an expected event of revision, reply, acknowledgment, or openness that failed to occur? If not, Λ cannot be cheaply activated.

**Α — Attractor** is weak-to-active as a tendency. Dogmatism is described as a tendency, which implies possible recurrence or stabilization: repeated protection of the frame, recurrent pre-containment of objections, path-dependent closure. But no concrete recurrence is supplied. Therefore Α remains structurally plausible rather than empirically established.

The overclaim would be to treat a single statement of certainty as a stable attractor. Α needs recurrence, repeated interaction, or stabilized patterning. In the current conceptual case, the language of tendency makes Α relevant, but not confirmed.

**Ω — Asymmetry** is weak and conditional. It is not established by the abstract material. Dogmatism becomes Ω-relevant if the protected framework operates through authority, teaching, gatekeeping, institutional power, dependency, vulnerability, or enforcement. In such cases, one position may impose closure while others carry the cost of exclusion or misrecognition.

But mere firmness, disagreement, or doctrinal confidence does not establish Ω. The overclaim would be to moralize dogmatism by assuming domination wherever there is certainty. PMS treats Ω as structural imbalance of power, exposure, capacity, or obligation. Such imbalance must be shown, not inferred from closure-language alone.

**Θ — Temporality** is active but scene-thin. Resistance to revision implies persistence across time and challenge, but the material gives no sequence. Dogmatism usually cannot be identified from a single moment; it requires some temporal profile of maintained closure, repeated refusal, or durable protection.

The calibration issue is to distinguish actual diachronic persistence from a static description of finality. A claim may sound final in one formulation but still be revisable in practice. The overclaim would be to infer a full developmental trajectory without evidence. In this case, Θ remains relevant because resistance implies time, but the article cannot claim a concrete temporal arc.

**Φ — Recontextualization** is major but weakly established. Dogmatism centrally concerns inhibited, refused, or pre-contained transformation of an existing account when placed into a rival frame, new context, objection, or unresolved uncertainty. The decisive question is whether rival framing is admissible as possible reinterpretation or excluded in advance by the already settled frame.

But refusal of recontextualization is not automatically failure. A rival frame may be incoherent, irrelevant, methodologically out of scope, or already addressed. PMS must therefore preserve the possibility of legitimate non-recontextualization. The overclaim would be to convert every resistance to reframing into incomplete development.

Φ is one of the places where the case is most analytically delicate. A dogmatic structure blocks rival framing not merely by rejecting a conclusion, but by preventing the rival frame from functioning as a possible context of reinterpretation. That is different from arguing against a rival frame. The former pre-contains; the latter engages.

**Χ — Distance** is a major calibration point. In the dogmatic pattern, Χ may be weak because the frame is fused with finality and protected from reflective suspension. Distance is the capacity to hold an account as bounded, revisable, accountable to uncertainty, and open to rival formalization without collapsing into total acceptance or total rejection.

But the analysis itself must also maintain Χ. It must not become a dogmatic capture of dogmatism. The overclaim would be to force a verdict, treat PMS as exhaustive, or present structural coherence as final authority. If a case shows explicit reflective distance toward its own foundations, the low-Χ reading must be weakened.

Χ is therefore both an object-level and method-level requirement. Object-level: does the framework maintain distance from its own closure? Method-level: does this article maintain distance from its own PMS/LOGIC rendering? The completed analysis requires both.

**Σ — Integration** is the central risk-point. Dogmatism / Σ-Totalization is not an assertion that mature PMS Integration is present. It is a case label for claimed integration as final closure. A framework may treat conflicts, rival readings, and uncertainties as already synthesized when they have actually been excluded, neutralized, pre-contained, or declared irrelevant.

Mature Σ depends on Χ and Φ. If Χ is weak and Φ is blocked, then apparent coherence may be pseudo-Σ, not valid Integration. The calibration question is whether the account preserves unresolved tension inside a coherent whole or merely suppresses it. PMS coherence is insufficient by itself. A smooth formula may still under-discriminate the philosophical case.

The central PMS Core result lies here: dogmatism is not the same as having an integrated theory. It is a closure-form in which Integration is over-claimed or totalized. The appearance of coherence may be precisely what makes the structure difficult to detect.

**Ψ — Self-Binding** is weak and conditional. It applies only if the settled account becomes durable self-binding, identity-like commitment, stable normativity, or responsibility-as-self-commitment for an agent, group, institution, or analytic stance. The abstract case does not establish such a subject or self-model.

Ψ is nevertheless relevant as an application discipline: the PMS analysis must bind itself to reversibility, Distance Χ, non-capture, and Dignity-in-Practice rather than binding itself to a totalizing verdict. The overclaim would be to attribute selfhood, subjectivity, or identity to an abstract concept or to infer doctrinal pathology from durable commitment alone.

The resulting PMS Core reading is that dogmatism is best modeled as claimed Integration under weakened Distance and blocked or pre-contained Recontextualization, stabilized around a possible closure-attractor inside a frame that contains uncertainty and rival interpretation as managed difference. Ω and Ψ remain conditional; Θ and Α remain scene-thin; Λ remains central but must not be inflated into automatic failure.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The dependency discipline is essential because dogmatism is a case where later operators are especially tempting. One might want to jump directly from a visible closure-demand to Σ-totalization, responsibility failure, or self-binding. The completed analysis prevents that shortcut.

Later operators cannot be used cheaply. Σ depends on Χ and Φ. If the account has not preserved reflective distance and admissible recontextualization, then a claim of mature Integration is not warranted. In this case, the safer formulation is “claimed Σ,” “pseudo-Σ,” or “closure under the sign of Σ.” Calling the case Σ-Totalization must not imply that PMS Σ has actually been achieved.

Ψ is even more conditional. It depends on Σ, Θ, and Χ. Without a concrete subject, institution, or stable self-model, Ψ cannot be asserted as a case finding. The abstract tendency of dogmatism does not by itself establish self-binding. Ψ can be used as a caution about how a framework might bind itself to finality over time, but that remains a conditional later-scene analysis.

Coherence is not proof. This is one of the strongest points of the analysis. A dogmatic framework may be highly coherent. It may organize its own terms elegantly, respond to many objections internally, and stabilize a powerful explanatory structure. PMS and LOGIC both warn that coherence can become a substitute for openness. Coherence_C may be active as apparent stabilization, while mature Σ remains unproven.

Non-capture is not failure. If PMS cannot fully capture a certain kind of theological, axiomatic, existential, mathematical, or methodological closure, that does not automatically count against the case or the rival framework. Non-capture may mark a real boundary of PMS. The analysis therefore preserves cases where closure is justified, where revision is legitimately out of scope, or where another formalism may discriminate better.

PMS must not become a closure machine. This is the reflexive danger of the case. If PMS analyzes dogmatism by absorbing every resistance, rival reading, or absence into its own drift vocabulary, it would reproduce the dogmatic structure. The analysis therefore keeps falsifiers, rival readings, and operationalization requirements explicit. It marks weak operators as weak instead of forcing the full chain.

Invalid dependency shortcuts include Δ/∇ directly to Ψ, where a perceived closure-pressure becomes irreversible labeling; □ directly to Σ, where a frame’s internal coherence is treated as mature integration; Λ directly to failure, where unresolved absence is treated as defect; Ω directly to blame, where asymmetry is moralized; and LOGIC reduced signatures directly to PMS Base, where overlay shorthand is mistaken for a new dependency path.

Another invalid shortcut would be to treat resistance itself as drift. A framework may resist a rival interpretation for good reason. It may refuse a frame because that frame is irrelevant to its declared purpose. It may preserve a boundary because without that boundary the analysis loses its object. PMS must not turn every refusal into blocked Φ.

A further invalid shortcut would be to treat non-assignment as negative evaluation. If Responsibility_R is not applicable, that does not mean the case is irresponsible. If Action_E is under-specified, that does not mean the case fails to act. If Ω is weak, that does not mean asymmetry is absent in all possible scenes. It means the supplied material does not warrant stronger coding.

The dependency discipline also preserves the distinction between PMS Core and PMS-LOGIC. LOGIC may focus on □, Λ, Χ, and Σ, but it cannot make those operators independent of their PMS dependencies. Reduced signatures are readability aids, not replacements for the full operator grammar. This is crucial in a case about dogmatism: an overlay that silently became a new base grammar would be an instance of the very totalization it is meant to prevent.

### Derived structures

The derived structures are formula-derived PMS constructs. They are not primitives, diagnoses, scores, moral rankings, or independent axioms. Their status in this case depends on whether the relevant operator formulas are actually supported by the conceptual material.

**Awareness_A** is weak. Its PMS formula is Θ + □ + Δ: sustained framed differentiation across time. In this case, the distinctions are clear: finality versus revisability, accepted frame versus rival interpretation, uncertainty versus closure. The frame is also clear at the conceptual level. But Θ is only implied, not evidenced. Awareness_A therefore appears as structural awareness of a conceptual field, not phenomenological consciousness or empirical awareness.

This matters because “awareness” can easily be overread. PMS Awareness_A does not mean lived self-awareness, insight, consciousness, or reflective virtue. It means a sustained framed differentiation. In the current case, the structure distinguishes important terms, but no subject’s awareness is assessed. The correct status is weak-to-active as a structural projection, not a person-level finding.

**Coherence_C** is active, but this is not a compliment and not a proof. Its PMS formula is Θ + Λ + □ + ∇: temporally stabilized structuring of impulse and expectation within a frame. Dogmatism may produce strong coherence by organizing closure-drive, frame rules, absence of revision, and persistence into a stable account. This makes Coherence_C structurally visible.

But the YAML repeatedly warns that coherence does not establish truth, adequacy, mature Integration, or non-dogmatism. Coherence may be precisely how totalization hides. A coherent dogmatic structure can be more dangerous analytically than an obviously incoherent one, because it may appear to have integrated everything when it has actually excluded or pre-contained what would challenge it.

**Responsibility_R** is not applicable in the current case. Its formula is Ψ + Φ + Θ + Ω. The abstract case lacks established self-binding, concrete recontextualization obligations, temporal sequence, and asymmetry. Responsibility_R would require a scene in which a role, agent, group, or institution is asymmetrically obligated across time and bound to handle recontextualization responsibly. No such scene is supplied.

Non-assignment here is not a low score; it is correct dependency discipline. The analysis does not say that dogmatism is irresponsible. It says that the present material does not warrant Responsibility_R. A future scene may change that if authority, exposure, durable commitment, and recontextualization obligations are supplied.

**Action_E** is under-specified. Its formula is Σ + Θ + ∇: integrated realization of directedness across time. The case describes a tendency, not an enactment. If dogmatism were enacted through exclusion, censorship, refusal, pedagogy, institutional gatekeeping, or doctrine enforcement, Action_E could become analyzable. In the abstract case, however, there is no concrete action trajectory.

This under-specification is important. It prevents the analysis from treating conceptual closure as if it had already become action. A dogmatic tendency may remain theoretical, rhetorical, institutional, pedagogical, or practical depending on the scene. Without a scene, Action_E remains under-specified.

**Dignity_in_Practice_D** functions primarily as an application constraint. Its formula is Ψ + Χ + Ω. Since Ω and Ψ are not established as case findings, D is not assigned as a scene-level result. Instead, it constrains how the analysis may be used. The case must not rank persons, shame dogmatic subjects, strip standing from communities, or convert conceptual closure into moral or ontological inferiority.

Dignity-in-Practice is praxeological, not ontological. It does not claim that some persons have more dignity than others. It regulates the handling of asymmetry and self-binding in application. Here, D prevents the article from becoming a tribunal.

**SELF_FIXPOINT** is analytic-only. Its full PMS chain is Ψ ∘ Σ ∘ Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ω ∘ Α ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ. The case does not establish literal subjectivity, identity, or selfhood. SELF_FIXPOINT may become relevant if a concrete agent, group, institution, or analytic stance binds itself to a settled account across time. In this conceptual case, it remains a derived structure and a reflexive warning: the PMS analysis itself must not bind itself into final closure.

The derived-structure result reinforces the main boundary discipline. Awareness_A and Coherence_C are structurally visible, but they do not prove adequacy. Responsibility_R and Action_E are not established. Dignity-in-Practice functions as constraint, not ranking. SELF_FIXPOINT remains analytic-only. The derived structures therefore do not upgrade the case into a stronger claim; they refine what can and cannot be said.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS drift-pattern layer remains deliberately restrained. The completed analysis does not classify Dogmatism / Σ-Totalization as a concrete drift event in a scene. It identifies drift-pattern relevance only where the abstract case structurally resembles certain PMS risks. This distinction is important: a drift pattern is not a diagnosis, not a person-type, not a moral verdict, not a defect score, and not a finding of immaturity. It is a conditional structural risk that becomes active only if the relevant operator configuration is actually supplied.

The first drift pattern considered is **AD_A>>E**, excessive distance between Awareness and Enactment. In the completed YAML, this pattern is marked as possible but not entailed. The case might resemble AD_A>>E if a dogmatic structure produced extensive awareness, evaluative framing, or conceptual judgment while suppressing responsive action, revision, or enactment. For example, a framework might recognize many distinctions, objections, and interpretive tensions, but repeatedly avoid the practical act of revising its own commitments. In such a later concrete scene, awareness could become over-expanded while action is suppressed or chronically delayed.

But the current case does not provide that scene. It does not describe an actor, institution, text sequence, decision process, or action history. There is no concrete action axis, no demonstrated delay, no observed refusal, and no stable asymmetrical relation. For that reason, the YAML correctly keeps both AD_A>>E axis effects under-specified: the awareness axis is not coded as over-expanded, and the action axis is not coded as suppressed or chronically delayed. The risk remains analytically visible, but not assigned.

This matters because dogmatism can easily invite moralized interpretation. One might be tempted to say that a dogmatic framework “knows better but refuses to act.” PMS does not permit that inference from the abstract material. Such a claim would require evidence that the framework had recognized alternatives, had capacity to revise, had a temporal opportunity to enact revision, and nevertheless stabilized non-enactment under identifiable conditions. None of that is supplied. Under-specification is therefore not a weakness in the analysis; it is the correct result.

The second drift pattern considered is **AD_Sigma_low**, fragmented integration or under-integration. In PMS terms, AD_Sigma_low becomes relevant where recontextualization outpaces integration: frames shift frequently, but Σ fails to consolidate coherent praxis. That is not the primary structure of this case. Dogmatism / Σ-Totalization is closer to the inverse risk. The problem is not that Φ is too active and Σ too weak. The problem is that Φ may be blocked or pre-contained while Σ is claimed too strongly. The dogmatic structure does not fail primarily by unstable recontextualization; it fails, if it fails, by over-claiming integration and suppressing the very recontextualization that would test it.

For this reason, AD_Sigma_low is not applicable in the present conceptual case. This non-assignment is analytically important. It prevents PMS from treating every problematic relation to Integration Σ as the same kind of drift. Some failures involve too little integration after excessive reframing. Dogmatism, by contrast, involves a closure-form that may appear highly integrated while denying the conditions of valid integration. The correct contrast is not “low Σ” but “claimed Σ without sufficient Χ and Φ.”

The related coherence risk is high. Dogmatism is structurally close to PMS’s own danger-zone because it concerns closure, totalization, and framework-protection. If PMS maps the case too smoothly, the analysis can become performatively dogmatic: it can use its own coherence as proof of capture. The completed analysis therefore names the risk that PMS coherence may become too smooth or under-discriminating. A well-formed operator chain does not prove the case is exhausted. A coherent PMS derivation must still show discriminative force against rival readings.

The under-discrimination risk is also high. The analysis must separate dogmatism from firm commitment, justified closure, axiomatic stipulation, methodological boundary-setting, disciplinary focus, legal finality, pedagogical simplification, and principled refusal of weak objections. If PMS cannot preserve those distinctions, its reading would become too broad.

The final PMS drift-pattern result is therefore restrained: **AD_A>>E is possible but not entailed; AD_Sigma_low is not applicable as the main pattern; the central risk is instead pseudo-Σ or Σ-totalization, later sharpened by the LOGIC overlay.** The Core layer does not classify a concrete drift finding. It identifies the structural risk that a framework may treat apparent coherence as mature Integration while weakening Distance and blocking Recontextualization.

### Add-on / overlay reading

The relevant add-on is **PMS-LOGIC**. It is applied as an overlay/application profile only. PMS.yaml remains the sole PMS Base reference. PMS-LOGIC does not redefine PMS operators, alter PMS dependencies, introduce new PMS axioms, or rewrite the PMS Core pass. Its reduced signatures are overlay shorthand: they mark analytic focus, not a new dependency path.

The add-on is justified because the case is not only about closure in general. The Core layer and the LOGIC overlay interact by division of labour.

PMS Core supplies the operator grammar. It determines what Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, and Ψ mean; it preserves their order and dependencies; it defines the derived structures; and it supplies the application discipline of Distance, reversibility, and Dignity-in-Practice. PMS-LOGIC sharpens the case by isolating the specifically logical risk: coherence or explanation becoming authority. It adds boundary-management vocabulary, reduced signatures, non-closure stability, failure-mode constraints, and drift catalogue visibility. It clarifies that the case is not merely about rigidity, but about a reasoning structure that treats its own ordering as final settlement.

What remains PMS Core is the operator structure. LOGIC does not make Λ a new kind of absence, does not make Σ a LOGIC-specific operator, does not make Χ optional, and does not redefine Ψ as responsibility in the moral sense. It cannot shortcut dependencies. It cannot turn reduced signatures into replacement formulas. It cannot introduce morality as an operator. It cannot turn post-moral readability into a normative layer.

What LOGIC sharpens is the distinction between legitimate stabilization and illegitimate totalization. PMS Core can say that Σ is risky when Χ and Φ are weak. LOGIC says more specifically that logic remains valid as ordering, but becomes unstable when it begins to function as final authority. The overlay therefore gives the Core reading more discriminative force in cases of justification, argument, truth, explanation, foundation, and closure-demand.

Non-closure remains visible across both layers. PMS Core marks Λ as structured absence or missing expected revision within a frame. LOGIC intensifies this by treating Λ as logical boundary: not defect, not nihilism, not ignorance, not metaphysical pointer, but the place where valid ordering must stop short of totalization. The shared result is strong but bounded: dogmatism is structurally legible as a closure-risk around claimed Σ, but the reading itself remains reversible and non-totalizing.

The interaction therefore does not escalate the case into a tribunal. It adds specificity without adding authority. PMS Core maps the operator structure. PMS-LOGIC names the specific reasoning failure-mode: logical ordering converted into final closure. MIP, AHP, and QC remain inactive because the case has not been moved into downstream governance, hardening, scoring, audit, or implementation use.

### Example 1 — The Closed Foundational Seminar

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** after “Scene and frame structure” or after the Φ/Χ/Σ discussion in “PMS Core structural reading.”

A philosophy seminar is organized around a foundational thesis: a certain account of justification is said to provide the final answer to skepticism. The seminar leader presents the thesis as internally coherent, historically sophisticated, and able to absorb all major objections. Students are invited to ask questions, but when objections arise, they are repeatedly classified as misunderstandings of the thesis, failures to grasp its proper frame, or attempts to import an illegitimate rival vocabulary. Over time, the seminar develops a recognizable pattern: questions are welcomed formally, but every serious rival framing is returned to the original account as already resolved.

The vignette is bounded. It does not claim that the seminar leader is psychologically dogmatic, morally deficient, clinically rigid, or intentionally manipulative. It also does not claim that the foundational thesis is false. The example concerns only the structure by which a frame treats itself as already complete.

What the example demonstrates is the difference between **strong commitment** and **Σ-totalization**. A strong foundational thesis may legitimately set terms, define scope, and reject weak objections. The dogmatism risk appears only when the frame makes rival interpretation inadmissible in advance while presenting its own coherence as final settlement. The structural issue is not that the account has a frame, but that the frame is protected against recontextualization.

In PMS Core terms, Δ appears in the distinction between accepted thesis and rival objection, settled foundation and unresolved challenge. ∇ appears as the pressure to preserve closure: the seminar wants the foundational account to remain complete. □ is the seminar’s governing frame: what counts as an admissible question, what counts as misunderstanding, and what counts as successful reply. Λ appears where an expected revision-event fails to occur. Students may expect that a serious objection could alter the frame, but the structure repeatedly converts objection into already-contained difference. Α appears if this pattern stabilizes across sessions. Θ is present only if the recurrence is temporal and not merely a single exchange. Ω may become relevant if the seminar leader controls evaluation, access, grades, or disciplinary legitimacy, but that must be shown rather than assumed.

Φ is the central test. A non-dogmatic seminar can say: “This rival frame is not persuasive, and here is why.” A dogmatic structure says, in effect: “No rival frame can count as a frame from which this account may be reinterpreted.” Χ is the second test. Does the seminar preserve reflective distance from its own foundation, or does it fuse the thesis with finality? Σ is the third test. Does the account genuinely integrate objections while preserving their force, or does it merely classify them as already answered? Ψ would remain conditional unless the seminar or institution binds itself durably to this closure as identity, doctrine, or policy.

The PMS-LOGIC overlay sharpens the example. The seminar’s logic may be genuinely strong: the thesis may be coherent and internally disciplined. LOGIC does not treat this as failure. The risk appears when coherence becomes authority. The active LOGIC hook is **LOGIC_boundary_management**: □, Λ, Χ, and Σ must remain distinguishable. If unresolved rival interpretation persists, Λ must be carried rather than erased. The relevant drift risk is `dogmatism_sigma_totalization`, but only as a risk unless the pattern is sufficiently documented. `authority_capture_omega_inflation` would become relevant only if the seminar’s authority structure converts the thesis into enforceable legitimacy.

MIP, AHP, and QC are not triggered by the vignette as stated. If the seminar’s framework became part of hiring, grading, publication access, or institutional policy, MIP might become relevant as downstream governance reading. AHP or QC would be relevant only if an analysis artefact were being hardened, audited, or prepared for institutional use.

The forbidden claims are clear. The example must not be used to diagnose the seminar leader, rank the students, declare the thesis false, or classify a philosophical school as pathological. It also must not be used to say that all foundational projects are dogmatic.

The non-capture note is essential: some foundations are explicitly methodological, axiomatic, or scope-bound. A seminar may reject rival frames for legitimate reasons. PMS and LOGIC capture the closure-risk only where rival framing is structurally pre-contained and where coherence is treated as final authority.

The article takeaway is that dogmatism does not consist in having a foundation. It consists in converting the foundation’s internal coherence into immunity from recontextualization while refusing to carry the remaining Λ.

### Example 2 — The Model That Became the Field

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** in the PMS-LOGIC overlay section, after “logical boundary management” or after the discussion of failure-mode constraints FMC2 and FMC3.

A research group develops a powerful explanatory model for a complex domain. The model performs well across several benchmark problems. It produces clean categories, stable predictions, and an elegant account of previously scattered phenomena. Over time, the model becomes the default language of the group. New observations are interpreted through it; anomalies are treated as measurement noise, implementation failure, or evidence that the observer has not understood the model’s deeper structure. When a rival model explains some edge cases better, the group does not compare frames. Instead, it says that the rival model is not really addressing the phenomenon.

The example is bounded as a structural vignette. It does not claim that the model is false, that the researchers are dishonest, that the field is corrupt, or that the group’s confidence is irrational. It also does not claim that benchmarks are illegitimate. The point is narrower: a successful explanatory frame may begin to function as if its success authorized final closure.

What the example demonstrates is the LOGIC distinction between **ordering power** and **authority over meaning**. The model’s coherence is real within its frame. Its explanatory success may be practically valuable. But PMS-LOGIC asks whether that success is being converted into the authority to decide in advance what rival frames are allowed to mean.

PMS Core hooks are visible throughout. Δ appears in the distinction between modeled and unmodeled phenomena, benchmark success and edge-case misfit, accepted model and rival model. ∇ appears as the pressure to preserve the model’s explanatory unity. □ is the modeling frame: the data regime, benchmarks, admissible variables, tolerated errors, and interpretation rules. Λ appears as unmodeled remainder: edge cases, anomalous observations, missing variables, or unresolved rival explanations. Α appears as the model becomes an attractor: repeated success stabilizes its use as default. Θ appears as the model’s authority accumulates over time through publications, infrastructure, training, and shared vocabulary. Ω may become relevant if senior researchers, journals, funders, or institutions enforce the model’s status. Φ is tested when the model is placed into a rival frame. Χ is tested by whether the group can step back from the model’s success and still name its limits. Σ is at risk when model coherence is treated as complete integration rather than provisional coordination. Ψ would become relevant if the field binds itself to the model as disciplinary identity or institutional standard.

The LOGIC overlay is central here. The active signature is **LOGIC_boundary_management**: the model may order a domain, but unresolved Λ must remain visible. The second active signature is **NON_CLOSURE_stability**: a mature research practice can stabilize around acknowledged limits without forcing closure. The main drift risk is `dogmatism_sigma_totalization`, where Σ is forced into total closure and the model’s coherence becomes final truth. A secondary risk is `technocratic_reduction_frame_overconstraint`, where the frame is mistaken for the whole field. `authority_capture_omega_inflation` remains conditional unless actual authority gradients are shown.

FMC2 is directly illustrated: forced closure where Λ persists produces structural instability. The instability does not come from the model having limits. Every model has limits. It comes from refusing to carry those limits and treating unresolved edge cases as if they were already resolved by the frame. FMC3 is also visible: explanatory power becomes sense-making authority. The model may explain much, but explanatory success does not automatically authorize it to define every rival account as irrelevant.

MIP, AHP, and QC remain inactive in the basic example. If the model were used for institutional classification, public policy, clinical triage, legal adjudication, or automated decision-making, MIP could become relevant as a governance layer. AHP or QC would be appropriate only if the analysis artefact itself required hardening, review, or audit discipline. Nothing in the vignette alone warrants those escalations.

The forbidden claims must remain explicit. The example must not be used to attack empirical modeling, reject benchmarks, accuse researchers of bad faith, or claim that all dominant paradigms are dogmatic. It must not infer motives. It must not turn model success into guilt, nor model limitation into failure.

The non-capture note is especially important. Some models legitimately exclude variables because of scope. Some rival accounts are weak. Some benchmarks are appropriate for the question asked. PMS-LOGIC captures the dogmatism risk only when frame-bound success is converted into frame-independent authority.

The article takeaway is that LOGIC does not oppose successful explanation. It protects explanation by preventing success, coherence, and integration from being laundered into final closure.

### Example 3 — The Axiom That Stayed Honest

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** in “Non-capture and rival readings” or immediately before “Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions.”

A theorist proposes an axiomatic framework for analyzing a class of problems. The framework begins by declaring its primitives, scope, admissible transformations, and intended output type. It does not claim to describe all of reality. It does not claim that rival frameworks are meaningless. It states that within this frame certain questions can be stabilized, but that other questions remain outside the system’s warrant. When challenged by a rival approach, the theorist does not immediately absorb the rival into the original frame. Instead, they distinguish three possibilities: the rival may expose a genuine limit, may operate under another frame, or may show that the original framework needs revision.

This example is included because it looks superficially close to dogmatism but should not automatically be coded as dogmatic. It is a guardrail example. The framework uses axioms, declares closure conditions, and rejects some questions as out of scope. Yet those features alone do not establish Σ-totalization.

The bounded vignette demonstrates the falsifier side of the case. A framework can be strong, formal, and closure-producing without being dogmatic if it preserves scope, revision, non-capture, and reflective distance. The presence of axioms does not equal dogmatism. The presence of boundaries does not equal suppression. The refusal to answer every question does not equal blocked Φ.

PMS Core hooks clarify the difference. Δ appears in the distinction between inside-frame and outside-frame questions. ∇ appears as the pressure to stabilize analysis. □ is explicit rather than hidden: the framework declares its primitives, scope, and admissibility rules. Λ is preserved because the framework names what it cannot settle. Α may stabilize through repeated use, but that stabilization does not become dogmatic unless it suppresses its own limits. Ω is not active unless the framework is imposed through authority or contest is blocked. Θ may appear if the framework persists over time, but persistence alone is not dogmatism. Φ remains possible because rival frames can recontextualize the original. Χ is maintained because the framework can step back from itself and mark its own boundary. Σ may be valid if tensions are integrated under declared scope while remaining open to revision. Ψ would be legitimate only if the framework binds itself to its own limits and revision discipline, not merely to finality.

The LOGIC overlay is also relevant, but in a negative or falsifying way. The framework practices **LOGIC_boundary_management** without collapsing into totalization. It uses logic as ordering, not as final authority. It carries Λ instead of erasing it. It allows non-closure to remain stable. It therefore does not activate `dogmatism_sigma_totalization` as a finding. At most, the risk remains general: any axiomatic framework can become dogmatic if it later treats its own frame as exhaustive.

This example also illustrates why the overlay must not become anti-axiomatic. PMS-LOGIC does not say that axioms are suspicious. It says that axioms become structurally risky when their declared scope is forgotten and when their coherence is converted into authority over rival frames. In the vignette, the axiom system remains honest because it distinguishes internal closure from final closure.

MIP, AHP, and QC are not triggered. If the axiomatic framework were used to govern decisions, classify persons, allocate resources, or produce public institutional outputs, additional layers might become relevant. But as a theoretical framework with explicit scope and revision boundaries, it remains within conceptual analysis.

The forbidden claims are crucial. The example must not be used to declare the framework superior, metaphysically true, empirically validated, or immune to critique. It also must not be used to say that all non-dogmatic systems are safe in application. A scope-disciplined framework can still be misused later under Ω, Θ, and Ψ.

The non-capture note is the point of the example. PMS and LOGIC must allow for legitimate closure. They must allow an account to say, “This is what can be settled here, and this is what remains outside.” If the analysis cannot distinguish this from dogmatism, the analysis itself becomes under-discriminating.

The article takeaway is that the opposite of dogmatism is not endless openness. It is disciplined closure that carries its Λ, declares its □, preserves Χ, permits Φ, and refuses to mistake valid Σ for final authority.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The case preserves multiple rival readings. These are not defects in the PMS or LOGIC analysis. They are part of the case’s anti-dogmatic discipline.

An **epistemological rival reading** may understand dogmatism as anti-fallibilism, epistemic vice, overconfidence, closure against defeaters, or refusal of defeasible revision. This reading preserves normatively loaded distinctions that PMS deliberately avoids. PMS does not decide whether dogmatism is a vice. It renders a structure of closure and non-closure. Epistemology may still be better suited to evaluate warrant, epistemic humility, defeasibility, or standards of rational belief.

A **phenomenological rival reading** may focus on lived certainty, felt conviction, existential need for security, anxiety before ambiguity, or the subjective experience of having a foundation. PMS does not capture inner experience. That is a boundary, not an embarrassment. The current case is structural, not phenomenological. If the relevant question is how dogmatic certainty is lived, suffered, desired, or experienced, another framework may be needed.

A **language-practice rival reading** may focus on discourse rules, speech acts, grammar, rule-following, ordinary-language use, or interpretive communities. It may preserve forms of pragmatic nuance that PMS does not fully render. PMS can map frames and recontextualization, but it does not replace philosophy of language, discourse analysis, or hermeneutic analysis.

An **ethical rival reading** may treat dogmatism as a failure of humility, respect, openness, generosity, or responsibility. PMS and LOGIC do not deny that such readings may be valuable. They simply do not derive ethical verdicts in this case. Dignity-in-Practice constrains the analysis, but it does not rank persons or doctrines.

An **ideology-critique rival reading** may emphasize domination, institutional reproduction, class power, theological authority, disciplinary gatekeeping, or exclusion. PMS leaves Ω weak because the abstract case does not supply a power scene. In concrete contexts, ideology critique may become more discriminating than the current PMS projection. If authority gradients, exclusion practices, or cost externalization are central, PMS would need scene data before making stronger claims.

A **foundationalist rival reading** may argue that some foundations are legitimate, rational, or unavoidable. PMS-LOGIC does not refute that. It only marks the risk of treating a foundation as protected against Λ and Φ. A foundation that declares its scope, preserves rival readability, and names revision conditions may not fit the dogmatism reading.

A **theological rival reading** may treat finality differently from philosophical closure. Some theological structures may not be adequately captured by PMS-LOGIC’s boundary-management vocabulary. The analysis must not automatically translate faith, revelation, doctrine, or commitment into dogmatism. The case concerns structural protection against uncertainty and rival interpretation; it does not adjudicate theology.

A **mathematical or axiomatic rival reading** may show that some closure is stipulated rather than discovered. Axioms can function without pretending to empirical or metaphysical exhaustiveness. PMS must distinguish axiomatic scope from dogmatic totalization. Example 3 makes this distinction explicit: an axiom system that declares scope, preserves Λ, and allows Φ is not dogmatic merely because it uses closure internally.

A **pragmatist rival reading** may say that closure is often a practical achievement: inquiry stops when doubt is no longer live for a purpose. PMS can accommodate this if closure remains frame-bound, revisable, and non-totalizing. But pragmatism may capture the functional legitimacy of closure better than a generic anti-totalization lens.

A **philosophy-of-science rival reading** may treat dogmatism as paradigm protection, normal science, research programme hardening, resistance to anomaly, or defence of a productive framework under pressure. PMS can map parts of this through Α, □, Λ, Φ, and Ω, but a concrete historical or institutional scene would be required.

An **anti-capture reading** remains especially important. It says that not every phenomenon becomes better understood when translated into PMS. Some cases may remain only partially legible or may require another grammar. This is not a failure of the case. It is required by the non-capture rule. A system that cannot admit its own limits would be structurally too close to the dogmatism it analyzes.

The examples reinforce these rival readings. The closed seminar shows how a strong foundation can become structurally closed, but it does not imply that all foundational seminars are dogmatic. The model example shows how explanatory success can become authority, but it does not condemn modeling or benchmarks. The honest axiom example shows that closure can remain legitimate when scope, Λ, Χ, and Φ are preserved. Together, the examples demonstrate why rival readings must remain open.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS reading is weakened if the framework explicitly preserves uncertainty. If it names what remains unresolved, allows rival interpretations to stay visible, and refuses final closure where closure is unavailable, the dogmatism reading loses force.

The reading is weakened if revision conditions are explicit. A framework that says “this account is stable unless these conditions change” is not automatically dogmatic. Revision hooks preserve Χ and Φ.

The reading is weakened if rival interpretations are meaningfully engaged. Dogmatism requires more than disagreement. If rivals are considered, tested, answered, and rejected within declared scope conditions, the case may be one of strong but revisable commitment rather than Σ-totalization.

The reading is weakened if recontextualization is possible. If the account can enter new frames without treating them as betrayal, nonsense, or illegitimate in advance, Φ-blockage is weakened.

The reading is weakened if reflective distance is preserved. A framework may be held strongly while still being understood as bounded, fallible, revisable, or purpose-specific. Such a case would not support a low-Χ reading.

The reading is weakened if apparent integration genuinely preserves tensions. If the framework integrates objections while keeping their force legible, Σ may be valid rather than pseudo-Σ.

The reading is weakened if closure is explicitly operational rather than final. Legal finality, mathematical stipulation, institutional deadlines, or methodological boundaries may require closure for a purpose without claiming exhaustive truth.

The reading is invalid or under-specified if Ψ is asserted without a self-binding subject, institution, or analytic stance. Abstract dogmatism alone does not establish Self-Binding.

The LOGIC overlay is weakened if logic is used only as a bounded ordering tool. If the framework distinguishes coherence from final authority, carries Λ, and keeps Χ active, then LOGIC drift is not active.

The LOGIC drift reading is weakened if non-closure is explicitly acknowledged as a structural boundary. Dogmatism_sigma_totalization requires denial or suppression of Λ. If Λ is preserved, the main drift loses force.

The reading becomes under-discriminating if it cannot distinguish dogmatism from justified closure. This is a serious risk. The analysis must not imply that every final-seeming statement is dogmatic. It must ask: final in what frame, under what scope, with what revision conditions, with what relation to rival interpretations, and with what exposure to Λ?

The reading becomes over-assimilative if resistance, disagreement, rupture, absence, or non-fit is automatically redescribed as failed Φ, low Χ, incomplete Σ, or drift. That would violate the non-capture rule and reproduce closure by translation.

The reading becomes performatively unstable if PMS or LOGIC claims final authority over the case. A dogmatism analysis that cannot carry its own Λ is itself structurally suspect.

The examples contribute to the falsifier logic rather than replacing it. The closed seminar demonstrates how a structure can become dogmatic if objections are pre-contained. The model example shows how explanatory success can become authority if Λ is denied. The honest axiom example shows what would weaken or falsify the dogmatism reading: explicit scope, carried Λ, maintained Χ, and preserved Φ.

Stronger claims would require operationalization. The completed YAML identifies the necessary evidence: a concrete scene; the settled claim or framework; rival interpretations; expected revision, reply, or openness; evidence of persistence; explicit frame rules; evidence of how recontextualization attempts are handled; markers of reflective distance; evidence that integration preserves or suppresses conflict; asymmetry conditions; self-binding conditions; and comparison with rival models.

Empirical validation is not fulfilled. Comparative validation is not fulfilled. Computational validation is not fulfilled. Legal validation is not relevant and not fulfilled. Clinical validation is not relevant and not fulfilled. Applied validation is not fulfilled. The case remains a conceptual PMS Core + PMS-LOGIC article rendering, not an operational case finding.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used as a diagnosis. It does not identify a dogmatic personality, cognitive defect, pathology, disorder, rigidity profile, or clinical condition.

It must not be used for person ranking. No person, group, doctrine, tradition, institution, or community is ranked as less mature, less dignified, less rational, morally inferior, or ontologically deficient.

It must not be used as a legal or forensic conclusion. No liability, culpability, intent, evidentiary finding, or enforcement recommendation follows.

It must not be used as metaphysical proof. The analysis does not prove that certainty is impossible, that foundations cannot exist, that all closure is false, or that PMS provides the final account of dogmatism.

It must not be used as empirical proof. The case material is abstract. No observed scene, corpus, behaviour, or institutional pattern is supplied.

It must not be used as implementation validation. The YAML and article are not a classifier, scoring model, detector, safety system, governance module, or audit tool.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. PMS Core may render the case well, but that does not prove PMS complete, final, uniquely minimal, or superior to all rival formalisms.

It must not treat LOGIC as a new operator layer. PMS-LOGIC is an overlay/application profile. Its reduced signatures are shorthand only. It cannot redefine PMS operators or dependencies.

It must not treat drift as verdict. `dogmatism_sigma_totalization` is a structural risk label. It is not a diagnosis, accusation, or moral sentence.

It must not treat non-closure as failure. Λ is not ignorance, defect, nihilism, or evidence that more force is needed. It is a structural boundary to be carried.

It must not close by authority. Neither PMS nor LOGIC may be used to silence rival readings, exclude counter-formalizations, or declare that dogmatism has been finally explained without remainder.

It must not turn examples into evidence. The examples are illustrative vignettes. They demonstrate how the analysis works, but they do not prove that any real seminar, model, field, doctrine, or axiom system is dogmatic.

It must not convert anti-dogmatism into dogmatism. The case does not license a new final account that condemns all closure. The point is not to prohibit foundations, axioms, models, or coherent theories. The point is to keep their frame, Λ-residue, Χ-distance, Φ-capacity, and Σ-status explicit.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result of the case is that Dogmatism / Σ-Totalization can be rendered as a bounded structural closure-pattern: a frame-bound account treats itself as finally settled, protects itself against unresolved uncertainty and rival interpretation, weakens reflective distance, blocks or pre-contains recontextualization, and presents claimed Integration as if it were mature closure. PMS Core makes this structure legible through Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, and Ψ, while preserving the crucial fact that several later operators remain weak, conditional, under-specified, or analytic-only. LOGIC then sharpens the case by showing how reasoning itself can overreach when coherence, explanation, foundation, or consistency begins to function as final authority.

The examples contribute by showing the discrimination work the analysis must perform. The closed foundational seminar demonstrates how a framework may formally welcome questions while structurally pre-containing rival frames. The model example demonstrates how explanatory success can be converted into authority over meaning when Λ is not carried. The honest axiom example demonstrates the opposite: closure can remain legitimate when it declares its frame, preserves non-closure, maintains distance, and allows recontextualization. The examples therefore do not prove the case. They show how the case should be read without flattening all closure into dogmatism.

What remains unclaimed is as important as what is claimed. The article does not prove that any doctrine is false, that foundations are impossible, that logic fails, that certainty is always illegitimate, or that all stable systems are dogmatic. It does not diagnose persons, rank communities, infer motives, assign guilt, generate legal conclusions, validate PMS Base, or implement a detection system. It does not activate MIP, AHP, or QC because no downstream governance use, hardening task, audit task, or implementation claim is present.

The boundary discipline is therefore internal to the result. PMS coherence is not proof. LOGIC overlay language is not authority. Drift risk is not drift finding. Non-assignment is not a low score. Non-capture is not failure. Resistance, refusal, non-fit, disagreement, rupture, or absence are not automatically drift. Dignity-in-Practice remains praxeological and application-constraining, not ontological ranking. PMS-LOGIC terms remain overlay handles, not PMS operators.

The final methodological takeaway is that dogmatism is not best understood here as having a strong view, using axioms, defending a foundation, or seeking coherence. It is structurally visible where closure refuses to carry its own Λ, where □ becomes immunizing enclosure, where Φ is blocked in advance, where Χ is suspended, and where claimed Σ becomes final authority. The AXIOM value of the case lies in showing that closure can be mapped without being either demonized or absolutized: legitimate closure remains possible, and only closure that denies its own residue, blocks recontextualization, and treats coherence as final authority becomes structurally legible as Σ-totalization risk. Even then, the result remains a bounded PMS/LOGIC reading rather than a verdict, diagnosis, metaphysical proof, or final theory of dogmatism.
