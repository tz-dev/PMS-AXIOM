### Why this case matters

The problem of other minds is one of the classical philosophical sites where a closure-demand becomes unusually sharp. The demand is not merely that one should describe another person’s behaviour, interpret another’s speech, or coordinate with another agent. The deeper demand asks how one can be justified in trusting, attributing, or understanding another mind when the other’s inner experience is not directly accessible. Ordinary praxis seems to presuppose other minds constantly: one trusts, doubts, promises, listens, interprets, responds, forgives, questions, expects, and recognizes. Yet if the demand is framed as direct access to inner experience, the expected access never arrives.

This makes the case structurally difficult. It is not simply a problem of missing information, because more behavioural evidence, more testimony, or more interaction may support trust without becoming direct access to lived interiority. It is also not simply a problem of skepticism, because ordinary action does not usually wait for metaphysical proof before treating others as minded. The difficulty lies in the tension between practical trust and inaccessible subjectivity: the other is present in speech, gesture, conduct, silence, responsiveness, and history, but not present as directly available inner experience.

The case therefore contains a closure-demand that cannot be satisfied by the same kind of evidence that generates the demand. A gesture may be meaningful; a statement may be sincere; a pattern of responsiveness may stabilize trust; a shared practice may render attribution workable. None of these, however, becomes direct possession of the other’s first-person life. The philosophical pressure arises because the frame demands a kind of closure that ordinary praxeological material can support only indirectly and partially.

For AXIOM, this case belongs in the index because it is a clean example of **trust under Λ**. The missing event is not accidental. Direct access to another mind is structurally unavailable within the frame that demands it. The absence is meaningful because the frame expects access, confirmation, warrant, or transparency. PMS Core can render this as a configuration of Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, and Ψ, but it must do so without turning the operator rendering into a solution. The case is valuable precisely because it shows both what PMS can make legible and what PMS must leave uncaptured.

The philosophical closure-demand can be stated in several forms. Epistemologically, it asks how an attribution of mind can be warranted. Phenomenologically, it asks whether the other is encountered directly as another subject or only inferred from signs. Linguistically, it asks whether public criteria and shared forms of life dissolve the demand for inner access. Ethically, it asks whether recognition of the other precedes proof. Practically, it asks how trust can proceed when certainty is structurally unavailable. PMS does not collapse these into one answer. It maps their shared pressure: a framed demand for closure around an unavailable interior.

The case is not treated as metaphysical because PMS does not claim access to being, consciousness, qualia, or inner essence. It is not treated as empirical because no dataset, behavioural record, interview, experiment, or observation series is supplied. It is not clinical or diagnostic because no person is being assessed. It is not legal or forensic because no credibility standard, liability claim, testimony dispute, evidentiary threshold, or adjudicative context is given. It is not an implementation case because no AI architecture, hidden-state model, simulation, or computational system is specified. It is also not a PMS Base validation case: showing that PMS can render the problem structurally does not prove PMS complete, superior, or exhaustive.

Its AXIOM value is therefore cartographic. The case shows where a closure-demand arises, which PMS operators make that demand legible, where the demand cannot be closed, and why the residue must remain visible. It is a case of philosophical pressure, not philosophical conquest. The outcome is not “PMS solves other minds.” The outcome is: PMS can render the problem as a disciplined structure of trust under Λ while preserving non-capture, rival readings, and reversibility.

### Scene and frame structure

Because the case is not a concrete scene, the “scene” is conceptual. The boundary is the philosophical problem of other minds as stated: trust, attribution, or understanding of another mind under inaccessible inner experience. The scene contains a structural relation between an interpreter and an other, but these are not empirical persons in a described event. They are positions inside a philosophical frame.

The operative frame, **□**, is the frame in which another’s inner experience is treated as relevant to trust, attribution, or understanding. Inside this frame, the central question is not simply whether behaviour can be observed. It is whether behaviour, testimony, expression, responsiveness, or ordinary interaction can justify an attribution of mindedness when the inner experience itself is not directly available. The frame therefore makes absence meaningful. Without the frame, the lack of direct access might not be a problem. Within the frame, it becomes the dominant closure-pressure.

Inside the frame are the following structures: the self/other distinction; the difference between inner experience and outward expression; the pressure to trust, attribute, or understand; the absence of direct access; the vulnerability created by acting under uncertainty; and the possibility of reframing the problem from metaphysical proof to praxeological trust. Also inside the frame is the need for **Χ**, because the analysis must not convert absence into either certainty or global skepticism.

Outside the frame are metaphysical proof, direct phenomenological possession of another’s experience, clinical assessment, legal credibility determination, empirical verification, implementation modelling, and person-level ranking. Also outside the frame is any claim that PMS has replaced or defeated rival accounts. A skeptical philosopher may insist that the problem remains a demand for certainty. A phenomenologist may insist that lived encounter changes the structure. A Wittgensteinian may argue that public criteria dissolve the demand for inner access. PMS does not eliminate these alternatives.

The protected constraints are those already stated by the PMS application gate: Distance (**Χ**), reversibility, and Dignity-in-Practice. Distance prevents premature closure. Reversibility prevents the PMS reading from hardening into finality. Dignity-in-Practice prevents the other from being reduced to an object of verification, a hidden container to be decoded, or a lesser being because their inner life is unavailable.

Roles are only structural. One role is the interpreter or attributor: the position from which trust, understanding, or attribution is sought. Another role is the other: the position whose inner experience is not directly accessible and who is vulnerable to over-attribution, objectification, forced transparency, or epistemic ranking. These are not psychological profiles. They are positions in the problem structure.

Temporality, **Θ**, is relevant but weak. Trust normally unfolds over time: repeated interaction, memory, promise, delay, repair, testimony, disappointment, and recognition all matter. But the case material does not supply a sequence. There is no “first encounter,” no “later confirmation,” no “broken trust,” no “silence after a promise,” and no “long-term relation.” Therefore Θ can be treated as conceptually relevant to trust, but not as an established trajectory.

The reversibility window is open. Because no concrete event has bound a trust decision, no Ψ-level commitment is established. Any attribution remains conceptual, revisable, and frame-bound. If a concrete scene were later supplied, the reversibility window would need to be recalibrated according to the frame: ordinary conversation, friendship, testimony, care, law, therapy, AI interaction, or institutional procedure would each change what counts as evidence, obligation, and permissible response.

Exposure and asymmetry are present but bounded. The interpreter is exposed to uncertainty: they may trust wrongly, distrust wrongly, misunderstand, or overinterpret. The other is exposed to being reduced to signs, interpreted without remainder, forced into transparency, or treated as epistemically deficient because their inner experience is inaccessible. That is **Ω**. But without a concrete power relation, role hierarchy, institutional authority, or decision consequence, Ω cannot be inflated into a full social asymmetry or responsibility finding.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading begins with **Δ — Difference**. The case depends on distinctions: self and other, inner and outer, direct access and indirect attribution, trust and distrust, understanding and non-understanding. Without Δ, the problem does not arise. There would be no gap to cross, no boundary to mark, no inaccessible interior to contrast with outward expression. In this case, Δ is active and foundational. But Δ must not be overclaimed. The distinction between self and other does not prove metaphysical separation in any final sense, nor does it justify treating the other as opaque object. It only makes the problem structurally legible.

**∇ — Impulse** is also active, though not as a psychological claim. The impulse is the directional pressure to understand, justify, trust, attribute, or resolve uncertainty. The case asks how trust or attribution can proceed, and that question itself generates ∇. But ∇ is not evidence that a particular subject desires closure, fears uncertainty, or seeks domination. In the abstract case, ∇ is structural directedness, not a motive. Overclaim would occur if the analysis inferred anxiety, suspicion, empathy, need, or pathology from the mere presence of closure-pressure.

**□ — Frame** is strongly active. The philosophical frame makes inaccessible inner experience matter. In ordinary practical frames, one may trust others through speech, conduct, shared practices, and responsiveness without demanding direct access to qualia. In the philosophical frame, however, the absence of direct access becomes problematic. □ therefore defines what kind of absence counts. It also determines what would count as closure: proof, public criteria, ethical recognition, practical trust, embodied encounter, or inferential warrant. The PMS reading must keep these frame alternatives visible. Overclaim would occur if PMS treated its own praxeological frame as the only legitimate framing of the other-minds problem.

**Λ — Non-Event** is the dominant operator. The expected event is direct access to another’s inner experience, or at least some decisive confirmation that bridges expression and interiority without remainder. That event does not occur. This absence is not mere nothingness. It is meaningful because the frame expects access, confirmation, warrant, or transparency. Λ therefore carries the philosophical pressure of the case. But Λ must not be converted into a verdict. The absence of direct access does not prove that other minds are unknowable in every sense. It does not prove that trust is irrational. It does not prove that the other is inaccessible as a person. It also does not prove PMS success merely because PMS can name the absence. The unresolved Λ remains a non-capture zone as well as a structural operator.

**Α — Attractor** is weak and conditional. Trust may stabilize through repeated interaction: reliable speech, consistent responsiveness, shared practice, testimonial uptake, embodied coordination, or repeated repair after misunderstanding. Such patterns could become attractors. They could make trust practically stable even without metaphysical proof. But the case material provides no recurrence. There is no history of repeated encounters. Therefore Α cannot support strong claims. Overclaim would occur if the analysis inferred stable trust from the abstract problem alone, or treated ordinary social practice as already established in this case.

**Ω — Asymmetry** is active but bounded. The asymmetry is not simply that one person has power over another. It is an epistemic and praxeological imbalance: one must trust or attribute under conditions where direct verification is unavailable. The interpreter is vulnerable to error; the other is vulnerable to objectification. Attribution can position the other as interpreted object, while skepticism can deny recognition. Trust can expose the interpreter; distrust can expose the other to exclusion or misrecognition. This is a real Ω-structure, but it remains limited because no institutional authority, coercive relation, or concrete cost distribution is supplied. Overclaim would occur if Ω became moral hierarchy, diagnostic authority, or a claim that the interpreter has superior standing.

**Θ — Temporality** is weak to moderate. Trust is rarely instantaneous in its full form. It develops, erodes, repairs, repeats, and binds across time. The other-minds problem becomes practically significant because one must often continue under uncertainty: one speaks again, waits for response, remembers prior conduct, interprets silence, revises expectation, or sustains recognition. The `PMS-ANTICIPATION` overlay later sharpens this temporal carrying-forward. But in PMS Core, Θ remains under-specified because no temporal scene is given. Overclaim would occur if the analysis described a trajectory, lock-in, delayed confirmation, or development of trust as if the case had supplied one.

**Φ — Recontextualization** is active as a philosophical possibility. PMS reframes the problem from “How can I directly access another inner experience?” to “How can trust, attribution, or understanding proceed structurally under Λ?” That recontextualization is useful because it shifts the demand away from metaphysical possession and toward praxeological handling. But Φ is also dangerous. Reframing can become assimilation. If PMS says, in effect, “the problem is only a praxis structure,” it may erase the philosophical force of skepticism, phenomenology, ethics, or language-practice accounts. Therefore Φ is active but bounded. It makes the case PMS-legible; it does not solve it.

**Χ — Distance** is strongly active and central to validity. The analysis must maintain distance from metaphysical proof, diagnostic inference, legal adjudication, empirical claim, person-ranking, and totalizing interpretation. Χ blocks both premature certainty and premature skepticism. It also blocks a subtler misuse: treating the elegance of the PMS formula as proof of capture. Distance is not indifference. It does not deny the problem. It allows the problem to remain structurally visible without forcing closure. Overclaim would occur wherever PMS terminology becomes a tribunal: “this is what the other really is,” “this proves trust,” “this proves distrust,” or “this exhausts the philosophical problem.”

**Σ — Integration** is weak. PMS can coordinate the elements: difference, frame, absence, asymmetry, temporality, distance, and recontextualization. That coordination gives a coherent case reading. But PMS coherence is not sufficient. A formula may be coherent and still under-discriminate. In this case, Σ would be overclaimed if the analysis treated structural integration as a solution to the problem of other minds. Strong Σ would require a concrete account of how trust, evidence, expression, recognition, and restraint are integrated in a practice. The case does not provide that. Therefore Σ is only partial.

**Ψ — Self-Binding** is weak and conditional. At case level, Ψ would appear if a concrete agent bound themselves over time to trust, restraint, recognition, or accountable attribution under unresolved Λ. Such self-binding might take the form of a promise to continue listening, a commitment not to reduce the other to signs, a disciplined refusal of skepticism, or a sustained practice of recognition. But none of this is specified. Ψ applies mainly as application discipline: the analysis itself is self-bound to reversibility, restraint, and non-overclaim. It does not bind the other to disclose, confirm, respond, or become transparent. It does not establish SELF_FIXPOINT.

The overall PMS Core formula is therefore intentionally restrained:

**Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ**

The expanded formula can include weak or conditional operators:

**Χ ∘ Σ? ∘ Φ ∘ Θ? ∘ Ω ∘ Α? ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ**

This ordering matters. The strong structure lies in Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Φ, and Χ. Ω and Θ matter but are bounded. Α, Σ, and Ψ cannot be strengthened without scene data. The PMS reading is powerful because it keeps closure pressure visible; it remains valid only by refusing to turn that visibility into closure. The formula is therefore not a reduction of the other-minds problem to PMS vocabulary. It is a bounded rendering of one structurally salient pressure: the demand for trust or attribution where direct inner access does not arrive. Its restraint is part of its result.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The PMS dependency discipline is crucial in this case because the other-minds problem tempts cheap later-operator use. It is easy to say that trust integrates absence, that recognition binds the self, or that social practice stabilizes the other. But PMS does not permit later operators to be used merely because they sound relevant. Later operators are structurally expensive.

Σ cannot be claimed simply because the analysis has produced a coherent explanation. In PMS terms, Σ depends on Χ and Φ. That means integration requires reflective distance and a valid recontextualization. In this case, Φ is available as a reframing from metaphysical access to trust under Λ, and Χ is strongly active as restraint. But that does not yet produce full Σ. Full integration would require the conflicting elements of the case to be coordinated in a practice: what counts as expression, what counts as evidence, how trust is revised, how silence is handled, how asymmetry is protected, and how rival frames are preserved. None of that is concretely supplied.

Ψ is even more expensive. It depends on Σ, Θ, and Χ. The case has Χ, but Θ is weak and Σ is partial. Therefore Ψ cannot be used as a strong case-level structure. The analysis may bind itself to restraint, but it cannot claim that a self, agent, relation, or institution has formed a stable self-binding practice. Any such claim would require a concrete agent, temporal horizon, commitment, and enacted response.

Ω also requires caution. PMS dependencies place Ω after Α, which means asymmetry in the full chain is linked to stabilized patterns. In this abstract case, Ω is conceptually visible even without empirical recurrence, because the structure of trust under inaccessible inner experience is asymmetrical. But that means Ω must remain bounded. It cannot be inflated into a stabilized social hierarchy or responsibility gradient unless recurrence, role, and cost distribution are supplied.

Θ cannot be used cheaply either. Trust is temporally suggestive, but not every conceptual reference to trust establishes a trajectory. A case can be temporally relevant without providing temporal evidence. Therefore the analysis may speak of temporal orientation, but not of established temporal development, lock-in, delayed confirmation, or path dependence.

The central dependency risk is **Λ without Χ**. If the absence of direct access is named without distance, the analysis can drift into forced skepticism, suspicion, or metaphysical despair. Conversely, if trust is asserted without Χ, the analysis can drift into naïve closure. Χ keeps both tendencies in check.

Another dependency risk is **Φ without non-capture**. Recontextualizing the problem as praxis under Λ may be illuminating, but it can also become a way of avoiding the original philosophical difficulty. If the PMS frame absorbs all resistance as merely incomplete integration, PMS becomes a closure machine. That would violate the case’s own discipline.

A third risk is **Σ as proof**. PMS can make the structure coherent: distinction, impulse, frame, absence, asymmetry, temporality, recontextualization, distance, integration. But coherent derivation is not the same as discriminative adequacy. A formula that fits too many problems may explain too little. The completed YAML explicitly marks the coherence risk as high because the other-minds problem fits PMS operators smoothly. That smoothness is useful but dangerous.

Non-capture is not failure. It is part of the case’s validity. Direct phenomenology of another mind may remain outside PMS. Metaphysical proof may remain outside PMS. Ethical recognition may not reduce to structural trust. Public criteria may dissolve the problem differently. Hidden-state inference may formalize the issue differently. These are not PMS defects unless PMS claims to exhaust them. Preserving them is what keeps the reading honest.

Invalid dependency shortcuts would include moving from Δ/∇ directly to Ψ: because there is uncertainty, one binds a trust or distrust verdict. Another shortcut would be moving from Λ to Σ: because the absence is named, it is integrated. Another would be moving from Φ to closure: because the problem has been reframed, it has been solved. Another would be moving from Ω to authority: because trust is asymmetrical, the interpreter may decide for the other. All of these shortcuts are invalid in this case.

PMS must therefore remain a cartographic grammar, not a closure machine. It can show where the demand for direct access arises, where the absence persists, and where trust may be structurally thinkable. It cannot decide by authority what another mind is, whether trust is justified, or whether the philosophical problem is dissolved.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS Core analysis treats drift patterns as **conditional structural risks**, not as findings. This distinction matters especially in the case of other minds because the philosophical problem itself invites strong closure-pressure. One can move too quickly from “I cannot directly access the other’s inner experience” to suspicion, skepticism, authority, forced transparency, or over-reflective paralysis. PMS can make those risks visible, but it must not assign them as if the abstract case material already demonstrated them.

The first relevant PMS drift-pattern is **AD_A>>E**, the excessive distance between Awareness and Enactment. In the completed YAML, this pattern is marked as a possible risk, not an established case result. The reason is straightforward: the philosophical problem can expand awareness of uncertainty almost indefinitely. One can continue reflecting on the gap between expression and inner experience, on the lack of direct access, on the fragility of attribution, and on the possibility of error. This can produce a structurally over-expanded awareness-axis while leaving enactment delayed, suspended, or undefined.

In this case, however, the action-axis is not demonstrably suppressed. No concrete scene asks an agent to act. There is no decision, promise, response, legal judgment, clinical interpretation, or institutional obligation. Therefore the YAML correctly refuses to convert AD_A>>E into a finding. The axis effects remain under-specified rather than active.

This is important because under-specification is not failure. It is not a low score. It does not mean the case is immature, deficient, incomplete, or structurally weak. It means the case material does not provide enough scene data to support stronger drift assignment. In AXIOM terms, the right move is not to fill the gap with interpretation, but to preserve the gap as Λ-residue.

The second relevant PMS drift-pattern is **AD_Sigma_low**, fragmented integration. This is also marked as possible but not entailed. The other-minds problem can easily become a field of competing frames: skeptical epistemology, phenomenology, language-practice, ethical recognition, inference from behaviour, testimony theory, ordinary trust, and computational hidden-state modelling. A reading may shift among these frames without consolidating them. PMS itself can become one more frame in this series. The risk is that recontextualization, Φ, outpaces integration, Σ.

Yet the case does not show actual volatility. It does not describe a person or institution moving from one frame to another in a destabilized way. It does not show inconsistent responsibility, failed integration, or unstable enactment. The coherence-axis and responsibility-axis are therefore under-specified. The analysis may name the risk, but it must not treat fragmented integration as established.

This drift discipline prevents PMS from becoming a covert verdict system. Drift catalogues are not diagnostic classifications. They are not person-types. They are not moral rankings. They are not evidence that someone is immature, avoidant, irresponsible, over-reflective, or incoherent. In this case, both AD_A>>E and AD_Sigma_low remain **risk markers**: they identify ways the philosophical structure could be mishandled if moved into a concrete scene or applied setting.

There is also a broader coherence risk. The completed PMS Core YAML explicitly marks the case as high coherence-risk because it fits PMS too smoothly. Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, and Ψ can all be made relevant. That is analytically useful, but it also creates under-discrimination danger. A structure that fits trust, testimony, empathy, skepticism, public criteria, and hidden-state inference equally well may become too general unless it preserves distinctions among them.

The PMS drift-pattern status therefore has a double function. It warns against philosophical closure-drift, and it warns against PMS closure-drift. The case must not be allowed to say: “Because PMS can render the other-minds problem coherently, the problem is structurally solved.” That would be precisely the form of Σ-totalization the analysis is designed to avoid.

### Add-on / overlay reading

The relevant add-on in this case is **PMS-ANTICIPATION**. It is applied only as an overlay or application profile. PMS.yaml remains the sole PMS Base reference. The overlay does not redefine PMS operators, does not change PMS dependencies, does not revise the Core case, and does not introduce new PMS operators. Its reduced signatures are shorthand only.

The add-on is justified because the problem of other minds is not only a static problem of absence. It is also a problem of carrying uncertainty forward. Trust under Λ often has a temporal orientation: one may continue to trust, withhold trust, revise trust, wait for expression, receive testimony, encounter silence, experience disappointment, or reinterpret a prior attribution. PMS Core already shows the structural centrality of Λ, Φ, and Χ. PMS-ANTICIPATION sharpens the question of how Λ is carried under weak Θ without becoming prognosis, prediction, certainty, or command.

The overlay therefore adds visibility to **future-oriented praxis under unresolved absence**. In the Core reading, direct access to another mind is absent. In the ANTICIPATION reading, that absence is carried forward. The future may bring expression, testimony, responsiveness, rupture, silence, or continued opacity. None of these possibilities is treated as already settled. This is the key difference between anticipation and prognosis.

PMS-ANTICIPATION defines anticipation as a structurally disciplined present stance toward possible future events and non-events under Θ and Ω, without closure claims. In this case, the stance is weak and conceptual. It does not predict that another person will reveal their mind. It does not predict that trust will be vindicated. It does not predict that silence will indicate absence of mind. It does not treat a future expression as already guaranteed. It simply asks how one may carry the open Λ forward without collapsing into certainty, coercion, skepticism, or action mandate.

The primary reduced signature is **ANT_min**, rendered in the overlay as:

**Δ + □ + Λ + Θ? + Χ**

This signature is marked weak. Δ, □, Λ, and Χ are clear: the case has distinction, frame, structured absence, and strong distance-discipline. Θ is marked with a question because temporality is only implied. Trust may unfold over time, but the case does not supply a temporal sequence. ANT_min therefore applies as a restrained shorthand: unresolved absence is carried in a frame under possible temporality and protected by distance.

The fuller viable anticipatory configuration, **ANT_viable_full**, is marked weak. It includes the full PMS operator inventory, but the case does not support strong activation of Α, Θ, Σ, or Ψ. There is no recurrence, no established time horizon, no integrated practice, and no concrete self-binding agent. This is exactly why the overlay remains subordinate. It cannot upgrade the case merely because anticipation is thematically relevant.

The planning or preparedness constraint is not applicable. The case does not involve planning, preparedness, intervention, policy, safety response, or downstream action design. PMS-ANTICIPATION explicitly distinguishes anticipation from planning. Planning may emerge downstream, but only under Χ, D, reversibility, and concrete scene evidence. None of that is present here. The overlay therefore cannot recommend preparation, action, intervention, or vigilance.

Several overlay constructs function as non-operator handles. They are not PMS operators. They include the **validity gate**, **viability criteria**, **anticipation-versus-prognosis contrast**, **formal invalidity markers**, **risk and drift markers**, **post-confirmation discipline**, **structural potentials and costs**, and the **minimal application protocol**. These handles organize the application of PMS-ANTICIPATION, but they do not alter Δ–Ψ.

The validity gate passes because Χ, reversibility, and Dignity-in-Practice are maintained. Χ remains active: the overlay does not fuse anticipation into future-truth authority. Reversibility remains active: the anticipatory stance can be revised if a concrete scene or rival formalization changes the frame. Dignity-in-Practice remains active: the other is not forced into transparency, ranked, shamed, or treated as less standing-bearing because their inner life is not directly accessible.

The viability criteria are mixed rather than globally strong. Λ is satisfied as carried openness: the absence of direct access remains active and is not closed. Χ is also satisfied: anticipation does not become authorization, command, prediction, or future-truth posture. Θ is respected but weak, because the analysis acknowledges temporal orientation without inventing a trajectory. Ψ is weak because it binds only the analytic stance to restraint, not another person to disclosure, response, or transparency. Σ is weak because the analysis coordinates uncertainty and restraint without claiming full integration. The overlay is therefore viable only as a restrained conceptual stance, not as a full anticipatory practice.

The overlay also guards against formal invalidity markers. Certainty does not replace Λ. Actionism does not neutralize Θ. Χ is not suspended by alleged superior knowledge. Ω is not instrumentalized as authority. Ψ is not externalized onto the other. These absences are important. The overlay would become invalid if uncertainty about another mind were used to demand disclosure, enforce recognition, require trust, require distrust, or claim epistemic superiority.

The prognosis contrast is central. Prognosis is a future-truth posture: it treats a future state as sufficiently settled to coordinate around it. Anticipation, in this overlay, is a present structural stance that carries uncertainty without closure. The case contains no prognosis. It has no probability estimate, forecast, outcome target, behavioural prediction, or future-state claim. It remains weak anticipation, not prediction.

The main ANTICIPATION drift risks are also conditional. **Moralization of uncertainty** is marked as a risk: uncertainty about another mind could become suspicion, obligatory trust, obligatory distrust, accusation, or virtue claim. But this is not a finding. The overlay blocks the drift by keeping Χ and D active.

**AD_A≫E** appears again under the anticipation lens as a risk of over-reflection and under-enactment. The philosophical problem may expand awareness while leaving action indeterminate. But because the case is not an action scene, this remains a risk, not a diagnosis or failure.

Several post-confirmation risks are also identified. If later expression or responsiveness seems to confirm a prior attribution, **Ψ-overbinding** may occur: the interpreter may bind their self-understanding to having been right. **Ω-inflation** may occur: confirmation may become an epistemic superiority claim. **Χ-erosion** may occur: distance may weaken after apparent confirmation. **Λ-rewrite** may occur: prior uncertainty may be rewritten as inevitability. The overlay’s discipline is that confirmation requires renewed distance, not entitlement. If one later appears to have been right, PMS-ANTICIPATION demands more restraint, not more authority.

The overlay’s final signature is therefore:

**ANT_min_weak: Δ + □ + Λ + Θ? + Χ**

with **ANT_viable_full** marked weak and non-authorizing. The formula is deliberately modest. It shows that trust under Λ may be future-oriented, but only as a restrained stance that preserves absence, reversibility, rival openness, and Dignity-in-Practice.

### Layer interaction

The PMS Core reading remains the base layer. It supplies the operator grammar, the case typing, the claim boundaries, the dependency discipline, the derived-structure formulas, and the misuse constraints. The ANTICIPATION overlay does not replace any of that. It sharpens one dimension of the already completed PMS reading: the carrying-forward of Λ under possible temporality.

What remains PMS Core is the central structure of the case: Δ distinguishes self and other, ∇ marks the pressure to trust or understand, □ frames the philosophical problem, Λ marks inaccessible inner experience, Ω marks bounded asymmetry, Θ remains weak, Φ reframes the problem, Χ restrains closure, Σ remains partial, and Ψ remains weak or analytic-only. The derived structures also remain PMS Core: Awareness_A, Coherence_C, Responsibility_R, Action_E, Dignity_in_Practice_D, and SELF_FIXPOINT are not redefined by ANTICIPATION.

What the add-on sharpens is the difference between **carrying Λ** and **closing Λ**. PMS Core can already say that direct access to another mind is absent. PMS-ANTICIPATION asks how that absence is carried forward in a present stance toward possible future expression, testimony, responsiveness, rupture, silence, or continued opacity. It also sharpens the distinction between anticipation and prognosis. This matters because the other-minds problem can easily slide into prediction: “they will reveal themselves,” “they will betray trust,” “they are probably sincere,” “they are probably opaque,” or “future behaviour will prove the attribution.” The overlay blocks this slide.

The add-on must not be allowed to redefine Θ. It may mark temporal orientation, but it cannot create a temporal trajectory where the case supplies none. It must not redefine Ψ. It may bind the analysis to restraint, but it cannot bind the other to disclose, confirm, or become intelligible. It must not redefine Ω. It may highlight vulnerability under future-oriented uncertainty, but it cannot turn interpretive asymmetry into authority. It must not redefine Λ. It carries absence; it does not solve it.

Overlay language could overreach in several places. First, “anticipation” could sound like prediction. The YAML explicitly blocks that. Second, “viability” could sound like success. The overlay defines viability independently of being right; a stance can be viable if it maintains Λ, Χ, D, reversibility, and self-binding to restraint. Third, “post-confirmation” could sound like validation. The overlay blocks that too: confirmation is a second-order test of whether restraint was maintained, not a warrant for superiority.

The interaction between Core and overlay is therefore asymmetrical. PMS Core authorizes the structural analysis. PMS-ANTICIPATION annotates a specific future-oriented aspect. The overlay may increase visibility, but not authority. It may add drift risks, but not findings. It may add reduced signatures, but not dependencies. It may add application handles, but not PMS Base.

Non-closure remains visible at both layers. At the Core layer, direct access to another mind remains Λ. At the ANTICIPATION layer, future expression or confirmation remains open and non-authorizing. The problem is not closed by being structurally mapped, and it is not closed by being carried forward.

### Example 1 — Waiting for a reply without turning silence into proof

A person sends a careful message to a close friend after a difficult conversation. The message is not accusatory; it asks whether the friend understood what had happened and whether they are willing to talk again. Hours pass, then a day. No reply arrives. The sender begins to wonder what the silence means. Does the friend feel hurt? Are they indifferent? Are they avoiding responsibility? Are they overwhelmed? Did the message fail to communicate what mattered?

This vignette is bounded. It does not ask what the friend “really” feels. It does not claim that silence proves indifference, care, avoidance, guilt, or pain. It only shows how the problem of other minds becomes structurally active in an ordinary trust scene. The sender has access to an event-field: the message was sent, no answer has arrived, prior interaction gives some context, and the relation carries expectations. But the friend’s inner experience remains unavailable. The central non-event is not merely “no reply”; it is the absent confirmation that would close the interpretive gap.

The PMS Core hooks are clear. **Δ** distinguishes self and other, message and reply, inner experience and outward sign. **∇** appears as the pressure to understand what the silence means. **□** is the relational frame: this is not a court, clinic, or institutional audit, but a friendship or interpersonal relation. **Λ** is central: an expected response does not arrive, and with it the hoped-for access to the other’s understanding remains absent. **Ω** is present but bounded: the sender is exposed to uncertainty, while the silent friend is exposed to possible overinterpretation. **Θ** is relevant because the silence unfolds over time, but even here it remains limited unless the relation’s longer history is specified. **Φ** may recontextualize the silence: from rejection to overwhelm, from avoidance to need for time, from indifference to uncertainty. **Χ** is the crucial guardrail: the sender must not convert silence into proof. **Σ** remains partial because several possible readings can be coordinated, but none fully integrates the other’s inner state. **Ψ** would only become stronger if the sender bound themselves to a durable practice of restraint, patience, or non-coercive follow-up.

The ANTICIPATION overlay sharpens the example. The sender is carrying **Λ under weak Θ**: the future may bring a reply, continued silence, rupture, repair, or recontextualization. This is anticipation, not prognosis. The sender may remain open to possible future expression, but cannot claim that a reply will come, that no reply proves something, or that the future has already selected one interpretation. The relevant reduced signature is **ANT_min_weak: Δ + □ + Λ + Θ? + Χ**. It is weak because the scene gives a temporal delay but not enough to establish a long-term trajectory, recurrence, or self-binding practice.

MIP, AHP, and QC are not active. This is not a governance artefact, not an analysis-hardening exercise, not a quality-control trace, and not an institutional decision. If the vignette were moved into a workplace grievance, clinical setting, legal testimony, or safety procedure, the stack would need to be reconsidered. In its present form, it remains a bounded interpersonal illustration.

The forbidden claims are decisive. The example must not claim that the friend is avoidant, dishonest, immature, uncaring, traumatized, manipulative, or responsible for repair. It must not claim that the sender is rationally required to trust or distrust. It must not turn non-response into evidence of inner absence. It must not treat the absence of reply as PMS drift, failed maturity, or incomplete integration.

The non-capture note is equally important. PMS can map the structure of waiting under absence, but it does not access the friend’s experience. The friend’s silence may have meanings not available to the sender or to the model. It may also resist interpretation. The example therefore demonstrates how PMS preserves Λ rather than filling it.

The article takeaway is that trust under Λ often begins in ordinary scenes where something expected does not arrive. PMS helps describe the pressure, the frame, the asymmetry, and the need for distance. It does not license closure.

### Example 2 — Public criteria and the rival reading of mindedness

In a seminar, one participant argues that the problem of other minds is unavoidable because another’s inner experience can never be directly inspected. Another participant responds that this formulation is already misleading. In ordinary language, we do not first inspect a private inner object and then infer that someone is in pain, joking, promising, grieving, or understanding. We learn and apply public criteria: expressions, contexts, practices, responses, shared forms of life, and the grammar of ordinary attribution. On this view, the demand for direct access may not be an unsatisfied requirement; it may be a distorted demand produced by a misleading picture of mind.

This vignette is bounded as a philosophical contrast. It does not decide who is right. It does not say that skepticism is refuted, that public criteria solve the whole problem, or that PMS supersedes language-practice philosophy. Its purpose is to show why rival readings must remain open inside the article. The problem of other minds is not structurally neutral: different frames generate different closure-demands.

The PMS Core hooks begin with **□**. The first participant’s frame treats direct inner access as the decisive missing event. Within that frame, **Λ** becomes dominant: the expected access does not arrive. The second participant shifts the frame through **Φ**. If mindedness is understood through public criteria and forms of life, then the absence of direct inner inspection may no longer function in the same way. The central question becomes not “How do I bridge an inner gap?” but “What are the rules and practices through which mental predicates are intelligibly used?”

This frame shift demonstrates why **Φ** is active but dangerous. PMS can recontextualize the problem as trust under Λ, but the language-practice reading may recontextualize it differently. The PMS reading must not absorb that rival framing as merely incomplete PMS integration. If the rival frame genuinely changes what counts as the problem, then PMS must preserve that non-capture rather than declare victory.

**Δ** remains active because the seminar distinguishes self and other, inner access and public criteria, skepticism and practice. **∇** appears as the pressure to settle which frame is correct. **Λ** may be central in the skeptical frame but less central in the public-criteria frame. **Ω** is minimal in the vignette unless one participant has authority to impose the frame. **Θ** is weak unless the debate is embedded in a longer intellectual tradition or institutional curriculum. **Χ** is essential: it prevents the PMS article from treating the rival reading as an error. **Σ** remains partial because the frames can be compared but not fully integrated in the example. **Ψ** is not case-level active unless a participant or institution binds itself to one interpretive regime.

The ANTICIPATION overlay is relevant only indirectly. The seminar may anticipate future clarification, continued disagreement, or later recontextualization, but the example is not primarily about future-oriented trust. ANT_min is therefore not the main signature here. The overlay’s relevance lies in its restraint: the article should not forecast that one frame will eventually absorb the others.

MIP, AHP, and QC are not active. This is not a governance case and not an artefact-hardening pass. It is a conceptual example for preserving rival formalizations.

The forbidden claims are: PMS proves the skeptical frame correct; PMS proves the public-criteria frame correct; PMS resolves the seminar dispute; the skeptical participant lacks practical maturity; the language-practice participant avoids the real problem; disagreement itself is drift. None of those claims is warranted.

The non-capture note is central. A Wittgensteinian or language-practice reading may preserve something PMS does not fully capture: the way ordinary criteria make mental attribution intelligible without requiring access to a hidden interior. A phenomenological reading may preserve embodied encounter. A skeptical reading may preserve the demand for certainty. PMS can map closure-demands across frames, but it cannot assume that all rival frames are subordinate to its own.

The article takeaway is that “other minds” is not a single closure-demand under all descriptions. PMS Core becomes stronger, not weaker, when it keeps rival framings visible and refuses to turn non-fit into failure.

### Example 3 — Later confirmation without retrospective authority

A person has long suspected that a colleague’s reserved behaviour during a project was not hostility but overload. During the project, the colleague gave short answers, missed informal check-ins, and rarely explained their state. The person did not confront them or turn the suspicion into a claim. They continued to cooperate, kept the interpretation provisional, and avoided telling others what the colleague “really” felt. Months later, the colleague says, “I know I seemed distant then. I was overwhelmed and did not know how to explain it.”

At first glance, this looks like confirmation. The person’s earlier interpretation appears to have been right. But the example is designed precisely to show why confirmation does not erase the prior Λ. At the earlier time, direct access to the colleague’s inner experience was absent. The later statement may support one interpretation, but it does not retroactively make the earlier uncertainty illegitimate. It also does not give the interpreter general authority over the colleague’s inner life.

The PMS Core hooks are layered. **Δ** distinguishes the colleague’s outward behaviour from possible inner states. **∇** appears as the pressure to understand the behaviour. **□** is the project-relation frame: colleagues coordinating under shared work demands, not a clinical, legal, or intimate setting. **Λ** is present in the earlier period because the expected clarification does not arrive. **Α** is weakly suggested by repeated reserved behaviour, but it must not be overread as a stable trait. **Ω** is bounded: misinterpretation could affect reputation or cooperation, but no formal authority is specified. **Θ** is more visible here than in the abstract case because the vignette contains before, delay, and later disclosure. **Φ** occurs when the later statement recontextualizes the earlier behaviour. **Χ** is the key operator: restraint was required before confirmation and remains required after it. **Σ** is partial: the later disclosure integrates some prior ambiguity, but not the whole inner life of the colleague. **Ψ** may describe the interpreter’s self-binding to restraint, but not a right to future certainty.

The ANTICIPATION overlay is directly relevant. During the project, the interpreter carried a possible reading forward without treating it as prognosis. The future might have confirmed, complicated, contradicted, or left unresolved the interpretation. The later statement becomes a test of **post-confirmation discipline**. PMS-ANTICIPATION says that confirmation should reopen distance rather than collapse it. The interpreter must not say, “I knew all along,” if that means denying the earlier uncertainty. They must not generalize: “I can read this colleague accurately.” They must not use confirmation to inflate Ω: “I understood them better than others did.” They must not bind Ψ to being right.

The relevant overlay risks are **Ψ-overbinding**, **Ω-inflation**, **Χ-erosion**, and **Λ-rewrite**. Each remains a risk, not a finding. Ψ-overbinding would occur if the interpreter made correctness part of their self-relation: “I am the one who sees what others miss.” Ω-inflation would occur if later confirmation became superiority. Χ-erosion would occur if future interpretations became less restrained because this one seemed right. Λ-rewrite would occur if the earlier uncertainty were narrated as inevitability: “It was obvious from the beginning.” The disciplined response is different: “That interpretation was possible, and later disclosure supported it, but it was not knowledge at the time.”

MIP, AHP, and QC remain inactive. The vignette does not authorize workplace action, performance evaluation, legal conclusion, clinical inference, or institutional policy. If the example were used for HR, compliance, therapy, or adjudication, a different stack would be required.

The forbidden claims are clear. The example must not claim that the colleague’s later statement reveals their entire prior inner state. It must not claim that the interpreter had knowledge before the disclosure. It must not claim that reserved behaviour generally means overload. It must not diagnose the colleague. It must not turn the later statement into proof that the PMS analysis is empirically validated.

The non-capture note is that even later testimony does not exhaust inner experience. The colleague’s statement is an event within a frame, not transparent access to the totality of what was lived. It may be sincere, partial, simplified, retrospective, or framed for the relationship. PMS can map how later disclosure recontextualizes prior Λ, but it cannot claim full capture.

The article takeaway is that confirmation does not license authority. In trust under Λ, the hardest discipline may come not before one is confirmed, but after. PMS-ANTICIPATION preserves the difference between being later supported and having had closure all along.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The completed analyses preserve several non-capture zones. The first and most obvious is **direct phenomenology**. PMS does not capture another’s qualia, lived experience, first-person givenness, or interiority. It can map the structural absence of direct access, but naming the absence is not equivalent to accessing what is absent. A PMS rendering of Λ must not become a substitute for phenomenology.

A second non-capture zone is **metaphysical proof**. The PMS reading does not prove that other minds exist. It also does not prove that they cannot be known. It does not decide the metaphysical status of consciousness. It only maps how the demand for access functions within a frame of trust and attribution.

A third non-capture zone is **actual justification of trust**. PMS can show how trust may be structurally thinkable under Λ, but it does not show that any actual trust is justified. The distinction between structurally legible trust and actually warranted trust must remain visible. That distinction is one of the most important safeguards in the case.

A fourth non-capture zone is **public criteria and language-practice**. A Wittgensteinian reading may argue that the problem is generated by a mistaken picture of inner access and that mindedness is embedded in public criteria, practices, forms of life, and language use. This reading preserves something PMS may not fully capture: the possibility that the demand for direct access is itself a grammatical confusion rather than a stable praxeological absence.

A fifth non-capture zone is **ethical recognition**. An ethical reading may hold that the other is to be recognized before being proven. Trust or acknowledgment may be a stance of responsibility rather than an inference from evidence. PMS can render some of this through Ω, Χ, Ψ, and D, but it does not replace the ethical claim. It must not reduce recognition to structural operation alone.

A sixth non-capture zone is **inferential or abductive explanation**. Some approaches treat other minds as best explanations of behaviour, expression, testimony, and responsiveness. Such readings may be more precise where evidence structures are available. PMS does not displace them. In fact, a concrete scene might require an inferential model rather than a pure PMS rendering.

A seventh non-capture zone is **computational hidden-state modelling**. In AI or cognitive science, the issue may be formalized as inference over hidden states. PMS-ANTICIPATION explicitly leaves this open. It does not become an implementation model and does not claim that hidden-state inference is reducible to Λ and Θ. If an AI architecture were supplied, operationalization would require separate modelling and validation.

The skeptical reading also remains open. It may insist that the problem is not solved by praxeological reframing because the original demand concerns certainty. PMS can respond by saying that it is not trying to solve that demand, only to map it. But that response does not defeat skepticism; it relocates the PMS contribution.

The phenomenological reading remains open as well. It may argue that the other is not first encountered as an inferred hidden interior, but as embodied presence, expression, or intersubjective encounter. PMS can map frame, absence, trust, and asymmetry, but it does not claim to describe lived encounter from within.

The anti-capture reading is especially important for AXIOM. It states that the other-minds problem may resist clean translation into PMS terms precisely because its philosophical force lies in the difference between structural readability and lived or metaphysical access. The YAML preserves this by saying that unresolved absence must not be converted into PMS success merely because Λ can name it.

Rival readings are therefore not defects. They are part of the case’s intellectual honesty. PMS-AXIOM does not require every rival to be wrong. It requires that the PMS reading state what it makes visible, what it cannot capture, and what remains open. This also means that rival readings must not be redescribed too quickly as incomplete PMS calibrations. Skeptical refusal, phenomenological insistence, public-criteria reframing, ethical priority, or computational formalization may each preserve a distinction that the PMS rendering only borders. In this case, openness to rivalry is not a concession after the analysis; it is one of the conditions under which the analysis remains valid.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS reading would be weakened if the intended case is strictly metaphysical and refuses praxeological recontextualization. If the question is only “Can one prove that another consciousness exists?” and no structural rendering of trust is admitted as relevant, then PMS can at most map the closure-demand; it cannot answer the question on its own terms.

The reading would also be weakened if the case requires direct phenomenological description rather than structural analysis. PMS can say that inner experience is unavailable as a direct event within the frame, but it cannot describe what the other lives. If the case’s target is the lived encounter with another subject, PMS may under-capture.

The reading becomes under-discriminating if the same operator formula cannot distinguish trust, attribution, empathy, testimony, skepticism, public criteria, and hidden-state inference. This is a serious risk. The PMS formula must not be so broad that every relation to another becomes simply “Λ under □.” To remain useful, it must preserve the difference between ordinary trust, metaphysical doubt, ethical recognition, linguistic practice, and empirical inference.

The reading becomes over-assimilative if unresolved absence, disagreement, rival framing, silence, or phenomenological non-fit is automatically redescribed as PMS drift, failed integration, incomplete calibration, or maturity failure. This is explicitly forbidden by the completed YAML. Non-fit may be genuine non-capture. Rival framing may be sharper. Silence may be part of the case rather than a defect. Absence may remain absence.

The reading is overextended if Χ, Φ, or Σ are used to imply that the problem has been solved because it has been reframed into PMS operator language. Φ can recontextualize; it cannot guarantee adequacy. Χ can preserve distance; it cannot deliver proof. Σ can integrate; it cannot eliminate Λ unless the frame and evidence warrant closure. In this case, they do not.

The reading is under-specified if it claims Responsibility_R, Action_E, SELF_FIXPOINT, strong Ψ, or concrete trust formation without a specified agent, temporal trajectory, scene, commitment, evidence form, or enacted response. This is a major operational boundary. The case remains abstract. Stronger derived structures require scene data.

The ANTICIPATION overlay has its own weakening conditions. It would be weakened if the case is interpreted as a purely static epistemic puzzle with no future-oriented stance. If temporality is irrelevant to the intended framing, then ANT_min becomes less applicable. It would also be weakened if the overlay begins to sound like prognosis. Any claim that future expression will occur, that trust will be confirmed, or that silence will indicate absence would violate the overlay.

The overlay would be invalid if it authorizes action. PMS-ANTICIPATION explicitly says that anticipation does not mandate readiness, execution, intervention, or preparedness in the absence of a valid downstream frame. In this case, there is no action window. Therefore any instruction to trust, distrust, test, pressure, disclose, intervene, or demand recognition would exceed the analysis.

The overlay would also be invalid if Ψ is externalized. The analysis may bind itself to restraint, but it cannot bind the other. It cannot say that the other must reveal their mind, answer the interpreter, supply confirmation, become transparent, or satisfy the need for epistemic closure. That would convert anticipation into coercion.

Operationalization remains unfulfilled. For a stronger empirical or applied analysis, several things would be needed.

A concrete scene would be required. One would need to know who is involved, what relation they stand in, what frame governs the interaction, what is at stake, what evidence is available, and what kind of trust or attribution is being considered.

Evidence forms would need to be specified. These might include speech, silence, gesture, testimony, responsiveness, history, promises, contradictions, delays, refusals, or repairs. Without such evidence, operators like Α, Θ, Σ, Ψ, Responsibility_R, and Action_E remain weak.

The temporal structure would need to be supplied. Is this a first encounter, repeated relation, broken promise, delayed response, institutional review, therapeutic setting, legal testimony, friendship, care relation, or AI interaction? Each temporal structure would change the analysis.

Asymmetry would need concrete calibration. Who has interpretive power? Who bears risk if trust fails? Who is vulnerable to misrecognition? Who can contest the attribution? Who can impose a frame? Without these details, Ω remains bounded.

If empirical validation were claimed, observable indicators would be required for each operator. If comparative validation were claimed, rival models would need explicit criteria. If computational validation were claimed, an architecture, representation, and testing regime would be necessary. If legal or clinical use were claimed, domain standards would be required. None of these is present.

The final weakening condition is perhaps the most important: if PMS coherence becomes too smooth, the reading must be treated with suspicion. A strong-looking PMS rendering can be analytically elegant and still under-discriminating. The completed YAML handles this correctly by marking coherence risk high and preserving rival readings.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It does not identify mental states, traits, pathologies, capacities, intentions, sincerity, empathy, opacity, maturity, or trustworthiness. PMS terms must not be used to classify a person as inaccessible, resistant, immature, insufficiently integrated, over-reflective, or deficient.

It must not be used for person-ranking. The fact that another’s inner experience is inaccessible does not rank the other below the interpreter. It does not make the interpreter epistemically superior. It does not make the other an object to be decoded, managed, or verified. Dignity-in-Practice forbids turning Ω into hierarchy.

It must not be used legally or forensically. The case provides no evidentiary standard, credibility assessment, liability determination, burden of proof, or tribunal procedure. It cannot determine whether someone should be believed, whether testimony is sufficient, whether intent is proven, or whether an attribution is admissible.

It must not be used as metaphysical proof. PMS does not prove that other minds exist. It does not disprove skepticism. It does not show that consciousness is structurally reducible. It does not give access to qualia. It does not settle the ontology of subjectivity.

It must not be used as empirical proof. No behavioural data, testimony set, interaction record, or experiment is supplied. The analysis does not establish that trust is warranted in any actual case. It does not validate an attribution.

It must not be used as implementation validation. The YAML is not an AI model. PMS-ANTICIPATION is not a predictive system. The case does not implement theory of mind, hidden-state inference, belief modelling, or agent architecture. No computational claim follows from the formal rendering.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. A successful case rendering does not prove that PMS is complete, final, uniquely ordered, or superior to rival theories. It shows only that this case can be rendered under PMS discipline.

It must not treat the add-on as an operator. ANT_min, viability, prognosis contrast, post-confirmation discipline, drift markers, and the minimal application protocol are overlay handles. They do not redefine Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, or Ψ. They do not change dependencies.

It must not treat drift as verdict. AD_A>>E, AD_Sigma_low, moralization of uncertainty, post-confirmation epistemic inflation, Ψ-overbinding, Ω-inflation, Χ-erosion, and Λ-rewrite are risks, not findings. They are not diagnoses, moral failures, person-types, or evidence of immaturity.

It must not close by authority. The analysis must not say, explicitly or implicitly, that because PMS has rendered the problem, the problem is closed. It must not use PMS vocabulary to force agreement, silence rival readings, or suppress the original philosophical difficulty. The case remains a map of closure-demand and Λ-residue, not a closure device.

### Case conclusion

The integrated result is that **Other minds (trust under Λ)** can be rendered as a disciplined PMS case without being solved in the strong metaphysical sense. PMS Core makes visible the structural grammar of the problem: Δ distinguishes self and other, ∇ generates pressure toward trust or understanding, □ frames the philosophical demand, Λ marks the absent direct access to inner experience, Ω introduces bounded asymmetry, Θ remains weak but relevant to trust over time, Φ allows praxeological recontextualization, Χ prevents closure, Σ remains partial, and Ψ remains analytic or conditional rather than case-level established. The core formula is therefore restrained: **Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ**, with later operators admitted only as weak or conditional where the abstract case material permits them.

The ANTICIPATION overlay adds a second-order visibility layer. It shows that trust under Λ is not only an absence-structure but a carried-forward stance: one may remain open to possible future expression, silence, responsiveness, disappointment, revision, or continued opacity. The overlay does not predict any of these. It does not convert anticipation into prognosis. Its reduced signature, **ANT_min_weak: Δ + □ + Λ + Θ? + Χ**, is useful precisely because it remains weak. It preserves the unresolved non-event, keeps temporality conditional, and binds the analysis to distance rather than authority.

The examples contribute by showing how the structural claims can be read without becoming evidence. The waiting-for-a-reply example shows how Λ becomes active in ordinary trust without turning silence into proof. The seminar example shows why rival frames must remain open and why public criteria, phenomenology, skepticism, and language-practice readings are not automatically PMS failures. The later-confirmation example shows why apparent confirmation does not retroactively erase prior uncertainty or authorize epistemic superiority. Together, the examples demonstrate the analysis; they do not prove it. They make the operator discipline readable while preserving the case boundary. Their function is calibrational: they help the reader see where Λ, Χ, Φ, weak Θ, bounded Ω, and ANT_min_weak become legible, while also showing where the article must stop.

What remains unclaimed is as important as what is claimed. The case does not prove that other minds exist. It does not describe another subject’s experience. It does not justify actual trust in any concrete scene. It does not diagnose, rank, validate, or authorize. It does not establish a legal, clinical, empirical, computational, or governance result. It does not validate PMS Base. It does not show that PMS defeats skeptical, phenomenological, Wittgensteinian, ethical, inferential, or computational rival readings. It shows only that the closure-demand can be mapped structurally while leaving Λ visible.

The boundary discipline is therefore the central methodological achievement of the case. PMS coherence is not proof. Non-capture is not failure. Drift risk is not drift finding. Under-specification is not low score. Resistance, refusal, silence, disagreement, rival framing, rupture, or absence must not be automatically translated into PMS drift, incomplete integration, failed maturity, or deficient praxis. Dignity-in-Practice remains a praxeological application constraint, not an ontological ranking. ANTICIPATION terms remain overlay handles, not PMS operators.

The final methodological takeaway is that PMS-AXIOM can treat a classical philosophical closure-demand without either dissolving it by fiat or inflating it into metaphysical authority. In this case, PMS does not give access to another mind. It clarifies the structure of acting, trusting, attributing, and waiting where such access does not arrive. The analysis is strongest when it preserves that absence: **other minds remain a site where structural legibility is possible, but final closure must not be claimed.**
