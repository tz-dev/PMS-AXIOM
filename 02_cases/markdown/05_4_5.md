### Why this case matters

The rule-following paradox matters because it exposes one of the most demanding forms of philosophical closure pressure: the demand to explain what makes an action count as following a rule rather than merely being compatible with some possible interpretation of that rule. The case is structurally difficult because it does not simply ask whether a rule has a meaning, whether an agent has an intention, or whether a community has a practice. It asks how rule, interpretation, application, action, criterion, and correctness can be linked without either infinite regress or illegitimate closure.

At first sight, rule-following appears to offer a stable form of order. A rule seems to determine what counts as correct application. Yet the paradox arises when the application of the rule appears to require interpretation. If the rule must be interpreted before it can guide action, then the interpretation itself seems to require interpretation, and so on. The expected closure event — the rule determining its application — does not arrive in the simple way demanded. The case therefore generates a strong Λ-structure: a meaningful absence of final closure inside a rule-application frame.

This makes the case especially suitable for AXIOM. AXIOM does not treat classical philosophical problems as targets for final metaphysical solution. It maps them as closure-demands inside explicit frames, identifies where PMS Core can make structural relations legible, and shows where add-on overlays are justified. The rule-following paradox is a paradigmatic LOGIC case because it is not merely about action, but about the limit of logical, interpretive, and justificatory closure. It asks whether the demand for rule-governed correctness can be satisfied by further interpretation, or whether such interpretation only relocates the demand.

The case belongs in AXIOM because it makes visible a structural pressure that appears across many philosophical and practical domains: the pressure to convert a frame into authority. A rule is a frame; interpretation is a recontextualization; application is an action-counting problem; correctness is an integration demand. If the analysis treats the rule as self-applying, it risks hiding the interpretive frame. If it treats interpretation as sufficient, it risks regress. If it treats practice, community, convention, intention, or formal semantics as the answer, it may stabilize the problem, but only under a declared rival formalization. AXIOM’s task is not to decide by authority which rival wins. Its task is to show the structural pattern of closure-demand and residue.

The case is not being solved metaphysically. PMS does not claim to reveal what rules “really are,” what meaning ultimately consists in, or whether facts about rule-following exist independently of practice, use, intention, or interpretation. The analysis also does not produce an empirical finding. No observed agent, community, institutional practice, or rule-application sequence is supplied. It is not clinical or diagnostic because no person, mental state, pathology, character type, competence profile, or psychological structure is being assessed. It is not legal or forensic because no violation, evidence record, liability frame, institutional sanction, or tribunal context is provided. It is not an implementation claim because no computational system, rule engine, formal interpreter, model architecture, or AI behavior is specified.

The philosophical difficulty is therefore preserved rather than neutralized. PMS Core can show that the case involves Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Θ, Φ, Χ, and contested Σ. PMS-LOGIC can show that the case is a boundary-management problem in which logic remains useful as ordering discipline but cannot become final authority. Yet neither layer may claim that the paradox has been eliminated. The correct structural result is disciplined non-closure: the case can be made legible without being closed by force.

The value of the case is thus not that it allows PMS to claim conquest over a classical problem. Its value is that it tests whether PMS and LOGIC can remain disciplined under a high-pressure closure demand. A weak reading would rush toward a solution. A stronger reading keeps the closure-demand visible, preserves the Λ-residue, distinguishes structural legibility from final justification, and leaves rival philosophical readings open.

### Scene and frame structure

The scene is conceptual rather than concrete. Its boundary is the philosophical formulation of the rule-following paradox: a rule is to be applied; application appears to require interpretation; interpretation itself may require further interpretation; and the question emerges what distinguishes following a rule from merely behaving in a way compatible with some possible interpretation.

The central frame □ is the rule-application frame. Inside this frame are the rule, possible interpretations of the rule, the action to be counted as following or not following, and the criteria by which such counting would be justified. The frame is not a legal frame, not a clinical frame, not an institutional governance frame, and not a computational implementation frame. It is a conceptual frame for asking what makes rule-following possible.

Inside the frame, several structural elements are active. First, there is a distinction between rule and action. Second, there is a distinction between interpretation and application. Third, there is a distinction between correctness and compatibility. Fourth, there is an expected closure: the rule should determine how it is to be followed. Fifth, there is a possible regress: if interpretation mediates application, interpretation itself may require interpretation. Sixth, there is a contested integration demand: rule, interpretation, criterion, and action must somehow be linked if rule-following is to be intelligible.

Outside the frame are stronger claims not warranted by the material. The analysis does not include a full theory of linguistic meaning, a phenomenology of understanding, an empirical account of training, a sociological account of community norm formation, a legal rule-interpretation doctrine, or a computational semantics of instruction execution. These may be rival or downstream frames, but they are not supplied as case material.

The protected constraints are reversibility, non-diagnosis, non-person-ranking, non-tribunal treatment, and preservation of Λ-residue. The case must not be forced into a PMS maturity story. It must not be used to claim that unresolved interpretation is a defect. It must not turn a rival philosophical account into “incomplete PMS integration.” It must preserve the possibility that the paradox is better handled by semantic, normative, communal-practice, pragmatic, skeptical, or formal-logical approaches.

No concrete roles are present. There is no named rule-follower, no evaluator, no authority, no affected party, no institution, and no sanctioning body. At most, one can identify conceptual roles: a rule, an interpreter, an action, an evaluator of correctness, and a possible practice or context in which rule-use is intelligible. These are not social roles in the strong PMS sense. Therefore Ω must remain weak unless a later scene supplies actual asymmetry.

Temporality Θ is nevertheless structurally relevant. Rule-following normally implies repeatability: the same rule is expected to constrain not only one action but possible future applications. The paradox depends on this temporal extension. If a rule applied only once in an isolated, non-repeatable way, the pressure to distinguish following from accidental compatibility would be less structurally intense. Rule-following involves continuity across cases, possible future correctness, and persistence of criteria.

The reversibility window is open. Because no concrete decision, binding institution, or enforcement frame is present, the PMS reading remains revisable. The analysis can be weakened, altered, or displaced if the case is reformulated as purely semantic, purely formal, practice-based, anti-regress, or skeptical in a way that changes the relevant operator weights. No Ψ-binding is warranted.

Exposure and asymmetry are weak. A rule may appear to hold authority over action, and criteria may appear to constrain performance, but this is only formal imbalance. It is not a concrete asymmetry of power, vulnerability, obligation, capacity, or exposure. Treating the rule-following paradox as if it already involved institutional authority or person-level responsibility would overextend Ω.

This conceptual-scene status has direct consequences for the entire article. It allows substantial structural analysis but forbids stronger downstream claims. It allows the case to be rendered as a LOGIC overlay case but not as a governance case. It allows examples to illustrate structure but not to function as evidence. It allows rival readings to remain live rather than being demoted to incomplete PMS calibrations.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading begins with Δ, Difference. The paradox depends on a precise set of distinctions: rule versus action, rule versus interpretation, following versus merely matching, correctness versus compatibility, and application versus retrospective rationalization. Without these distinctions, the problem dissolves into generic ambiguity. Δ is therefore active and major. Its calibration is relatively strong because the case material explicitly concerns what makes an action count as following a rule rather than merely fitting some possible interpretation. The overclaim would be to treat these distinctions as already resolving the problem. Δ can mark the difference; it cannot decide what grounds the distinction.

∇, Impulse, is weak-active. The case contains directional pressure toward application, determinacy, justification, and closure. Once a rule and an action are distinguished, the question presses: what makes this action an instance of following this rule? Yet no concrete motivational impulse, desire, institutional urgency, or agent-specific drive is supplied. ∇ therefore operates as conceptual application-pressure rather than psychological force. It would be an overclaim to infer a practical agent’s motivation or an institutional demand from this operator alone.

□, Frame, is active and central. The rule functions as a frame: it establishes a space in which some actions are treated as correct, incorrect, relevant, or irrelevant. Interpretation may function as a sub-frame or successor frame: it places the rule into a context of application. The paradox arises because each frame seems to require another frame to determine its own application. □ is therefore one of the strongest operators in the case. Its calibration requires explicitly distinguishing the rule-frame, interpretation-frame, and application-frame. The overclaim would be to say that because PMS describes the rule as a frame, it has explained the nature of rules.

Λ, Non-Event, is highly active. The expected event is closure: the rule determines its application. The paradox arises because this event does not arrive in the demanded form. Instead, interpretation appears, and interpretation may itself call for interpretation. Λ is the structured absence of final determinacy within the rule-application frame. This absence must not be treated as mere failure, ignorance, nihilism, or defect. It is the structural center of the case. Its calibration must preserve the possibility that non-closure is philosophically constitutive rather than simply a problem to be repaired.

Α, Attractor, is weak and conditional. The regress can be modeled as a recurrent pattern: rule calls for interpretation, interpretation calls for further interpretation, and so on. In that sense, Α can mark a recursive structure. But the case does not provide an empirical pattern, a stabilized practice, a habit, an institution, or a role-script. It is a philosophical regress, not an observed attractor in praxis. The overclaim would be to classify the paradox as a concrete attractor pattern or drift dynamic. Α may be used as formal analogy, not as a strong finding.

Ω, Asymmetry, is weak and conditional. One can identify formal imbalance between rule and application, criterion and performance, or rule-authority and interpretive flexibility. However, PMS Ω concerns structural imbalance in power, exposure, capacity, or obligation. The case material does not provide such conditions. No one is exposed to sanction; no authority imposes interpretation; no vulnerable role bears cost. Therefore Ω must not be inflated. Its calibration is one of the most important guardrails in the case. If Ω is pushed too far, the analysis risks importing governance, responsibility, or moral authority into a purely conceptual problem.

Θ, Temporality, is active but structural rather than empirical. Rule-following presupposes temporal extension because rules are expected to apply across cases. The paradox concerns not only a single action but the continuity of application: how can the same rule guide present and future actions? Θ is therefore necessary for understanding rule-following as repeatable, persistent, and criterion-bearing over time. Yet no concrete sequence is supplied. The overclaim would be to treat Θ as observed chronology or developmental trajectory.

Φ, Recontextualization, is active and central. Interpretation recontextualizes the rule. Each interpretation places the rule into an application frame; each further interpretation may recontextualize the prior interpretation. This is where the regress becomes visible. However, Φ must be calibrated carefully. Recontextualization is not automatically correction, maturity, drift, or success. In this case, Φ marks the movement by which a rule is placed into interpretive context, while the stability of that movement remains contested. The overclaim would be to treat every rival framing as Φ-success or every unresolved interpretive problem as incomplete integration.

Χ, Distance, is active in two ways. First, the paradox itself requires reflective distance from immediate rule-use. One steps back from ordinary application and asks what makes rule-following possible. Second, the PMS analysis requires Χ to avoid closure-by-analysis. PMS must not claim to settle the paradox simply because it can model its structure. Χ suspends final metaphysical, empirical, legal, diagnostic, and implementation claims. It also preserves rival readings. The overclaim would be to abandon distance and turn the PMS reading into a verdict.

Σ, Integration, is the central contested operator. The case asks whether rule, interpretation, action, and criterion can be integrated into a coherent account of following without circularity, infinite regress, hidden appeal to practice, or forced closure. Σ is therefore not achieved; it is at issue. PMS can identify integration-pressure, but it cannot treat its own coherent mapping as the missing philosophical integration. This is the highest coherence-risk point in the case. If Σ is pushed too far, PMS becomes a closure machine: it redescribes the problem elegantly and mistakes elegance for resolution.

Ψ, Self-Binding, is not active as a case-core operator. It would become relevant only if the case introduced an agent, community, institution, or practice binding itself to a rule across time. The material does not specify such a self-binding structure. There is no promise, identity commitment, role duty, institutional standard, or accountable self-relation. Ψ remains a possible downstream extension, not a present finding. The overclaim would be to infer self-binding merely because rule-following can involve commitment in other contexts.

The resulting PMS Core formula is best kept bounded:

Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Λ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ

with weak conditional extensions through Α and Ω, contested Σ, and unestablished Ψ. This formula keeps the strongest case-supported operators in view while preventing weak operators from being silently upgraded.

The formula should not be read as a solution sequence. It is a structural rendering of the case’s pressure points. Δ makes the contrast legible; ∇ pressures application; □ frames the rule; Λ marks missing closure; Θ extends the problem across possible applications; Φ marks interpretation as recontextualization; Χ holds reflective distance; Σ is demanded but not delivered; Ψ is not established.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The completed YAML places heavy emphasis on dependency discipline because this case invites shortcuts. Rule-following is precisely the kind of problem where a model can appear powerful by naming the relations too smoothly. PMS must therefore avoid using later operators cheaply.

The first dependency risk concerns Φ. PMS treats Φ as dependent on Θ, Ω, and □. In the case, □ and Θ are active, but Ω is weak. Therefore Φ can be used structurally for interpretation and recontextualization only with caution. It cannot be treated as full asymmetry-bearing transformation unless a later scene supplies concrete power, obligation, exposure, or role-differentiation.

The second dependency risk concerns Σ. Since Σ depends on Χ and Φ, and since Φ itself depends partly on weak Ω, Σ cannot be claimed as achieved integration. The paradox is about whether integration is possible, not an occasion for PMS to assert integration. The correct calibration is: Σ is at issue. It is a contested integration demand, not a result.

The third dependency risk concerns Ψ. Ψ depends on Σ, Θ, and Χ. Since Σ is not achieved and no self-binding agent is specified, Ψ cannot be activated as a finding. This prevents the analysis from smuggling in responsibility, commitment, stable normativity, or autobiographical coherence.

The fourth dependency risk concerns Ω inflation. It is tempting to treat the rule as authoritative and therefore asymmetrical. But PMS Ω is not merely logical priority. It concerns structural imbalance of power, exposure, capacity, or obligation. Without a concrete scene, Ω remains weak/formal. If it were inflated, the case would drift toward moral, legal, or governance language without warrant.

The fifth dependency risk concerns Α. A regress resembles recurrence, but not every recurrence is an attractor. Α in PMS refers to recurrent pattern or stabilization emerging from repeated structural interaction. The rule-following paradox supplies formal recursion, not necessarily stabilized praxis. Α therefore remains weak and conditional.

Coherence is not proof. This is the key methodological point. A PMS analysis can be internally coherent while failing to capture the philosophical force of the paradox. It can correctly identify Δ, □, Λ, Φ, Χ, and contested Σ, yet still leave unresolved what makes an action count as following a rule. In fact, if PMS coherence were treated as sufficient, the analysis would repeat the very closure error that the case warns against.

Non-capture is not failure. Some features of rule-following may remain only partially legible in PMS terms. The normative force of “following,” the semantic question of meaning, the role of communal practice, the skeptical challenge, and the anti-regress response may each preserve something that PMS does not fully capture. The analysis remains stronger, not weaker, when it marks those zones explicitly.

PMS must not become a closure machine. This means the analysis cannot convert every non-fit into Λ-defect, every rival reading into Φ-recontextualization, every unresolved tension into low Σ, or every absence into drift. The case demands exactly the opposite discipline: carry the absence, mark the frame, preserve distance, and keep integration unclaimed where it is not earned.

Invalid dependency shortcuts would include: using Ψ without a self-binding agent; using Σ as completed resolution; using Ω as concrete authority without asymmetry data; using Α as concrete pattern without praxis repetition; using Φ as automatic maturity; or using Λ as proof that the rival position fails. Each would exceed the case material and weaken the artefact.

The most tempting shortcut is Σ-totalization. Because the PMS analysis can make the paradox structurally legible, it may seem to have integrated the problem. But the case is precisely about the difference between making a structure legible and providing the criterion that closes rule-following. The analysis can show why closure is demanded and why it remains withheld; it cannot simply become the closure it describes.

The second tempting shortcut is Ψ-import. Because rule-following often involves commitment, one might be tempted to treat the case as self-binding. But no agent, community, institution, or practice has been specified as binding itself to a rule. Ψ therefore remains outside the core result. If a later example introduces a practice that adopts a convention, Ψ may appear locally, but that would be a feature of the example’s bounded scene, not the philosophical case as such.

The third tempting shortcut is Ω-import. Because “rule” can sound authoritative, one may hear power where only formal constraint is present. PMS blocks this. A rule may structure a frame without yet creating a concrete asymmetry of power, exposure, capacity, or obligation. If an institution enforces the rule, Ω becomes stronger; but the supplied case does not include such enforcement.

Dependency discipline is therefore not a technical appendix. It is the core safeguard that prevents AXIOM from becoming a verdict machine. The rule-following paradox should leave the analysis more disciplined, not more ambitious.

### PMS drift-pattern status

The PMS Core analysis does not classify a drift pattern as present. This is important. The rule-following paradox can resemble certain PMS drift configurations, but resemblance is not enough for assignment. A conceptual paradox is not a person, practice, institution, or action scene. It does not contain the empirical or role-structured conditions required to treat drift as a finding.

The first relevant pattern is **AD_A>>E**, excessive distance between Awareness and Enactment. At a superficial level, the rule-following paradox may look like a case where reflective awareness expands while enactment is delayed. The more one asks what it means to follow a rule, the less immediately available the action appears to be. Reflection seems to outrun application. However, the completed PMS Core YAML deliberately refuses to classify this as AD_A>>E. The reason is dependency discipline: AD_A>>E requires asymmetry-loaded structures, attractor formation, and recontextualization dynamics that affect the relation between awareness and action. The present case supplies no concrete action, no agent, no repeated scene, no exposure gradient, no institutional asymmetry, and no chronic delay pattern.

The correct status is therefore **under-specified**, not “low,” “failed,” or “pathological.” Awareness_A is structurally active because the case sustains framed distinctions across time, but Action_E is under-specified because no concrete action is supplied and Σ is unresolved. That does not mean the case has an action deficit. It means the case is not the kind of artefact in which action-axis suppression can be responsibly assigned.

The second relevant pattern is **AD_Sigma_low**, fragmented or low integration. Again, the paradox can resemble this pattern. Interpretation proliferates; integration remains contested; rule, interpretation, action, and criterion do not settle into a stable account. But PMS does not classify the case as AD_Sigma_low. The case contains a formal analogy to recontextualization outpacing integration, yet it lacks the concrete responsibility axis, asymmetry scene, repeated praxis trajectory, and role-bound stakes needed to treat AD_Sigma_low as a case finding.

Here too the correct status is **possible-but-not-entailed**. Coherence_C is weak and at issue; Responsibility_R is not applicable. That combination blocks drift assignment. Under-specification is not failure. Non-assignment is not a low score. PMS is doing its work precisely by refusing to turn a philosophical non-closure into a maturity verdict.

The major coherence risk is that the PMS mapping may become too smooth. The sequence Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Θ, Φ, Χ, and contested Σ fits the rule-following paradox well. That fit creates a danger: the analysis could begin to treat structural readability as philosophical solution. The YAML explicitly guards against that risk. PMS coherence is insufficient by itself. A coherent PMS derivation must still show discriminative force, and it must preserve rival readings that may capture aspects PMS does not.

The under-discrimination risk is also high. Many interpretive or justificatory problems can be mapped as framed difference plus closure pressure plus non-event plus recontextualization plus contested integration. If the same formula were applied indiscriminately to ordinary ambiguity, legal interpretation, semantic underdetermination, hermeneutic circularity, computational instruction-following, and the rule-following paradox, the PMS reading would become too general. The drift-pattern discipline prevents that. It says: this case has structural similarities to drift risks, but no drift is assigned.

This is not a weakness of the artefact. It is a quality-control feature. PMS does not become more powerful by assigning more patterns. It becomes more reliable by withholding patterns where the evidence is absent.

A drift risk can be analytically relevant without becoming a finding. That distinction is essential for this case. The paradox does create pressure toward over-reflection, forced integration, and boundary closure. But those pressures are features of the philosophical structure, not evidence of a deficient agent, practice, or institution. The case therefore teaches a methodological restraint: do not convert structural resemblance into classification; do not convert non-closure into failure; do not convert under-specification into low performance.

PMS-LOGIC reads the case through its tripartition: **pre-moral justification**, **logical boundary management**, and **post-moral effects**.

At the **pre-moral justification** level, the case is active. The rule-following paradox becomes structurally legible before moral or normative interpretation. The central LOGIC result is therefore sustainable non-closure. The case reaches a point where the analysis can say: closure is structurally demanded, but it is also structurally withheld. The counter-readings remain active. The most important is that the analysis may “win by translation,” not by final superiority. PMS-LOGIC may be able to translate the paradox into its terms, but translation success is not philosophical settlement. The relevant test is whether the grammar preserves differentiating force under pressure. This counter-reading is not a defect. It is part of the overlay’s validity discipline.

### Example 1 — The arithmetic continuation that does not settle itself

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** After “Scene and frame structure,” before the PMS Core operator-by-operator reading.

A familiar way to make the rule-following paradox readable is to imagine a simple arithmetic instruction: continue the sequence by adding 2. Up to a certain point, the sequence appears unproblematic: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10. A learner then writes 12, and the continuation is accepted. In this bounded vignette, nothing dramatic has happened. The rule seems to guide the action, the action seems to follow the rule, and the frame appears stable.

The paradox appears only when the scene is shifted from ordinary use into reflective analysis. What makes “12” count as following the rule “add 2”? One answer is: because “add 2” means add 2. But that answer repeats the rule rather than explaining how the rule fixes its application. Another answer is: because the learner interpreted the rule correctly. Yet the interpretation itself may now be questioned. What makes that interpretation the correct one? If the interpretation is explained by another interpretation, the same problem returns.

The point of the example is not that arithmetic is impossible, nor that learners cannot follow rules. It demonstrates the structural pressure identified in the case: an apparently stable rule-frame can generate a demand for closure when one asks what makes an action count as following the rule rather than merely matching a possible continuation. The example makes Δ visible: rule versus action, following versus compatibility, interpretation versus application. It also activates □, because “add 2” operates within a frame of arithmetic practice. Λ appears when the expected final event — the rule determining its application without remainder — fails to arrive under reflective pressure.

The PMS Core hooks are therefore clear but bounded. Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Θ, Φ, and Χ are active. Θ matters because the rule is expected to hold across future applications, not only for one step. Φ appears when the rule is placed into an interpretive frame. Χ appears when ordinary rule-use is suspended and analyzed reflectively. Σ remains at issue: the example does not prove that rule, interpretation, action, and criterion have been integrated into a final account. Ψ is not established, because no self-binding agent or institution is being analyzed.

The PMS-LOGIC overlay sharpens the example. Logic can clarify the structure of the continuation, but it cannot become final authority simply by restating the rule or adding another interpretation. The active overlay signature is LOGIC_boundary_management: the rule-frame demands application, Λ marks missing final closure, Χ prevents forced resolution, and Σ remains contested. NON_CLOSURE_stability is also relevant: the example can carry the absence of final philosophical closure without treating ordinary arithmetic practice as defective.

MIP, AHP, and QC are not activated here. The vignette is not a governance artefact, not a policy case, not a high-stakes institutional output, and not an analysis-hardening exercise. It remains illustrative.

The forbidden claims are important. This example does not show that arithmetic has no meaning, that rule-following is impossible, that learners are confused, or that PMS solves the paradox. It does not diagnose the learner, evaluate competence, or make an empirical claim about mathematical education. It also does not prove a skeptical thesis. It shows only how the closure-demand becomes structurally visible.

The non-capture note is that ordinary arithmetic practice may be better explained by training, communal correction, formal semantics, or use. PMS does not replace those readings. It marks the structural closure-pressure that appears when the practice is reflectively questioned.

**Article takeaway:** The example shows why the case is not about whether rules can be used, but about what kind of closure is being demanded when one asks what makes use count as following.

### Example 2 — The board-game rule and the boundary move

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** In the PMS Core section under Φ, Χ, and Σ, or later in the LOGIC overlay section under “logical boundary management.”

Consider a board game with a written rule: “A piece may move to any adjacent open square.” In ordinary play, the rule seems clear. If a square shares an edge with the current square and no piece occupies it, the move is allowed. Several turns proceed without dispute. Then a boundary case appears: a square touches the current square only at a corner. One player says it is adjacent; another says adjacency means edge-sharing only. The written rule does not specify which meaning is intended.

The players now interpret the rule. One says that in ordinary speech, “adjacent” can mean touching at any point. Another says that in this game’s diagrams, movement has always followed edge-lines. A third suggests checking the designer’s notes. A fourth proposes a house convention. Each interpretation can be made plausible inside some frame. Yet each interpretation also raises a further question: why is that frame the relevant one? Why should ordinary speech, diagram convention, designer intent, prior play, or current agreement decide the rule?

The example demonstrates the paradox in a concrete but bounded way. The issue is not that the rule is useless. The game can continue. The players may agree on a convention. But the philosophical closure-demand remains: what makes one interpretation the rule-following one rather than merely one compatible way to continue the practice?

In PMS Core terms, Δ appears through the distinction between legal and illegal move, edge-adjacent and corner-adjacent, rule and interpretation, agreement and correctness. ∇ is the pressure to settle the move so the game can proceed. □ is the game frame: the rule has force only inside the practice of this game. Λ appears because the expected determinacy of the rule does not arrive at the boundary case. Α is weak: prior play and diagram habits may stabilize an interpretation, but the example does not prove a full attractor structure. Ω remains weak unless one player has authority to impose a ruling. Θ is active because a ruling now affects future turns. Φ is central because each proposed interpretation recontextualizes the rule. Χ is required to avoid turning urgency into forced closure. Σ is contested: the group may integrate rule text, prior play, and convention into a workable decision, but that practical integration is not a metaphysical solution to rule-following. Ψ would arise only if the group binds itself to the new convention across future play; even then, it would be a local commitment, not a theory of rules.

The PMS-LOGIC overlay is especially useful here. The case shows the difference between operational settlement and final authority. The players may need a decision. But PMS-LOGIC prevents the decision from being treated as proof that the rule always contained the answer in a final, interpretation-free way. LOGIC_boundary_management is active: □ frames the dispute, Λ marks the missing closure, Χ restrains forced certainty, and Σ may produce a local integration. NON_CLOSURE_stability is relevant because the game can proceed while the philosophical residue remains visible.

Effective capacity and responsibility are not activated by default. If the vignette were turned into a tournament with official rules, penalties, ranking, and authority, MIP might become relevant. But as stated, this is not a governance case. AHP is not needed because no downstream institutional artefact is being hardened. QC is not separately invoked.

Forbidden claims include: the players are irrational; the rule has no meaning; the dispute proves relativism; the majority’s decision is metaphysically correct; or PMS has found the true interpretation. The example also must not be used to infer legal interpretation principles or adjudication doctrine. It is a bounded illustration of frame, boundary, non-closure, and provisional integration.

The non-capture note is that a language-practice reading may explain the case through training and shared usage; a formal game-design reading may solve it by adding definitions; a pragmatic reading may emphasize playability over philosophical closure. PMS preserves those rivals.

**Article takeaway:** The example shows how a rule can remain practically usable while still leaving a Λ-residue when the demand shifts from play to final justification.

### Example 3 — The instruction-following system and the false comfort of formal clarity

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** In the LOGIC overlay section, especially after “failure-mode constraints” or before “Layer interaction.”

Imagine a simplified instruction-following system. It receives a natural-language instruction: “Sort the messages by importance.” The system ranks messages using a defined procedure: sender priority, keywords, deadline proximity, and previous user interactions. In most ordinary cases, the output looks reasonable. A message from a supervisor about a deadline appears above a newsletter. A meeting cancellation appears above a general update. The rule appears to work.

A boundary case then appears. One message contains no urgent keywords but comes from a close collaborator and concerns a slowly developing risk. Another contains urgent language but is routine marketing. The system ranks the marketing message higher because it matches the formal importance markers. A human reviewer says the system did not really follow the instruction; it merely followed one interpretation of importance.

This example must be handled carefully. It is not an implementation analysis and does not validate or criticize any real AI system. It is an abstract vignette designed to make the LOGIC overlay readable. The instruction “sort by importance” functions as a rule-frame. The procedure is an interpretation of that frame. The output is an action-like result inside the system’s bounded task. The dispute arises when compatibility with the formal procedure is questioned as genuine following of the instruction.

PMS Core identifies the structure. Δ differentiates instruction, interpretation, procedure, output, and successful following. ∇ appears as the pressure to operationalize “importance.” □ is the task frame: sorting messages according to a declared procedure. Λ appears when the expected closure — the procedure fully capturing importance — fails at a boundary case. Α may be weakly visible if repeated procedure-use stabilizes a default ranking pattern, but no empirical attractor is claimed. Ω remains weak unless the system’s ranking affects people under institutional authority. Θ is active because repeated sorting can create path dependence in what receives attention. Φ is active because “importance” shifts across contexts: urgency, relationship, risk, institutional role, and user preference may each reframe it. Χ is required to avoid treating procedure as final authority. Σ is at issue because heterogeneous criteria must be integrated. Ψ is not established unless an institution binds itself to this ranking standard.

The PMS-LOGIC overlay makes the key point sharper: formal clarity can produce false comfort. A procedure may be consistent and still fail to settle the rule-following question. LOGIC_boundary_management is active because the formal frame orders the task but does not exhaust the meaning of the instruction. NON_CLOSURE_stability is active because the boundary case should not be hidden by saying “the system followed the rule because it followed the procedure.” Technocratic reduction / □-overconstraint is a risk: the formal frame may be mistaken for the whole reality of importance. Dogmatism / Σ-totalization is also a risk if the integrated ranking procedure is treated as final. Authority capture / Ω-inflation would become relevant if an institution used the ranking to allocate attention, opportunity, or sanction without contest channels.

MIP and AHP are not activated in the vignette as written. They would become relevant only if the instruction-following artefact were used in a real institutional setting where outputs affected people, priorities, compliance, or accountability. At that point, governance relevance, auditability, effective capacity, contestability, and claim-hardening would need to be assessed. The present example remains guardrail-only.

Forbidden claims are strict. This example does not claim that AI systems cannot follow rules, that formal procedures are useless, that humans always understand importance better, or that PMS-LOGIC validates an AI safety result. It does not make an implementation claim, empirical claim, legal claim, diagnostic claim, or metaphysical claim about machine understanding. It also does not assign blame to designers, users, or systems.

The non-capture note is important. A formal semantics reading might specify “importance” operationally and declare the procedure correct inside that system. A pragmatic design reading might say the solution is to improve the ranking criteria. A human-computer interaction reading might focus on user feedback and correction loops. PMS-LOGIC does not replace these. It shows the boundary risk: a formal interpretation of a rule can be useful without becoming final authority over what the rule means.

**Article takeaway:** The example shows why PMS-LOGIC is needed as an overlay: it protects the difference between operational procedure and final rule-following closure, especially where formal clarity may conceal Λ-residue.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The analysis preserves multiple rival readings. They are not defects. They mark what PMS and PMS-LOGIC may not fully capture.

A **semantic reading** treats the case as a problem about meaning and reference. On this view, the central question is not primarily praxis structure but how signs, concepts, intentions, or meanings determine extensions. PMS can model rule and interpretation as frames, but it does not thereby solve the semantic question of what fixes meaning.

A **normative reading** treats the core issue as correctness. What makes one application correct and another incorrect? The PMS analysis can mark correctness as a closure-demand and integration pressure, but it does not generate normativity. PMS-LOGIC explicitly refuses to derive oughts. Therefore the normative force of “following” may remain partially non-captured.

A **communal-practice reading** treats rule-following as grounded in shared practices, training, correction, and public criteria. PMS can describe practice stabilization through Α, Θ, □, and possibly Ψ in later scenes, but the present case does not supply such a concrete practice. A practice-based account might preserve the ordinary functioning of rules better than a purely conceptual regress model.

A **skeptical reading** treats the paradox as showing that no fact fixes rule-following beyond compatible interpretation. PMS can carry Λ as non-closure, but it does not decide whether the skeptical conclusion is correct. LOGIC can prevent dogmatic closure, but it cannot refute skepticism by structural description alone.

An **anti-regress reading** rejects the assumption that every application requires interpretation. It may argue that ordinary rule-use is not mediated by an explicit interpretive act at every step. This reading challenges the case’s central closure-demand. If successful, it would weaken the PMS reading by reducing Φ and Λ.

A **formal-logical reading** treats rule-following as relation among symbol, function, input, and output. PMS-LOGIC can clarify the boundary of formal interpretation, but it does not supply a full formal semantics. If a formal system defines rule application internally, the PMS case would need to be reframed around implementation and formal closure.

A **pragmatic reading** treats rule-following as stabilized by use, training, correction, and successful coordination. This reading preserves the practical workability of rules without requiring metaphysical finality. PMS can support parts of this through Α, Θ, and □, but the present conceptual case does not provide the empirical or practice-level data needed to claim that stabilization.

A **phenomenological reading**, though not central in the YAML, may ask how rule-following is experienced as grasp, understanding, or directedness. PMS explicitly does not make phenomenological claims. Awareness_A is structural, not experiential. Any such reading remains outside the present artefact.

An **epistemological reading** may emphasize justification: how do we know that an action follows a rule? PMS-LOGIC is closest to this reading, but still does not claim final epistemic closure. It can show boundary-management; it cannot guarantee warrant.

An **anti-capture reading** objects that translating the paradox into PMS already loses its philosophical force. This is the most important methodological challenge. PMS must answer not by insisting on its own adequacy, but by preserving non-capture zones and falsifier conditions. The case remains stronger when this objection remains visible.

The three examples above help display these non-capture zones without resolving them. The arithmetic example shows how ordinary rule-use can function while philosophical closure remains unsettled. The board-game example shows how practice can locally stabilize a rule without metaphysical finality. The instruction-following example shows how formalization can be useful while still leaving boundary residue. None of the examples defeats rival readings. Each keeps open the possibility that another framework may preserve a feature PMS renders only partially.

The semantic rival may say: the issue is meaning, not operator structure. The normative rival may say: the issue is correctness, not closure-pressure. The communal-practice rival may say: the issue is public criteria and training, not interpretive regress. The formal rival may say: the issue is well-defined semantics. The pragmatic rival may say: the issue is use and correction. PMS-LOGIC’s proper response is not to absorb these rivals as incomplete PMS. Its response is to mark the structural closure-demand they address and the residue that remains if closure is overclaimed.

Non-capture is therefore not a concession of failure. It is part of the case’s methodological integrity. The rule-following paradox is exactly the kind of case where total capture would be suspicious. If the analysis sounded too final, it would betray the boundary discipline it claims to demonstrate.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS reading would be weakened if the case were reformulated so that rule, interpretation, action, and criterion were not distinct. Δ is the entry point. If that differentiation disappears, the PMS mapping loses force.

It would also be weakened if the case did not involve interpretation. If rule application were shown to proceed without interpretive mediation in the relevant sense, Φ would become weak or inactive, and Λ might no longer mark interpretive non-closure. This is especially relevant for anti-regress and practice-based readings.

The reading would be weakened if no temporality or repeatability were involved. Rule-following is structurally tied to possible future applications. If the case were reduced to a timeless one-off relation, Θ would become overextended.

It would be weakened if no closure-demand were present. PMS-LOGIC depends on the idea that rule-following generates a demand for final determination. If a rival reading shows that no such final determination is required, then LOGIC_boundary_management remains useful but less central.

The reading would become under-discriminating if the same formula were applied to too many problems without differentiation. Ordinary ambiguity, legal interpretation, semantic underdetermination, hermeneutic circularity, computational instruction-following, and rule-following paradoxes are not identical. PMS must show why this case specifically involves rule-application, interpretation-regress, and action-counting.

The reading would become over-assimilative if every rival framing were redescribed as Φ, every unresolved tension as Λ, every coherence demand as Σ, and every resistance as incomplete integration. This would turn PMS into a universal absorber. The YAML explicitly blocks this.

The LOGIC overlay would be weakened if the case were not about logical, justificatory, interpretive, or closure-boundary pressure. It would also be weakened if a rival formalization supplied a determinate account of rule application without regress and without hidden non-closure. In that case, LOGIC might still describe boundary discipline, but it would no longer be the primary overlay.

The LOGIC reading would overreach if it treated non-closure as final wisdom, logic as superior authority, or boundary-management as solution. It would also overreach if it activated post-moral effects, moral readability, responsibility, non-innocence, or effective capacity without concrete scene data.

Operationalization remains unfulfilled. For a stronger PMS application, the analysis would need a specified rule, a concrete action or sequence of actions, a frame of application, criteria for correctness, whether interpretation is explicit or implicit, and whether a community, institution, evaluator, or agent is involved. It would need temporal scope, observable markers of closure and non-closure, rival formalizations, and criteria for when Φ is productive recontextualization versus regress. It would need criteria for when Σ is achieved, failed, or merely asserted.

For empirical operationalization, one would need observations of rule application, disagreement, correction, training, or interpretation. For computational operationalization, one would need a formal rule system, execution semantics, input-output traces, and a distinction between rule-following and post-hoc compatibility. For legal operationalization, one would need a legal text, jurisdiction, interpretive doctrine, parties, evidence, and decision authority. For clinical operationalization, which is not warranted here, one would need entirely different data and ethical controls; the present case does not invite that layer.

For MIP activation, one would need downstream governance relevance: a case artefact intended to guide institutional use, public legitimacy, policy, audit, or responsibility handling. For AHP activation, one would need second-order analysis-artifact hardening: claim testing, counterfactual handles, revision hooks, or anti-overclaim safeguards for a high-stakes output. Neither is invoked here.

The examples also show what would be required for stronger claims. The arithmetic example would need a theory of mathematical practice, pedagogy, or formal semantics before it could support claims about actual learning. The board-game example would need a concrete game authority, rulebook, convention, or dispute procedure before it could support governance or responsibility claims. The instruction-following example would need an actual system, task specification, evaluation protocol, and downstream effect before it could support implementation, AI safety, or institutional claims. As written, all three examples remain illustrative.

A key falsifier condition is loss of discriminative force. If PMS-LOGIC can only say that the case involves “non-closure under a frame,” it has not done enough. The case must be specifically located as rule-following non-closure: the gap between rule, interpretation, application, and action-counting. If that specificity is lost, the analysis becomes a generic closure template.

Another falsifier condition is authority smuggling. If the final article implies that PMS or LOGIC has solved the paradox by naming its structure, the case fails its own discipline. The correct conclusion must preserve what remains unclaimed.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It does not describe a person’s ability to follow rules, interpret language, understand norms, comply with instructions, or reason coherently. It does not identify a deficit, disorder, maturity level, or psychological trait.

It must not be used for person ranking. No person, community, philosophical school, language practice, or institution is ranked as more mature, more coherent, more dignified, more rational, or more legitimate. Dignity-in-Practice is an application constraint, not a scale of worth.

It must not be used as a legal or forensic conclusion. It does not decide whether someone followed a rule, violated a rule, intended a violation, acted negligently, or bears liability. Legal rule interpretation requires its own frame and evidence.

It must not be used as metaphysical proof. PMS does not prove what rules are, what meaning is, whether normativity is real, whether skepticism is correct, or whether communal practice grounds rule-following.

It must not be used as empirical proof. The case contains no observations, experiments, interviews, institutional records, or behavioral traces. It is conceptual.

It must not be used as implementation validation. It does not show that an AI system, formal interpreter, software agent, legal engine, or instruction-following model actually follows rules. It does not validate computational rule execution.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. A successful case rendering does not prove PMS_1.3 complete, uniquely ordered, or superior to all rival frameworks.

The LOGIC overlay must not be treated as an operator system. Its reduced signatures are shorthand only. LOGIC does not add operators to PMS, modify dependencies, or override PMS Core. It sharpens boundary visibility.

Drift must not become verdict. AD_A>>E and AD_Sigma_low are not assigned. LOGIC drift risks are risks, not findings. Dogmatism, authority capture, pseudo-ought injection, Λ-suppression, and □-overconstraint are not attributed to any person or tradition.

There must be no closure-by-authority. The analysis must not end with “PMS says so” or “LOGIC resolves it.” The proper result is disciplined non-closure: what can be structurally mapped is mapped; what remains unresolved remains visible.

Examples must not be converted into evidence. The arithmetic, board-game, and instruction-following examples illustrate the structural reading. They do not prove the paradox, validate PMS, defeat rivals, or establish empirical claims. They help make the operator structure readable without carrying evidentiary authority.

Resistance, refusal, non-fit, disagreement, rupture, or absence must not automatically be treated as drift. A rival reading may preserve something real. A disagreement may mark a frame difference. An absence may be a Λ-residue rather than a defect. A refusal to accept PMS may be a legitimate anti-capture challenge, not incomplete integration.

Dignity-in-Practice remains praxeological, not ontological. It constrains how the analysis handles asymmetry, attribution, and possible misuse. It does not prove moral superiority, assign dignity levels, or rank beings.

No add-on term may be smuggled into PMS Base. Logic, morality, non-innocence, effective capacity, failure-mode constraint, and LOGIC drift are overlay handles. They do not become Δ–Ψ operators.

The misuse boundary is therefore not external to the article. It is part of the case result. The rule-following paradox becomes an AXIOM case only because its closure-demand is mapped without turning the map into authority.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result is that the rule-following paradox is best rendered as a structurally reflective rule-application problem in which framed difference and application-pressure encounter a meaningful absence of final closure. PMS Core makes the operator architecture visible: Δ distinguishes rule, action, interpretation, following, and compatibility; ∇ marks pressure toward application; □ frames the rule-application space; Λ marks the non-event of final rule-determined closure; Θ gives rule-following its repeatable temporal scope; Φ marks interpretation as recontextualization; Χ preserves reflective distance; Σ remains contested; and Ψ is not established. Α and Ω remain weak or conditional, not absent from all possible extensions but not strong enough for concrete findings in this case.

The LOGIC overlay adds a second layer of precision. It shows that the case is not merely about rule-use but about logical boundary management. Rule, interpretation, derivation, consistency, and application can organize inquiry, but they cannot simply become final authority. PMS-LOGIC therefore identifies the strongest overlay signature as **LOGIC_boundary_management + NON_CLOSURE_stability**, with **PRE_MORAL_justification** active and post-moral effects inactive. This preserves the difference between structural legibility and philosophical resolution.

The examples contribute by making the same boundary visible at different scales. The arithmetic continuation shows how ordinary rule-use can function while reflective closure remains unsettled. The board-game rule shows how practical agreement or local convention can stabilize play without eliminating Λ-residue. The instruction-following vignette shows why formal clarity can conceal rather than solve the difference between operational procedure and final rule-following closure. None of these examples serves as proof, evidence, implementation validation, or rival-reading defeat. They demonstrate the analysis without replacing it, and they show why the article must preserve the difference between usable local settlement and final philosophical closure.

What remains unclaimed is substantial. The article does not claim a metaphysical theory of rules or meaning. It does not claim that rule-following is impossible. It does not claim that practice, semantics, intention, community, formal systems, or pragmatics fail. It does not derive a norm, obligation, blame structure, or responsibility attribution. It does not classify a drift pattern as present. It does not validate PMS, PMS-LOGIC, an implementation, or a governance artefact. It does not settle whether the rule-following paradox should ultimately be read semantically, normatively, pragmatically, communally, skeptically, formally, or anti-regressively.

The boundary discipline is the central achievement. PMS Core is allowed to map the case, but not to close it. PMS-LOGIC is allowed to sharpen logical non-closure, but not to transform boundary-management into final authority. Drift risks remain risks, not findings. Dignity-in-Practice remains a restraint on analysis, not an ontological ranking. MIP and AHP remain inactive because no downstream governance or hardening task has been supplied. Coherence is not proof. Non-capture is not failure. Non-assignment is not a low score.

The final methodological takeaway is that the rule-following paradox is an AXIOM case precisely because it resists cheap closure. PMS and PMS-LOGIC can show where the closure-demand arises, which operators carry the case, which derived structures remain inactive, which drift risks must be guarded against, and where Λ-residue must remain visible. The result is not a verdict on rule-following, not a replacement for semantic, normative, communal-practice, skeptical, pragmatic, or formal-logical readings, and not a PMS Base validation claim. It is a disciplined cartography of why the demand for final rule-application closure cannot be satisfied by simply adding another interpretation, and why a responsible structural analysis must stop short of claiming more.
