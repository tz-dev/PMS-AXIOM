### Why this case matters

“Free Will vs Determinism” is one of the classical closure-demand cases because it appears to ask for a final settlement between two competing pictures of action. On one side, the language of free will seems to require that agents could have acted otherwise, that responsibility is not merely an after-effect of causal forces, and that action can be meaningfully attributed to someone as their own. On the other side, determinism presses toward causal continuity: every event appears as part of a sequence of prior conditions, constraints, mechanisms, histories, environments, and dependencies.

The case matters for AXIOM because it is a paradigmatic example of a philosophical problem that often demands more closure than the frame can legitimately deliver. The closure-demand is not merely conceptual. It is loaded with consequences: responsibility, blame, accountability, punishment, excuse, agency, autonomy, and standing are often drawn into the same field. Yet the title itself supplies no concrete action, no agent, no evidence, no institutional setting, no legal standard, no empirical study, and no actual attribution scene. It only supplies a high-level opposition.

That makes the case structurally difficult in a precise way. PMS can map the distinction between causal explanation and responsibility attribution, but it cannot decide whether ultimate metaphysical freedom exists. It can show how responsibility may be structurally modeled through frame, asymmetry, temporality, integration, and self-binding. It can also show where a demand for metaphysical closure leaves a Λ-residue. But the analysis must not convert structural readability into metaphysical solution.

This is why the case belongs in AXIOM. It tests whether PMS can remain disciplined when a famous problem invites premature victory. The strength of the analysis lies not in saying “PMS solves free will,” but in saying exactly what PMS may say, what it may not say, and where rival formalizations remain intact.

The case is therefore not being solved metaphysically. PMS does not show whether humans are ultimately free or determined. It is not being solved empirically, because no neuroscientific, behavioral, psychological, or historical evidence is supplied. It is not being solved clinically or diagnostically, because no person, symptom, pathology, capacity assessment, or mental state is under analysis. It is not being solved legally or forensically, because no proceeding, standard of proof, culpability threshold, or evidentiary record is present. It is not an implementation case, because no AI system, agent architecture, simulation, or software model is specified. Finally, it is not PMS Base validation. A successful structural rendering of this case does not prove PMS complete, uniquely ordered, or superior to rival grammars.

The difficulty of the case is that it invites several illegitimate shortcuts at once. It invites a metaphysical shortcut: if responsibility is structurally readable, then free will must exist. It invites a deterministic shortcut: if causal explanation is available, then responsibility must disappear. It invites a compatibilist shortcut: if the two can be mapped into different frames, the debate is solved. It invites a practical shortcut: if responsibility works socially, metaphysical residue can be ignored. PMS resists each shortcut by keeping the frame, operator dependencies, non-capture zones, and claim boundaries explicit.

The first structural fact is not that either side is true, but that a difference has become legible. The relevant difference is between agency-language and causal-necessity-language. Secondary differences follow: choice versus constraint, responsibility versus explanation, authorship versus prior condition, practical accountability versus metaphysical causality. Δ is therefore active and carries major analytic weight. The calibration risk is conflation: if the analysis treats all these differences as the same difference, or treats the title as proof that the opposition is exhaustive, Δ is overextended.

∇, Impulse, is weak but present. The “versus” structure creates directional pressure. The debate does not merely list two topics; it pressures the reader toward comparison, adjudication, reconciliation, or refusal. This is an argumentative impulse, not a psychological drive. PMS must not infer subjective desire, anxiety, or need for closure from the title alone. The operator becomes overclaimed if the analysis treats the debate’s pressure as evidence of a concrete actor’s motivation.

□, Frame, is active and major. The frame is the philosophical-conceptual frame. It defines the case as a question about the relation between freedom and causal explanation rather than as a legal proceeding, clinical evaluation, empirical study, theological doctrine, or implementation problem. This frame is crucial because many errors in free-will debates arise when conclusions travel silently across frames. A metaphysical claim is treated as a legal conclusion; an empirical finding is treated as a moral excuse; a responsibility practice is treated as proof of ultimate freedom. PMS keeps the frame explicit so that such transport does not occur unnoticed.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The dependency discipline is central because this case strongly tempts shortcutting. One common shortcut is Δ/∇ → Ψ: a distinction and closure-pressure are converted directly into responsibility, self-binding, blame, or agency. In the free-will debate, this appears when someone says: because there is choice-language, the agent must be ultimately responsible; or because there is causal explanation, the agent cannot be responsible. Both moves skip frame declaration, non-event residue, asymmetry calibration, temporal trajectory, recontextualization, distance, and integration.

A second invalid shortcut is Θ → determinism. PMS temporality is an operator for trajectory, persistence, sequence, delay, and development. It is not a metaphysical doctrine of causal necessity. Treating Θ as proof of determinism would redefine PMS and overstate the case.

A third shortcut is Φ → resolution. Reframing the debate as practical responsibility rather than metaphysical freedom may be useful, but it does not defeat metaphysical rivals. Compatibilism, hard determinism, libertarian free will, theological determinism, existential agency, and empirical agency research remain possible rival formalizations. PMS must not treat recontextualization as automatic improvement.

A fourth shortcut is Χ → evacuation. Distance is necessary, but if it merely neutralizes all claims, the analysis loses discriminative force. Χ must hold the field open while still making structural distinctions.

A fifth shortcut is Σ → proof. Integration may produce a coherent PMS account: causal explanation and responsibility attribution can coexist within different frames. But coherence is insufficient by itself. A coherent PMS rendering does not show that the rendering is exhaustive, empirically true, metaphysically correct, legally valid, or superior to rival theories.

A sixth shortcut is Ψ → metaphysical selfhood. PMS self-binding can model how commitments stabilize across time. It cannot establish consciousness, ultimate authorship, or metaphysical freedom. In this case, Ψ remains conditional.

Non-capture is not failure. If PMS cannot settle free will, that does not mean PMS has failed. It means the case contains questions PMS is not authorized to answer under the supplied material. A disciplined boundary result is still a valid AXIOM output. In fact, the case is valuable because it shows that PMS can resist becoming a closure machine.

The dependency table matters because late operators are structurally expensive. Ω depends on pattern stabilization; Θ depends on asymmetry and attractor conditions; Φ depends on temporality, asymmetry, and frame; Χ depends on recontextualization, temporality, and frame; Σ depends on distance and recontextualization; Ψ depends on integration, temporality, and distance. This does not mean a case must always activate every dependency strongly before later operators can be mentioned. It means late operators must be marked accurately: active, weak, guarded, analytic-only, or not applicable. In this case, most late operators are not active findings.

### Example 1 — The delayed apology

**Type:** micro
**Recommended insertion point:** after “Scene and frame structure”

A person misses an important commitment and later says: “I could not help it; the situation determined what happened.” Another person replies: “You still need to take responsibility.” The vignette is deliberately small. It does not ask whether the first person possesses metaphysical free will. It also does not decide whether the missed commitment legally matters, clinically indicates anything, or morally defines the person. The example only stages the structural difference between causal explanation and responsibility attribution.

What the example demonstrates is the basic PMS split between two frames. In one frame, the question is explanatory: what sequence of prior constraints, delays, pressures, habits, or conditions led to the missed commitment? In another frame, the question is praxeological: what commitment was active, who was exposed to the non-event, what repair remains possible, and how should the relation remain reversible rather than collapse into blame? The free-will/determinism debate becomes structurally visible because the two questions are often forced into one closure demand.

The PMS Core hooks are straightforward. **Δ** marks the difference between causal explanation and responsibility attribution. **∇** appears as the pressure to settle the matter quickly: either “not responsible, because determined” or “fully responsible, because free.” **□** separates the explanatory frame from the responsibility frame. **Λ** appears as the missing closure: no final metaphysical proof arrives that would eliminate the practical remainder. **Θ** is present only weakly, through the sequence of commitment, missed act, delay, and possible repair. **Ω** remains guarded: there may be exposure asymmetry between the person who missed the commitment and the person affected by it, but the example does not license blame, ranking, or sanction. **Χ** is the key guardrail: it prevents the example from becoming a verdict. **Σ** and **Ψ** remain conditional; they would require a fuller account of repair, integration, and renewed self-binding.

No add-on is required. PMS Core already makes the frame distinction visible. MIP, AHP, QC, and QC-EXT are not invoked because the vignette is not a governance artefact, not an analysis-hardening task, not a technical workflow, and not an implementation case.

The forbidden claims are important. The example does not prove free will. It does not prove determinism. It does not decide whether the person “really could have done otherwise.” It does not diagnose avoidance, irresponsibility, compulsion, or character. It does not establish legal liability or moral desert.

The non-capture note is that the person’s lived experience of choosing, feeling constrained, regretting, or wanting repair is not captured by the structural map. PMS can clarify the relation between explanation, commitment, and repair, but it does not exhaust phenomenology or metaphysics.

The article takeaway is that responsibility can remain structurally discussable without pretending that metaphysical freedom has been solved. PMS does not close the debate; it keeps the frames apart so that explanation does not automatically become excuse and responsibility does not automatically become blame.

### Example 2 — The institutional review

**Type:** compact
**Recommended insertion point:** after “PMS dependency and calibration discipline”

An institutional review board examines a failed procedure. One group argues that the failure was caused by systemic constraints: workload, unclear handovers, poor documentation, and inherited process design. Another group insists that someone must still be accountable, because harm occurred and future repair requires ownership. The situation is familiar, but the example remains bounded. It is not a legal case, not a forensic finding, not a clinical evaluation, and not a policy recommendation. It is a structural illustration of how free-will/determinism pressure appears when causal explanation and responsibility attribution compete for closure.

The example demonstrates why later PMS operators cannot be used cheaply. It is tempting to move directly from **Δ** and **∇** to **Ψ**: something went wrong, pressure rises, and the institution wants a responsible party. This would be a dependency shortcut. PMS requires the frame to be declared first. Is the frame causal explanation, operational repair, legal liability, professional accountability, or institutional learning? Without **□**, the word “responsibility” floats between incompatible meanings.

Once the frame is declared, **Λ** becomes visible. The review may not contain the expected decisive event: no single causal story, no complete handover record, no final proof of individual freedom, no clean division between system and agent. That missing closure must not be erased. **Α** may become relevant if the same institutional pattern repeats, such as recurring handover failures or default blame scripts. But recurrence would need evidence; it cannot be asserted merely because the example sounds plausible.

**Ω** is structurally important but must be carefully calibrated. Institutions often have the power to assign responsibility, while exposed roles bear reputational, workload, or disciplinary costs. But PMS does not moralize that asymmetry. It only marks that attribution power and exposure may be uneven. **Θ** appears through the time structure: procedures were inherited, warnings may have accumulated, repair may be delayed, and responsibility may lock in through records. **Φ** is needed because the frame can shift: a causal analysis can become an accountability inquiry; an accountability inquiry can become a sanction process; a sanction process can be reframed as institutional learning. These frame shifts must be marked rather than smuggled.

**Χ** is the central guardrail. It prevents the review from collapsing into either deterministic absolution or blame absolutism. **Σ** may become legitimate only if the review integrates causal structure, role duties, authority, capacity, and repair obligations without suppressing residue. **Ψ** may become legitimate only where responsibility is explicitly bound into a role, policy, commitment, or repair process under valid conditions. It cannot be inferred from the mere fact that harm occurred.

No add-on is active in this example. A MIP layer could become relevant only if the review artefact were intended for governance use. AHP could become relevant only as second-order hardening of the analysis artefact, not as rescoring and not as a person finding. QC/QC-EXT is not relevant.

The forbidden claims are strict. The example does not decide who is legally responsible. It does not rank the participants. It does not diagnose negligence, intent, incompetence, or moral failure. It does not prove that systemic causation cancels agency, and it does not prove that accountability survives every causal explanation.

The non-capture note is that legal standards, institutional rules, actual evidence, professional duties, and lived harm may require separate frameworks. PMS can map how closure pressure operates, but it cannot replace those standards.

The article takeaway is that free-will/determinism pressure often appears practically as a demand to choose between causal explanation and responsibility. PMS shows why that choice is too crude: causal explanation, repair ownership, asymmetry, temporality, and self-binding must be distinguished before any stronger conclusion is allowed.

### Example 3 — The predictive model and the “determined” user

**Type:** compact
**Recommended insertion point:** after “Non-capture and rival readings”

A platform uses a predictive model to estimate whether users will miss a payment deadline. One user is flagged as highly likely to miss. A manager says, “The model shows they were always going to fail; there is no real choice here.” Another manager says, “They were warned, so they are fully responsible.” This example is intentionally guardrail-heavy because it sits near several misuse risks: determinism language, responsibility language, institutional asymmetry, and possible implementation confusion.

The example demonstrates how quickly a structural map can be mistaken for a verdict. A prediction may be causally useful within a technical frame, but it does not establish metaphysical determinism. A warning may matter within an accountability frame, but it does not establish ultimate freedom or moral desert. The conflict arises because predictive explanation and responsibility attribution are being collapsed.

The PMS Core hooks begin with **Δ**: prediction is not the same as determination, and warning is not the same as freedom. **∇** appears as institutional pressure to classify the user: excused, responsible, risky, compliant, negligent, or failed. **□** is decisive. The model operates in a predictive or operational frame; the payment deadline operates in a policy frame; responsibility belongs to yet another frame involving notice, capacity, option-space, contestability, and repair. If those frames are not separated, the example becomes a closure machine.

**Λ** appears in several forms: missing information about the user’s actual constraints, missing evidence about available alternatives, missing contest channels, and the absent metaphysical proof that prediction equals inevitability. **Α** could arise if model outputs repeatedly stabilize institutional scripts: flagged users are treated as already failed, or warnings are treated as sufficient proof of responsibility. But this would require evidence across cases. **Ω** is relevant because the platform can classify, warn, restrict, or sanction, while the user may have limited visibility or contest capacity. **Θ** matters because predictions, deadlines, records, and sanctions unfold over time and may lock in. **Φ** is required when the model output travels from forecasting to governance, from governance to blame, or from blame to status assignment.

**Χ** again carries the main restraint. It blocks both “the model proves determinism” and “the warning proves free responsibility.” **Σ** would require an explicit integration of prediction, policy, user constraints, notice, appeal, repair, and uncertainty. **Ψ** would require legitimate binding conditions, such as a declared policy or commitment structure, not merely a risk score or deadline notice.

No add-on is used in the article’s current PMS Core case. However, the example shows where future layers could become relevant if the case were expanded. MIP could be triggered if the analysis became an institutional governance artefact. AHP could harden the analysis by forcing claim boundaries, evidence hooks, and failure conditions. QC or QC-EXT would be relevant only if the case concerned a technical workflow requiring that overlay; the mere presence of a predictive model is not enough to activate it here. None of these layers is active in the current example.

The forbidden claims are especially important. The example does not claim the user was determined to fail. It does not claim the user was freely blameworthy. It does not validate the model. It does not assess the user clinically. It does not decide legal liability. It does not approve platform enforcement. It does not show that PMS can govern predictive systems.

The non-capture note is that the actual ethics, legality, validity, fairness, and empirical accuracy of the predictive system require separate evidence and governance frameworks. PMS can identify the structural risks of frame-collapse and closure overreach, but it cannot replace model auditing, legal review, or institutional accountability.

The article takeaway is that deterministic language becomes dangerous when prediction, causation, responsibility, and enforcement are fused. PMS helps keep those frames apart. It allows the article to show why “determined” and “responsible” are not safe verdicts unless the relevant frame, evidence, asymmetry, temporality, and reversibility conditions have been explicitly established.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The analysis preserves several non-capture zones. PMS does not capture metaphysical free will or ultimate authorship. It does not decide causal closure. It does not capture phenomenological experience of freedom, deliberation, agency, or inner choice. It does not adjudicate moral desert. It does not determine legal culpability. It does not settle theological foreknowledge, divine causality, grace, sin, or providence. It does not resolve existential authenticity or lived responsibility. It does not substitute for empirical neuroscience or behavioral science.

The compatibilist rival reading preserves the possibility that responsibility and determinism can coexist if responsibility is framed in terms of reasons-responsiveness, internal states, social practices, or self-governance rather than metaphysical alternative possibilities. PMS can structurally resemble some compatibilist moves, but it must not claim that compatibilism is proven.

The incompatibilist rival reading preserves the claim that genuine freedom and determinism cannot both hold. PMS cannot dismiss this merely by recontextualizing responsibility as practical self-binding. The incompatibilist may argue that practical responsibility without ultimate freedom is not the responsibility at issue.

The libertarian free-will reading preserves the demand for genuine alternatives, agent causation, or non-determined authorship. PMS does not supply or refute such a metaphysics.

The hard-determinist reading preserves the claim that free will is illusory or reducible to causal process. PMS does not refute this by showing that responsibility practices remain structurally workable.

The legal-forensic reading preserves the need for institutional standards, evidence, capacity, mens rea, liability thresholds, and procedural safeguards. PMS responsibility is not legal responsibility.

The empirical-neuroscientific reading preserves questions about decision processes, prediction, readiness potentials, behavior, cognitive control, and experimental evidence. PMS does not answer those without data.

The theological reading preserves questions about divine foreknowledge, providence, sin, grace, and ultimate accountability. PMS does not enter that frame unless explicitly scoped.

The existential or phenomenological reading preserves the lived experience of choosing, owning, regretting, resolving, or confronting necessity. PMS structural awareness is not phenomenological consciousness.

The pragmatic or language-practice reading preserves the possibility that “free will” and “determinism” function as vocabularies within practices rather than metaphysical descriptions. PMS can interface with such a reading, but it does not absorb it by default.

None of these rival readings is a defect. Rivalry is methodologically required. PMS translation does not defeat rival formalizations unless explicit comparison criteria are supplied and applied.

The examples sharpen this non-capture discipline. The delayed apology shows that lived regret and repair are not reducible to a metaphysical verdict. The institutional review shows that governance, law, and evidence remain separate from PMS structural readability. The predictive-model example shows that technical prediction, responsibility, and enforcement must not be fused. In each case, PMS clarifies a closure-demand while leaving domain-specific claims unclaimed.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS reading would be weakened if the case material later showed that the title does not name a meaningful distinction, opposition, or closure-demand. If “Free Will vs Determinism” were used only as a neutral heading for unrelated material, Δ and ∇ would need recalibration.

The reading would be under-discriminating if it could not distinguish compatibilism, incompatibilism, libertarianism, hard determinism, empirical decision science, legal responsibility, theological determinism, existential agency, and PMS-praxeological framing. If all rivals collapse into the same PMS formula, the formula is too smooth.

The reading would be over-assimilative if disagreement, resistance, absence, or rival framing were automatically described as incomplete integration, drift, failed maturity, missing calibration, or recontextualization residue. PMS must preserve genuine non-capture.

The reading would overextend if Θ were treated as metaphysical determinism, if Ψ were treated as proof of ultimate authorship, if Σ were treated as compatibilist victory, or if Ω were imported as blame structure without a concrete scene.

The reading would become invalid as a PMS application if Χ were suspended to force a verdict. It would also fail if Dignity-in-Practice were violated through shaming, person ranking, standing-stripping, coercive blame, deterministic devaluation, or irreversible labeling.

Stronger claims would require stronger material. A metaphysical claim would require explicit philosophical argument and standards of metaphysical evaluation. An empirical claim would require data and method. A legal claim would require a legal frame, evidence, and procedural standard. A responsibility claim would require an actual role, duty, action, asymmetry, and temporal structure. An implementation claim would require an architecture or system. PMS Base validation would require comparative model-level argument, not a single case.

Operationalization remains unfulfilled. The minimum requirements would be: a specified text or scene; the active frame; the claim being tested; criteria for operator activation; negative cases; rival-model comparison; and evidence appropriate to the intended domain. None of these is supplied by the title alone.

If the delayed-apology example were turned into a real case, it would require context: what commitment existed, what conditions interfered, what repair was possible, who was affected, and what frame governed responsibility. If the institutional-review example were turned into a real case, it would require records, authority structures, handover evidence, institutional rules, and domain-specific standards. If the predictive-model example were turned into a real case, it would require model validation, governance review, user rights, contest channels, legal standards, and empirical performance data. Without such additions, the examples remain illustrative, not evidential.

The final falsifier condition is methodological: if PMS coherence becomes so smooth that it seems to settle the debate without external criteria, the reading has failed its own discipline. Coherence must remain subordinate to discrimination, reversibility, and non-capture.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It does not evaluate a person’s mental state, agency capacity, pathology, responsibility capacity, rationality, maturity, or character.

It must not be used for person ranking. No person, group, doctrine, or worldview is ranked as more dignified, mature, free, determined, responsible, or inferior.

It must not be used legally or forensically. It cannot determine culpability, liability, excuse, punishment, competence, mens rea, or evidentiary sufficiency.

It must not be used as metaphysical proof. PMS does not prove free will, determinism, compatibilism, hard determinism, libertarian agency, or ultimate authorship.

It must not be used as empirical proof. No data are supplied. No neuroscience, psychology, sociology, behavioral science, or AI evidence is evaluated.

It must not be used as implementation validation. The YAML and Markdown renderings are formal artefacts, not agent architectures, policy systems, simulations, or deployed models.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. A disciplined application does not prove PMS complete, uniquely ordered, or superior to all rival grammars.

It must not treat add-ons as PMS operators. No add-on is active here, and future overlay language must remain subordinate to PMS Base.

It must not treat drift as verdict. AD_A>>E and AD_Sigma_low are only possible watch conditions. They are not findings, diagnoses, scores, or moral classifications.

It must not create closure by authority. PMS does not become true because it can map the case. The correct output is bounded structural readability with preserved Λ-residue and rival openness.

It must not turn the examples into evidence. The delayed apology, institutional review, and predictive-model vignette are demonstrations of frame handling and operator discipline. They are not proof of free will, determinism, responsibility, legal liability, model validity, or PMS superiority.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result is bounded and deliberately non-triumphal. PMS Core renders “Free Will vs Determinism” as a conceptual closure-demand generated by a framed opposition between agency-language and causal-determination-language. Δ makes the distinction legible; ∇ supplies weak argumentative pressure; □ establishes the philosophical frame; Λ marks missing metaphysical closure; Θ and Φ remain relevant but bounded; Ω, Α, Σ, and Ψ remain weak, guarded, or conditional; Χ carries the main application discipline. The case does not activate the full Δ–Ψ chain as an empirical or metaphysical finding. Its chapter-ready formula is therefore not the full SELF_FIXPOINT chain, but: **Χ as application gate + (□ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ), with weak monitored Λ/Α/Ω/Θ/Φ and guarded non-primary Σ/Ψ**. It yields a boundary-oriented structural projection.

The examples contribute by showing how the abstract opposition becomes readable in nearby practical settings without becoming evidence. The delayed apology shows how causal explanation and responsibility attribution can be separated without deciding metaphysical freedom. The institutional review shows why dependency discipline matters before responsibility is bound into roles, policy, or blame. The predictive-model example shows how determinism language can become dangerous when prediction, responsibility, and institutional enforcement are fused. These examples prepare the case for later conclusion work by demonstrating the same boundary logic under increasingly applied conditions, while preserving the fact that no applied conclusion is yet warranted.

What remains unclaimed is extensive and essential. PMS does not prove free will. It does not prove determinism. It does not validate compatibilism. It does not refute incompatibilism. It does not adjudicate libertarian agency, hard determinism, theological foreknowledge, phenomenological freedom, moral desert, empirical decision science, legal culpability, or AI model validity. It does not diagnose persons, rank agents, or authorize enforcement. It does not validate PMS Base.

The boundary discipline is therefore the central achievement. The analysis refuses the common closure shortcut in which causal explanation becomes excuse, responsibility becomes blame, prediction becomes determination, coherence becomes proof, or practical accountability becomes metaphysical solution. PMS keeps these moves visible as frame shifts, dependency risks, or possible non-capture zones. Dignity-in-Practice remains praxeological: it constrains how asymmetry and responsibility-language may be handled; it does not become an ontological ranking.

The final methodological takeaway is that PMS is strongest here when it does not solve too much. The case shows how AXIOM can map a classical philosophical closure-demand without becoming a verdict machine. It shows where structural clarity is possible, where late operators require stronger evidence, where drift remains only a risk, and where Λ-residue must remain visible. The result is not a final answer to free will versus determinism, but a disciplined account of why the demand for such an answer must be framed, bounded, and kept reversible.
