### Why this case matters

“Meaning” and “sense” are among the most closure-demanding philosophical terms because they invite almost immediate expansion. A question about meaning can turn into a question about lived experience, language, reference, truth, value, intelligibility, action, symbolic order, existential orientation, metaphysical grounding, social recognition, or practical coordination. The term is unusually receptive: almost any theory can appear to say something plausible about it. That is precisely why the case belongs in AXIOM.

The AXIOM task is not to solve meaning metaphysically. It is not to decide what meaning ultimately is, where sense resides, whether meaning is subjective or objective, whether language grounds meaning, whether meaning depends on use, whether existential meaning is possible, or whether meaning is an ontological feature of the world. Such questions may remain legitimate in their own frames, but they are not the PMS-Core claim here. AXIOM asks a narrower and more disciplined question: under what conditions does meaning/sense become structurally analyzable as a praxis phenomenon?

The case is structurally difficult because meaning often seems to demand closure while simultaneously resisting it. When something “makes sense,” a frame has been stabilized. When something “has meaning,” some difference has become relevant. When meaning is contested, rival frames may compete. When meaning is missing, the absence may be structural, but it may also indicate non-capture rather than a PMS-coded non-event. When meaning is imposed, asymmetry may matter. When meaning is stabilized into standards, narratives, doctrines, or commitments, Ψ may become relevant. Yet none of these later structures may be assumed merely because the word “meaning” is present.

This makes the case a useful AXIOM test of restraint. PMS can render a minimal structural grammar of meaning/sense, but the grammar must not become a closure machine. The successful PMS rendering is not proof of capture. It is only a bounded articulation of when meaning/sense is analyzable as situated praxis: when distinctions become relevant within a frame and are temporally maintainable enough to orient intelligibility, expectation, or enactability.

The philosophical closure-demand is therefore transformed. Instead of asking “what is meaning in itself?”, the case asks: what structural conditions must be present before PMS may responsibly say that meaning/sense is operating as a praxis configuration? The answer is conservative: Δ, □, and Θ carry the minimal formula. ∇ and Φ are conditional. Λ, Α, Ω, Χ, Σ, and Ψ remain weak, analytic-only, or not object-level active unless a concrete scene supplies the missing structure.

This is why the case is not empirical, clinical, diagnostic, legal, forensic, metaphysical, or implementation-oriented. The material provides no observations, no person, no transcript, no institution, no data, no legal dispute, no software system, and no clinical context. The case is a conceptual domain projection. It belongs in AXIOM because it demonstrates how a classical closure-demand can be made structurally legible without being solved by authority.

### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading proceeds through all eleven operators in order, but it does not activate them equally. The discipline of the case lies precisely in not treating the full Δ–Ψ chain as automatically present.

**Δ — Difference** is active and carries major analytic weight. Meaning/sense requires distinction. Something becomes meaningful only insofar as it is distinguished from something else: signal from noise, relevant from irrelevant, sense-bearing from non-sense-bearing, framed from unframed, praxis-relevant from merely abstract. In this case, Δ first separates meaning/sense as praxeologically analyzable from meaning/sense as semantic, phenomenological, existential, theological, symbolic, or metaphysical object. The calibration risk is overbreadth. If Δ simply marks every possible distinction as meaning, the analysis loses discrimination. The operator must identify the difference that makes sense structurally legible within praxis, not meaning in every possible register.

**∇ — Impulse** is weak-to-conditional. Meaning/sense may orient interpretation, expectation, attention, or action. If something “makes sense” in a praxis setting, it may direct what comes next. But no concrete impulse is supplied. There is no specified desire, urgency, affect, pressure, task, or action trajectory. ∇ may therefore be used only as structural directedness, not as psychological motivation. The overclaim would be to infer desire, intention, need, affective drive, or subjective concern from the mere concept of meaning.

**□ — Frame** is active and central. The case is PMS-analyzable only because meaning/sense is restricted to situated praxis. The frame defines what counts as relevant, what counts as a signal, what is admissible, and what the case is allowed to claim. Without □, “meaning” becomes too general and PMS can appear to explain everything while actually discriminating very little. The frame is therefore not decorative; it is the condition of validity. The calibration risk is silent frame expansion: shifting from praxis to phenomenology, semantics, existential value, or metaphysics without marking the transition.

**Λ — Non-Event** is weak. Meaning can certainly be structured through absence: missing context, silence, failed clarification, absent explanation, delayed feedback, or non-arrival of an expected signal. But the abstract case supplies no expected event. There is no specific missing answer, omitted action, absent response, failed sign, or delayed evidence. Therefore Λ cannot be strongly activated. The dependency risk is especially important here: absence, ambiguity, resistance, disagreement, or non-fit must not automatically become PMS Λ. It may instead indicate non-capture.

**Α — Attractor** is weak. Meaning/sense can stabilize into routines: interpretive habits, institutional scripts, recurring narratives, role expectations, symbolic defaults, or practices of relevance. But the case material supplies no recurrence. A philosophical concept is not automatically an attractor. Α requires repeated structural interaction or stabilization over time. The overclaim would be to treat any recognizable meaning-pattern as already recurrent, scripted, or path-dependent.

**Ω — Asymmetry** is weak and not automatic. Situated praxis may contain asymmetry: some roles can impose meanings, define relevance, exclude rival interpretations, or force others to live under an adopted meaning. Yet none of that is given here. There is no authority role, exposed role, institutional frame, cost distribution, contest channel, or obligation gradient. Ω must therefore remain possible but not active as a finding. The overclaim would be to infer power, responsibility, or exposure from situatedness alone.

**Θ — Temporality** is active but carefully bounded. Meaning/sense as praxeological intelligibility requires temporal maintainability. A distinction must persist long enough to orient sense; otherwise it is only an isolated contrast. Θ therefore marks minimal continuity: meaning is not merely a flash of difference but a frame-bound distinction that can be carried, repeated, expected, revised, or used. Still, Θ does not here mean empirical history, existential temporality, developmental trajectory, responsibility arc, or long-term institutional lock-in. Because Α and Ω are weak, Θ must remain bounded to the minimal condition that sense is maintainable across more than an instantaneous distinction.

**Φ — Recontextualization** is weak-to-conditional. Meaning often changes when moved from one frame to another. A sign, action, event, claim, silence, or phrase can mean differently in different contexts. This makes Φ relevant as a possibility. But the case supplies no source frame and no target frame. Therefore Φ cannot be strongly claimed. The overclaim would be to absorb all rival readings as PMS recontextualizations. A rival semantic, phenomenological, or hermeneutic theory may not be a Φ shift inside PMS; it may be a genuinely different formalization.

**Χ — Distance** is active as application discipline, not as a case-internal scene operator. The analysis itself must maintain distance from its object and from its own coherence. Χ prevents the PMS rendering from becoming possession of meaning. It suspends metaphysical, empirical, diagnostic, legal, implementation, and validation claims. It keeps the reading reversible and prevents closure by authority. Without Χ, the case would drift into exactly what it is meant to avoid: “PMS explains meaning” as a totalizing claim. Case-internally, however, no reflective actor or distancing event is supplied.

**Σ — Integration** is weak. Meaning can involve integration: heterogeneous signals, distinctions, expectations, constraints, and narratives may be synthesized into a coherent sense-structure. But no concrete plurality or conflict is given. A coherent PMS formula is not yet Σ. This is a major calibration point. PMS coherence is not proof of integration; it may instead signal that the case is so broad that the framework can map it too smoothly. Σ requires synthesis of disparate or conflicting elements, not merely conceptual elegance.

**Ψ — Self-Binding** is not object-level active. Meaning/sense can become self-binding if a person, institution, community, or system commits to a meaning across time: a promise, standard, doctrine, identity narrative, or stable role script. But the present case contains no such commitment. Ψ applies only as analytic self-binding of the PMS application: the analysis must bind itself to its own constraints, including reversibility, non-capture, and dignity-in-practice. The overclaim would be to infer identity, maturity, responsibility, or selfhood from the abstract presence of meaning.

The resulting PMS Core formula is therefore conservative:

**M/Sₚ = Θ ∘ □ ∘ Δ**

Meaning/sense within situated praxis is minimally modeled as temporally maintainable framed differentiation. Δ marks that meaning requires distinction. □ marks that the distinction becomes relevant only within a frame. Θ marks that sense must be maintainable across minimal temporal continuity rather than appearing as an isolated difference.

∇ may be added only if orientation is specified. Φ may be added only if a frame shift is specified. Λ, Α, Ω, Χ, Σ, and Ψ are not object-level components unless a concrete scene supplies expectation-failure, recurrence, asymmetry, reflective distance, integration, or self-binding.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

The case is an exercise in dependency discipline. Later PMS operators cannot be used merely because the topic is philosophically rich. The full Δ–Ψ chain is available as PMS grammar, but the case material does not justify full activation.

The first shortcut to avoid is Δ/∇ inflation. A distinction does not automatically become orientation. Meaning may guide praxis, but unless a direction, task, expectation, interpretation pressure, or enactment vector is specified, ∇ remains partial. The word “meaning” tempts this shortcut because it often feels intrinsically directional. PMS must resist that feeling.

The second shortcut is □ → Λ. A frame does not automatically create a meaningful non-event. Λ requires an expected occurrence within a frame: a missing reply, absent clarification, delayed signal, failed event, or unmet expectation. In this case, no such event is supplied. Ambiguity or non-fit may remain outside PMS capture.

The third shortcut is Λ → Α. Even if an absence were specified, one absence would not establish a pattern. Α requires recurrence or stabilization. Meaning can become routinized, but the case material does not show routine formation.

The fourth shortcut is Α → Ω. Stabilization does not automatically imply asymmetry. A meaning-pattern may become asymmetrical if one role imposes interpretation and another bears cost, but that must be shown. Without a concrete scene, Ω remains weak.

The fifth shortcut is Θ expansion. Θ is active only as minimal temporal maintainability. It must not be inflated into history, development, lock-in, responsibility, or institutional persistence unless the case supplies those structures.

The sixth shortcut is Φ absorption. Recontextualization is not the same as rivalry. A semantic theory of meaning, a phenomenological account of lived sense, or a hermeneutic theory of interpretation is not automatically a PMS frame shift. It may preserve something PMS does not capture.

The seventh shortcut is Χ → Σ. Distance is not integration. Suspending closure does not synthesize competing elements. It only prevents premature fusion.

The eighth shortcut is Σ → Ψ. Coherence is not self-binding. A coherent reading is not a commitment, identity, promise, standard, doctrine, or responsibility structure.

This is why coherence is not proof. The PMS formula may be elegant, but elegance is not adequacy. A model can map a broad concept smoothly because the concept has been under-specified. That is the central coherence risk in this case. PMS must show discriminative force: it must distinguish praxeological meaning/sense from semantic reference, phenomenological lived sense, existential value, symbolic depth, and metaphysical meaning. If it cannot, the analysis becomes under-discriminating.

Non-capture is not failure. It is part of the output. Meaning/sense may exceed PMS in several ways. The correct PMS response is not to force those excesses back into the operator chain. It is to preserve them as possible rival readings or non-capture zones.

PMS must therefore not become a closure machine. Its task is to map where closure is structurally possible and where Λ-residue or non-capture must remain visible. In this case, PMS does not close the theory of meaning. It closes only a minimal structural projection: meaning/sense as temporally maintainable framed differentiation within situated praxis.

### Example 1 — A pause that only becomes meaningful inside a frame

**Type:** compact
**Recommended insertion point:** After “Scene and frame structure,” where the article explains why meaning/sense requires □ and why the case is not a concrete-scene finding.

A project meeting ends with a question from one participant: “Can we still deliver this by Friday?” The room becomes quiet for several seconds. In one frame, the pause may be treated as simple processing time: people are checking calendars, dependencies, or available resources. In another frame, the same pause may become meaningful as hesitation, uncertainty, disagreement, or tacit refusal. In a third frame, it may not be meaningful at all; it may be ordinary conversational spacing.

The example demonstrates the core PMS point: meaning does not arise from the pause by itself. The pause becomes sense-bearing only when a frame specifies what counts as relevant. The same observable difference — silence rather than immediate answer — can remain noise, become a coordination signal, or become a contested sign depending on the operative □. The minimal structure is therefore not “silence means X,” but: a difference becomes interpretable within a frame and must remain temporally available long enough to orient expectation.

The PMS Core hooks are limited and precise. **Δ** marks the contrast between response and non-response. **□** determines whether the contrast counts as relevant. **Θ** appears minimally because the pause must be held across a short temporal interval to become interpretable. **∇** may be weakly present if the pause generates orientation pressure — for example, “we need clarification before deciding.” **Λ** is not automatically active. It becomes active only if an answer was specifically expected within the frame and its non-arrival matters structurally. **Ω** is not automatically active either; it would require asymmetry, such as a manager’s silence imposing uncertainty on exposed team members. **Σ** and **Ψ** remain inactive unless the group later integrates the interpretation into a shared account or binds it into a decision.

No add-on is required. The example does not need LOGIC, CRITIQUE, CONFLICT, QC, MIP, or AHP because it is not about justification regimes, governance, enforcement, conflict escalation, claim hardening, or audit quality. PMS Core is sufficient to show the bounded structural mechanism.

MIP / AHP / QC are not activated. There is no governance artefact, no policy decision, no institutional accountability claim, no quality-control analysis, and no downstream evaluation layer. If the pause later became evidence in an audit, legal dispute, or organizational review, the stack would have to be reconsidered. That is not claimed here.

The forbidden claims are essential. The example does not show what anyone “really meant.” It does not diagnose hesitation, deception, incompetence, fear, avoidance, or resistance. It does not establish intent. It does not prove that silence has a stable meaning. It does not license a legal, moral, or managerial conclusion.

The non-capture note is equally important. The lived experience of the participants, the emotional texture of the silence, and the broader interpersonal history may exceed the PMS rendering. PMS captures only the praxeological structure under which the pause becomes analyzable as frame-bound sense.

**Article takeaway:** Meaning/sense is not located in the event alone. It becomes structurally analyzable when a difference is framed and temporally held as relevant for praxis.

### Example 2 — The same phrase across frames

**Type:** compact  
**Recommended insertion point:** Inside “PMS Core structural reading,” after the discussion of Φ, because the vignette shows why recontextualization is relevant but must remain conditional.

A colleague says, “That was an interesting choice.” In a design critique, the phrase may function as an invitation to explain a decision. In a strained interpersonal exchange, it may be heard as indirect criticism. In a teaching context, it may be a prompt for reflection. In a written performance review, it may become an ambiguous evaluative marker with possible downstream consequences.

The sentence is identical, but its sense shifts with the frame. The point is not that one interpretation is secretly correct. The point is that meaning/sense is not structurally stable without specifying the frame in which the phrase is being used. The phrase acquires different relevance, exposure, and possible action implications depending on whether the situation is exploratory, evaluative, relational, instructional, or institutional.

This example demonstrates **Φ** without overclaiming it. Recontextualization occurs only when a structure is moved from one frame into another. Here, the phrase moves between critique, interpersonal exchange, teaching, and review. Each frame changes what counts as admissible evidence for interpretation. Tone, timing, role relation, documentation status, and response opportunity may matter differently. The phrase is not simply “the same meaning” traveling across contexts; nor is every rival interpretation automatically PMS drift. The meaning changes because the frame changes.

The PMS Core hooks remain bounded. **Δ** marks the interpretive contrast: explanation prompt, criticism, reflection cue, evaluative marker. **□** determines which interpretation is admissible. **Θ** appears because meaning persists through uptake: what is said may later be remembered, documented, acted on, or revised. **Φ** is conditionally active because the phrase can be re-read under a new frame. **Χ** is required as analytic restraint: the reader must not collapse ambiguity into verdict. **Λ** may be present only if expected clarification is absent, not merely because the phrase is ambiguous. **Ω** may become relevant if one role has interpretive authority, such as a supervisor whose ambiguous phrase affects an employee’s record. But the example itself does not assert such asymmetry as a finding.

No add-on is activated. A LOGIC overlay might become relevant if the case concerned justification of an interpretation. A CRITIQUE overlay might become relevant if interpretive authority were structurally contested. MIP or AHP might become relevant if the phrase entered a governance artefact. But as presented, the vignette only illustrates PMS Core frame dependence and conditional Φ.

MIP / AHP / QC status remains inactive. The example is not an audit trace, policy review, legal record, clinical note, or implementation test.

Forbidden claims must remain excluded. The example does not decide what the colleague intended. It does not rank the hearer as oversensitive or the speaker as manipulative. It does not diagnose indirect aggression. It does not show harassment, misconduct, competence, or bad faith. It does not prove that all meaning is contextual in a metaphysical sense.

**Article takeaway:** Recontextualization can make meaning shift, but Φ must be tied to explicit frame movement. Rival interpretations are not automatically PMS drift or failed integration.

### Example 3 — When a working interpretation hardens into a rule

**Type:** worked
**Recommended insertion point:** After “Derived structures,” because the example shows why Awareness_A may be weakly active while Σ, Ψ, Responsibility_R, and drift patterns require stronger conditions.

A small team receives user complaints about a confusing interface. After reviewing several messages, the team adopts a working interpretation: “Users do not understand the setup step.” This interpretation helps coordinate action. Designers rewrite the instructions, support staff adjust their replies, and the product manager tracks whether fewer complaints arrive. At this stage, the meaning of the complaints is practical and frame-bound: within the product-support frame, they are treated as signals about setup confusion.

The initial interpretation demonstrates the minimal PMS structure. **Δ** marks a difference between expected user flow and observed complaint pattern. **□** frames the complaints as product-support signals rather than, for example, market research, user incompetence, or moral failure. **Θ** appears because the interpretation must persist long enough to guide revision and feedback. **∇** is present as orientation pressure: the team needs a usable account to act.

But the example becomes more interesting when the interpretation begins to harden. Suppose the phrase “users do not understand setup” becomes a standing internal label. New complaints are automatically routed under that category. Alternative readings — confusing pricing, inaccessible language, mismatch between marketing and product, or missing functionality — are considered less often. The original interpretation has begun to stabilize as a routine.

At that point, **Α** may become conditionally relevant: repeated use forms an interpretive attractor. **Σ** may become relevant if the team integrates multiple signals into a coherent account. But Σ is not guaranteed by repetition. A repeated label can be a shortcut, not an integration. If the team merely compresses all complaints into one slogan, PMS should not treat that as mature coherence.

**Ψ** becomes relevant only if the interpretation is bound into a standard, policy, product doctrine, or durable organizational commitment: for example, all future complaints of this type are classified under “setup misunderstanding” unless proven otherwise. That binding may be useful, but it also raises the risk of lock-in. Still, the example does not automatically classify drift. It only shows the conditions under which later operators might become active.

**Λ** must remain visible. Missing evidence may include unreviewed user sessions, absent accessibility testing, delayed outcome data, or no contest channel for support staff who see other patterns. If those missing events matter within the frame, Λ is active. If they are merely imaginable gaps, Λ remains possible rather than asserted.

**Ω** may become relevant if managers or system owners can impose the interpretation while support staff or users bear the costs of misclassification. But again, this requires a concrete asymmetry. The example does not rank the team, diagnose the organization, or declare misuse. It shows what evidence would be needed.

No add-on is required for the basic example. PMS Core already explains how meaning/sense can move from framed distinction to orientation, routine, possible integration, and possible binding. An add-on could be justified only if the article later wanted to analyze governance effects, critique interpretive authority, or audit claim quality. Since this example is illustrative, no LOGIC, CRITIQUE, MIP, AHP, or QC layer is activated.

MIP / AHP / QC status remains inactive. If the interpretation were used in a formal product audit, customer-impact review, or governance report, MIP or AHP might become relevant as downstream artefact discipline. That is not the present claim.

Forbidden claims are clear. The example does not prove what users really mean. It does not diagnose the team as biased. It does not rank the product manager, support staff, or users. It does not establish negligence, liability, empirical validity, or implementation success. It does not validate PMS.

The non-capture note matters because user complaints may carry affective, linguistic, accessibility, market, cultural, or experiential dimensions that PMS Core does not exhaust. A UX research method, discourse analysis, or accessibility audit might capture dimensions that this structural reading only brackets.

**Article takeaway:** Meaning/sense can become action-guiding and even policy-like, but Σ and Ψ require explicit evidence. Repetition is not integration, and a useful interpretation must not be mistaken for exhaustive capture.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The case preserves several non-capture zones. These are not defects. They are necessary boundaries.

A **phenomenological reading** may treat meaning as lived sense prior to structural analysis. It preserves first-person givenness, felt meaningfulness, intentionality, horizon, affective salience, or experiential depth. PMS does not capture this unless and until it becomes structurally analyzable in praxis. Even then, PMS would capture only the praxeological form, not the lived quality itself.

A **semantic reading** may treat meaning as reference, use, truth-conditions, inferential role, compositional structure, or language-game. It preserves distinctions within language theory that PMS does not attempt to replace. PMS can model semantic meaning only if a specific praxis frame is supplied: for example, a rule-following scene, an institutional definition, a coordination practice, or a dispute over admissible reference.

A **hermeneutic reading** may treat meaning as interpretation within tradition, history, textual horizon, or dialogical understanding. It preserves the depth of interpretive mediation and historical situatedness. PMS may model some of that through □, Θ, Φ, and Σ, but it must not claim to exhaust hermeneutic sense.

An **existential reading** may treat meaning as orientation toward life, finitude, value, suffering, purpose, or project. It preserves a register of significance that may not reduce to coordination or praxeological intelligibility. PMS can map existential meaning only if the case specifies how such meaning becomes situated praxis.

A **social-theoretical reading** may treat meaning as institutionally mediated symbolic order. It preserves power, normativity, recognition, ideology, and social reproduction. PMS may later activate Ω, Α, Θ, Φ, and Ψ in such a scene, but the current abstract case does not supply enough structure.

An **anti-capture reading** may argue that meaning/sense partly exceeds formal operator representation. This is not a failure for AXIOM. It is exactly the kind of residue the paper is designed to keep visible.

These rival readings remain open. PMS translation does not defeat them. The PMS reading is valid only as a bounded structural projection. If rival formalizations distinguish types of meaning more sharply, preserve lived or semantic dimensions better, or explain the target case with less assimilation, then the PMS reading must be weakened or restricted.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS reading would be weakened if meaning/sense in the target use cannot be tied to situated praxis. If no frame, distinction, relevance structure, orientation, temporal maintainability, role-space, or enactability can be specified, then PMS has no legitimate entry point beyond verbal analogy.

It would also be weakened if the meaning at issue is purely semantic, phenomenological, metaphysical, theological, or existential with no praxeological structure supplied. In such cases, PMS may still offer a comparison, but it cannot claim capture.

The reading would become under-discriminating if the same formula applies equally to semantic reference, lived sense, existential value, symbolic order, and practical intelligibility without preserving the differences among them. The formula Θ ∘ □ ∘ Δ is useful only if it distinguishes praxeological sense from other kinds of meaning.

The reading would become over-assimilative if resistance, rupture, disagreement, non-fit, ambiguity, missing evidence, or rival framing were automatically translated into PMS drift, incomplete calibration, failed integration, recontextualization, or maturity failure. The analysis must allow non-capture to remain non-capture.

The reading would be overextended if ∇ were treated as desire, Λ as any absence, Α as any recognizable pattern, Ω as any situatedness, Θ as full development, Φ as any rival reading, Χ as proof of integration, Σ as coherence alone, or Ψ as meaning’s intrinsic self-binding.

The reading would also fail if PMS coherence were treated as sufficient. A coherent PMS derivation must still show discriminative force. It must explain why the case is better rendered as praxeological meaning/sense rather than as a semantic, phenomenological, hermeneutic, existential, or metaphysical case.

Operationalization would require substantial additional material. For Δ, one would need observable or explicitly coded distinctions. For ∇, evidence of orientation or directedness. For □, a specified scene frame. For Λ, a defined expected event and its absence or delay. For Α, repeated interactions or stabilized routines. For Ω, a directional imbalance of authority, exposure, capacity, obligation, or interpretive power. For Θ, temporal sequence, persistence, anticipation, or trajectory. For Φ, source and target frames. For Χ, observable or procedural distance and reversibility. For Σ, actual synthesis of disparate elements. For Ψ, self-binding, commitment, standard, identity relation, or durable obligation.

None of these operational requirements is fulfilled by the supplied conceptual case. Therefore empirical validation, comparative validation, computational validation, applied validation, governance validation, legal validation, and clinical validation are all absent.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It does not identify a person’s meaning-making capacity, psychological coherence, maturity, pathology, confusion, resistance, or integration level.

It must not be used for person ranking. PMS fit does not make a person, group, theory, culture, or practice more meaningful, more mature, more coherent, or more dignified.

It must not be used legally or forensically. It does not establish intent, culpability, liability, evidence relevance, testimony quality, responsibility, or interpretive authority.

It must not be used metaphysically. It does not prove what meaning is, where meaning resides, whether meaning is objective or subjective, or whether ultimate meaning exists.

It must not be used empirically. It supplies no observations, measurements, corpus, or test results.

It must not be used as implementation validation. YAML representation is not an implemented meaning system, AI architecture, interpretive engine, or software model.

It must not validate PMS Base. A case application is not proof of PMS completeness, uniqueness, minimality, or superiority.

It must not treat an add-on as an operator. No add-on is active here, and no overlay vocabulary may redefine PMS Core.

It must not turn drift into verdict. Drift patterns are not findings in this case. Even if drift risks later become relevant in a concrete scene, they remain structural risks, not diagnoses or moral judgments.

It must not create closure by authority. The correct output is bounded structural legibility: where meaning/sense can be modeled, where stronger claims would require evidence, and where non-capture must remain visible.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result of the case is deliberately narrow but structurally useful: meaning/sense, restricted to situated praxis, can be minimally rendered as **temporally maintainable framed differentiation**. The final PMS Core formula, **M/Sₚ = Θ ∘ □ ∘ Δ**, does not solve meaning as a metaphysical or phenomenological problem. It states the minimum condition under which meaning/sense becomes PMS-analyzable: a distinction must become relevant within a frame and remain maintainable across enough temporal continuity to orient intelligibility, expectation, interpretation, or possible enactment.

The examples contribute by making that restraint visible. The pause in the meeting shows that an event does not carry structural meaning by itself; it becomes sense-bearing only inside a frame. The phrase “that was an interesting choice” shows that recontextualization can shift meaning, but only where a source frame and target frame are distinguishable; rival readings are not automatically drift. The product-team example shows how a working interpretation can orient action and even begin to stabilize into routine, while also demonstrating why Α, Σ, Ω, Ψ, and drift-pattern claims require additional evidence. Together, the examples demonstrate the analysis without replacing it: they illustrate PMS hooks, but they do not function as proof, do not add scene-level findings, and do not authorize stronger claims than the YAML analysis permits.

What remains unclaimed is as important as what is claimed. The case does not define meaning in itself. It does not capture lived sense, semantic reference, existential value, symbolic depth, theological meaning, hermeneutic horizon, or social meaning as such. It does not diagnose interpretation, rank persons, assign responsibility, validate a legal reading, establish an empirical finding, or implement a meaning model. It does not validate PMS Base. It does not show that rival theories are merely incomplete PMS translations.

The boundary discipline is therefore the real output. PMS Core may render meaning/sense structurally, but only under declared scope: no metaphysical proof, no empirical proof, no diagnostic use, no legal or forensic conclusion, no implementation validation, no drift-as-verdict, and no coherence-as-proof. Dignity-in-Practice remains a praxeological constraint on the application: the analysis must not become a tool for shaming, ranking, coercive interpretation, or authority-driven closure.

The final methodological takeaway is that AXIOM does not need to solve meaning to make the case useful. Its contribution is cartographic: it shows where meaning/sense becomes structurally legible, which operators are warranted, which operators remain weak or inactive, what would be needed for stronger claims, and where non-capture must remain visible. Meaning/sense is therefore not closed by PMS. It is bounded, rendered, and disciplined: enough to support a structural article, not enough to erase the plurality of meanings that remain outside the frame.
