### Why this case matters

Truth is one of the classical closure-demand terms. It tends to invite finality. Once a claim is called “true,” pressure often appears to end dispute, stabilize orientation, authorize action, settle correction, bind a frame, or license coordination. In philosophical contexts, this pressure often becomes metaphysical or epistemological: truth is asked to name what ultimately corresponds to reality, what coheres within a system, what is warranted, what is disclosed, or what can be asserted without remainder. In practical contexts, truth often functions more modestly but also more dangerously: it tells a scene what can be treated as correct enough, stable enough, or admissible enough to act.

This case matters for AXIOM because it sits precisely at the threshold between useful closure and closure-overreach. Truth is structurally necessary for orientation, correction, and coordination, but it is also easily converted into authority. A functional truth claim can help a system decide what to do next; it can also become a coercive verdict, a silencing device, or a way of treating disagreement as failure. PMS Core is well suited to this tension because it does not need to decide what truth is metaphysically. It can instead ask what truth is doing inside a frame.

The difficult structure is that functional truth must discriminate without becoming a tribunal. It must orient without pretending that orientation is proof. It must correct without collapsing correction into domination. It must coordinate without treating coordination as truth itself. It must allow integration while preserving the fact that coherence is not sufficient by itself. These are not merely stylistic cautions. They are the central structural difficulty of the case.

The case therefore belongs in AXIOM because AXIOM maps closure-demands. “Truth” is a closure-demand of unusually high intensity: it asks for the closing of ambiguity, the stabilization of relevance, and the authorization of action. But the PMS reading refuses to solve truth metaphysically, empirically, legally, clinically, or diagnostically. It treats the supplied material as a functional-praxeological concept: truth as orientation, discrimination, correction, and coordination within situated praxis.

That means the article does not claim that PMS has solved the theory of truth. It does not replace correspondence, coherence, pragmatic, semantic, proof-theoretic, phenomenological, scientific, religious, legal-forensic, or deflationary accounts. It shows how one functional reading of truth becomes structurally legible through PMS, while preserving what remains non-captured.

### Scene and frame structure

Because this is not a concrete praxis scene, the “scene” is conceptual. The scene boundary is the functional use of truth in situated praxis. Inside that boundary are the structural operations by which truth helps a practice distinguish, orient, correct, and coordinate. Outside that boundary are metaphysical theories of truth as such, empirical verification of a particular claim, clinical interpretation of truthfulness, legal proof, and implementation design.

The active frame, □, is therefore not “the world as a whole” and not “truth in all possible senses.” The frame is: **truth as functional validity within situated praxis**. In this frame, truth is relevant insofar as it helps a scene decide what counts as correct enough, admissible enough, discriminating enough, or coordinated enough for a purpose. But “for a purpose” does not mean “merely useful.” A useful falsehood may coordinate action while failing as truth. A coherent story may stabilize a group while remaining false. A procedurally accepted claim may be legally usable while remaining metaphysically or empirically disputed. The frame must therefore preserve distinctions rather than collapse them.

Inside the frame are orientation, discrimination, correction, coordination, revisability, and bounded integration. Inside the frame are also missing evidence, error, disagreement, frame shifts, uncertainty, contestability, and the possibility that closure is not warranted. Outside the frame are final metaphysical settlement, person evaluation, enforcement guidance, and PMS Base validation.

Protected constraints include Distance, reversibility, Dignity-in-Practice, non-capture, and rival openness. Distance prevents the analysis from turning truth into immediate verdict. Reversibility keeps the reading open to correction. Dignity-in-Practice constrains truth-use under possible asymmetry: no shaming, ranking, humiliation, silencing, or irreversible labeling may be smuggled into the analysis. Non-capture preserves the possibility that some forms of truth are not adequately rendered by this functional PMS projection. Rival openness prevents PMS from converting rival theories into mere incomplete PMS readings.

Roles are not concretely supplied. The completed YAML nevertheless marks possible structural roles only at the level of future operationalization: a claim-maker, validator, gatekeeper, affected party, or frame-setter could appear in a concrete truth-practice. But none is present in the supplied case material. Therefore no role-based responsibility, exposure, authority, or contest position is assigned.

Temporality, Θ, is active at the conceptual level because truth as correction and coordination unfolds over time. A truth-practice may begin with difference, move through pressure for correction, declare a frame, encounter missing evidence or non-fit, stabilize routines, shift frames, maintain distance, and integrate a provisional result. Exposure and asymmetry conditions are weak and calibration-dependent. ### PMS Core structural reading

The PMS Core reading treats truth functionally as a structured praxis configuration. The recommended formula is:

**Σ ∘ Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ωᶜ ∘ Αᶜ ∘ Λᶜ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ**

Here the superscripted calibration marks are essential. Λ, Α, and Ω are not fully established by the abstract case. They are structurally relevant, but they require concrete scene information before being asserted strongly. Ψ is not in the primary formula because the case does not itself establish truth as self-binding. Ψ enters only as application discipline, or conditionally where a concrete truth-practice involves sustained commitment to correction across time.

**Δ, Difference, is active.** Functional truth begins by making distinctions. It distinguishes fit from non-fit, claim from counterclaim, correction from error, signal from noise, orientation from disorientation, and coordination from breakdown. But Δ must be calibrated carefully. If the analysis does not specify which difference truth is supposed to make, then “truth” becomes a vague honorific. The risk is under-discrimination: truth could be confused with usefulness, coherence, agreement, validity, evidence, admissibility, or successful coordination. Δ therefore carries the first guardrail: truth must discriminate, but the relevant distinction must be named.

**∇, Impulse, is active.** Truth-practices are not static; they are directed. The case material explicitly concerns orientation and correction, and both imply directed tension. Something does not fit, something must be clarified, something must be coordinated, something must be revised. But ∇ must not be psychologized. It is not a claim about desire, sincerity, or inner motivation. It is a structural directedness toward correction or orientation. If pushed too far, ∇ becomes urgency: the demand to settle now, enforce now, correct now, or silence uncertainty. That would already distort the case.

**□, Frame, is active and central.** Truth in this case is not unscoped truth-as-such. It is truth within situated praxis. The frame defines relevance, admissibility, tolerances, correction standards, and decision purpose. Without □, truth becomes an unbounded status claim. But □ also carries a risk: a frame may be narrowed until closure becomes easy. If the frame silently excludes rival evidence, alternative standards, or inconvenient residues, then frame discipline becomes frame laundering. The PMS reading therefore requires explicit frame declaration and preserves the possibility that other frames produce different truth-conditions.

**Λ, Non-Event, is weak-active and calibration-dependent.** Truth-practices often turn on what is missing: missing evidence, failed confirmation, absence of expected correction, silence where a response is expected, contradiction, delay, or non-fit. But the case material does not identify a concrete expected event. Therefore Λ cannot be claimed as a specific absence. It can only be marked as a structural possibility. The misuse risk is serious: absence, silence, disagreement, resistance, rupture, or rival framing must not automatically be interpreted as error, avoidance, drift, or incomplete integration. Λ requires an expectation inside a frame. Without that, it is overclaim.

**Α, Attractor, is weak-active.** Functional truth-practices can stabilize into routines: verification practices, correction rituals, review cycles, documentation standards, default narratives, institutional habits, or repeated inquiry patterns. But the abstract case does not demonstrate recurrence. Therefore Α remains plausible but not established. Pushed too far, Α would treat any repeated truth-language as stable truth-practice. That would confuse repetition with warrant and routine with adequacy.

**Ω, Asymmetry, is weak-active and a major calibration point.** Truth is often asymmetrical in practice. Some roles can declare truth, set evidentiary standards, control contest channels, or externalize error costs. Others may bear the consequences of being declared wrong, corrected, exposed, or silenced. But no concrete Ω structure is supplied here. The correct status is therefore conditional. Ω must be monitored, not asserted as a finding. The overclaim would be to treat every truth claim as domination, or every disagreement as asymmetrical coercion. The opposite overclaim would be to ignore asymmetry because the case is philosophical. PMS requires neither suspicion nor innocence by default; it requires calibration.

**Θ, Temporality, is active.** Truth as correction is temporal. A claim is proposed, tested, revised, stabilized, displaced, transported, or withdrawn. A frame may remain open or close. A correction may arrive early, late, or not at all. Records may lock in. Future action may depend on past closure. Θ therefore gives the case trajectory. But temporality cannot be inflated into development, maturity, or selfhood unless those conditions are supplied. In this case, Θ supports revision and coordination; it does not support claims about the temporal development of a person or institution.

**Φ, Recontextualization, is active and delicate.** Functional truth often changes when a claim moves between frames. A laboratory result may become a policy fact; a policy fact may become a public narrative; a public narrative may become an identity claim. The same sentence can function differently across frames. Φ therefore makes visible how correction may require reframing. But Φ is also one of the main overreach risks. PMS must not recontextualize every rival theory as merely an incomplete PMS reading. Recontextualization must preserve genuine alterity. A rival correspondence theory, semantic theory, or phenomenological account may not be “really” PMS in disguise.

**Χ, Distance, is active and critical.** Truth has a tendency to become verdict. Distance interrupts that tendency. It requires reflective withdrawal from urgency, enforcement, humiliation, and premature closure. It keeps truth-claims revisable and prevents “truth” from becoming a weapon of shaming or silencing. But Χ is not avoidance. It does not mean refusing to discriminate or never acting. The calibration challenge is to preserve enough distance to avoid coercive closure, without dissolving truth into endless suspension. If Χ is suspended, the PMS application becomes invalid as a disciplined PMS application.

**Σ, Integration, is active but bounded.** Functional truth coordinates distinctions, frames, evidence, correction, and action. It integrates heterogeneous elements into a usable structure. But this is where the main coherence risk appears: integration is not truth. A coherent account may be false. A coordinated system may be unjustified. A smooth PMS derivation may be under-discriminating. Σ must therefore preserve the differences generated by Δ and the residues carried by Λ. If Σ suppresses conflict, rival readings, or missing evidence, it becomes forced coherence rather than integration.

**Ψ, Self-Binding, is weak and conditional.** The abstract case does not establish truth as a self-binding subject, nor does it give a concrete truth-practice in which an agent binds itself to correction over time. Ψ is active only as application discipline: the analysis itself is self-bound to Distance, reversibility, non-capture, and Dignity-in-Practice. A conditional Ψ extension would be appropriate if a concrete case involved a person, institution, or system committing itself to truth-practice across time, such as a revision protocol, audit duty, or public correction norm. Without such material, Ψ must not be overused.

### PMS dependency and calibration discipline

Later PMS operators cannot be used cheaply. This is especially important in a case about truth, because truth-language invites shortcutting. The most dangerous shortcut is Δ/∇ → Ψ: a difference appears, pressure for closure follows, and the result is immediately hardened into doctrine, standard, identity claim, or irreversible commitment. That shortcut bypasses frame declaration, Λ preservation, asymmetry calibration, temporal revision, recontextualization, Distance, and integration.

Σ also cannot be used cheaply. Integration depends on Distance and Recontextualization. If a truth-reading integrates evidence or concepts without Χ, it risks becoming a verdict machine. If it integrates without Φ, it ignores frame shifts. If it integrates without Λ, it suppresses missing evidence or unresolved absence. A smooth synthesis may be rhetorically powerful while structurally invalid.

Ψ is even more expensive. Self-binding depends on Σ, Θ, and Χ. A claim cannot be bound responsibly if it has not been integrated, temporalized, and distanced. In this case, the primary formula excludes Ψ as content because the abstract material does not warrant it. Ψ appears only as application discipline unless a concrete self-binding truth-practice is supplied.

Coherence is not proof. PMS can show that a functional truth reading is structurally coherent. That does not show that truth is exhausted by function, that a particular claim is true, that the frame is adequate, or that PMS is superior to rival theories. Coherence is a necessary feature of the PMS rendering, not a truth guarantee.

Non-capture is not failure. Some aspects of truth may resist this functional reading. Semantic truth, mathematical truth, correspondence truth, phenomenological disclosure, legal proof, and empirical adequacy may require other formalisms or additional layers. PMS does not need to absorb them to remain useful. The point is not total capture; it is disciplined structural mapping.

PMS must not become a closure machine. The case itself warns against that. If PMS turns truth into PMS-legibility, then it commits the same closure error it is designed to expose. A valid PMS application must preserve its own limits.

Invalid dependency shortcuts include treating repeated success as warrant, treating coherence as truth, treating frame control as adequacy, treating absence as drift, treating disagreement as incomplete integration, treating recontextualization as assimilation, and treating self-binding as final authority. Each shortcut converts a structural grammar into a closure authority, which the case explicitly forbids.

### Example 1 — Measurement truth under a declared tolerance

A laboratory team calibrates a sensor used to monitor temperature in a controlled storage room. The question in the scene is not “What is temperature metaphysically?” and not “What is truth ultimately?” The question is narrower: whether the statement “the room is at 4°C” is true enough for the operational frame that governs storage conditions. The frame specifies an admissible measurement device, a tolerance range, a sampling interval, and a procedure for recalibration. Within that frame, “true” means that the claim is admissible and sufficient for the purpose of storage control.

The vignette is bounded. It does not establish a universal theory of truth, and it does not prove that the measured value corresponds perfectly to reality. The sensor may have drift, the room may have local gradients, the sampling interval may miss short fluctuations, and another instrument might report a slightly different value. The scene therefore contains truth-practice, not metaphysical finality. It shows how truth functions as orientation and correction: if the reading falls outside tolerance, action is taken; if it remains inside tolerance, the system continues.

The example demonstrates why □ is indispensable. Without the frame, “the room is at 4°C” floats as an unscoped claim. Inside the frame, the claim becomes functionally meaningful: it supports coordination among storage staff, monitoring software, audit records, and maintenance procedures. Δ marks the relevant distinctions: within tolerance or outside tolerance, calibrated or uncalibrated, actionable or not actionable. ∇ appears as the pressure to decide whether correction is needed. Θ appears because the reading matters across time: the room is monitored repeatedly, records persist, and a later audit may rely on earlier values.

Λ remains visible. The system does not eliminate all uncertainty. It does not know every molecular state of the room. It does not guarantee that no local variation occurred between readings. It does not prove that another valid frame would produce exactly the same truth-status. These absences are not failures of the example. They are part of its structural honesty. Truth here requires usable closure while carrying residue.

Χ is the guard against overclaim. A disciplined statement would say: “Under this measurement frame and tolerance, the room is within the accepted 4°C range.” An undisciplined statement would say: “It is simply true, full stop,” and then treat any challenge as irrational. Χ keeps the truth claim revisable: recalibration, new instruments, changed tolerances, or a different storage standard may recontextualize the claim through Φ.

Σ is active but bounded. The final operational claim integrates device reading, tolerance, calibration status, sampling rule, and storage purpose. That integration is useful and coherent, but it is not proof that truth has been exhausted. Ψ would become relevant only if the organization formally binds itself to this measurement protocol as an auditable standard. Even then, the binding would be procedural, not metaphysical.

No add-on hook is activated. PMS Core is sufficient because the example concerns functional truth as frame-bound orientation, correction, and coordination. A LOGIC overlay could be introduced in a different article if the focus shifted to warrant, induction, or justification gaps. That is not needed here. MIP, AHP, and QC are also not activated. The example is not being used for governance approval, artefact hardening, or implementation validation.

The forbidden claims are central. This example does not prove empirical truth beyond the measurement frame. It does not provide a legal or forensic finding. It does not diagnose a faulty team, negligent operator, or dishonest actor. It does not validate PMS Base. It does not show that truth is “nothing but” operational sufficiency.

The non-capture note is equally important. A physicist, metrologist, semantic theorist, or metaphysician may give a more precise account of measurement, reference, uncertainty, or correspondence. PMS does not replace those accounts. It shows how a truth claim becomes structurally usable inside praxis while preserving the limits of that usability.

The article takeaway is that functional truth can be structurally real without becoming metaphysically final. Truth orients action by discriminating within a frame, but the frame, tolerance, uncertainty, and revision pathway must remain visible.

### Example 2 — Correction without humiliation in an interpersonal coordination scene

Two colleagues are preparing a public presentation. One says, “The client approved the revised figures yesterday.” The other checks the shared record and finds that the client approved the layout but has not yet approved the figures. A correction is needed because the presentation plan depends on which items are settled. The truth issue is concrete but modest: what exactly was approved, under which record, and for which purpose.

The bounded vignette is intentionally ordinary. It does not ask who is a truthful person. It does not diagnose carelessness, deception, denial, incompetence, or bad faith. It does not decide a legal dispute. It does not establish moral blame. It shows a functional truth-practice: a claim enters a coordination scene, a difference becomes visible, correction pressure arises, and the team must restore orientation without turning the correction into a person-ranking event.

Δ appears in the distinction between “layout approved” and “figures approved.” ∇ appears as the pressure to correct the plan before the presentation is finalized. □ is the working frame: the shared project record, the client approval process, and the immediate coordination purpose. The relevant truth is not “what kind of person made the statement?” but “which approval status is admissible for this project decision?”

Λ is present as a structured absence only if the frame supports it. The expected approval of figures is absent from the record. But that absence must be handled carefully. It does not automatically mean deception, avoidance, or failure. It may mean the approval has not happened, has happened elsewhere, was communicated informally, or was recorded under a different label. PMS requires the absence to remain tied to the frame rather than inflated into a verdict.

Ω may be weak or active depending on the roles. If the correcting colleague has authority over the presenter, the correction can become humiliating. If the presenter is junior, public correction may carry exposure costs. If the decision deadline is close, urgency can intensify asymmetry. The example demonstrates why truth-practice must track who can declare the correction, who bears embarrassment, and who has room to contest or clarify.

Φ appears when the initial claim is recontextualized. The statement “approved yesterday” may shift from a simple error into a more precise frame: layout approved, figures pending. This reframing preserves the useful part of the original orientation while correcting its overreach. But Φ must not be abused. It would be over-assimilative to redescribe every disagreement as merely a need for recontextualization. Sometimes the claim is simply unsupported; sometimes the frame is wrong; sometimes the record is incomplete.

Χ is the key operator. A truth correction can be structurally valid but pragmatically coercive if Distance collapses. “You lied about the approval” is a different act from “The record shows layout approval, but not figure approval; we should mark the figures as pending.” The second formulation preserves correction without person-ranking. It keeps the claim scene-bound and revisable.

Σ integrates the corrected record, the presentation plan, the client process, and the next action. The team may decide to label the figures as provisional and request explicit approval. That is coherent coordination, not metaphysical proof. Ψ would become relevant only if the team binds itself to a future rule, such as “approval claims must cite the record before public use.” Such binding would be a practice commitment, not a judgment about anyone’s worth.

No add-on is needed. PMS Core already captures the closure-demand, the correction process, the asymmetry risk, the need for Distance, and the bounded integration. MIP, AHP, and QC remain inactive because the example is not a governance assessment, claim-hardening artefact, or quality-control validation.

Forbidden claims must remain explicit. The example must not be used to infer lying, incompetence, moral failure, clinical traits, or legal liability. It must not turn truth into a disciplinary weapon. It must not treat the corrected person as less mature, less worthy, or structurally defective. It must not validate PMS Base by showing that PMS can describe the scene.

The non-capture note matters because other theories can illuminate aspects PMS does not address. A speech-act theory might analyze assertion and correction. A virtue epistemology might discuss intellectual responsibility. A workplace governance frame might require formal documentation rules. PMS does not replace those readings. It maps the structural truth-function: discrimination, correction, reframing, Distance, and coordination under possible asymmetry.

The article takeaway is that truth can correct action without becoming humiliation. A valid functional truth-practice does not merely identify what is wrong; it preserves frame, reversibility, asymmetry awareness, and Dignity-in-Practice.

### Example 3 — A model claim transported from technical frame to public narrative

A research group publishes a model evaluation stating that a system achieved “92% accuracy” on a benchmark. Inside the technical report, the claim has a defined frame: a particular dataset, task formulation, metric, test split, preprocessing pipeline, and evaluation date. Within that frame, the statement may be admissible and useful. It helps compare model versions, coordinate development, and decide whether further testing is needed.

Later, the same number appears in a public communication as “the system is 92% accurate.” The sentence looks similar, but the frame has shifted. The public version may imply broader reliability, general competence, or readiness for use. The PMS issue is not whether the original metric was fabricated. The issue is whether a truth claim changed function when transported across frames.

The vignette demonstrates Φ in a high-clarity way. Recontextualization is not decorative; it changes the truth conditions of the claim. “92% accuracy on Benchmark X under Procedure Y” is not identical to “92% accurate in practice.” The former may be a bounded technical truth. The latter may be under-specified, overextended, or misleading if the deployment domain, error costs, distribution shift, and user exposure are not declared.

Δ marks the relevant distinctions: benchmark performance versus real-world performance, metric accuracy versus practical reliability, internal documentation versus public claim, bounded evidence versus generalized trust. ∇ appears as closure pressure: stakeholders want a simple number that orients belief and action. □ is the technical evaluation frame at first, then the public communication frame later. The frame shift is where risk enters.

Λ is substantial but must remain precise. Missing elements may include deployment evidence, subgroup performance, robustness tests, failure modes, uncertainty intervals, or error-cost analysis. These absences do not automatically prove deception, bad faith, or implementation failure. They show that the public truth claim may exceed the support supplied by the original frame.

Α may become active if the organization repeatedly converts benchmark statements into broad public claims. But in a single vignette, Α should remain only a possible risk. Ω may also become relevant: the organization may have authority, visibility, or marketing power, while users or affected parties bear the cost of misunderstood reliability. But the example should not assert wrongdoing without a concrete evidential scene. It marks asymmetry as a condition to inspect.

The longer the generalized claim circulates, the more costly correction becomes. Reversal may require public clarification, updated documentation, or revised decision processes.

No add-on is activated in this article example. However, it contains possible hooks for later nearby sections. Forbidden claims are especially important here. The example does not establish empirical invalidity of the model. It does not prove deceptive marketing. The non-capture note preserves rival methods. The article takeaway is that functional truth requires frame discipline when claims travel. A statement may be true enough in one frame and misleading in another, not because truth is arbitrary, but because admissibility, sufficiency, exposure, and revision conditions have changed.

### Non-capture and rival readings

The case preserves multiple non-capture zones. A functional PMS reading may not capture metaphysical correspondence truth, where truth is treated as relation between claim and reality. PMS can represent the praxis of making and using correspondence claims, but it does not prove correspondence itself.

It may not capture semantic truth, where truth is formal satisfaction under a language-model relation. PMS can model how semantic truth is used in praxis, but it does not replace formal semantics.

It may not capture proof-theoretic or mathematical truth, where derivability, proof, consistency, or formal construction may matter more than practical coordination. A mathematical truth may remain true regardless of immediate praxis-function.

It may not capture deflationary or minimalist accounts, where truth is not a substantial property but a logical or linguistic device. PMS may describe how such devices function in praxis, but it does not settle whether the deflationary account is correct.

It may not capture phenomenological disclosure, where truth is treated as unconcealment, appearing, or disclosure of being. PMS can map frame and praxis effects, but it does not claim to exhaust lived disclosure or phenomenological givenness.

It may not capture religious, revelatory, aesthetic, or existential truth, where truth may be bound to forms of disclosure, fidelity, witness, beauty, or transformation that are not reducible to coordination.

It may not capture legal-forensic truth, where truth is established under institutional rules of evidence, burdens of proof, admissibility standards, and procedural safeguards. PMS can map such a scene if supplied, but this case does not supply one.

It may not capture empirical-scientific truth, where observation, measurement, replication, prediction, and error correction are domain-specific. PMS can model the praxis structure of empirical inquiry, but it does not itself supply evidence.

Rival readings therefore remain genuine rivals, not defects. A correspondence reading preserves the relation between claim and world. A coherence reading preserves systemic consistency. A pragmatic reading preserves inquiry-success and practical consequences. A deflationary reading preserves linguistic economy. A semantic reading preserves formal satisfaction. A proof-theoretic reading preserves derivability or warranted assertion. A phenomenological reading preserves disclosure. A legal-forensic reading preserves procedural fact-finding. A scientific-realist reading preserves empirically constrained approximation to mind-independent structures.

The three examples do not eliminate these rival readings. The measurement example may invite a metrological or correspondence account. The interpersonal correction example may invite speech-act theory, virtue epistemology, or organizational communication analysis. The model-claim example may invite statistical validation, scientific methodology, auditing, or legal advertising analysis. PMS does not displace those. It shows the structural praxis-form through which truth-claims orient, correct, coordinate, and overreach.

The PMS reading therefore does not defeat rival theories. It clarifies what functional truth does inside praxis and where such a reading must stop.

### Weakening, falsifier, and operationalization conditions

The PMS reading would be weakened if the case required a theory of truth that does not involve praxis, orientation, discrimination, correction, or coordination. If the relevant truth-domain is purely formal, metaphysical, semantic, or proof-theoretic, PMS may still offer a praxis-side map, but it would not capture the main object.

The reading would be weakened if functional coordination succeeds while truth fails. For example, a false story may coordinate a group; a misleading model may guide action temporarily; an institutional claim may stabilize records while being factually wrong. Such cases would show why Σ and coordination cannot be equated with truth.

The reading would be weakened if coherent PMS integration produced a false result. This is a central falsifier condition: PMS coherence is not proof. If PMS makes a claim legible but loses discriminative force, the reading becomes under-discriminating.

The reading would be weakened if a rival formalization explains the case with sharper distinctions and less assimilation. If a semantic, correspondence, proof-theoretic, or empirical account better captures the relevant truth-conditions, PMS must preserve that limit rather than absorb the rival.

The reading would be weakened if absence, disagreement, resistance, or non-fit were automatically interpreted as drift or incomplete integration. That would violate the non-capture rule. A rival framing may be a genuine alternative, not a PMS residue.

The reading would be weakened if the operative frame cannot be specified. Without □, truth becomes unscoped. The analysis would then fail to distinguish truth from authority or rhetorical closure.

The reading would be weakened if correction criteria cannot be distinguished from power, preference, usefulness, or agreement. If “true” merely means “what the frame-setter declares,” then the PMS reading has not preserved truth; it has mapped authority.

The reading would be weakened if truth is reduced to PMS-legibility. PMS must not become the thing it is warning against: a closure machine that converts its own coherence into authority.

Operationalization would require more than the present case material supplies. A stronger empirical application would need observable indicators for Δ, ∇, □, Λ, Α, Ω, Θ, Φ, Χ, Σ, and possibly Ψ. It would need a specific scene, evidence base, frame, expected events, recurrence criteria, asymmetry map, temporal sequence, recontextualization rules, distance conditions, integration criteria, and self-binding criteria where relevant.

Comparative validation would require testing PMS against rival models. Computational validation would require architecture-specific implementation and evaluation. Legal validation would require legal standards. Clinical validation would require clinical domain evidence and would still not permit diagnosis unless explicitly warranted. Applied validation would require external accountability and public intelligibility. None of these are fulfilled here.

The examples clarify these conditions rather than remove them. The measurement example shows how operational truth needs declared tolerance and calibration. The correction example shows why truth-practice needs Distance and Dignity-in-Practice under possible asymmetry. The model-claim example shows how Φ transport can weaken a truth claim if the new frame is not declared. In all three cases, the examples demonstrate the analysis; they do not validate PMS, prove truth, or convert risks into findings.

### Misuse boundary

This case must not be used diagnostically. It does not identify truthful or untruthful persons, sincere or insincere persons, mature or immature persons, rational or irrational persons, healthy or pathological persons.

It must not be used for person ranking. Truth-language cannot be converted into dignity ranking, worth ranking, epistemic caste, moral superiority, or status assignment.

It must not be used as a legal or forensic conclusion. The case supplies no evidentiary standard, burden of proof, testimony structure, admissibility rule, or adjudicative procedure.

It must not be used as metaphysical proof. PMS does not prove what truth ultimately is.

It must not be used as empirical proof. The case supplies no evidence.

It must not be used as implementation validation. YAML representation is not a system, protocol, verification engine, fact-checker, or agent architecture.

It must not be used as PMS Base validation. The fact that PMS can render the case coherently does not prove PMS completeness, uniqueness, finality, or superiority.

It must not treat an add-on as an operator. No add-on is activated here, and no overlay may redefine Δ–Ψ.

It must not treat drift as verdict. AD_A>>E and AD_Sigma_low are possible risks, not findings.

It must not close by authority. A truth claim becomes invalid as PMS application if it suspends Χ, suppresses Λ, ignores Ω, collapses rival framings into failure, or hardens Σ into Ψ without warrant.

The core misuse boundary is simple: this case maps how truth-closure may function inside praxis; it does not authorize anyone to declare final truth, silence contest, rank persons, or enforce closure.

### Case conclusion

The integrative result is that **Truth (functional)** can be rendered through PMS Core as a bounded praxis structure: truth discriminates, orients, corrects, recontextualizes, distances, and integrates coordination within a declared frame. The primary structural formula, **Σ ∘ Χ ∘ Φ ∘ Θ ∘ Ωᶜ ∘ Αᶜ ∘ Λᶜ ∘ □ ∘ ∇ ∘ Δ**, captures this without claiming metaphysical finality. Δ, ∇, □, Θ, Φ, Χ, and Σ carry the main analytic weight. Λ, Α, and Ω remain calibration-dependent because the case is abstract and does not supply a concrete expected absence, recurrent practice, or asymmetry structure. Ψ remains conditional: it belongs to the application discipline itself, and to future concrete truth-practices only where sustained self-binding is explicitly supplied.

The examples contribute three forms of clarification. The measurement example shows how truth becomes operationally useful only under a declared frame, tolerance, and revision pathway. The interpersonal correction example shows how truth can correct coordination without becoming humiliation, diagnosis, or person-ranking. The model-claim example shows how a bounded claim can become under-discriminating when transported across frames without explicit recontextualization. Together, the examples demonstrate the PMS reading without replacing it, proving it, or turning it into evidence for PMS Base.

What remains unclaimed is as important as what is claimed. The article does not prove a theory of truth. It does not decide between correspondence, coherence, pragmatic, semantic, proof-theoretic, phenomenological, empirical, legal-forensic, or deflationary accounts. It does not diagnose truth-users, rank persons, validate implementations, establish empirical findings, or supply legal conclusions. It does not claim that PMS coherence is truth. It does not claim that non-capture is failure.

The boundary discipline is therefore decisive. Functional truth is not arbitrary, but neither is it final authority. It requires frame declaration, discrimination, residue preservation, asymmetry awareness, temporal revision, recontextualization discipline, Distance, and bounded integration. If any of these are skipped, truth-practice can become closure laundering: difference plus urgency becomes doctrine; coherence becomes proof; correction becomes coercion; integration becomes verdict; self-binding becomes irreversible authority.

The final methodological takeaway is that PMS Core can make functional truth structurally legible without solving truth by authority. AXIOM’s contribution here is cartographic: it shows where truth-closure is possible, where it remains useful, where it becomes risky, and where Λ-residue and rival readings must remain visible.
