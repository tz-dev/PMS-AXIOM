# Block Article Generation

## Prompt 1

Guidance for rewriting long case Markdown articles into chapter-ready case sections

ASSUMPTION:
The general AXIOM / PMS boundary discipline is already stated at the beginning of the relevant Part or chapter.

The Part introduction already explains:
- which stack applies in that Part
  - PART I — PMS Core only
  - PART II — PMS + LOGIC
  - etc.
- when add-ons are used and why
- that add-ons remain overlay lenses and do not replace PMS operators
- that PMS / AXIOM does not diagnose
- that PMS / AXIOM does not evaluate or rank persons
- that PMS / AXIOM does not produce legal, forensic, clinical, empirical, metaphysical, or implementation conclusions
- that PMS application does not validate PMS Base
- that risk is not finding
- that coherence is not proof
- that non-capture remains possible
- that rival readings remain open
- that Dignity-in-Practice is praxeological, not ontological
- that drift patterns are not verdict engines
- that examples are illustrative, not evidentiary
- that PMS operators are named at first systematic case-specific use in the rewritten section, e.g. `Δ (Difference)`, `∇ (Impulse)`, `□ (Frame)`, `Λ (Non-event / residue)`, `Α (Attractor)`, `Ω (Asymmetry)`, `Θ (Temporality)`, `Φ (Recontextualization)`, `Χ (Distance)`, `Σ (Integration)`, `Ψ (Self-binding)`

THEREFORE, THE INDIVIDUAL CASE SECTION SHOULD NOT REPEAT IN FULL:
- stack
- output type
- add-on decision
- long claim-boundary lists
- general misuse-boundary lists
- general PMS / AXIOM discipline
- general explanations that no diagnosis, person-ranking, legal conclusion, metaphysical proof, empirical proof, implementation validation, or PMS Base validation is being made
- generic add-on discipline already stated at the Part opening
- generic MIP / AHP / QC discipline unless the specific case requires it

EXCEPTION:
If a case creates a distinctive misuse risk, that risk must still be mentioned inside the case itself.

Examples:
- Free Will vs Determinism:
  - Θ must not be read as metaphysical determinism.
  - Ψ must not be read as ultimate authorship.
  - Responsibility must not be read as legal culpability.

- Personal identity over time:
  - SELF_FIXPOINT must not be read as metaphysical proof of numerical identity.
  - Memory, body, narrative, law, and recognition remain open rival framings.
  - Dignity-in-Practice protects against personhood ranking.

- MIP-specific cases:
  - If MIP is active only for selected cases, mention it specifically in those cases.
  - If MIP is active throughout a Part, such as EDEN or SEX, state this at the Part opening.
  - Inside the individual case, preserve only the case-specific MIP result.
  - Score hygiene, non-assignment, working-note status, IA non-assignment, D-module status, and no-person-finding discipline should be preserved only where they are case-specific.

GOAL:
Rewrite the long case articles into shorter chapter subsections.

Not:
- a tiny case box
- a shallow summary
- a YAML audit dossier
- a repetition of the general Part boundaries

But:
- a readable, analytically substantial case section
- retaining the case-specific structural movement
- moving generic boundary discipline to the Part opening
- preserving the examples in full
- preserving case-specific limits where needed
- adding no new analysis
- adding no new case claims
- rewriting no model

EXAMPLES RULE:
Examples must not be shortened or analytically thinned.
They may only be lightly polished for flow or relocated within the case section.
Their vignette, demonstration, PMS/add-on hooks, boundaries, non-capture note, and takeaway must remain intact.

Example-generation metadata must be removed from the final chapter section.
Do not preserve lines such as:
- `**Type:** worked`
- `**Type:** compact`
- `**Type:** micro`
- `**Type:** flagship`
- `**Recommended insertion point:** ...`

These lines are drafting metadata, not article prose.
Preserve the example title and the example body, but remove type and insertion-point labels.

IMPORTANT:
The examples must never be shortened.
They may be relocated into the shortened case section, but their internal content should be preserved except for removed drafting metadata.

CASE CAPSULE RULE:
Do not preserve the audit-style case capsule inside the shortened chapter section.
Stack, add-on decision, output type, and general claim boundary are handled at the Part opening or in the case index.
Only retain case-file references if the target chapter format explicitly requires them.

LAYER COMPLEXITY RULE:
The individual case section may range from PMS Core only to a full-stack case.

Do not repeat the full stack as an audit capsule.
The Part opening already states which stack applies.

Instead, preserve only the case-specific contribution of each active layer.

Core-only cases:
- Preserve the PMS Core structural movement.
- Mention no add-on absence unless the case specifically requires it.
- Do not include generic “no add-on” explanations.

One-add-on cases:
- Preserve what the add-on makes visible beyond PMS Core.
- Do not re-explain the add-on in general.
- Do not repeat generic overlay discipline.
- Make clear, where needed, that add-on handles remain overlay handles and do not become PMS operators.
- Keep the add-on contribution case-specific.

Core + add-on + MIP cases:
- Preserve the case-specific MIP result only where it matters.
- If MIP is active but non-scoring, state that as a case-specific result where relevant.
- Do not repeat generic MIP theory.
- Do not preserve full MIP audit tables.
- Preserve score hygiene only in case-specific form:
  - non-assignment is not a low score
  - working-note status is not a verdict
  - no person finding follows
- In Parts where MIP is always active, such as EDEN or SEX, the Part opening should state this once.
- Individual cases should then mention MIP only where the case-specific MIP result is analytically relevant.

Full-stack cases:
- Full-stack means PMS Core plus one or more add-ons plus downstream MIP / AHP / QC / QC-EXT where applicable.
- Do not narrate the full stack mechanically.
- Do not preserve every layer’s full audit apparatus.
- Preserve the layered movement:
  1. what PMS Core maps
  2. what the add-on sharpens
  3. what MIP adds or refuses to assign
  4. what AHP / QC / QC-EXT hardens, checks, or constrains, but only if that layer is actually active in the case
- If AHP is present, state only the case-specific hardening result.
- AHP does not rescore, does not activate D, does not upgrade transmission status, and does not create new case findings.
- If QC or QC-EXT is present, preserve structural-not-physical discipline and non-guarantee language only where the case specifically requires it.
- If QC or QC-EXT is NOT present, DO NOT MENTION IT IN A NON-QC CASE!
- Do not mention QC or QC-EXT in Core-only, non-QC, or unrelated cases merely as inactive possibilities.
- Full-stack does not mean stronger verdict.
- Full-stack means more explicit layer separation.

For SEX and EDEN Parts:
- MIP is normally active as part of the Part-level stack.
- Do not repeat this in every case as a full stack statement.
- Mention MIP inside the case only when its result matters:
  - non-scoring
  - working-note status
  - IA non-assignment
  - D-module off / D not activated
  - score hygiene
  - no person finding
  - no maturity or adultness verdict
- If MIP produces a substantive case-specific structure, preserve that structure.
- If MIP mainly refuses assignment, preserve that refusal as the result, not as a failure.

General rule:
The more layers a case has, the more important it is to preserve layer separation.
The rewritten case should not become longer because it repeats each layer’s generic boundary discipline.
It should remain substantial because it preserves what each active layer specifically contributes to this case.

RECOMMENDED CASE STRUCTURE:

## [Exact case title]

### Why this case is here

Give a case-specific explanation of:
- which closure demand or structural tension the case shows
- why it belongs in this Part / chapter
- what it makes visible for AXIOM
- why the case is mapped rather than solved

If the YAML marks the case as a conceptual projection rather than a concrete scene, state this briefly and explain the consequence: operators such as responsibility, action, asymmetry, and self-binding may be structurally rendered but must not become scene-specific findings without additional evidence.

Do not repeat the general boundary discipline.

### Structural reading

Give a compact but substantial rendering of:
- the main PMS Core movement
- the most important active operators or operator groups
- relevant dependencies and calibrations
- in add-on Parts: what the add-on makes visible beyond PMS Core
- in MIP / AHP / QC cases: the specific additional layer only where it matters
- in examples, mention downstream layers only if they are active or analytically necessary; otherwise use neutral wording such as “a downstream layer would become relevant only if the vignette were transformed into an institutional policy artefact”

Do not preserve a full operator-by-operator audit list unless the case genuinely requires it.

The internal logic of the case must remain understandable.

### What the analysis shows

State the case-specific result:
- central structural formula or movement
- what PMS / the add-on makes visible
- what kind of closure is possible
- where Λ-residue or non-capture remains visible

### Case-specific boundary

Mention only the distinctive boundaries of this concrete case:
- no generic misuse list
- no repetition of all AXIOM rules
- only the risks that this case specifically invites

Example:
“Here, Ψ marks structural self-binding only; it does not prove metaphysical personal identity.”

### Example(s)

Preserve the examples in full, but remove drafting metadata such as `**Type:** ...` and `**Recommended insertion point:** ...`.

The examples should remain:
- concrete enough to make the case readable
- illustrative, not evidentiary
- bounded by the completed analysis
- free of new claims
- free of verdicts, diagnosis, person-ranking, legal/forensic claims, clinical claims, metaphysical proof, empirical proof, implementation validation, or PMS Base validation

Examples may be placed where they best support the section’s argument.

### Case takeaway

Give a short synthesis:
- what the case shows methodologically
- how it should be used in the chapter
- which boundary or Λ-residue remains visible

LENGTH GUIDANCE:
Do not impose a strict shortness target.
The section will already become shorter by moving general boundary material, stack explanations, add-on decisions, misuse discipline, and repeated claim limitations to the Part opening.

The rewritten case should be as long as it needs to be to preserve:
- the case-specific closure demand
- the case-specific PMS / add-on structure
- relevant operator calibration
- the relevant formula or structural movement
- relevant rival readings and non-capture, in condensed but meaningful form
- relevant falsifier / weakening conditions, where case-specific
- all examples in full

REMOVE OR STRONGLY CONDENSE:
- audit-style case capsule
- stack / output type / add-on decision
- generic claim-boundaries
- generic misuse-boundaries
- generic PMS Base discipline
- full operator lists where they do not add case-specific value
- repeated explanations of risk is not finding / coherence is not proof / non-capture
- long generic lists already handled at the Part opening

PRESERVE:
- exact case title
- case-specific closure demand
- case-specific PMS / add-on structural reading
- case-specific operator calibration
- case-specific formula or structural movement
- case-specific non-capture and rival readings
- case-specific weakening / falsifier conditions where relevant
- all examples in full
- case-specific boundary warnings
- final case takeaway

Go!
